Cleveland Guardians (68-75) vs. San Francisco Giants (72-70)

The 2023 Major League Baseball season continues Monday, September 11, with the Cleveland Guardians facing off against the San Francisco Giants in the interleague battle at Oracle Park in San Francisco, California.   

Cleveland and San Francisco open a three-game series. They lock horns for the first time in 2023, and the Giants eye their sixth straight victory over the Guardians. Last year, the Giants swept a three-game set at Progressive Field in Cleveland. 

The Guardians continue their west-coast trip

Sunday’s games have been excluded from this preview, and the 68-75 Cleveland Guardians played the Los Angeles Angels in the closer of a four-game set at Angel Stadium in Anaheim. The Guardians have dropped five of their previous seven contests including a pair of losses against the Halos, 3-2 and 6-2. 

Cleveland was second in the AL Central on Sunday morning, trailing the Minnesota Twins by 7.5 games. The Guardians are slashing .255/.321/.388 with 27 extra-base hits and 11 home runs in the last two weeks and 466 at-bats, while their bullpen has compiled a pedestrian 5.40 ERA and 5.22 FIP during that span (58.1 innings pitched). 

Gavin Williams will take the hill for the Guardians, and the 24-year-old rookie right-hander is 2-5 with a 3.34 ERA, 1.26 WHIP, and 72/31 K/BB ratio in 14 starts (70 IP) in 2023. Williams hasn’t thrown more than five innings in five straight starts. He’s registered a 4.43 ERA, 4.54 FIP, and .772 opposing OPS over those five starts and 20.1 innings of work. Last Wednesday, Williams pitched five frames of a one-run, one-hit ball in a winning effort against the Minnesota Twins.

The Giants chase the wild card in the National League

The 72-70 San Francisco Giants hosted the Colorado Rockies on Sunday night, looking to sweep a three-game series at Oracle Park. After stopping a six-game losing streak with a 9-8 victory on Friday night, the Giants trounced the Rockies 9-1 on Saturday. San Francisco was two games behind the final wild-card spot in the National League on Sunday morning, and the Giants desperately need to stay on the winning track. 

Over the last couple of weeks, the Giants are hitting .244/.313/.407 with 30 extra-base hits and 13 home runs in 430 at-bats. Their pitching staff has recorded a 4.70 ERA, 1.25 WHIP, and .254 batting average against in those two weeks (111 IP). 

The Giants haven’t announced who’ll get the starting call Monday. Alex Cobb is expected to return to the rotation after throwing just three innings due to a hip injury on September 3. The 35-year-old righty is 7-6 with a 3.74 ERA, 1.32 WHIP, and 128/35 K/BB ratio in 26 starts (144.1 IP) this season. The Giants bullpen has recorded an underwhelming 4.66 ERA and 4.34 FIP in the past two weeks and 48.1 innings of work.
