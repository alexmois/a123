The Miami Marlins and Milwaukee Brewers will play Monday at American Family Field. First pitch is at 6:40 p.m. CT.

Miami (73-69 SU and 65-77 RL) is starting left-hander Jesús Luzardo. The 25-year-old is 9-8 with a 3.59 ERA and a 1.20 WHIP in his 28 outings this year.

Milwaukee (79-62 SU and 67-74 RL) is starting right-hander Brandon Woodruff. The 30-year-old is 4-1 with a 2.30 ERA and a 0.79 WHIP in eight outings.

The Brew Crew are -158 moneyline favorites and the game total is eight runs.

Miami is in the thick of the NL Wild Card race

Miami wrapped up a series with Philadelphia on Sunday. It's coming off consecutive series wins over the Dodgers and Nationals, helping it keep pace with the other NL Wild Card contenders.

The Marlins average 4.11 runs (25th) and hit .259 (7th) with a .719 OPS (19th). They've hit 145 long balls (24th) and stolen 69 bases (28th) this season. The MIA pitching staff has posted a 4.20 ERA (16th) and a 1.28 WHIP (15th) with 47 quality starts (17th).

Luzardo will toe the rubber for the visitors in the series opener. In his last start, the fifth-year southpaw held Los Angeles to two runs and four hits with six strikeouts in six innings. He finished August with a couple of quality starts after surrendering 17 runs (16 earned) in his first three starts (12.1 IP). Luzardo is 5-4 with a 4.34 ERA and 1.34 WHIP on the road this season (11 outings).

Milwaukee is going to be a tough out this postseason

Milwaukee was victorious in two of its last three series, beating the Yankees and Phillies and losing to the Pirates. Can it hold onto its lead in the NL Central standings?

The Brewers score 4.45 runs per game (18th) and hit .237 (26th) with a .698 OPS (25th). They've launched 145 long balls (23rd) and stolen 114 bases (10th) this year. The Milwaukee pitching staff sports a 3.94 ERA (7th) and a 1.20 WHIP (3rd) with 61 quality starts (8th).

Woodruff will take the hill for the home team on Monday night. In his last start, the seventh-year pro held Pittsburgh scoreless in seven innings. He was 2-1 with a 3.45 ERA and 0.80 WHIP in five starts last month (28.2 IP). Woodruff is 4-0 with a 2.10 ERA and 0.87 WHIP in five night games this year.

Best Bets for this Game

Full-Game Side Bet

Insiders Status:

Rating:

Milwaukee has its ace on the mound and is coming off a series win over the Yankees. Miami was held in check by Philly over the weekend and doesn't hit right-handers nearly as well (.251 BA/.309 OBP/.397 SLG/.706 OPS) as lefties (.285 BA/.331 OBP/.429 SLG/.761 OPS). It hasn't produced at the plate away from home, too (.249 BA/.307 OBP/.401 SLG/.708 OPS).

The Brew Crew hits left-handers (.243 BA/.318 OBP/.396 SLG/.714 OPS) slightly better than right-handers (.235 BA/.316 OBP/.375 SLG/.692 OPS) and produces more often at the dish at home (4.7 runs/game), where it's 40-28 straight-up this year. Luzardo has been inconsistent recently, with three poor performances and three good showings.

This could turn out to be a pitcher's duel, but I'm not as confident in Luzardo as I am in Woodruff. One way or another, I expect Milwaukee to come out on top, so I'm very comfortable betting the Brewers ML!

Prediction: Brew Crew ML (-158)

Full-Game Total Pick

Insiders Status:

Rating:

I don't expect Luzardo to continue his streak of quality starts against the Brewers, who are scoring about 4.9 runs per game since the All-Star break. They scored 17 runs in their first two games versus the Yankees, and while they didn't score too many runs on Sunday, I'm predicting their bats will warm up again at home against the inconsistent Marlins starter. Should he be forced to exit the game too soon, the Miami bullpen (4.19 ERA) is likely to surrender a couple of more runs.

While I'm not expecting too much of a contribution to the total from the Fins' lineup, I feel a bet on seven or fewer runs is too risky with Luzardo far past his career high in innings pitched and likely to continue to show signs of fatigue down the stretch of the regular season.

Prediction: Over 8
