The Toronto Blue Jays (79-63) will be trying to increase their lead in the Wildcard standings when they face the Texas Rangers (77-64) in the first game of their three-game series on Monday night.

Chris Bassitt, 14-7, 3.69 ERA, will get the start for the Blue Jays. The Rangers will counter with Andrew Heaney, 9-6, 4.22 ERA.

Toronto is 6-4 in its last 10 games against Texas.

**This preview was written before Sunday’s games were played**

Rangers Trying To Keep Wildcard Hopes Alive

The Rangers didn’t play well over the past week and they’ve lost four of their last five games. They are now 1.5 games behind the Blue Jays in the Wildcard standings and three games behind Houston in the AL West standings. They will try to reduce the distance with a win on Monday, which will give them their second win in their last six games.

Texas is averaging 5.45 runs per game. Their .266 batting average is second in the league. Their .337 on base percentage is third, as is their .452 slugging percentage.

Corey Seager leads the Rangers with a .335 batting average, while Adolis Garcia leads the team with 34 home runs and 100 RBI.

Texas’ pitching has been good, with the team giving up 4.48 runs per game. Opponents have a .246 batting average against the Rangers, which is 17th in the league. Their 4.31 ERA is also 17th, while their 1.27 WHIP is 13th.

In his last start, Heaney gave up five hits and three runs in 4.2 innings, leading to a 13-6 loss to Houston. They will need a better effort from him if they want to win this game.

Blue Jays Going For Sixth Win In Seven Games

The Blue Jays are playing well at the moment and they’ve won five of their last six games. They will try to keep the momentum going with a win over the Rangers, which will give them their sixth win in their last seven games.

Toronto is averaging 4.61 runs per game. Their .260 batting average is fifth in the league. Their .333 on base percentage is fourth, while their .421 slugging percentage is 12th.

Bo Bichette leads the Blue Jays with a .315 batting average, while Vladimir Guerrero Jr. leads the team with 21 home runs and 84 RBI.

Toronto’s pitching has been good, with the team giving up 4.06 runs per game. Opponents have a .239 batting average against the Blue Jays, which is sixth in the league. Their 3.70 ERA leads the league, while their 1.23 WHIP is sixth.

In his last start, Bassitt gave up seven hits and one run in eight innings, leading to a 7-1 win over Oakland. They will need a similar effort from him if they want to get the win.

Brandon Belt (Back) is questionable for this game.
