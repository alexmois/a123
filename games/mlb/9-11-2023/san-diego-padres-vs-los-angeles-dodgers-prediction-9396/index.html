The San Diego Padres face the Los Angeles Dodgers with both teams heading in opposite directions to close out the season. The Padres have a 67-77 record, have the second-worst record in the National League West Division, and with consecutive losses, they have been eliminated from postseason contention. The Dodgers have an 87-55 record and have the best record in the National League West Division. For the upcoming game, the Padres will turn to Pedro Avila as their starting pitcher while the Dodgers will turn to Gavin Stone who has allowed only five runs in his last two starts.

Padres hope Avila helps out lineup

The Padres entered the season with World Series aspirations but might not finish above .500 this year. Pedro Avila has been one of the bright spots as he's allowed only 14 runs in 37 innings pitched while tossing 38 strikeouts. In addition, with a .321 expected opponent Slugging Percentage, a 4.0 Barrel Percentage, and a 3.85 expected ERA, opponents are making weak contact and struggling to drive in runs against Avila.

Avila has stepped up but the lineup has struggled, scoring only 4.57 runs per game. Juan Soto is slashing .257/.398/.483 with 28 home runs and 242 total bases while Ha-Seong Kim is slashing .271/.362/.419 with 17 home runs and 201 total bases but the rest of the lineup has struggled. Only five batters have an OPS+ over 100 and opposing pitchers can limit the top-heavy lineup as a result.

Dodgers hope Stone and lineup start series strong

The Dodgers are having a great season and hope to make a push to finish with the best record in the American League. Gavin Stone will start the upcoming game and he is having a rough season on the mound, allowing 22 runs in 18 innings pitched. Moreover, with a .340 expected opponent Batting Average, a .532 expected opponent Slugging Percentage, and a 7.48 expected ERA, opponents are making contact and powering the ball against Stone to easily drive in runs.

Stone has struggled but the lineup has carried the Dodgers, scoring 5.62 runs per game. Mookie Betts is slashing .314/.411/.609 with 38 home runs and 314 total bases while Freddie Freeman is slashing .335/.413/.573 with 25 home runs and 320 total bases but the rest of the lineup has stepped up as well. J.D. Martinez is slashing .262/.312/.557 with 26 home runs and 200 total bases while Max Muncy and James Outman have added 52 home runs and 382 total bases to round out the lineup, making the Dodgers tough to put away at the plate.
