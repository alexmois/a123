The Chicago White Sox play host to the Kansas City Royals in an AL Central showdown on Monday night from Guaranteed Rate Field. Both these teams have struggled this season and with neither having playoff aspirations at this point, it'll certainly be interesting to see how each side navigates this one. On the season, this has been one of baseball's more balanced series, with each team grabbing five wins over the last ten matchups. Expect a back-and-forth affair in this one, especially as both sides put out interesting lineup combinations.

Royals Aim To Finish 2023 Strong

The Royals have been one of baseball's worst teams for much of the season and after a better run of play to end the summer, the fall has seen a return to their struggles. They only have 44 wins on the season, to go along with a .306 winning percentage, while going 3-7 over their last ten games simply isn't going to cut it. It doesn't help matters that they're 3-7 over their last ten games, which is one of the worst marks in baseball. Having to open a road series on Monday night could be a challenge for them anyway but adding in the lack of confidence and momentum that has plagued them of late, only adds to the issues at hand that will need to be fixed this off-season.

According to MLB.com, the Royals are uncertain on who they'll be handing the ball off to in this one, which is a dangerous sign. As a team, they have a 5.21 ERA, with a .261 average against, while the starters have been even worse, to the tune of a 5.38 ERA. Whoever gets the ball, will have the added issue of making a road start, as starting fast has been something difficult for this staff to overcome.

It doesn't help matters that the offense is one of the worst in baseball, which only serves to put additional pressure on the rotation,. They average 4.1 runs per game, hitting .239 as a team. The season actually started fast for them but injury to Vinnie Pasquantino (shoulder) quickly derailed some of the momentum they had. Bobby Witt Jr. (.272 average, 28 home runs, 86 RBI) remains one if the most exciting young bats in baseball but without much help, they could struggle to keep pace in this one.

White Sox Struggles Continue

After another season after which off-season acquisitions gave promise that the White Sox would be a playoff-bound, it has been another quiet season for this group. The only two teams worse than them in the entire American League are Kansas and Oakland, while they sit 20 games behind the Twins in the division, which also happens to be the worst one in baseball. All signs pointed towards a great 2023 but between injuries and various struggles, both internally and externally, they already have their eyes set on 2024. Even a 28-40 record doesn't leave much to be expected, especially given the uncertainty in the lineup that will be out there nightly.

According to MLB.com, the White Sox are in a similar position to their divisional foes in this one. With uncertainty surrounding their rotation, it remains to be seen who they'll hand the ball off to on Monday night. Their ERA is quite a bit better than that of Kansas City, sitting at 4.88 as a team and 5.20 as a starting rotation. The bullpen has been stronger of late, though it might not be enough.

It shouldn't come as a surprise that the offense has taken a serious step back this season as well. Once one of the most feared lineups in baseball, they are only averaging a hair over four runs s game themselves, while their team average is actually lower than Kansas City's. The lineup has gotten healthier as the season has progressed, while Yoan Moncada (knee) is the only position player of note listed, as he's questionable. Luis Robert Jr. (.270 average, 35 home runs, 73 RBI) has been the lone bright spot for the team and much like their opponent, without much help, it's difficult to find success.
