Run for the Wildcard

The Arizona Diamondbacks are where they want to be with roughly three weeks to go in the regular season. They sit in the third spot in the National League Wildcard standings as they battle five teams for three spots and the right to get into the 2023 playoffs. The Diamondbacks were the talk of baseball in the first half as they raced out to the lead in the National League West Division. The team cooled off and appeared to be heading to another playoff-less season but rallied in late August into September. Arizona arrives in New York on a roll after winning four straight games heading into the series finale against the Cubs in Chicago on Sunday. Going for the sweep, the Diamondbacks fell short in a 5-2 defeat. The D'Backs had nine hits in the game but left nine runners on base.

The Diamondbacks turn to former Mets righty Zach Davies in the series opener. Davies is 2-5 in 15 starts this season including three quality starts.s He has allowed 81 hits in 71.1 innings pitched with an ERA of 6.81. On the road, Davies i 1-1 with an ERA of 8.38 in four starts with 15 strikeouts in 19.1 innings pitched. In his last five starts, Davies is 1-1 with an ERA of 7.43 in 23 innings. He has allowed 19 hits in 19 innings in that span with 20 strikeouts.

Not the Kings of Queens

The Mets had hoped to be driving toward the playoffs at this point in the season but, instead, they are well out of the race and can only hope to spoil the playoff plans of other teams, like Arizona, the rest of the way. The Mets come into this series on a downturn after losing four of six heading into Sunday's series finale against the Twins. On Sunday afternoon, five Mets pitchers held the Twins to just four hits in a 2-0 win to avoid a sweep.

Start us off, Nimmo! 🙌 pic.twitter.com/Ys2MO7IoBM

The Mets will turn to lefty Jose Quintana in the series opener against the Diamondbacks. Quintana has missed over half the season with injury but has been one of the more consistent starters for the Mets since his return. Quintana is 2-5 in nine starts including an impressive seven quality starts thus far. He has allowed 47 hits in 54 innings pitched with an ERA of 3.00. Quintana has a K/9 rate of 6.5 thus far and his WHIP sits at 1.26. In his last five starts, Quintana is 2-2 with a 2.67 ERA. He's allowed 23 hits in 30.1 innings pitched to go along with 23 strikeouts in that time.

Key Injuries: RF Sterling Marte is out.
