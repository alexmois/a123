The Atlanta Braves (93-49, 75-67 RL, 77-63-2 O/U) collide with the Philadelphia Phillies (78-64, 62-80 RL, 62-69-11 O/U) in game one of Monday’s doubleheader on Monday afternoon. The Braves will have Charlie Morton on the mound. The Phillies will reply with Taijuan Walker. The Braves lead the season series 4-2. 

Morton Starts Opening Game of DH

Charlie Morton will get the nod in the opening game. Morton was solid in his previous start, allowing just two runs but only pitched four innings against the Dodgers and was charged with the loss. The veteran continues to shine, allowing two or fewer runs in five consecutive starts, and is sporting a 3.32 ERA and a 1.38 WHIP accompanied by a 14-11 record in 151.2 innings. Morton conceded just two runs in 5.2 innings in the lone meeting against the Phillies this year and has a poor 4.73 ERA and a 5-7 record in 91.1 career innings against the rivals. 

Atlanta opens a road trek in this one. They are just as good on the road, winning eight of their last ten bouts. The Braves rebounded from a series loss to the Cardinals with a series victory against the Pirates this past weekend, scoring 17 runs. The Braves are 6-4 in their last ten clashes and have three more wins than the next closest squad.

Ronald Acuna Jr. continues to shine at the plate, delivering five homers with eight RBIs on the month. The 25-year-old star now has 35 homers with 93 RBIs and an elite 1.002 OPS on the season. Acuna Jr. has four RBIs and a decent .793 OPS against the Phillies this season.

Atlanta leads the big leagues with 5.82 runs per game. The pitching is outstanding, carrying a 3.90 ERA, good for fifth.

Walker Starts, Phillies Stay in 1st WC Spot

The Phillies will give Taijuan Walker the nod. Walker had more trouble last time out, conceding four runs on five hits in five innings against the Padres, and was able to register the win. The veteran posted a weak 4.76 ERA last month and has subpar numbers, recording a 4.15 ERA and a 1.32 WHIP accompanied by a 15-5 record in 147.1 innings pitched on the season. Walker has allowed three runs on 10 hits in 6.2 innings against the Braves the season and has posted a 4.93 ERA and a 2-2 record in 34.2 career innings against the Braves. 

The Phillies have won three of their last five series. They collected two wins in three road meetings in San Diego followed by a series home loss against the Marlins, despite allowing just 12 runs. The Phillies stand in the 1st wildcard spot and are two games ahead of the Cubs.  The squad is 4-6 in their last ten decisions.

Alec Bohm has accrued four RBIs in his last eight games. The 27-year-old could still reach 100 RBIs. He has collected 86 RBIs accompanied by a .766 OPS on the season. Bohm has three RBIs and a solid .800 OPS against the Braves this season. He is hitting .423 with five RBIs against Morton.

The Phillies are posting an average of 4.88 runs on the year, marking them ninth. The pitching is strong, recording a 4.00 ERA, placing them 10th.
