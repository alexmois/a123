The Tampa Bay Rays (87-56, 2nd AL East) hit the road Monday to open a three-game series against the American League Central-leading Minnesota Twins (75-67, 1st AL Central). Tampa Bay won its second straight when defeating the Seattle Mariners 7-5 on Saturday. Tampa Bay has won four of the last five. The Rays are second in the AL East four games behind first and lead the AL wildcard chase. Minnesota defeated the New York Mets 8-4 on Saturday for their second straight win and fourth in the last five. The Twins lead the AL Central by 7 ½ games.

Tampa Bay will play without six pitchers as well as position players Wander Franco and Manuel Margot. Minnesota will play without six pitchers, as well as position players Byron Buxton, Joey Gallo, Nick Gordon, Jose Miranda and Michael A. Taylor.

Tampa Bay is solid at the plate and on the mound

Although Tampa Bay is second in the American League East, the Rays have put up strong numbers both at the plate and on the mound. Tampa Bay is in the top 9 of four major hitting categories. The Rays are 4th in both runs scored with 760 and slugging percentage at .444, while 6th in on-base percentage at .331 and 9th in team batting average with .258. Isaac Paredes (questionable) is leading Tampa Bay in both home runs with 29 and RBI with 89. Jose Siri is second-best in home runs with 25 and Randy Arozarena is second-best in RBI with 78.

On the mound, Tampa Bay is only 16th in Quality Starts with 47 but is first in WHIP at 1.18, second in team batting average with .230 and third in team ERA at 3.79. Zach Eflin is leading Tampa Bay in victories with 13 and strikeouts with 158. On Monday, Tampa Bay will hand the ball to Tyler Glasnow. This season the right-hander is 8-5 with an ERA of 3.07 and 134 Ks.

Minnesota strength is on the mound

Minnesota leads the American League Central thanks to excellent performances on the mound. The Twins are in the top seven of four major pitching categories. Minnesota is first in Quality Starts with 69, while fourth in both WHIP at 1.21 and batting average allowed with .237. Minnesota has the seventh-best team ERA at .393.  Pablo Lopez is leading Minnesota in victories with 10 and in strikeouts with 199. Minnesota will hand the ball on Monday to Sonny Gray. The right-hander this season is 7-6 with an ERA of 2.98 and 160 Ks

 

Minnesota's performance at the plate has been hampered by injuries to several key hitters but nonetheless, the Twins are in the top half of three major hitting categories including 11th in slugging percentage at .425, 14th and runs with 664 and 15th in on-base percentage with .323. Minnesota drops the 20th in team batting average at .243. Max Kepler is the leader in home runs for Minnesota with 21. Carlos Correa is the leader in RBI with 63. Michael A. Taylor, Byron Buxton, and Joey Gallo have a combined 58 home runs and 129 RBI but are all out injured.
