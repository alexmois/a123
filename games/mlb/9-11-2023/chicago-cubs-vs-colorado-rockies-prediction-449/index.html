The Chicago Cubs face the Colorado Rockies with both teams heading in opposite directions this season. The Cubs have a 77-67 record and currently hold the second wild card spot in the National League. The Rockies have a 51-91 record and with only two wins in their last 10 games, they have the worst record in the National League. For the upcoming game, the Cubs will turn to Jordan Wicks as their starting pitcher while the Rockies will turn to Kyle Freeland to start the game.

Cubs hope Wicks and lineup start series strong

The Cubs are having a great season and hope to secure a spot in the postseason for the first time since 2020. Jordan Wicks is one of their best young pitchers and he has stepped up on the mound, allowing only four runs in 16.2 innings pitched while tossing 13 strikeouts. Moreover, with a .320 expected opponent Slugging Percentage, a 2.0 Barrel Percentage, and a 3.07 expected ERA, opponents are making weak contact and struggling to drive in runs against Wicks this season.

Wicks has stepped up but the lineup has carried the Cubs, scoring 5.03 runs per game. Cody Bellinger is slashing .319/.362/.547 with 24 home runs and 235 total bases while Seiya Suzuki is slashing .276/.346/.466 with 16 home runs and 208 total bases but the rest of the lineup has stepped up as well. Ian Happ is slashing .243/.359/.418 with 17 home runs and 213 total bases and Patrick Wisdom and Christopher Morel have added 41 home runs and 275 total bases to round out the lineup, making the Cubs tough to put away at the plate.

Rockies hope Freeland and awful lineup step up

The Rockies are having a season to forget and hope to avoid finishing with the worst record in the National League. Kyle Freeland has shown flashes with a 6.4 Walk Rate but is having a rough season, allowing 92 runs in 146.2 innings pitched. In addition, with a .502 expected opponent Slugging Percentage, a 14.2 Strikeout Percentage, and a 5.58 expected ERA, opponents are making hard contact and putting together strong plate appearances against Freeland to easily drive in runs.

Freeland has struggled but the lineup has also been a disappointment, scoring only 4.40 runs per game. Nolan Jones is slashing .281/.363/.521 with 15 home runs and 152 total bases while Charlie Blackmon is slashing .284/.376/.439 with 127 total bases but the rest of th lieneup has struggled. Only two batters have an OPS+ over 100 and opposing pitchers can eliminate the hapless lineup as a result.
