The Washington Nationals and Pittsburgh Pirates will take the field on Monday at PNC Park. First pitch is scheduled for 6:35 p.m. ET.

Washington (64-79 SU and 78-65 RL) is starting southpaw Patrick Corbin. The 34-year-old is 9-13 with a 5.23 ERA and a 1.51 WHIP in 28 outings this season.

Pittsburgh (66-77 SU and 72-71 RL) has not named a starting pitcher for Monday's game at the time this article was published. Right-hander Andre Jackson followed Luis Ortiz on the last turn through the rotation.

Can Corbin turn in a quality start?

The Nationals wrapped up a series with the Dodgers over the weekend. Prior to that, they were swept in four games by the Marlins and split a two-game set with the Mets. Can they at least play spoiler over the few remaining weeks of the regular season?

Washington averages 4.33 runs (21st) and hits .256 (11th) with a .711 OPS (20th). It's hit 130 long balls (29th) and stolen 106 bases (13th) in 2023. The Nats pitching staff has compiled a 4.97 ERA (27th) and a 1.45 WHIP (28th) with 45 quality starts (20th).

Corbin will start for the visitors in the series opener Monday. The veteran left-hander is 5-5 with a 5.26 ERA and 1.53 WHIP on the road (14 starts) and 3-6 with a 6.41 ERA and 1.61 WHIP in 12 night games this season. In his most recent outing, he gave up seven runs and eight hits, including three home runs, in a loss to the Mets. Corbin held the Pirates to three runs on seven hits in another losing effort on April 29.

The Pirates are just 34-37 SU at home in 2023

Pittsburgh dropped its series finale with Atlanta on Sunday after splitting the first two games. It won its three previous series against Milwaukee, St. Louis, and Kansas City.

The Pirates score 4.22 runs per game (23rd) and hit .236 (27th) with a .703 OPS (24th). They've hit 140 homers (27th) and stolen 103 bases (14th) this year. The Pitt pitching staff has posted a 4.52 ERA (20th) and a 1.38 WHIP (22nd) with 48 quality starts (15th).

Jackson may be in line to make Monday's start at PNC Park. The 27-year-old has made 14 appearances (four starts) this season, posting a 5.21 ERA and a 1.22 WHIP. In his last outing, he gave up six runs in 4.1 innings vs. Milwaukee. He also started the Pirates' August 30 game against Kansas City, holding the Royals to one run and two hits with seven strikeouts in a winning effort.

The Pirates have a higher batting average and OBP at home and have hit left-handed pitchers (.247 BA/.323 OBP/.384 SLG/.706 OPS) better than right-handers (.231/.309/.392/.701) in 2023. Corbin has been an easy fade this year, and I don't expect that to change on the road in Pittsburgh. The Bucs have been playing better baseball recently, winning three of their last four series.

The Nationals sport a .312 OBP on the road this season and had a .307 OBP in 28 August games. They'll struggle to keep pace with the home team, as Corbin will give up a couple of early runs and fail to give the bullpen (5.15 ERA) enough length to keep the Bucs' bats in check.

Full-Game Total Pick

Insiders Status:

Rating:

These are two low-scoring teams coming off series defeats. In the last month of the season, I doubt much will change in Washington and Pittsburgh, where fans will be happy to put away their baseball caps and swap them for their football jerseys.

The Nats don't score with consistency, no matter the venue or opposing pitcher. Despite holding a winning record in the second half of the season, they're scoring less than a half-run per game than in the first half (36-54 SU). Jackson has pitched well enough against mediocre teams this season and the Bucs' bullpen (ninth-lowest ERA in the NL) is solid enough to keep Washington from breaking out offensively.

Pittsburgh could knock around Corbin, but the veteran did hold the Pirates to three runs in his last start against them (April 23). As long as the Nationals don't score four of five runs, I don't envision the home team producing enough to carry the total over themselves.
