Los Angeles Angels (66-77) vs. Seattle Mariners (79-63)

The Major League Baseball betting action goes on Monday, September 11, 2023, with a 15-game card including a doubleheader at Citizens Bank Park in Philadelphia. In this preview, we are taking a closer look at the AL West showdown from T-Mobile Park in Seattle to get you the best Angels vs. Mariners betting pick. 

The Angels and Mariners open a three-game set in Seattle. It’s their final series of the season, and the Mariners are 6-4 against the Angels in 2023. 

The Angels hope for Ohtani’s return

Sunday’s games have been excluded from the analysis, and the 66-77 Los Angeles Angels hosted the Cleveland Guardians, looking to clinch a four-game home series. The Angels sandwiched a 6-3 defeat with a pair of wins over Cleveland, 3-2 and 6-2. Shohei Ohtani wasn’t in the lineup on Sunday, and the two-way superstar hasn’t played since September 3 due to an oblique injury. He’s questionable to play Monday in Seattle. 

The Halos have only won three of their previous 13 games overall. During that stretch, the Angels have recorded a modest .245/.314/.415 triple-slash (21 extra-base hits, 17 home runs) in 441 at-bats to go with a pedestrian 5.95 ERA, 1.63 WHIP, and .288 batting average against. 

Reid Detmers is projected to toe the slab Monday at T-Mobile Park. The 24-year-old left-hander carries a 3-10 record, 4.82 ERA, 1.40 WHIP, and 147/54 K/BB ratio across 130.2 innings pitched (25 starts) in 2023. He’s been solid over his last four starts, allowing eight runs (seven earned) on 21 hits and ten walks through 23 innings of work. 

Detmers will meet Seattle for the third time this season. He hasn’t been involved in the decision in the previous two while posting a hideous 9.35 ERA and 2.08 WHIP (8.2 innings pitched).

The Mariners continue to chase the postseason

The 79-63 Seattle Mariners played the Tampa Bay Rays on Sunday, trying to avoid their third straight loss. After winning the opener of a four-game series at Tropicana Field in St. Petersburg, 1-0, the Mariners have dropped the next two contests, 7-4 and 7-5. Seattle was second in the AL West on Sunday morning, trailing the Houston Astros by 1.5 games. The M’s held the second wild card in the American League. 

Seattle has gone 4-7 over its previous 11 games overall. The Mariners are slashing .236/.315/.438 with 23 extra-base hits and 23 round-trippers in the past two weeks and 461 at-bats while tallying a 4.47 ERA, 1.32 WHIP, and .263 batting average against. 

Logan Gilbert will be on the bump Monday, and the 26-year-old right-hander is 13-5 with a 3.61 ERA, 1.07 WHIP, and 171/30 K/BB ratio in 28 starts (167 IP) this season. Gilbert has done a terrific job over his last four starts, going 3-0 with a 2.52 ERA, 3.58 FIP, and .736 opposing OPS in 25 innings of work. He’ll meet the Angels for the second time in 2023 after surrendering seven runs (six earned) across three frames in a losing effort on June 11.
