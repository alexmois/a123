The Texas Rangers (80-64, 2nd AL West) play the fourth and final game of a series on the road Thursday when facing the Toronto Blue Jays (80-65, 3rd AL East). Texas defeated Toronto 6-3 on Tuesday, 10-0 on Wednesday and has won each of the first two games of their four-game series with the Blue Jays. Texas has won four straight and is second in the American League West trailing Houston by one game and is currently holding the second of three AL wild card spots. Toronto has lost two straight after falling to Texas on Tuesday. The Blue Jays are third in the AL East, 11 ½ games behind first and are currently tied with Seattle for the third and final AL wildcard. Texas will play without six pitchers as well as position players Josh Jung and Brad Miller. Toronto will play without two pitchers as well as position players Brandon Belt, Matt Chapman, Danny Jansen and Otto Lopez.

After losing 16 of 20, Texas is regaining its footing

The Texas Rangers have had a tough month after losing 16 of 20 but have regained their footing winning each of the last four and have moved to within one game of first place Houston in the American League West and currently hold the second of three AL wild card spots. Texas’ bats have been the strength of the Rangers all season. Texas is second in team batting average at .266, while third in each of the following: runs (793), on-base percentage (,338), and slugging percentage (.455). Adolis Garcia has 34 home runs and 100 RBI to lead Texas in both categories, while Corey Seager is close behind with 30 home runs and 89 RBI. Texas has four players with 83 or more RBI.

 

Texas pitching is currently 17th in team ERA with 4.29 and 12th in WHIP at 1.27. The Rangers have 60 Quality Starts and are holding opposing hitters to an average of .246 which is 16th. Thursday's starting pitcher Nathan Eovaldi is the team leader in wins with 11 and Andrew Heaney is the leader in strikeouts with 141. Eovaldi is 11-4 with an ERA of 2.90 and 115 strikeouts.

Toronto pitching keeping Blue Jays in the postseason hunt

Toronto leads baseball in team ERA at 3.73 and is 6th in both Quality Starts with 64 and batting average allowed at .239. The Blue Jays are 7th in WHIP at 1.24. Chris Bassitt is the leader in victories with 14, while Thursday's starting pitcher Kevin Gausman is the leader in strikeouts with 217. Gausman is 11-8 with an ERA of 3.28. 

Toronto's bats are also holding their own, as the Blue Jays are fourth in both team batting average with .259 and on-base percentage at .332. Toronto dips to 12th in slugging percentage at .420 and 16th in runs scored with 667. Vladimir Guerrero Jr is the team leader in home runs with 21 and RBI with 84, while George Springer is second in home runs with 19 and tied for second in RBI with Bo Bichette at 64. Toronto has hit 265 doubles, 14 triples and 168 home runs in 145 games.
