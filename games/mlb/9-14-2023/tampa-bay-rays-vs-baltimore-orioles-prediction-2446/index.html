Down to the Wire

The Tampa Bay Rays have proven to be one of the most resilient teams in baseball and are very much alive to win the division heading into this critical four-game weekend series. With over 90 wins, the Rays are all but assured of a playoff spot either way but winning the division would bring them a bye during Wildcard Weekend. On Wednesday afternoon, Randy Arozarena belted a two-run home run in the top of the ninth inning to give the Rays the lead for good in a 5-4 win over the Twins in Minnesota. The Rays have now won five of six and seven of nine games heading into the series with Baltimore.

The Rays will hand the ball to righty Aaron Civale in game one of this series vs. the Orioles. Civale is 7-3 on the season with an ERA of 2.98 in 20 starts between Cleveland and Tampa Bay. Since joining the Rays, Civale has gone 2-1 in seven starts with an ERA of 4.29. He has allowed 38 hits in 35.2 innings pitched and has a K/9 rate of 10.1. Civale has been very good on the road this season with a record of 4-2 in ten starts and an impressive 2.01 ERA.

Key Injuries: CF Jose Siri is out.

O's Looking to Close Out Rays

The Baltimore Orioles have an opportunity this weekend to put away the Rays and all but clinch the AL East Division and the top seed in the American League heading into the playoffs. Baltimore has won eight out of ten games heading into Wednesday night's game with the Cardinals but has been unable to fully shake the Rays in the standings. On Wednesday night, the Orioles were held to just three hits and fell 1-0 to the Cardinals and hold just a 2.5 game lead in the AL East heading into this series.

The Orioles will turn to righty Kyle Bradish in the opener on Thursday night. Bradish is 11-6 in 26 starts including a team-leading 16 quality starts. He has allowed 120 hits in 145.2 innings pitched with an ERA of 3.03. Bradish has a K/9 rate of 9.0 and his WHIP is 1.09. Bradish is 4-3 at home this season with an impressive 2.26 ERA and 52 strikeouts in 59.2 innings pitched. He's been excellent for the O's down the stretch with a 4-0 record in his last five starts and an ERA of 2.40. Bradish is 1-1 with a 3.27 ERA in two starts against Tampa this season. He has allowed 10 hits in 11 innings with 13 strikeouts in the two games.

As resilient as the Rays have been this season, the Orioles have been relentless. The Orioles took the lead in the division in July and have clung tightly to it since that time. Bradish has arguably been the Orioles' most reliable starter this season and that has never been more evident than in his last five starts when the right-hander has gone 4-0 with an ERA under two and a half. In addition, the Orioles have won each of Bradish's last seven starts heading into Thursday night and he has an ERA under two and a half at home as well. Civale has pitched very well in his stint with the Rays and has been excellent on the road. This game will come down to execution and the Orioles have excelled at that all season. Expect the Orioles to work the pitch count and commit to a small-ball mentality on Thursday night. They are fifth in baseball in sacrifice hits this year and have solid team speed. Baltimore has taken six of the nine games against Tampa Bay this season heading into the series opener. I like Bradish to get the Orioles deeper into this game and the Orioles bullpen to close out a tight game.

Take Baltimore with the money line.
