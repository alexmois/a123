Rivals will clash for the final time on Thursday in a game that will determine whether the AL East standings are tied. Except, atypically of each franchise, the New York Yankees (73-72; before play on Wednesday) and Boston Red Sox (73-72) are trying to avoid last place. It's been a rough year for baseball in the Northeast, but that won't stop them from trying to win the series finale. The final 2023 meeting between these clubs is scheduled to start at 7:15 p.m. EDT. A national audience will be able to tune into the game live from Fenway Park. Let's see who will punctuate the final chapter of this 2023 rivalry.

 

*Wendesay's game was rained out so this will be the second game of a day-night doubleheader.

The Doubleheader Double Whammy

It would be impossible to discuss this year's Yankees team without mentioning injuries. It's not the only reason that they've failed, but it hasn't helped. Especially on the pitching front, where eight relievers and five starting pitchers are on the IL during this series. Clarke Schmidt gets the nod in the second game. He is coming off a quality start against Detroit on Sept. 6, allowing three runs and five hits in 6.1 innings. He last faced the Red Sox on August 20 and held them to two runs and four hits in 5.2 innings, but Boston won the game 6-5. New York may have the lowest bullpen ERA in the majors this year, but Tuesday's doubleheader will probably have a lingering impact on availability in this game.  

Injuries are one of New York's issues this season, with the other being an offense that's 23rd in runs per game. The Bronx Bombers moniker still fits, as the club is sixth in home runs this year along with the eighth-highest hard-hit rate. They're also fourth in walk rate after Tuesday's twin-billing. However, the compliments should stop there. Tuesday's two games dropped the Yankees to MLB's lowest batting average. Even with all of the walks and long balls, New York is only 26th in OBP and 21st in slugging percentage. They're a terrible baserunning team, including the fifth-lowest stolen base percentage. The Yankees are 22nd in strikeout rate too. Home run or bust won't cut it, especially not with the new rules in place.

A Fenway Flop

The Boston Red Sox really dropped the ball on Tuesday, losing both games of the doubleheader at home. Facing a (then) last-place team twice while a pair of teams they trail in the standings faced each other, Boston had a golden opportunity to gain ground. Instead, they're stuck with their archnemesis 7.0 games out of a spot with 17 left to play. An offense that's eighth in runs per game this season went cold at the wrong time this Tuesday. They average 5.22 runs per game at home in 2023 but only mustered three total between the two games. Despite a lineup that's top-10 in batting average, OBP, and slugging percentage, 10 different pitchers subdued them during those two losses. Even with a low walk rate (8.0%; 24th) and suspect baserunning, Boston is usually a threat at the plate. Yet, when they needed to come through the most, the hitters vanished. 

As for the pitching staff, it has struggled for most of the season. There is one notable exception, and that would be against the Yankees. Through 11 matchups, Boston was holding New York to fewer than three runs per game. A staff ERA of 2.45 against them also explains an 8-3 record. Manager Alex Cora hasn't indicated how Boston will pitch on Thursday, but he should feel confident no matter who toes the rubber first. Everyone he trots out there shuts New York down. Behind Thursday's mystery starter is a bullpen that's 11th in WAR thanks to the league's fewest blown saves. One last shutdown performance seems possible.
