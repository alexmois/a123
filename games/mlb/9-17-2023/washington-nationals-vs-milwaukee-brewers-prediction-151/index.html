Closing with a Wimper

The Washington Nationals are in last place in the NL East Division but have shown improvement this season as the team continues what has been a long rebuild since winning their first World Series title in 2019. Lately, however, the Nats have fallen on hard times with four straight losses and five out of six heading into Saturday night's game. In the opener of this series, the Nats fell 5-3 in a game that was tied heading into the bottom of the fifth. On Saturday night, the Nationals came back from deficits of 3-0 and 5-1 only to lose on an 8th-inning grand slam. They have now lost five straight games heading into the finale.

The Nationals will hand the ball to lefty Patrick Corbin to close out the series in Milwaukee. Corbin is 10-13 in 29 starts this season including 14 quality starts. He has allowed 195 hits in 165 innings pitched with an ERA of 5.13. Corbin has a K/9 of just 6.5 and his WHIP is 1.48. On the road, Corbin is 6-5 with an ERA of 5.06 this season in 15 starts. He has struck out 69 batters in 85.1 innings pitched. In his last five starts, Corbin is 3-2 with an ERA of 6.51 with 28 strikeouts in 27.2 innings pitched.

BrewCrew Closing In

The Milwaukee Brewers come into Saturday night's game six games ahead of the second-place Chicago Cubs in the lost column and a five-and-a-half-game lead in the division. The Brewers look to be well on their way to getting back in the playoffs after missing last season due to a poor second half. The Brewers have won four out of five heading into Saturday night's game including a 5-3 win over the Nats in the series opener. Trade deadline acquisition Carlos Santana belted solo home runs in his final two at-bats to pull the Brewers to victory. On Saturday, Mark Canha capped off a 3-for-5 night with a grand slam in the bottom of the eighth inning to break a 5-5 tie and propel the Brewers to victory.

The Brewers will turn the ball over to righty Brandon Woodruff on Sunday afternoon. For the second straight season, Woodruff has suffered through an injury-plagued season but appears back on form when the team needs him most. Woodruff is 5-1 on the season in nine starts including six quality starts. He has allowed just 31 hits in 56 innings pitched with a sparkling 1.93 ERA. Woodruff has a k/9 rate of 10.0 in his nine starts and his WHIP is an impressive 0.79. At home, Woodruff is 3-1 with an ERA of 1.07 in four starts this season with 31 strikeouts in 25.1 innings pitched. In his last five starts, Woodruff is 3-0 with an ERA of 1.89 and 36 strikeouts in 33.1 innings pitched.
