How good are the Giants?

San Francisco is in a tight National League Wild Card race. Is it talented enough to make it to the finish line with a postseason-worthy record?

The Giants score 4.30 runs per game (21st) and hit .240 (23rd) with a .705 OPS (22nd). They've hit 160 long balls (19th) and stolen 51 bases (30th) in 2023. The SF pitching staff has compiled a 3.97 ERA (10th) and a 1.25 WHIP (8th) with 47 quality starts (20th).

Manaea will take the mound for the visitors on Sunday afternoon in Denver. The veteran left-hander is 3-1 with a 6.19 ERA and a 1.54 WHIP in 17 road games (two starts) this year. In his last appearance, he gave up three runs (one earned) and two hits in 5.2 innings vs. Cleveland. It was his first start since early May.

Colorado needs more consistency from Flexen

Colorado plays in one of the most competitive divisions in baseball, and while expectations were low in 2023, it didn't give fans much to cheer about. Will the organization make any significant changes this coming offseason?

The Rockies average 4.40 runs (20th) and hit .248 (16th) with a .709 OPS (20th). They've hit 142 homers (28th) and stolen 61 bases (29th) this year. The Colorado pitching staff sports a 5.64 ERA (30th) and a 1.53 WHIP (30th) with 37 quality starts (28th).

Flexen will toe the rubber for the Rockies on Sunday. He has made nine straight starts since late July after pitching strictly out of the bullpen for Seattle in May and June. In his most recent appearance, he gave up three runs on nine hits in 5.1 innings against the Cubs. Flexen is 0-4 with a 5.81 ERA and a 1.56 WHIP in 14 home outings (eight starts) this season.
