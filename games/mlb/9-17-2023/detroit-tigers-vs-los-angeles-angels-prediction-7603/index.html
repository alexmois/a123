Two American League arrivals on the verge of being eliminated from postseason contention meet Sunday when the Detroit Tigers (68-79, 3rd AL Central) conclude a three-game series on the road against the Los Angeles Angels (68-80, 4th AL West). Detroit defeated Los Angeles 11-2 on Friday in the opening game of the series. Detroit has won two straight and four of the last six but with 15 games remaining on the regular season schedule is 9 ½ games behind first place and is 13 games behind the third and final American League wild card spot. Los Angeles has lost three straight after losing Friday and is 15 games behind first place in the AL West and 13 ½  games behind the third AL wildcard spot. Detroit will play without seven pitchers as well as position players Riley Greene and Austin Meadows. Los Angeles will play without 8 pitchers as well as several position players including Mike Trout, CJ Cron, Mickey Moniak, Mike Moustakas, Anthony Rendon, Max Stassi, Gio Urshela and Taylor Ward amongst others.

Detroit must find help at the plate during the off season

The Detroit Tigers must acquire better hitters during the off-season via free agency or trades. Detroit is 27th in on-base percentage, 28th in batting average, and 29th in both slugging percentage and runs scored. Spencer Torkelson is the team leader in home runs with 28 and in RBI with 83. Kerry Carpenter is second in home runs with 20 and in RBI with 62. Javier Baez has had a below-par season, in part due to injury, as the shortstop has 14 doubles, and four triples but just nine home runs and 58 RBI.

 

Detroit has fared better on the mound than at the plate but must improve as well. Detroit is 10th in WHIP, 11th in batting average allowed, while 18th in ERA and 26th in Quality Starts. Eduardo Rodriguez is leading victories with 11 and in strikeouts with 132.  As of the publication of this article, the Detroit Tigers had not yet released the name of their starting pitcher for Sunday's final game of a three-game series against the Los Angeles Angels.

Injuries have decimated the Angels lineup

The Los Angeles Angels have the 8th best slugging percentage in baseball, but dropped to 14th in runs scored while 17th in on-base percentage and 18th in team batting average. Shohei Ohtani is practically the only star left standing in the Angels lineup. The DH is leading the Angels in home runs (44), RBI (95), hits (151), on-base percentage (.412) and batting average (.304). Brandon Drury is second in home runs with 21 and in RBI with 70.

The Angels are struggling on the mound as well. Los Angeles is 21st in Quality Starts, 23rd in both batting average allowed and ERA, while 25th in WHIP.  The Angels are without the pitching services of Ohtani, who leads the club in wins (10) and strikeouts (167), but can only hit due to an elbow injury. Reid Detmers gets the nod on the mound Sunday for LA. This season the southpaw is 3-10 with an ERA of 4.77 and 154 strikeouts.
