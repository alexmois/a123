Reds Won't Go Away

The Cincinnati Reds are still very much alive in the NL wildcard race heading into the season's final two weeks. Billed by many as too young and inexperienced, the Reds have bucked the odds and stayed right in the race down to the wire. Heading into Saturday night's game, the Reds have won four of five games and moved into a tie for the final wildcard spot in the NL. On Friday night, the Reds pulled out a 5-3 win thanks to a tie-breaking home run from Jonathan India in the 7th inning. On Saturday night, the Reds squeaked out a 3-2 win using five pitchers to hold the Mets to eight hits and two runs to remain tied for the third wildcard spot.

The Reds will turn to lefty Williamson in the series finale against the Mets. Williamson is 4-4 in 20 starts with six quality starts on the season. He has given up 96 hits in 102.2 innings with an ERA of 4.47. He has a K/9 rate of 8.0 and his WHIP is 1.28. Williamson is 2-1 in 11 road starts this season with an ERA of 4.70. He has allowed 51 hits in 51.2 innings pitched. In his last five starts, he's gone 0-1 with a 4.28 ERA while striking out 27 in 27.1 innings pitched.

Key Injuries: SS Matt McClain is out.

Still Have a Role to Play

The Mets are far removed from the NL playoff picture but they still have a role to play down the stretch. This weekend the Mets can put a dent in the Reds' playoff chances by taking the three-game series with them. On Friday night, the Reds drew first blood with a 5-3 win as they scored two runs off the Mets bullpen late to steal a close game. On Saturday night, the Mets left 10 runners on base and went just 2-for-8 with runners in scoring position and lost 3-2 to the Reds.

The Mets will turn to veteran lefty Jose Quintana in the series finale on Sunday. Quintana is 2-5 in what has been an abbreviated season for him. Quintana has made 10 starts on the season, including seven quality starts. He has allowed 52 hits in 59 innings pitched with an ERA of 3.05. Quintana has a K/9 rate of 6.6 and his WHIP sits at 1.27. Quintana has allowed two runs or less in four of his last five starts and has gone at least six innings in three of those starts as well.

Key Injuries: RF Sterling Marte is out.
