Each of these teams is pursuing a division crown, but at different stages right now. The Los Angeles Dodgers (89-57) are secure in their NL West standing. For the Seattle Mariners (81-66), one bad day could be the difference between a long overdue AL West championship celebration or missing playoff baseball entirely. Their paths intersect for the final time on Sunday, with a first pitch scheduled for 4:10 p.m. EDT. Who will come out on top in T-Mobile Park? Let's dig in and find out.

 

*Published before Saturday's game

The Ever-Dominant Dodgers

A 10th NL West title in the past 11 seasons is imminent after Friday night's victory. The question is if it's going to happen Saturday, Sunday, or Monday with the Dodgers magic number down to two. Regardless of when it happens, it's crystal clear why the Dodgers have gotten to this point. Mookie Betts and Freddie Freeman lead an offense that's second in runs per game this season. They're also second in OBP, slugging percentage, team OPS, and home runs. Their walk rate is the second-highest, and Los Angeles is fifth in hard-hit rate, batting .257 as a team (8th), and successfully swiping bases 81% of the time. The Dodgers quietly have the 10th-lowest strikeout rate too. Facing this lineup can't be fun. 

 

For those wondering how this team may not win 100 games with an offense like that, turn to a pitching staff that struggled for half of the season. However, a 4.50 team ERA in the first half shrunk to 3.75 in the second half, predictably leading to more wins. Manager Dave Roberts has had to navigate a ton of injuries, but Lance Lynn, Joe Kelly, and Ryan Yarbrough were all trade deadline pickups that have helped weather the storm. Also of note is signing reliever Ryan Brasier to a minor league contract in June, shortly after he was released by Boston. All he's done in 34.1 innings as a Dodgers is post a wacky 558 ERA+. For context, 100 is the league average, and Mariano Rivera's single-season record was 316. His 0.670 WHIP is leading a reliever core that is second in WAR among MLB bullpens. Los Angeles may not have announced a starter yet, but a lot of things on the mound seem to be working in their favor post-All-Star break.

Seattle Searching for Another Streak

A red-hot run had taken the Mariners into first place very briefly. Now, trailing Houston by 1.5 games and Texas by 1.0, another would be magnificent. That's without mentioning that Toronto is only 0.5 games behind Seattle for the final AL wild-card spot. Pitching is how the Mariners win games. Only one team is allowing fewer runs per game this season. The bullpen is electric, owning a top-five ERA, FIP, and WAR. It'll be on Logan Gilbert to pass the baton in a tight game. Seattle's right-hander has been strong since the calendar flipped to July. In those 13 starts, he has a 2.98 ERA. Seattle was victorious in 10 of those games. They need victories now, and this is one of their best shots.

When the Mariners were beating everyone, the offense was what made them unstoppable. Playing in T-Mobile Park doesn't help, but Seattle is 10th in runs per game this year. They succeed at the plate despite MLB's second-highest strikeout rate. Seattle is 11th in hard-hit rate and 12th in homers. They also rank 13th in OBP, slugging percentage, and stolen bases (with an 80% success rate). The Mariners draw walks at a decent clip too. This isn't the August version of the Mariners right now, but pitchers still need to tread lightly.
