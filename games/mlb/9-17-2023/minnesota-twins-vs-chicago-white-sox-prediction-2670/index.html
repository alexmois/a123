Two American League Central rivals headed in vastly different directions face off on the diamond Sunday when the Minnesota Twins (78-70, 1st AL Central) visit the Chicago White Sox (56-92, 4th AL Central). The Minnesota Twins defeated Chicago in the second game of their four game series on Friday 10-2. Minnesota is first in the American League Central and lowered its magic number to seven. Chicago has lost four straight and six of the last seven following its loss to Minnesota on Friday and have been eliminated from playoff contention. Minnesota will play without six pitchers as well as position players Michael A. Taylor, Jose Miranda, Nick Gordon, Joey Gallo and Byron Buxton. Chicago will play without 5 pitchers as well as outfielder Romy Gonzalez

Minnesota close to clinching AL Central despite injuries

The Minnesota Twins do not have key players such as Michael A. Taylor, Byron Buxton, Joey Gallo, Nick Gordon and Jose Miranda amongst others including six pitchers. Nevertheless, at the plate, players such as Carlos Correa, Max Kepler, Kyle Farmer and others have stepped up. Kepler is leading the Twins in home runs with 22 and Correa has a team high 64 RBI. The Twins are only 21st in team batting average but have the 10th best slugging percentage that has produced the 12th most runs.

 

Minnesota's pitching has been one of the biggest reasons the Twins are first in the AL Central. Minnesota is first in Quality Starts with 71, fourth in both WHIP at 1.20 and batting average allowed with .237, while fifth in team ERA at 3.90. Pablo Lopez is the leader in victories for Minnesota with 10 and is the leader in strikeouts with 213. Minnesota will hand the ball to Sonny Gray on Sunday this season the right hander is 7-7 with an ERA of 2.96 and 165 strikeouts.

White Sox need help both at the plate and on the mound

The Chicago White Sox have been eliminated from postseason contention and need help at the plate and on the mound. Chicago is 24th in team batting average, 25th in runs scored, 26th in slugging percentage and last or 30th in on base percentage. Luis Robert Jr has hit a team-best 35 home runs and knocked in a team-high 75 runs. Andrew Vaughn is second in home runs with 20 and is tied for the lead in RBI with 75. The White Sox have hit 243 doubles, 13 triples and 159 home runs in 148 games. 

Chicago is performing poorly on the mound as well, as the White Sox are 19th in batting average allowed, 22nd in Quality Starts and 26th in both WHIP and team ERA. Sunday’s starter Dylan Cease is leading Chicago in both victories with seven and strikeouts with 191. This season the right-hander is 7-7 with an ERA of 4.87.
