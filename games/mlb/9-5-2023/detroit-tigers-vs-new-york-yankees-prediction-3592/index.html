Back at It

The Detroit Tigers get set to square off against the New York Yankees less than a week after losing three of four to the Bombers in Detroit. The Tigers were able to avert a sweep with a win in the series finale last Thursday. Detroit currently sits in third place in the AL Central Division despite a sub-.500 record, well ahead of both the White Sox and Royals. Following the series with New York, the Tigers traveled to Chicago for a three-game weekend series with the White Sox. They took care of the White Sox in Friday night's opener by the score of 4-2. The Tigers continued their winning ways on Saturday and Sunday with a 10-0 win on Saturday and a 4-3 win on Sunday.

The Tigers will turn to lefty Joey Wentz in game one of the series in New York. Wentz is just 2-10 on the season in 20 appearances including 17 starts. He has allowed 109 hits in 89.1 innings pitched with an ERA of 6.65. On the road this season, Wentz is 2-6 with a 5.79 ERA and 40 strikeouts in 42 innings pitched. In his last five appearances, Wentz has gone 1-2 with a 6.45 ERA. He has allowed 32 hits in 22.1 innings pitched. The Tigers will likely take a long look at Wentz over his final four to five starts as they look to see the young lefty rebound after a tough season.

Key Injuries: OF Riley Greene is out.

Trying to Close Strong

The New York Yankees have shown some pride over the last week as they try to scramble out of the cellar in the AL East Division. They will get a chance to put together some more wins this week when they meet up with the Tigers again. The Yankees won the first three games of their four-game series with the Tigers last week before dropping the finale. They followed that up with a surprising win over the Astros on Friday night with a 6-2 win. The game featured the debut of rookie outfielder Jasson Dominguez, who homered in his first at-bat. The Yankees kept winning on Saturday with a 5-4 win over the Astros. On Sunday night, going for the series sweep, the Yankees delivered with a 6-1 win. Rookie Jasson Dominguez hit his second home run of the series and Gleybor Torres went 2-for-4 with a home run as well.

The Yankees will turn to their ace in the series opener, Gerrit Cole. Cole is making a bid for the AL Cy Young Award this season with a record of 12-4 in 28 starts including a league-best 20 quality starts. He has allowed just 136 hits in 174 innings pitched with an ERA of 2.95. Cole has a K/9 rate of 9.7 and his WHIP is 1.04. Cole picked up the win over Detroit last Wednesday while pitching six innings and allowing four hits and just two runs while striking out seven. In his last five starts, Cole is 2-2 with an ERA of 4.40 while striking out 31 batters in 30.2 innings pitched.

Key Injuries: 1B Anthony Rizzo is out.

Full-Game Side Bet

Insiders Status:

Rating:

The Yankees dominated last week's series with the Tigers, winning three of four games. The Yankees outscored the Tigers 14-5 in the three wins before falling 4-3 in extra innings. Yankees' starter Cole certainly has the motivation to pitch well down the stretch with the AL Cy Young Award well within his grasp. The Yankees have been somewhat cautious with him recently, giving him an extra day's rest here and there and getting him out of the Tigers' start last week after just six innings. He should be relatively fresh and ready to dominate a Tigers' lineup that is ranked just 29th in baseball in runs per game. The Tigers are also 23rd in strikeouts per game so expect a lot of swings and misses against Cole on Tuesday night. Tigers' starter Wentz has had a rough second season after pitching well after his call-up at the end of 2022. The Bombers should have their way with Wentz in the series opener.

Take the Yankees with the run line.

Prediction: Yankees -1.5
