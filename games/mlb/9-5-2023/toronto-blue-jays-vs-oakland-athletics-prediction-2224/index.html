Toronto Blue Jays (75-62) vs. Oakland Athletics (42-95)

The 2023 MLB season goes on Tuesday, September 5, with the Toronto Blue Jays facing off against the Oakland Athletics in the American League showdown at Oakland Coliseum, and the first pitch is set for 9:40 PM ET. 

The Blue Jays and Athletics continue a three-game series in Oakland, and Monday’s opener has been excluded from the analysis. Toronto opens as a -188 moneyline fave for Tuesday night, while the totals sit at 8.5 runs.  

The Jays are desperate for a win

The Toronto Blue Jays edged the Colorado Rockies 7-5 this past Sunday to clinch a three-game set at Coors Field in Denver and improve to 75-62 on the season. It was their fifth victory in eight games, and the Jays were sitting a game and a half behind the final wild card in the American League on Monday morning. 

The Blue Jays, who miss Bo Bichette (quad), Matt Chapman (finger), and Danny Jansen (finger), are slashing a marvelous .348/.434/.569 with 17 extra-base hits and seven home runs in the last seven days and 181 at-bats. Over the last couple of weeks, the Jays have recorded a .842 OPS while slugging 30 extra-base hits and 17 dingers, and Davis Schneider has gone 12-for-28 with three homers and 11 RBI during that span. 

Chris Bassitt will be on the bump Tuesday in Oakland, and the 34-year-old righty is 13-7 with a 3.81 ERA, 1.19 WHIP, and 151/52 K/BB ratio in 28 starts (165.1 innings pitched) this season. Bassitt went 3-1 in August, posting a 3.03 ERA and 4.57 FIP across five starts and 32.2 innings of work. He was terrific last Wednesday, throwing eight scoreless innings in a 7-0 blanking of the Washington Nationals, who mustered just three hits off Bassitt.

The A’s are not the worst team in baseball anymore

The Oakland Athletics entered the Blue Jays series searching for their fourth straight win. Last Sunday, the A’s beat the Los Angeles Angels 10-6 to sweep a three-game home series and move to 42-95 on the season. It was Oakland’s fourth victory in five games, and the A’s have climbed out of last in the majors, leaving the Kansas City Royals behind them. 

The Athletics are hitting a strong .276/.319/.494 with 15 extra-base hits and seven round-trippers in the last seven days and 170 at-bats. They’ve slugged 19 home runs over the past two weeks, and catcher Shea Langeliers has been responsible for five of those 19 dingers. 

Zach Neal will toe the rubber Tuesday, and the 34-year-old right-hander is 1-0 with a bloated 6.55 ERA, 1.64 WHIP, and 17/9 K/BB ratio in two starts and seven relief appearances (22 IP) in 2023. Neal will make his third straight turn through the Athletics rotation and over the previous two, he’s surrendered seven runs (five earned) on eight hits and five walks across ten innings of work. Last Wednesday, Neal tossed five frames of a three-run ball in a no-decision against the Seattle Mariners on the road.

Chris Bassitt will meet the A’s for the second time this season. He yielded four earned runs on five hits and two walks in a no-decision against Oakland on June 23 (Toronto eventually lost 5-4). The current A’s are 8-for-30 with no extra-base hits or home runs against Bassitt, so I’m expecting a former Athletic to pitch well in this game. Bassitt holds a shiny 2.43 ERA and 1.04 WHIP in 266.1 innings of work at Oakland Coliseum. 

Hereof, I’m backing the Blue Jays to come out on top and beat the runline. It’s hard to trust Zach Neal, though the Athletics’ bullpen has recorded a sturdy 2.81 ERA and a 3.60 FIP over the last ten days and 32 innings pitched. Still, the Blue Jays have accounted for a 147 wRC+ against right-handed pitchers and 167 wRC+ against left-handed pitchers during that span, so I’m expecting them to score early and often.
