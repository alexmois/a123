The Seattle Mariners (77-69, 1st AL West) play game two of a three-game interleague series on the road at Great American Ballpark Tuesday against the Cincinnati Reds (71-68, 3rd NL Central). Seattle lost to the New York Mets on Sunday 6-3 but remained first in the American League West by one game ahead of Houston and Texas. The Mariners have won six of the last nine. Cincinnati was defeated by the Chicago Cubs 15-7 on Sunday snapping a two-game winning streak for the Reds. Cincinnati is 6 ½ games behind first in the National League Central and tied with three other clubs for the third and final NL wildcard spot. Seattle will play without five pitchers as well as position players Jarred Kelenic, Tom Murphy and Evan White. Cincinnati will play without 11 pitchers as well as position players Curt Casali, Jonathan India, Matt McLain, Kevin Newman and Joey Votto.

Seattle in a three-way battle for first in the AL West

Seattle's pitching and much improvement at the plate are keeping the Mariners in a three-way battle for first place in the American League West. Seattle leads the division by one game over Houston and Texas. The Mariners lead baseball in both team ERA and WHIP at 3.67 and 1.17 respectively. Seattle's starting rotation has the second most Quality Starts with 66 and the pitching staff holds opposing hitters to an average of .237 which is fourth lowest in baseball. Logan Gilbert has 12 wins to lead Seattle and Luis Castillo has 183 strikeouts to lead the club. Right-hander Bryce Miller will start on the mound for Seattle and this season is 8-4 with an ERA of 3.93 with 100 strikeouts.

 

Seattle's production at the plate has improved although the Mariners are averaging just .244 which is 19th but improves to 12th in on base percentage at .325 and 14th in slugging percentage with .418. Those stats have produced 652 runs which is 11th best in baseball. Julio Rodriguez has been the most productive at the plate for Seattle with 32 doubles, two triples, 24 home runs and 88 RBIs. Rodriguez leads the club in doubles and RBI and is second in HRs. Cal Raleigh is leading Seattle in home runs with 25.

Rookie right-hander will debut on the mound for Cincinnati

The Cincinnati Reds have struggled with injuries both to position players and pitchers and because of that the Reds have called up rookie right-hander Connor Phillips (4-5, 3.86 ERA, 154 strikeouts), who will start on the mound Tuesday for the Reds in his major league debut. On the season, Cincinnati is 23rd in WHIP at 1.41 and 24th in each of the following: ERA (4.72), Quality Starts (40) and batting average allowed (.257).

At the plate, Cincinnati has scored the 10th most runs in baseball with 655 but drops to 13th in on base percentage at .323, 17th in slugging percentage with .410 and 18th in team batting average at .247. These numbers are also affected by injuries as Cincinnati is playing without several productive hitters such as Jonathan India, Kevin Newman, Joey Votto and Matt McLain. Spencer Steer is leading Cincinnati in home runs with 19 and an RBI with 73. Jake Fraley is third in home runs with 15 and 2nd in RBI with 63. McLain is second in home runs with 16, but is on the IL.
