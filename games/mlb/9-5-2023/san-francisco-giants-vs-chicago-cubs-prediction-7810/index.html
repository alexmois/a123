The San Francisco Giants (70-68, 66-71 RL, 57-77-3 O/U) take on the Chicago Cubs (74-64, 71-67 RL, 71-63-4 O/U) in the second of a three-game series on Tuesday night. The Giants will likely be using their bullpen. The Cubs will counter with Kyle Hendricks. The Cubs lead the season series 3-1.

Giants Expected to Use Bullpen

The Giants are going with a bullpen start. They have been using Sean Manaea and Jake Junis in these outings. Manaea has a 4.96 ERA and a 1.32 WHIP on the year. Junis tossed four scoreless innings against the Padres in his latest appearance. The veteran now has a strong 3.87 ERA and a 1.29 WHIP accompanied by a 4-3 record in 76.2 innings pitched on the season. He has conceded four runs in three innings against the Cubs this season.

The Giants are near the end of a road trek. They won the first bout of the series against the Padres this past weekend but proceeded to lose the next three due to the slumping offense. They only managed to score 11 runs in the series.  The squad has been shaky in the last month but stands in the last wildcard spot. They are 4-6 in their last ten clashes.

Thairo Estrada had a big series against the Cubs earlier this season, bashing two homers with five RBIs but was hitless on Monday. The 27-year-old second baseman is having a solid year, collecting 41 RBIs and a .744 OPS. Estrada leads the Giants in hits but doesn't have any RBIs in his last five games. Michael Conforto remains on the IL.

San Francisco is scoring an average of 4.26 runs on the year, marking them 22nd. The pitching has been solid and is carrying a 3.97 ERA, placing them 10th.

Hendricks Starts, Cubs Shine at Home

Kyle Hendricks will get the nod on Tuesday. Hendricks was stellar in his previous start, limiting a hot Brewers lineup to one run in six innings, resulting in a no-decision in a game the Cubs won. The veteran is in a groove, conceding two or fewer runs in three consecutive outings, and has recorded a 3.59 ERA and a 1.13 WHIP accompanied by a 5-7 record in 110.1 innings pitched on the season. Hendricks has dominated the Giants in his career, sporting a sharp 2.03 ERA and a 6-2 record in 71 innings. 

Chicago posted two wins in four meetings against the Reds on the weekend, propelled by the offense, scoring 24 runs. The Cubs have especially been excellent at home where they have won three of their last four series. They stand in the 1st wildcard spot and have a cushion of three games. The squad is 7-3 in their last ten bouts.

Christopher Morel has enjoyed plenty of big moments this season but has been slumping at the plate recently, recording zero RBIs in his last ten games. The 24-year-old second baseman has 19 homers with 60 RBIS and a .794 OPS on the year. He is 3 for 11 with three RBIs against the Giants.

Chicago is averaging 5.05 runs per game, ranking them fifth. The pitching staff has reported a 4.10 team ERA, positioning them 15th.
