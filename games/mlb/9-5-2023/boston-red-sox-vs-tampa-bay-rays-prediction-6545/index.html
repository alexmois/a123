Red Sox Trying to Claw Back into AL Wild Card Picture

Boston won their second straight after a five-game slide as they downed the Royals on the road in the rubber game of their set Sunday. The Red Sox entered Monday fourth in the AL East with a 71-66 record, leaving them 14.5 games behind the Orioles for the top spot in the division. They were 5.5 games behind the Rangers and Astros, who were tied for the final two wild card spots in the AL playoff picture. On Sunday, Boston got a pair of hits from Rafael Devers (RBI) and Masataka Yoshida (run, three RBI) to lead a nine-hit attack. Adam Duvall (his 19th) and Yoshida (his 14th) each went deep in the victory. Chris Sale (6-3) earned the win on the mound as he threw five shutout innings, allowing two hits with two walks and five strikeouts.

Kutter Crawford gets the call for the Red Sox in this one as he makes his 27th appearance and 19th start of the season. He is 6-7 with a 4.08 ERA, a 1.138 WHIP, 26 walks and 102 strikeouts over 103.2 innings of work on the year. Crawford took the loss in his last start, which came Thursday at home against the Astros. He threw 2.2 innings, allowing six runs on seven hits with one walk and one strikeout in a 7-4 Red Sox defeat. In his last three starts, Crawford is 1-1 with a 5.93 ERA, a 1.17 WHIP, four walks and 13 strikeouts over 13.2 innings of work. Crawford makes his sixth career appearance and third start against the Rays in this contest. He is 1-0 with a 2.21 ERA, a 0.689 WHIP, one walk and 24 strikeouts over 20.1 innings of work against them. Crawford has no record with a 3.00 ERA, a 0.75 WHIP, no walks and 13 strikeouts over 12 innings in three career appearances, one start, at Tropicana Field.

Tampa Bay Rays Looking to Chase Down AL East Top Spot

Tampa Bay snapped a two-game slide to win for the fifth time in seven games as they downed the Guardians in the finale of a three-game set on the road Sunday afternoon. The Rays entered Monday 83-54 on the year and stood second in the AL East, 2.5 games behind Baltimore for the top spot. They held the top wild card spot in the American League playoff picture and sat eight games ahead of Toronto to remain on the right side of the cut line. On Sunday, Tampa Bay banged out 13 hits with Brandon Lowe (run) leading the way with three while Taylor Walls (run, RBI) added two. Rene Pinto hit his second homer of the season in the victory. Taj Bradley didn’t factor in the decision as he threw five innings, allowing two runs (one earned) on three hits with five walks and seven strikeouts. Colin Poche (10-3) got the final two outs of the seventh, allowing no runs on one hit with no walks and one strikeout, to earn the win in relief. Pete Fairbanks got the final two outs, allowing no runs on no hits with no walks or strikeouts, to record his 19th save.

Zach Eflin is on the mound for the Rays as he makes his 27th start of the season in this contest. He comes in 13-8 with a 3.40 ERA, a 1.022 WHIP, 20 walks and 151 strikeouts over 150.2 innings of work this season. Eflin didn’t factor in the decision in his last start, which came Thursday on the road against the Marlins. He threw 6.1 innings, allowing no runs on four hits with no walks and four strikeouts in a game the Rays eventually won 3-0 in 10 innings. In his last three starts, Eflin is 1-1 with a 1.47 ERA, a 0.93 WHIP, two walks and 19 strikeouts over 18.1 innings of work. Eflin makes his third career start against the Red Sox in this contest. He has no record with a 7.00 ERA, a 1.889 WHIP, four walks and 14 strikeouts over nine innings of work. Eflin is 10-5 with a 3.19 ERA, a 0.99 WHIP, 12 walks and 102 strikeouts over 96 innings in 17 career appearances, 16 starts, at Tropicana Field.
