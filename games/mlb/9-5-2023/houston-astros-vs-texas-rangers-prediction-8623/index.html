The Houston Astros and Texas Rangers will resume their AL West rivalry on Tuesday at Globe Life Field. First pitch is at 7:05 p.m. CT.

Houston (78-61 SU and 70-69 RL) is starting left-hander Framber Valdez. The 29-year-old is 10-9 with a 3.38 ERA and a 1.10 WHIP in 26 outings this season.

Texas (76-61 SU and 73-64 RL) is countering with right-hander Nathan Eovaldi. The 33-year-old is 11-3 with a 2.69 ERA and a 1.01 WHIP in 19 starts.

Is Houston the best in the West?

Houston could have used Texas' recent struggles to its advantage, but it couldn't get the job done at home against the Yankees over the weekend. With three teams fighting for the top spot in the division, every game feels like a must-win.

The Astros score 5.00 runs per game (6th) and hit .257 (8th) with a .755 OPS (8th). They've hit 180 long balls (9th) and stolen 95 bases (17th) this season. The pitching staff has compiled a 3.95 ERA (8th) and a 1.29 WHIP (18th) with 61 quality starts (5th).

Valdez will take the hill for the visitors on Tuesday. In his last start, the sixth-year pro gave up four runs (two earned) and five hits in a six-inning win vs. Boston. He only lasted 3.2 innings on July 26 against Texas, surrendering six runs and eight hits in a losing effort. Valdez also faced the Rangers on April 16, giving up five runs (one earned) on four hits in another loss.

Texas needs its mojo back

Texas entered play on Monday losers of 12 of its last 16 games. Can it find its groove at home against its rival?

The Rangers average 5.53 runs (3rd) and hit .267 (2nd) with a .793 OPS (3rd). They've launched 190 homers (7th) and stolen 71 bases (25th) in 2023. The pitching staff has posted a 4.13 ERA (15th) and a 1.24 WHIP (8th) with 59 quality starts (9th).

Eovaldi will toe the rubber for the home team on Tuesday night at Globe Life Field. The 12th-year pro is making his first start since July 18 after a stint on the injured list with a forearm injury. He is 5-2 with a 2.60 ERA and a 1.09 WHIP in eight home starts this year.

This has the potential to be a high-scoring game, as Valdez has been knocked around by the Rangers this season. He's pitched better as of late (two earned runs in his last 13 IP), though. Eovaldi is a bit of a wild card, as he was having a great season prior to his IL stint. Will he pick up where he left off or struggle to find his groove?

The latter isn't ideal considering the opponent. Houston tore the cover off the ball in August, slashing .286 BA/.367 OBP/.474 SLG/.842 OPS in 28 games (6.0 runs/game). It didn't play its best ball against the Yankees last series, but it got back on track on Monday, scoring 13 runs on 16 hits with five home runs.

In this battle between in-state foes, I'm predicting a Texas-sized win for the Astros on Tuesday night!

The over, 38-25-6 in Rangers home games, is my bet on the total in Tuesday's game.
