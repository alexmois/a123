The St. Louis Cardinals face the Atlanta Braves with both teams on opposite ends of the standings to close out the season. The Cardinals have a 59-78 record and are in last place in the National League Central Division. The Braves have a 90-46 record and with eight wins in their last 10 games, they have the best record in baseball. For the upcoming game, the Cardinals will turn to Miles Mikolas as their starting pitcher who has allowed 15 runs in his last three starts while the Braves will turn to Mike Soroka to start the game.

Cardinals hope Mikolas and lineup step up

The Cardinals are having a rough season and hope to put together a strong few weeks to bring optimism into next season. Miles Mikolas will start the upcoming game and he hopes to improve on a rough season as he's allowed 87 runs in 164.1 innings pitched. In addition, with a .284 expected opponent Batting Average, a 16.0 Strikeout Percentage, and a 4.96 expected ERA, opponents are making contact and putting together strong plate appearances against Mikolas to easily drive in runs.

Mikolas has struggled but the lineup has also sputtered, scoring only 4.49 runs per game. Lars Nootbar is slashing .279/.381/.452 with 13 home runs and 154 total bases while Paul Goldschmidt is slashing .274/.363/.451 with 22 home runs and 234 total bases but the rest of the lineup has struggled. Only four batters have 15 home runs or more and only five have 160 total bases or more and opposing pitchers can limit the top-heavy lineup as a result.

Braves hope dominant lineup helps out Soroka

The Braves are having a great season and look poised to finish with the best record in baseball. Mike Soroka has battled injuries in recent years and is having a rough season on the mound because of them, allowing 18 runs in 29.1 innings pitched. Moreover, with a .278 expected opponent Batting Average, a 17.6 Strikeout Percentage, and a 5.51 expected ERA, opponents are making contact and putting together strong plate appearances to easily drive in runs against Soroka.

Soroka has struggled but the lineup has been the best in baseball, scoring 5.80 runs per game with 43 runs in the last seven games. Ronald Acuna Jr. is slashing .335 .416 .578 with 32 home runs and 318 total bases while Matt Olson is slashing .266/.373/.581 with 44 home runs and 299 total bases but the entire lineup has stepped up. Sean Murphy is slashing .268/.377/.515 with 20 home runs and 169 total bases while Austin Riley and Marcell Ozuna have added 64 home runs and 508 total bases to round out the lineup, making the Braves tough to stop at the plate.
