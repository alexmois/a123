Big Offseason Looming

The Angels have essentially thrown up the white flag for the 2023 season despite the front office making a last-ditch effort at the trade deadline to get the right pieces together to make a playoff push. Instead, the Angels went into a funk and have fallen well in the back of the pack both in the AL West Division and the AL Wildcard chase. The Angels recently shut down Mike Trout after he made a one-game return from the IL and it seems unlikely he will return before the season ends. Additionally, MVP-favorite Shohei Ohtani was disclosed as having a UCL injury and will not return to the mound this season. The Angels will now play out the string while simultaneously wondering if they will be able to re-sign Ohtani in the offseason. This was emphasized on Tuesday afternoon when the Angels placed starter Lucas Giolito, relievers Matt Moore and Reynaldo López, and outfielders Hunter Renfroe and Randal Grichuk on waivers. In the first two games against the Phillies, the Angels have been outscored 18-11 and dropped each contest.

The Angels will turn to lefty Reid Detmers on Wednesday afternoon. Detmers is 3-10 on the season in 23 starts, including six quality starts. Detmers has allowed 116 hits in 120 innings pitched with an ERA of 5.03. On the road, Detmers is 1-6 with a 5.69 ERA in 10 starts. He has given up 46 hits in 49 innings pitched and struck out 47 batters in those ten starts. Detmers is 1-2 in his last five starts with an inflated 7.71 ERA while striking out 17 batters in 23.1 innings pitched.

Key Injuries: CF Mike Trout, SS Zach Neto, and 1B CJ Cron are out.

The Phillies are charging toward a second straight season with a wildcard berth as they stand atop the wildcard standings despite trailing the NL East-leading Braves by over ten games. Last season, the Phillies rode their wildcard berth all the way to the World Series so hopes are high in Philadelphia. Heading into this series, the Phillies have won five of six games including a sweep of the Cardinals over the weekend. In games one and two against the Angels, the Phillies' offense flexed their muscles in scoring 18 runs combined in the two wins. The Phillies homered eight times in the two games including three by Trea Turner and two by Bryce Harper. The Phillies have won five in a row and seven of their last eight games.

The Phillies will hand the ball to young lefty Christopher Sanchez in the series finale against the Angels. Sanchez is 2-0 in his last five games with an ERA of 3.86. He has allowed 24 hits in 28 innings pitched while striking out 26 in that time. At home this season, Sanchez is 2-3 with an ERA of 4.20 in nine starts. He has allowed 47 hits in 49.1 innings pitched and struck out 49 batters.

Key Injuries: OF Cristian Pache is out.
