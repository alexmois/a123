The San Diego Padres (62-71, 64-69 RL, 54-72-7 O/U) and the St. Louis Cardinals (57-76, 62-70 RL, 61-66-5 O/U) conclude a three-game series on Wednesday afternoon. The Padres are sending veteran Rich Hill to the mound. The Cardinals will have Miles Mikolas on the rubber. The Padres collected a 4-1 victory in Monday’s opener and the Cards replied with a 6-5 win on Tuesday.

Hill Starts Last Game of Series

Rich Hill will take the mound on Wednesday afternoon in place of an injured Yu Darvish. The 43-year-old southpaw was used out of the bullpen last time out due to his struggles on the mound. The veteran southpaw has an abysmal 9.00 ERA since arriving in San Diego and a 5.21 ERA and a 1.50 WHIP to go with a 7-13 record in 133 innings pitched on the season. Hill held the Cardinals to one run in 6.2 innings earlier this season and has reported a 4.09 ERA and a 5-2 record in 50.2 career innings. 

San Diego is concluding a road trek in this game. They were swept by the Brewers before this series basically ending their wildcard hopes considering they are seven games out. The Padres' pitching is usually strong but was exploited in Milwaukee but rebounded by holding the Cards to one run on Monday. The Padres are 4-6 in their last ten decisions.

Mikolas Eyes First Win of August

Miles Mikolas will get the nod in this one. Mikolas was rocked last time out, conceding five runs on eight hits in six innings against the Phillies, and was hit with the loss. The veteran righty was sharp early in the month but has not been at his best in three straight outings and has recorded a 4.66 ERA and a 1.30 WHIP accompanied by a 6-10 record in 158.1 innings pitched on the year. Mikolas has silenced the Padres, sporting a remarkable 2.08 ERA and a 2-2 record in 30.1 innings.

The Cardinals were also dealt a sweep in their previous series in Philly. They have now dropped three consecutive series due to a slumping offense. The squad hasn't been any better at home recently, dropping four of their last five home decisions including a series loss to the Mets in that span. They are just 2-8 in their last ten decisions.
