Arizona Diamondbacks (69-63) vs. Los Angeles Dodgers (81-49)

The 2023 Major League Baseball season continues Wednesday, August 30, with a full 15-game slate, including the NL West showdown from Dodger Stadium in Los Angeles, California, so we bring you the best Diamondbacks vs. Dodgers betting pick. 

The Diamondbacks and Dodgers wrap up a three-game series in LA. The Dodgers outlasted the D-backs 7-4 in Monday’s opener, while the middle game of the series has been excluded from the analysis. 

The D-backs are fighting for the playoffs

The Arizona Diamondbacks fell to 69-63 on the season following Monday’s loss at Dodger Stadium. Zac Gallen had a bad day in the office, surrendering six earned runs on nine hits and three walks through 5.1 innings of work, and the Diamondbacks recorded the third defeat in their last 13 games overall. Arizona held the final wild card in the National League on Tuesday morning, just half a game ahead of the San Francisco Giants and a game and a half ahead of the Cincinnati Reds. 

Over the last couple of weeks, Arizona is hitting a nice .270/.349/.429 with 25 extra-base hits and 12 home runs across 434 at-bats. The Diamondbacks have thrown 119 innings during that span while posting a 3.93 ERA, 1.22 WHIP, and .224 batting average against. 

Brandon Pfaadt will take the mound Wednesday in LA, and the 24-year-old rookie right-hander is 1-6 with a pedestrian 5.91 ERA, 1.36 WHIP, and 60/20 K/BB ratio in 13 starts (67 innings pitched) in 2023. Pfaadt took his first major-league win last Friday, tossing 5.1 frames of a two-run ball in a 10-8 home victory over the Reds. He’s only allowed two earned runs on four hits and four walks over his last two starts and 12.1 innings of work. 

Pfaadt will meet the Blue Crew for the second time this season. He was charged with a loss in a 5-4 home defeat to the Dodgers on August 8, yielding four earned runs on six hits and a pair of free passes across 4.2 innings.

The Dodgers continue their dominance in August

The Los Angeles Dodgers moved to 81-49 on the season following that 7-4 victory against the Diamondbacks. It was LA’s 18th win in 21 games, and the NL West-leading Dodgers were 13 games above Arizona on Tuesday morning. The Boys in Blue were also four games behind the Atlanta Braves, who boast the best record in the majors. 

The Dodgers are slashing a solid .277/.337/.461 with 28 extra-base hits and 18 long balls in the past two weeks and 451 at-bats. They’ve thrown 115 frames in that span, compiling a 3.60 ERA, 1.08 WHIP, and .225 batting average against. 

Lance Lynn (10-9; 5.56 ERA) will be on the bump Wednesday. The 36-year-old right-hander has done a good job since he joined the Dodgers at the trade deadline, tallying a 2.03 ERA, 4.69 FIP, and .681 opposing OPS in five starts and 31 innings of work (4-0 record). Last Friday, he gave up four runs (three earned) on ten hits across six frames of work and took a win in a 7-4 road victory against the Boston Red Sox. 

Lynn hasn’t met the Diamondbacks since 2020. He’s 6-1 with a strong 2.96 ERA in 12 career starts and a couple of relief appearances against Arizona. The current Diamondbacks are 11-for-60 with four doubles and four RBI against Lynn.
