The Tampa Bay Rays (80-52) will be trying to cut into Baltimore’s lead in the AL East standings when they face the Miami Marlins (66-65) in the final game of their two-game interleague series on Wednesday night.

Zach Eflin, 13-8, 3.55 ERA, will get the start for the Rays. The Marlins will counter with Jesus Luzardo, 9-8, 3.77 ERA.

Tampa Bay is 8-2 in its last 10 games against Miami.

**This preview was written before Tuesday’s game was played**

Rays Going For Seventh Win In Eight Games

The Rays played well over the past week and they’ve won six of their last seven games. However, they are still 2.5 games behind Baltimore in the AL East standings and will try to reduce the distance with a win on Wednesday, which will give them their seventh win in their last eight games.

Tampa Bay is averaging 5.33 runs per game. Their .260 batting average is fourth in the league. Their .331 on base percentage is fifth, while their .447 slugging percentage is fourth.

Yandy Diaz leads the Rays with a .326 batting average, while Isaac Paredes leads the team with 26 home runs and 80 RBI.

Tampa Bay’s pitching has been good, with the team giving up 4.02 runs per game. Opponents have a .232 batting average against the Rays, which leads the league. Their 3.87 ERA is fifth, while their 1.19 WHIP is second.

In his last start, Eflin gave up seven hits and two runs in six innings, leading to a 6-2 loss to the Yankees. They will need another solid start from him if they want to get the win.

Marlins Trying To Stay In Wildcard Race

The Marlins didn’t play well over the past week and they’ve lost three of their last five games. Despite their slump, they are only 2.5 games behind the San Francisco Giants, who are in the final NL Wildcard spot. They will try to reduce the gap with a win over the Rays, which will give them their third win in their last six games.

Miami is averaging 4.02 runs per game. Their .258 batting average is seventh in the league. Their .314 on base percentage is 22nd, while their .400 slugging percentage is 21st.

Luis Arraez leads Miami with a .350 batting average, while Jorge Soler leads the team with 35 home runs and 71 RBI.

Miami’s pitching has been good, with the team giving up 4.38 runs per game. Opponents have a .243 batting average against the Marlins, which is 13th in the league. Their 4.15 ERA is 15th, while their 1.27 WHIP is 13th.

In his last start, Luzardo gave up two hits and no runs in six innings, leading to a 3-0 win over San Diego. They will need a similar effort from him if they want to win this game.
