It’s the finale of an early week series on the diamond between National League teams in drastically different situations heading to the final month of the season in the Mile-High City. The Atlanta Braves are on the road as they make the trip to finish a three-game series with the Colorado Rockies on Wednesday night. In the opening game of the series Monday night, Atlanta piled up runs and pulled away late to earn a one-sided 14-4 triumph, drawing first blood in the set. The Braves won again on Tuesday, prevailing 3-1. Will the Braves take care of business again here or will the Rockies’ bats play spoiler on getaway day?

Atlanta Braves Looking to Earn Road Series Win

Atlanta won for the fifth time in six games as they dropped the hammer on the Rockies late to take the opening game of the series Monday night. The Braves improved to 86-45 on the year after Tuesday's 3-1 win and hold a hefty 12.5-game lead over the Phillies for the top spot in the NL East. On Monday, Atlanta rapped out 18 hits with Ronald Acuna Jr. (four runs, five RBI) leading the way with four while Sean Murphy (two runs, RBI) and Orlando Arcia (run, RBI) each added three. Acuna Jr. (his 29th) and Austin Riley (his 31st) each homered in the victory for the Braves. Bryce Elder (11-4) wasn’t sharp, allowing four runs on eight hits with one walk and four strikeouts over six innings, but picked up the victory, nonetheless.

This would be Spencer Strider’s turn in the rotation on regular rest, but they are saving him for the big showdown on Friday in LA against the Dodgers. Instead, according to MLB.com, rookie Darius Vines will get the start. This is the right-hander's first appearance of the season. The 25-year-old was called up from Triple-A Gwinnett on Monday.

Colorado Rockies Trying to Play Spoiler

Colorado lost for the seventh time in their last eight games as they were clobbered in the opening game of this series Monday night. The Rockies fell to 49-83 after Tuesday's 3-1 loss and sit in the basement of the NL West, 33.5 games behind the Dodgers for the top spot. They were 20 games behind the Giants for the final wild-card spot in the National League playoff picture. On Monday night, Colorado got two hits each from Charlie Blackmon (run), Ryan McMahon (run, two RBI) and Hunter Goodman (RBI) though it wasn’t nearly enough on this night. McMahon clobbered his 22nd homer of the season in a losing effort. Austin Gomber didn’t factor in the decision as he threw 2.2 innings, allowing two runs on four hits with two walks and one strikeout. Karl Kauffmann (1-4) took the loss in relief as he allowed three runs on four hits with no walks and one strikeout over three innings of work.

Kyle Freeland is on the hill for the Rockies as he takes the ball for his 26th start of the season in this contest. He is 5-13 with a 5.00 ERA, a 1.467 WHIP, 37 walks and 86 strikeouts over 135 innings of work this season. Freeland didn’t factor in the decision in his last start, which came against the Orioles on the road Friday night. He threw six innings, allowing three runs on eight hits with no walks and three strikeouts in a game the Rockies eventually lost 5-4. In his last three starts, Freeland is 1-1 with a 6.19 ERA, a 1.50 WHIP, two walks and 10 strikeouts over 16 innings of work. Freeland makes his eighth career start against the Braves in this contest. He is 1-3 with a 5.80 ERA, a 1.537 WHIP, 17 walks and 31 strikeouts over 40.1 innings of work in those outings. Freeland is 32-28 with a 4.76 ERA, a 1.442 WHIP, 153 walks and 377 strikeouts over 480.2 innings in 91 career appearances, 88 starts, at Coors Field.
