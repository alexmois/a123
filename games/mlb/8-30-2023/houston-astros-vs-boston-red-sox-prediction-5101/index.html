The Houston Astros face the Boston Red Sox with both teams having great seasons in the American League. The Astros have a 76-58 record and with a 26-17 record since the All-Star break, they have closed the gap on first place in the American League West Division lead. The Red Sox have a 69-64 record and are only a few games back of a wild card spot in the American League but hope to bounce back from three straight losses. For the final game of the series, the Astros will turn to Framber Valdez as their starting pitcher, who looks to build off a seven-scoreless inning outing while the Red Sox will turn to Kutter Crawford who has only allowed three runs in his last two starts.

Astros hope lineup helps out Valdez

The Astros are having a great season and in a tight race, they look to take the division title for the third year in a row. Framber Valdez has pitched well recently but he has struggled on the mound, allowing 67 runs in 161.2 innings pitched. In addition, with a .248 expected opponent Batting Average, a .403 expected opponent Slugging Percentage, and a 4.19 expected ERA, opponents are making contact and powering the ball against Valdez to easily drive in runs.

Valdez has had a rough year on the mound but the lineup has carried the Astros, scoring 5.04 runs per game. Yordan Alvarez is slashing .288/.394/.560 with 22 home runs and 173 total bases while Jose Altuve is slashing .322/.415/.529 with 10 home runs and 128 total bases but the rest of the lineup has been great as well. Kyle Tucker is slashing .293/.374/.521 with 26 home runs and 247 total bases while Alex Bregman and Chas McCormick have added 40 home runs and 260 total bases to round out the lineup, making the Astros tough to stop at the plate.

Red Sox hope Crawford and lineup end series strong

The Red Sox are having a great season and hope to make a push in the wild card race in the final weeks of the season. The team will turn to Kutter Crawford to start the upcoming game and he hopes to build off a strong season as he's only allowed 42 runs in 101 innings pitched while tossing 101 strikeouts. In addition, with a .212 expected opponent Batting Average, a 6.1 Walk Rate, and a 3.43 expected ERA, opponents are making minimal contact against Crawford and struggling to drive in runs as a result.

Crawford has stepped up but the lineup has carried the Red Sox this year, scoring 4.99 runs per game. Adam Duvall is slashing .272/.335/.582 with 17 home runs and 139 total bases while Justin Turner is slashing .290/.361/.495 with 22 home runs and 225 total bases but the entire lineup has been great this year. Rafael Devers is slashing .269/.345/.512 with 29 home runs and 243 total bases while Triston Casas and Alex Verdugo have added 33 home runs and 386 total bases to add depth to the lineup, making the Red Sox lineup tough to stop.
