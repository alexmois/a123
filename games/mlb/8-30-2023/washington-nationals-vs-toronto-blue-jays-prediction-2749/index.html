Monday kicked off this three-game set, with the Washington Nationals (61-71; before Tuesday night's results) falling by three runs against the Toronto Blue Jays (72-60). They play again on Tuesday before each club catches a flight on Wednesday. Between then, the final matchup of the season between these interleague foes will happen Wednesday afternoon. Toronto's Rogers Centre will host the action yet again. The first pitch is scheduled for 3:07 p.m. EDT in the Canadian city.

 

*Article published before Tuesday's game

Washington Run Slowing Down

Between August 11 and August 26, the Nationals went 11-3. However, two straight losses followed that stretch as the month approached its end. They're still competing, but this club is coming back down to earth. With how well Patrick Corbin has pitched this month, maybe another win will delay the fall. In August, the lefty has allowed only eight runs (seven earned) over 23.1 innings pitched. Three of those four starts ended with Washington wins. Home runs are still an issue, as he coughed up six in that stretch, but they've mostly been solo shots. The Nationals' bullpen is among the five worst teams in WAR, FIP, and ERA this season, so the recent success has mostly hinged on Corbin's arm. A strong showing in Toronto would punctuate his best full month since 2019. 

Washington's offense has had a solid month but tends to hit worse on the road. With a walk rate that's 28th and the second-fewest home runs league-wide, scoring will seldom come easy for this year's club regardless of the venue. What the Nationals do well is put the ball in play at least, with a 19.0% strikeout rate (2nd) leading to a .258 team batting average (6th). They're also a decent base-stealing team, with 98 swipes (13th) and an 80% success rate. Adding pop and drawing more walks should be the club's focus for 2024.

Fire and (Playoff) Desire

This year's Blue Jays were expected to make the playoffs, no excuses. When September rolls around, Toronto will be at the very least half a game out of a spot. The worst-case scenario is that they're 4.5 games back when the final month commences. Pitching hasn't been the issue, as only one team has a lower ERA than the Blue Jays. Chris Bassitt's track record in the Rogers Centre this season may lower that number on Wednesday. In 13 home starts, Bassitt is 6-3 with a 3.07 ERA. His last three home outings have ended with losses, so he seems due for a win. If Bassitt is off, the Blue Jays can at least turn to a bullpen that is top 10 in WAR, FIP, and ERA.

Offensively, Toronto is 16th in runs per game and performs worse at home. What they have in their favor on Wednesday is facing a lefty, a matchup that their heavy right-handed lineup takes advantage of often. Like the Nationals, the Blue Jays don't whiff often, ranking sixth in strikeout rate. Toronto is ninth in batting average and OBP. Even with middling power numbers (15th in slugging percentage), the Blue Jays bats are dangerous. It's the team's baserunning that hinders run production. Limiting those blunders may be the difference between a playoff spot and an early vacation.

Full-Game Side Bet

Washington's Patrick Corbin has been having his best month of the 2020s, and it almost seems too good to be true. This is why the Blue Jays, who slash .275/.345/.421 against southpaws will probably spoil the fun. After all, Corbin is still a below-average pitcher this season, with a 4.70 ERA, 5.24 FIP, 1.473 WHIP, and 91 ERA+ for the year. When he departs, the Nationals will turn to a bullpen that's collectively much worse.

As for Toronto, their pitching staff is quietly one of MLB's best. Their relievers can be trusted late, and starter Chris Bassitt has been a rock at home. That group is in a must-win game against a team that they're better than. Washington's offense isn't terrible, but it's also a below-average unit in 2023. There's no reason why the Blue Jays won't be able to slow them down again. Expect Toronto to jump in front early and never look back.

Full-Game Total Pick

Insiders Status:

Rating:

Patrick Corbin has been a poor pitcher for the past four seasons. He has a 5.51 ERA, 5.04 FIP, and 1.55 WHIP since 2020. This game pits him against a team that has a collective .766 OPS against lefties. If he gets a quick hook, he's ceding to a bullpen that owns a 5.22 ERA (27th) and -0.3 WAR (29th). Toronto seems destined for a healthy day at the plate.

On the other side, Chris Bassitt has given up at least four runs in his last three home starts. Washington is averaging over 4.5 runs per game in August and can take advantage of his struggles. Adding another three or four runs to Toronto's total should easily bring this game to nine or more runs. The over will hit in the series finale.
