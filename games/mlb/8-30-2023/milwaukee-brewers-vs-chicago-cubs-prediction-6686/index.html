The Milwaukee Brewers (74-58, 62-70 RL, 59-68-5 O/U) collide with the Chicago Cubs (70-62, 68-64 RL, 69-59-4 O/U) in an NL Central rivalry showdown on Wednesday afternoon. The Brewers will give the starting nod to Brandon Woodruff. The Cubs will counter with Kyle Hendricks. The Brewers lead the season series 5-4.

Woodruff Starts, Brewers Stay Hot

Brandon Woodruff will get the nod on Wednesday afternoon. Woodruff came through with a gem in his latest effort, limiting the Padres to just one run in six innings, and was awarded the victory. The Brewers veteran ace has allowed two or fewer runs in three of his last four outings since returning from the IL and is sporting a 2.65 ERA and a minuscule 0.88 WHIP complemented by a 3-1 record in 34 innings on the year. Woodruff held the Cubs to only one run in six innings in April and has posted a 3.14 ERA and a 2-2 record in 80.1 innings against the rivals.

The Brewers are hot, winning nine consecutive bouts entering Tuesday night, and have built a comfortable four-game lead ahead of the Cubs in the division. The bats have been shaky at times but are thriving in the streak, collecting 22 runs in three meetings against the Padres before this series, winning all three games. They had won four straight road bouts up until Tuesday's 1-0 loss to the Cubs.

Milwaukee continues to shine at the plate and is posting an average of 4.43 runs, marking them 18th. The pitching is excellent, amassing a 4.00 ERA, placing them 10th.

Hendricks Gets the Nod, Cubs Remain in WC Spot

The Cubs will have Kyle Hendricks on the mound. Hendricks was solid in his previous outing, conceding two runs in 5.1. innings against the Pirates but didn’t get any help from the offense and was charged with the loss. The veteran has reported a weak 5.00 ERA this month but has stellar numbers, issuing a 3.80 ERA and a 1.14 WHIP accompanied by a 5-7 record in 104.1 innings on the season. Hendricks limited Milwaukee to just two runs (one earned) in July and has registered a strong 3.51 ERA and a 10-8 record in 182 career innings. 

Chicago is home after going 4-2 on a road trek that concluded with three wins in four encounters with the Pirates led by the hot bats. They have also yet to lose a home series in August. The Cubs stand in the second wildcard spot, holding a one-game cushion. They stand at 7-3 in their last ten decisions.

Chicago is averaging a solid 5.07 runs per game, good for fifth. The pitching staff has registered a 4.17 ERA, placing them 15th.
