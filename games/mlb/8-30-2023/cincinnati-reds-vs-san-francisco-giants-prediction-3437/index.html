A pair of teams in the midst of a crowded National League wild-card race close out a series on the diamond in the city by the bay. The Cincinnati Reds are on the road as they play the finale of a three-game set with the San Francisco Giants on Wednesday night. In the opening game of the series Monday night, the Giants jumped in front early and held the advantage the rest of the way en route to a 4-1 victory. Will the Reds regroup and pick up a win here on getaway day or will the Giants clinch the series victory by prevailing at home? Reds Hoping to Gain Ground In NL Wild Card Chase

Cincinnati dropped their second straight and fourth in the last five games as they were beaten in the opening game of this set Monday night. The Reds entered Tuesday third in the NL Central with a 68-65 record and stood seven games behind the Brewers for the top spot. They were 1.5 games behind the Diamondbacks for the final wild card spot in the National League. On Monday night, Cincinnati finished with six hits with no player having more than one in the contest. Elly De La Cruz knocked in the Reds’ lone run on a RBI double in the eighth inning. Andrew Abbott (8-4) took the loss on the mound as he allowed three runs on five hits with three walks and six strikeouts over 3.1 innings of work.

Hunter Greene is on the mound for the Reds on getaway day as he makes his 17th start of the season in this contest. He comes in 2-6 with a 5.06 ERA, a 1.525 WHIP, 39 walks and 108 strikeouts over 80 innings of work on the year. Greene took the loss in his last start, which came on the road against the Diamondbacks Friday night. He threw 3.2 innings, allowing five runs on five hits with five walks and four strikeouts in a game the Reds eventually dropped 10-8. In his last three starts, Greene is 1-2 with a 10.66 ERA, a 2.53 WHIP, 12 walks and 11 strikeouts over 12.2 innings of work. Greene, in his 41st career major league start, pitches against the Giants for the first time here. As a result, this marks his first career outing at Oracle Park.

San Francisco Giants Looking to Secure Series Win

San Francisco won their second straight after a stretch where they dropped four of five as they took the opening game of this series Monday night. The Giants entered Tuesday 68-63 on the season and stood third in the NL West, 13.5 games behind the Dodgers for the division lead. They were half a game behind the Diamondbacks for the final wild card spot in the National League. On Monday, San Francisco finished with eight hits with J.D. Davis (run) the lone player to record two in the contest. Kyle Harrison (1-0) earned the win on the mound as he threw 6.1 innings, allowing no runs on three hits with two walks and 11 strikeouts. Camilo Doval worked a 1-2-3 ninth inning for his 35th save of the year.

Logan Webb is on the bump for the Giants as he logs his 28th start of the year on getaway day in this one. He is 9-10 with a 3.51 ERA, a 1.096 WHIP, 27 walks and 163 strikeouts over 174.1 innings of work on the year. Webb took the loss in his last start, which came against the Braves at home Friday night. He threw 5.1 innings, allowing five runs on six hits with one walk and one strikeout in a game the Giants dropped 5-1. In his last three starts, Webb is 0-1 with a 4.50 ERA, a 1.15 WHIP, two walks and 12 strikeouts over 20 innings of work. Webb makes his fifth career appearance and fourth start against the Reds in this contest. He is 2-0 with a 0.90 ERA, a 0.75 WHIP, three walks and 18 strikeouts over 20 innings of work against them. Webb is 22-15 with a 2.86 ERA, a 1.15 WHIP, 75 walks and 300 strikeouts over 308.2 innings of action in 53 career appearances, 50 starts, at Oracle Park.

Full-Game Side Bet

Rating:

These two teams are in the thick of a crowded field battling for the final two wild card spots in the National League playoff picture. The biggest weakness for each team is a lack of depth in the starting rotation. Cincinnati is trying to roll with a bunch of young guys and Greene has struggled in his two starts after spending two months on the IL. Meanwhile, the Giants have tried rolling with Webb and Alex Cobb while cobbling their way through the other spots in the rotation. Harrison may be a shot in the arm for them if he can pitch like he did Monday night. In this one, Webb has the upper hand on the mound given his consistency this season: he leads the National League in innings pitched on the year. Look for San Francisco to earn a road win here to take the series.

Prediction: San Francisco Giants -155
