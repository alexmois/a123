The Cleveland Guardians and Minnesota Twins will wrap up their three-game series on Wednesday at Target Field. First pitch is scheduled for 1:10 p.m. ET.

Cleveland (62-79 SU and 66-66 RL) is starting right-hander Tanner Bibee. The 24-year-old is 10-3 with a 3.01 ERA and a 1.20 WHIP in his 21 outings this season (119.2 IP).

Minnesota (69-63 SU and 63-69 RL) is starting righty Sonny Gray, The 33-year-old is 7-6 with a 3.06 ERA and a 1.19 WHIP in 26 outings.

The Twins are -135 moneyline favorites and the game total is eight runs.

Cleveland needs to finish red hot to win the Central

Cleveland took two of three games from Toronto in its last series. The season isn't over, but it needs to play more consistently in September to overtake Minnesota for the AL Central crown.

The Guardians score 4.06 runs per game (26th) and hit .248 (17th) with a .691 OPS (26th). They've launched 101 home runs (30th) and stolen 109 bases (8th) in 2023. The Cleveland pitching staff has posted a 3.88 ERA (6th) and a 1.29 WHIP (17th) with 48 quality starts (13th).

Bibee will take the hill for the visitors in the series finale. In his last start, the rookie hurler beat Toronto, holding the Jays to two runs with five strikeouts in six innings. He's held 12 straight opponents to three or fewer runs and has only allowed four or more runs twice this year. In his most recent outing (June 1) against the Twins, Bibee gave up three runs and six hits in five innings.

Is Minnesota a postseason dark horse or an easy out?

Minnesota has a comfortable lead over Cleveland and Detroit in the AL Central, but it can't simply coast to the finish line. If it's going to make any noise in the postseason, a strong finish will be a necessary catalyst.

The Twins average 4.54 runs (14th) and hit .241 (21st) with a .743 OPS (15th). They've hit 185 long balls (5th) and stolen 68 bases (24th) this season. The Minny pitching staff has posted a 3.94 ERA (8th) and a 1.21 WHIP (3rd) with a league-leading 64 quality starts.

Gray will toe the rubber for Minnesota on Wednesday afternoon at Target Field. In his last start, the veteran righty beat Texas, holding the Rangers to one run and six hits in seven innings. He's given up nine earned runs and 24 hits across five August outings (32.2 IP). Gray has faced the Guardians twice in 2023, surrendering six runs and 14 hits in 11.2 combined frames.

Minnesota is 40-27 straight-up at Target Field and 52-38 SU as favorites this season. The Twins sport a 24-19 record against AL Central opponents.

Cleveland is 29-37 SU on the road and 28-37 as underdogs (16-26 SU as road underdogs). The Guardians are 19-22 against division opponents this year.

Back Sonny Gray and the Twins on Wednesday afternoon in the series finale. The Twins' starter is 4-1 with a 2.29 ERA and a 1.14 WHIP in nine day games, holding opponents to a .191 batting average. He also boasts a 3.05 ERA and a 1.16 WHIP at home this season (13 starts). I'm confident he will turn in a quality start against the Guardians' slumping lineup (.226 BA/.284 OBP/.357 SLG/.641 OPS in August).

Cleveland starter Tanner Bibee has been a less reliable bet on the road this season, with an ERA (3.94) that's nearly double his home ERA (2.02). He's only made one more road start than home starts but has surrendered 25 more hits and nine more home runs. The Twins are having their best month at the plate (.254/.341/.463/.804), averaging over five runs per game. They've been a higher-scoring club since the All-Star break, producing 5.3 runs post-All-Star weekend vs. 4.1 runs per game in the first half of the season.

At -135 moneyline odds, Minnesota is my pick to win straight-up tomorrow.
