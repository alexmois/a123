Two American League West rivals meet on the diamond Wednesday when the Oakland Athletics (38-94, 5th AL West) visit the Seattle Mariners (75-56, 1st AL West) to play the third and final game of a three-game series at T-Mobile Park. Oakland was defeated by Seattle in the opening game of the series on Monday 7-0 and the Athletics have lost three straight. Oakland is last in the AL West and out of playoff contention. With its victory over Oakland on Monday, Seattle has won four straight and 12 out of the last 13 to take over first place in the AL West. Oakland will play without 10 pitchers as well as position player JJ Bleday. Seattle will play without five pitchers and position players Jarred Kelenic, Tom Murphy and Evan White.

 

This article was published prior to the results from Tuesday's game between the two.

Oakland is equally poor at the plate and on the mound

The Oakland Athletics are last in three major hitting categories. Oakland is last in team batting average (.222), in  runs scored (476) and slugging percentage (.364), while 27th in on base percentage (.300). Brent Rooker has been the best overall hitter for Oakland with 15 doubles, one triple, 22 home runs and 55 RBI. Rooker is the team leader in home runs and RBI. Shea Langeliers is second in home runs with 15 and in RBI with 45. Oakland has hit 178 doubles, 18 triples and 134 home runs in 132 games.

 

Oakland's pitching is also last in three major pitching statistics. Oakland is last in team ERA (5.76), WHIP (1.54) and Quality Starts (24). Oakland improves to 28th in batting average allowed at .272.  JP Sears is the leader in strikeouts for Oakland with 135 and the leader in victories is Trevor May with four. Oakland will hand the ball to Zach Neal on Wednesday. This season the right-hander is 1-0 with a 6.88 ERA and 11 Ks.

Seattle's pitching is one of baseball's best

The Seattle Mariners are first in each of the following pitching statistics; team ERA (3.64), WHIP (1.17) and Quality Starts (64). Seattle is 4th in batting average allowed at .235. Logan Gilbert is the leader in victories for Seattle with 12 and Luis Castillo is the leader in strikeouts with 181. Seattle will hand the ball on Wednesday to Bryce Miller. This season the right-hander is 8-4 with an ERA of 3.90 and 95 strikeouts.

Seattle is also producing well at the plate as the Mariners are 10th in runs scored (634), 11th in on-base percentage (.326) and 12th in slugging percentage (.420). Seattle is 19th in team batting average (.246). Cal Raleigh is leading Seattle in home runs with 85. Julio Rodriguez is the most productive hitter for Seattle with team highs in doubles with 31 and RBI with 87. Rodriguez is second in home runs with 24. Seattle has hit 238 doubles, 10 triples and 172 home runs in 131 games.
