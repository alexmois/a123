The Chicago White Sox and Baltimore Orioles will wrap up their three-game series in Baltimore on Wednesday. The White Sox are in the midst of a dismal season and have been reduced to the role of spoilers the rest of the way. Moving on from 2023

The Chicago White Sox have had a forgettable season in 2023 and the month of September will likely be spent reviewing the roster and considering which pieces will be critical to future success. The White Sox already parted ways with several players at this year's trade deadline and more will likely follow this offseason. Despite winning two straight heading into this series, the White Sox are over 25 games under .500 and in fourth place in the AL Central Division. The White Sox were shut out 9-0 in game one and followed that up with a 9-3 loss in game two of the series.

The White Sox will hand the ball over to right-hander Dylan Cease in the series finale on Wednesday afternoon. Cease is 5-7 on the season in 27 starts including 10 quality starts. He has allowed 140 hits in 142.1 innings pitched with an ERA of 4.81. Cease is just 1-3 in his last five starts with an ERA of 8.22 in that span. He has allowed 29 hits in 23 innings pitched including 26 strikeouts. Cease has faced the Orioles once this season, getting a no-decision while going six innings and allowing six hits and four runs although only two were earned.

Key Injuries: C Seby Zavala is out.

O's in a Dog Fight

The Baltimore Orioles took over first place in the AL East in early August but have been unable to pull away from a pesky Tampa Bay Rays team. Late last week, the Orioles were forced to put all-star closer Felix Bautista on the IL with a UCL injury. There is fear within the organization that he could be lost for the season. The Orioles still own the best record in the American League and reeled off seven wins in 10 games heading into this series. In games one and two, the Orioles took care of business by outscoring the White Sox 18-4 in the two wins. Baltimore remains two and a half games ahead of the Rays in the AL East Division race.

The Orioles will turn to right-hander Kyle Gibson in the finale against the White Sox. Gibson is 13-7 on the season in 27 starts including 14 quality starts. He has allowed 163 hits in 158.1 innings pitched with an ERA of 4.89. At home, Gibson is 6-3 with a 5.09 ERA with 58 strikeouts in 63.2 innings pitched. In his last five starts, he is 3-0 with an ERA of 5.74 while striking out 32 batters in 31.1 innings pitched. Gibson has faced the White Sox one time this season, earning a no-decision while going 5.1 innings and allowing seven hits and four runs.

Key Injuries: CL Felix Bautista and OF Aaron Hicks are out.

Full-Game Side Bet

Insiders Status:

Rating:

The Orioles will outlast the White Sox in the final game of this series in what, on paper, appears to be a stellar pitching matchup. Gibson has been the ace of the Orioles staff this season, leading the team in wins, innings pitches, quality starts, and strikeouts. Cease has not been as sharp as he's been in years past but still possesses swing-and-miss stuff. Unfortunately, Cease has not been very good in his last five starts with an ERA over eight in that time. He faces an Orioles lineup that specializes in working the count and running the bases. The Orioles will work Cease deep into counts and get to the White Sox bullpen early in this one. Prior to the trade deadline, the White Sox bullpen was a strength but the team has since parted with three key arms at the backend of the rotation. Baltimore will exploit this and pull away late in the game for the win.

Take the Orioles with the money line.
