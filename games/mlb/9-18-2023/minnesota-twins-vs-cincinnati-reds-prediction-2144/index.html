The  American League Central-leading Minnesota Twins (78-71) play game 1 of a three-game interleague series on the road Monday when paying a visit to the National League Central third-place Cincinnati Reds (78-72). Minnesota's magic number to clinch the AL Central remained at 7 after losing to the Chicago White Sox 7-6 on Saturday. Cincinnati defeated the New York Mets 3-2 on Saturday and has won five of the last six. Cincinnati is seven games behind first place in the National League Central and is currently tied with Arizona for the third and final NL wildcard spot. Minnesota will play without seven pitchers as well as position players Jorge Alcala, Nick Gordon, Byron Buxton, Jose Miranda, Joey Gallo and Michael A. Taylor.  Cincinnati will play without seven pitchers as well as position players Curt Casali, Matt McLain, Kevin Newman, Stewart Fairchild and Luke Maile.

Minnesota’s strength is on the mound

The Minnesota Twins are in the top 10 of four major pitching categories. Minnesota is first in Quality Starts with 71, while fourth in WHIP at 1.20 and in batting average allowed at .237. The Twins have the 7th best team ERA at 3.92. Pablo Lopez is leading Minnesota in victories with 10 and in strikeouts with 221. Minnesota will hand the ball to Joe Ryan on Monday. This season the right-hander is 10-9 an ERA of 4.20 and 176 strikeouts.

 

Minnesota is performing better at the plate but remains without a number of productive hitters. The Twins have scored the 12th most runs with 701, while 21st in team batting average at .242, 13th in on-base percentage at .323 and 10th in slugging percentage with .425. Max Kepler is leading Minnesota in home runs with 22 and Carlos Correa is the leader in RBI with 65. Players out injured for Minnesota have a combined 62 home runs and 142 RBI.

Cincinnati must improve on the mound

The Cincinnati Reds are battling for one of the three National League wild-card spots but if they hope to reach the postseason and have a deep run, they must improve their pitching. Cincinnati is 23rd or worse in four major pitching categories. The Reds are 23rd in WHIP (1.40), 24th in batting average allowed (.255), 24th in team ERA (4.71) and 25th in Quality Starts (41). Alexis Diaz is the leader in victories for Cincinnati with nine and Hunter Greene has a team-best 129 strikeouts. As of the publication of this article, Cincinnati has not yet released the name of its starting pitcher for Monday's opening game against Minnesota

At the plate, Cincinnati has scored the 10th most runs with 706. Cincinnati is 12th in on base percentage at .325, while 17th in both team batting average with .247 and slugging percentage with .412. Spencer Steer is leading Cincinnati in home runs with 22 and in RBIs with 81. Jonathan India is second in home runs with 16 and Jake Fraley is second in RBI with 64. Cincinnati has hit 246 doubles, 33 triples and 174 home runs in 150 games.
