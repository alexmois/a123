The New York Mets (69-80, 66-83 RL, 60-80-9 O/U) clash with the Miami Marlins (78-72, 70-80 RL, 70-76-4 O/U) in the first of a three-game series on Monday evening. The Mets will be sending Jose Butto to the mound. The Marlins will reply with Edward Cabrera. The Mets lead the season series 5-2.

Butto Starts, Mets Avoid Sweep

The Mets will have Jose Butto on the rubber. Butto posted his best outing of the season in his previous outing, allowing just one run in five innings against the Diamondbacks, and was awarded the victory. The rookie made a few starts in April and was recalled in August. The righty has excelled, allowing just three runs in his last two outings and now has a 3.46 ERA and a 1.46 WHIP accompanied by a 1-2 record in 26 innings on the year. This is his first career meeting against the Marlins.

New York has been solid recently. They won three of four against the Diamondbacks last week, scoring 28 runs in the series. Next, the squad dropped a home series to the Reds on the weekend, avoiding the sweep with an 8-4 win on Sunday. The Mets are 5-5 in their last ten games.

Cabrera Gets the Nod, Marlins Sweep Braves

Edward Cabrera will take the mound in the series opener. Cabrera exited after allowing two runs on one hit in 4.2 innings against the Brewers last time out and was charged with the loss. The righty has struggled with his command all season which has resulted in a subpar 4.52 ERA and a 1.44 WHIP accompanied by a 6-7 record in 85.2 innings. Cabrera has conceded four runs in 6.2 innings against the Mets this season and has a poor 6.65 ERA and a 1-2 record in 21.2 career innings. 

The young Marlins continue to battle for a wildcard spot. They rebounded from a series road loss against the Brewers with a dominating sweep of the Braves on the weekend. The bats piled on the runs against the Braves, accumulating 35 runs in three games. The Marlins are 6-4 in their last ten games and stand in the last wildcard spot.

Miami is averaging 4.15 runs on the year, ranking them 25th. The pitching is solid, reporting a strong 4.23 team ERA, placing them 16th.
