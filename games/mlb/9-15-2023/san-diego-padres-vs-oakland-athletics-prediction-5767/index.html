San Diego Padres (69-78) vs. Oakland Athletics (46-100)

The 2023 MLB season goes on Friday, September 15, with the San Diego Padres taking on the Oakland Athletics in the interleague showdown at Oakland Coliseum, and the first pitch is set for 9:40 PM ET. 

San Diego and Oakland open a three-game series. The Padres are listed as firm -195 money line favorites, while the Athletics are +178 home underdogs with a total of 8.5 runs. 

The Padres aim for their second straight W

The San Diego Padres are coming off Wednesday’s 6-1 road victory over the mighty Los Angeles Dodgers. The Padres took two out of three games from the Blue Crew, improving to 69-78 on the season. They were seven games behind the final wild card in the National League on Thursday morning. 

San Diego is now 7-5 in September. The Padres have slashed .259/.327/.473 with 21 doubles and 22 home runs across 406 at-bats so far this month while posting an underwhelming 4.80 ERA, 1.44 WHIP, and .248 batting average against. 

Seth Lugo will be on the bump Friday in Oakland, and the 33-year-old right-hander carries a 6-7 record with a 3.80 ERA, 1.23 WHIP, and 119/30 K/BB ratio in 23 starts (125.2 innings pitched) in 2023. Lugo has compiled a strong 2.54 ERA, 3.36 FIP, and .622 opposing OPS over his previous five outings. But he was awful last time out, yielding six earned runs on seven hits and three walks across 4.1 innings of work in a 7-5 defeat at the Houston Astros.

The A’s just stunned the Astros

The Oakland Athletics suffered a 6-2 defeat at the Houston Astros this past Wednesday and missed an opportunity to sweep a three-game road series against the reigning World Series champions. The A’s blanked the Astros 4-0 in Monday’s opener and took a 6-2 win in the middle game of the series. 

Oakland has gone 7-5 so far this month, posting a .242/.303/.451 slash line with 26 extra-base hits and 19 home runs in 401 at-bats. On the pitching side of things, the Athletics hold a 3.82 ERA, 1.42 WHIP, and .234 batting average in September. 

Sean Newcomb will toe the rubber Friday, and the 30-year-old left-hander is 1-0 with a 0.75 ERA, 0.75 WHIP, and 15/6 K/BB ratio in one start and five relief appearances (12 IP) this season. Newcomb got his first starting call of the season last Saturday. He pitched four scoreless innings in a no-decision against the Texas Rangers on the road, allowing just one hit and two free passes while punching out five.

The Padres are doing a great job against left-handed pitchers in September, tallying a 138 wRC+, .886 OPS, and .317 ISO across 70 plate appearances. They are 8-for-25 with a couple of doubles and home runs against Sean Newcomb. On the other side, the A’s are 2-for-5 with no extra-base hits or RBI versus Seth Lugo. They hold a 115 wRC+, .784 OPS, and .227 ISO against right-handed pitchers in September, but I’m expecting the Padres’ offense to make a difference. 

Over the last ten days, the Athletics bullpen has recorded a 4.17 ERA and 4.72 FIP through 41 innings of work. The Padres ‘pen has thrown 38 innings in that span while notching a 4.03 ERA and 3.86 FIP.
