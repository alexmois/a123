Los Angeles Dodgers (88-57) vs. Seattle Mariners (81-65)

The 2023 MLB betting action continues Friday, September 15, with the Los Angeles Dodgers facing off against the Seattle Mariners in the marquee interleague battle at T-Mobile Park in Seattle, Washington, and the first pitch is set for 10:10 PM ET.  

The Dodgers and Mariners open a three-game set in Seattle. At the moment, both teams sit at -105 moneyline odds, while the totals are listed at 8.0 runs.  

The Dodgers’ shaky form in September continues

The Los Angeles Dodgers fell to 88-57 on the season following a 6-1 defeat to the San Diego Padres. The Blue Crew dropped a three-game set against the Padres, and the Dodgers have only won five of their previous 13 games overall. They are still comfortably topping the NL West 13 games ahead of the Arizona Diamondbacks. 

During that 13-game stretch, the Dodgers have slugged 19 doubles and 19 home runs while slashing .261/.355/.435 in 448 at-bats. On the other side of the ball, they’ve posted a troublesome 4.90 ERA to go with a 1.17 WHIP and .238 batting average against. 

Bobby Miller is projected to take the mound Friday in Seattle. The 24-year-old rookie right-hander is 9-3 with a 3.98 ERA, 1.14 WHIP, and 92/28 K/BB ratio in 18 starts (101.2 innings pitched) in 2023. Miller hasn’t been at his best of late, allowing four or more earned runs in three of his previous four starts. Last Saturday, Miller pitched seven innings in a no-decision against the Washington Nationals on the road, surrendering five earned runs on six hits and a pair of walks.

The Mariners eye their third straight win

The Seattle Mariners moved to 81-65 on the season following Wednesday’s 3-2 home victory to the Los Angeles Angels. After a tough 8-5 defeat in 11 innings last Monday, the Mariners blanked the Halos 8-0 on Tuesday night to stop a four-game losing streak. Seattle was third in the crowded AL West on Thursday morning, trailing the leading Houston Astros by 1.5 games. 

The M’s are slashing .237/.301/.425 with 19 doubles and 21 home runs through their first 13 outings in September (447 at-bats). On the pitching side of things, the Mariners have compiled a 4.47 ERA, 1.38 WHIP, and a .262 batting average against. 

George Kirby will toe the slab Friday, and the 25-year-old right-hander is 10-9 with a 3.48 ERA, 1.05 WHIP, and 151/16 K/BB ratio in 27 starts (165.2 IP) this season. Kirby has struggled over his last four starts, giving up 15 runs (14 earned) on 27 hits and two walks across 21 innings of work. Last Thursday, Kirby tossed 6.1 frames of a four-run ball in a no-decision against the Tampa Bay Rays.

Rating:

Frankly, this game could easily go either way, and the stake is enormous for the home team. The Mariners have never seen Bobby Miller before, while just three guys from this LA team have faced George Kirby, combining for three hits in 13 at-bats. I’ve mentioned Kirby’s recent struggles, but he’s only allowed one run through his previous 21 innings of work at T-Mobile Park. 

I’m taking the Dodgers because of their offense. The Boys in Blue hold a 134 wRC+ against right-handed pitchers in September, slashing .288/.393/.461 in 269 plate appearances. On the other side, the Mariners have recorded a 109 wRC+ and .237/.307/.447 triple-slash against the righties so far this month. Furthermore, the Mariners’ bullpen has registered a 4.84 ERA and 6.08 FIP over the last couple of weeks, whereas the Dodgers have posted a 2.98 ERA and 3.47 FIP in that span.

Prediction: Los Angeles Dodgers (-105)

Anyway, the totals are another tricky wager in this matchup. Both teams own plenty of dangerous bats, and the Dodgers boast one of the most dangerous lineups in the majors. I lean toward the under but wouldn’t be surprised by a high-scoring affair.
