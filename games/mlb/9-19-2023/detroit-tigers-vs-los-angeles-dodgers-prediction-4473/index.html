The Los Angeles Dodgers play host to the Detroit Tigers on Tuesday night in a September showdown from Dodger Stadium. These two come into this one in very different positions when it comes to the standings, as the Dodgers have already clinched the division, on the back of a strong run of play lately, while the Tigers need a miraculous final push to catch the Twins, who sit just over eight games ahead of them. When these two squared of last season, which was the first meeting since 2017, the Dodgers took two of three at home, as they look make it a sweep in this week's rendition of the series.

Tigers Eyeing One Final Push

It has been a number of years since Detroit was even in the conversation of the playoffs come September, so regardless of what happens over the last few weeks, it's clear that this team has taken a step forward in 2023. The Tigers sit just under ten games behind the Twins in the AL Central and while it would take a wild finish in the division over the last few weeks to sneak into the playoffs, Detroit will want to continue to put their best foot forward. A 7-3 record over their last ten games at least has them playing strong baseball and catching a Los Angeles team who is coasting towards the playoffs, might be exactly what the doctor ordered. The 27-48 road record is a massive concern though, as it represents one of worst road marks for teams still in playoff consideration. After losing two of three in Los Angeles last season, it's clear that this could be a tough spot for this group.

According to MLB.com, the Tigers remain undecided on who they'll be giving the ball to in this one. A young cast of pitchers has left the staff wondering where to turn over the final few weeks, especially with the injuries they've been navigating as well. Whoever gets the ball is likely to have their hands full against one of baseball's best and most potent offenses, though a team ERA of 4.34 is an impressive mark. The bullpen could see extended innings in this one too and with a 4.42 ERA themselves, will hope inconsistency doesn't haunt them in this one.

The offense is where the Tigers will struggle the most on the road in this one. They only average 4.1 runs per game, which is one of the worst marks in baseball as a whole, let alone of teams that haven't been eliminated yet. Being without Riley Greene (elbow) has hurt of late, though the continued absence of Austin Meadows (personal) has proven to be tough to patch over. Spencer Torkleson (28 home runs, 83 RBI) has been a catalyst in the lineup but without much help, it hasn't been a surprise to see this group struggle. Javier Baez remains the x-factor, though his .219 average on the season gives reason for concern.

Dodgers Divisional Winners

After another first half of the season that had fans thinking that things might come down to the wire in the NL West, the Dodgers put on a masterclass in the second-half to clinch the divisional crown. They won't catch the Braves when it comes to the best record in the National League but they can continue to build on momentum. Even despite securing their spot in the postseason, this team has continued to turn heads of late, having gone 7-3 over their last ten games. Add to that a 48-26 home record, which is the best record in baseball, they'll be feeling confident in their chances to take down the Tigers at home and dominate this series for the second-straight year.

Much like their opponent in this one, the Dodgers are also undecided on who will get the ball, according to MLB.com. Injuries have made things difficult in September and while there is still plenty of talent in the rotation, getting healthy seems to be the key. Six starters and seven relievers are all on the injury report for various reasons, while three of the five anticipated starters for the year are unavailable. A 4.60 ERA for the starters shows the struggles they've dealt with but if they can get to their bullpen unharmed, they can lean on one of baseball's best groups.

Despite the pitching woes, this team hasn't slowed down mainly due to their offense. They average 5.8 runs per game and are hitting .253 as a team, along with plenty of pop throughout the lineup. They haven't seemed to miss a beat since Gavin Lux (knee) went down on the first weekend, while being without Jake Marisnick (hamstring) is a more recent concern. Mookie Betts (39 home runs, 103 RBI) and Freddie Freeman (.335 average, .413 OBP) continue to lead the way and with plenty of others alternating stepping up nightly, this offense will continue to flourish.
