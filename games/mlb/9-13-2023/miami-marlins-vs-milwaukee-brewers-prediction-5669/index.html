The Miami Marlins and Milwaukee Brewers will play Wednesday at American Family Field. First pitch is at 6:40 p.m. CT.

Miami (74-71 SU and 66-78 RL) is starting left-hander Braxton Garrett. The 26-year-old is 8-6 with a 3.82 ERA and a 1.16 WHIP in 28 appearances (27 starts). The Fins recently lost both starting pitcher Sandy Alcantara (forearm) and designated hitter Jorge Soler (oblique injury) for their playoff push and possibly longer.

Milwaukee (81-63 SU and 69-74 RL) is countering with right-hander Trevor Megill. The 29-year-old is 1-0 with a 3.38 ERA and a 1.23 WHIP in 26 relief appearances (29.1 IP). Left fielder Christian Yelich has missed the last three games with a back injury, leaving his status questionable for Wednesday.

Miami is down a few key players for its postseason run

Miami has won three consecutive series wins over the Phillies, Dodgers, and Nationals. Is it going to be enough to earn a Wild Card spot without Alcantara and Soler?

The Marlins average 4.08 runs (26th) and hit .258 (9th) with a .716 OPS (19th). They've hit 146 long balls (24th) and stolen 72 bases (26th) this season. The MIA pitching staff has posted a 4.25 ERA (16th) and a 1.29 WHIP (15th) with 47 quality starts (17th).

Garrett will take the hill for the visitors on Wednesday night in Milwaukee. The fourth-year pro is 5-2 with a 2.74 ERA and a 1.03 WHIP in 13 road starts this season. In his last outing, he only lasted 3.1 innings vs. LA, giving up one run on three hits and three walks. He was 3-2 with a 3.18 ERA and a 1.06 WHIP in six August appearances.

Milwaukee was hot in August

Milwaukee was victorious in two of its last three series, beating the Yankees and Phillies and losing to the Pirates. Can it hold onto its lead in the NL Central standings?

The Brewers score 4.50 runs per game (18th) and hit .238 (25th) with a .699 OPS (25th). They've launched 146 long balls (23rd) and stolen 114 bases (11th) this year. The Milwaukee pitching staff sports a 3.8 ERA (4th) and a 1.19 WHIP (3rd) with 63 quality starts (6th).

Megill will serve as the Brewers' opener on Wednesday. The third-year pro is 1-0 with a 3.60 ERA and a 1.53 WHIP in 13 appearances (15.0 IP) at home. He has a 2.08 ERA and a 0.92 WHIP this month (4.1 IP) and didn't surrender a run in 7.1 innings last month.

Best Bets for this Game

Full-Game Side Bet

Insiders Status:

Rating:

I'm placing a wager on the Marlins to win straight-up on Wednesday night in Milwaukee. Garrett has been dependable on the road this season (2.74 ERA and a 1.03 WHIP) and pitched well last month after a mediocre July. He's held eight straight opponents to three or fewer runs (43.2 IP). If Yelich is out again Wednesday, he'll have a better shot at navigating the Milwaukee lineup, too.

Megill is facing a Fins lineup minus Soler, but Miami can win without its star slugger with small ball. The Brewers' bullpen is legit, but Garrett's low road ERA gives Miami the advantage in the pitching matchup. Should he last only three or four innings, the Milwaukee bullpen will have its hands full. The Marlins are still in the thick of the NL Wild Card race and are riding the momentum of three consecutive series wins. I see significant value in a bet on Miami to win Wednesday and recommend betting four units on the MIA ML.

Prediction: Marlins ML -102

Full-Game Total Pick

Insiders Status:

Rating:

These teams could be missing key pieces in their lineup tomorrow — I'll bet on the under.

The Fins only scored 3.5 runs per game in August with Soler and couldn't muster up a run in game one of the series. They're slashing .245 BA/.294 OBP/.403 SLG/.697 OPS in the second half of the season, averaging less than four runs per game. With or without a great small-ball performance, this team isn't going to score a lot of runs tomorrow.

On Monday, the Brewers got a spark from Josh Donaldson, who was released by New York after hitting .142 with a .225 on-base percentage and .434 slugging percentage in 33 games. He isn't a reliable bet to stay hot by Wednesday, and without Yelich, this lineup lacks some pop. Marlins starter Garrett should be able to give his team five or six innings of quality pitching given his recent play, holding the Brew Crew in check.

I'm predicting a low-scoring Marlins win — bet on the under!
