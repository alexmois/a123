The Atlanta Braves (95-50, 76-69 RL, 80-63-2 O/U) collide with the Philadelphia Phillies (79-66, 64-81 RL, 65-69-11 O/U) in the last of a four-game divisional series on Wednesday evening. The Braves will give the nod to Spencer Strider. The Phillies will answer with Cris Sanchez. The Braves lead the season series 6-3.

Strider Eyes 17th Win

Spencer Strider will get the nod in this one. Strider was clobbered in his previous start, allowing six runs in just 2.2 innings against the Cardinals, and was hit with the loss. The young ace has struggled in two consecutive outings and is sporting a 3.83 ERA and a 1.09 WHIP complemented by a 16-5 record in 162 innings on the season. Strider has held the Phillies to three runs in 12 innings this season and has issued a dazzling 1.62 ERA and a 6-0 record in 33.1 career innings against the Phillies. Strider has 250 strikeouts. 

Atlanta has the division already won but these games have meaning as they strive to secure the best record in the NL and the MLB to ensure home-field if they make it to the NCLS and World Series. The Braves rebounded from a series loss to the Cardinals by winning two of three against the Pirates before this series. They are 5-5 in their last ten decisions.

Matt Olson continues to go deep, bashing seven homers along with a remarkable 15 RBIs on the month. The 29-year-old first baseman is having an incredible season. He leads the Major Leagues with 50 homers along with 127 RBIs and a 1.00 OPS. Olson has five homers with 12 RBIs and a 1.1 OPS against the Phillies. 

Atlanta is scoring an average of 5.84 runs which continues to lead MLB. The pitching is great, reporting a 3.92 team ERA, placing them sixth.

Sanchez On the Mound to Conclude the Series

Cristopher Sanchez will be on the mound on Wednesday evening. Sanchez was brilliant last time out, completing five scoreless innings against the Marlins, leading to a no-decision in a game the Phillies lost. The young lefty has been outstanding, conceding two or fewer runs in four of his last five outings, and has recorded a 3.26 ERA and a 1.04 WHIP accompanied by a 2-3 record in 80 innings on the year. Sanchez has allowed one run (zero earned) in two career innings against the Braves. 

The Phillies have lost two of their last three series but are in great shape, standing 1.5 games ahead of the Cubs for the 1st wildcard spot. Philly lost two of three to the Marlins this past weekend. They should be able to secure the 1st spot considering their last three series of the season are against the Pirates and Mets. The Phillies are 5-5 in their last ten bouts.

Nick Castellanos has knocked in four runs in his last four games. The veteran slugger has been slumping recently, posting a weak .474 OPS on the month, and now has 22 homers with 89 RBIs and a .758 OPS on the season. Castellanos has four RBIs and a .789 OPS against the Braves.

Philadelphia is consistently producing and is averaging 4.91 runs on the year, marking them ninth. The pitching is strong, carrying a 4.03 team ERA, placing them 13th.
