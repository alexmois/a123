The Los Angeles Angels face the Seattle Mariners with both teams on opposite ends of the standings to close out the season. The Angels have a 68-77 record and are in fourth place in the American League Central Division. The Mariners have a 79-65 record but with only three wins in their last 10 games, they have fallen out of wild card position in the American League. For the upcoming game, the Angels will turn to Chase Silseth as their starting pitcher while the Mariners will turn to Luis Castillo who has only allowed five runs in his last three starts.

Angels hope lineup helps out Silseth

The Angels are having a rough season and hope to finish off strong to eclipse the .500 mark by the end of the year. Chase Silseth has shown flashes with a 49.2 Ground Ball Percentage but has struggled on the mound, allowing 25 runs in 48.1 innings pitched. In addition, with a 10.2 Barrel Percentage, an 11.2 Walk Rate, and a 4.21 expected ERA, opponents are making good contact off the bat and driving in runs with ease against Silseth this year.

Silseth has struggled but the lineup has been a bright spot, scoring 4.68 runs per game. Shohei Ohtani is slashing .304/.412/.654 with 44 home runs and 325 total bases while Mickey Moniak is slashing .280/.307/.490 with 13 home runs and 149 total bases but the back half of the lineup has stepped up as well. Brandon Drury is slashing .259/.300/.480 with 21 home runs and 202 total bases while Logan O'Hoppe and Matt Thaiss have added 19 home runs and 154 total bases to add depth to the lineup.

Mariners hope Castillo and lineup step up

The Mariners are having a great season but hope to snap out of their slump to reach the postseason for the second year in a row. Luis Castillo will start the upcoming game and he has been one of the best pitchers in baseball, allowing only 68 runs in 175.1 innings pitched while tossing 191 strikeouts. In addition, with a .229 expected opponent Batting Average, a 6.2 Walk Rate, and a 3.73 expected ERA, opponents are making minimal contact and struggling to drive in runs against Castillo this season.

Castillo has been remarkable but the lineup has also stepped up, scoring 4.77 runs per game. Julio Rodriguez is slashing .287/.342/.504 with 30 home runs and 295 total bases while J.P. Crawford is slashing .263/.380/.423 with 15 home runs and 196 total bases but the rest of the lineup has stepped up as well. Cal Raleigh is slashing .237/.315/.479 with 28 home runs and 212 total bases while Teoscar Hernandez and Eugenio Suarez have added 44 home runs and 460 total bases to round out the lineup, making the Mariners tough to stop at the plate.
