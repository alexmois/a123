The St. Louis Cardinals and Baltimore Orioles will take the field on Wednesday at Oriole Park at Camden Yards. First pitch is at 6:35 p.m. ET.

St. Louis (63-81 SU and 67-77 RL) is starting left-hander Drew Rom. The 23-year-old is 0-2 with a 7.79 ERA and a 2.02 WHIP in four starts this season.

Baltimore (91-52 SU and 89-54 RL) is countering with right-hander Kyle Gibson. The 35-year-old is 14-8 with a 5.12 ERA and a 1.34 WHIP in his 29 outings.

The Orioles are -165 moneyline favorites and the game total is 10.5.

The Cardinals won't go out without a battle

St. Louis is coming off consecutive series wins over Cincinnati and Atlanta. It's not making a playoff push, but it can still be a competitive team a few nights a week.

The Cardinals average 4.58 runs (17th) and hit .253 (13th) with a .753 OPS (9th). They've launched 196 home runs (9th) and stolen 91 bases (21st) in 2023. The STL pitching staff has compiled a 4.83 ERA (25th) and a 1.46 WHIP (28th) with 44 quality starts (22nd).

Rom is expected to take the hill for the Redbirds on Wednesday. In his last start, he gave up four runs and seven hits, including two homers, in 3.2 innings vs. the Reds. The rookie southpaw sports an 11:10 K:BB ratio in four outings against Philadelphia, Pittsburgh (twice), and Cincy.

B-More is the Beast of the East

Baltimore has won seven straight series dating back to mid-August. It has its work cut out if it's going to hold its lead in the AL East, however, as Tampa Bay is still right on its heels.

The Orioles score 5.17 runs per game (6th) and hit .259 (6th) with a .756 OPS (8th). They've launched 170 long balls (15th) and stolen 99 bases (17th) this season. The Baltimore pitching staff has compiled a 4.05 ERA (14th) and a 1.27 WHIP (12th) with 61 quality starts (9th).

Gibson will start for the home team on Wednesday night at Camden Yards. He is 6-4 with a 5.69 ERA and a 1.44 WHIP in 12 home starts this season. In his last outing, he held the LA Angels to three runs and six hits in six innings. Gibson was 3-2 with a 7.89 ERA and a 1.48 WHIP in five starts last month.

Best Bets for this Game

Full-Game Side Bet

Insiders Status:

Rating:

I don't see value in a bet on St. Louis to keep the game close or in a wager on Baltimore to win by multiple runs, so my best bet on the side is the Orioles ML at a reasonable -165.

The O's have a more experienced pitcher on the mound, and the offense is capable of breaking out offensively every game, but there isn't enough of an advantage in either department to warrant a bet on a cover. Gibson has an ERA north of five at home, and the Cardinals slash a respectable .255 BA/.330 OBP/.430 SLG/.759 OPS vs. right-handed pitchers this season. Gibson may have won three of his last four starts, but he's also pitched a few duds recently, including a seven-run shelling vs. the White Sox on August 30.

Rom is not experienced enough to confidently bet on against the first-place Orioles, who slash .265 BA/.338 OBP/.440 SLG/.778 OPS vs. lefty pitchers and hit consistently well at home. He's held two of his four opponents to just five combined runs, but he is prone to the long ball (four HRs) and surrendered 12 runs in his other two outings.

The Cardinals may play well enough to keep the game close, but I wouldn't bet on it. If you need a winner, wager three units on the Baltimore ML.

Prediction: Orioles ML

Full-Game Total Pick

Insiders Status:

Rating:

My most confident bet in this game is that the total will exceed 10.5 runs, as it did in Monday's game, an 11-5 Orioles victory.

The over is 75-57-11 in Baltimore games this season, the second-best over record in MLB. With a rookie starter on the mound for St. Louis, the Orioles will look to tee off. They're coming off their most productive month at the plate (.278 BA/.332 OBP/.448 SLG/.78ust) and have carried that hot hitting streak into September (.311 BA/.379 OBP/.507 SLG/.886 OPS), scoring 7.1 OPS in Aug6 runs per game. Rom has walked ten batters and given up 25 hits in 17.1 innings, plus he gave up two home runs in his last outing. That doesn't bode well for the under's odds on Wednesday.

As for the St. Louis, I'm confident it can score against Gibson. He has surrendered 26 runs on 42 hits, including eight homers, in his last five starts. The vet hasn't been a good bet at home this year, either. The Cardinals have scored 59 runs in their last ten games — they'll take advantage of their opportunities if he puts too many runners on base.

Prediction: Over 10.5
