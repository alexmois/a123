The Washington Nationals (65-80, 79-66 RL, 69-70-5 O/U) and the Pittsburgh Pirates (67-78, 73-72 RL, 78-62-4 O/U) resume a four-game series on Wednesday night. The Nationals will have rookie Jackson Rutledge on the mound. The Pirates have yet to name a pitcher. The Pirates lead the season series 3-2.

Rutledge Makes Debut

The Nationals will have Jackson Rutledge make his MLB debut in this one. Rutledge was selected in the first round in the 2019 draft.  The rookie was solid in the minor leagues this year, posting a 3.71 ERA and a 1.27 WHIP accompanied by an 8-4 record in 119 innings. The right-hander has recorded a 4.53 ERA and a 1.32 WHIP in four seasons in the minor leagues. He is the Nationals' #13 prospect. 

The Nationals are in the middle of a road trek. They actually have more road wins than home wins this season. The young squad has won two of their last three road series. It's been a rough go in recent games. They dropped two of three home bouts to the Dodgers this past weekend and rebounded with a 6-2 win on Monday. The Nationals are 3-7 in their last ten games.

Joey Meneses is slumping, hitting .138 with a poor .331 OPS in September. The 31-year-old DH is having a productive season, knocking in 78 runs along with a decent .724 OPS. Meneses is hitting a dazzling .412 with two RBIs and a solid .824 OPS against the Pirates this season.

The Nationals have cooled at the plate and are scoring an average of 4.33 runs, ranking them 21st. The pitching is weak, reporting a 4.97 team ERA, pegging them 27th.

Pirates Competitive at PNC Park

The Pirates have yet to name a starter as of Tuesday evening. They just added Quinn Priester to the taxi squad and he could be the starter. Priester struggled with the Pirates in July and early August, conceding 29 runs, resulting in an abysmal 9.10 ERA and a 1.92 WHIP along with a 2-3 record in 28.2 innings. The rookie posted a 4.00 ERA and a 1.33 WHIP in 108 innings at Triple-A this season and is one of the Pirates' top prospects.

Pittsburgh won their previous home series against the Brewers and is quite competitive at PNC Park where they stand only four games below the .500 mark with a 34-38 record entering Tuesday. The Pirates lost a road series to the Braves on the weekend but have been competitive, going 5-5 in their last ten decisions.

Bryan Reynolds has knocked in six runs with a strong .824 OPS on the month. The 28-year-old is the Pirates' best player. He has bashed 20 homers with 71 RBIs and a .796 OPS on the year. Reynolds is a stellar 6 for 14 with four RBIs against the Nationals. Andrew McCutchen remains on the IL.

Pittsburgh is averaging 4.19 runs per game, marking them 24th. The pitching is subpar, recording a 4.52 team ERA, positioning them 20th.
