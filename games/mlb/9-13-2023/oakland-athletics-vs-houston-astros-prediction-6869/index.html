When the bottom of the AL West, the Oakland Athletics (46-99) first ventured into Texas this week, most expected them to get swept. Then they beat the first-place Houston Astros (82-64) in the first two games to throw a wrench in their rivals' plans. On paper, most Oakland games have been lopsided this year. Lately, the Athletics are making teams fight a bit more. Wednesday night will be the final fight between the Astros and Athletics in 2023. Minute Maid Park will host the game at 7:10 p.m. EDT. Both clubs want to win, Houston needs to, but only one can. Let's see who it will be.

 

 

Oakland No Longer Last

Oakland spent most of this season knowing that the playoffs weren't going to happen. Still, that hasn't stopped them from taking a game off four AL contenders lately. The Astros joined the Mariners, Blue Jays, and Rangers as victims of a (relative) Athletics surge late in the year. A 7-4 start to September has been possible through an A's offense that put up 54 runs during that stretch. For an offense that's dead last in runs per game (under 4.00 for the entire year), that's a nice run. Oakland is also one of the four worst MLB teams in batting average, OBP, slugging percentage, hard-hit rate, and strikeout rate. Base stealing, led by Esteury Ruiz's AL-best 58 swipes, is the only area in which the A's excel offensively. Of course, that alone isn't enough most of the time.

 

Things on the mound have been just as ugly this year, although, September has been Oakland's best pitching month through the first 10 games. An MLB-worst bullpen (by WAR) will be backing up Paul Blackburn on Wednesday. Blackburn faced Houston in July, holding them to one run over 5.1 innings in a win. Generally, he has been pitching well lately too, with a 2.56 ERA in his past seven starts. Oakland is 5-2 in those outings. Will he take down the defending champions again?

Astros Atop AL West

It's taken help from their rivals, but the Houston Astros are in front as they seek a sixth division title over the past seven seasons. A 1.0-game lead over the Rangers and a 1.5-game cushion ahead of the Mariners endured Monday's loss. Now, they'll try to gain ground back with rookie Hunter Brown on the mound. Brown has taken on the A's thrice this season, holding them to six runs (five earned) across 19.0 innings pitched. All three games ended with Houston winning. Brown has struggled recently, allowing 19 runs (17 earned) in his past 16.0 innings pitched. A familiar foe may help him bounce back on Wednesday. 

If not, the Astros can at least bank on an offense that is fifth in runs per game. Since August 1, only the Atlanta Braves have scored more per contest. The Astros are top-10 in strikeout rate, home runs, batting average, OBP, and slugging percentage. Offensively, the team is finally healthy too. Even after getting blanked on Monday, this team can best be described as red-hot at the plate. Oakland's pitching has rarely given them problems anyway.

The Athletics have been tough lately, and this game should provide another example of that. Paul Blackburn has been mowing opponents down on the mound recently.
