The Minnesota Twins (69-65) will be trying to increase their lead in the AL Central standings when they face the Texas Rangers (75-58) in the second game of their three-game series on Saturday night.

Dallas Keuchel, 1-1, 3.50 ERA, will get the start for the Twins. The Rangers will counter with Jordan Montgomery, 8-10, 3.19 ERA.

Minnesota and Texas split their last 10 meetings.

**This preview was written before Friday’s game was played**

Twins Going For Fifth Win In Eight Games

The Twins played well over the past week and they’ve won four of their last seven games. They currently have a five-game lead over Cleveland in the AL Central standings and will try to increase their division lead with a win over the Rangers, which will give them their fifth win in their last eight games.

Minnesota is averaging 4.50 runs per game. Their .239 batting average is 22nd in the league. Their .319 on base percentage is 17th, while their .421 slugging percentage is 11th.

Carlos Correa leads the Twins with a .222 batting average and 57 RBI, while Max Kepler leads the team with 21 home runs.

Minnesota’s pitching has been good, with the team giving up 4.16 runs per game. Opponents have a .238 batting average against the Twins, which is fifth in the league. Their 3.94 ERA is 10th, while their 1.21 WHIP is third.

In his last start, Keuchel gave up five hits and no runs in five innings, leading to a 7-6 win over Texas. They will need a similar effort from him if they want to get the win.

Rangers Going For Fourth Win In Six Games

The Rangers are playing well at the moment and they’ve won three of their last five games. They are one-game behind Houston ad Seattle in the AL West standings and will try to close the gap with a win on Saturday, which will give them their fourth win in their last six games.

Texas is averaging 5.55 runs per game. Their .268 batting average is second in the league. Their .338 on base percentage is third, as is their .454 slugging percentage.

Nathaniel Lowe leads the Rangers with a .282 batting average, while Adolis Garcia leads the team with 32 home runs and 97 RBI.

Texas’ pitching has been good, with the team giving up 4.25 runs per game. Opponents have a .241 batting average against the Rangers, which is 10th in the league. Their 4.09 ERA is 14th, while their 1.23 WHIP is seventh.

In his last start, Montgomery gave up four hits and three runs in 5.1 innings, leading to a 7-6 loss to the Twins. They will need a better performance from him if they want to win this game.
