The San Francisco Giants face the San Diego Padres with both teams heading in opposite directions to close out the season. The Giants have a 70-64 record and hold on to the final wild card spot in the National League. The Padres have a 62-73 record and with a 10-18 record in August, they have fallen into fourth place in the National League West Division and are all but out of the playoff picture. For the upcoming game, the Giants will turn to Kyle Harrison as their starting pitcher who has only allowed two runs in his two starts this season while the Padres turn to Blake Snell who has only allowed five runs in his last three starts.

Giants hope Harrison builds off hot start

The Giants are having a great season and hope to keep their wild card position in the final month of the season. Kyle Harrison is one of their top prospects and has impressed in his two starts this season, allowing only two runs in 9.2 innings pitched while tossing 16 strikeouts. Additionally, with a .200 expected opponent Batting Average, a 40.0 Strikeout Percentage, and a 2.97 expected ERA, opponents have made minimal contact against Harrison and are struggling to drive in runs as a result.

Harrison has looked promising but the lineup has struggled, scoring only 4.33 runs per game. Wilmer Flores is slashing .291/.352/.528 with 19 home runs and 172 total bases while Joc Pederson is slashing .246/.359/.428 with 12 home runs and 122 total bases but the rest of the lineup has struggled. Only five active batters have an OPS+ over 100 and opposing pitchers can limit the top-heavy lineup as a result.

Padres hope Snell helps out lineup

The Padres are having a season to forget and have been one of baseball's biggest disappointments, entering the season with championship aspirations but they might not finish with an above .500 record. Blake Snell has been one of the bright spots as he's having a Cy Young-caliber season, allowing only 45 runs in 149 innings pitched while striking out 193 batters. Moreover, with a .213 expected opponent Batting Average, a 31.1 Strikeout Percentage, and a 3.96 expected ERA, opponents are making minimal contact against Snell and struggling to drive in runs as a result.

Snell has been great on the mound but the lineup has let the Padres down this season, scoring only 4.47 runs per game with only 4.28 runs per game in August. Juan Soto is slashing .261/.403/.480 with 25 home runs and 226 total bases while Ha-Seong Kim is slashing .277/.369/.436 with 17 home runs and 195 total bases but the rest of the lineup has struggled. Only five batters have an OPS+ over 100 and opposing pitchers can limit the lineup as a result.
