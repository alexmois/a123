Baltimore Orioles Looking to Build AL East Lead

Baltimore had won six of their previous seven games but couldn’t hold the White Sox in check in the finale of their set Wednesday afternoon. The Orioles entered Friday 83-50 on the year and held a 1.5-game lead over the Rays for the top spot in the AL East race. On Wednesday, Baltimore got two hits from Jorge Mateo, Adam Frazier (RBI) and Anthony Santander (two runs, two RBI) though they tallied just one run over the final eight innings after scoring four times in the first. Santander hit his 26th homer of the season in a losing effort. Kyle Gibson (13-8) took the loss on the mound as he threw 4.1 innings, allowing seven runs on nine hits with no walks and two strikeouts.

Kyle Bradish is on the mound as he makes his 25th start of the season for the Orioles in this contest. He comes in 9-6 with a 3.03 ERA, a 1.107 WHIP, 36 walks and 130 strikeouts over 133.2 innings of work on the season. Bradish earned the win in his last start, which came at home against the Rockies last Saturday. He threw six innings, allowing two runs on six hits with one walk and eight strikeouts in an eventual 5-4 Baltimore victory. Over his last three starts, Bradish is 2-0 with a 2.00 ERA, a 0.89 WHIP, three walks and 23 strikeouts over 18 innings of work. Bradish, in his 48th career major league start, pitches against the Diamondbacks for the first time here. As a result, this marks his first career outing at Chase Field as well.

Arizona Diamondbacks Fading in NL Playoff Picture

Arizona lost for the fourth time in five games as they were swept by the Dodgers on the road, getting outscored 23-5 in the process, after getting blanked Wednesday night. The Diamondbacks entered Friday 69-65 on the season and stood third in the NL West, 14.5 games behind the Dodgers for the division lead. They stood one game behind the Giants for the final wild card spot in the National League. On Wednesday, Arizona got two hits each from Buddy Kennedy and Lourdes Gurriel Jr. though that was nowhere near enough in the contest. The Diamondbacks finished the game zero for seven with runners in scoring position in the game. Brandon Pfaadt (1-7) took the loss on the mound as he allowed six runs (five earned) on eight hits with two walks and four strikeouts over four innings of work.

Slade Cecconi is on the bump for the Diamondbacks as he takes the ball for his sixth major league appearance and fourth start of the season here. He has no record, a 2.57 ERA, a 0.905 WHIP, four walks and 14 strikeouts over 21 innings of work this season. Cecconi didn’t factor in the decision in his last start, which came Sunday at home against the Reds. He threw 5.2 innings, allowing one run on three hits with no walks and five strikeouts in a game the Diamondbacks went on to win 5-2. In his last three appearances, two starts, Cecconi has no record with a 2.45 ERA, a 0.89 WHIP, three walks and 11 strikeouts over 14.2 innings of work. Cecconi, in his sixth career appearance and third major league start, pitches against the Orioles for the first time. He makes his fourth career appearance and second start at Chase Field here. Cecconi has no record, a 1.46 ERA, a 0.568 WHIP, no walks and eight strikeouts over 12.1 innings of work in those outings. Prior to his recall, Cecconi was 4-8 with a 6.38 ERA, a 1.379 WHIP, 29 walks and 104 strikeouts over 103 innings in 20 starts with Triple-A Reno of the Pacific Coast League.
