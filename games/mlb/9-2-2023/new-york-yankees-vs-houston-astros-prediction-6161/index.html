As Major League Baseball enters its final month of the regular season, the American League East last place New York Yankees (65-69, 5th AL East) pay a visit to the Lone Star State to play the second game of a three-game series against the American League West co-leading Houston Astros (77-58, T-1st AL West). New York was defeated by the Detroit Tigers 4-3 on Thursday snapping a three-game winning streak. New York is 10 ½ games behind the third and final AL wildcard spot. Houston was off on Thursday and the Astros completed a three-game sweep of the Boston Red Sox with a 7-4 victory on Wednesday, to remain tied for first with Seattle in the AL West. New York will play without eight pitchers as well as position players Billy McKinney, Anthony Rizzo and Jose Trevino. Houston will play without three pitchers as well as third baseman Grae Kessinger.

Yankees production, or lack thereof, at the plate has been its weak link

New York has the 16th-best slugging percentage in baseball at .402, but falls to 23rd in runs scored with 565, 26th in on-base percentage at .303 and is 29th or next to last in team batting average at .228. Aaron Judge has hit a team-best 29 home runs, while Gleyber Torres is tied with Anthony Volpe for the lead in RBI with 57 and Judge is a close third with 56. New York has hit 169 doubles, 13 triples and 190 home runs in 134 games.

 

New York's pitching staff has held its own this season with the 12th-best team ERA 3.99, the eighth-best WHIP at 1.24 and is second-best in batting average allowed at .232. The starting rotation has struggled, posting just 39 Quality Starts in 134 games which is 24th. Gerrit Cole is leading New York in victories with 12 and in strikeouts with 188. New York will hand the ball to Luis Severino on Saturday. This season the right-hander is 4-9 with an ERA of 6.64 and 71 strikeouts.

Houston’s bats are hard to keep quiet

Houston has climbed its way to the top of the American League West thanks to excellent production at the plate and a solid pitching staff. Houston is 8th or better in four major hitting categories as the Astros are 8th in both team batting average (.258) and slugging percentage (.428), while sixth best in on base percentage (.330) and fifth best in runs scored (683). Kyle Tucker has 26 home runs and 97 RBI to lead Houston in both while Alex Bregman has hit the third-most home runs with 22 and knocked in the second-most runs with 90. Yordan Alvarez has 23 home runs.

Houston's pitching staff has the 7th-best team ERA at 3.92 but drops off to 18th in WHIP at 1.29. The starting rotation has 60 Quality Starts which is tied for fourth and the pitching staff is holding opponents to an average of .245 at the plate which is 16th. Framber Valdez is leading Houston in victories with 10 and in strikeouts with 164. Houston will hand the ball to Hunter Brown on Saturday. This season the right-hander is 10-9 with an ERA of 4.47 and 152 strikeouts.

Before losing to Detroit by one run on Thursday, New York had been victorious in each of the first three games of its four-game series with the Tigers. In that four-game series with Detroit, the Yankees surrendered just nine runs for an average of only 2.25 runs per game. New York starting pitcher Luis Severino has struggled this season but over the last two games has pitched 13 ⅔ innings of shutout baseball while allowing just six hits, with 10 strikeouts and a pair of walks. Over the last two starts, Houston’s Hunter Brown has surrendered eight runs in 7 ⅔ innings including giving up six runs to Seattle on August 20 in just 2 ⅔ innings of work. Brown has alternated between solid starts and poor starts in each of the last four, allowing a total of 11 runs in his two poor outings and if that trend continues he is coming off a strong start and therefore will be below average on Saturday.
