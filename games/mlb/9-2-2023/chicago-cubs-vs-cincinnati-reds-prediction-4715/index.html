The Chicago Cubs (72-63, 69-65 RL, 69-61-4 O/U) and the Cincinnati Reds (70-67, 84-52 RL, 66-70 O/U) continue a four-game NL Central series on Saturday evening. The Cubs will have Javier Assad on the rubber. The Reds are going with  Andrew Abbott. The Reds lead the season series 6-5.

Assad Continues Dominance, Eyes Fourth Win

Javier Assad is the Cubs starting pitcher on Saturday evening. Assad continued his steady performance last time out, limiting the Pirates to only one run on three hits in seven innings, and was awarded for his excellence with a 10-1 victory. The young right-hander has been dominant, recording a 2.48 ERA in five outings in August, and is sporting a 2.96 ERA and a 1.20 WHIP accompanied by a 3-2 record in 79 innings on the year. Assad has silenced the Reds, conceding two runs in 7.1 innings this season, and has allowed just six runs with an 0-1 record in 18 innings.

Chicago is back on the road after winning two of three home meetings against the Brewers. They held a hot Brewers offense to only eight runs in the series. Chicago has climbed into the 2nd wildcard spot with a two-game cushion. The division is still in reach considering they play the Brewers late in the season. The Cubs are 7-3 in their last ten games.

Jeimer Candelario has been outstanding since he was acquired. The veteran third baseman has collected seven RBIs in his last ten games and posted an .809 OPS in August. He now has 65 RBIs accompanied by an .817 OPS on the year. He has two RBIs and a 1.2 OPS against the Reds this season.

Chicago is averaging 5.02 runs on the year, landing them sixth. The pitching has been solid and now has a 4.11 team ERA, placing them 15th.

Abbott Starts on Saturday

Andrew Abbott will be on the mound in this one. Abbott wasn’t at his best in his latest effort, conceding three runs on five hits in only 3.1 innings against the Giants, and was charged with the loss. The rookie lefty has been shaky this month but is having a promising season, posting a 3.35 ERA and a 1.22 WHIP complemented by an 8-4 record in 88.2 innings. He squandered four runs in 3.1 innings in his only meeting against the Cubs in July.

The Reds have dropped three out of their last four series after a tough road trek and have plummeted two games out of a wildcard spot, making this a huge series. The bats have cooled down which is not ideal considering the pitching has been a problem at times. The Reds are 4-6 in their ten decisions.

Spencer Steer has been a tough out against the Cubs, recording seven RBIs and a dazzling 1.1 OPS. The 25- year-old is having a breakout season, delivering 18 homers with 72 RBIs and a solid .808 OPS. Steer has just two RBIs in his last eight games. The Reds claimed Henter Renfroe and Harrison Bader off waivers which should help a young squad in the final month.

The Reds are posting an average of 4.75 runs, good for 12th. The pitching is struggling and has produced a 4.74 team ERA, pegging them 24th.
