The Pittsburgh Pirates continue their three-game weekend series with the Cardinals in St. Louis on Saturday night. The two teams are in a battle for the fourth and fifth spots in the NL Central Division with just under a month to go in the regular season. The pitching matchup on Saturday will feature Pirates' lefty Bailey Falter (1-8, 5.04) taking on Cardinals' lefty Drew Rom (0-2, 8.00). The first pitch is scheduled for 7:10 PM EST.

Coming in Hot

The Pittsburgh Pirates arrive in St. Louis having won four of their last six games. This was highlighted with a three-game sweep of the Royals in Kansas City. The Pirates' pitching took center stage in the series, allowing just four runs total in the three games. The three-game sweep helped the Pirates hold a three-game lead over the Cardinals at the bottom of the NL Central Division standings heading into the series opener. On Friday night, the Pirates won their fourth straight game with a 4-2 win in 10-innings. Five Pirates' pitchers held the Cardinals to six hits and two runs in the win.

The Pirates will likely turn to lefty Bailey Falter in game two of the series with the Cardinals. Falter last worked on Sunday in a loss to the Cubs. On the season, Falter is 1-8 in 13 appearances with both the Cardinals and Pirates including 11 starts. Since joining the Pirates, Falter is 1-1 in five appearances including four starts. He's given up 28 hits in 24 innings pitched with an ERA of 4.88. Falter's K/9 rate is 7.9 since joining the Pirates and his WHIP is 1.46.

Key Injuries: RF Henry Davis is out.

Fall from Grace

At this time last year, the Cardinals were streaking toward an NL Central Division title after a dominant second half of the season. This season, the Cardinals are trying their best to chase down the Pittsburgh Pirates and avoid going from first to worst in the division this season. St. Louis comes into this series on a high note after winning back-to-back walk-offs against the Padres to end a four-game losing streak. In game one against the Pirates on Friday night, the Cardinals brief two-game win streak came to an end in a 4-2 loss to the Pirates in 10-innings.

The Cardinals will hand the ball to lefty Drew Rom in game two of the series. Rom was picked up from the Orioles as part of the deal sending pitcher Jack Flaherty to the Orioles. Rom has made two starts for the Cardinals since coming over and is 0-2 in those starts. He has allowed 13 hits in nine innings pitched with an ERA of 8.00. Rom's K/9 rate is 10.0 while his WHIP is 2.00 in the two games.

Key Injuries: CF Lars Nootbar is out.
