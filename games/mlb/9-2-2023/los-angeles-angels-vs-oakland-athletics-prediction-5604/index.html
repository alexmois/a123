The Los Angeles Angels and Oakland Athletics will play on Saturday at Oakland Coliseum. First pitch is scheduled for 1:07 p.m. PT.

Los Angeles (64-70 SU and 62-72 RL) is starting right-hander Griffin Canning. The 27-year-old is 7-4 with a 4.38 ERA and a 1.23 WHIP in 19 outings (17 starts) this season.

Oakland (39-95 SU and 64-70 RL) is countering with righty Paul Blackburn. The 29-year-old is 3-4 with a 3.93 ERA and a 1.46 WHIP in 16 outings (15 starts).

The Angels are -130 moneyline favorites and the game total is 8.5 runs.

It has been a rollercoaster season in Anaheim

The Angels are in a freefall, dropping seven of their last ten games before this weekend's series with the A's. They acquired a few players at the trade deadline to make a postseason push, but it hasn't worked out to plan.

Los Angeles scores 4.76 runs per game (11th) and hits .250 (15th) with a .758 OPS (8th). It's launched 195 homers (3rd) and stolen 63 bases (27th) in 2023. The LA pitching staff has compiled a 4.68 ERA (23rd) and a 1.41 WHIP (24th) with 41 quality starts (21st).

Canning will take the hill for the visitors on Saturday afternoon in Oakland. In his last start, the fourth-year pro gave up just one run with nine strikeouts in seven innings against the New York Mets. It was his only start in August. Canning faced the Athletics on April 25, holding them to three runs with seven Ks in a five-inning victory.

Oakland isn't going down without a fight

Oakland has played better lately, winning five of its last ten overall. That streak included a series win over Kansas City and a series split with the Chicago White Sox. Can it keep the momentum going against the Angels?

The A's average 3.60 runs (30th) and hit .223 (30th) with a .666 OPS (30th). They've launched 137 home runs (24th) and stolen 123 bases (5th) this year. The Oakland pitching staff has posted a 5.71 ERA (30th) and a 1.54 WHIP (30th) with 25 quality starts (30th).

Blackburn will toe the rubber for the home team on Saturday. In his last start, the seventh-year pro surrendered four runs (two earned) and eight hits in a six-inning loss to the White Sox. He sports a 3.77 ERA and a 1.35 WHIP in eight home starts. Blackburn has not faced the Angels in 2023, but he is 2-0 with a 2.73 ERA in four previous starts against LA.

Best Bets for this Game

Full-Game Side Bet

Insiders Status:

Rating:

I'm betting on the Athletics ML on Saturday at +120 odds. The A's have been playing better baseball lately, and Blackburn is coming off an impressive month, while the Angels have fallen from grace.

In his five August outings, Blackburn posted a 2.35 ERA and a 1.27 WHIP, tallying 30 strikeouts in 30.1 innings. I predict he will continue to impress on Saturday at home, where he has performed his best in 2023. LA scored a lot of runs in their last two games against Philadelphia, but I don't expect that to continue in this series. Even with those high-scoring showings, the Angels hit just .222 BA/.279 OBP/.378 SLG/.657 OPS in August and averaged 3.8 runs per game. With another bat, Hunter Renfroe, no longer in the lineup, LA's offense is a good bet to fall short on the road in Oakland.

Canning has been a solid, but not spectacular arm for LA this season. He is coming off his longest start of the season (seven innings) but typically only lasts four or five innings. If he isn't on his A-game, one of those innings will be rocky for the visitors, who will then turn to a below-average bullpen (4.68 ERA and a 1.48 WHIP with 67 HR surrendered) to close out the game.

I'm betting on Blackburn to outlast and outpitch Canning, allowing the Oakland offense to squeak out a narrow victory.

Prediction: Athletics ML

Full-Game Total Pick

Insiders Status:

Rating:

The under is my bet on the total in Saturday's Los Angeles-Oakland game.

The A's can win a low-scoring game with Blackburn and will likely need to in order to come out on top. They average just 3.2 runs at Oakland Coliseum and 2.9 runs in day games, plus they slash .219/.299/.362/.662 vs. right-handed pitchers. Canning likely won't blank the Athletics on Saturday, but he's unlikely to give up four or more runs, either.

The Angels are dropping like flies, with Mike Trout sidelined, Renfroe headed to Cleveland, Shohei Ohtani banged up, and CJ Cron out of the lineup. Anthony Rendon, Taylor Ward, Gio Urshela, Max Stassi, and Jo Adell are all on the 60-day IL, too. Blackburn had some success last month and will look to build on it starting tomorrow. With its season unofficially over, I don't envision LA celebrating much in Oakland this weekend. It wouldn't shock me if the Angels averaged less than four runs per game this month, too!

Prediction: Under 8.5
