The Tampa Bay Rays and Cleveland Guardians will play on Saturday at Progressive Field. First pitch is at 6:10 p.m. ET.

Tampa Bay (82-52 SU and 69-65 RL) is starting right-hander Zack Littell. The 27-year-old is 3-4 with a 4.45 ERA and a 1.21 WHIP in 22 outings (nine starts) this season.

Cleveland (64-70 SU and 68-66 RL) is countering with left-hander Logan Allen. The 24-year-old is 6-7 with a 3.61 ERA and a 1.35 WHIP in his 20 starts.

The Rays are -118 moneyline favorites and the game total is 8.5 runs.

Is Tampa Bay setting up for a stellar finish to a promising season?

Tampa Bay won eight of its last ten overall prior to its weekend set with Cleveland, including series over the Rockies, Yankees, and Marlins. It's still chasing down Baltimore in the AL East standings but will it have to settle for a Wild Card spot?

The Rays score 5.36 runs per game (4th) and hit .260 (5th) with a .779 OPS (4th). They've launched 194 home runs (4th) and stolen 141 bases (2nd) in 2023. The TB pitching staff has compiled a 3.83 ERA (4th) and a 1.18 WHIP (2nd) with 44 quality starts (17th).

Will Cleveland have a September to remember?

Cleveland is hosting Tampa Bay on the heels of consecutive series victories over Toronto and Minnesota, pulling within a few games of the Twins in the division standings. What will it take for Cleveland to overtake Minnesota for the AL Central crown?

The Guardians average 4.07 runs (26th) and hit .248 (16th) with a .691 OPS (26th). They've hit 103 long balls (30th) and stolen 112 bases (7th) this season. The Cleveland pitching staff has compiled a 3.85 ERA (5th) and a 1.28 WHIP (16th) with 48 quality starts (13th).

He was much better in his other four August starts (five runs in 23 innings). Saturday's game will be Allen's first against the Rays in 2023.

Best Bets for this Game

Full-Game Side Bet

Insiders Status:

Rating:

I'm wagering on the Guardians to win straight-up on Saturday.

Cleveland has a winning record (12-11 SU) as moneyline underdogs at home in 2023, one of only six teams in MLB that can say that. It will take all it has tomorrow to upset Tampa Bay, but it can pull it off.

The Guardians are being overlooked despite winning consecutive series over the division-leading Twins and the Wild Card-contending Blue Jays. They may not be anyone's favorite to win the Central today, but that could change with a few more victories. Allen likely shares that sentiment with his teammates, as he's undervalued despite his impressive play.

The 24-year-old has looked like a veteran at times in his first big league season and will be out to prove it again versus a dangerous Rays lineup. While some will write him off, I'm betting on the southpaw to get the job done. He's been a consistent pitcher both at home (3.70 ERA) and on the road (3.51 ERA) this season and is coming off an impressive string of starts in August. The Blue Jays benefitted from seeing him twice in three weeks, which is likely why they had more success against him in the second outing. Allen was otherwise very impressive, allowing four runs in each of his first four outings and no more than two runs in any of his appearances last month.

Rays starter Littell, on the other hand, gave up four runs in both of his previous starts. He's given up six homers in his last four outings, allowing at least one HR in each game. After starting the season in the bullpen, he may be getting too stretched out. Saturday's game will be his seventh consecutive start and I feel confident we will see more cracks in his armor vs. the Guardians.

Prediction: Guardians ML

Full-Game Total Pick

Insiders Status:

Rating:

The under is my bet on the total, as I expect a better performance from the lower-scoring Guardians than the higher-scoring Rays in this game.

The under is 77-54-3 in Guardians games this season, the highest under winning percentage in MLB. I predict we'll see another under tomorrow, as Logan Allen will tame the Rays lineup, and Cleveland will score enough runs to win, but not by a landslide. As long as Allen gives the Guardians five or six quality innings, I am confident the bullpen (fifth-lowest ERA in MLB) will shut the door on TB.

The Guardians average 3.5 runs per game at home and managed only 3.7 runs per game overall in August. If they can score three or four against Littell, that would be a success, but I wouldn't expect them to pile it on against the Rays' bullpen.

Prediction: Under 8.5
