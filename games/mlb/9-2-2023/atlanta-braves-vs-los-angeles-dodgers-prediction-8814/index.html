Atlanta Braves (88-45) vs. Los Angeles Dodgers (83-50)

The 2023 Major League Baseball season continues Saturday, September 2, with the Atlanta Braves taking on the Los Angeles Dodgers in the National League battle at Dodger Stadium in Los Angeles, California, and the first pitch is set for 9:10 PM ET.  

The Braves meet the Dodgers for the third contest of a four-game series in LA. Atlanta took the first blood 8-7, while the second game of the set has been excluded from the analysis. 

The Braves beat the Dodgers as Ronald Acuna makes history

Ronald Acuna Jr. stole the show last Thursday, hitting a grand slam in the top of the second inning to give Atlanta a 5-1 lead. It was his 30th long ball in 2023, and Acuna became the first player in MLB history with 30 home runs and 60 steals in a single season. The Braves improved to 88-45 on the season, increasing their lead over the Dodgers for the best record in the majors to five games.  

Atlanta’s offense has been outstanding all season long. The Braves lead the big leagues in home runs (253), OPS (.845), OPS+ (124), and runs scored per game (5.85). They are slashing .292/.345/.499 with 26 extra-base hits and 21 home runs in the past two weeks and 455 at-bats. 

Bryce Elder is projected to take the mound Saturday in LA, and the 24-year-old right-hander is 11-4 with a 3.50 ERA, 1.21 WHIP, and 107/49 K/BB ratio in 26 starts (149.1 innings pitched) in 2023. Elder, who was terrific in the first half of the season, had a bad August, going 3-2 with a 4.88 ERA and 4.89 FIP in five starts and 27.2 innings of work. Last Monday, Elder yielded four earned runs on eight hits and a walk in a 14-4 victory at the Colorado Rockies. 

Elder will face the Dodgers for the second time this season. Back in May, he tossed six frames of a one-run, seven-hit ball in a no-decision (Atlanta eventually won 4-3).

The Dodgers went 24-5 in August

The Los Angeles Dodgers enjoyed a wonderful August but still finished the month with a loss. Lance Lynn was shellacked for seven earned runs last Thursday, and the Dodgers’ mighty lineup couldn’t complete a comeback despite hitting three solo dingers down the stretch. 

The Dodgers are second in the majors in runs scored per game (5.67), home runs (212), OPS (.802), and OPS+ (115). They are slashing .298/.363/.520 with 32 extra-base hits and 23 round-trippers in the last couple of weeks and 460 at-bats. 

At the time of writing, it was unknown who would get the starting call for Saturday’s game. Bobby Miller is projected to take the mound in Monday’s closer, so the Dodgers might go with a bullpen day in the third game of the series. Gavin Stone should work a few innings after earning his first major league win this past Sunday. The Dodgers beat the Red Sox 7-4, and Stone yielded four earned runs on five hits (three homers) through six innings of work. 

If Stone gets the ball, he’ll meet the Braves for the second time this season. The 24-year-old rookie right-hander gave up five earned runs on five hits and five walks across four innings in a no-decision at Truist Park in Atlanta on May 22 (Los Angeles eventually won 8-6).
