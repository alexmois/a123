Toronto Blue Jays Fighting in AL Wild Card Chase

Toronto took two of three from Washington as they prevailed in the rubber game of that set Wednesday afternoon. The Blue Jays entered Friday 73-61 and stood third in the AL East, 10.5 games behind the Orioles for the top spot in the division. They were 2.5 games behind the Rangers for the final wild card spot in the American League playoff picture. On Wednesday, Toronto got three hits from Alejandro Kirk (two runs, three RBI) while Ernie Clement (run, RBI) and Santiago Espinal (two RBI) each added two in the win. Chris Bassitt (13-7) earned the win on the mound as he tossed eight shutout innings, allowing three hits with one walk and three strikeouts, before departing.

Yusei Kikuchi is on the mound for the Blue Jays as he makes his 27th start of the season in this contest. He enters the game 9-4 with a 3.63 ERA, a 1.23 WHIP, 38 walks and 146 strikeouts over 139 innings of work on the year. Kikuchi didn’t factor in the decision in his last start, which came Sunday at home against the Guardians. He threw six innings, allowing four runs on six hits with two walks and eight strikeouts in a game the Blue Jays dropped 10-7 in 11 innings. In his last three starts, Kikuchi has no record with a 4.32 ERA, a 1.20 WHIP, three walks and 21 strikeouts over 16.2 innings of work. Kikuchi lost his lone career start against the Rockies, which came with the Mariners at home on August 7, 2020. He threw 5.2 innings, allowing four runs on six hits with one walk and three strikeouts in a game Seattle dropped 8-4. Kikuchi, in his 129th career major league appearance and 117th start, pitches at Coors Field for the first time here.

Colorado Rockies Buried in NL West Cellar

Colorado lost their third straight and their ninth in 10 games as they were beaten in the finale of their three-game set at home by the Braves Wednesday night. The Rockies entered Friday 49-84 on the season and were last in the NL West, 32 games behind the Dodgers for the top spot in the division. They were 20.5 games behind the Giants for the final wild-card spot in the National League. On Wednesday, Colorado finished with five hits with Nolan Jones the lone player with two in the contest. Alan Trejo hit his third homer of the season in a losing effort for the Rockies. Kyle Freeland (5-14) took the loss as he was tagged for seven runs (six earned) on eight hits with one walk and three strikeouts over 5.2 innings of work.

Ty Blach is on the bump for the Rockies as he logs his 15th appearance and eighth start of the season for the Rockies here. He comes in 1-1 with a 3.94 ERA, a 1.417 WHIP, 12 walks and 26 strikeouts over 48 innings of work on the year. Blach didn’t factor in the decision in his last start, which came on the road against the Orioles on Sunday. He threw seven innings, allowing one run on three hits with one walk and seven strikeouts in a game the Rockies eventually won 4-3. In his last three starts, Blach has no record with a 3.38 ERA, a 1.38 WHIP, six walks and 13 strikeouts over 16 innings of work. Blach, in his 131st career major league appearance and 53rd start, pitches against the Blue Jays for the first time. He makes his 26th career appearance and sixth start at Coors Field in this contest. Blach is 2-1 with one save, a 5.03 ERA, a 1.42 WHIP, 14 walks and 30 strikeouts over 62.2 innings of work in those contests.
