The Miami Marlins (68-67, 60-75 RL, 60-71-4 O/U) meet with the Washington Nationals (62-74, 76-60 RL, 64-67-5 O/U) in a divisional clash on Saturday evening. The Marlins will be using the bullpen for this game. The Nationals will reply with Trevor Williams. The Marlins lead the season series 8-2 entering Friday.

(edit: The Marlins took the opener 8-5 in 11 innings on Friday night).

Marlins in Need of Wins

J.T. Chargois is expected to be the opener for Bryan Hoeing. Chargois has a 3.45 ERA and a 1.15 WHIP on the season. Bryan Hoeing is expected to be the bulk innings pitcher. The right-hander has allowed just two runs in 10.2 innings this month and has recorded a 4.02 ERA and a 1.25 WHIP accompanied by a 2-2 record in 56 innings on the season. Hoeing has only conceded one run in four innings this season and has squandered three runs in 5.2 career innings against the Nationals.

The Marlins have been struggling to win games and somehow are only three games out of a wildcard spot. They have lost five consecutive series after a sweep at the hands of the Rays due to a struggling offense. The squad rebounded with a convincing 6-1 win on Thursday to begin this series. The Marlins stand at 3-7 in their last ten decisions.

Jorge Soler has stellar numbers against the Nationals pitching this season, bashing four homers with nine RBIs and a 1.051 OPS. The 31-year-old DH is having a remarkable season, delivering 35 homers with 71 RBIs and an .843. OPS. Soler has missed the last few games but is expected to play.

The Marlins are scoring an average of 3.99 runs per game, marking them 27th. The pitching is a strength, amassing a 4.18 team ERA, placing them 18th.

Williams Starts, Nationals Show Progress

Trevor Williams will get the nod in this one. Williams came through with a solid performance last time out, limiting the Marlins to only two runs on five hits in seven innings but had no help and was charged with the loss. The veteran has recorded a 5.32 ERA this month a 4.82 ERA and a 1.46 WHIP accompanied by a 6-8 record in 130.2 innings pitched on the season. He has allowed 10 runs in 17 innings against the Marlins this season and has issued a 4.18 ERA and a 1-3 record in 47.1 career innings against the rivals.

Washington is home after a successful road trek that resulted in a 5-4 record. They collected series wins against the Yankees and the Marlins but dropped two of three bouts to the Blue Jays to conclude the trek. The Nationals are showing consistent improvement as the season progresses and stand at 5-5 in their last ten decisions

Joey Meneses continues to shine at the plate, accumulating seven RBIs in his last eight games. The 31-year-old DH doesn’t have power, collecting just 11 homers but has 76 RBIs and a .746 OPS on the season. Meneses has collected six RBIs and a .713 OPS against the Marlins this season. 

The Nationals are averaging 4.30 runs on the year, ranking them 21st. The pitching has been decent recently and has a 4.90 team ERA, positioning them 26th.
