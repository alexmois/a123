Brewers hope Rea and lineup step up

The Brewers are having a great season and look to close out the division title in the upcoming weeks. Colin Rea will start the upcoming game and he has had a rough year on the mound, allowing 61 runs in 104.2 innings pitched. Moreover, with a .441 expected opponent Slugging Percentage, a 42.7 Hard-Hit Percentage, and a 4.54 expected ERA, opponents are making hard contact and crushing the ball against Rea to easily drive in runs.

Rea has struggled but the lineup has also been a disappointment, scoring only 4.40 runs per game. William Contreras is slashing .279/.357/.457 with 15 home runs and 206 total bases while Christian Yelich is slashing .274/.363/.436 with 17 home runs and 226 total bases but the rest of the lineup has struggled. Only two batters have an OPS+ over 100 and opposing pitchers can limit the top-heavy lineup as a result.

Yankees hope lineup continues to improve

The Yankees looked like a team poised to finish under .500 but a recent resurgence has given them an outside chance to make the postseason. Luis Severino will start the upcoming game and he has been one of the worst pitchers in the majors this season, allowing 71 runs in 85.1 innings pitched. In addition, with a .288 expected opponent Batting Average, a .502 expected opponent Slugging Percentage, and a 6.03 expected ERA, opponents are making contact and powering the ball against Severino to easily drive in runs.

While the lineup has struggled all season, scoring only 4.25 runs per game, the Yankees have recently stepped up at the plate, scoring 29 runs in the last six games. Aaron Judge is slashing .267/.397/.622 with 31 home runs and 184 total bases while Gleyber Torres is slashing .272/.340/.465 with 24 home runs and 239 total bases but the rest of the lineup has struggled. The hope is that rookie Jasson Dominguez, who has three home runs and 17 total bases in only six games, can continue to add a spark to the lineup which has looked hapless otherwise.
