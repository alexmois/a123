Los Angeles Dodgers Trying to Get On Track Despite Fractured Pitching Staff

Los Angeles dropped their second straight and fifth in their last six games as they were drubbed by the Marlins Wednesday night. The Dodgers entered Thursday 84-54 on the season and held a 13-game lead on the Diamondbacks for the top spot in the NL West. On Wednesday night, Los Angeles got three hits from Jason Heyward (two runs, RBI) and two from David Peralta (run) in the game. The problem for the Dodgers is that the rest of the team went a combined one for 25 in the game. Heyward (his 14th) and James Outman (his 18th) each homered in the loss for Los Angeles. Lance Lynn (10-11) took the loss as he was tagged for eight runs on seven hits with three walks and one strikeout in 4.2 innings of work.

Emmet Sheehan is on the hill for the Dodgers as he makes his 10th appearance and ninth start of the season in this contest. He is 3-1 with one save, a 5.31 ERA, a 1.299 WHIP, 21 walks and 36 strikeouts over 42.1 innings of work this season. Sheehan didn’t factor in the decision in his last start, which came against the Braves at home Saturday. He threw four innings, allowing one run on three hits with three walks and six strikeouts in a game the Dodgers lost 4-2 in 10 innings. In his last three appearances, two starts, Sheehan has no record with one save, a 2.08 ERA, a 0.92 WHIP, five walks and 14 strikeouts over 13 innings of work. Sheehan, in his 10th major league appearance and ninth start, pitches against the Nationals for the first time. As a result, this marks his first career outing at Nationals Park. Prior to being recalled, Sheehan was 4-2 with a 2.43 ERA, a 0.905 WHIP, 29 walks and 102 strikeouts over 63 innings in 15 appearances, 11 starts, split between Double-A Tulsa of the Texas League and Triple-A Albuquerque of the Pacific Coast League this season.

Washington Nationals Looking to Prevail at Home

Washington snapped a six-game skid as they beat the Mets in the finale of a two-game series Wednesday night. The Nationals entered Thursday 63-77 on the season and sat in the basement of the NL East, 28 games behind the Braves for the top spot. In the NL wild-card picture, the team stood 9.5 games behind the Marlins for the final wild-card spot. On Wednesday night, Washington finished with nine hits as Dominic Smith (run) had three while Jacob Young (RBI) added a pair, including the walk-off RBI single in the bottom of the ninth. Joan Adon didn’t factor in the decision as he threw five innings, allowing two runs on six hits with three walks and six strikeouts. Kyle Finnegan (7-4) earned the win with a scoreless inning of relief as he allowed no hits with one walk and no strikeouts.

MacKenzie Gore is on the mound for his 27th start of the season for the Nationals in this contest. He comes in 7-10 with a 4.28 ERA, a 1.383 WHIP, 55 walks and 147 strikeouts over 132.1 innings of work on the year. Gore earned the win in his last start, which came last Tuesday on the road against the Blue Jays. He threw five innings, allowing one run on six hits with two walks and two strikeouts in a game the Nationals eventually won 5-4. In his last three starts, Gore is 1-1 with a 1.76 ERA, a 1.04 WHIP, five walks and 13 strikeouts over 15.1 innings of work. Gore didn’t factor in the decision in his lone start against the Dodgers, which came on the road with the Padres on July 3, 2022. He threw 5.2 innings, allowing one run on five hits with four walks and three strikeouts in a game the Padres went on to win 4-2. Gore is 2-4 with a 3.46 ERA, a 1.39 WHIP, 24 walks and 60 strikeouts over 54.2 innings in 11 career starts at Nationals Park.
