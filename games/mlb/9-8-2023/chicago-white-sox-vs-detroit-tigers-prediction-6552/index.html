The Chicago White Sox continue their AL Central Division road swing as they travel to Detroit to take on the Tigers. The White Sox are in fourth place in the AL Central Division while the Tigers sit in third place. Both teams are out of the playoff picture and will likely have some significant roster turnover this offseason. The pitching matchup on Friday will be White Sox righty Mike Clevinger (6-7, 3.90) taking on Tigers' righty Reese Olson (3-6, 4.65). The first pitch is scheduled for 6:40 PM EST.

Sinking Sox

The Chicago White Sox appear to be locked into fourth place in the underachieving AL Central Division with three weeks to go in the regular season. The White Sox are over ten games ahead of last-place Kansas City and are just under ten games behind the third-place Tigers. In their three-game series against the Royals in Kansas City, the White Sox lost each of the first two games by scores of 12-1 and 7-6. In the second game, the White Sox blew a 6-3 lead. In the series finale on Wednesday, the White Sox hung on for a 6-4 win thanks to a strong start from Touki Toussaint and a 3-for-4 night from Andrew Vaughn including a home run.

The White Sox will give the ball to righty Mike Clevinger on Friday night. Clevinger was placed on waivers by the White Sox earlier this month but went unclaimed. He is 6-7 on the season in 19 starts including six quality starts. He has allowed 94 hits in 101.2 innings pitched with an ERA of 3.90. He has a K/9 rate of 7.7 and a WHIP of 1.30. On the road this year, Clevinger is 2-4 in nine starts with an ERA of 4.17. In his last five starts, Clevinger is 2-2 with an ERA of 4.34 while allowing 25 hits in 29 innings pitched.

Home, Sweet Home

The Detroit Tigers are glad to be back home after a six-game trip through Chicago and New York. The Tigers were able to sweep the White Sox in Chicago before a three-game set in the Bronx. The Tigers dropped the opener 5-1, making it four losses in five games against the Yankees in just over a week. The Tigers fell again in game two 4-3. Starter Matt Manning left the game after just one inning after being hit on the foot with a come-backer. In the finale on Thursday, the Tigers avoided a sweep with a 10-3 win over the Bombers. Spencer Torkelson hit two home runs and starting pitcher Eduardo Rodriguez went six innings and allowed four hits and three runs to pick up the victory.

The Tigers will send rookie right-hander Reese Olson to the hill in the series opener on Friday night. Olson is 3-6 on the season in 17 appearances including 14 starts. He has given up 72 hits in 79.1 innings pitched with an ERA of 4.65. His K/9 rate is 9.0 and his WHIP is 1.21. In his last five starts, Olson is 2-1 with a 4.01 ERA while allowing 21 hits in 24.2 innings pitched with 29 strikeouts. Olson has a 4.01 ERA in those five starts.

Full-Game Side Bet

Insiders Status:

Rating:

This is such a strange start for Clevinger, his second start since being waived by the White Sox and going unclaimed. In his first start after being waived, Clevinger allowed 12 hits in four innings and gave up eight runs to these same Tigers. He will get a second crack at the Tigers on Friday night but I don't expect him to rebound. The White Sox have been miserable this season, particularly after tearing up their bullpen at the trade deadline. Olson was on the other end of that 10-0 blowout on September 2nd. He allowed just four hits in seven shutout innings against the White Sox. Olson will not need to replicate that performance here but he should be strong enough to get the Tigers into the win column. Olson did not get many swings and misses last week against the White Sox but I expect that number to increase in this start. The White Sox have the look of a team just waiting for this season to end while the Tigers have been battling throughout the season as they continue to rebuild.

Take the Tigers with the money line.

Prediction: Detroit -125
