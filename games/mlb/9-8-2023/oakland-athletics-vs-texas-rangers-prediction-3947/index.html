The Oakland Athletics and Texas Rangers will play this Friday at Globe Life Field. First pitch is at 7:05 p.m. CT.

Oakland (43-97 SU and 69-71 RL) is starting right-hander Paul Blackburn. The 29-year-old is 4-4 with a 3.81 ERA and a 1.46 WHIP in 17 outings (16 starts) this season.

Texas (76-63 SU and 73-66 RL) is countering with left-hander Jordan Montgomery. The 30-year-old is 8-10 with a 3.46 ERA and a 1.23 WHIP in his 27 starts.

The Rangers are -245 moneyline favorites and the game total is nine runs.

Can Oakland keep stealing wins?

Oakland has been playing better baseball recently, winning five of its last eight games straight-up. That included a series sweep of division rival Los Angeles, a series they won by a combined score of 21-9!

The Athletics average 3.68 runs per game (30th) and hit .225 (30th) with a .671 OPS (30th). They've hit 147 home runs (22nd) and stolen 130 bases (5th) this season. The pitching staff has compiled a 5.61 ERA (29th) and a 1.53 WHIP (30th) with 27 quality starts (30th).

Blackburn will start for the visiting A's on Friday evening. The seventh-year pro is 2-2 with a 4.10 ERA, a 1.58 WHIP, and a 40:12 K:BB ratio in eight road appearances (seven starts) this year. He hasn't faced the Rangers in 2023 but has not fared well against them in the past (0-3 with a 13.00 ERA in five appearances). In his most recent outing, he held the Angels to one run on four hits and three walks in a five-inning victory.

Are the Rangers limping to the finish line?

Texas has been reeling, losing six of its last seven and three pivotal games to Houston. It's now or never if it's going to climb back into the AL West title chase!

The Rangers average 5.48 runs (3rd) and hit .266 (2nd) with a .790 OPS (3rd). They've launched 196 homers (8th) and stolen 73 bases (25th) in 2023. The Texas pitching staff has posted a 4.31 ERA (17th) and a 1.27 WHIP (11th) with 59 quality starts (10th).

Montgomery will take the hill for Texas tomorrow. The seventh-year pro is 2-1 with a 3.60 ERA and a 1.17 WHIP in six starts with the Rangers. He sports a 3.83 ERA and 1.31 WHIP in 14 home starts with St. Louis and Texas. He struggled in his last start, surrendering six runs and seven hits in just 3.2 frames vs. Minnesota.

Texas sports a 52.5% run line winning percentage as a home favorite, the third-best record in the big leagues this season. It also has a 23-16 RL record against division opponents.

The Rangers are three games out of first place in the AL West. After losing consecutive series to playoff contenders Minnesota and Houston, they'll get back to their winning ways against the lowly A's. Montgomery held them to two runs in early August and looks primed to bounce back after a poor performance in his last start. Oakland hit .222 BA/.291 OBP/.394 SLG/.686 OPS last month, scoring only 3.5 runs per contest.

Texas needs wins badly and should be ready to go get them against Oakland. Blackburn puts too many runners on base, walking 11 batters in August (30.2 IP) and three more in his first September outing. The Rangers have struggled with runners in scoring position recently, but I believe that will change at home against the A's. They're slashing an impressive .277/.351/.459/.810 vs. left-handed pitchers this season (1,256 ABs) and .275/.346/.493/.839 overall at home (72 games).

Bet on Texas to get back on track, giving Montgomery enough run support to cover the 1.5-run line!

Full-Game Total Pick

Insiders Status:

Rating:

It would take quite the breakout to get the Rangers out of their slump. A ten-run over appears too high for this matchup, as Texas has struggled recently (four runs combined in their last two games), and Blackburn has held seven of his last eight opponents to two or fewer earned runs. Texas can score more than that tomorrow night at home, but I'm not counting on six or seven runs from them, either.

As for Oakland, it's challenging to predict a three or four-run performance against the steady Montgomery. He's held six of his last seven opponents to three or fewer runs. He's allowed three or fewer runs in 21 of 27 starts between the NL and AL this season, too.

Bet on eight or fewer runs when these clubs take the field on Friday!
