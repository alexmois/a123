Get ready to witness a powerful battle with a ton at stake. The Seattle Mariners (78-61; before Thursday's game) are taking on the Tampa Bay Rays (85-55) four times over the weekend. Each team is squarely in the American League playoff hunt, and also vying for their division. Game two of this important set is scheduled for Friday at 6:40 p.m. EDT. Tropicana Field will host the festivities once again. A lot of eyes will be on this series, which could change a lot of the AL postseason picture. Here's how each team looks before Friday.

 

*Published before Thursday's game

Seattle in Second

For a brief moment, the Mariners were unstoppable and alone in first place. Well, they cooled off, the Astros swept the Rangers (who are in third by 3.0 games), and Seattle began this series 1.0 game out of first. Every game could be the difference in this tight AL West race. Fortunately, the Mariners have allowed the second-fewest runs per game this season. It's been a team effort, featuring a bullpen that's top-four in WAR, FIP, and ERA. However, strong starting pitching still leads to success, which is why George Kirby is the man on the mound to watch this Friday. Kirby faced the Rays once this season, holding them to two hits and two runs over 7.0 innings. This outing may not be as successful, as he brings a 3.86 ERA in away games to the Trop. Kirby's last three starts were on the road, totaling 14.2 innings, 22 hits allowed, and 11 runs surrendered. He gave up a homer in each one too, which doesn't bode well against this Tampa lineup.

Even if Kirby doesn't have it, Seattle can slug their way to a win. Since August 1, the Mariners are averaging 5.7 runs per game (6th in that span), bringing them to 10th in scoring for the entire season. Seattle overcomes the second-highest strikeout rate with a top-10 walk rate and home run tally. Across the board, the Mariners have better batting numbers on the road too. The team has a .266/.348/.473 slash line in the second half, led by an unstoppable Julio Rodriguez.

Rays Losing the Race

Only one club came into this series allowing fewer runs per game than the Mariners, and that would be the Tampa Bay Rays. Trailing the Orioles by 3.5 games for the AL's best record (and AL East lead) it hasn't translated to the results that Tampa desires quite yet. With rookie Taj Bradley lined up for this start, a step forward isn't guaranteed. The right-handed rookie gave up nine hits and five runs across 3.1 innings when he faced the Mariners in July. Bradley didn't pitch at all in August, returning on September 3 to snap a six-start losing streak that began in June. That game was against a weak offense, which doesn't apply to this one. Tampa's bullpen isn't special this year, even if they've logged the fourth-most innings league-wide, so Bradley has a lot of weight on his shoulders.

The Rays' offense has also been on fire lately, scoring 5.81 runs per game since August 1, the fourth-most during that stretch. Only three teams have outscored the Rays this season. Tampa is third in homers and second in steals, giving pitchers multiple causes for concern. As a team they're eighth in batting average, sixth in OBP, and fourth in slugging percentage. Even without Wander Franco, this lineup has been thriving down the stretch.

In a game pitting two red-hot offenses head-to-head, pitching should determine who wins. In Seattle's corner is George Kirby, who dominated the Rays once this year in a winning effort. Along with that, he's backed by an elite bullpen. The Mariners' relievers are no worse than fifth in WAR, FIP, ERA, and LOB% this season.

As for the Rays, they will start Taj Bradley, who couldn't make it out of the fourth inning against Seattle before getting yanked. He gave up five runs in that game, in one of the most pitcher-friendly parks in the majors. Now he shifts to his home park, where he has a 5.88 ERA this season. Tampa's bullpen behind him is solid, but not as strong as Seattle's. Take the visitors to win on Friday.

Full-Game Total Pick

Insiders Status:

Rating:

This shouldn't even be a question at 7.5 runs. Taj Bradley gave up five against the Mariners on the road, and now he's home where his ERA swells to 5.88. If the total is 7.5, Bradley has allowed multiple runs in 14 of his 17 starts, so that alone would be a quarter of the total as a best-case scenario. That doesn't even factor the Seattle offense which is averaging more than 5.5 runs per game in their past 33 played.

The home offense is also putting up more than 5.5 runs per contest over a 31-game stretch beginning on August 1. They're third in homers league-wide and will face George Kirby, who has allowed a homer in three straight starts. All of those outings were on the road, with Kirby giving up at least three runs in each battle. None of those offenses were at Tampa's level. This one easily finishes over the total.

 

Prediction: Over 7.5
