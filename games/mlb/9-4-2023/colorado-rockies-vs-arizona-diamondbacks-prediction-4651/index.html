The Colorado Rockies (50-86, 65-71 RL, 63-73-1 O/U) meet with the Arizona Diamondbacks (70-67, 72-65 RL, 60-70-7 O/U) in the first of a three-game series on Monday afternoon. The Rockies will give the nod to Peter Lambert. The Diamondbacks will reply with Merrill Kelly. The Diamondbacks lead the season series 8-2.

Lambert Seeks Fourth Win

Peter Lambert will get the nod on Monday. Lambert was able to evade danger in his previous outing, allowing three runs on nine hits in five innings, and was hit with the loss. The righty has been decent in recent performances, allowing three or fewer runs in five of his last seven outings, and has a weak 4.95 ERA and a 1.36 WHIP accompanied by a 3-5 record in 76.1 innings. Lambert conceded one run in 2.2 innings in his only meeting against Arizona this season and has squandered four runs in 7.2 career innings. 

Colorado is at the bottom of the MLB standings in what’s going to be a rough September. They have been decimated with injuries to the pitching all season and don’t have enough offense. They have dropped four consecutive series after losing two of three to the Blue Jays to dip to 2-8 in their last ten clashes.

Colorado is scoring an average of 4.41 runs on the year, ranking them 18th. The pitching is horrible, posting a 5.61 team ERA, pegging them 29th.

Kelly Starts, D-Backs Remain in Wildcard

The Diamondbacks will have Merrill Kelly on the rubber. Kelly had a rough one last time out, squandering seven runs in five innings against a hot Dodgers squad, and was pelted with the loss. The veteran has been sharp in recent outings, posting a 3.57 ERA in August, and has issued a 3.31 ERA and a 1.20 WHIP complemented by a 10-6 record in 141.1 innings on the season. Kelly has limited the Rockies to three runs in 12 innings this year and is carrying a 4.15 ERA and a 4-3 record in 69.1 career innings against the rivals.

Arizona was dealt a sweep by the Dodgers last week and dropped two of three home meetings against the Orioles, giving up 17 runs. They remain just one game out of a wildcard spot and have a big series against the Cubs after this one. The Diamondbacks stand at 4-6 in their last ten bouts.

Arizona is averaging 4.68 runs per game, marking them 13th. The pitching has been exploited recently and has a 4.65 team ERA, placing them 23rd.
