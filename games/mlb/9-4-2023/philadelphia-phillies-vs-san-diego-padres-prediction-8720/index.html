The Philadelphia Phillies continue their road trip as they travel to the West Coast to battle the Padres in San Diego on Monday. The Phillies are currently atop the National League wildcard standings with under a month to go in the season. The Padres have faded since the trade deadline and will likely be resigned to the role of spoilers down the stretch. The pitching matchup on Monday will feature Phillies' righty Taijuan Walker (14-5, 4.05) taking on Padres' lefty Rich Hill (7-13, 5.26). The first pitch is scheduled for 6:40 PM EST.

Wild Ride Again?

The Philadelphia Phillies arrive in San Diego on Monday night in what will be a rematch from last year's NLCS in which the Phils rolled to a five-game series win on their way to the World Series. This year, the Phillies again look like a team that will earn a wildcard spot while the Padres will likely have to wait til next year. The Phillies come in after battling NL Central Division-leading Milwaukee over the weekend. The Phillies dropped the opener 7-5 after coughing up the lead in the bottom of the eighth inning. After dropping game two after coughing up another lead, the Phillies were able to avert a sweep with a 4-2 win on Sunday thanks to home runs from Alec Bohm and J.T. Realmuto in the eighth inning.

The Phillies will hand the ball to the team leader in wins, Taijuan Walker, on Monday afternoon in San Diego. Walker is 14-5 on the season in 26 starts including 10 quality starts. He has allowed 127 hits in 142.1 innings pitched with an ERA of 4.05. On the road this season, Walker is 7-3 in 12 starts with an ERA of 4.98 while striking out 51 in 65 innings pitched. In his last five starts, Walker is 3-1 with an ERA of 4.30 and 21 strikeouts in 29.1 innings pitched.

Disappointing Season in San Diego

The Padres come into this series among the most disappointing teams in baseball for 2023. After reaching the National League Championship Series last season and then signing all-star shortstop Xander Bogaerts in the offseason, hopes were high that the Padres would return to the World Series for the first time since 1998. Things have not gone according to form for the Padres and their all-star lineup this season and they will likely have to be spoilers down the stretch. The Padres played NL West rival San Francisco over the weekend. The Padres won the opener 7-3. The Padres won again on Saturday night thanks to another stellar performance from starter Blake Snell in a 7-3 win. The Padres completed the sweep on Sunday after Seth Lugo pitched six shutout innings and sluggers Manny Machado and Juan Soto each homered. The Padres are now 65-73 on the season.

The Padres will turn to veteran lefty Rich Hill in game one of their series with the Phillies. Hill is 7-13 on the season in 27 appearances, 26 of which were starts and an ERA of 5.26. Since joining the Padres in a trade deadline deal, Hill is 0-3 in five appearances, including four starts. He has allowed 23 hits in 18 innings pitched with an ERA of 8.50. He has a K/9 rate of just 6.5 with the Padres and his WHIP is 1.61.

Key Injuries: 1B Jake Cronenworth and 1B/DH Jin Man Choi are out.
