Milwaukee (76-60) takes on Pittsburgh (63-74) on Monday night from PNC Park in the Steel City.

In the last 10 matchups between these teams, the Brewers have won seven games.

Brewers take two of three from Phillies

Even though Milwaukee fell on Sunday, 4-2 to Philadelphia, they wound up winning the series at home 2-1. The more important news is that they are 3.5 games up on the Chicago Cubs in National League Central. They lost their last road series to the Cubs 2-1. But that was after the Brewers had won nine straight games and had taken three straight series.

Milwaukee will send right-hander Corbin Burnes to the mound on Monday night. He's 9-7 with a 3.55 ERA this season. Against the Pirates, Burnes is 7-1 with a 3.33 ERA in 22 career games and 10 starts. This season, Burnes is 1-0 with a 2.77 ERA in two starts against Pittsburgh.

Nice road trip

Some teams have packed it in this season, but to Pittsburgh's credit, they have kept playing. Despite losing to St. Louis, 6-4 on Sunday, the Pirates went 5-1 on their recent road trip, which included a sweep of Kansas City. In their loss to St. Louis on Sunday, Johan Oviedo struggled on the mound, allowing five runs (three earned)  in 3.2 innings.

The Pirates have not announced a starter for this game as of Monday morning. In terms of stats, Pittsburgh owns a 4.50 ERA (20th), a 1.38 WHIP (22nd) and is 14th in quality starts with 47. On offense, the Pirates rank 24th in runs per game (4.21), 28th in batting average (.235), 23rd in OPS (.700) and 29th in home runs (133).

Pittsburgh has played well, but Burnes is very tough against them in his career and even this season. He's 5-4 on the road with a 2.95 ERA, so he's actually better on the road than at home (4.35 ERA). The Pirates are most likely using their bullpen, which is something they've done lately. That can work against the Royals, but the Brewers are in first place in the NL Central for a reason.
