The San Francisco Giants and Chicago Cubs will kick off a three-game series on Monday. First pitch from Wrigley Field is at 1:20 p.m. CT.

San Francisco (70-65 SU and 66-69 RL) is starting right-hander Logan Webb. The 26-year-old is 9-11 with a 3.49 ERA and a 1.10 WHIP in 28 outings this season.

Chicago (72-63 SU and 69-66 RL) is starting left-hander Justin Steele. The 28-year-old is 15-3 with a 2.69 ERA and a 1.17 WHIP in his 25 outings.

San Francisco needs to play better to make the playoffs

San Francisco wrapped up a four-game series in San Diego on Sunday. It's going to take a strong finish for it to make the postseason, as it's just 21-24 since the All-Star break.

The Giants average 4.32 runs (21st) and hit .239 (24th) with a .701 OPS (23rd). They've launched 145 long balls (21st) and stolen 47 bases (30th) in 2023. The SF pitching staff sports a 3.95 ERA (9th) and a 1.25 WHIP (10th) with 43 quality starts (20th).

Webb will toe the rubber for the visiting Giants on Monday afternoon in Chicago. The fifth-year pro held Cincinnati to two runs in six innings on August 30, but it wasn't enough to avoid a loss. Webb went 1-2 with a 3.49 ERA and a 1.09 WHIP in six August outings (38.2 IP). He's 4-5 with a 4.63 ERA and a 1.22 WHIP in 14 road starts this year.

Are the Cubs capable of staying this hot?

Chicago finished a four-game series with Cincinnati over the weekend. It's made a move up the standings in the second half of the season, winning 30 of 46 games. Can it finish the job in September?

The Cubbies score 5.01 runs per game (6th) and hit .252 (14th) with a .745 OPS (11th). They've hit 162 homers (14th) and stolen 115 bases (6th) this year. The pitching staff has compiled a 4.09 ERA (14th) and a 1.27 WHIP (14th) with 60 quality starts (5th).

The lefty Steele will take the hill for the home team on Labor Day. He picked up a victory against Milwaukee in his last outing, pitching six scoreless frames. Steele was 4-0 with a 3.41 ERA and a 1.37 WHIP in his six starts last month. The third-year pro has been a consistent winner at home, posting an 11-2 record with a 2.46 ERA and a 1.23 WHIP in his 15 outings at Wrigley.

Webb has been a bad bet on the road this season and has been putting too many runners on base lately. In his last five starts, he's allowed 35 hits and 14 runs (13 earned) in 31.2 innings. He lost his last two starts and gave up four runs and nine hits three starts ago. I don't trust him against the red-hot Cubs, who are scoring 5.7 runs per game since the All-Star break.

Steele has been steady and consistent, especially under the sun. He's 8-2 with a 2.47 ERA and a 1.17 WHIP in 15 day games. He will earn his 12th victory of the year at home against a Giants club that's hitting .220 with a .295 OBP in the second half of the season, averaging a paltry 3.7 runs per game.

Full-Game Total Pick

Insiders Status:

Rating:

The over is 62-50-5 when the Cubs play with no rest. It's also 35-29-4 in Chicago's home games this season.

The Cubs' bats didn't show up on Friday and Saturday in Cincy, but I'm betting on them breaking out on Monday in their return home. They score a half-run more at home (5.2 runs per game) and averaged 5.5 runs per game overall in August. The Cubs will get on base early and often (.335 OBP vs. right-handed pitchers) against Webb, pushing the total over.

I don't predict too much scoring from the visitors, as it will take an out-of-character performance from Steele for that to happen. That said, the Cubs bullpen has walked the seventh-most batters in MLB this season (215), which could help the Giants score an extra run or two once Webb exits the game.
