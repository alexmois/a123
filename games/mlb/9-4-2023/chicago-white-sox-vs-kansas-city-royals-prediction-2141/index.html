The Chicago White Sox and Kansas City Royals will kick off the start of a three-game series on Monday afternoon in Kansas City. The two teams are in fourth and fifth place in the AL Central Division this season, both well out of playoff contention. The pitching matchup on Labor Day will feature White Sox righty Jesse Scholtens (1-6, 3.97) taking on Royals' lefty Cole Ragans (5-4, 3.27). The first pitch is scheduled for 2:10 PM EST.

White Sox Regrouping

In what has been a lost season, the White Sox have also begun to rebuild in preparation for next season. The team parted ways with its GM and hired Chris Getz as team as senior vice president and general manager. He will continue to remodel a roster that started to make changes at the trade deadline, parting with three relievers and starter Lance Lynn. In the meantime, the White Sox' current roster finishes out a disappointing season that will result in a second-straight sub-.500 record. Over the weekend, the White Sox battled another Central Division foe the Detroit Tigers. The White Sox dropped the opener 4-2. Things went from bad to worse for the ChiSox on Saturday in a 10-0 loss and the Tigers completed the sweep with a 3-2 win on Sunday afternoon.

The White Sox will hand the ball to reliever-turned-starter Jesse Scholtens on Monday afternoon. Scholtens is 1-6 in 22 appearances this season including eight starts. In those eight starts, he has three quality starts. On the season, he has allowed 70 hits in 70.1 innings pitched with an ERA of 3.97. He has faced Detroit once on the season, he didn't get a decision while allowing four hits in 4.2 innings pitched with two runs allowed. Scholtens is 0-2 in his last five starts with an ERA of 5.47.

Key Injuries: C Seby Zavala is out.

Hope for the Future

Despite approaching 100 losses for the season, the Kansas City Royals have reason for hope in the near future. The Royals have watched shortstop Bobby Witt Jr. push himself to the brink of superstardom, have a number of promising pitchers that should be back and ready to go in 2024, and may have found an ace in the hole in today's starter, Cole Ragans, after acquiring him in a deal with the Texas Rangers prior to the trade deadline. Over the weekend, the Royals met up with a sinking Red Sox team in Kansas City. On Friday night, the Royals rolled to a 13-2 win over the Sox. Friday night would end up being the highlight of the series for the Royals as the Red Sox would outscored them 16-8 over the final two games to take two of three from the Royals.

The Royals will hand the ball to lefty Ragans in game one against the White Sox. Ragans has been a revelation since arriving in Kansas City in a deal for closer Adolis Chapman. Ragans is 5-4 overall on the season in 24 appearances, seven of them starts since joining the Rangers. With Texas, Ragans has been downright dominant. He has five quality starts out of the seven he's made since joining the rotation. Ragans has allowed 34 hits in 41.2 innings with a sterling 1.73 ERA. He has a K/9 rate of 12.1 and his WHIP is 1.08.

Key Injuries: 1B Nick Pratto is out.
