The Seattle Mariners (77-58, 1st AL West) look to take a firmer grasp of first place in the American League West when opening a three-game interleague series on the road against the Cincinnati Reds (71-67, 3rd NL Central) on Monday. Seattle took sole possession of first place in the American League last when defeating the New York Mets 8-7 on Saturday coupled with a loss by the Houston Astros. Cincinnati defeated the Chicago Cubs 2-1 on Saturday and is 6 ½ games behind first in the National League Central. The Reds are tied with Arizona and San Francisco for the third and final NL wildcard spot with 24 games remaining on the schedule. Seattle will play without five pitchers as well as position players Jarred Kelenic, Evan White and Tom Murphy. Cincinnati will play without 10 pitchers and several position players including Jonathan India, Matt McClain and Joey Votto amongst others.

 

This article was published prior to the results from Sunday’s games.

Seattle pitching has carried the Mariners to the top

The Seattle Mariners have sole possession of first place in the American Lane West thanks in a big part to their pitching staff. Seattle is first in both team ERA at 3.66 and WHIP at 1.17 while second in Quality Starts with 66 and 5th in batting average allowed at .237. Logan Gilbert is leading Seattle in victories with 12 and Luis Castillo is the leader in strikeouts with 183. Right-hander Bryan Woo starts on the mound for Seattle on Monday and is 2-3 with an ERA of 4.15 and 68 strikeouts.

 

At the plate, Seattle has scored the 10th most runs, has the 12th best on-base percentage and the 13th best slugging percentage while the Mariners are 19th in batting average at .245. Cal Raleigh has a team-best 25 home runs, while Julio Rodriguez is second in home runs with 24 and is the team leader in RBI with 88. Teoscar Hernandez is tied for second in home runs with 24 and is second in RBI with 83. Seattle has three players with 83 or more RBI.

Cincinnati needs help on the mound

Cincinnati is 23rd out of 30 in team ERA at 4.66 and in WHIP at 1.40, while the Reds are 24th in both Quality Starts with 40 and batting average allowed at .256. Andrew Abbott is leading Cincinnati in victories with eight and Hunter Green is the leader in strikeouts with 114, but Green is on the injured list. Cincinnati has not released the name of its starting pitcher for Monday's opening game of a three-game set at home versus Seattle.

At the plate, Cincinnati has been average this season with much of that due to injuries to productive hitters. Cincinnati has scored the 11th most runs with 648, is 13th in on-base percentage at .323, 17th in slugging percentage at .408 and 18th in team batting average with .247. Spencer Steer is the leader in home runs with 18 and RBI with 72. Matt McLain is second in home runs with 16 but is out injured. Jake Fraley, who has played only 95 of Cincinnati's 138 games, is third in home runs with 15 and second in RBI with 63. In only 51 games played Joey Votto has 13 home runs and 33 RBI but is also out at this time
