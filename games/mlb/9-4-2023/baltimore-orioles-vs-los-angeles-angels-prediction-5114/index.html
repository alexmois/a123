Baltimore Orioles (84-51) vs. Los Angeles Angels (64-72)

The 2023 Major League Baseball season goes on Monday, September 4, with an 11-game slate including the American League showdown from Angel Stadium in Anaheim, California, so we bring you the best Orioles vs. Angels betting pick.  

The Orioles and Angels open a three-game series in Anaheim. These two foes lock horns for the second time this term after splitting a four-game set in Baltimore back in May. 

The O’s are still topping the AL East

Sunday’s games have been excluded from the analysis, and the 84-51 Baltimore Orioles visited the Arizona Diamondbacks to decide the winner of a three-game series at Chase Field in Phoenix, Arizona. After a 4-2 loss in Friday’s opener, the O’s beat the D-backs 7-3 on Saturday night. It was Baltimore’s seventh victory in ten games, and the Orioles were topping the AL East two and a half games above the Tampa Bay Rays. 

The O’s are slashing a sturdy .288/.340/.469 with 26 extra-base hits and 16 home runs in the last two weeks and 416 at-bats. Anthony Santander has mashed five of those 16 dingers to go with four doubles and 14 RBI across 41 at-bats, while Gunnar Henderson has gone 16-for-50 with five doubles, a triple, and a pair of homers during that span. 

Grayson Rodriguez is expected take the mound Monday at Angel Stadium, and the 23-year-old rookie right-hander is 4-3 with a 5.03 ERA, 1.35 WHIP, and 97/35 K/BB ratio in 18 starts (93 innings pitched) this season. Rodriguez was pretty bad in the first half of the season. He turned the corner in August, going 2-1 with a strong 2.64 ERA, 3.23 FIP, and .519 opposing OPS in five starts and 30.2 innings of work. Last Monday, Rodriguez tossed six scoreless innings in a 9-0 dismantling of the lowly Chicago White Sox while surrendering just one hit and a walk.

The Angels cannot wait for the end of the season

The 64-72 Los Angeles Angels hosted the Oakland Athletics on Sunday afternoon, hoping to avoid a sweep in a three-game set. The Halos have only won three of their previous 12 games overall. With Mike Trout on the IL and Shohei Ohtani playing through a torn UCL in his right elbow, the season is pretty much over for the Angels, who trailed the final wild card in the American League by 11.5 games on Sunday morning. 

The Halos are hitting a mediocre .237/.329/.404 with 23 extra-base hits and 12 home runs over the last couple of weeks and 371 at-bats. Luis Rengifo and Brandon Drury have combined for five round-trippers and 21 RBI during that span, while Ohtani has gone 11-for-36 with five doubles, a triple, and a home run. 

The Angels haven’t announced who’ll get the starting call on Monday and could opt for a bullpen day. Their relief pitching has been awful over the last ten days, posting a 6.84 ERA and 5.61 FIP through 26.1 innings of work.  (edit: left-hander Ken Rosenburg is expected to start. He's 0-0 with a 6.43 ERA and has started twice.)

Full-Game Side Bet

Insiders Status:

Rating:

Grayson Rodriguez was money last Monday, and I’m expecting to see another strong performance when he takes on the Angels’ depleted lineup. The Orioles’ bullpen has done a great job over the last ten days, notching a 2.73 ERA and 2.80 FIP through 26.1 innings, so I’m backing Baltimore to beat the runline. The prize for the Orioles to win straight up is not tempting at -170. It’s a good look if you want to build a parlay. 

I don’t trust the Angels’ pitching staff to keep the O’s in check. Over the last ten days, the Orioles’ bats have registered a 127 wRC+ against the righties (239 plate appearances) and an 84 wRC+ versus the southpaws. The Angels have four lefties in their bullpen, but just one of those four guys holds an ERA south of 5.74. 

Prediction: Baltimore Orioles -1.5 (-105)
