The latest chapter of the Lone Star State rivalry gets underway on the diamond as a pair of AL West rivals collide in Arlington. It’s the Houston Astros making a road trip within the state as they take on the Texas Rangers in the opening game of a three-game series Monday afternoon. Houston tried to avoid a home sweep at the hands of the Yankees Sunday afternoon as they finished a three-game series. The Astros fell 5-4 in the middle game of the set Saturday night. Texas went into Sunday looking to avoid a home sweep at the hands of the Twins as they were beaten 9-7 in 10 innings Saturday in the middle game of that series. In the season series, Houston holds a 6-4 advantage, including taking two of three at home in the most recent set on July 24-26.

Houston Astros Looking to Climb in AL West Race

Houston suffered their second straight loss as they were beaten by the Yankees Saturday night in the middle game of that series. The Astros entered Sunday 77-60 on the year and stood second in the AL West, one game behind the Mariners for the top spot. They held the second wild card spot in the American League and stood 2.5 games clear of Toronto to remain in the playoff field. On Saturday night, Houston got two hits each from Michael Brantley (two runs, two RBI) and Yanier Diaz (run, two RBI) only to fall short in the contest. Brantley (his first) and Diaz (his 20th) each went deep in a losing effort. Hunter Brown (10-10) took the loss on the mound as he allowed five runs (three earned) on five hits with four walks and five strikeouts in four innings of work.

J.P. France is on the bump for the Astros as he logs his 21st appearance and 20th start of the season in this contest. He is 10-5 with a 3.49 ERA, a 1.293 WHIP, 34 walks and 86 strikeouts over 116 innings of work this season. France earned the win in his last start, which came against the Red Sox on the road Tuesday. He threw 5.2 innings, allowing two runs on five hits with one walk and three strikeouts in a 6-2 Houston victory. In his last three starts, France is 1-2 with a 9.00 ERA, a 1.93 WHIP, four walks and 13 strikeouts over 14 innings of work. France beat the Rangers in his lone career start against them, which came at home on July 25. He threw seven innings, allowing one run (none earned) on five hits with two walks and three strikeouts in a 4-3 Houston victory. France, in his 21st career major league appearance and 20th start, pitches at Globe Life Field for the first time here.

Texas Rangers Hoping to Break Out of Late Season Slide

Texas dropped their third straight and their 12th in the last 15 games as they fell in extra innings to the Twins at home Saturday night. The Rangers entered Sunday 75-60 on the season and stood third in the AL West, two games behind the Mariners for the top spot in the division. They held the final wild card spot in the American League playoff picture and were 1.5 games ahead of the Blue Jays to remain in the playoff picture. On Saturday, Texas got three hits from Robbie Grossman (run, RBI) while Leody Taveras (RBI) added a pair in the contest. Adolis Garcia (his 33rd) and Sam Huff (his second) each went deep in the loss. Jordan Montgomery didn’t factor in the decision as he was tagged for six runs on seven hits with two walks and no strikeouts over 3.2 innings of work. Aroldis Chapman (5-4) took the loss in relief as he allowed three runs (two earned) on two hits with one walk and one strikeout in two-thirds of an inning of work.

Andrew Heaney is on the hill for the Rangers as he makes his 27th start of the season in this contest. He is 9-6 with a 4.16 ERA, a 1.347 WHIP, 50 walks and 134 strikeouts over 127.2 innings of work this season. Heaney didn’t factor in the decision in his last start, which came against the Mets on the road Tuesday night. He threw 5.1 scoreless innings, allowing five hits with one walk and seven strikeouts in a 2-1 Rangers victory. In his last three starts, Heaney has no record with a 4.05 ERA, a 1.65 WHIP, three walks and 15 strikeouts over 13.1 innings of work. Heaney makes his 15th career start against the Astros in this contest. He is 4-4 with a 3.20 ERA, a 1.211 WHIP, 22 walks and 79 strikeouts over 76 innings of work against them. Heaney is 6-5 with a 4.72 ERA, a 1.439 WHIP, 33 walks and 88 strikeouts over 82 innings of work in 17 career starts at Globe Life Field.
