Toronto Blue Jays Trying to Gain Ground in AL Wild Card Chase

Toronto saw their two-game win streak snapped as they were beaten in the middle game of their series with the Rockies on the road Saturday night. The Blue Jays dropped to 74-62 on the season and stood third in the AL East, 10.5 games behind the Orioles for the top spot in the division. They were 1.5 games behind the Rangers for the final wild-card spot in the American League playoff chase. On Saturday, Toronto got two hits each from Santiago Espinal (two RBI), Whit Merrifield (run, RBI) and Daulton Varsho (RBI) only to fall short. Kevin Kiermaier hit his seventh homer of the season but the Blue Jays left 10 men on base in the contest. Yusei Kikuchi (9-5) took the loss on the mound as he allowed six runs (two earned) on six hits with four walks and six strikeouts over 4.2 innings of work.

Jose Berrios takes the ball for the Blue Jays as he logs his 28th start of the season in this contest. He comes in 9-10 with a 3.70 ERA, a 1.222 WHIP, 46 walks and 146 strikeouts over 158 innings of work this season. Berrios took the loss in his last start, which came Tuesday on the road against the Nationals. He threw six innings, allowing five runs on six hits with two walks and two strikeouts in a game Toronto lost by a 5-4 score. In his last three starts, Berrios is 0-2 with a 5.09 ERA, a 1.25 WHIP, six walks and 14 strikeouts over 17.2 innings of work. Berrios makes his 10th career start against the A’s in this contest. He is 2-3 with a 3.81 ERA, a 1.192 WHIP, 13 walks and 50 strikeouts over 52 innings of work against them. Berrios is 0-2 with a 4.15 ERA, a 1.20 WHIP, eight walks and 21 strikeouts over 21.2 innings in four career starts at the RingCentral Coliseum.

Oakland A’s Looking to Play Spoiler in September

Oakland won their second straight game as they turned back the Angels, giving them a shot at a rare sweep Sunday afternoon. The A’s entered Sunday 41-95 on the season and stood last in the AL West, 36.5 games behind the Mariners for the top spot in the division. They have been eliminated from playoff contention. On Saturday, Oakland finished with just five hits with no player recording more than one but managed to put the game in the win column. Brent Rooker hit his 23rd homer of the season in the fourth inning to give the A’s a lead they would not relinquish. Paul Blackburn (4-4) earned the win on the mound as he threw five innings, allowing one run on four hits with three walks and four strikeouts. Trevor May worked a scoreless ninth, allowing no hits with two walks and no strikeouts, for his 16th save of the year.

Ken Waldichuk is on the bump for Oakland as he makes his 31st appearance and 20th start of the season in this contest. He is 2-7 with one save, a 5.92 ERA, a 1.667 WHIP, 64 walks and 114 strikeouts over 114 innings of work on the season. Waldichuk didn’t factor in the decision in his last start, which came against the Mariners on the road Tuesday night. He threw four innings, allowing one run on one hit with five walks and three strikeouts in a game the A’s eventually won 3-1. In his last three starts, Waldichuk has no record with a 4.91 ERA, a 1.43 WHIP, eight walks and 14 strikeouts over 14.2 innings of work. Waldichuk didn’t factor in the decision in his lone appearance against the Blue Jays, which came in relief on the road back on June 25. He threw 2.1 innings, allowing six runs on six hits with one walk and four strikeouts in a game the A’s went on to drop 12-1. Waldichuk is 2-3 with one save, a 4.24 ERA, a 1.294 WHIP, 24 walks and 66 strikeouts over 68 innings in 16 career appearances, 12 starts, at the RingCentral Coliseum.
