Most may not have expected it before the season, but the Baltimore Orioles (83-51; before Saturday's game) and Arizona Diamondbacks (70-65) are focused on October. For each team, how they survive in September will greatly affect that mission. With this series finale on Sunday, a grueling test may leave either clubhouse in an undesirable position. This is a big battle for the standings in both leagues, scheduled to start at 4:10 p.m. EDT. Live from Chase Field, this is how each team looks heading into the battle.

 

 

*Published before Saturday's game

Baltimore Can't Budge Now

Leading the Rays by fewer than two games for both the division lead and the American League's best record, the Orioles need to keep soaring in this tremendous year. It'll be on the offense's shoulders to continue producing at a top-10 level. Baltimore is only 22nd in walk rate, so don't expect a lot of free passes. Instead, anticipate the Orioles driving up pitch counts and then finding a hole in the defense anyway. Even with middling home runs numbers, Baltimore is 10th in team slugging percentage league-wide. They avoid strikeouts while stealing bases well too. The Orioles offense can punish pitchers in multiple ways.

On the mound, Baltimore is dealing with a clipped wing right now. All-Star closer, Felix Bautista, arguably the season's best reliever, is injured. He, and fellow All-Star Yennier Cano, spearhead a bullpen that leads the majors in WAR and FIP. Games are a little longer for the Orioles now, unless trade acquisition Jack Flaherty can put on a show Sunday afternoon. In a Baltimore uniform, Flaherty has given up 21 hits and 14 runs across 19.2 innings pitched. After debuting with a win, his last three outings have been losses. As a Cardinal this season (birds seem to really like him), Flaherty faced Arizona twice. He gave up 12 hits and seven runs during those 11.0 innings. There isn't a lot working in his favor for this game, but Flaherty is also a veteran with plenty of pennant chase experience. Most players in this game can't say the same.

Snakes Still Slithering Along

A tie for the final NL wild-card spot isn't as glamorous as Baltimore's position, but it's a date in the dance all the same. Changing that status from tied to outright will depend on the club's last 27 games. As with the Orioles, the offense carries the Diamondbacks forward. Particularly speed and contact, as Arizona ranks fourth in strikeout rate, third in stolen bases, and second in stolen base percentage. They also draw walks at a modest rate. The Snakes don't hit many homers, especially at home, but it doesn't matter. Their hitters typically cross the plate at some point anyway. August was a rough month, but no slump lasts forever.

If this team doesn't play in the postseason, pitching can take a lot of the blame. Well, NL Cy Young candidate Zac Gallen can probably be excluded from that narrative. At home, he levels up, bringing an 11-1 record and 1.79 ERA into this start after 13 home outings. That includes 103 strikeouts to 13 walks in 85.1 Chase Field innings. If Arizona's pitching falters in this game, it'll likely be because of a bullpen that's among the 10 worst units in WAR, FIP, and ERA.

Best Bets for this Game

Full-Game Side Bet

Insiders Status:

Rating:

With Zac Gallen getting the start in Chase Field, always expect Arizona to win. Only twice have the Diamondbacks lost his home starts, and in one of them, the bullpen gave up five runs late to lose it. Implosions like that are an anomaly, but Gallen stifling the opposing lineup is not.

So, it'll be on Arizona to give him run support if they want to take this game. Considering that they scored four against Flaherty the first time they faced him and three the second time, the Diamondbacks should race out to a lead in this one. By avoiding Baltimore's best bullpen arms, they'll probably plate a few more too. Don't be surprised if the Snakes win by multiple runs.

Prediction: Arizona Diamondbacks -1.5 (+160)

Arizona's Zac Gallen has a 1.79 home ERA this season. The Diamondbacks finished August scoring fewer than four runs per game and didn't break out when September started. If this game comes down to the Diamondbacks, the under will surely hit.
