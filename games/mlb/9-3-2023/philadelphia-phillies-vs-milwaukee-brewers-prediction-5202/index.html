Phillies Playoff Push

The Philadelphia Phillies are in a similar position as in 2022 when they earned a wildcard spot and raced to the National League Championship. This season, the Phillies are again behind the Braves in the NL East Division standings but are also at the top of the Wildcard standings. Heading into this series with the Brewers, the Phillies are 7-3 in their last ten games, all at home. They also have gotten fully healthy with the return of outfielder Cristian Pache and today's starter Ranger Suarez. In games one, the Phillies blew a 5-3 lead in the eighth on their way to a 7-5 loss. On Saturday, the Phillies fell 7-5 after leaving runners on second and third in the ninth.

The Phillies will give the ball to returning starter Ranger Suarez in the series finale against the Brewers. Suarez is just 2-6 on the season in 17 starts including nine quality starts. He has allowed 104 hits in 97.1 innings pitched with an ERA of 3.88. He has an 8.2 K/9 rate and a WHIP of 1.43. On the road, Suarez is 2-3 with a solid 2.76 ERA in eight starts. He has struck out 38 batters in 49 innings pitched this season. In his last five starts, he is 0-2 with a 3.99 ERA with 27 strikeouts in 29.1 innings pitched.

BrewCrew Heating Up

The Milwaukee Brewers are on top of the NL Central Division once again and are looking to win their second NL Central title in three seasons. Heading into this potential playoff preview with the Phillies, the Brewers have gone 9-2 after losing two straight to the Cubs to end a nine-game winning streak. In the series opener, the Brewers made it 10 wins in 12 games with a 7-5 win over the Phillies. On Saturday night, the Brewers made it 11 wins in 13 games with a 7-5 win over the Phillies. Closer Devin Williams struck out Trea Turner with runners on second and third in the ninth to preserve the win.

The Brewers will give the ball to lefty Wade Miley in the series finale on Sunday afternoon. Miley is 7-3 on the season in 18 starts including eight quality starts. He has allowed 80 hits in 93.2 innings pitched with an ERA of 3.17. He pitches to contact, with a K/9 rate of just 5.8. At home this season, Miley is 4-1 with an ERA of 3.31 in nine starts including 33 strikeouts in 49 innings pitched. In his last five starts, Miley is 1-1 with an ERA of 3.46 while allowing 21 hits in 26 innings pitched.

Key Injuries: Outfielders Blake Perkins and Jesse Winker are out.
