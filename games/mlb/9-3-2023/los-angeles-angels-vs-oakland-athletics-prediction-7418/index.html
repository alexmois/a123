The Oakland Athletics (40-95) will be going for the series win when they face the Los Angeles Angels (64-71) in the final game of their three-game series on Sunday afternoon.

Kyle Muller, 1-5, 7.67 ERA, will get the start for the Athletics. The Angels will counter with Tyler Anderson, 5-6, 5.58 ERA.

Los Angeles and Oakland split their last 10 meetings.

**This preview was written before Saturday’s game was played**

Angels Going For Second Win In Three Games

The Angels followed up their win over the Phillies with a loss to the Athletics in the first game of the series. They will try to bounce back from the loss with a win on Sunday, which will give them their second win in their last three games.

Los Angeles is averaging 4.74 runs per game. Their .250 batting average is 15th in the league. Their .323 on base percentage is also 15th, while their .435 slugging percentage is seventh.

Shohei Ohtani has been the big bat for the Angels. He leads the team with a .307 batting average, 44 home runs, and 95 RBI.

Los Angeles’ pitching hasn’t been good, with the team giving up 5.13 runs per game. Opponents have a .252 batting average against the Angels, which is 22nd in the league. Their 4.72 ERA is 25th, while their 1.41 WHIP is 26th.

In his last start, Anderson gave up seven hits and six runs in five innings, leading to a 12-7 loss to Philadelphia. They will need a better performance from him if they want to win this game.

Athletics Going For Third Win In Four Games

The Athletics are playing well at the moment and they’ve won two of their last three games. They will try to keep the momentum going with another win over the Angels, which will give them their third win in their last four games.

Oakland is averaging 3.64 runs per game. Their .224 batting average is 30th in the league. Their .301 on base percentage is 27th, while their .368 slugging percentage is 30th.

Esteury Ruiz leads the Athletics with a .251 batting average, while Brent Rooker leads the team with 22 home runs and 55 RBI.

Oakland’s pitching hasn’t been good, with the team giving up 5.87 runs per game. Opponents have a .270 batting average against the Athletics, which is 28th in the league. Their 5.68 ERA is 30th, as is their 1.53 WHIP.

In his last start, Muller gave up nine hits and six runs in four innings, leading to a 7-0 loss to Seattle. They will need a better effort from him if they want to get the win.
