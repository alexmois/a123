The Boston Red Sox and Kansas City Royals will take the field on Sunday at Kauffman Stadium. First pitch is scheduled for 1:10 p.m. CT.

Boston (69-65 SU and 67-67 RL) is starting left-hander Chris Sale. The 34-year-old is 5-3 with a 4.75 ERA and a 1.18 WHIP in 15 outings this season.

Kansas City (41-94 SU and 54-81 RL) is countering with righty Zack Greinke. The 39-year-old is 1-13 with a 5.28 ERA and a 1.27 WHIP in his 24 appearances (23 starts).

Is Boston's season over?

The Red Sox have been in a tailspin, losing six of their previous seven games heading into play on Saturday. They were thoroughly outplayed by the Royals on Friday, losing 13-2. Can this team save face with a better showing in the series finale?

Boston scores 4.96 runs per game (8th) and hits .265 (3rd) with a .769 OPS (5th). It's launched 159 homers (15th) and stolen 91 bases (18th) in 2023. The Red Sox pitching staff has compiled a 4.48 ERA (19th) and a 1.34 WHIP (19th) with 37 quality starts (26th).

Kansas City has heart

Kansas City embarrassed Boston in game one, scoring seven runs in the first three innings and piling on six more runs in the sixth inning. The victory snapped a six-game losing streak, but can it sustain momentum?

The Royals average 3.93 runs (29th) and hit .240 (21st) with a .685 OPS (28th). They've hit 128 homers (28th) and stolen 130 bases (4th) this season. The KC pitching staff has posted a 5.23 ERA (28th) and a 1.42 WHIP (26th) with 29 quality starts (29th).

Full-Game Total Pick

Insiders Status:

Rating:

The Red Sox are due to break out offensively after a string of losing performances. They're not one of the best teams in baseball, but their lineup can be deadly. Greinke has consistently struggled on the mound in 2023, surrendering four or more runs in nine of his outings. He has also failed to last past the fifth inning in the majority of his starts. He and the KC bullpen (5.30 ERA) will find it tough to get outs on Sunday.

The Royals could also contribute more runs to the total than you might assume. They had their best month at the plate in August (.257/.304/.417/.721), scoring 4.5 runs per game. They're scoring a half-run more per game in the second half of the season. Sale can be frustrating at times and the Red Sox bullpen is far from perfect (4.30 ERA and a 1.40 WHIP) — I wouldn't be shocked if KC scored four or five runs tomorrow.
