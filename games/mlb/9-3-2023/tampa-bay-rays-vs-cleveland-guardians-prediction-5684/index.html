A pair of teams in second place will cross paths again, with the Cleveland Guardians (65-70; before Saturday's game) hosting the Tampa Bay Rays (82-53) on Sunday afternoon. It will be the last time they face each other this season. Through four battles, the series was tied 2-2. The final first pitch is scheduled for 4:10 p.m. EDT. What will the action from Progressive Field deliver?

 

 

*Published before Saturday's game

Rays Racing for the Top

Will the Rays overcome their 1.5-game deficit in the AL East? Can they take the American League's top spot? Those are questions that will be answered during this final month. Tampa scored 6.08 runs per game in August (3rd), a level that will go a long way toward a prime position. After all, it led them to a 17-8 month. For the season, the Rays are sixth in batting average, fourth in OBP, and fourth in slugging percentage. They're also second in stolen bases and fourth in homers. Even without Wander Franco, this is an elite bunch.

Tampa's pitching staff has had to withstand plenty of absences all season too. Still, the Rays are fourth in team ERA after Friday. With no starter decided for this game, manager Kevin Cash may be turning to one of his patented bullpen games. The Rays' stable hasn't been as elite as usual, ranking 15th in ERA, 16th in FIP, and 18th in WAR. A top-10 walk rate is offset by a bottom-10 strikeout rate from the Rays' relievers. They may have a long day on Sunday.

Can Guardians Gain on Twins?

The greatest perk of playing in the AL Central is that a losing record after 135 games hasn't eliminated them from the division race yet. However, if this offense doesn't have their best month of the season, the Guardians probably won't see a playoff series. Cleveland finished 27th in runs per game for August, averaging fewer than four each time out. For the season, they're 26th in runs per game. A great strikeout rate (2nd) and base-stealing numbers mean nothing because they have little power. The Guardians have MLB's fewest homers, leading to a slugging percentage that's ranked 27th. They also don't draw walks often, so they're 24th in OBP. Josh Naylor, the team's slugging percentage leader, is still out. Cleveland's offensive outlook isn't promising.

Elite performance on the mound has kept the Guardians afloat. Only two bullpens own a lower ERA than Cleveland this season. As for the rotation, young arms have delivered through injuries and trades that should've depleted the group. Xzavion Curry is up on Sunday, trying to get revenge on the Rays. When he faced them on August 11, Curry got tagged for five runs in 5.0 innings. He may bring a 3.00 home ERA into this start, but August was an ugly month everywhere he went. Across those 21.2 August innings, Curry gave up 17 runs and 28 hits. Let's see if September will be any better.

Even without knowing how the Rays plan on attacking this game pitching-wise, they have the clear edge. Whoever Tampa tosses out there will take on a Cleveland offense with no power threat. The Guardians struggle to score against everyone, so don't expect them to light up the scoreboard in this one.

Meanwhile, Cleveland is trusting Xzavion Curry against the Rays again, at least to begin the game. Considering that he gave up five runs last time, that doesn't bode well. The last time he was on the mound was his worst outing of the year, ending with six runs surrendered over 2.0 innings. As for Tampa Bay, their offense has been on fire lately and will probably pounce on this version of Curry. Expect domination from the Rays to close the series.

Full-Game Total Pick

Insiders Status:

Rating:

This pitching matchup won't feature anything special. The known element is Xzavion Curry, who Tampa has already scratched five runs against in 2023. Curry has a 7.65 ERA in his past five starts. Taking on a Rays bunch that is a top-five run-scoring team for the season, he'll probably have another rough day.

What's unknown is how the Rays will pitch. If it's a bullpen game, Tampa's depth isn't what it has been in years past. That's a mediocre group that Cleveland will score on a few times. If they find a starter somewhere, it probably won't be an arm that strikes fear in MLB lineups. The Guardians can also hit whoever that may be.
