The Seattle Mariners face the New York Mets with both teams on opposite ends of the standings to close out the season. The Mariners have a 77-58 record and with seven wins in their last 10 games, they have leaped into first place in the American League West Division. The Mets have a 62-73 record and have the second-worst record in the National League East Division. For the final game of the series, the Mariners will turn to George Kirby as their starting pitcher who has allowed only seven runs in his last three starts while the Mets will turn to Tylor Megill who has allowed only six runs in his last three starts.

Mariners hope Kirby and lineup end series strong

The Mariners are having a great season and hope to win the division for the first time since 2001. George Kirby will start the upcoming game and he hopes to build off a strong season on the mound as he's allowed only 59 runs in 156.1 innings pitched while tossing 142 strikeouts. Moreover, with a 31.9 Chase Percentage, a 2.3 Walk Rate, and a 3.79 expected ERA, opponents are struggling to make contact and are barely plating baserunners against Kirby this year.

Kirby has stepped up but the lineup has carried the Mariners, scoring 4.78 runs per game with 47 runs in the last eight games. Julio Rodrigues is slashing .288/.347/.484 with 24 home runs and 261 total bases while J.P. Crawford is slashing .268/.386/.428 with 13 home runs and 180 total bases but the entire lineup has stepped up. Cal Raleigh is slashing .229/.308/.468 with 25 home runs and 192 total bases while Teoscar Hernandez and Eugenio Suarez have added 41 home runs and 429 total bases to round out the lineup, making the Mariners tough to stop at the plate.

Mets hope Megill and lineup step up

The Mets are having a season to forget and hope to avoid finishing the year with the worst record in the division. Tylor Megill has stepped up in recent starts but is having a rough season, allowing 66 runs in 97 innings pitched. Additionally, with a .296 expected opponent Batting Average, a .498 expected opponent Slugging Percentage, and a 6.39 expected ERA, opponents are making contact and powering the ball against Megill to easily plate baserunners.

Megill has struggled but the lineup has let the Mets down this year, scoring only 4.33 runs per game. Pete Alonso is slashing .220/.325/.514 with 39 home runs and 238 total bases while Brandon Nimmo is slashing .260/.355/.440 with 20 home runs and 218 total bases but the rest of the lineup has struggled. Only four active batters have an OPS+ over 100 and opposing pitchers can limit the top-heavy lineup as a result.
