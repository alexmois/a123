The Detroit Tigers and Chicago White Sox will wrap up their three-game series on Sunday at Guaranteed Rate Field. First pitch is at 1:10 p.m. CT.

Detroit (60-74 SU and 69-65 RL) is starting left-hander Tarik Skubal. The 26-year-old is 3-3 with a 3.93 ERA and a 1.07 WHIP in his ten outings this year.

Chicago (53-81 SU and 66-68 RL) is countering with right-hander Michael Kopech. The 27-year-old is 5-12 with a 5.08 ERA and a 1.53 WHIP in his 25 starts.

Don't write off the Tigers just yet

The Tigers dropped three of four games to the Yankees in their previous series but scored a 4-2 win on Friday night. Can they go on a longer run and make some late noise in the AL Central title race?

Detroit scores 3.93 runs per game (28th) and hits .233 (28th) with a .678 OPS (29th). It's launched 137 long balls (23rd) and stolen 62 bases (28th) in the 2023 season. The pitching staff has compiled a 4.52 ERA (20th) and a 1.28 WHIP (16th) with 33 quality starts (28th).

The Tigers will hand the game ball to Skubal on Sunday afternoon in Chicago. The young southpaw surrendered three runs (two earned) to the Yankees in his last start, recording nine strikeouts in six innings. He is 1-2 with a 7.20 ERA and a 1.67 WHIP in four road games this year.

Chicago could use some fresh faces

Chicago lost two of three games versus Baltimore in its last series. The Southsiders have lost or split their last five series.

The White Sox average 4.16 runs (25th) and hit .241 (20th) with a .688 OPS (27th). They've hit 149 home runs (19th) and stolen 79 bases (22nd) this year. The pitching staff has posted a 4.88 ERA (26th) and a 1.41 WHIP (25th) with 41 quality starts (23rd).

Kopech will take the hill for the home team in the series finale. In his last start, the fourth-year pro gave up four runs on seven hits in four innings to the Orioles. He faced Detroit on June 4, holding the Tigers to two runs with nine strikeouts in seven frames. Kopech is 1-6 with a 4.43 ERA and a 1.39 WHIP in 13 home starts this year.

I predict a Chicago win on Sunday, with Kopech leading the way.

The right-hander sports a 3.73 ERA and a 1.26 WHIP in 11 day games this season. After a couple of below-average months, I feel confident he'll get off on the right foot in his first September outing. The Tigers have endured three consecutive losing months and don't hit consistently on the road (.224 BA/.291 OBP/.376 SLG/.667 OPS). They average just 3.8 runs under the sun, too.

Skubal has struggled a lot on the road this year — I don't expect that to change on Sunday. Bet on the Chicago bats to give Kopech just enough run support to pick up the home victory.

Full-Game Total Pick

Insiders Status:

Rating:

The under is 36-28-2 in Detroit's road games and 34-30-2 in Chicago's home games. It's also 22-14-1 in the White Sox's division games and 24-17 in Detroit's division games.

I'm betting on a low-scoring game between two low-scoring clubs on Sunday. Kopech and Skubal won't have to dominate to keep the offenses in check — these teams are plenty familiar with each other. Skubal has struggled on the road, but he's been very good in day games (2.88 ERA and a 1.04 WHIP in five starts), too. Kopech is a better pitcher than he's looked in the dog days of summer, and I'm betting he will end the regular season with a couple of decent performances.

The Tigers' bats rarely show up away from Comerica Park and the White Sox have failed to produce at the plate no matter the venue. Bet on more of the same tomorrow at Guaranteed Rate Field!
