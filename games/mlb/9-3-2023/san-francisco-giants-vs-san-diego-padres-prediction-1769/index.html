The San Francisco Giants (70-66, 66-68 RL, 57-74-3 O/U) and the San Diego Padres (64-73, 66-70 RL, 57-71-8 O/U) conclude a four-game divisional series on Sunday afternoon. The Giants are going with Alex Cobb. The Padres will reply with Seth Lugo. The Padres lead the season series is 5-4.

Cobb Nearly Tosses No-Hitter, Collects Win

Alex Cobb will take the mound on Sunday. Cobb came one out away from a no-hitter in his previous start and ended up allowing a run to ruin the complete game shutout. The right-hander was horrible in his opening four outings of August but rebounded with two solid outings to conclude the month and has issued a 3.57 ERA and a 1.30 WHIP accompanied by a 7-5 record in 141.1 innings pitched on the season. Cobb allowed three runs in five innings in the only meeting against the Padres this season and has produced a 4.38 ERA and a 1-1 record in five career meetings.

San Francisco has rebounded from a slump by winning two of three against the Reds prior to this series. They now stand in the last wildcard spot and have a huge series against the Cubs next. The Giants offense is weak but if they can produce with more than consistency they should be able to qualify for the postseason. They stand at 5-5 in their last ten clashes.

JD Davis has struggled against the Padres this season, hitting only .217 with a .650 OPS. The 30-year-old slugger has 15 homers with 60 RBIs and a .722 OPS on the season. Davis only has three RBIs in his last 10 games. He was out of the lineup on Friday due to a hand injury and is questionable for this one.

San Francisco is posting an average of 4.32 runs on the year, marking them 21st. The pitching has been sharp and has reported a 3.92 team ERA, placing them ninth.

Lugo Starts, Padres Losses Continue to Pile

Seth Lugo will get the nod to conclude the series. Lugo was solid last time out, allowing only three runs (two earned) in six innings against the Cardinals, resulting in a no-decision in a bout the Padres lost. The veteran has conceded two or fewer runs in four consecutive outings and has recorded a 3.67 ERA and a 1.22 WHIP accompanied by a 5-6 record in 115.1 innings. Lugo held the Giants to one run in five innings in the only encounter this season and has a 3.03 ERA and a 2-2 record in 29.2 career innings. 

San Diego is out of postseason contention after losing three of their last four series. They dropped two of three road bouts against the Cardinals from Monday to Wednesday despite scoring 13 runs, and have collected nine runs in the opening two bouts of this series. The squad is only 3-7 in their last ten decisions.

Fernando Tatis Jr. has been a tough out against the Giants this season. He only has five RBIs but has a stellar 1.035 OPS against the rivals. The 24-year-old is having a productive season, delivering 21 homers with 69 RBIs and a .792 OPS. Tatis Jr. has four RBIs in his last ten games.

The Padres are only averaging 4.49 runs per game, ranking them 16th. The pitching has been a bright spot, posting a 3.84 team ERA, positioning them seventh.
