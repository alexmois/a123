New York Yankees (66-69) vs. Houston Astros (77-59)

Another Sunday Night Baseball is upon us, so we are breaking down the marquee American League showdown from Minute Maid Park in Houston, Texas, to get you the best Yankees vs. Astros betting pick.  

The Yankees and Astros wrap up a three-game series in Houston. New York won the opener 6-2, while the middle game has been excluded from the analysis. The Astros are listed as -155 moneyline favorites for Sunday’s closer. 

The Yanks close down a ten-game road trip

The New York Yankees moved to 66-69 on the season following Friday’s victory at Minute Maid Park. After taking one out of three games from the Tampa Bay Rays and three out of four from the Detroit Tigers, the Yanks continued their ten-game road trip with a big win over the Astros. New York took Justin Verlander deep twice in the first inning and never looked back. 

The Yankees are slashing .192/.273/.460 with five doubles and a whopping 16 home runs in the last seven days and 198 at-bats. Gleyber Torres, Anthony Volpe, and DJ LeMahieu have slugged three homers apiece during that span, while Aaron Judge has gone 4-for-20 with a couple of long balls. Torres missed Friday’s clash against the Astros due to a back injury and is listed day-to-day. 

Michael King will toe the rubber Sunday in Houston, and the 28-year-old right-hander is 3-5 with a 2.96 ERA, 1.15 WHIP, and 87/25 K/BB ratio in three starts and 40 relief appearances (73 innings pitched) this season. King will make his third straight turn through the Yankees rotation. He’s pitched 6.2 innings over his previous two outings while allowing just one unearned run on four hits and a pair of walks. Last Tuesday, King tossed four scoreless innings at Comerica Park in Detroit, scattering three hits and punching out five.

The Astros continue to chase the playoffs

The Houston Astros fell to 77-59 on the season following Friday’s loss against the Yankees, putting an end to a five-game winning streak. The Astros were second in the NL West on Saturday morning, as the 76-58 Seattle Mariners had a slightly better winning percentage, while the Texas Rangers were just one game behind Houston. 

Over the last seven days, the Astros are hitting a splendid .343/.423/.596 with 14 doubles, three triples, and ten home runs across 198 at-bats. Yordan Alvarez has gone 10-for-20 with three doubles, two dingers, and eight RBI in his last five games, while Jeremy Pena is 10-for-22 with four doubles, two triples, and four RBI over his previous five games. 

Cristian Javier will be on the bump Sunday, and the 26-year-old right-hander is 9-2 with a 4.66 ERA, 1.31 WHIP, and 119/51 K/BB ratio in 25 starts (131.1 IP) in 2023. Javier struggled mightily in August, posting a bloated 6.17 ERA and 7.42 FIP in five starts and 23.1 innings of work. Last Monday, he gave up four earned runs on six hits and a staggering six walks through four innings in a no-decision against the Boston Red Sox (Houston eventually won 13-5).

Full-Game Side Bet

Insiders Status:

Rating:

Michael King met the Astros twice in August, throwing 2.2 scoreless frames in relief. The current Astros are 4-for-19 with a solo home run against him. On the other side, Cristian Javier started in a 4-3 defeat at Yankee Stadium on August 3 and yielded three earned runs on four hits and three walks through 4.2 frames in a no-decision. Still, the current Yankees are only 8-for-51 with three homers and four RBI against Javier. 

I like the Yankees in this spot even though Michael King doesn’t have enough stamina to last for too long. The Yankees’ bullpen has registered a 3.74 ERA and 4.12 FIP over the last ten days and 33.2 innings of work, while the Astros’ bullpen has tossed 36 frames in that span, posting a troublesome 4.50 ERA and 4.84 FIP. If Cristian Javier continues to struggle with his command, he’ll be in big trouble. The Yanks don’t hit for average and swing for the fences, so walking people is not optimal at all. 

Prediction: Take the New York Yankees (+142)
