The Minnesota Twins (70-65) will be going for the series win when they face the Texas Rangers (75-59) in the final game of their three-game series on Sunday afternoon.

Kenta Maeda, 3-7, 4.69 ERA, will get the start for the Twins. The Rangers will counter with Jon Gray, 8-7, 3.79 ERA.

Minnesota is 6-4 in its last 10 games against Texas.

**This preview was written before Saturday’s game was played**

Twins Trying To Increase Division Led

The Twins are playing well at the moment and they’ve won five of their last eight games. They now have a five-game lead over Cleveland in the AL Central standings and will try to increase their lead with another win over the Rangers, which will give them their sixth win in their last nine games and the series win.

Minnesota is averaging 4.50 runs per game. Their .239 batting average is 23rd in the league. Their .319 on base percentage is 17th, while their .421 slugging percentage is 11th.

Carlos Correa leads the Twins with a .222 batting average and 57 RBI, while Max Kepler leads the team with 21 home runs.

Minnesota’s pitching has been good, with the team giving up 4.13 runs per game. Opponents have a .237 batting average against the Twins, which is fifth in the league. Their 3.92 ERA is seventh, while their 1.20 WHIP is third.

In his last start, Maeda gave up seven hits and six runs in four innings, leading to a 10-6 win over Cleveland. They will need a significantly better performance from him if they want to get the win.

Rangers Going for Fourth Win In Seven Games

The Rangers didn’t play well over the past week and they split their last six games. Fortunately for them, they are only one game behind Houston and Seattle in the AL West standings and they will try to move into a tie for first with a win over the Twins, which will give them their fourth win in their last seven games.

Texas is averaging 5.51 runs per game. Their .267 batting average is second in the league. Their .337 on base percentage is third, as is their .453 slugging percentage.

Nathaniel Lowe leads the Rangers with a .282 batting average, while Adolis Garcia leads the team with 32 home runs and 97 RBI.

Texas’ pitching has been good, with the team giving up 4.25 runs per game. Opponents have a .241 batting average against the Rangers, which is ninth in the league. Their 4.09 ERA is 15th, while their 1.23 WHIP is eighth.

In his last start, Gray gave up four hits and three runs in six innings, leading to a 4-3 win over the Mets. They will need another solid start from him if they want to win this game.
