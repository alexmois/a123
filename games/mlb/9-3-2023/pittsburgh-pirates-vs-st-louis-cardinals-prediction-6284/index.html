It’s the conclusion of a series between a pair of NL Central teams that are playing out the string on the diamond in the Gateway City. The Pittsburgh Pirates are on the road as they finish up a three-game series with the St. Louis Cardinals Sunday afternoon. In the opening game of the series Friday night, Pittsburgh broke open a tie game late en route to a 4-2, 10-inning win, to draw first blood in the series. The Pirates followed that up with a 7-6 win on Saturday. Will the Pirates earn the win on getaway day, or will the Cardinals find a way to get a home victory?

Pirates Look to Maintain Recent Winning Ways

Pittsburgh prevailed in extra innings Friday night to run their win streak to four games heading into the middle game of the series here. The Pirates made it 5 straight on Saturday, winning 7-6 to improve to 63-73. They stand fourth in the NL Central, 13.5 games behind the Brewers for the top spot. They were seven games behind the Reds, Giants and Diamondbacks, who were tied for the final NL wild-card spot in the playoff picture. On Friday night, Pittsburgh finished with seven hits with Liover Peguero (two runs) the lone player to record two in the contest. Mitch Keller pitched well, allowing one run on five hits with three walks and eight strikeouts over six innings, but didn’t factor in the decision. Colin Selby (2-0) earned the win as he threw a scoreless ninth inning, allowing one hit with no walks or strikeouts. David Bednar worked the 10th, allowing one run (none earned) on no hits with no walks or strikeouts, for his 30th save of the season.

Johan Oviedo is on the bump for the Pirates as he makes his 28th start of the season in this contest. He is 8-13 with a 4.20 ERA, a 1.276 WHIP, 64 walks and 134 strikeouts over 154.1 innings of work on the year. Oviedo earned the win in his last start, which came against the Royals on the road Monday night. He threw a complete game shutout, allowing no runs on two hits with two walks and five strikeouts in a game the Pirates won 5-0. In his last three starts, Oviedo is 2-0 with a 2.50 ERA, a 1.06 WHIP, eight walks and 13 strikeouts over 18 innings of work. Oviedo makes his fifth career start against the Cardinals in this contest. He comes in 1-2 with a 2.45 ERA, a 1.136 WHIP, four walks and 20 strikeouts over 22 innings of work against them. Oviedo is 1-5 with a 3.39 ERA, a 1.197 WHIP, 19 walks and 54 strikeouts over 61 innings in 16 career appearances, nine starts, at Busch Stadium.

St. Louis Cardinals Stuck in NL Central Cellar

St. Louis saw their two-game win streak snapped as they lost for the fifth time in seven games by dropping the opener of this series. The Cardinals lost again on Saturday and fell to 58-78 and stand in the basement of the NL Central, 18.5 games behind the Brewers for the top spot. They were 12 games behind the Giants and Diamondbacks for the final wild-card spot in the National League playoff picture. On Friday night, St. Louis finished with six hits with Willson Contreras (run, RBI) the lone player recording two. Contreras hit his 16th homer of the season for the Cardinals in a losing effort. Dakota Hudson didn’t factor in the decision as he threw seven innings, allowing one run on three hits with two walks and no strikeouts. JoJo Romero (4-2) took the loss in relief as he allowed three runs (two earned) on four hits with one walk and two strikeouts over 1.2 innings of work.

Zack Thompson is slated to get the start for the Cardinals, according to MLB.com. The lefty makes his 5th start of the season in this contest. He comes in 3-5 with a 3.92 ERA, 18 walks and 47 strikeouts over 39 innings of work on the year.
