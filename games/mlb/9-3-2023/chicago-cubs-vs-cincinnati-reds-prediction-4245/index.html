Weekend Showdown for Cubbies

The Cubs have made a big turnaround in the second half of the season to get right back in the NL playoff picture, both in their division and the NL Wildcard race. Chicago comes into this series with the Reds having won five out of their last seven games against two other NL Central Division teams, the Pirates and the Brewers. In game one of this series, the Cubs kept the winning going with a 6-2 win in the opener of their day-night doubleheader. In the second game of the doubleheader, the Cubs blew a 2-1 lead in the bottom of the ninth and blew the chance at a doubleheader sweep. On Saturday, the Cubs coughed up two runs in the bottom of the ninth and blew their second straight ninth-inning lead in a 2-1 loss.

The Cubs will close out this critical series against Cincinnati with veteran righty James Taillon on the hill. Taillon is 7-9 in 24 starts this season including five quality starts. He has allowed 132 hits in 121.2 innings with an ERA of 5.62. On the road this season, Taillon is 4-3 with a 5.34 ERA with 44 strikeouts in 55.2 innings pitched. He is 1-0 against the Reds this season in two starts. He has gone 9.2 innings while allowing 13 hits in the two starts with ten strikeouts. In his last five games, Taillon has gone 1-3 with an inflated 6.51 ERA in that time. He's given up 30 hits in 27.2 innings pitched with 24 strikeouts.

Reds Still in the Running

The Reds are still alive in the National League playoff picture but they have fallen on hard times recently. Heading into this series, the young Reds have lost five of six games and lost critical ground in both the NL Central Division standings and the NL Wildcard standings. In game one of Friday's doubleheader with the Cubs, the Reds fell 6-2 to fall four games in back of the Cubs in the standings. In game two of the doubleheader, the Reds rallied for two runs in the ninth to take a walk-off 3-2 win. In game three on Saturday, the Reds rallied for two in the ninth to take a 2-1 win. The Reds are now just two games in back of the Cubs in the NL Central.

The Reds will hand the ball to lefty Brandon Williamson to close out this series with the Cubs. Williamson is 4-4 in 19 starts this season including six quality starts. He has allowed 89 hits in 98.2 innings pitched with an ERA of 4.20. At home this season, Williamson is 2-2 with an ERA of 4.24 in nine starts. He has allowed 45 hits in 51 innings pitched with 45 strikeouts. Williamson has faced the Cubs twice this season without a decision and given up 10 hits in 8.2 innings pitched. In his last five starts, Williamson has gone 1-1 with an ERA of 2.70 while striking out 32 batters in 30 innings pitched.

Key Injuries: 2B Jonathan Indian, SS Matt McLain, DH Joey Votto are out.
