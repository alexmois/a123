The Toronto Blue Jays (74-62, 3rd AL East) are in Mile High City on Sunday to play the third and final game of an interleague series against the Colorado Rockies (50-85, 5th NL West). Toronto defeated Colorado 13-9 in a slugfest to start the series while Colorado won game two 8-5. Toronto is 10 ½ games behind first in the AL East, and just 1 ½ games behind the third and final AL wildcard qualifying spot. Colorado has lost 10 of its last 12 games. The Rockies are last in the NL West and on the verge of being eliminated from postseason contention. Toronto will play without three pitchers as well as position players Bo Bichette, Matt Chapman, Otto Lopez and Danny Jansen. Colorado will play without seven pitchers and left fielder Kris Bryant.

Injuries are taking their toll on Toronto

Over the last week, Toronto has placed Bo Bichette and Matt Chapman on the injured list while starting catcher Danny Jansen fractured a finger in Friday's victory over Colorado and will miss an undetermined amount of time. Toronto is 5th in team batting average at .260 and 12th in slugging percentage with .420. However, Bichette had a team-leading .314 batting average with 18 home runs and 62 RBIs before being injured and Matt Chapman had 15 home runs and 50 RBI before being placed in the IL. Leading Toronto in home runs is Vladimir Guerrero Jr with 20 and the first baseman also has the team lead in RBI with 80.

Toronto has a very strong pitching staff that is second-best in baseball with a 3.71 ERA and is seventh in WHIP with 1.23. Toronto's starting rotation has 59 Quality Starts which is eighth and the pitching staff is holding opposing hitters to an average of .239 which is seventh lowest. Chris Bassitt is the leader in victories for Toronto with 13. Toronto will hand the ball to Kevin Gausman on Sunday. This season the right-hander is 10-8 with an ERA of 3.80 and a team-leading 200 to strikeouts.
