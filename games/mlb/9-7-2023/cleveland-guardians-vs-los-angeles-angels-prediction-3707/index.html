Cleveland Guardians (66-73) vs. Los Angeles Angels (64-75)

The Major League Baseball betting action goes on Thursday, September 7, with a small six-game card including the American League clash from Angel Stadium in Anaheim, California, so we have prepared the best Guardians vs. Angels betting pick.  

The Guardians and Angels open a four-game series in Anaheim. They lock horns for the second time this season, and the Guardians won two out of three contests at home in May. 

The Guardians are losing the pace in the AL Central

Wednesday’s games have been excluded from the analysis, and the 66-73 Cleveland Guardians hosted the Minnesota Twins for the closer of a three-game set. The Guardians were looking to avoid a sweep and put an end to their three-game losing streak. 

Cleveland was seven games behind the AL Central-leading Twins on Wednesday morning. The Guardians are slashing a mediocre .243/.303/.399 with 27 extra-base hits and 15 home runs over the last couple of weeks and 474 at-bats while posting an awful 5.93 ERA, 1.56 WHIP, and .280 batting average against on the pitching side of things (124 innings pitched). 

Cal Quantrill will take the hill Thursday at Angel Stadium, and the 28-year-old right-hander is 2-6 with a bloated 6.16 ERA, 1.51 WHIP, and 40/25 K/BB ratio in 14 starts (73 IP) this season. After spending nearly two months on the IL, Quantrill returned to the Guardians rotation on the first day of September. He pitched well in a no-decision against the Tampa Bay Rays, allowing two earned runs on a couple of hits and three walks across six innings of work. 

Quantrill will meet the Halos for the second time in 2023. Back on May 13, he tossed six frames of a three-run, six-hit ball in a no-decision (Cleveland eventually won 8-6). The current Angels are 15-for-43 with four doubles and two homers against Quantrill.

The Angels have been disastrous lately

The Los Angeles Angels played the Baltimore Orioles on Wednesday night, hoping to avoid a sweep in a three-game home series. Last Tuesday, the Angels suffered a 5-4 defeat in ten innings, extending their losing streak to five games. The slumping Halos have only won three of their previous 15 games overall and were 13 games behind the final wild-card spot in the American League on Wednesday morning. 

The Angels are hitting .247/.332/.407 with 29 extra-base hits and 13 home runs in the past two weeks and 450 at-bats. Their pitching staff has registered an ugly 5.95 ERA, 1.57 WHIP, and .277 batting average against over those two weeks.  

Griffin Canning will get the starting call Thursday, and the 27-year-old right-hander is 7-5 with a 4.30 ERA, 1.19 WHIP, and 117/28 K/BB ratio in 18 starts and a couple of relief appearances ( 104.2 IP) in 2023. Canning has done an excellent job over his previous three outings and 16.1 frames of work, yielding just four earned runs on 11 hits and a couple of walks while punching out 21. He’ll meet Cleveland for the first time this season, and the current Guardians are 7-for-21 with three doubles and three round-trippers versus Canning.

Shohei Ohtani has missed the first two contests of the Orioles Series due to an oblique injury. He’s considered day-to-day, and Mike Trout (wrist) is on the IL, but I still like the Angels in this spot. Although the Guardians have a solid record against Griffin Canning, the Halos righty has pitched at a high level over the last couple of weeks. 

Over the last ten days, the Guardians have registered a .690 OPS and 88 wRC+ against right-handed pitchers, whereas the Angels have recorded a .778 OPS and 109 wRC+ versus the righties. Of course, I’m a bit worried about the Angels’ relief pitching. The Halos ‘pen has compiled a brutal 9.79 ERA and 6.52 FIP in the last ten days and 26.2 innings of work. On the other side, the Guardians’ bullpen has thrown 46.2 frames in that span while tallying a pedestrian 5.40 ERA and 5.27 FIP.
