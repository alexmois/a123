The Miami Marlins (71-67) will be going for the series win when they face the Los Angeles Dodgers (84-53) in the final game of their three-game series on Thursday night.

Braxton Garrett, 8-5, 3.86 ERA, will get the start for the Marlins. The Dodgers have not announced their starter for the game.

Los Angeles is 7-3 in its last 10 games against Miami.

**This preview was written before Wednesday’s game was played**

Dodgers Trying To End Slump

The Dodgers aren’t playing well at the moment and they’ve lost four of their last five games. They will try to turn things around with a win over the Marlins, which will give them their second win in their last six games.

Los Angeles is averaging 5.58 runs per game. Their .256 batting average is 11th in the league. Their .340 on base percentage is second, as is their .457 slugging percentage.

Freddie Freeman leads the Dodgers with a .335 batting average, while Mookie Betts leads the team with 38 home runs and 99 RBI.

Los Angeles’ pitching has been good, with the team giving up 4.39 runs per game. Opponents have a .243 batting average against the Dodgers, which is 11th in the league. Their 4.12 ERA is 15th, while their 1.27 WHIP is 13th.

They will need a good performance from their pitchers if they want to win this game.

Max Muncy (Shoulder) and Austin Barnes (Head) are questionable for this game.

Marlins Trying To Move Into Final Wildcard Spot

The Marlins played well over the past week and they’ve won six of their last eight games. They are half-a-game behind Cincinnati in the NL Wildcard standings and will try to displace the Reds with a win over the Dodgers, which will give them their seventh win in their last nine games and the series win.

Miami is averaging 4.09 runs per game. Their .260 batting average is sixth in the league. Their .315 on base percentage is 20th, as is their .404 slugging percentage.

Luis Arraez leads the Marlins with a .355 batting average, while Jorge Soler leads the team with 35 home runs and 71 RBI.

Miami’s pitching has been good, with the team giving up 4.39 runs per game. Opponents have a .243 batting average against the Marlins, which is 11th in the league. Their 4.15 ERA is 12th, while their 1.27 WHIP is 13th.

In his last start, Garrett gave up three hits and one run in six innings, leading to a 6-1 win over Washington. They will need a similar effort from him if they want to get the win.
