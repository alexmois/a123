There's quite a disparity on paper between the Atlanta Braves (85-45) and the Colorado Rockies (49-82). Atlanta took all four meetings between the clubs in June. However, each side has been competitive against playoff teams lately, and Coors Field can quickly humble any visitors. Denver's MLB park will be the venue again for Tuesday's round two. With a scheduled 8:40 p.m. EDT first pitch, the top of the National League will take on the bottom once again. Here's how it should go down.

 

 

Braves Bracing for October

Beginning the week with a sizable 12.5-game NL East lead, the Atlanta Braves will probably cruise to another division title. The Orioles, Dodgers, and Rays are lurking, but Atlanta seems destined for MLB's best record this season too. It begins with an offense that is leading the league in scoring. They were one of four teams scoring more than six runs per game in August as of Monday morning. The Braves rule the MLB leaderboard in all three slash line categories, OPS, and home runs. Atlanta also owns a top-10 strikeout rate, stolen base total, and stolen base success rate. Led by Matt Olson (NL HR leader) and Ronald Acuna Jr. (NL MVP favorite), Atlanta's lineup stresses most pitching staffs. 

Don't overlook Atlanta's arms, who have allowed the fourth-fewest runs per game this year. The Braves have a bullpen ranked top-three in WAR, FIP, and ERA. In the rotation, their pitchers are also dominant. Veteran Charlie Morton is lined up for Tuesday's start, trying to collect a fourth straight winning decision. He can also rebound from a five-run (three earned) performance across 5.0 innings against Colorado in June. Considering that he's thrown 18.0 straight scoreless innings, redemption may be on the menu.

A Suddenly Combative Colorado

The Rockies may not have a playoff appearance in their immediate future, but that hasn't stopped them from playing pesky lately. They return home, where they have a much better 28-33 record, after dropping five of six against the Rays and Orioles. However, only one of those losses was by more than two runs, an impressive feat considering how Colorado's road contests usually unfold. This week's homecoming should revitalize an offense that averages 5.15 runs per game in Coors Field, the sixth-highest home scoring average. They slash .271/.332/.445 at home. The Rockies strike out often, don't hit many homers, rarely steal bases, and draw few walks. They simply hit, and at home, it's at a dangerous clip.

On the mound, Colorado isn't pitching well anywhere. Still, the nature of Coors Field makes their pitching staff abysmal at home most days. This year's Rockies entered this series allowing 6.61 runs per game at home. For context, the Athletics are 29th in run prevention at home, giving up 5.62 per contest. Reliever turned starter Peter Lambert will be the man tasked with trying to lower that number against a lethal Atlanta lineup. He tossed 2.0 innings against them earlier this year, ceding one walk and no hits. Starting is a different beast though, especially with Lambert's 6.61 home ERA in the mix. His starting numbers so far are better across the board, but this will be his toughest task by far.

There's not much value in the Braves on Tuesday, but Colorado's options are great. Considering that they fought tooth and nail against two playoff teams on the road recently, there's no reason they shouldn't do it at home. The Rockies are a much more competitive team at home, bringing a 28-33 Coors Field record into the new week.

Colorado's starter, Peter Lambert, has two strong innings under his belt against the Braves already. Now a starting pitcher, he has a 3.38 ERA, 1.050 WHIP, and .212 BAA in his new role. Offensively, Colorado knocks around a lot of pitchers in Coors Field. Charlie Morton should know, as he has a career 5.35 ERA and .788 OPS against in seven Coors Field starts. The Rockies already, well, rocked him in Atlanta, so this game could get even uglier. Expect Colorado to give the Braves a hard time on Tuesday.

Coors Field, the gorgeous, thin-aired park that hosts the Colorado Rockies, is a run-producing haven. Enter the Atlanta Braves, who hit harder than every team and score more than every other MLB club. They're facing a Colorado team that is 29th in ERA and has a horrible bullpen.
