Pittsburgh Pirates (59-73) vs. Kansas City Royals (41-92)

The 2023 MLB betting action continues Tuesday, August 29, with the Pittsburgh Pirates squaring off against the Kansas City Royals in the interleague showdown at Kauffman Stadium in Kansas City, Missouri, and the first pitch is set for 8:10 PM ET. 

Pittsburgh and KC lock horns in the middle contest of a three-game series, after Pittsburgh won Monday’s opener 5-0.

The Bucs have lost five of their last six sets

The Pittsburgh Pirates fell to 58-73 on the season following Sunday’s 10-1 defeat to the Chicago Cubs. It was the Bucs’ second loss in a row and the fourth in five games. Pittsburgh has only won one of its previous six series. But they got a win on Monday, winning 5-0 over KC. The Pirates are 10 games behind the final wild-card spot in the National League. 

The Bucs are slashing an underwhelming .226/.327/.358 with 32 extra-base hits and just eight home runs in the last two weeks and 433 at-bats. Their pitching staff has struggled too, compiling a 4.97 ERA, 1.42 WHIP, and .253 batting average against through 114 innings during that span. 

The Pirates haven’t announced who’ll get the starting call Tuesday in Kansas City. We should see Andre Jackson, who tossed six frames of a two-run, six-hit ball in a no-decision against the Cubs this past Thursday. Jackson is 0-1 with a 4.91 ERA, 1.23 WHIP, and 36/8 K/BB ratio across 36.2 innings of work between the LA Dodgers and Pirates in 2023. The 27-year-old right-hander has recorded a 3.32 ERA, 3.48 FIP, and .630 opposing OPS since joining Pittsburgh (19 innings pitched). 

Luis Ortiz is another option for the Pirates, but the 24-year-old righty has struggled a lot as of late. Ortiz has allowed 15 earned runs on 22 hits and nine walks over his previous three starts and 11.1 innings of work. Last Wednesday, Ortiz was charged with a loss in a 6-4 defeat to the St. Louis Cardinals, yielding five earned runs on seven hits and a whopping five walks through 3.1 innings. It was his first outing in the majors in more than a month.

The Royals are having an awful month

The 41-92 Kansas City Royals entered the Pirates series riding a four-game losing streak. They just got swept by the red-hot Seattle Mariners, who outscored the Royals 25-9 throughout a three-game series in Seattle. The slumping Royals have only won two of their previous 12 games overall. 

Kansas City is hitting .250/.298/.390 with 21 extra-base hits and 12 home runs in the past two weeks and 436 at-bats. On the pitching side of things, the Royals have amassed a pedestrian 5.76 ERA, 1.50 WHIP, and .276 batting average through 111 innings of work in that span. 

Cole Ragans is projected to toe the rubber Tuesday, and the 25-year-old left-hander carries a 5-4 record with a 3.66 ERA, 1.27 WHIP, and 71/24 K/BB ratio across 59 innings of work between the Texas Rangers and Kansas City Royals. Ragans will make his seventh start with KC. Over his previous six outings, the talented lefty has gone 3-1 while posting a shiny 2.08 ERA, 1.80 FIP, and .599 opposing OPS. He fanned 11 across six scoreless frames in a 4-0 victory at the Oakland Athletics this past Wednesday.
