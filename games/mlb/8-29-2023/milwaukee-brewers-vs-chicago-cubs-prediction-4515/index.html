The Chicago Cubs play host to the Milwaukee Brewers on Tuesday night in an NL Central clash from Wrigley Field. The Brewers sit in first place in the division, with their lead at 5.0 games. A series win in this mid-week clash would be crucial for either team going forward, especially with these looking like two of the hottest teams in baseball over the last few weeks. Milwaukee won Monday night's opener, 6-2, and has won five of eight meetings on the season, though going on the road could make things challenging. In what is likely the best series of the week, fans won't want to miss out on this one.

Brewers Sprinting Towards September

The Brewers started the season slowly but have put together a strong last few months en route to sitting in first place in the NL Central standings. They actually have the third-most wins in the National League, while their 9-1 record over their last ten games continues to turn heads. Being on the road could make things challenging, though with a winning record, they haven't been strangers to winning away from home. In April to start the season, the Brewers took two of three games at Wrigley Field, but with the Cubs playing better of late, a game like Tuesday's could be tough.

According to MLB.com, the Brewers will hand the ball off to Corbin Burnes in this one, as the righty looks to set the tone early in this one. With a 9-6 record and 3.65 ERA, he's impressed at times this season. He struggled the last time out, allowing six runs across six innings, while allowing 11 runs across his last three starts combined. Against the Cubs in the season opener, he went 5.0 innings, allowing four runs across five innings, which he'll be looking to improve on.

Milwaukee has leaned heavily on their pitching to lead the way, as the offense has struggled at times. They average 4.5 runs per game and are hitting .234 as a team, though the lack of depth is concerning, especially in a game where they might need a strong performance. Carlos Santana (ankle) is questionable for this one, though the rest of the lineup remains relatively healthy. Christian Yelich (.282 average, 68 RBI) leads the way, while Willy Adames (21 home runs) has turned into a solid option. Adding a number of others hitting over .300 over the last week shows this lineup is growing into itself as the season progresses.

Cubs Relishing Opportunity

The Cubs have been one of the best teams over the last month and while they still sit behind the Brewers in the standings, they know that being at home in this one presents them with a great opportunity. They've gone 7-3 in their last ten games and have a 35-30 record at home, both of which should provide them with some confidence. With the Wild Card and divisional races both looking like they might come down to the wire, every game is of monumental importance going forward. It has helped that the Cubs have been able to get healthy on both sides of the ball, but especially within their lineup, which has paid dividends as well.

According to MLB.com, the Cubs will have the luxury of leaning on one of baseball's best arms in this one, as Justin Steele will take the mound. With a 14-3 record and 2.80 ERA, both of those marks are nearly the best in baseball. In his July start against the Brewers, he allowed three runs in six innings of work, though was lucky it wasn't worse, considering he also allowed nine hits. Another strong start is the expectation for the potential Cy Young winner in this one.

Chicago's offense has set the tone of late and, now averaging over 5.0 runs per game, could give them the confidence to outpace the Brewers. They're hitting .250 as a team, adding in 8.6 hits per game and a .328 OBP. Cody Bellinger (.320 average, 75 RBI) has been on a tear of late, while Patrick Wisdom (21 home runs) has been a consistent power threat. With some others continuing to show off the depth of the lineup, Wednesday could be a memorable night for this offense.
