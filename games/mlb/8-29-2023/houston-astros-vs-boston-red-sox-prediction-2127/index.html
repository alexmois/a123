Houston Astros Battling in Crowded AL West Race

Houston piled up 26 runs in their final two games of the set on the road in the Motor City as they put a three-game skid in the rear-view mirror. The Astros improved to 74-58 and entered Monday tied for second in the AL West with the Rangers, one game behind the Mariners for the top spot. They were tied for the final two wild card spots in the American League with Texas, 2.5 games ahead of the Blue Jays to remain in the playoff picture. On Sunday, Houston ripped Detroit pitching to the tune of 25 hits with Jeremy Pena (three runs, three RBI) leading the way with five. Martin Maldonado (his 11th), Kyle Tucker (his 26th), Mauricio Dubon (his seventh) and Yainer Diaz (his 19th) each went deep in the victory. Justin Verlander (10-6) earned the win on the mound as he tossed five innings, allowing no runs on two hits with two walks and seven strikeouts.

J.P. France is on the bump for the Astros as he logs his 20th appearance and 19th start of the season in this contest. He is 9-5 with a 3.51 ERA, a 1.305 WHIP, 33 walks and 83 strikeouts over 110.1 innings of work this season. France was shelled and took the loss in his last start, which came against the Red Sox at home Thursday. He threw 2.1 innings, allowing 10 runs on 11 hits with two walks and three strikeouts in a 17-1 Houston defeat. In his last three starts, France is 1-2 with an 8.22 ERA, a 1.70 WHIP, four walks and 15 strikeouts over 15.1 innings of work. France’s start against the Red Sox last week was his first career outing against them. He is 0-1 with a 38.57 ERA, a 5.571 WHIP, two walks and three strikeouts over 2.1 innings of work. France, in his 20th career major league appearance and 19th start, pitches at Fenway Park for the first time here.

Boston Red Sox Trying to Gain Ground in AL Wild Card Picture

Boston battled but fell short in the rubber game of their three-game set at home against former teammate Mookie Betts and the Dodgers Sunday afternoon. The Red Sox entered Monday fourth in the AL East with a 69-62 mark and stood 12.5 games behind the Orioles for the top spot in the division. They were 4.5 games behind the Rangers and Astros, who held the final two wild card spots in the American League. On Sunday, Boston had seven hits with Triston Casas (two hits, two RBI) and Adam Duvall (two runs, RBI) each recording two in the loss. Justin Turner (his 22nd), Duvall (his 16th) and Casas (his 21st) each homered in a losing effort for the Red Sox. Tanner Houck (3-8) took the loss on the mound as he threw four innings, allowing one run on five hits with two walks and four strikeouts.

Brayan Bello is on the mound for the Red Sox as he makes his 23rd start of the season in this contest. He is 10-7 with a 3.56 ERA, a 1.282 WHIP, 34 walks and 104 strikeouts over 126.1 innings of work on the year. Bello earned the win in his last start, which came on the road against the Astros on Thursday. He threw seven innings, allowing one run on nine hits with three walks and four strikeouts in a 17-1 Boston triumph. In his last three starts, Bello is 2-1 with a 3.06 ERA, a 1.58 WHIP, four walks and 13 strikeouts over 17.2 innings of work. Bello makes his third career appearance and second start against the Astros in this contest. He is 1-0 with a 1.17 ERA, a 1.826 WHIP, three walks and six strikeouts over 7.2 innings of work against them. Bello is 5-9 with a 3.63 ERA, a 1.389 WHIP, 31 walks and 85 strikeouts over 94.1 innings in 18 career appearances, 17 starts, at Fenway Park.
