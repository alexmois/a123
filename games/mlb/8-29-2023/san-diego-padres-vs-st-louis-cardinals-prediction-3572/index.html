The San Diego Padres (61-70) will be trying to pick up the series win when they face the St. Louis Cardinals (56-75) in the second game of their three-game series on Tuesday night.

Seth Lugo, 5-6, 3.70 ERA, will get the start for the Padres. The Cardinals will counter with Zack Thompson, 3-5, 3.86 ERA.

St. Louis is 7-3 in its last 10 games against San Diego.

**This preview was written before Monday’s game was played**

Padres Trying To Snap Road Losing Streak

The Padres aren’t playing well at the moment, especially on the road where they have lost five straight games. They will try to put an end to their streak with a win over the Cardinals, which will give them their second win in their last seven road games.

San Diego is averaging 4.50 runs per game. Their .237 batting average is 25th in the league. Their .324 on base percentage is 14th, while their .408 slugging percentage is 17th.

Ha-Seong Kim leads the Padres with a .274 batting average, while Juan Soto leads the team with 25 home runs and 78 RBI.

San Diego’s pitching has been good, with the team giving up 4.09 runs per game. Opponents have a .239 batting average against the Padres, which is sixth in the league. Their 3.87 ERA is fifth, while their 1.28 WHIP is 14th.

In his last start, Lugo gave up three hits and no runs in six innings, leading to a 4-0 win over Miami. They will need a similar effort from him if they want to get the win.

Cardinals Going For Second Consecutive Home Win

The Cardinals struggled on their road trip, losing five of their six games. They will try to turn things around with a win over the Padres, which will give them their second straight home win.

St. Louis is averaging 4.50 runs per game. Their .254 batting average is 10th in the league. Their .329 on base percentage is seventh, while their .427 slugging percentage is eighth.

Nolan Arenado has been the big bat for the Cardinals. He leads the team with a .279 batting average, 26 home runs, and 87 RBI.

St. Louis’ pitching hasn’t been good, with the team giving up 5.07 runs per game. Opponents have a .275 batting average against the Cardinals, which is 29th in the league. Their 4.73 ERA is 24th, while their 1.44 WHIP is 28th.

In his last start, Thompson gave up six hits and two runs in five innings, leading to a 6-4 win over Pittsburgh. They will need another solid start from him if they want to win this game.

Tyler O'Neill (Knee) is questionable for this game.
