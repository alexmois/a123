The Cincinnati Reds (68-64) will be trying to keep their playoff hopes alive when they face the San Francisco Giants (67-63) in the second game of their three-game series on Tuesday night.

Brandon Williamson, 4-3, 4.18 ERA, will get the start for the Reds. The Giants will counter with Alex Cobb, 6-5, 3.74 ERA.

Cincinnati is 6-4 in its last 10 games against San Francisco.

**This preview was written before Monday’s game was played**

Reds Trying To Reduce Gap In Wildcard Race

The Reds aren’t playing well at the moment and they’ve lost three of their last four games. They are now six games behind Milwaukee in the NL Central standings and 1.5 games behind the Diamondbacks, who are in the final Wildcard spot. They will try to cut into Arizona’s lead with a win over the Giants, which will give them their second win in their last five games.

Cincinnati is averaging 4.81 runs per game. Their .248 batting average is 18th in the league. Their .325 on base percentage is 11th, while their .413 slugging percentage is 16th.

TJ Friedl leads the Reds with a .273 batting average, while Spencer Steer leads the team with 18 home runs and 71 RBI.

Cincinnati’s pitching hasn’t been good, with the team giving up 4.98 runs per game. Opponents have a .258 batting average against the Reds, which is 25th in the league. Their 4.74 ERA is also 25th, as is their 1.41 WHIP.

In his last start, Williamson gave up six hits and no runs in six innings, but they lost to Arizona 3-2. They will need a similar effort from him if they want to get the win.

Giants Going For Third Win In Five Games

The Giants snapped their two-game losing streak with a win in their last game. They are tied with Cincinnati in the Wildcard standings and will try to cut into Arizona’s lead with a win on Tuesday, which will give them their third win in their last five games.

San Francisco is averaging 4.32 runs per game. Their .239 batting average is 23rd in the league. Their .314 on base percentage is 21st, while their .388 slugging percentage is 25th.

LaMonte Wade Jr. leads San Francisco with a .256 batting average. Wilmer Flores leads the team with 19 home runs, while J.D. Davis leads the team with 60 RBI.

San Francisco’s pitching has been good, with the team giving up 4.34 runs per game. Opponents have a .253 batting average against the Giants, which is 22nd in the league. Their 3.99 ERA is 11th, while their 1.25 WHIP is 10th.

In his last start, Cobb gave up five hits and two runs in five innings, leading to an 8-6 win over Philadelphia. They will need another solid start from him if they want to win this game.
