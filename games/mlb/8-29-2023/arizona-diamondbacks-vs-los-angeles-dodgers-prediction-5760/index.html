The Arizona Diamondbacks face the Los Angeles Dodgers with both teams in the middle of great seasons. The Diamondbacks have a 69-62 record and are in second place in the National League. The Dodgers have an 80-49 record and with a 21-4 record in August, they have the second-best record in the National League. For the upcoming game, the Diamondbacks will turn to Merrill Kelly as their starting pitcher who has only allowed four runs in his last three starts while the Dodgers will turn to Clayton Kershaw who has only allowed three runs in his last three starts.

Diamondbacks hope lineup helps out Kelly

The Diamondbacks are having a great season and hope to secure a wild card spot in the final few weeks of the season. Merrill Kelly has pitched well in recent starts but is having a rough season on the mound, allowing 51 runs in 136.1 innings pitched. In addition, with a .245 expected opponent Batting Average, an 8.8 Walk Rate, and a 4.13 expected ERA, opponents are making contact and putting together strong plate appearances against Kelly to easily drive in runs.

Kelly has had a rocky season but the lineup has been great this year, scoring 4.75 runs per game. Corbin Carroll is slashing .281/.364/.520 with 22 home runs and 233 total bases while Christian Walker is slashing .270/.345/.523 with 28 home runs and 248 total bases but the rest of the lineup has stepped up as well. Ketel Marte is slashing .274/.356/.480 with 21 home runs and 226 total bases while Lourdes Gurriel Jr. and Evan Longoria have added 31 home runs and 285 total bases to add depth to the lineup.

Dodgers turn to Kershaw and potent lineup to carry them

The Dodgers are having a great season and hope to secure the best record in the National League for the second year in a row. Clayton Kershaw will start the upcoming game and he has put together a great season, allowing only 33 runs in 107.1 innings pitched while tossing 115 strikeouts. Moreover, with a .230 expected opponent Batting Average, a 27.1 Strikeout Percentage, and a 3.56 expected ERA, opponents are making minimal contact and struggling to drive in runs against Kershaw.

Kershaw has stepped up but the lineup has carried the Dodgers, scoring 5.61 runs per game. Mookie Betts is slashing .315/.407/.611 with 35 home runs and 295 total bases while Freddie Freeman is slashing .341/.417/.581 with 23 home runs and 298 total bases but the entire lineup has been great this year. Will Smith is slashing .268/.365/.454 with 16 home runs and 168 total bases while Max Muncy and James Outman have added 46 home runs and 341 total bases to round out the lineup, making the Dodgers tough to stop at the plate.
