Guardians Hanging On

It is now or never for the Cleveland Guardians as they enter this three-game series with the Twins in Minnesota. The Guardians' only road to the playoffs will be as AL Central Division champions this season with the team entering this series seven games under .500 and six games behind the first-place Twins. Over the weekend, the Guardians were able to take two out of three games from the Blue Jays in Toronto. The Guardians now must gain ground against the Twins in this three-game series. On Monday night, the Guardians started strong with four runs in the top of the second inning but then allowed six to the Twins in the bottom of the inning on their way to a series-opening 10-6 loss.

The Guardians will turn to rookie righty Gavin Williams on Tuesday night in Minnesota. Williams is just 1-5 in 12 starts this season while allowing 56 hits in 64 innings pitched. Williams has an ERA of 3.52 with a K/9 rate of 9.6 and a WHIP of 1.28. On the road, Williams is 0-2 in four starts while giving up 14 hits in 22 innings pitched and 26 strikeouts with an ERA of just 2.05. In his last five starts, Williams is 0-3 with a 3.76 ERA while striking out 37 batters in 26.1 innings pitched.

Key Injuries: 1B Josh Naylor is out.

Twins Closing In

The Minnesota Twins look like a team not ready to repeat very recent history. Last season, the Twins were in first place in the AL Central at the all-star break but fell apart in the second half and finished behind these same Cleveland Guardians. This season, Minnesota remains the only team in the division with a winning record and proved their mettle over the weekend by taking three of four games from the Rangers in Minnesota. On Sunday night, the Twins came from five runs back to beat the Rangers 7-6 in 13-innings. At 68-63, the Twins welcomed the Guardians to town on Monday night with a six-game lead in the division and a chance to nearly put the Guardians out of the race. In game one, the Twins bounced back from an early 4-0 deficit to pick up a 10-6 win to move a season-high seven games ahead of the Guardians in the AL Central Division standings. Royce Lewis led the way with a home run and four RBI in the contest.

The Twins will turn to righty Pablo Lopez on Tuesday night against the Guardians. Lopez is 9-6 in 26 starts this season including a team-leading 16 quality starts. He has allowed 140 hits in 158.2 innings pitched with an ERA of 3.69. Lopez has a team-best 10.8 K/9 rate and a WHIP of 1.13. At home, he is 4-2 with a 4.56 ERA in 13 games including 93 strikeouts in 75 innings pitched. Lopez has been dominant as of late with a record of 4-0 in his last five starts and an impressive 1.80 ERA. Lopez faced Cleveland just once this season and had a no-decision while allowing eight hits and six runs in 5.2 innings of work.

Key Injuries: 1B Alex Kirilloff and DH/CF Byron Buxton are out.
