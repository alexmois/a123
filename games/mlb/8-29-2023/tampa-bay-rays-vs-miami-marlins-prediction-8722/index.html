The Tampa Bay Rays and Miami Marlins will go head-to-head in the Citrus Series on Tuesday at loanDepot Park. The first pitch is at 6:40 p.m. ET.

Tampa Bay (80-52 SU and 67-65 RL) is starting right-hander Aaron Civale. The 28-year-old is 6-3 with a 2.59 ERA and a 1.10 WHIP in 17 outings this year (97.1 IP).

Miami (66-65 SU and 58-73 RL) is starting righty Sandy Alcantara. The 27-year-old is 6-11 with a 4.16 ERA and a 1.19 WHIP in his 26 appearances.

The Marlins are -110 moneyline favorites and the game total is 7.5 runs.

Tampa Bay is coming for the number one spot

Tampa Bay has won eight of its last ten, keeping within a couple of games of division leader Baltimore. It's one of only four teams with 80 wins this season.

The Rays average 5.33 runs (4th) and hit .260 (4th) with a .778 OPS (4th). They've hit 191 long balls (3rd) and stolen 138 bases (2nd) in 2023. The TB pitching staff has compiled a 3.87 ERA (6th) and a 1.19 WHIP (2nd) with 43 quality starts (17th).

Miami needs to find its footing fast

The Marlins dropped two of three to Washington in their last series, falling three games out of the National League Wild Card picture. Can they pick up momentum for the final month of the regular season against their in-state rivals?

Miami scores 4.02 runs per game (27th) and hits .258 (7th) with a .714 OPS (20th). It's launched 126 home runs (28th) and stolen 66 bases (26th) in 2023. The Marlins' pitching staff has posted a 4.17 ERA (16th) and a 1.27 WHIP (13th) with 43 quality starts (18th).

Alcantara will start for the home team on Tuesday night. In his last outing, he gave up four runs and seven hits in 6.2 innings versus San Diego. The seventh-year pro has held four of his last six opponents to three or fewer runs. Alcantara is 2-2 with a 2.90 ERA and 30 strikeouts in five career appearances (31.0 IP) against Tampa Bay.

Alcantara has been giving up fewer runs recently, but he's still putting too many runners on base. The Marlins' Tuesday starter has surrendered allowed seven or more baserunners in seven of his last ten outings. He has given up seven long balls in his last four starts and 15 homers in his last ten. That's a cause for concern against a Tampa team that sports a .331 OBP and the third-most long balls in baseball.

Civale is a consistent pitcher. He has fanned 20 batters and walked only two since joining Tampa and has held his last six opponents to nine total runs (34.1 innings). His nine strikeouts vs. the Rockies in his last start tied his season high. The Marlins have scored 15 total runs in their last eight games and are averaging 3.5 runs per game in the second half of the season. They sport a .311 OBP in night games and a .309 OBP against right-handed pitchers, as well.

At almost even odds, I feel confident wagering four units on the Rays to win straight-up tomorrow evening.
