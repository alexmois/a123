The Atlanta Braves (87-45, 69-62 RL, 70-59-2 O/U) collide with the Los Angeles Dodgers (82-49, 75-56 RL, 76-49-6 O/U) in an NL showdown on Thursday night. The Braves will give the starting nod to Spencer Strider. The Dodgers send Lance Lynn to the mound. The Dodgers lead the season series 2-1.  

Strider Collects 15th Win

Spencer Strider will be on the mound to begin the series on Thursday. Strider resumed his dominance last time out, limiting the Giants to one run in seven innings, striking out nine batters in a 5-1 victory. The Braves ace has allowed just one run in his last three performances and is sporting a 3.46 ERA and a 1.06 WHIP complemented by a 15-4 record in 153.1 innings pitched. Strider allowed four runs (two earned) in six innings against the Dodgers early in the season and has conceded only four runs (two earned) with an 0-1 record in 12 career innings. 

Atlanta continues a 10-game road trek. They won two of three against the Giants to begin the trek followed by two wins in the opening two meetings against the Rockies, scoring 17 runs. The Braves are going to win the NL East and remain at three more wins than the Orioles for the most victories in the big leagues. The Braves stand at 7-3 in their last ten games.

Ronald Acuna Jr. is hitting a stellar. 345 with a .985 OPS in August. The 25-year-old outfielder now has 29 bombs along with 79 RBIs and a robust .988 OPS on the season. He has eclipsed 60 stolen bases. Acuna was kept in check by the Dodgers in the first series, going 1 for 11 with zero RBIs.

Atlanta has an incredible offense that leads the Major Leagues with 5.82 runs per game. The pitching is solid, carrying a 3.80 team ERA, good for third.

Lynn Starts, Dodgers Record Another Series Win

Lance Lynn will be on the mound in this one. Lynn was clobbered in his previous start, allowing four runs (three earned) on 10 hits in six innings against the Red Sox and able to come away with the victory. The veteran has been outstanding since he was acquired in a deal with the White Sox, posting a 2.03 ERA in August and has a 5.56 ERA and a 1.38 WHIP accompanied by a 10-9 record in 150.2 innings on the year. Lynn gave up four runs in 5.1 innings against the Braves earlier this season and has registered a 3.32 ERA and a 4-3 record in 43.1 career innings. 

Los Angeles continues to dominate. They did not lose a single series in all of August and have just three losses in the month. The Dodgers are going to win the NL West yet again. They won the opening two bouts against the Diamondbacks this week, and continue to pile on the runs. The Dodgers are 8-2 in their last ten bouts.

Max Muncy continues to generate offense, knocking in eight runs in his last five clashes. The veteran second baseman could reach 100 RBIs. He has 31 homers along with 85 RBIs and an .822 OPS on the season. Muncy is 2 for 14 with two RBIs against the Braves this season.

LA is averaging 5.65 runs on the year, marking them second. The pitching is improving, posting a 4.17 team ERA, placing them 17th.
