Yankees Already Cleaning House

It has been a lost season for the Bronx Bombers in 2023 and the team has already started to make roster moves ahead of what should be a very interesting offseason in New York. On Tuesday afternoon, the Yankees waived two veterans as they parted ways with 3B Josh Donaldson by outright releasing him and placed outfielder Harrison Bader on waivers. More is sure to come for a Yankees team that has underperformed after winning the AL East Division in 2022. The Yankees have played well in this series, winning the first two games by scores of 4-1 and 4-2. On Wednesday night, Gerrit Cole picked up his 12th win of the season after allowing four hits and two runs in a 6-2 win to make it three straight Yankees' wins in the series.

The Yankees will hand the ball to righty Clarke Schmidt in the fourth and final game of the series. Schmidt is 8-8 in 26 starts this season with four quality starts overall. He has allowed 136 hits in 129.2 innings pitched with an ERA of 4.51. Schmidt's K/9 rate is 8.7 and his WHIP is 1.32. On the road, Schmidt is 5-3 with an ERA of 5.25 while striking out 58 batters in 58.1 innings pitched. He is 1-2 in his last five starts with an ERA of 5.04 while striking out 27 batters in 25 innings pitched.

Key Injuries: 1B Anthony Rizzo is out.

Tigers Trying to Finish Strong

The Detroit Tigers have quietly improved in 2023 and are currently in third place ahead of both the White Sox and Royals in the AL Central Division. While the team would certainly prefer to be battling for a playoff spot at this point, a third-place finish would mark improvement for a team that has been hovering around the basement of the division the last few seasons. The Tigers have been tamed by the Yankees in each of the first two games of this series, scoring a combined three runs in the two losses. On Wednesday night, the Tigers' lineup struck out 12 times and had just four hits in a 6-2 loss to the Yankees.

The Tigers will hand the ball to right-handed rookie Matt Manning in the finale against the Yankees. Manning is 5-4 in 13 starts this season including three quality starts. He has allowed 57 hits in 71 innings pitched with an ERA of 3.93. Manning pitches to contact with a K/9 rate of just 5.7 this season while his WHIP is a solid 1.06. At home, Manning is 1-2 with a 4.56 ERA while striking out 18 batters in 25.2 innings pitched. In his last five games, Manning is 2-2 with an ERA of 3.38. He has allowed 25 hits in 29.1 innings pitched in that span.
