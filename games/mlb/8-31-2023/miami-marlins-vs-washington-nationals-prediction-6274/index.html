A pair of NL East rivals open up a weekend series in search of victory in the nation’s capital. The Miami Marlins are on the road as they travel to open a four-game series with the Washington Nationals on Thursday night. Marlins Looking to Earn Road Triumph

Miami was clobbered in the opening game of their interleague set with Tampa Bay at home Tuesday night for their seventh loss in the last nine games. The Marlins entered Wednesday 66-66 on the season and stood third in the NL East, 20.5 games behind the Braves for the top spot. They were three games behind the Giants for the final wild card spot in the National League playoff picture. On Tuesday, Miami got two hits each from Jon Berti and Jazz Chisholm Jr. (run, two RBI) but went just two of 13 with runners in scoring position in the game. Chisholm Jr. hit his 13th homer of the season in the loss. Sandy Alcantara (6-12) took the loss on the mound as he threw 5.2 innings, allowing four runs on seven hits with two walks and four strikeouts.

Braxton Garrett is on the mound as he makes his 27th appearance and 26th start of the season for the Marlins in this contest. He is 7-5 with a 3.96 ERA, a 1.172 WHIP, 21 walks and 135 strikeouts over 134 innings of work this season. Garrett took the loss in his last start, which came against the Nationals at home Friday night. He threw six innings, allowing three runs on seven hits with no walks and three strikeouts in a game the Marlins dropped 7-4. In his last three starts, Garrett is 1-2 with a 3.18 ERA, a 1.06 WHIP, two walks and 10 strikeouts over 17 innings of work. Garrett makes his sixth career start against the Nationals in this contest. He comes in 2-2 with a 3.21 ERA, a 1.071 WHIP, five walks and 23 strikeouts over 28 innings of work against them. Garrett is 1-0 with a 1.35 ERA, a 0.75 WHIP, two walks and 12 strikeouts over 13.1 innings in two career starts at Nationals Park.

Washington Nationals Trying to Stay Strong in Stretch Run

Washington snapped a two-game slide as they beat Toronto Tuesday for their fourth win in six games. The Nationals entered Wednesday 62-71 and stood fourth in the NL East, 25 games behind the Braves for the top spot. They were 7.5 games behind the Giants for the final wild card spot in the National League. On Tuesday, Washington got two of their eight hits from Carter Kieboom (run, two RBI) in the victory. Kieboom (his third) and Keibert Ruiz (his 16th) each went deep for the Nationals in the win. MacKenzie Gore (7-10) earned the win on the mound as he threw five innings, allowing one run on six hits with two walks and two strikeouts. Kyle Finnegan worked the ninth, allowing one run on two hits with one walk and one strikeout, for his 25th save of the season.

Joan Adon takes the hill for the Nationals as he logs his seventh appearance and fifth start of the season in this contest. He is 2-0 with a 5.25 ERA, a 0.958 WHIP, six walks and 20 strikeouts over 24 innings of work on the year. Adon earned the win in his last start, which came on the road against the Marlins Friday. He threw six scoreless innings, allowing three hits with no walks and three strikeouts in a 7-4 Washington victory. In his last three starts, Adon is 1-0 with a 5.54 ERA, a 1.15 WHIP, four walks and nine strikeouts over 13 innings of work. Adon makes his fourth career start against the Marlins in this contest. He is 1-2 with a 5.93 ERA, a 1.244 WHIP, two walks and seven strikeouts over 13.2 innings of work against them. Adon is 1-5 with a 6.39 ERA, a 1.601 WHIP, 26 walks and 43 strikeouts over 49.1 innings in 11 career appearances, 10 starts, at Nationals Park.
