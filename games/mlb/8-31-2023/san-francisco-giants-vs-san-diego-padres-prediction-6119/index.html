The San Francisco Giants and San Diego Padres will take the field on Thursday at Petco Park. First pitch is scheduled for 6:40 p.m. PT.

San Francisco (69-64 SU and 65-68 RL) has not named a probable starter, but signs point to left-hander Sean Manaea taking the mound. The 31-year-old is 4-5 with a 5.05 ERA and a 1.28 WHIP in 31 games (six starts) this season.

San Diego (62-72 SU and 65-69 RL) is starting right-hander Pedro Avila. The 26-year-old is 0-1 with a 2.63 ERA and a 1.24 WHIP in eight games (two starts).

San Francisco is still in the postseason race

The Giants wrapped up a three-game set with Cincinnati on Wednesday with their first series win since the first week of the month. They're in the thick of the Wild Card race but don't have much breathing room. Can they hold on to make the postseason?

San Francisco scores 4.31 runs per game (22nd) and hits .238 (24th) with a .700 OPS (24th). It's launched 143 long balls (21st) and stolen 47 bases (30th) in 2023. The SF pitching staff sports a 3.95 ERA (9th) and a 1.24 WHIP (10th) with 43 quality starts (20th).

Manaea is a likely starter for San Francisco in Thursday's series opener in sunny San Diego. The eighth-year pro pitched 3.2 innings vs. Atlanta on Saturday, surrendering two runs and three hits with five strikeouts. He's lost consecutive outings but did pick up a win over Tampa Bay on August 15. Manaea has faced the Padres twice this season, surrendering five runs and four home runs in two innings on April 29 and holding them scoreless in one inning on June 22.

The Padres lost consecutive winnable games in St. Louis

The Padres return home Thursday following a six-game road trip in Milwaukee and St. Louis. They didn't take care of business, falling even further out of playoff contention. Is their season officially over?

San Diego averages 4.49 runs (16th) and hits .239 (23rd) with a .734 OPS (16th). It's hit 168 homers (13th) and stolen 103 bases (10th) in 2023. The SD pitching staff has compiled a 3.86 ERA (5th) and a 1.27 WHIP (14th) with 61 quality starts (3rd).

Avila will start for the home team on Thursday in downtown San Diego. A few days ago, the fourth-year pro was knocked around by the Brewers (five runs in 4.1 innings) in his first start since August 1. He's made six appearances this month, lasting four-plus innings in five of his August outings. Prior to Saturday's defeat, he had held all of his 2023 opponents to one or zero runs.

Rating:

San Francisco endured a challenging August, with games against the Reds, Braves (twice), Phillies, Rays, and Rangers. They were only able to win one of those series, but they're still in the Wild Card race. On Thursday, they'll benefit from a game against a San Diego team that blew consecutive winnable games on Tuesday and Wednesday versus a last-place St. Louis squad.

The Padres don't have the competitive edge to beat good teams or even win the games they should. They have consistently choked away leads, no matter the competition. Plus, when they don't have one of their top starters on the mound, they're almost unwatchable. The offense has erratic highs and lows, scoring nine runs one day and zero the next night.

The Giants have not been a great hitting club since the All-Star break, but they're still a better bet to come out on top than the Padres. Avila is coming off his poorest outing of the year, and optimism in the San Diego clubhouse has dropped to a season-low. San Francisco likely doesn't need to score six runs to win on Thursday, especially if it has any early momentum in the ballgame.

Bet on the Giants to win straight-up tomorrow in SoCal.
