Kansas City Royals (41-89) vs. Seattle Mariners (72-56)

The MLB betting action continues Sunday, August 27, 2023, with the Kansas City Royals taking on the Seattle Mariners in the American League showdown at T-Mobile Park in Seattle, Washington, and the first pitch is set for 4:10 PM ET. 

Kansas City and Seattle wrap up a three-game set at T-Mobile Park. The Mariners beat the Royals 7-5 in Friday’s opener, while the middle game has been excluded from the analysis. These two foes also met each other earlier this month, and the Mariners took three out of four games from the Royals in Kansas City.  

The Royals are struggling on the mound

The Kansas City Royals fell to 41-89 on the season following Friday’s defeat in Seattle. It was their eighth loss in ten games, and the Royals have allowed 53 runs during that stretch while scoring 45 in return. Interestingly, each of those eight defeats has come by fewer than three runs. 

Kansas City is slashing .270/.314/.409 with 20 extra-base hits and 11 home runs in the last two weeks and 408 at-bats. On the pitching side of things, the Royals have amassed a pedestrian 4.93 ERA, 1.46 WHIP, and .271 batting average against 104 innings in those two weeks. 

The Royals haven’t announced who’ll get the starting call Sunday in Seattle. I’m expecting to see Alec Marsh on the mound, and the 25-year-old rookie right-hander is 0-6 with a poor 5.56 ERA, 1.60 WHIP, and 50/25 K/BB ratio in six starts and four relief appearances (43.2 innings pitched) this season. Last Monday, Marsh tossed 5.2 innings in a no-decision against the Oakland Athletics on the road, allowing three earned runs on five hits and a walk while punching out nine. Over his previous five outings this month, Marsh has compiled a 3.92 ERA, 4.96 FIP, and .853 opposing OPS through 20.2 innings of work.

The Mariners continue to impress

The Seattle Mariners moved to 72-56 on the season following Friday’s victory over the Royals. It was Seattle’s ninth win in ten games, and the red-hot Mariners were tied with the Texas Rangers for the top spot in the AL West on Saturday. 

During that ten-game stretch, the Mariners have scored a staggering 72 runs while slashing a tremendous .327/.415/.545 with 19 home runs across 447 plate appearances. They’ve also stolen 19 bases over those ten contests, while the Mariners’ bullpen has recorded a 3.08 ERA and 3.14 FIP in 38 innings of work (5-1 record with seven saves). 

Luis Castillo will be on the bump Sunday, and the 30-year-old righty is 10-7 with a sturdy 3.15 ERA, 1.04 WHIP, and 175/37 K/BB ratio in 26 starts (157.1 IP) in 2023. Castillo has emerged victorious in each of his previous three outings, posting a 2.70 ERA, 2.37 FIP, and .600 opposing OPS through 20 innings of work. Last Monday, Castillo fanned nine across seven innings of a one-run ball in a 14-2 dismantling of the Chicago White Sox.
