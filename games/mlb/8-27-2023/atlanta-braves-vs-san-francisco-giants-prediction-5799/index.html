Braves Rolling Again

The Atlanta Braves, after a rare two-game losing skid, bounced back to win four straight games. Overall, they have now won nine of 11 games heading into the series finale on Sunday night. On Saturday, Atlanta pulled out a 7-3 win over the Giants with Max Fried gutting out six innings to pick up the win. Austin Riley hit his 30th home run and Orlando Arcia also homered for the Braves while Matt Olson had three hits and two RBI in the win. Atlanta is now a major league-best 84-44.

Atlanta could turn to righty Bryce Elder on regular rest on Sunday night. Elder last pitched on Tuesday night and went 5.1 innings and allowed just one run against the Mets in a 3-2 Braves' win. The fact that the Braves have not announced him as the starter may indicate the team's desire to give the young righty an extra day's rest as he has now more than doubled his career-high in innings pitched with over 143 innings pitched. Elder is currently scheduled to go on Tuesday so the Braves will likely turn to a bullpen game on Sunday night. The Braves have several candidates to open the game including Collin McHugh (4-1, 3.35) and Michael Tonkin (5-2, 3.38). The Braves have several quality arms and one of the league's best bullpens to back anyone who gets the nod on Sunday night.

Key Injuries: 2B Ozzie Albies is out.

Giants in the Hunt

The San Francisco Giants have picked a bad time to go into a funk with four losses in five games and just 3-7 in their last ten overall. The Giants are still just a game and a half out of a wildcard spot in the National League with still over a month to go in the season. The Giants have dropped each of the first two games to the Braves by a combined 15-4 score. They are now 66-63 on the season. On Saturday, the Giants went through five pitchers after starting with an opener and went just 1-for-6 with runners in scoring position on the day.

Like the Braves, the Giants are pushing back one of their starters for additional rest instead of throwing him on regular rest on Sunday. Rookie Kyle Harrison will not make his second start until Monday night, on a full five day's rest. With two of their five starters out with injury, the Giants will likely have to go back-to-back games with an opener. Scott Alexander, who pitched just a third of an inning on Friday night, could get the call for the Giants on Sunday night. For the season, the lefty is 6-2 in 46 appearances including seven starts. He's allowed 40 hits in 39 innings pitched this season and has a 5.5 K/9 rate. After utilizing five different pitchers on Saturday, the Giants will be a bit strapped if the Braves knock anyone out early.

Key Injuries: SS Brandon Crawford, OF Mike Yastrzemski, and OF Michael Conforto are all out.
