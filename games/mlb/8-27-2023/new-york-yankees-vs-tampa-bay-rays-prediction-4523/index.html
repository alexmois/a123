The New York Yankees (62-66) will be going for the series win when they face the Tampa Bay Rays (78-52) in the final game of their three-game series on Sunday afternoon.

Carlos Rodon, 1-4, 6.27 ERA, will get the start for the Yankees. The Rays will counter with Zack Littell, 2-4, 4.27 ERA.

New York and Tampa Bay split their last 10 meetings.

**This preview was written before Saturday’s game was played**

Yankees Going For Third Win In Four Games

The Yankees have played well since they snapped their nine-game losing streak, winning two of their last three games. They will try to keep the momentum going with another win over the Rays, which will give them their third win in their last four games and the series win.

New York is averaging 4.25 runs per game. Their .230 batting average is 29th in the league. Their .305 on base percentage is 26th, while their .403 slugging percentage is 19th.

Gleyber Torres leads New York with a .270 batting average, while Aaron Judge leads the team with 28 home runs and 55 RBI.

New York’s pitching has been good, with the team giving up 4.38 runs per game. Opponents have a .232 batting average against the Yankees, which leads the league. Their 4.05 ERA is 13th, while their 1.24 WHIP is eighth.

In his last start, Rodon gave up six hits and one run in six innings, leading to a 2-1 loss to Washington. They will need another solid start from him if they want to win this game.

Rays Going For Fifth Win In Six Games

The Rays played well over the past week and they’ve won four of their last five games. Despite their hot play, they are still three games behind Baltimore in the AL East race and will try to cut into the gap with a win over the Yankees, which will give them their fifth win in their last six games.

Tampa Bay is averaging 5.34 runs per game. Their .260 batting average is fourth in the league. Their .331 on base percentage is also fourth, as is their .449 slugging percentage.

Yandy Diaz leads the Rays with a .326 batting average, while Isaac Paredes leads the team with 26 home runs and 80 RBI.

Tampa Bay’s pitching has been good, with the team giving up 4.05 runs per game. Opponents have a .234 batting average against the Rays, which is third in the league. Their 3.90 ERA is sixth, while their 1.20 WHIP is second.

In his last start, Littell gave up seven hits and four runs in 5.1 innings, leading to a 12-4 win over Colorado. They will need a better effort from him if they want to get the win.
