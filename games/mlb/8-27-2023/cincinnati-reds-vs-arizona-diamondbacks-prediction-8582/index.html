Reds Hanging In

The Cincinnati Reds don't appear to be going away any time soon. With just over a month to go in the season, the Reds are right in the thick of the wildcard race in the National League and still in the hunt for the NL Central Division title in a three-team battle with the Brewers and Cubs. After sweeping the reeling Angels on the road, the Reds arrived in Arizona and dropped each of the first two games. The Reds dropped the opener 3-2 on Thursday night and then lost a 10-8 slugfest on Friday night.

*Article written before the completion of Saturday night's game.

The Reds will hand the ball to righty Graham Ashcraft in Sunday's finale against the D-Backs. Ashcraft is 7-8 in 24 starts this season with a team-leading 14 quality starts. He has allowed 135 hits in 134 innings pitched with an ERA of 4.84. On the road this season, Ashcraft is 4-1 with an ERA of 3.79. He has struck out 48 batters in 57 innings on the road. In his last five starts, Ashcraft is 2-1 with an ERA of 2.57. He has allowed 25 hits in 35 innings pitched with 26 strikeouts.

Key Injuries: 1B/DH Joey Votto, 2B Jonathan India, and RF Jake Fraley are all out.

D-Backs Sizzling

The Arizona Diamondbacks looked like they were fading after a rough start to the month of August. The team has rebounded nicely and now finds itself very much in the National League Wildcard picture, currently holding the second position behind the Phillies. The D-Backs have reeled off six straight wins heading into Saturday night, including the first two games of this series against the Reds. The Diamondbacks are hitting nearly .300 during this stretch and averaging over six runs per game.

*Article written before the completion of Saturday night's game.

The Diamondbacks will give the ball to rookie right-hander Slade Cecconi in the series finale. Cecconi has not had a decision yet in four appearances, including two starts thus far. He has allowed 12 hits in 15.1 innings pitched with an ERA of 2.93. Cecconi has a K/9 rate of just 5.3 thus far but a very solid 1.04 WHIP. In his last start, Cecconi went five innings and allowed just three hits and a run while striking out two in an eventual 4-3 Arizona win in 10-innings over the Rangers.

Full-Game Side Bet

Insiders Status:

Rating:

I like the Reds to close out this series with a win against the D-Backs. Ashcraft has been the best Reds pitcher over the last month and has an ERA under three in his last five starts. He has also been a better pitcher on the road this season than at home. The Diamondbacks will send Cecconi to the hill and the 24-year-old has only lasted over four innings one time this season. He faces a Reds lineup that is banged up but still ranks ninth in baseball in runs per game. They should have a chance to put up numbers against Cecconi, who lives on balls put in play. I like the Reds' power to shine in one of baseball's better hitter's parks this season while Ashcraft can tame the D-Backs and end this series on a high note.

Take the Reds with the money line.
