Washington Nationals Playing Tough Down the Stretch

Washington improved to 10-3 in their last 13 games as they took the opening game of this series Friday night on the road. The Nationals entered Saturday 60-69 on the year and stood fourth in the NL East, 24 games behind the Braves for the top spot in the division. They were 7.5 games behind the Cubs for the final wild card spot in the National League playoff picture. On Friday night, Washington finished with 13 hits as Kelbert Ruiz (run, RBI), Alex Call (RBI), Michael Chavis (run) and Ildemaro Vargas (run) each had two. Carter Kieboom hit his second homer of the season in the victory. Joan Adon (2-0) earned the win as he threw six shutout innings, allowing two hits with no walks and three strikeouts. Hunter Harvey worked a scoreless ninth, allowing one hit with no walks and one strikeout, for his 10th save.

Trevor Williams is expected to take the mound for his 26th start of the season for the Nationals in this contest. He comes in 6-7 with a 4.95 ERA, a 1.488 WHIP, 43 walks and 94 strikeouts over 123.2 innings of work on the year. Williams earned the win in his last start, which came last Sunday at home against the Phillies. He threw six innings, allowing no runs on two hits with three walks and four strikeouts in a game the Nationals went on to win 4-3. In his last three starts, Williams is 1-1 with a 6.60 ERA, a 1.47 WHIP, six walks and 15 strikeouts over 15 innings of work. Williams makes his 12th career appearance and seventh start against the Marlins in this contest. He is 1-2 with a 4.46 ERA, a 1.215 WHIP, seven walks and 38 strikeouts over 40.1 innings of work against them. Williams is 0-2 with a 5.19 ERA, a 1.269 WHIP, three walks and eight strikeouts over 8.2 innings of action in four career appearances, one start, at LoanDepot Park.

Marlins Hoping to Get on Track Again

Miami lost for the seventh time in their last nine games as they were dropped in the opening game of this series Friday night. The Marlins entered Saturday 65-64 on the year and stood third in the NL East, 19 games behind the Braves for the top spot in the division. They were 2.5 games behind the Cubs for the final wild card spot in the National League playoff picture. On Friday night, Miami finished with eight hits with Bryan De La Cruz (run) the lone player with two in the contest. Braxton Garrett (7-5) took the loss as he allowed three runs on seven hits with no walks and three strikeouts over six innings of work.

Jesus Luzardo is expected to take the ball for Miami in this contest as he makes his 27th start of the season. He comes in 9-8 with a 3.77 ERA, a 1.249 WHIP, 42 walks and 167 strikeouts over 143.1 innings of work this year. Luzardo earned the win in his last start, which came against the Padres on the road Tuesday night. He threw six innings, allowing no runs on two hits with one walk and seven strikeouts in a game the Marlins went on to win 3-0. In his last three starts, Luzardo has a 1-2 record with an 8.31 ERA, a 1.85 WHIP, five walks and 12 strikeouts over 13 innings of work. Luzardo makes his seventh career start against the Nationals in this contest. He is 1-2 with a 5.52 ERA, a 1.548 WHIP, 10 walks and 24 strikeouts over 31 innings of work against them. Luzardo is 10-11 with a 4.19 ERA, a 1.285 WHIP, 64 walks and 201 strikeouts over 172 innings of work in 32 career starts at LoanDepot Park.
