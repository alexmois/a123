The Colorado Rockies face the Baltimore Orioles with both teams on opposite ends of the standings this year. The Rockies have a 48-81 record and with six losses in a row, they have the worst record in the National League. The Orioles have an 81-48 record and with four consecutive wins, they have the best record in the American League. For the final game of the series, the Rockies will turn to Ty Blach as their starting pitcher who has allowed only six runs in his last three starts while the Orioles will turn to Jack Flaherty who has allowed 10 runs in his last two starts.

Rockies hope Blach and lineup step up

The Rockies are having a season to forget and are poised to finish the year with the worst record in the National League. Ty Blach has pitched well in recent starts but has struggled this season, allowing 23 runs in 41 innings pitched. Moreover, with a .332 expected opponent Batting Average, a 10.3 Strikeout Percentage, and a 6.68 expected ERA, opponents are making contact and putting together strong plate appearances to easily drive in runs.

Along with Blach struggling, the lineup has been a disappointment, scoring only 4.40 runs per game. Nolan Jones is slashing .282/.359/.510 with 13 home runs and 125 total bases while Charlie Blackmon is slashing .285/.372/.463 but the rest of the lineup has struggled. Only three active batters have an OPS+ over 100 and opposing pitchers can eliminate the hapless lineup with ease.

Orioles hope lineup helps out Flaherty

The Orioles are having a great season and look to secure the best record in the American League. Jack Flaherty has shown flashes, with a 6.8 Barrel Percentage and a 36.5 Hard-Hit Percentage, but is having a rough year on the mound, allowing 67 runs in 123.2 innings pitched. In addition, with a .254 expected opponent Batting Average, an 11.3 Walk Rate, and a 4.70 expected ERA, opponents are making contact and putting together strong plate appearances to easily drive in runs.

Flaherty is having a rough year but the lineup has been one of the best in baseball, scoring 4.94 runs per game with 53 runs in the last eight games. Ryan O'Hearn is slashing .296/.333/.496 with 10 home runs and 119 total bases while Gunnar Henderson is slashing .251/.329/.486 with 22 home runs and 203 total bases but the rest of the lineup has stepped up as well. Adley Rutschman is slashing .276/.370/.428 with 16 home runs and 203 total bases while Anthony Santander and Ryan Mountcastle have added 46 home runs and 392 total bases to round out the lineup, making the Orioles tough to put away at the plate.
