San Diego Padres Trying to Find a Spark

San Diego continues to underwhelm despite their massive spending in free agency as they were dropped in the opening game of this set Friday night. The Padres fell to 61-69 on the season after a 5-4 loss on Saturday. They stand fourth in the NL West, 19 games behind the Dodgers for the top spot, and 7 games behind Arizona for the final wild card spot in the National League playoff picture. On Friday night, San Diego got two hits from Garrett Cooper and Fernando Tatis Jr. though that was nowhere near enough. Manny Machado (his 25th) and Gary Sanchez (his 17th) each homered in a losing effort for the Padres. Yu Darvish (8-10) took the loss on the mound as he threw four innings, allowing five runs on six hits with one walk and three strikeouts.

Michael Wacha is on the mound for the Padres as he makes his 18th start of the season in this contest. He comes in 10-2 with a 2.63 ERA, a 1.073 WHIP, 28 walks and 85 strikeouts over 96 innings of work on the year. Wacha earned the win in his last start, which came against the Marlins at home Monday night. He threw 5.1 innings, allowing one run on five hits with two walks and seven strikeouts in a game the Padres won 6-2. In his last three starts, Wacha is 3-0 with a 1.17 ERA, a 1.17 WHIP, five walks and 16 strikeouts over 15.1 innings of work. Wacha makes his 17th appearance and 16th start against the Brewers in this contest. He is 6-1 with a 3.99 ERA, a 1.468 WHIP, 28 walks and 76 strikeouts over 79 innings of work in those outings. Wacha is 3-0 with a 3.24 ERA, a 1.29 WHIP, 15 walks and 34 strikeouts over 33.1 innings of action in seven career appearances, six starts, at American Family Field.

Milwaukee Brewers Seeking to Keep Rolling

Milwaukee ran their win streak to a season-high six games as they took the opening game of this series Friday. The Brewers improved to 72-57 after Saturday's win and hold a four-game lead over the Cubs for the top spot in the NL Central race. On Friday night, Milwaukee got two hits each from Carlos Santana (run), Willy Adames (two runs, RBI), Rowdy Tellez (run, four RBI) and Mark Canha in the victory. Tellez hit his 13th homer of the year for the Brewers in the victory. Brandon Woodruff (3-1) earned the win on the mound as he threw six strong innings, allowing one run on three hits with three walks and 11 strikeouts.

Adrian Houser is on the mound as he makes his 19th appearance and 17th start of the year for the Brewers in this contest. He comes in 5-4 with a 4.28 ERA, a 1.46 WHIP, 27 walks and 75 strikeouts over 88.1 innings of work this season. Houser earned the win in his last outing, which came against Texas on the road last Sunday. He threw five innings, allowing one run on six hits with one walk and seven strikeouts in a game the Brewers won by a 6-2 score. In his last three outings, Houser is 1-1 with a 4.70 ERA, a 1.17 WHIP, two walks and 18 strikeouts over 15.1 innings of work. Houser makes his seventh career appearance and sixth start against the Padres in this contest. He is 0-1 with a 1.86 ERA, a 0.897 WHIP, eight walks and 24 strikeouts over 29 innings of work. Houser is 10-15 with a 3.57 ERA, a 1.302 WHIP, 101 walks and 213 strikeouts over 252 innings in 62 career appearances, 44 starts, at American Family Field.
