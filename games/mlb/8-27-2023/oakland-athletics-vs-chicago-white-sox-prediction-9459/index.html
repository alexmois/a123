The Oakland Athletics (38-91) will be going for their second consecutive series win when they face the Chicago White Sox (50-79) in the final game of their four-game series on Sunday afternoon.

Paul Blackburn, 3-3, 4.00 ERA, will get the start for the Athletics. The White Sox will counter with Mike Clevinger, 5-6, 3.47 ERA.

Chicago and Oakland split their last 10 meetings.

**This preview was written before Saturday’s game was played**

Athletics Going For Fifth Win In Six Games

The Athletics played well over the past week and they’ve won four of their last five games. They will try to keep the momentum going with another win over the White Sox, which will give them their fifth win in their last six games and their second consecutive series win.

Oakland is averaging 3.67 runs per game. Their .224 batting average is 30th in the league. Their .302 on base percentage is 27th, while their .369 slugging percentage is 30th.

Brent Rooker has been the most consistent hitter for the Athletics. He leads the team with a .248 batting average, 22 home runs, and 55 RBI.

Oakland’s pitching hasn’t been good, with the team giving up 5.94 runs per game. Opponents have a .270 batting average against the Athletics, which is 28th in the league. Their 5.73 ERA is 30th, as is their 1.53 WHIP.

In his last start, Blackburn gave up four hits and two runs in six innings, leading to a 6-4 win over Kansas City. They will need another solid start from him if they want to win this game.

White Sox Going For Third Win In Seven Games

The White Sox aren’t playing well at the moment and they’ve lost four of their last six games. They will try to turn things around with a win over the Athletics, which will give them their third win in their last seven games.

Chicago is averaging 4.13 runs per game. Their .239 batting average is 22nd in the league. Their .295 on base percentage is 30th, while their .388 slugging percentage is 24th.

Andrew Benintendi leads Chicago with a .275 batting average, while Luis Robert Jr. leads the team with 34 home runs and 69 RBI.

Chicago’s pitching hasn’t been good, with the team giving up 5.16 runs per game. Opponents have a .248 batting average against the White Sox, which is 18th in the league. Their 4.87 ERA is 26th, while their 1.41 WHIP is 24th.

In his last start, Clevinger gave up six hits and four runs in five innings, leading to a 6-3 loss to Seattle. They will need a better effort from him if they want to get the win.
