This 2018 World Series rematch between the Los Angeles Dodgers (79-49) and Boston Red Sox (66-61) started on Friday. Boston took an early 3-0 lead, but Los Angeles returned to their hotel rooms with a 7-4 victory. Boston won game two 8-5. These are two explosive offenses battling head-to-head. Mookie Betts is back in Boston, Justin Turner is facing his former Dodgers teammates, and historic Fenway Park gets to play host. At 1:35 p.m. EDT on Sunday, it'll mark the last meeting between the teams in 2023 unless October has other ideas.

 

 

L.A. Heat

Friday's comeback pushed the Dodgers to 20-3 in August. The NL West is comfortably theirs, but suddenly the MLB-best Braves are only a handful of games away. It begins with an offense that's second in runs per game this season. MVP candidates (and former MVPs) Mookie Betts and Freddie Freeman are leading the way. As a unit, Los Angeles is third in OBP, third in slugging percentage, and third in team OPS. They're second in walk rate, second in home runs, and 11th in strikeout rate. Good luck getting through this lineup.

Most of the season has been rough for the Dodgers on the mound, but August is going to finish as the staff's strongest month by far. They finished every other month (excluding two games in March) with an ERA of at least 4.46. Through 205.0 August innings, the Dodgers' ERA was 2.77. Opponents have a .214 batting average against them this month. Los Angeles' staff collectively has a 1.034 WHIP, 3.60 K/BB, and 79 tOPS+ in August. That tOPS+ is the first monthly number below 100 (the league average is set at 100, and lower is better for pitchers) in 2023. This game's pitching plan is undecided, but with how things are going, manager Dave Roberts should trust any Dodger that toes the rubber.

Boston Bouncing Up and Down

Inconsistencies have plagued the Red Sox all year, and it may be the only thing that keeps them out of the postseason. As it stands on Saturday, they're 3.5 games back of the last AL wild-card spot. Pitching is what makes or breaks Boston's success. Tanner Houck gets this start hoping to successfully contribute to the Red Sox's efforts. Houck returned from an IL stint on August 22, allowing three runs over 5.0 innings in Houston. Now he's back home, where he's earned a 5.75 ERA after seven starts. Of his last eight starts, Boston has only won one of them. Maybe a long-awaited homecoming will change things.

Of course, most Boston pitchers expect run support in Fenway. This year's Red Sox team delivers, putting up 5.3 runs per game at home. As a team, Boston is third in batting average, sixth in OBP, and fifth in slugging percentage. They're also sixth in strikeout rate, forcing the defense to make plays often. The Red Sox may not draw many walks, but they still punish pitching, resulting in only five teams outscoring them this season.
