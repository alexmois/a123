The Houston Astros and Detroit Tigers will take the field on Sunday at Comerica Park. First pitch is at 1:40 p.m. ET.

Houston (72-58 SU and 64-66 RL) is starting right-hander Justin Verlander. The 40-year-old is 9-6 with a 3.19 ERA and a 1.18 WHIP in 20 outings with New York (NL) and the Astros.

Detroit (59-69 SU and 68-60 RL) is countering with righty Alex Faedo. The 27-year-old is 2-4 with a 4.91 ERA and a 0.97 WHIP in his ten starts this year.

The Astros are -190 moneyline favorites and the game total is nine runs.

How will Verlander fare against his old club?

Houston is hunting down Texas in its division, but Seattle is right there with it. Will the AL West division title race come down to the wire?

The Astros average 4.85 runs (8th) and hit .252 (14th) with a .743 OPS (12th). They've hit 167 long balls (9th) and stolen 93 bases (14th) this year. The Houston pitching staff has posted a 3.96 ERA (9th) and a 1.29 WHIP (18th) with 59 quality starts (3rd).

Verlander will start against his old team on Sunday. The 18th-year veteran, who spent the first 12-plus years with Detroit, held Boston scoreless in his last start. It was a better showing than in his previous start when he gave up five runs (four earned) and nine hits to Miami. Verlander has held nine of his last ten opponents to three or fewer runs.

Detroit has shown progress in 2023

The Tigers may not be ready for the playoffs, but they did offer fans some excitement and hope that they're on the right path. Will they earn their stripes against Verlander on Sunday?

Detroit scores 4.00 runs per game (29th) and hits .235 (28th) with a .681 OPS (29th). It's launched 131 homers (24th) and stolen 58 bases (28th) this year. The pitching staff has compiled a 4.41 ERA (19th) and a 1.26 WHIP (11th) with 31 quality starts (28th).

Faedo will start for the home team on Sunday afternoon at Comerica. In his last start, the second-year pro gave up four runs (two earned) and three hits in six innings vs. the Cubs. He has surrendered only five earned runs in his last four starts combined (21.2 IP).

Insiders Status:

Detroit scored a come-from-behind victory on Friday, continuing its stretch of solid play. The loss was another blow to a Houston team that was defeated 17-1 by Boston the previous day. Detroit will ride the momentum of that win into Sunday's game against the former Tiger Verlander, keeping the game close at the least.

The Houston starter has been a below-average pitcher on the road in 2023, compiling a 3-3 record with a 4.41 ERA and a 1.43 WHIP in nine road starts. While he was solid in his last outing at home vs. Boston, he has surrendered 16 hits and seven runs (six earned) in two road starts with the Astros. The 40-year-old had just six strikeouts in those outings, compared to 16 in his two home starts. Against a hungry Tigers club, I believe he will come up short, giving up four or five runs.

Houston sports a 15-22 run line record as road favorites this season. Bet on Detroit to cover in Sunday's matinee game at Comerica Park!

Full-Game Total Pick

Insiders Status:

Rating:

The over is 35-26-2 in Detroit's home games this season, the fourth-highest home over winning percentage (57.4%) in MLB. It's 27-22-1 (55.1%) when Detroit is a home underdog, too. The over is 52-43-1 (54.7%) when Houston is the betting favorite.

I'm placing a wager on over nine runs in tomorrow's Houston-Detroit matinee.

The over, 7-3 in Houston's last ten games, looks like a winning bet given Sunday's pitching matchup. The youngster Faedo is facing an Astros club hitting .263 BA/.344 OBP/.435 SLG/.779 OPS in August and averaging 5.1 runs per game. The 'Stros score 5.1 runs per game on the road this season, as well. Faedo hasn't given up a lot of runs recently, but he's walked 11 batters in his last five starts and given up two HRs in three of his ten outings. Houston, 9th in MLB in homers, will take advantage of those opportunities.

Verlander has been a sub-par road starter in 2023, a trend I expect to continue tomorrow. The Tigers are not backing down from anyone these days and will be chomping at the bit to prove they belong against the former Detroit star. Its team batting average (.249), slugging percentage (.440), and OPS (.748) in August are higher than any other month, and it has been a better hitting club in the second half than the first half of the season.
