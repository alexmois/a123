Friday's 3-1 win by the Los Angeles Angels (62-67 before Saturday's outcome) was their first win in Queens since May 2017. After this Sunday afternoon battle against the New York Mets (59-70), the Halos will say goodbye to Citi Field until 2025. The goal is for the Angels to leave with another win, but the Mets are gunning for the same thing. At 12:05 p.m. EDT, these two teams will test each other for the final time this season.

*This game was published before Saturday's results

Angels August Fall From Grace

The Angels went all-in at the trade deadline, hoping to force their way into October. Instead, this month has been their worst yet, with Friday's win bringing them to 6-16. August is going to be their first losing month of the season. It begins with a pitching staff that has an ERA over 6.00 as August's conclusion approaches. Between the rotation and bullpen, it has been a disaster on the mound. Griffin Canning rejoined the team on August 14, and his first two appearances came out of the bullpen. He's ready to start again on Sunday, looking to lead Los Angeles to another victory. Three of his last four outings have ended with a win. Canning has allowed five runs in the 7.0 innings he's pitched this month. His home run problems have persisted through his absence too. Still, his 4.61 ERA in 2023 is a welcome sight compared to what the team is getting lately.

Scoring hasn't been a problem for the Halos this year, even with a ton of injuries. That includes Mike Trout, who came back for one game before returning to the IL. Los Angeles is fortunate that MLB home run leader Shohei Ohtani can bat through his torn UCL. He paces the Angels lineup, which is fourth in long balls and sixth in slugging percentage. Those big blasts have the team 12th in runs per game despite strikeout and base-stealing problems. If trade acquisitions Randal Grichuk and C.J. Cron ever heat up they could cause even more issues for opposing pitchers.

New York Blues

What a miserable Metropolitans season. A record-high payroll that featured two future Hall of Fame starting pitchers (both traded), arguably MLB's best closer (who hasn't pitched an inning all season), and two-star infielders in the heart of the batting order has crumbled. Friday night saw the team tumble into last place when the expectation for 2023 was improving upon 2022's 101-win season. Friday also was New York's third straight game scoring fewer than three runs, predictably resulting in three losses. The Mets are great at drawing walks, avoiding strikeouts, and hitting home runs. However, mastering the three true outcomes has them only 20th in runs per game. They're only 25th in batting average, 22nd in hard-hit rate, 21st in slugging percentage, and 19th in OBP. It seems as if hitting more singles and doubles would help this team a lot. 

On the mound, the Mets haven't been much better this year. With the three biggest healthy arms on new teams now, it'll be on the rest of the roster to get outs moving forward. Enter David Peterson, who would probably only pitch at home if given the option. In 34.1 home innings, he has only allowed 12 runs (11 earned), whereas he owns a 7.74 ERA on the road. Between the rotation and bullpen, only one of his home outings ended with more than two runs charged to his name. That was against the Braves back in April. The lefty doesn't typically pitch deep into games, so New York's battered bullpen will get plenty of time to pitch. However, there's an excellent shot that they'll take the hill with the lead with Peterson leading the way at home.
