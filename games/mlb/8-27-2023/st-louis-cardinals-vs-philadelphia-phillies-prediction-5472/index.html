The St. Louis Cardinals (56-74, 61-67 RL, 60-63-5 O/U) and the Philadelphia Phillies (71-58, 54-73 RL, 54-62-11 O/U) conclude a three-game series on Sunday afternoon. The Cardinals will send rookie Drew Rom to the mound. The Phillies will reply with veteran Aaron Nola. The Phillies recorded a 7-2 win on Friday and a 12-1 win on Saturday.

Rom Makes Second Start

Drew Rom will make the start to conclude the series. Rom was clobbered in his MLB debut, conceding eight runs (six earned) in 3.2 innings in a road bout against the Pirates, and was charged with the loss. The rookie was acquired in a deal with the Orioles last month. He didn’t post strong numbers in the minor leagues, issuing a 4.82 ERA and a 1.57 WHIP accompanied by a 9-6 record in 97 innings pitched. Rom has a 3.71 ERA and a 1.57 WHIP in his minor league career. 

The Cardinals are at the end of a road trip. They have dropped back-to-back series, losing three of four home meetings against the Mets followed by two defeats in three road meetings in Pittsburgh. The pitching continues to be exploited, surrendering 21 runs against the Pirates, and are now 3-7 in their last ten clashes.

Nolan Arenado has been quiet, recording only 10 RBIs this month and three in his last ten games. The veteran star is having another outstanding year, delivering 26 homers with 87 RBIs and an .833 OPS. Arenado is 3 for 18 with one RBI in his career against Nola.

The Cardinals are scoring an average of 4.56 runs per game, marking them 14th. The pitching continues to be exploited and has a 4.70 team ERA, pegging them 24th.

Nola Starts, Phillies Rebound

Aaron Nola will take the mound on Sunday. Nola rebounded with a solid performance last time out, limiting the Giants to only two runs in seven innings and collecting the win. The veteran has been shaky, posting a 4.84 ERA this month, and has a 4.49 ERA and a 1.15 WHIP accompanied by an 11-8 record in 160.1 innings pitched on the season. Nola has shut down the Cardinals, recording a 2.69 ERA and a 5-3 record in nine career meetings.

Philadelphia rebounded from a series road loss to the Nationals by earning two victories in three home bouts against a struggling Giants team this week, allowing 13 runs in the series. They have a solid three-game cushion in the top spot of the NL wildcard picture. The Phillies are only 5-5 in their last ten games.

Alec Bohm went a stellar 8 for 19 with three RBIs against the Cardinals last season and 2 for 4 with a home run on Friday. The 27-year-old first baseman is having a breakout season, collecting 77 RBIs and a .765 OPS. Bohm has posted 12 RBIs on the month and a .782 OPS on the month.

The Phillies are averaging 4.76 runs on the year, marking them 11th. The pitching staff is carrying a 3.98 team ERA, good for 10th.
