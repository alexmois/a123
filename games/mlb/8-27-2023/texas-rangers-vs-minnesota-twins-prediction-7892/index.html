The struggling American League West co-leading Texas Rangers (73-56, T-1st AL West) complete a four-game series Sunday on the road at Target Field against the American League Central-leading Minnesota Twins (67-63, 1st AL Central). Texas was defeated by Minnesota 12-2 on Friday in the second game of a four-game series. But on Saturday, the Rangers snapped an eight-game losing streak with a 6-2 win. The Rangers are tied for 1st in the AL West with Seattle while the Twins hold a six-game lead at the top of the AL Central. Texas will play without four pitchers as well as position players Josh Jung and Brad Miller. Minnesota will play without six pitchers as well as position players Byron Buxton, Willi Castro, Nick Gordon, Alex Kirilloff, and Josh Miranda.

 

Texas producing at the plate, but pitching staff is struggling

The Texas Rangers are second in four major hitting categories. Texas is second best in runs scored (715), team batting average (.269), on base percentage (.338) and slugging percentage (.457). Adolis Garcia is the team leader in both home runs with 31 and RBI with 93. Corey Seager is second in home runs with 24 and Marcus Semien is second in RBI with 80. Texas misses All-Star Josh Jung who has 24 doubles, one triple, 22 home runs and 67 RBI but is on the IL. Texas has hit 276 doubles, 15 triples and 178 home in 128 games.

Texas is 7th in WHIP at 1.24, 8th in Quality Starts with 56, 11th in batting average allowed at .242 and 14th in team ERA at 4.13. Nathan Eovaldi is the leader in victories for Texas with 11 and Andrew Heaney is the leader in strikeouts with 127. Texas will hand the ball to Jordan Montgomery on Sunday. This season the left-hander is 8-10 with an ERA of 3.12 and 134 strikeouts.

Minnesota is first in the AL Central thanks to solid pitching

The Minnesota Twins are in the top seven of four major pitching categories. Minnesota is first in Quality Starts (64), third in WHIP (1.21), 5th in batting average allowed (.238) and 7th in team ERA (3.92). Pablo Lopez is leading Minnesota in victories with 9 and in strikeouts with 191. Minnesota will hand the ball to Bailey Ober on Sunday. This season the right hander is 6-6 with an ERA of 3.41 and 116 strikeouts.

Minnesota has been average at the plate this season as the Twins are 10th in slugging percentage (.423), 15th in runs scored (580), 16th in on base percentage (.320) and 21st in team batting average (.241). Max Kepler is the team leader in home runs with 21 while Joey Gallo has hit 20 and Michael A. Taylor has smacked 19. Carlos Correa is the leader in RBI with 57 and Kepler is second with 49. Byron Buxton is out injured, and in just 85 games played has 17 home runs and 42 RBI. Minnesota has hit 218 doubles, 17 triples and 180 home runs in 129 games.
