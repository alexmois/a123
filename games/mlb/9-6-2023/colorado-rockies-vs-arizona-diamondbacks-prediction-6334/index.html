The Arizona Diamondbacks play host to the Colorado Rockies on Wednesday night in an NL West showdown from Chase Field. The Rockies have been one of baseball's worst teams for much of the season but have played better in the second-half, as they've played the role of spoiler. For the Diamondbacks, they catch themselves in a unique position, as they are right in the thick of the National League Wild Card race. Arizona has dominated the series between these two this season but even being at home in a rematch, should make for an interesting matchup in this one.

Rockies Limping Through September

The Rockies enter Wednesday afternoon with the worst record in the National League and while they've been better in the second half, as mentioned earlier, they have seen some of those same woes emerge of late. They've only gone 2-8 over their last ten games and have one of baseball's worst records on the road this season. At 21-48 away from home, they will need plenty of help in this one if they want to spring the upset against a divisional rival. Given their struggles against the Diamondbacks as well this season, it'll be interesting to see how they carry themselves as they head into this day game tilt.

According to MLB.com, Chris Flexen will get the ball on Wednesday as he looks for his second win of the season. With a 1-6 record and 6.89 ERA, his numbers have left quite a bit to be desired as the season winds down. His last time out against Arizona, he went six innings, allowing four runs, and while far from an elite performance, at least allowed him to eat up some innings at the bullpen's benefit. Expect a similar performance in this one, though going on the road for the rematch could make things interesting.

Offensively, the Rockies have struggled to give their pitchers much support this season. Kris Bryant (finger) remaining out hasn't helped matters, though he remains the only absence in the lineup. Ryan McMahon (22 home runs, 69 RBI) has established himself as the premier bat in the lineup, though the lack of help around him has been their downfall. Arizona's staff should be able to navigate this lineup with ease, especially with one of the lowest team batting averages in baseball.

Diamonds Caught In NL Wild Card Race

After a red-hot start to the season, Arizona has cooled off since the All-Star break and suddenly they find themselves in the thick of the National League Wild Card race. The division is out of reach but Arizona knows that they have to make the most out of their remaining games against Colorado, especially if they want to find themselves playing postseason football. They've gone 4-6 over their last ten games, which is another mark that they need to turn around. Add in a winning record at home and dominance over Colorado this season, should have them feeling good about their chances in this one.

According to MLB.com, Zach Davis will get the ball in this one, as the experienced righty looks to make his mark on the rotation. He enters with a 2-5 record and a 6.45 ERA, both of which are numbers that will need improvement if they want his starts to coincide with wins. He's been sharp in each of his last two starts, combining to go 11 innings, allowing one run in each start. If that's a sign of things to come over these next few weeks, Arizona will be feeling great.

Arizona's offense has lacked a punch as well of late, though with Flexen on the mound on the other side, they have to be thinking that this is a great opportunity for them. They're only down Dominic Fletcher (finger) in the lineup and get some consistency going over these last few weeks could be key. Corbin Carroll (.282 average, .361 OBP) leads the way, while Christian Walker (30 hom runs, 90 RBI) has established himself as one of the premier power bats in the National League of late. Add in a few other All Stars and this team simply has too much firepower for Colorado to be able to match.
