Philadelphia Phillies Maintaining Hold on Top Wild Card Spot in National League

Philadelphia won their second straight and seventh in the last 10 games as they took the opening game of this series Monday night. The Phillies entered Tuesday 76-61 on the year and stood second in the NL East, 14.5 games behind the Braves for the top spot in the division. They held the top wild-card spot in the National League and were six games clear of the Marlins to remain on the right side of the playoff cut line. On Monday night, Philadelphia finished with 16 hits as Bryson Stott (run) had four and Alec Bohm (three RBI) added three in the game. Trea Turner (his 22nd), Kyle Schwarber (his 40th) and Edmundo Sosa (his ninth) each homered in the victory for the Phillies. Taijuan Walker (15-5) earned the win on the mound as he allowed four runs on five hits with three walks and four strikeouts over five innings of work. Jose Alvarado worked the ninth, allowing no runs on no hits with two walks and two strikeouts, for his seventh save of the season.

Zack Wheeler is on the bump for the Phillies as he logs his 28th start of the season in this contest. He comes in 10-6 on the year with a 3.62 ERA, a 1.067 WHIP, 30 walks and 185 strikeouts over 164 innings of work to date. Wheeler didn’t factor in the decision in his last start, which came Friday on the road against the Brewers. He threw six innings, allowing three runs on five hits with no walks and 10 strikeouts in a game the Phillies dropped 7-5. In his last three starts, Wheeler is 1-1 with a 3.60 ERA, a 0.80 WHIP, one walk and 25 strikeouts over 20 innings of work. Wheeler makes his ninth career start against the Padres in this contest. He is 3-1 with a 2.31 ERA, a 1.026 WHIP, eight walks and 60 strikeouts over 50.2 innings of work against them. Wheeler is 1-0 with a 2.12 ERA, a 1.471 WHIP, four walks and 28 strikeouts over 17 innings of action in three career starts at Petco Park.

San Diego Padres Running Out of Time to Make Playoff Push

San Diego saw their three-game win streak snapped as they were beaten in the opening game of this series Monday night. The Padres entered Tuesday 65-74 and stood fourth in the NL West, 20.5 games behind the Dodgers for the top spot in the division. They were 6.5 games behind the Diamondbacks and Reds, who were tied for the final wild-card spot in the National League. On Monday night, San Diego got two hits each from Ha-Seong Kim (three RBI), Manny Machado (run) and Trent Grisham (run, RBI) only to fall short. The Padres were four of 15 with runners in scoring position and left 12 runners on base in the game. Fernando Tatis Jr. hit his 22nd homer of the year in a losing effort. Rich Hill (7-14) took the loss on the mound as he was tagged for six runs on seven hits with two walks and three strikeouts in 1.1 innings of work.

Michael Wacha is on the mound for the Padres as he makes his 20th start of the season in this contest. He comes in 11-2 with a 2.85 ERA, a 1.118 WHIP, 35 walks and 96 strikeouts over 107.1 innings of work on the year. Wacha earned the win in his last start, which came against the Giants at home Friday night. He threw six innings, allowing two runs on six hits with two walks and six strikeouts in a game the Padres won 7-3. In his last three starts, Wacha is 2-0 with a 3.78 ERA, a 1.44 WHIP, nine walks and 18 strikeouts over 16.2 innings of work. Wacha makes his 10th appearance and eighth start against the Phillies in this contest. He is 3-2 with a 4.98 ERA, a 1.615 WHIP, 16 walks and 39 strikeouts over 43.1 innings of work in those outings. Wacha is 11-3 with a 2.39 ERA, a 1.106 WHIP, 31 walks and 77 strikeouts over 94 innings of action in 16 career starts at Petco Park.
