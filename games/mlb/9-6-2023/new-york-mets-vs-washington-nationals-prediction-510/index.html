With this Wednesday battle, the New York Mets (63-74; before Tuesday's results) and Washington Nationals (62-76) will collide for the final time in 2023. Depending on how Tuesday's game finishes, it could be the deciding game in the season series. While neither side will see the postseason, bragging rights are always nice. Nationals Park will host the last battle between the NL East's worst teams. The first pitch is scheduled for 7:05 p.m. EDT on Wednesday. Here's what lies ahead.

 

*Published before Tuesday's game

The Miring Mets

🐻‍❄️💥 pic.twitter.com/gndsdvOBbv

— New York Mets (@Mets) September 3, 2023

So, a record-high payroll wasn't enough for the Mets to make the playoffs, let alone bring another title to Queens. Still, building toward the future parade begins now. On offense, rumors are swirling that Pete Alonso, who has 41 homers (3rd) in 129 games, may be traded this offseason. For now, he'll continue to lead a lineup that's top 10 in walk rate and strikeout rate, while ranking 12th in home runs. New York also has a higher stolen base percentage than every other club. With all of that, most would assume this is an offense that scores pretty often. However, that assumption would be wrong, with the Mets only 20th in runs per game entering this series. They are only 24th in batting average and run-scoring percentage, a sign that timely hits are what the team has been missing all season. 

On the mound, injuries have plagued the Mets all season. Thanks to a few trades, a lot of new names are getting a shot to pitch too. With this game's starter undecided, a new arm may be delivering the ball on Wednesday. If manager Buck Showalter is smart, he'll do everything in his power to avoid a bullpen game. New York's relievers began the series last in collective WAR at -0.2. The bullpen is 27th in FIP, 21st in ERA, and 23rd in walk rate. Best of luck to whoever starts this game, because pitching deep into the night may be the Mets' only shot at winning.

Washington's Woeful September Start

It may not matter much toward a title, but the Nationals dropped their first three games of the month as part of a five-game losing streak. They come into this series looking to snap that first, then win this game second to take the season set against the Mets. If this becomes a tiebreaker, Joan Adon will be tasked with the start and trying to tip the scales in Washington's favor. He has given up 20 hits, five walks, four home runs, and 16 runs in 15.0 home innings. Adon's last five outings (all in August) are his five starts of the season. He has a 6.00 ERA in the role this year. Washington also has a rough bullpen, one that's 27th in ERA, 28th in WAR, and 30th in FIP. Adon needs to deliver if the Nationals want to take Wednesday's contest.

As for the offense, the Nationals rack up hits. Thanks to the league's lowest strikeout rate, Washington had a .257 team batting average (9th) before the series started. Due to a higher soft-hit rate than every other team, the Nationals are only 22nd in slugging percentage despite the high average. Only one club has fewer long balls. Washington is an above-average base-stealing team both in total and success rate, but a 6.9% walk rate (27th) hinders that success. The final product is a lineup that is only 21st in runs per game, one that needs to add dimensions other than consistent contact to the mix.
