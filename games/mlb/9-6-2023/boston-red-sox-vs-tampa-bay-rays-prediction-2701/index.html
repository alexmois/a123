The Boston Red Sox and Tampa Bay Rays will wrap up their three-game series on Wednesday at Tropicana Field. First pitch is at 6:40 p.m. ET.

Boston (71-66 SU and 69-68 RL) is starting right-hander Nick Pivetta. The 30-year-old is 9-7 with a 4.49 ERA and a 1.20 WHIP in 33 appearances (11 starts).

Tampa Bay (83-54 SU and 70-67 RL) is countering with right-hander Tyler Glasnow. The 30-year-old is 7-5 with a 3.17 ERA and a 1.05 WHIP in 16 outings this season.

The Red Sox are solid spoilers

Boston scored a 7-3 win on Labor Day, holding Tampa Bay scoreless after a three-run first inning. It's 8-3 in its last 11 road games.

The Red Sox score 5.00 runs per game (8th) and hit .264 (3rd) with a .768 OPS (5th). They've launched 165 home runs (15th) and stolen 92 bases (18th) this season. The pitching staff has compiled a 4.53 ERA (21st) and a 1.35 WHIP (20th) with 38 quality starts (26th).

Tampa Bay is good, but can it be great?

Tampa Bay lost two of three in Cleveland last week and couldn't find its footing on Monday. Can it get back on track before Baltimore pulls away in the AL East title chase?

The Rays average 5.33 runs (4th) and hit .259 (6th) with a .775 OPS (4th). They've hit 198 long balls (4th) and stolen 147 bases (2nd) this year. The TB pitching staff has posted a 3.84 ERA (5th) and a 1.18 WHIP (2nd) with 45 quality starts (18th).

Glasnow will toe the rubber for the home team on Wednesday at Tropicana Field. The eighth-year pro is 3-2 with a 2.98 ERA and a 0.86 WHIP in eight home starts this season. In his last outing, he held the Guardians to three runs with six strikeouts in seven innings. He faced the Red Sox on June 3, holding them to one run and four hits with six strikeouts in 5.1 innings.

Full-Game Side Bet

Insiders Status:

Rating:

I'm betting on the Rays to cover the run line on Wednesday at home with Glasnow toeing the rubber. The big righty has been reliable at home (2.98 ERA) and in night games (2.97 ERA and 0.99 WHIP) and should have plenty in the tank for September after starting the season on the IL (93.2 IP in 2023).

The Red Sox aren't going to make the postseason. They won two of three vs. Kansas City but lost too many important games recently against the Dodgers and Astros (1-5 record). They can still play spoiler on occasion, but for the most part, they're overmatched against the elite clubs. On Wednesday, they will struggle against Glasnow, failing to give their starter the run support he needs to keep the game close.

Tampa Bay has a significant advantage on the mound and in the batter's box — bet on it to cover tomorrow!

The over is 42-26-1 in Rays home games and 22-15-1 in their division games. The over is also 39-28-2 in Red Sox road games and 21-13 in their division games.

Boston and Tampa Bay will score plenty of runs, pushing the total over in Wednesday's series finale at The Trop.
