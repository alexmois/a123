The Toronto Blue Jays wrap up their West Coast trip in Oakland on Wednesday against the Athletics. The pitching matchup on Wednesday will feature Blue Jays' lefty Hyun Jin Ryu (3-1, 2.48) taking on A's lefty JP Sears (3-11, 4.60). The first pitch is scheduled for 3:37 PM EST.

Down to the Wire

The Toronto Blue Jays are fighting for one of the three wildcard spots in the American League with the season over in less than a month. Coming into this series, the Jays went just 5-5 in their last ten games but picked up series wins in each of their last two series against the Nationals and on the road in Colorado. On Monday night, the Blue Jays took a 6-5 win in 10 innings. Toronto scored three runs in the top of the tenth and held on after closer Romano allowed two runs in the bottom half of the tenth.

The Blue Jays will give the ball to lefty Hyun Jin Ryu in the series finale on Wednesday afternoon. Ryu is 3-1 in six starts since returning from the IL in July. Ryu has allowed 23 hits in 29 innings pitched with an ERA of 2.48 since returning to the rotation. He has a K/9 rate of 7.1 and his WHIP is 1.03. In his last outing, Ryu went five innings and allowed four hits and two runs in an eventual 13-9 Blue Jays' win over Colorado. Ryu has gone five innings in each of his last four starts but the Jays have yet to stretch him beyond that as he continues his comeback from Tommy John surgery.

Key Injuries: C Dan Jansen, SS Bo Bichette, and 3B Matt Chapman are out.

Bottoms UP

The Oakland A's are once again in the cellar in the AL West Division and the only intrigue remaining in their season will be whether or not the team finishes with the worst record in baseball as they battle the Royals for that dubious distinction over the final month of the season. Over the weekend, the A's completed a rare sweep of the sinking Angels and have won four of five games heading into this series with the Blue Jays. Oakland fell 6-5 in the series opener in 10 innings. Trailing 6-3, Lawrence Butler belted a two-run homer in the bottom of the tenth to close the gap to a run.

The Oakland A's will turn to southpaw JP Sears in the series finale against Toronto. Sears is 3-11 in 27 starts including eight quality starts. He has allowed 140 hits in 146.2 innings pitched with an ERA of 4.60. He has a K/9 rate of 8.5 and his WHIP is 1.22. Sears is 1-4 at home this season with an ERA of 4.98 in 12 starts. He has struck out 56 batters in 65 innings at home. In his last five starts, Sears is 1-2 with an ERA of 7.20. He has allowed 37 hits in 25 innings pitched with 27 strikeouts.

Full-Game Side Bet

Rating:

The Blue Jays are by far the more desperate team heading into the series finale on Wednesday afternoon. In a game that will likely feature both bullpens by the sixth inning, I'm leaning heavily toward the Blue Jays. Toronto strengthened their bullpen at the trade deadline while the A's bullpen has been one of the worst in baseball this season and is also banged up currently with two relievers on the IL. Ryu will get the Jays to their middle relievers with minimal damage. He has gone five innings in four straight starts and has allowed just two runs in three of the starts. Sears has been banged around in his last five starts with an ERA over seven in that span. Look for the hungry Jays to close this series out with a win on Wednesday afternoon.

Take the Jays with the money line.
