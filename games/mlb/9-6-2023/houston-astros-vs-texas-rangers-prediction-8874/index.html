The Houston Astros (79-61, 71-69 RL, 74-63-3 O/U) collide with the Texas Rangers (76-62, 73-65 RL, 68-60-10 O/U) in a divisional showdown on Wednesday evening. The Astros will be sending Justin Verlander to the mound. The Rangers will reply with Max Scherzer. Houston leads the season series 8-4.

Verlander Seeks Rebound from Rare Bad Outing

The Astros will have Justin Verlander on the mound on Wednesday. Verlander is looking to rebound from a bad outing where he surrendered six runs on eight hits in six innings against the Yankees in a loss. The veteran ace had registered 11 consecutive scoreless innings prior to that outing and is sporting a 3.34 ERA and a 1.18 WHIP complemented by a 10-7 record in 129.1 innings pitched on the year. Verlander has not pitched against Texas this season and has a 2.64 ERA and a 20-8 record in 211.1 career innings. 

The Astros recently won five in a row but were dealt a sweep by the Yankees this past weekend. They rebounded with a blowout win in Monday's opener and a 14-1 win on Tuesday. They now lead the division by one game over Seattle atop the division. The Astros have actually been stronger on the road in recent series and have a better road record on the year. The squad is 7-3 in their last ten games.

Scherzer Continues Dominance

Max Scherzer will get the nod in this one. Scherzer was brilliant last time out, allowing zero runs on one hit in six innings against Minnesota, resulting in a no-decision. The veteran has been outstanding since he was acquired, allowing two or fewer runs in four of his last five outings, and is sporting a 3.55 ERA and a 1.10 WHIP accompanied by a 12-5 record in 144.1 innings. Scherzer held the Astros to one run in eight innings in July and has registered a 2.98 ERA and a 5-1 record in 60 career innings. 

Texas once held a huge lead atop the division but has dropped four of their last five series and now stands outside of a wildcard spot. They are a half-a-game behind the Blue Jays for the final spot. The squad needs series wins now. Texas has an upcoming series against the Jays who are just ahead of them in the standings. The Rangers are 4-6 in their last ten decisions.

Adolis Garcia has bashed three homers with 10 RBIs and a subpar .695 OPS against the Astros this season. The 30-year-old has been inconsistent recently but has 34 home runs along with 100 RBIs and a strong .823 OPS. Garcia has bashed two home runs and three RBIs this month. 

The Rangers continue to shine at the plate and are averaging 5.53 runs on the year, marking them third. The pitching staff has reported a 4.18 team ERA, positioning them 15th.
