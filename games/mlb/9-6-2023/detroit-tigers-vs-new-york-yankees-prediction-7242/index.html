The Detroit Tigers and New York Yankees will take the field on Wednesday at Yankee Stadium. First pitch is scheduled for 7:05 p.m. ET.

Detroit (63-74 SU and 71-66 RL) is starting right-hander Matt Manning. The 25-year-old is 5-4 with a 3.62 ERA and a 1.03 WHIP in 14 outings this season.

New York (68-69 SU and 67-70 RL) is starting righty Clarke Schmidt. The 27-year-old is 8-8 with a 4.56 ERA and a 1.34 WHIP in 28 appearances (27 starts).

The Tigers earned their stripes this season

Detroit bounced back after losing three of four to New York last week by sweeping the White Sox in Chicago. Can it finish strong with a winning record in September?

The Tigers score 3.97 runs per game (29th) and hit .235 (28th) with a .681 OPS (29th). They've hit 139 dingers (24th) and stolen 68 bases (27th) in 2023. The Detroit pitching staff sports a 4.45 ERA (19th) and a 1.27 WHIP (15th) with 36 quality starts (27th).

Manning will start for the visitors on Wednesday night in the Bronx. The third-year pro is 4-2 with a 3.57 ERA and a 1.10 WHIP in eight road starts this year. The Baby Bombers have breathed new life into NY

New York capitalized on the momentum it generated in its near-sweep of the Tigers in Detroit with a three-game sweep of the Astros in Houston. Can it continue its hot play at home?

The Yankees average 4.25 runs (22nd) and hit .228 (29th) with a .707 OPS (22nd). They've hit 197 home runs (5th) and stolen 86 bases (20th) in 2023. The NY pitching staff has compiled a 3.95 ERA (8th) and a 1.23 WHIP (6th) with 39 quality starts (25th).

Schmidt will take the mound for the Yankees on Wednesday. The fourth-year pro is 3-5 with a 3.91 ERA and a 1.21 WHIP in 15 outings (14 starts) at home. In his last start, he held the Tigers to three runs with seven strikeouts in 4.2 innings. He was 1-2 with a 5.16 ERA in six appearances last month.

Full-Game Side Bet

Insiders Status:

Rating:

Manning outpitched Schmidt last week in Detroit but wasn't awarded the win because Tigers relief pitcher Alex Lange gave up a three-run bomb with two outs in the bottom of the ninth. That won't happen twice.

The 25-year-old pitched well last month, posting a 2.80 ERA and a 0.96 WHIP in six starts (35.1 IP). His last four outings, in particular, were impressive, as he got off to a rocky start against Pittsburgh and Tampa Bay. In the last start, he held New York to two hits in six innings (no runs). I feel confident he'll continue his recent hot streak against the middling Yankees. New York has been warming up, but that's bound to cool by the end of this series.

Schmidt was less impressive in August, giving up 19 runs (17 earned) and 33 hits in six starts (29.2 IP). He has struggled with his command (seven walks in his last four starts) and has given up a long ball in four of his last five starts. It's likely his innings count is starting to wear on him — he has pitched a career-high 134.1 innings in 2023 after pitching just 57.2 innings in his first true big league season last year.

Manning has pitched well enough on the road this season to predict another solid start from the youngster on Wednesday in the Bronx. Bet on the Tigers to win straight-up!

Full-Game Total Pick

Insiders Status:

Rating:

The under cashed when these starters faced off last week, which I predict will be the winner again tomorrow.

The under is 37-29-2 in Tigers road games, the third-highest under winning percentage (56.1%) in MLB. It's also 53-41 in their games versus American League foes.

The Yankees scorched the Astros over the weekend, hitting seven long balls. Manning has only given up 11 this season, eight of which came in three games. He's been able to hold most of his opponents in the yard, something the Bronx Bombers rely on to score. New York hasn't fared well at the dish anywhere, even at Yankee Stadium (.227 BA/.302 OBP/.420 SLG/.722 OPS). I don't anticipate that changing in the final month of the regular season.

Detroit averages 4.0 runs on the road and only scored 3.9 runs per game overall last month. Schmidt's home ERA (3.91) is nearly a run and a half lower than his road ERA. He didn't have his best stuff against the Tigers last week, but still held them to three runs with seven strikeouts in 4.2 innings. If he can keep the ball in the yard tomorrow, I'm confident he and the Yanks' bullpen (lowest ERA in MLB) will do their part to keep the total low.
