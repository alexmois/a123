The San Francisco Giants face the Chicago Cubs with both teams elbowing for wild card position in the National League. The Giants have a 70-69 record but with only one win in their last seven games, they are on the outside of a National League wild card spot looking in. The Cubs have a 75-64 record and with seven wins in their last 10 games, they have leaped into the second wild card spot in the National League. For the series finale, the Giants will turn to Tristan Beck as their starting pitcher while the Cubs will turn to Jordan Wicks who has only allowed two runs in his last two starts.

Giants hope Beck helps out lineup

The Giants are having a great season but hope their recent skid leaves them out of the wild-card spot by the end of the year. Tristan Beck will start the upcoming game and he is having a strong season on the mound, allowing only 37 runs in 75.2 innings pitched while tossing 62 strikeouts. Additionally, with a .395 expected opponent Slugging Percentage, a 6.3 Walk Rate, and a 3.72 expected ERA, opponents are making weak contact and struggling to drive in runs against Beck this year.

Beck has stepped up on the mound but the lineup has struggled, scoring only 4.23 runs per game with only 12 runs in the last five games. Wilmer Flores is slashing .285/.351/.524 with 20 home runs and 178 total bases while LaMonte Wade Jr. is slashing .250/.369/.398 with 13 home runs and 148 total bases but the rest of the lineup has struggled. Only two active batters have 15 home runs or more while only six active batters have an OPS+ over 100 and opposing pitchers can limit the lineup as a result.

Cubs hope lineup and Wicks step up

The Cubs are having a great season and look to secure a spot in the postseason in the final few weeks of the season. Jordan Wicks is one of the team's top pitching prospects and he's stepped up in the two games he's started, allowing only two runs in 10 innings pitched while striking out 12 batters. In addition, with a .206 expected opponent Batting Average, a .292 expected opponent Slugging Percentage, and a 2.87 expected ERA, opponents have made minimal contact and are struggling to drive in runs against Wicks this year.

Wicks has stepped up but the lineup has carried the Cubs, scoring 5.05 runs per game with 31 runs in the last three games. Cody Bellinger is slashing .320/.363/.549 with 23 home runs and 225 total bases while Ian Happ is slashing .247/.364/.427 with 17 home runs with 209 total bases. Seiya Suzuki is slashing .267/.340/.450 with 15 home runs and 192 total bases while Patrick Wisdom and Christopher Morel have added 40 home runs and 270 total bases to add depth to the lineup, making the Cubs lineup tough to stop.
