The Pittsburgh Pirates (64-74) will be going for the series win when they face the Milwaukee Brewers (76-61) in the final game of their three-game series on Wednesday afternoon.

Freddy Peralta, 11-8, 3.35 ERA, will get the start for the Brewers. The Pirates will counter with Mitch Keller, 11-8, 3.53 ERA.

Milwaukee is 6-4 in its last 10 games against Pittsburgh.

**This preview was written before Tuesday’s game was played**

Brewers Going For Third Win In Five Games

The Brewers followed up their two-game winning streak by losing their last two games. They will try to keep the momentum going with a win over the Pirates, which will give them their third win in their last five games.

Milwaukee is averaging 4.38 runs per game. Their .236 batting average is 27th in the league. Their .315 on base percentage is 21st, while their .378 slugging percentage is 28th.

William Contreras leads the team with a .282 batting average. Willy Adames leads the team with 22 home runs, while Christian Yelich leads the team with 69 RBI.

Milwaukee’s pitching has been good, with the team giving up 4.23 runs per game. Opponents have a .231 batting average against the Brewers, which leads the league. Their 3.95 ERA is sixth, while their 1.21 WHIP is third.

In his last start, Peralta gave up two hits and one run in six innings, leading to a 7-5 win over Philadelphia. They will need a similar effort from him if they want to get the win.

Pirates Going For Seventh Win In Eight Games

The Pirates played well over the past week and they’ve won six of their last seven games. They will try to keep the momentum going with a win over the Brewers, which will give them their seventh win in their last eight games.

Pittsburgh is averaging 4.21 runs per game. Their .235 batting average is 26th in the league. Their .313 on base percentage is 22nd, while their .389 slugging percentage is 24th.

Ke'Bryan Hayes leads the Pirates with a .267 batting average. Jack Suwinski leads the team with 23 home runs, while Bryan Reynolds leads the team with 67 RBI.

Pittsburgh’s pitching hasn’t been good, with the team giving up 4.80 runs per game. Opponents have a .251 batting average against the Pirates, which is 20th in the league. Their 4.48 ERA is also 20th, while their 1.38 WHIP is 22nd.

In his last start, Keller gave up five hits and one run in six innings, leading to a 4-2 win over St. Louis. They will need another solid start from him if they want to win this game.
