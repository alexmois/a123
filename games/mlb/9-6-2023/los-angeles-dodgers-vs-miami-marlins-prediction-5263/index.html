Dodgers Cruising Toward Postseason

Los Angeles managed to take the finale of a four-game home set with the Braves in a showdown of the top teams in the National League Sunday afternoon to avoid a sweep. The Dodgers entered Tuesday 84-52 on the season and held a 14-game lead over Arizona for the division lead in the NL West. On Sunday, Los Angeles got three hits from Mookie Betts (RBI) while Will Smith and Jason Heyward (run) each added two in the win. Bobby Miller (9-3) earned the win on the mound as he threw seven innings, allowing one run on three hits with one walk and five strikeouts, before departing. Brusdar Graterol worked the ninth, allowing no runs on one hit with one walk and one strikeout, for his seventh save of the season.

Lance Lynn is on the mound for the Dodgers as he makes his 28th start of the season overall and his seventh since coming over in a trade from the White Sox. He is 10-10 with a 5.81 ERA, a 1.40 WHIP, 53 walks and 171 strikeouts over 155 innings of work this season. Lynn took the loss in his last start, which came against the Braves at home Thursday night. He threw 4.1 innings, allowing seven runs on seven hits with two walks and one strikeout in a game the Dodgers eventually dropped 8-7. In his last three starts, Lynn is 1-1 with a 5.19 ERA, a 1.44 WHIP, four walks and five strikeouts over 17.1 innings of work. Lynn makes his 10th career appearance and eighth start against the Marlins in this contest. He is 4-2 with one save, a 4.35 ERA, a 1.524 WHIP, 19 walks and 46 strikeouts over 41.1 innings of work against them. Lynn is 2-1 with a 7.08 ERA, a 1.82 WHIP, 12 walks and 23 strikeouts over 20.1 innings of work in four career starts at LoanDepot Park.

Marlins Fighting for NL Wild Card Spot

Miami swept the Nationals on the road in a four-game series over the weekend, sending them into this series with some momentum. The Marlins entered Tuesday 70-67 on the year and were third in the NL East, 20.5 games behind the Braves for the top spot in the division. They were half a game behind the Diamondbacks and Reds, who were tied for the final wild-card spot in the National League playoff picture. On Sunday, Miami got two hits each from Luis Arraez (two runs, RBI), Josh Bell (run) and Jesus Sanchez (run, RBI) in the contest. Arraez (his sixth) went deep in the victory for the Marlins. Sandy Alcantara (7-12) earned the win on the mound as he threw eight innings, allowing four runs (two earned) on nine hits with two walks and three strikeouts. Tanner Scott worked a scoreless ninth, allowing one hit with no walks and one strikeout, to earn his seventh save.

The Marlins have not officially named a starter but the expectation is that Edward Cabrera could be recalled from Triple-A Jacksonville of the International League to make his 18th start of the season here. He is 5-6 with a 4.79 ERA, a 1.468 WHIP, 52 walks and 93 strikeouts over 77 innings of work on the year. Cabrera didn’t factor in the decision in his last start, which came July 31 against the Phillies at home. He threw three innings, allowing two runs on two hits with six walks and three strikeouts in a game the Marlins went on to lose 4-2. In his last three starts, Cabrera is 0-1 with a 5.40 ERA, a 1.90 WHIP, 11 walks and nine strikeouts over 10 innings of work. Cabrera lost his lone career start against the Dodgers, which came at home on August 28, 2022. He threw 5.2 innings, allowing six runs on five hits with three walks and five strikeouts in an 8-1 Marlins loss. Cabrera is 8-4 with a 3.95 ERA, a 1.317 WHIP, 47 walks and 86 strikeouts over 82 innings of work in 17 career starts at LoanDepot Park.

Miami is in a crowded wild card field in the National League but pitching may prove to be their Achilles heel down the stretch.
