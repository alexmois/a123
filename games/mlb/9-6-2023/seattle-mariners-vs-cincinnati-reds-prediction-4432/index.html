On Wednesday, the Seattle Mariners (77-60, T-1st AL West) play the third and final game of a three-game series on the road against the Cincinnati Reds (72-68, 3rd NL Central). Seattle was defeated by Cincinnati 6-3 in the opening game of the series on Monday. Seattle has lost three of the last four and is now tied for first in the American League West with Houston. With its victory over Seattle on Monday, Cincinnati has won three of the last four. The Reds are 5 ½ games back in the National League Central while tied with Arizona for the third and final NL wildcard qualifying spot. Seattle will play without five pitchers as well as position players Jarred Kelenic, Evan White and Tom Murphy. Cincinnati will play without 11 pitchers as well as position players Curt Casali, Jonathan India, Matt McLain, Kevin Newman and Joey Votto.

Seattle has one of a baseball's best pitching staffs

Seattle is currently tied for first place in the American League West and the Mariners are battling for a division title thanks to a strong pitching staff and a much improved lineup. Seattle is first in team ERA with 3.69 as well as in WHIP at 1.18. Seattle has 66 Quality Starts which is second-best in baseball and the Mariners' pitching staff holds opposing hitters to a .237 average which is fourth-best in baseball. Wednesday’s starting pitcher Logan Gilbert is leading Seattle in victories with 12 and Luis Castillo is the leader in strikeouts with 183. Gilbert is 12-5 with an ERA of 3.56 and 162 strikeouts.

 

The Seattle lineup is becoming more and more productive. The Mariners batting average is below average at .244 (19th), but Seattle has scored 655 runs (11th), with an on-base percentage of .324 (12th) and a slugging percentage of .419 (13th). Cal Raleigh and Julio Rodriguez are tied for the team lead in home runs with 25 and Teoscar Hernandez is a close third with 24 dingers. Rodriguez is the leader in RBI with 89, while Hernandez is second with 84 and Eugenio Suarez is third with 83. Seattle has hit 244 doubles, 11 triples and 181 home runs in 137 games.

Spencer Steer is carrying the Reds offensively

With players such as Jonathan India, Matt McLain, Joey Votto and Kevin Newman out injured, Spencer Steer has stepped up his game to help carry the Reds. The first baseman has 31 doubles, two triples, 20 home runs and 76 RBI. Steer leads the Reds in doubles, home runs and RBI. Cincinnati as a team is batting .247, which is 18th and the Reds are 10th in runs scored with 661, 13th in on base percentage at .324 and 17th in slugging percentage .410.

Cincinnati has struggled all season on the mound. The Reds are 24th in four major pitching categories including ERA at .470, WHIP at 1.41, Quality Starts at 40 and batting average allowed at .257. Andrew Abbott is leading Cincinnati in victories with eight while Hunter Green, currently on the IL, is the leader in strikeouts with 114. Cincinnati will hand the ball to Lyon Richardson on Wednesday. The 23-year-old right-hander is 0-1 with an ERA 6.75 and 9 strikeouts.
