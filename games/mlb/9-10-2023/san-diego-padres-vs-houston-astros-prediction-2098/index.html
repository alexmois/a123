The San Diego Padres (67-75) will be going for the series win when they face the Houston Astros (80-62) in the final game of their three-game series on Sunday afternoon.

Rich Hill, 7-14, 5.60 ERA, will get the start for the Padres. The Astros will counter with J.P. France, 10-5, 3.72 ERA.

San Diego is 8-2 in its last 10 games against Houston.

**This preview was written before Saturday’s game was played**

Padres Going For Sixth Win In Eight Games

The Padres played well over the past week and they’ve won five of their last seven games. They will try to keep the momentum going with another win over the Astros, which will give them their sixth win in their last eight games and the series win.

San Diego is averaging 4.56 runs per game. Their .240 batting average is 23rd in the league. Their .326 on base percentage is 11th, while their .410 slugging percentage is 17th.

Ha-Seong Kim leads the Padres with a .273 batting average, while Juan Soto leads the team with 28 home runs and 85 RBI.

San Diego’s pitching has been good, with the team giving up 4.05 runs per game. Opponents have a .238 batting average against the Padres, which is sixth in the league. Their 3.79 ERA is fourth, while their 1.28 WHIP is 16th.

In his last start, Hill gave up seven hits and six runs in 1.1 innings, leading to a 9-7 loss to Philadelphia. They will need a significantly better performance from him if they want to win this game.

Astros Going For Fourth Win In Five Games

The Astros had their three-game winning streak snapped by the Padres in their last game. They will try to bounce back from the loss with a win on Sunday, which will give them their fourth win in their last five games and increase their lead in the AL West standings.

Houston is averaging 5.15 runs per game. Their .260 batting average is fifth in the league. Their .332 on base percentage is also fifth, while their .436 slugging percentage is sixth.

Kyle Tucker has been the big bat for the Astros. He leads the team with a .288 batting average, 26 home runs, and 99 RBI.

Houston’s pitching has been good, with the team giving up 4.35 runs per game. Opponents have a .244 batting average against the Astros, which is 14th in the league. Their 3.97 ERA is ninth, while their 1.29 WHIP is 17th.

In his last start, France gave up six hits and five runs in five innings, leading to a 13-6 win over Texas. They will need a better effort from him if they want to get the win.
