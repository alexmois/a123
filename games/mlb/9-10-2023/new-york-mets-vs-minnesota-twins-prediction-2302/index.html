New York Mets (64-76) vs. Minnesota Twins (74-67)

The Major League Baseball betting action goes on Sunday, September 10, 2023, with the New York Mets taking on the Minnesota Twins in the interleague battle at Target Field in Minneapolis, Minnesota, and the first pitch is set for 8:10 PM ET. 

New York and Minnesota close down a three-game series. The Twins won Friday’s opener 5-2, while the middle game of the series has been excluded from the analysis. Minnesota is a -175 moneyline fave for Sunday’s closer, and the totals sit at 8.5 runs. 

The Mets mustered just four hits in the opener

The New York Mets fell to 64-76 on the season following that 5-2 defeat at Target Field. It was their second straight loss and the third in five games, as the Mets’ offense had a bad day in the office. New York notched only four hits against Dallas Keuchel and four relievers, while Sean Reid-Foley took a loss after surrendering three earned runs in relief. 

The Mets are hitting .255/.313/.483 with six extra-base hits and nine home runs in the last seven days and 149 at-bats. On the pitching side of things, their bullpen holds a 1-3 record with two saves and a pedestrian 5.94 ERA in September (16.2 innings pitched). 

Tylor Megill is projected to take the hill on Sunday in Minneapolis. The 28-year-old right-hander is 8-7 with a 5.28 ERA, 1.66 WHIP, and 88/49 K/BB ratio in 21 starts (102.1 IP) in 2023. Megill has struggled all season long. He’s registered a 5.52 ERA, 5.33 FIP, and .920 opposing OPS over his last six starts and 31 innings of work.

The Twins are heating up at the right time

The Minnesota Twins improved to 74-67 on the season following Friday’s victory against the Mets. Carlos Correa launched a mammoth 439-foot solo home run, while Royce Lewis went 2-for-4 with a couple of RBI. It was Minnesota’s fifth win in seven games, and the Twins were topping the AL Central six and a half games above the Cleveland Guardians. 

The Twins are slashing a sturdy .293/.388/.500 with 13 extra-base hits and nine round-trippers in the last seven days and 198 at-bats. Their bullpen has thrown 26.2 frames so far this month, tallying a 3-1 record with two saves and a solid 3.04 ERA. 

Pablo Lopez will be on the bump Sunday, and the 27-year-old right-hander is 10-7 with a 3.64 ERA, 1.18 WHIP, and 199/46 K/BB ratio in 28 starts (170.2 IP) this season. Lopez has pitched very well of late, posting a 2.14 ERA, 3.43 FIP, and .708 opposing OPS over his previous seven starts and 42 innings of work.
