The Los Angeles Angels play host to the Cleveland Guardians on Sunday afternoon in an American League showdown from Angel Stadium. The Angels are well out of the playoff race, simply looking to play the role of spoiler over the last few weeks. For the Guardians, they've been unable to close the gap on the Twins atop the AL Central. This has been an even series over the course of this season and with little separating the two in the win-loss column, this one expects to be a great matchup as well.

Guardians Coming To Final Stretch

After a season which has season Cleveland move all over the AL Central standings, time is quickly running out on this group. With only a few weeks left, they still sit more than a handful of games behind the Twins atop the division, with little sign of seriously turning things around. They've only gone 5-5 over their last ten games, while a losing record on the road also has them wondering how they'll be able to close out the weekend. The schedule seems stacked against them as well, putting even more pressure on getting their confidence and momentum in a positive direction prior to their mid-week series kicks off.

According to MLB.com, the Guardians will give the ball off to Tanner Bibee, who has been a pleasant surprise in the rotation this season. The righty has a 10-3 record and an impressive 3.05 ERA, having not allowed more than two runs since back on August 19th. He is good for a steady five or six innings of action and has been able to keep the ball in the ballpark, which are both signs that should give Cleveland confidence in this one.

Bibee will need to be sharp in this one, considering some of the offensive struggles for Cleveland this season. They only average 4.1 runs per game and the struggles in the power department have haunted them at times, especially on the road. They are finally healthy, within the lineup, which is a good sign, though have struggled to get consistency going of late. Steven Kwan (.274 average) remains their spark plug, whole Jose Ramirez (21 home runs, .348 OBP) remains dangerous as well. It'll take a complete effort to find success in this one but having Bibee on the mound should take some pressure off this group early.

Angels Already Eyeing 2024

For the Angels, it has become an all too familiar tale, as they have plenty of talent and plenty of remarkable performances, only to fall short as the summer turns to fall. They sit in fourth place in the AL West, 15 games behind the Astros, nearly eliminated. It hasn't helped that they've gone 2-8 over their last ten games, though part of that comes from interesting lineup choices, as they tinker toward the off-season. Surprisingly, despite the poor record, they've been quite a bit better at home, playing nearly .500 football, though it might not be enough in this one.

According to MLB.com, Kenny Rosenberg will get the ball for the Angels in this one, with little known on the lefty. He went six innings in his last start against a strong Baltimore lineup, allowing six hits and three earned runs. In two innings in his previous appearance, he only allowed one run. With little tape on the lefty, it could play into their advantage in this matchup.

The lineup was dangerous at one point but injuries have been too much for them to overcome. While the numbers look impressive, the fact that they don't have many of the players that helped contribute to those numbers, makes things challenging. Looking at the injury report, the names of the jump of the page are Shohei Ohtani (oblique) and Mike Trout (wrist), who are both out. Beyond that, they are without nearly everyone slated to start on Opening Day. Over the last week, Luis Rengifo and Mike Moustakas have led the way, but with little help, this offense could struggle on Sunday.
