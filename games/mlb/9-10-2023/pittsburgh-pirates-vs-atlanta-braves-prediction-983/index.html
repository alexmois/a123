Pittsburgh Pirates Try to Earn Win on Getaway Day

Pittsburgh lost for just the third time in their last 10 games as they were beaten in the opening game of this series Friday night. The Pirates entered Saturday at 65-76 and stood fourth in the NL Central, 13.5 games behind the Brewers for the division lead. They were 8.5 games behind the Diamondbacks for the final wild card spot in the National League. On Friday night, Pittsburgh finished with six hits with no player recording more than one in the contest. Jack Suwinski hit his 24th homer of the season in the loss for the Pirates. Mitch Keller (11-9) took the loss on the mound as he allowed eight runs on 12 hits with no walks and four strikeouts over five innings of work.

The Pirates have not officially named a starter, but the expectation is that Luis Ortiz will make his 15th appearance and 13th start of the season here. He enters the game 4-4 with a 4.90 ERA, a 1.735 WHIP, 36 walks and 48 strikeouts over 68 innings of work on the year. Ortiz earned the win in his last outing, which was a start against the Brewers at home Monday night. He threw six innings, allowing two runs on five hits with three walks and four strikeouts in a game the Pirates won 4-2. In his last three appearances, two starts, Ortiz is 2-1 with a 5.02 ERA, a 1.74 WHIP, 10 walks and 14 strikeouts over 14.1 innings of work. Ortiz, in his 19th career major league appearance and 17th start, pitches against the Braves for the first time here. As a result, this marks his first career outing at Truist Park.

Atlanta Braves Rolling Toward Postseason

Atlanta won their second straight and eighth in the last 11 games as they rolled to the win in the opening game of this set Friday night. The Braves entered Friday with a record of 92-48 on the year and held a 15-game lead on the Phillies in the NL East, so they are cruising toward a division crown. Against Pittsburgh Friday night, Atlanta got three hits each from Ronald Acuna Jr. (two runs, two RBI) and Orlando Arcia (run, RBI) to lead a 12-hit attack. Eddie Rosario (his 21st) and Acuna Jr. (his 35th) each went deep in the victory. Bryce Elder (12-4) earned the win on the mound as he threw seven innings, allowing two runs on four hits with one walk and nine strikeouts.

The Braves have yet to name a starter for this contest but the buzz is that Allan Winans will get the call for his fourth start of the season. He is 1-1 with a 5.17 ERA, a 1.404 WHIP, four walks and 18 strikeouts over 15.2 innings of work this season. Winans took the loss in his last start, which came against the Mets back on August 21 at home. He threw 4.1 innings, allowing seven runs on nine hits with one walk and four strikeouts in a game the Braves lost 10-4. In his fourth career major league start, Winans faces the Pirates for the first time in this contest. His start against the Mets on August 21 was his lone career outing at Truist Park, leaving him 0-1 with a 14.54 ERA, a 2.31 WHIP, one walk and four strikeouts over 4.1 innings of work. In the minors this season, Winans is 9-4 with one save, a 2.95 ERA, a 1.082 WHIP, 34 walks and 108 strikeouts over 122 innings in 22 appearances, 16 starts, with Triple-A Gwinnett of the International League.
