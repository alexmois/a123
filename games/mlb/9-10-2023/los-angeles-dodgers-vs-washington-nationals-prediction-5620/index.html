Getting out of Dodge

The Los Angeles Dodgers are again on top in the National League West Division and head home after today's game after stops in Miami and Washington, D.C. The Dodgers' trip started slowly while losing two of three games in Miami. They bounced back to win the opener against the Nationals on Friday night by the score of 8-5. On Saturday, after a long rain delay, the Dodgers and Nationals battled into extra innings. The Dodgers pushed across a run in the top of the 10th to take a 6-5 lead courtesy of a Kike Hernandez single. In the bottom of the 10th, the Nats tied it at six. The Dodgers stranded two runners in the 11th and lost the game 7-6 in the bottom of the 11th thanks to a wild pitch.

With the expanded rosters since September, the Dodgers wisely decided to give veteran lefty Clayton Kershaw an extra day's rest and he will pitch on Monday. The Dodgers have 15 pitchers on staff at the moment and plenty of candidates to give them innings on Sunday. The leading candidate to earn a multiple-innings look is right-hander Ryan Yarbrough. Yarbrough last pitched on Tuesday and went three innings while allowing three hits and three runs while striking out three batters. On the season, Yarbrough has made seven starts while pitching for the Royals. In seven appearances for the Dodgers, he is 3-1 with 2 saves and an ERA of 2.82.

Key Injuries: Mookie Betts is out.

Trying to Spoil the Fun

The Washington Nationals are once again in last place in the NL East Division and have fallen on hard times in the month of September. In their last ten games heading into Saturday, the Nats are just 2-8 in that span. The Nats dropped the opener to the Dodgers on Friday but showed some fight on Saturday against the NL West-leading Dodgers. After blowing a 5-2 lead in the late innings, the Nationals were able to tie the game back up in the 10th inning at six apiece. The Nationals were able to strand two runners in the 11th and picked up the walk-off win when Michael Chavis scored on a wild pitch in the bottom of the 11th.

In the series finale on Sunday, the Nationals will hand the ball to righty Trevor Williams. Williams is 6-9 in 27 starts including five quality starts. He has allowed 158 hits in 134.2 innings pitched with an ERA of 5.21. Williams is 3-4 with a 5.21 ERA this season in 13 starts. In his last five starts, Williams is 1-3 with an ERA of 7.27. He has struck out 23 in 26 innings pitched. On the season, Williams has coughed up 33 home runs on the season.
