The Oakland A's face the Texas Rangers with both teams on opposite ends of the standings to close out the season. The A's have a 44-98 record and are in last place in the American League West Division. The Rangers have a 77-64 record but with only three wins in their last 10 games, they have fallen out of a postseason position in the American League. For the final game of the series, the A's will turn to Luis Medina as their starting pitcher who has allowed seven runs in his last three starts while the Rangers will turn to Jon Gray who has allowed 11 runs in his last three starts.

A's hope Medina and lineup can step up

The A's are having a season to forget and hope to avoid finishing with the worst record in the American League. Luis Medina will start the upcoming game and he hopes to salvage a rough season on the mound as he's allowed 59 runs in 90.2 innings pitched. In addition, with a .250 expected opponent Batting Average, an 11.2 Walk Rate, and a 4.70 expected ERA, opponents are making contact and putting together strong plate appearances against Medina to easily drive in runs.

Medina has struggled but the lineup has been the worst in baseball, scoring only 3.70 runs per game. Ryan Noda is slashing .234/.373/.425 with 14 home runs and 142 total bases while Brent Rooker is slashing .243/.328/.463 with 23 home runs and 187 total bases but the rest of the lineup has struggled. Only two batters have an OPS+ over 100 and opposing pitchers can eliminate the hapless lineup as a result.

Rangers hope lineup helps out Gray

The Rangers are having a great season but a recent skid could see them miss the postseason unless they turn things around. Jon Gray has shown flashes with a 5.7 Barrel Percentage but has struggled this season, allowing 63 runs in 142.2 innings pitched. Additionally, with a .255 expected opponent Batting Average, a 21.8 Strikeout Percentage, and a 4.27 expected ERA, opponents are making contact and driving in runs at will against Gray this year.

Gray has struggled but the lineup has carried the Rangers, scoring 5.46 runs per game. Corey Seager is slashing .338/.401/.651 with 29 home runs and 254 total bases while Nathaniel Lowe is slashing .276/.371/.435 with 15 home runs and 236 total bases but the rest of the lineup has stepped up as well. Marcus Semien is slashing .278/.350/.464 with 22 home runs and 270 total bases while Mitch Garver and Jonah Heim have added 31 home runs and 295 total bases to round out the lineup, making the Rangers tough to stop at the plate.
