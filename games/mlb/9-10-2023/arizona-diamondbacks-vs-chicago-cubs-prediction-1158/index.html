The Arizona Diamondbacks and Chicago Cubs will wrap up a four-game set on Sunday at Wrigley Field. First pitch is at 1:20 p.m. CT.

Arizona (74-68 SU and 75-67 RL) is starting right-hander Brandon Pfaadt. The 24-year-old is 1-8 with a 6.27 ERA and a 1.46 WHIP in 15 outings this season.

Chicago (76-66 SU AND 74-68 RL) is countering with righty Kyle Hendricks. The 33-year-old is 5-7 with a 3.73 ERA and a 1.14 WHIP in his 20 starts.

Is Arizona warming up again?

Arizona won the first two games of the series, regaining some of the momentum it lost in recent series losses to the Dodgers and Orioles. Will it continue to stack up wins down the stretch of the regular season?

The Diamondbacks average 4.68 runs (12th) and hit .253 (14th) with a .741 OPS (15th). They've hit 156 long balls (19th) and stolen 142 bases (3rd) this year. The pitching staff has posted a 4.58 ERA (22nd) and a 1.33 WHIP (19th) with 53 quality starts (12th).

Pfaadt will toe the rubber for the D-Backs in the series finale. The young righty is 0-3 with a 5.03 ERA and a 1.15 WHIP in his six road starts this season. On September 5 against Colorado, he gave up three runs and six hits in 3.2 innings, picking up the loss. He posted a 1-3 record with a 4.01 ERA and a 1.19 WHIP in six August outings. The rookie Pfaadt has never faced the Cubs.

Chicago could use a bounce-back win

Chicago has climbed back into the division and wild card races in the second half of the season, posting a 34-19 SU record. Can it finish the job with a strong finish in September?

The Cubbies score 5.06 runs per game (7th) and hit .253 (13th) with a .746 OPS (13th). They've launched 170 homers (14th) and stolen 118 bases (7th) in 2023. The Chicago pitching staff sports a 4.04 ERA (13th) and a 1.27 WHIP (11th) with 65 quality starts (4th).

The veteran Hendricks will take the hill for the home team on Sunday afternoon at Wrigley. In his last start, he surrendered five runs (four earned) on seven hits, including two home runs, to the Giants. Hendricks was 1-2 with a 4.09 ERA and a 1.30 WHIP in his six August outings. The tenth-year pro is 2-4 with a 4.53 ERA in 11 home starts this season.

The Cubs sport a 38-35 run line record at home, the fourth-best home RL record in MLB. They have a 51-45 RL record vs. NL competition.

Chicago will cover the run line at home in the series finale with the trusty vet Hendricks on the hill. The over is my bet on the total in the D-Backs-Cubs game.

Chicago averages over five runs per game at home and scored 5.5 runs per game overall last month. Its second-half slugging percentage (.454) and OPS (.787) are much higher than in the first half (.396 and .721). It won't hesitate to take advantage of its scoring opportunities against Pfaadt.
