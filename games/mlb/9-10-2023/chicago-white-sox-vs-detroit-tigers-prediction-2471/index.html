The Chicago White Sox (55-86, 70-71 RL, 64-68-9 O/U) and the Detroit Tigers (64-77, 72-68 RL, 67-68-5 O/U) conclude a three-game divisional series on Sunday afternoon. The White Sox will send Jesse Scholtens to the mound. The Tigers will reply with Sawyer Gipson-Long. The Tigers lead the season series 6-5 entering Saturday. 

Scholtens Eyes Second Win

Jesse Scholtens will be on the mound to conclude the series on Sunday. Scholtens was clobbered in his previous effort, squandering five runs in only 3.2 innings against the Royals, and was hit with the loss. The rookie has not been able to find a groove, allowing at least five runs in three of his last four starts, and has a 4.38 ERA and a 1.38 WHIP accompanied by a 1-7 record in 74 innings. He has allowed two runs in 4.2 innings against the Tigers this season.

Chicago continues a road trek. They lost two of three to the Royals before this series and have not won a series since early August. The offense has strong hitters but has not been able to produce with any consistency. They are 3-7 in their last ten decisions including a series defeat against the Tigers in that span.

Gipson-Long Makes Debut

The Tigers have promoted Sawyer Gipson-Long to make his debut on Sunday. Gipson-Long has spent four years in the minor leagues. The right-hander posted a mediocre 4.33 ERA and a strong 1.13 WHIP accompanied by an 8-8 record in 99.2 innings pitched in the minor leagues this season. He was acquired in a trade last year and has a 4.45 ERA and a 1.23 WHIP in his minor league career.

Detroit is home after losing two of three to the Yankees after avoiding the sweep with a 10-3 win on Thursday. The young squad has now dropped four of their last five series with the only series victory in that span occurring against the White Sox. The Tigers have a decent young core but need more offense. They are 5-5 in their last ten bouts.

Riley Greene has landed on the IL.

Detroit is struggling at the plate and is only averaging 3.96 runs on the year, pegging them 29th. The pitching has been a bright spot, reporting a 4.45 team ERA, placing them 19th.
