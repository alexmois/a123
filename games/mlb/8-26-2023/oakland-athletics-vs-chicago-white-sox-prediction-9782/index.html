The Oakland Athletics (37-91) will be trying to pick up the series win when they face the Chicago White Sox (50-78) in the third game of their four-game series on Saturday night.

JP Sears, 2-10, 4.61 ERA, will get the start for the Athletics. The White Sox will counter with Touki Toussaint, 1-6, 5.30 ERA.

Chicago is 6-4 in its last 10 games against Oakland.

**This preview was written before Friday’s game was played**

Athletics Going For Fourth Win In Five Games

The Athletics are playing well at the moment and they’ve won three of their last four games. They will try to keep the momentum going with another win over the White Sox, which will give them their fourth win in their last six games.

Oakland is averaging 3.60 runs per game. Their .223 batting average is 30th in the league. Their .300 on base percentage is 28th, while their .367 slugging percentage is 30th.

Brent Rooker has been the big bat for the Athletics. He leads the team with a .245 batting average, 22 home runs, and 55 RBI.

Oakland’s pitching hasn’t been good, with the team giving up 5.95 runs per game. Opponents have a .271 batting average against the Athletics, which is 28th in the league. Their 5.76 ERA is 30th, as is their 1.54 WHIP.

In his last start, Sears gave up nine hits and seven runs in four innings, leading to a 12-1 loss to Baltimore. They will need a significantly better effort from him if they want to get the win.

White Sox Trying To Bounce Back From Loss

The White Sox followed up their win over the Mariners with a loss to the Athletics in the first game of the series. They will try to bounce back from the loss with a win on Saturday, which will give them their second win in their last three games.

Chicago is averaging 4.13 runs per game. Their .240 batting average is 22nd in the league. Their .295 on base percentage is 30th, while their .388 slugging percentage is 25th.

Andrew Benintendi leads Chicago with a .277 batting average, while Luis Robert Jr. leads the team with 34 home runs and 69 RBI.

Chicago’s pitching hasn’t been good, with the team giving up 5.11 runs per game. Opponents have a .247 batting average against the White Sox, which is 18th in the league. Their 4.82 ERA is 26th, while their 1.40 WHIP is 23rd.

In his last start, Toussaint gave up seven hits and seven runs in four innings, leading to a 14-2 loss to Seattle. They will need a better performance from him if they want to win this game.
