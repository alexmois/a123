Cubs Still Charging

Just over a month ago, the Cubs seemed destined for another lost season and faced with the real possibility of trading away some key veterans to bring back prospects in return. Fast forward to the present day and the Cubs have shot up the standings and are very much in the fight for the NL Central Division crown or a wildcard spot. Chicago has won four of its last five games heading into this series after taking two of three from the Royals and sweeping the Tigers on the road. In the series opener on Thursday night, the Cubs squeaked out a 5-4 win in 10-innings. On Friday night, the Cubs were held to just five hits and one run in a 2-1 loss to the Pirates.

Chicago will send out Javier Assad on Saturday against the Pirates. Assad has gone from being an opener to a full-fledged starter for the Cubs in the last month. He is 2-2 on the season in 23 appearances including now five starts. Assid has allowed 60 hits in 72 innings pitched and has an ERA of 3.13. In his last five games, four of which were starts, Assid is 1-0 with a 2.45 ERA. He has allowed just 19 hits in 25.2 innings of work and struck out 17 batters. On the road this season, Assid has an ERA of just 2.39 in 26.1 innings of work.

 

Next Year it is

The Pittsburgh Pirates started the season with such promise but things cooled quickly and the focus has now shifted to the next season with just over a month to go in the 2023 season. The Pirates come into this series after taking two of three from the Cardinals in Pittsburgh as the team continues to show it has fight left. Pittsburgh fell back down to earth on Thursday night, losing 5-4 in ten innings to the Cubs. Friday night, the Pirates got eight shutout innings from starter Mitch Keller on their way to a 2-1 win to even the series at a game apiece.

The Pirates have yet to announce a starter for Saturday as the team has given some additionals pitchers some looks as of late. They have incorporated an opener in this spot in the rotation recently with righty Osvaldo Bido getting the majority of the innings either out of the bullpen or as the opener. In his last appearance, Bido went 3.1 innings and gave up four hits, three runs, and struck out six batters. He will be fresh on Saturday, having not pitched since the previous Sunday. On the season, Bido is 2-4 with a 5.08 ERA. He also possesses an 8.7 K/9 rate.

Key Injuries: RF Henry Davis is out.
