The Cleveland Guardians and Toronto Blue Jays will play Saturday at Rogers Centre. First pitch is at 3:07 p.m. ET.

Cleveland (60-68 SU and 64-64 RL) is starting left-hander Logan Allen. The 24-year-old is 6-6 with a 3.31 ERA and a 1.34 WHIP in his 19 outings this season.

Toronto (79-58 SU and 59-69 RL) is countering with lefty Hyun Jin Ryu. The 36-year-old is 2-1 with a 1.89 ERA and a 1.05 WHIP in four starts.

The Blue Jays are -170 moneyline favorites and the game total is 8.5 runs.

*Article published before the conclusion of Friday's game.

Allen is finding his groove

Cleveland beat Los Angeles 8-3 in game one of its last series, but couldn't keep its momentum in the final two games against the Dodgers. It lost six of its previous eight games before traveling to Toronto for this weekend's series with the Blue Jays.

The Guardians score 4.00 runs per game (28th) and hit .249 (17th) with a .688 OPS (27th). They've launched 93 homers (30th) and stolen 106 bases (7th) in 2023. The Cleveland pitching staff has compiled a 3.80 ERA (4th) and a 1.28 WHIP (16th) with 47 quality starts (13th).

Allen will take the mound for the visitors on Saturday afternoon. In his last start, the rookie southpaw gave up two runs on four hits in six innings vs. Detroit. He's having a fine month of August, holding four opponents to five total runs (23.0 IP). That includes a five-inning outing against the Blue Jays (one run on four hits and three walks).

The Blue Jays need to stack some wins

Toronto dropped two of three to Baltimore in its last series. It's 5-5 in its last ten overall, falling 1.5 games out of the American League Wild Card picture.

The Blue Jays average 4.45 runs (16th) and hit .257 (9th) with a .741 OPS (13th). They've hit 148 home runs (18th) and stolen 82 bases (19th) in 2023. The Toronto pitching staff has posted a 3.68 ERA (1st) and a 1.24 WHIP (7th) with 58 quality starts (5th).

Ryu will start for the Blue Jays on Saturday afternoon. The veteran left-hander has made four appearances since returning from the injured list in August, holding his last three opponents scoreless* after surrendering four runs in his first start of the year. He's earned consecutive victories, holding the Cubs to two hits in five frames and the Reds to four hits with seven strikeouts.

*Ryu gave up two runs to Chicago and two runs to Cincinnati, but they were all unearned runs.

Best Bets for this Game

Full-Game Side Bet

Insiders Status:

Rating:

Toronto has dropped out of the last Wild Card spot and needs to beat the teams it should to get back into the playoff picture. The under-.500 Guardians certainly fall into that category!

Cleveland is hitting just .229 BA/.292 OBP/.352 SLG/.644 OPS vs. left-handed pitchers this season, and Ryu has pitched well since returning from the IL. I am confident he will coast against a Guardians lineup that's having its worst month at the plate this season (.225/.285/.331/.616 in August).

The Jays slash an impressive .275/.347/.416/.763 vs. southpaw pitchers and .270/.348/.430/.778 in day games. Allen held them to one run earlier this month, but that was all the scoring they needed to pull off the road win. I'm predicting more runs from Toronto on Friday, giving Ryu plenty of support.

At -170, the moneyline is too rich for my taste, and I'm confident the Blue Jays will win by more than one run given their advantage in the batter's box. With that said, I believe the best value is on Toronto to cover at +120.

Prediction: Blue Jays RL

Full-Game Total Pick

Insiders Status:

Rating:

The under is 51-35-3 when the Blue Jays are favorites, including 29-19-3 when they are home favorites. The under is 32-29 when the Guardians are underdogs, too.

I'm betting on eight or fewer total runs between these clubs on Saturday. The Guardians are scoring just 3.2 runs in August and 3.8 runs post-All-Star weekend. Their splits vs. left-handed pitchers are below average, and they're facing a confident veteran in Ryu, who hasn't allowed an earned run since his first outing of the season. I'm predicting two or fewer runs from Cleveland tomorrow afternoon.

While I expect Toronto to lead the scoring, run production might not come too easily against Allen. The young southpaw hasn't been a bad bet this month (five runs in 23 innings), and he held the Jays to just one run on August 9. The Blue Jays have a lot of pressure on them to make up ground in the Wild Card standings, too. Whether or not they can handle the pressure of postseason expectations is unknown. It would surprise me if they produced more than four runs against Allen and the Cleveland bullpen (fifth-lowest ERA in MLB).

Prediction: Under 8.5
