The New York Yankees and Tampa Bay Rays will play on Saturday at Tropicana Field. First pitch is scheduled for 1:10 p.m. ET.

New York (61-66 SU and 60-67 RL) is starting right-hander Clarke Schmidt. The 27-year-old is 8-7 with a 4.68 ERA and a 1.34 WHIP in 26 appearances (25 starts).

Tampa (78-51 SU and 65-64 RL) is countering with righty Tyler Glasnow. The 30-year-old is 6-4 with a 3.35 ERA and a 1.09 WHIP in 14 outings this season.

The Rays are -170 moneyline favorites and the game total is seven runs.

The Yankees have a long offseason ahead of them

New York is 1-9 in its last ten overall, falling five games under .500. The 2023 season has been a "disaster," per general manager Brian Cashman. It may be time for the team to cut ties with its longtime GM!

The Yankees score 4.24 runs per game (23rd) and hit .230 (29th) with a .707 OPS (22nd). They've launched 176 homers (7th) and stolen 80 bases (20th) this year. The New York pitching staff has compiled a 4.07 ERA (14th) and a 1.25 WHIP (8th) with 35 quality starts (26th).

Schmidt will start for the visitors on Saturday afternoon in Florida. In his last outing, the fourth-year pro gave up two runs and four hits with eight strikeouts in 5.2 innings vs. Boston. It was a significant bounce-back performance, as the 2017 first-round pick gave up eight runs and nine hits in his previous start (2.1 IP) vs. Atlanta. Schmidt has not faced the Rays this season.

Is Tampa Bay closing in on Baltimore?

Tampa Bay has won seven of its last ten overall, pulling within two games of division leader Baltimore in the AL East standings. Can it overtake the O's by the end of the regular season?

The Rays average 5.36 runs (4th) and hit .261 (4th) with a .783 OPS (4th). They've launched 189 home runs (3rd) and stolen 132 bases (2nd) in 2023. The TB pitching staff has posted a 3.89 ERA (6th) and a 1.20 WHIP (2nd) with 41 quality starts (16th).

Glasnow will take the mound for the home team on Saturday afternoon. The veteran right-hander is coming off one of his worst outings of the season, an eight-hit, seven-run (five earned) performance vs. the Angels. He held his previous six opponents to two or fewer runs, though, totaling 47 strikeouts during that span (38.2 IP). That included a stellar start against the Yankees (one run on three hits with eight Ks in seven frames).

Insiders Status:

Rating:

I can't understand why the total is just seven runs in Saturday's game. This is giving the Yankees too little credit and the starting pitchers far too much respect, in my opinion.

New York has fallen short of expectations this season, but its bats responded to the criticism on Wednesday and Thursday as the media outlets made a mockery of its nine-game losing streak. While the bullpen blew a winnable game on Thursday, there's no denying that the Yanks came to play. With a leader like Judge serving as the team's captain, I won't be surprised when they show similar passion this weekend in Florida. Besides, Glasnow was rocked by an Angels team facing similar criticism in his last outing.

As for Schmidt, the 27-year-old has shown promise this season, but he's far from a surefire bet to hold the explosive Rays offense in check. They already knocked him around for seven runs earlier this season. He didn't have his best stuff a couple of starts ago vs. Atlanta, either (eight runs on nine hits in 2.1 frames).
