Cincinnati Reds (67-62) vs. Arizona Diamondbacks (67-61)

The 2023 MLB season goes on Saturday, August 26, with the Cincinnati Reds squaring off against the Arizona Diamondbacks in the National League battle at Chase Field in Phoenix, Arizona, and the first pitch is set for 8:10 PM ET.  

Cincinnati and Arizona lock horns in the third of a four-game series at Chase Field. The Diamondbacks outlasted the Reds 3-2 in the opener, while the second game of the series has been excluded from the analysis. 

The Reds stopped a three-game win streak in the opener

The Cincinnati Reds fell to 67-62 on the season following Thursday’s defeat at Chase Field. After sweeping a three-game road series against the Los Angeles Angels (4-3, 9-4, and 7-3), the Reds mustered just three hits versus Merrill Kelly and a couple of relievers. It was Cincinnati’s fifth loss in ten games, and the Reds were sitting four games behind the NL Central-leading Milwaukee Brewers. 

The Reds are hitting an underwhelming .232/.320/.384 with 21 extra-base hits and 12 home runs in the last two weeks and 409 at-bats. Their pitching staff has done a solid job during that span, tallying a 3.53 ERA to go with a 1.22 WHIP and .224 batting average against. 

Brett Kennedy is projected to toe the rubber Saturday at Chase Field. The 29-year-old right-hander is 1-0 with a 3.00 ERA, 1.00 WHIP, and 6/3 K/BB ratio in a couple of starts and one relief appearance (12 innings pitched) in 2023. His last outing came on August 18 when Kennedy tossed five scoreless innings in a no-decision against the Toronto Blue Jays, allowing just three hits and a walk. 

The Diamondbacks are back in the playoff picture

The Arizona Diamondbacks outhit the Reds 10-3 in Thursday’s opener, and Corbin Carroll slugged a two-run go-ahead homer in the bottom of the eighth to put his team back in the wild-card spot. The D-backs moved to 67-61 on the season, tallying their fifth win on the spin and the tenth in 12 games. 

Arizona is slashing .264/.336/.428 with 22 extra-base hits and 15 home runs in the past two weeks and 428 at-bats. On the pitching side of things, the Diamondbacks have compiled a 3.77 ERA, 1.26 WHIP, and .235 batting average against in those two weeks. 

Zach Davies is scheduled to return from the IL on Saturday and make his first start since July 18. The 30-year-old right-hander is 1-5 with a hideous 7.38 ERA, 1.62 WHIP, and 46/27 K/BB ratio in 12 starts (57.1 IP) this season. Davies has been a complete disaster over his previous six starts, surrendering 31 runs (30 earned) on 34 hits and 14 walks across 29.1 innings of work.

Rating:

I don’t trust the starting duo in this contest. Brett Kennedy pitched very well last time out, but the Diamondbacks had enough time to prepare for the Reds righty, who spent the previous four years in the minors after his debut in the majors in 2018. On the other side, Zach Davies is coming back from a back injury. He’s been awful so far this season, and the current Reds are 7-for-24 with two RBI against him. 

However, the Reds have struggled to hit the righties in the last ten days, notching a 62 wRC+ and .617 OPS through 201 plate appearances. The Diamondbacks have accounted for a 124 wRC+ and a .307/.391/.428 versus right-handed pitchers in the last ten days and 193 plate appearances, so I’m going with Arizona because of its offense. They should get to Kennedy early, and the Reds have just two lefties in their bullpen including Alex Young, who served a two-run dinger in Thursday’s opener. 

Prediction: Take the Arizona Diamondbacks
