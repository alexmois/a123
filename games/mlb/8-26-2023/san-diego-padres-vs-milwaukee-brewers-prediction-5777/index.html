It’s the continuation of a series between National League teams on the diamond as a pair of teams looking to earn a victory meet in Cream City. The San Diego Padres are on the road as they play the middle game of a three-game series with the Milwaukee Brewers on Saturday night. San Diego won the rubber game of a three-game set at home against the Marlins as they prevailed 4-0 Wednesday night. Milwaukee swept a two-game interleague set with the Twins in a battle of division leaders as they won 8-7 in 10 innings Wednesday afternoon. Milwaukee won the opening game of the series at American Family Field Friday night by the score of 7-3.

San Diego Padres Hoping to Gain Ground in NL Wild Card Picture

San Diego took the rubber game of their series at home against the Marlins to give them a little momentum heading into this series. The Padres are 61-68 on the season after Friday's 7-3 loss and stand fourth in the NL West, 19 games behind the Dodgers for the top spot. They were 6.5 games behind the Diamondbacks for the final wild-card spot in the National League playoff picture. On Wednesday, San Diego had seven hits with Garrett Cooper the lone player with two in the contest. Xander Bogaerts hit his 14th homer of the season in the victory. Seth Lugo (5-6) earned the win on the mound as he threw six scoreless frames, allowing three hits with one walk and four strikeouts, before turning things over to the bullpen.

Pedro Avila is on the mound as he makes his eighth appearance and second start of the season for the Padres as he gets a chance to prove his mettle as a starter in the spot that belonged to Rich Hill. He has no record, a 1.17 ERA, a 1.174 WHIP, nine walks and 28 strikeouts over 23 innings of work on the season. Avila didn’t factor in the decision in his last outing, which came in relief against the Marlins Monday night at home. He threw two-thirds of an inning, allowing one run on two hits with no walks and one strikeout in a 6-2 Padres victory. In his last three outings, all in relief, Avila has no record with a 0.96 ERA, a 0.75 WHIP, one walk and seven strikeouts over 9.1 innings of work. Avila, in his 12th career major league appearance and fourth start, pitches against the Brewers for the first time here. As a result, this marks his first career outing at American Family Field.

Brewers Seeking to Maintain Recent Momentum

Milwaukee won their fifth straight and ninth in the last 12 games as they rallied to beat the Twins in extra innings Wednesday afternoon. The Brewers are now 71-57 after Friday's win and hold a four-game lead over the Cubs for the top spot in the NL Central race. On Wednesday, Milwaukee got two hits each from Carlos Santana (two runs), Brice Turang (RBI), Willy Adames (two runs, three RBI) and Tyrone Taylor (run, RBI) to lead their 11-hit attack. Taylor (his fifth), Adames (his 21st) and William Contreras (his 13th) each homered in the game for the Brewers. Corbin Burnes didn’t factor in the decision as he threw six innings, allowing six runs on eight hits with one walk and five strikeouts. Elvis Peguero (4-4) earned the win in relief as he threw one inning, allowing one run (none earned) on one hit with two walks and no strikeouts.

Freddy Peralta is on the hill for the Brewers as he makes his 25th start of the season in this contest. He is 10-8 with a 3.97 ERA, a 1.182 WHIP, 50 walks and 168 strikeouts over 133.2 innings of work on the year. Peralta earned the win in his last start, which came on the road against the Rangers last Saturday. He threw 5.2 innings, allowing one run on four hits with two walks and 11 strikeouts in a game the Brewers won 6-1. In his last three starts, Peralta is 3-0 with a 0.96 ERA, a 0.75 WHIP, five walks and 30 strikeouts over 18.2 innings of work. Peralta makes his fifth career appearance and second start against the Padres in this contest. He is 1-1 with a 4.22 ERA, a 1.125 WHIP, two walks and 14 strikeouts over 10.2 innings of work. Peralta is 23-14 with a 3.45 ERA, a 1.01 WHIP, 107 walks and 380 strikeouts over 295 innings in 75 career appearances, 47 starts, at American Family Field.
