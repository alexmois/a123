The Washington Nationals (60-69, 71-55 RL, 58-63-5 O/U) battle the Miami Marlins (65-64, 58-70 RL, 57-67-4 O/U) in the second of a three-game series on Saturday evening. The Nationals will have Jake Irvin on the rubber. The Marlins reply with rookie Eury Perez. The Marlins lead the season series 6-1.

Nationals Collect Another Series Win

Jake Irvin will get the nod. Irvin was brilliant last time out, completing six shutout innings against the Phillies, resulting in a no-decision in a game the Nationals lost. The young right-hander is showing improvement, reporting an outstanding 2.65 ERA in August, and has a 4.47 ERA and a 1.40 WHIP accompanied by a 3-5 record in 96.2 innings on the season. Irvin limited the Marlins to one run in five innings in his only career meeting. 

Washington continues to show improvement. They have won four consecutive series due to consistent offensive production. They scored 15 runs in three games against the Phillies this past weekend and won two of three at Yankee Stadium from Tuesday to Thursday. The Nationals are 8-2 in their last ten decisions.

Joey Meneses continues to shine, knocking in seven runs in his last seven games, and has a .783 OPS in August. The 31-year-old slugger now has 70 RBIs accompanied by a .741 OPS on the season. He is 4 for 14 with three RBIs against the Marlins this season. Keibert Ruiz is questionable.

Washington is scoring an average of 4.38 runs per game, ranking them 20th. The pitching has been decent recently and has a 4.91 team ERA, pegging them 27th.

Perez Starts, Marlins Return Home

The Marlins will have Eury Perez take the mound. Perez was brilliant last time out, completing six shutout innings against the potent Dodgers, striking out 10 batters in a game Miami lost by a 3-1 score. The rookie is going to be an ace. He is enjoying a spectacular rookie year, sporting a stellar 2.91 ERA and a 1.07 WHIP accompanied by a 5-4 record in 68 innings pitched. Perez limited the Nationals to one run in five innings in a 5-3 win in May.

Miami is home after going 2-4 on a six-game road trek that resulted in a series loss to the Dodgers and a series defeat against the Padres. They are two games out of the last wildcard spot and need to take advantage of a subpar Nationals team in this series. The Marlins stand at 4-6 in their last ten decisions entering Friday night.

Jesus Sanchez has recorded a solid .815 OPS this month. The 25-year-old right fielder is quietly having an outstanding season, bashing 10 homers with 40 RBIs and a strong .789 OPS. He is 2 for 7 with two RBIs against the Nationals this season.

The Marlins are struggling at the plate and are only averaging 4.08 runs on the year, marking them 26th. The pitching is a strength, reporting a 4.20 team ERA, positioning them 17th.
