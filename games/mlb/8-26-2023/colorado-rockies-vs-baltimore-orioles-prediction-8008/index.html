With this weekend series, the Colorado Rockies (48-79; before Friday's game) get to play in Baltimore for the first time since 2016. Unfortunately for them, venturing to face 2023's Baltimore Orioles (79-48) doesn't end well for most teams. Saturday night, Colorado will get their second crack at Baltimore's young squad. Oriole Park at Camden Yards will play host again, with a scheduled first pitch at 7:05 p.m. EDT. Here's a look at this long-overdue interleague matchup.

*Published before last night's results.

A Team as Cold as the Rockies

Colorado has the NL's worst record and brings a four-game losing streak into this contest. 2023 can't come to an end soon enough for them. Even away from home, pitching has been the main culprit of this recent slide. Chris Flexen will seek to stop the bleeding on Saturday. He has five starts with Colorado, allowing 20 runs (18 earned) over those 25.2 innings. He's given up 34 hits and eight dingers with the club. Flexen's latest start was his strongest in a Rockies uniform, lasting 6.0 innings and allowing only two runs (one earned). Still, the Rockies lost that game because the bullpen (which stinks) allowed eight runs in three innings following Flexen's departure.

Playing on the road means that Colorado's offense won't be as dangerous either. Only Kansas City averages fewer runs per game as a visiting team. Regardless of the venue, the Rockies are a bottom-five team in walk rate, strikeout rate, and stolen bases. They're ranked 25th in home runs and OBP too. Again, it can't be stressed enough how much those numbers dip on the road. Coming into this series, the Rockies were slashing .231/.291/.366 in away games. It may be another long weekend in that dugout.

Baltimore's Beautiful Year Continues

If there was an award for the team that has the most fun in a season, the 2023 Orioles would be favored. Everything not involving ownership has been immaculate in Charm City. With the AL's top record and a narrow lead in the AL East, they can't afford to let up now. An offense putting up more than five runs per game this month should lead the way. Baltimore is ninth in slugging percentage, knocking pitchers around constantly. Their 8.3% walk rate is only 22nd league-wide, but that's probably the only flaw in the lineup. The Orioles know how to score and pitches in the zone get punished.

The pitching staff has steadily improved as the season progresses, part of the reason they overtook the Rays for the division lead. It begins with a bullpen which leads MLB in FIP and WAR entering the weekend. Considering that the Orioles' pitching plan for this game is still undecided, they may get an extensive look against the Rockies. Regardless of who starts, it's known that All-Stars Yennier Cano and Felix Bautista are waiting when the Birds get the lead. It'll be on whoever gets the call to step up on Saturday.
