The Offseason Looms

The Los Angeles Angels tried to make all the right moves at the trade deadline to stay afloat in the American League playoffs but their moves can only be described as an unmitigated disaster. The team has sunk down in the standing in the AL West Division and lost both of their star players, Mike Trout and Ohtani, for the season. They had into this series with Tampa having lost 13 of their last 20 games. On Tuesday night in the opener, the Angels bullpen coughed up four runs in the eighth inning and turned a 2-2 tie into a 6-2 Angels' win.

The Angels have not announced a starter for game two of this series with the Rays. The most likely candidate is lefty Reid Detmers who was scratched from his last start on Saturday due to illness. Detmers is 3-10 with an ERA of 4.77 in 26 starts this season. He is 1-6 on the road in 12 starts this season with an ERA of 5.40. He has allowed 55 hits in 60 innings pitched on the raod with 57 strikeouts. Detmers is 1-0 with an ERA of 3.00 in his last five games and has 23 strikeouts in 30 innings pitched.

Key Injuries: 3B Mike Moustakis, CF Mike Trout, DH Shohei Ohtani and CF Mickey Moniak are all out.

Rays Still Hopeful

The Tampa Bay Rays have overcome a ton of adversity this season, including injuries and off-the-field incidents, but still managed to earn a playoff berth and still have an outside chance of winning the AL East Division. The Rays have won seven out of their last ten games heading into Tuesday night's battle with the Angels. They did close out the Orioles series with two straight losses to end a four-game winning streak. In the opener against the Angels, the Rays scored four times in the bottom of the eighth and pull out a 6-2 win.

The Rays will send trade acquisition Aaron Civale to the mound on Wednesday night against the Angels. Civale is 7-3 on the season overall and is 2-1 in eight starts since being acquired by the Rays at the deadline. He has pitched 40.2 innings and allowed 42 hits with an ERA of 4.43 for the Rays. Civale has an impressive 10.6 K/9 rate for the Rays and his WHIP is 1.25. He has not faced the Angels yet this season but is 1-1 in five starts against the AL West this season with an ERA of 3.68.

Key Injuries: CF Jose Siri is out.
