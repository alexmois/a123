The Atlanta Braves and Colorado Rockies will play on Monday at Coors Field. First pitch is at 6:40 p.m. MT.

Atlanta (84-44 SU and 67-61 RL) is starting right-hander Bryce Elder. The 24-year-old is 10-4 with a 3.39 ERA and a 1.20 WHIP in 25 outings this year.

Colorado (48-81 SU and 63-66 RL) is countering with left-hander Austin Gomber. The 29-year-old is 9-9 with a 5.48 ERA and a 1.47 WHIP in 26 starts.

Atlanta is coasting to the finish line

Atlanta wrapped up its series with San Francisco over the weekend. It's 12-3 in its last 15 games, extending its lead over the rest of the National League.

The Braves score 5.79 runs per game (1st) and hit .274 (1st) with a .843 OPS (1st). They've hit 243 homers (1st) and stolen 107 bases (7th) in 2023. The Atlanta pitching staff has posted a 3.78 ERA (3rd) and a 1.26 WHIP (12th) with 49 quality starts (12th).

Elder will start for the visitors on Monday at Coors. The second-year pro faced New York in consecutive outings, holding the Mets to one run on three hits in 12.1 combined frames. Elder faced Colorado on June 17, surrendering one run and four hits in six innings.

Colorado played competitively in Baltimore

Colorado finished its series with Baltimore on Sunday. The Rockies have been tough to watch, dropping eight of their last ten games and 24 of 38 games post-All-Star weekend.

The Rocks average 4.40 runs (18th) and hit .250 (15th) with a .714 OPS (21st). They've hit 130 home runs (25th) and stolen 49 bases (29th) this year. The Colorado pitching staff has posted a 5.57 ERA (29th) and a 1.51 WHIP (29th) with 33 quality starts (27th).

Gomber will start for the home team on Monday night. The fifth-year pro has pitched pretty well this month, holding the Cardinals, Dodgers, and Rays to five runs in 17 combined innings. He didn't fare as well, however, in his other start (six runs in 5.1 innings vs. Arizona). Gomber has not faced Atlanta in 2023.

Full-Game Side Bet

Insiders Status:

Rating:

Atlanta hasn't lost a series since dropping two of three to the Cubs in the first week of August. It should continue to roll against the Rockies in Colorado Monday night.

Elder is 7-0 with a 2.44 ERA and a 1.07 WHIP in 14-night game starts this season and handled the Rockies without much of a problem earlier this season. He looks like a good bet to have a similar performance on Monday against a Colorado team hitting .236 BA/.299 OBP/.394 SLG/.694 OPS in August.

I expect the Braves, who are slashing .295/.370/.517/.887 this month, to give him enough run support to cover the run line. Gomber has a 7.06 ERA and 1.57 WHIP with 15 HRs in 14 starts at Coors Field this season. He and the Rocks' bullpen (5.19 ERA and 1.49 WHIP) will struggle to string together enough outs on Monday to keep the Braves from pulling away with a comfortable lead.

Prediction: Atlanta RL

Full-Game Total Pick

Insiders Status:

Rating:

Gomber's high home ERA and the Braves' impressive road splits (.268/.339/.483/.822) suggest Atlanta will have a field day in the batter's box tomorrow night. The Braves score 5.7 runs per game on the road and are averaging 6.5 runs per game overall in August. They have scored 6.2 runs per game since All-Star weekend.

Elder's road ERA is nearly four, and he's had a few sub-par outings recently, losing starts versus the Cubs and Pirates. He has surrendered five or more runs in four of his last eight starts. While I don't anticipate a poor outing from the young righty tomorrow, keeping the ball in the yard at Coors Field can be difficult.

With that said, the over is my bet on the total in Monday's game.
