The Chicago White Sox (52-79, 65-66 RL, 59-64-8 O/U) battle the Baltimore Orioles (81-49, 80-50 RL, 68-56-6 O/U) in the first of a three-game series on Monday evening. The White Sox will send Michael Kopech to the hill. The Orioles will reply with Grayson Rodriguez. The Orioles lead the season series 2-1.

Kopech Starts, White Sox Open Road Trek

Michael Kopech will get the nod on Monday. Kopech was solid last time out, recording four shutout innings against the Mariners before exiting with leg cramps. The young right-hander has not been able to contain opponents with any consistency, issuing a 4.95 ERA and a 1.49 WHIP accompanied by a 5-11 record in 120 innings on the year. Kopech took on the O’s in April and allowed three runs including two home runs in five innings in a no-decision. He has conceded six runs with a 0-1 record in 13 career innings against the O's.

Chicago heads on the road where they are 18 games below .500. The squad had plans to be a contender but is having a disastrous season and just dismissed their president and GM. They continue to stumble, losing two of four home meetings against the Athletics, allowing 23 runs in the series, and are 4-6 in their last ten bouts.

Yoan Moncada is one of many players having a disappointing season on this squad. The 28-year-old was once considered a top prospect but has not been able to stay healthy. He only has 28 RBIs and a weak .669 OPS in 67 games on the season. Moncada did not play in the first series against the Orioles.

The White Sox are only averaging 4.13 runs per game, ranking them 25th. The pitching is a weakness, posting a 4.84 team ERA, pegging them 25th.

Rodriguez Seeks Fourth Win, O's Remain in 1st

Grayson Rodriguez will be on the mound to open the series on Monday. Rodriguez wasn’t quite as sharp as usual last time out, but held the Jays to only three runs on eight hits in six innings, resulting in a no-decision in a game the Orioles lost. The rookie right-hander has been reliable, conceding two or fewer runs in two of his last three outings but has a weak 5.38 ERA and a 1.43 WHIP accompanied by a 3-3 record in 87 innings. Rodriguez squandered four runs in five innings in his only career meeting against the White Sox in a game the Orioles won.

Baltimore is looking good. They recently won five in a row and stand three games ahead of the Rays atop the division after winning two of three home bouts against the Rockies this past weekend, scoring 13 runs. The Orioles have now won three out of their last four series to improve to 7-3 in their last ten decisions.

Anthony Santander is swinging a hot bat, launching three homers along with five RBIs in his last five games. The 28-year-old slugger now has 24 big flies accompanied by 70 RBIs and an .804 OPS. Santander is 3 for 14 with two RBIs against the White Sox this season.

Baltimore is posting an average of 4.95 runs on the year, good for eighth. The pitching is decent, amassing a 4.15 team ERA, placing them 12th.
