Washington Nationals Looking to Continue Strong Play

Washington improved to 11-3 in their last 14 games as they rallied to down the Marlins on the road Saturday for their third straight win. The Nationals entered Saturday 61-69 on the year and stood fourth in the NL East, 24 games behind the Braves for the top spot in the division. They were seven games behind the Diamondbacks for the final wild card spot in the National League playoff picture. On Saturday, Washington finished with only five hits with no player having more than one, but rallied to win with a pair of runs in the ninth inning to get the win. Jake Irvin took a tough no-decision as he threw six innings, allowing one run on four hits with three walks and three strikeouts. Jordan Weems (4-0) earned the win in relief despite allowing one run on one hit with one walk and no strikeouts over two-thirds of an inning. Kyle Finnegan worked a scoreless ninth, allowing one hit with no walks or strikeouts, to pick up his 24th save.

Josiah Gray is on the mound for the Nationals as he makes his 26th start of the season in this contest. He enters the game 7-10 with a 3.85 ERA, a 1.445 WHIP, 67 walks and 118 strikeouts over 135.2 innings of work on the year. Gray didn’t factor in the decision in his last start, which came against the Yankees on the road Tuesday. He threw six innings, allowing one run on one hit with five walks and four strikeouts in a game the Nationals went on to win 2-1. In his last three starts, Gray is 0-1 with a 6.59 ERA, a 1.61 WHIP, 10 walks and 13 strikeouts over 13.2 innings of work. Gray didn’t factor in the decision in his lone career start against the Blue Jays, which came at home back on August 18, 2021. He threw six innings, allowing two runs on five hits with one walk and four strikeouts in a game the Nationals eventually won 8-5. Gray, in his 68th career major league appearance and 67th start, pitches at Rogers Centre for the first time here.

Blue Jays Battling for Playoff Position in Crowded AL Race

Toronto snapped a three-game losing streak as they took the middle game of their three-game home set with the Guardians Saturday afternoon. The Blue Jays entered Sunday third in the AL East with a 71-59 record and stood 10.5 games behind the Orioles for the top spot in the division. They were 2.5 games behind the Mariners for the final wild card spot in the American League. On Saturday, Toronto got three hits from Davis Schneider (three runs, three RBI) and two from Bo Bichette (RBI) to lead an 11-hit attack. Schneider hit his fourth homer of the season in the victory. Hyun-Jin Ryu (3-1) earned the win as he threw five innings, allowing three runs (two earned) on four hits with no walks and five strikeouts.

Kevin Gausman is on the mound for the Blue Jays as he makes his 26th start of the season in this contest. He is 9-8 with a 3.23 ERA, a 1.157 WHIP, 38 walks and 195 strikeouts over 150.1 innings of work this year. Gausman took the loss in his last start, which came on the road against the Orioles Wednesday night. He threw six innings, allowing two runs on five hits with one walk and eight strikeouts in a game Toronto eventually dropped 7-0. In his last three starts, Gausman is 1-2 with a 3.44 ERA, a 1.09 WHIP, four walks and 18 strikeouts over 18.1 innings of work. Gausman makes his 12th career appearance and 11th start against the Nationals in this contest. He comes in 6-3 with a 4.77 ERA, a 1.427 WHIP, 19 walks and 41 strikeouts over 54.2 innings of work against them. Gausman is 10-13 with a 3.86 ERA, a 1.316 WHIP, 51 walks and 243 strikeouts over 209.2 innings in 40 career appearances, 37 starts, at Rogers Centre.
