The San Diego Padres (61-70, 63-68 RL, (54-70-7 O/U) and the St. Louis Cardinals (56-75, 62-69 RL, 60-65-5 O/U) open a three-game series on Monday evening. The Padres will send Blake Snell to the mound. The Cardinals will reply with veteran Adam Wainwright. The Cardinals went 4-2 against the Padres last season.

Snell Gets the Nod, Padres Lose in Milwaukee

Blake Snell will get the nod on Monday. Snell allowed three runs including one home run in six innings against the Orioles last time out and was charged with the loss. The left-hander is having a solid year but has not been quite as dominant in August where he has reported a 3.91 ERA. He now has a 2.73 ERA and a 1.27 WHIP along with a 10-9 record in 142 innings on the year. Snell has dominated the Cardinals, conceding just four runs with a 1-0 record in 20 career innings. 

San Diego hasn't been able to make up any ground in the wildcard picture, standing 6.5 games out in what’s been an incredibly disappointing season. They won two of three home meetings against the Marlins last week followed by a sweep at the hands of the Brewers. The squad is 3-7 in their last ten games.

Fernando Tatis Jr. has been slumping at the dish which is happening at the worst possible time as San Diego looks to keep their dim playoff hopes intact. The 24-year-old has recorded a weak .655 OPS in August and has 20 homers with 66 RBIs and a .781 OPS on the season. Jake Cronenworth is out for the remainder of the season.

San Diego is scoring an average of 4.49 runs per game, ranking them 15th. The pitching is a strength, recording a 3.82 team ERA, placing them fourth.

Wainwright Starts, Cards Return home

The Cards will give Adam Wainwright the nod in the series opener. Wainwright had more issues in his latest outing, squandering six runs in 4.2 innings against the Pirates, and was hit with another defeat. The veteran has not been able to find a groove and is going to retire at the end of the season. He has issued an abysmal 8.61 ERA and a 1.99 WHIP accompanied by a 3-9 record in 77.1 innings. Wainwright has excelled in his career against the Padres, recording a 2.73 ERA and a 7-6 record. 

The Cardinals are home after more struggles on the road, going 1-5 on a road trek. They lost two of three in Pittsburgh and were dealt a sweep by the Phillies this past weekend, allowing 21 runs in the series. They have now dropped three consecutive series. The Cards are 2-8 in their last ten bouts.

The Cards are glad to have Nolan Gorman back in the lineup after missing a few weeks. The 23-year-old is enjoying a breakout season, clubbing 24 homers with 67 RBIs and a solid .805 OPS. Gorman is slumping in recent games. He went 3 for 3 with two RBIs against the Padres last season.

St. Louis is averaging 4.50 runs on the year, marking them 15th. The pitching is a weakness, posting a 4.75 team ERA, pegging them 25th.
