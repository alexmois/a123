Oakland Athletics (38-93) vs. Seattle Mariners (74-56)

Another week of the 2023 Major League Baseball is in the books, and the MLB betting action goes on Monday, August 28, with the Oakland Athletics taking on the Seattle Mariners in the AL West showdown at T-Mobile Park in Seattle, Washington.  

The A’s and M’s open a three-game series in Seattle. They lock horns for the first time since May when the Mariners swept a three-game set in Oakland and a four-game series in front of the home fans. 

The Athletics hope to snap a two-game skid

The 38-93 Oakland Athletics just split a four-game road set against the Chicago White Sox. After scoring 20 runs in a couple of strong displays at Guaranteed Rate Field (8-5 and 12-4), the A’s have only put three runs through the final two games against the lowly White Sox (6-2 and 6-1). Oakland had just two hits in Sunday’s 6-1 defeat in Chicago, though the A’s have slugged 12 home runs over their previous seven games and 227 at-bats. 

The Athletics have recorded a .232/.311/.406 triple-slash in the last couple of weeks and 470 at-bats while striking out 137 times (fourth-most in the majors). Their pitching staff has compiled a pedestrian 5.43 ERA, 1.60 WHIP, and .295 batting average against during that span (122.2 innings pitched). 

The A’s haven’t announced who’ll get the starting call Monday in Seattle, and I’m expecting to see Kyle Muller working as a follower. Muller tossed four innings in a follower role last Wednesday and wasn’t involved in the decision in a 4-0 home defeat to the Kansas City Royals. He issued a couple of free passes and gave up just one hit, serving a solo homer to Bobby Witt Jr. 

Muller is 1-4 with a hideous 7.28 ERA and 1.89 WHIP in 11 starts and a couple of relief appearances (59.1 IP) this season. The 25-year-old left-hander had a 7.79 ERA and 6.07 FIP in the first half of the season before landing on the IL on the first day of July. He returned on August 18, throwing 3.1 frames of a two-run ball in a no-decision against the Baltimore Orioles.

The Mariners need to extend their dominance over the A’s

The Seattle Mariners moved to 74-56 on the season following Sunday’s 3-2 victory against the Kansas City Royals. The M’s swept a three-game home set, notching the 12th win in their last 13 games overall. The red-hot Mariners are now topping the AL West, just one game ahead of the Texas Rangers and Houston Astros. 

Seattle’s offense has been outstanding since the All-Star break, slashing .271/.352/.475 with 66 home runs in 1393 at-bats. Over the last seven days, the M’s are hitting a marvelous .315/.417/.568 across 213 at-bats and will look to keep the good times rolling in the Athletics series.  

Bryan Woo is projected to toe the slab Monday, and the 23-year-old rookie right-hander carries a 1-3 record with a 4.58 ERA and 1.22 WHIP across 12 starts and 59 innings of work. Woo returned from a short stint on the IL last Monday, pitching four innings of a one-run ball in a no-decision against the White Sox. He’s allowed three earned runs on seven hits and three walks over two starts (10 IP) so far this month.
