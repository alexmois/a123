The New York Yankees continue their road trip as they travel to Detroit to take on the Tigers at the start of a four-game series. Both teams are likely out of the playoff picture heading into the final month of the season and will be reduced the role of spoilers down the stretch. Lost Year for the Yanks

The Yankees limp into Detroit in the midst of their worst season since the early 1990s. The Yankees lost two of three over the weekend in Tampa Bay and have lost 10 of their last 12 games overall to sink to 62-68. They are firmly in last place in the AL East Division. On Sunday, the Yankees trailed 2-0 early before jumping out to a 4-2 lead on the heels of home runs from Kyle Higashioka, DJ LeMahieu, and Anthony Volpe. The Yankees' bullpen, however, could not make it hold up and they fell 7-4 to close the series.

The Yankees will hand the ball to struggling veteran Luis Severino in game one on Monday night. Severino is 3-8 in 16 appearances this season including 15 starts. He has allowed 98 hits in just 74.1 innings pitched with an ERA of 7.26. On the road, Severino is 0-6 and his ERA jumps to 9.56 in 37.2 innings while giving up a whopping 62 hits. Severino is 1-4 in his last five starts with an ERA of 9.45 while striking out 18 batters in 20 innings pitched.

Key Injuries: 1B Anthony Rizzo is out.

Tigers Looking to Finish Strong

Like the Yankees, the Tigers are not on the fast track to the playoffs this season. Unlike the Yankees, however, the Tigers' season can still be considered a success as the team continues to rebuild into a contender. The Tigers come into this series after losing two of three games to the Astros over the weekend and have now dropped three of their last four games to fall to 59-71 on the season. On Sunday, the Tigers were routed by the Astros 17-4 as former Tigers' ace Justin Verlander picked up the win. The Tigers dropped the final two games of the series by a combined score of 26-6.

The Tigers will turn to rookie right-hander Reese Olson to open the series against the Bronx Bombers. Olson is 2-5 on the season in 15 appearances including 12 starts this season. He has allowed 65 hits in 68 innings pitched with an ERA of 5.29. Olson is 2-3 at home this season with an ERA of 5.53 and 47 strikeouts in 42.1 innings pitched. In his last five games, he has gone 1-1 with a 6.66 ERA and allowed 27 hits in 24.1 innings pitched while striking out 23 batters.

Full-Game Side Bet

Insiders Status:

Rating:

As noted in the write-up, the Tigers have the right to view this season as a marginal success as the team is currently in third place and has shown signs of improvement with a young staff and some up-and-coming everyday players. The Yankees, meanwhile, have fallen hard this season going from a division winner to a likely last-place team. The Yankees come to Detroit having lost 10 of their last 12 games and looking very much like a team that simply wants this season to end. They have several veterans who will likely be playing their last year in pinstripes and may soon have to decide whether or not to continue playing a banged-up Aaron Judge or resting him and allowing him to fully recuperate from a toe injury that has hampered him all season. Yankee starter Severino has had his worst year as a professional and has been pounded this season, including an ERA of nearly ten on the road. While Tigers' starter Olson has been less than effective lately as well, he's done a better job than Severino in limiting hits and preventing big innings. That trend will continue against a Yankees team that has struggled all year to string together hits. I'll take the hungrier team, the Tigers, to win on Monday night.

Take the Tigers with the money line.

Prediction: Tigers -125
