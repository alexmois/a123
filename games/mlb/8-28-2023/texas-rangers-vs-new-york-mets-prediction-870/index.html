It’s the opening game of an early week interleague series on the diamond between a pair of teams in different situations when it comes to goals for the rest of the season in the Big Apple. The Texas Rangers are on the road as they open up a three-game series with the New York Mets on Monday night. New York played the finale of their three-game set with the Angels in interleague action Sunday afternoon and avoided a sweep by prevailing 3-2 in walk-off fashion. Rangers Looking to Get Back on Track

Texas used a big ninth inning at the plate Saturday night to snap an eight-game losing skid by downing the Twins on the road. The Rangers entered Sunday 73-56 on the season and stood tied with the Mariners for the top spot in the AL West race. On Saturday, Texas finished with nine hits with Adolis Garcia (two runs, RBI) and Mitch Garver (run, RBI) each recording a pair in the game. Garcia (his 32nd) and Garver (his 12th) each hit solo homers in the game while the Rangers’ rally in the ninth was fueled by a pair of run-scoring walks, a run-scoring hit batter and a sacrifice fly. Max Scherzer didn’t factor in the decision as he threw seven innings, allowing two runs on four hits with one walk and 10 strikeouts. Brock Burke (5-2) earned the win with a scoreless eighth, allowing one hit with no walks and one strikeout.

Jon Gray takes the ball for Texas as he makes his 24th start of the season in this contest. He comes in 8-7 with a 3.76 ERA, a 1.23 WHIP, 44 walks and 111 strikeouts over 131.2 innings of work on the season. Gray took the loss in his last start, which came Tuesday on the road against the Diamondbacks. He threw four innings, allowing five runs on seven hits with three walks and five strikeouts in a game the Rangers went on to lose 6-3. In his last three starts, Gray is 1-2 with a 3.00 ERA, a 1.11 WHIP, five walks and 16 strikeouts over 18 innings of work. Gray makes his seventh career start against the Mets in this contest. He comes in 1-3 with an 8.04 ERA, a 1.714 WHIP, 11 walks and 29 strikeouts over 28 innings of work against them. Gray is 0-3 with a 7.45 ERA, a 1.707 WHIP, nine walks and 21 strikeouts over 19.1 innings in four career starts at Citi Field.

New York Mets Trying to Build Off Walk-off Win

New York managed to pick up the win in the finale of their set with the Angels Sunday to snap a four-game slide. The Mets improved to 60-71 on the season and were in the basement of the NL East, 25.5 games behind the Braves for the top spot entering late Sunday afternoon. They were nine games behind the Diamondbacks for the final wild card spot in the National League playoff picture. On Sunday, New York got two hits each from Pete Alonso (RBI) and Dan Vogelbach while Rafael Ortega knocked in the winning run with a walk-off RBI single. David Peterson didn’t factor in the decision as he threw seven innings, allowing one run on three hits with three walks and eight strikeouts. Adam Ottavino (1-4) earned the win in relief with a scoreless ninth, allowing no hits with no walks and one strikeout.

Tylor Megill takes the hill for the Mets in the series opener as he logs his 20th start of the year in this contest. He comes in 7-7 with a 5.54 ERA, a 1.725 WHIP, 46 walks and 74 strikeouts over 91 innings of work this season. Megill took the loss in his last start, which came against the Braves on the road Tuesday night. He threw 4.2 innings, allowing three runs on eight hits with two walks and five strikeouts in a game the Mets lost 3-2. In his last three starts, Megill is 1-2 with a 6.00 ERA, a 1.93 WHIP, seven walks and 13 strikeouts over 15 innings of work. Megill, in his 53rd career appearance and 47th major league start, pitches against the Rangers for the first time here. He makes his 26th career appearance and 24th start at Citi Field in this contest. Megill is 9-5 with a 4.14 ERA, a 1.295 WHIP, 48 walks and 123 strikeouts over 119.2 innings of work in those outings.
