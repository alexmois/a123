The Philadelphia Phillies play host to the Los Angeles Angels on Monday night from Citizens Bank Park in a crucial series opener. The Phillies enter with playoff aspirations, while the Angels already have shifted their focus to the 2024 season. Last season the Phillies swept this series and while they might be hard-pressed to win all three games this week, they’ll continue to be heavy favorites for at least Monday’s opener.

Angels Eyeing 2024

The Angels head into this mid-week series simply looking to spring an upset. After continuing to crawl back in the AL West standings, they know that a playoff push is out of reach now, especially with the recent run of injuries. Los Angeles continues to be a team filled with talent but with some poor luck, though they hope that they can still put on a solid performance in this one. The Angels have gone 4-6 over their last ten games and without much to play for, it remains interesting to see how they operate and put things together over the last month.

https://m.youtube.com/watch?v=cESzg000FSo

According to MLB.com, Lucas Giolito will get the ball in this one, as the righty looks to leave his mark on the road. Once one of the more dominant pitchers in baseball, he’s struggled this year, which could be tough for the Angels to overcome. With a 7-10 record and 4.32 ERA, his performances continue to leave quite a bit to be desired.

On the offensive end, one promising offense has struggled to keep up with opponents of late. Averaging 4.8 runs per game and hitting .246 as a team, the injuries have made things difficult. Shohei Ohtani has been shut down for the season, which has been a brutal blow, joining Mike Trout as one of the best duos that fans will miss out on over the last month. Add in injuries to nine others who were either starters or the first off the bench, it shouldn’t come as a surprise that they’ve struggled of late. Against another solid foe on this one, it’ll likely fall on the offense to lead the way.

Phillies Eyeing October Baseball

After another slow start to the season, the Phillies have put together an impressive second half yet again. They sit in first place in the NL Wild Card race, in a great position as August turns to September. They’ve gone 7-3 over their last ten games, which is one of the best marks of late in baseball, showing how far this group has come. The injuries were an issue at the start but since finding more consistency of late, it’s clear that this team has high hopes for the postseason. With a reeling Los Angeles team coming to town, this is a prime opportunity for them to start the week off strong.

https://m.youtube.com/watch?v=vcYZaDOjevU

According to MLB.com, it’ll be Taijuan Walker getting the ball for the Phillies. The experience should be helpful for him in this, even though has has shown some sporadic signs this season. He brings in a 13-5 record, close to the tops when it comes to win totals, while also sitting on a 4.02 ERA. He needs to keep the ball down against the Angels but with some much inexperience in their lineup, he’ll be confident in finding success.

The Phillies are averaging 4.9 runs per game, just barely more than the Angels, though with them playing so well of late, it’s difficult to put a ton of stock into that number. The lineup has been getting healthier and healthier and it’s clear that this offense can make opposing pitchers pay. They continue to be without Christian Pache but with depth being an advantage all season, they’ll continue not to miss a beat without him.
