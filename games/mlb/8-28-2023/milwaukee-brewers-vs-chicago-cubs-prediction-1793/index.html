First meets second on Monday when the National League Central-leading Milwaukee Brewers (72-57, 1st NL Central) play game one of a three-game series on the road at Wrigley Field against the second-place Chicago Cubs (68-61, 2nd NL Central). Milwaukee has won seven straight after defeating the San Diego Padres 5-4 Saturday, maintaining a four-game lead over Chicago after the Cubs defeated the Pittsburgh Pirates 10-6 on Saturday for their third win in four games. Milwaukee will play without four pitchers as well as position players Garrett Mitchell, Blake Perkins, Darin Ruf and Jesse Winker. Chicago will play without seven pitchers.

 

This article was published prior to the results from Sunday’s games

Milwaukee's pitching is carrying the Brewers

The Milwaukee Brewers are in the top 10 of four major pitching categories. Milwaukee is second best in batting average allowed at just .233, while fourth in WHIP with 1.22 and ninth in Quality Starts with 56. Milwaukee has a team ERA of 3.97 which is 10th in baseball. Freddy Peralta is leading Milwaukee in victories with 11 and in strikeouts with 177. Milwaukee will hand the ball to Wade Miley on Monday. This season the left-hander is 6-3 with an ERA of 3.18 and 59 strikeouts.

 

At the plate, Milwaukee is 20th in on-base percentage at .315, 21st in runs scored with 564, and 27th in both slugging percentage at .379 and team batting average with .235. Willy Adames is leading Milwaukee in home runs with 21. Christian Yelich has been Milwaukee's most productive player with 30 doubles, one triple, 16 home runs and a team-leading 67 RBI. Milwaukee has hit 189 doubles, 11 triples and 350 home runs in 129 games.

Chicago continues to produce at the plate

The Chicago Cubs have scored the fifth most runs in baseball with 652 and are 7th in on base percentage .330. Chicago is 12th in team batting average at .254 and 13th in slugging percentage with .418. Cody Bellinger has been very productive for Chicago although he has played just 98 games. The center fielder has 21 doubles, one triple, a team-leading 20 home runs and a team-best 70 RBI. Patrick Wisdom has also hit 20 home runs while Dansby Swanson is second in RBI with 65.

On the mound, Chicago has the 7th most Quality Starts with 57 but drops to 14th in batting average allowed at .244, 16th in team ERA at 4.18 and 17th in WHIP with 1.28. Justin Stelle is leading Chicago in victories with 14, in strikeouts with 133 and in ERA for starters with 2.80. Chicago will hand the ball to Jameson Taillon on Monday. This season the right-hander is 7-8 with an ERA of 5.60 and 100 strikeouts.
