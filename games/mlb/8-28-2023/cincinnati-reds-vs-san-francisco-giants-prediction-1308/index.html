Reds Machine Still Humming

The Cincinnati Reds have been one of the feel-good stories of the baseball season this summer with a young and exciting team battling for a playoff spot in a crowded National League field. The Reds come into this series having lost three of four over the weekend at Arizona to fall six games behind first-place Milwaukee in the NL Central Division and two games behind the third and final wildcard spot in the National League. On Sunday, the Reds lost 5-2 to the Diamondbacks despite another strong start from Graham Ashcraft. Ashcraft went 6.2 innings and allowed just two runs on seven hits.

The Reds will give the ball to rookie righty Andrew Abbott in game one against the Giants. Abbott is 8-3 on the season in 15 starts including eight quality starts. He has allowed 69 hits in 85.1 innings pitched with an ERA of 3.16 and an impressive K/9 rate of 9.8. Abbott is 5-1 with an ERA of 3.51 on the road this season in eight starts. He has struggled recently, however, going 2-1 with an ERA of 6.46 in his last five starts while allowing 29 hits in 23.2 innings pitched. Abbott has faced the Giants once this season, picking up the win while going eight innings and allowing just one hit without a run and striking out six batters.

Key Injuries: 2B Jonathan India, RF Jake Fraley, and 1B/DH Joey Votto are out.

Giant Hill to Climb

The San Francisco Giants are still in the playoff picture despite a rough stretch over the last several weeks. The Giants have gone 3-7 in their last ten games heading into Sunday night's finale against the Braves at home. The Giants dropped each of the first two games to Atlanta by a combined 12-4 score. On Sunday night in a near-must-win game, the Giants used a four-run fourth inning to upend the Braves 8-5 and avert a series sweep. Catcher Patrick Bailey's three-run double highlighted the scoring. The Giants are now 67-63 heading into this series.

The Giants will give the ball to rookie left-hander Kyle Harrison on Monday night. Harrison did not earn a decision in his one and only start this season for the Giants. Harrison went just 3.1 innings pitched while allowing five hits and two runs while striking out five and walking one batter in an eventual 4-3 loss at Philadelphia. Harrison threw 65 pitches in the outing before giving way to the Giants' bullpen.

Key Injuries: RF Michael Conforto, CF Mike Yastrzemski and SS Brandon Crawford are all out.

While Abbott has struggled in recent outings, the young righty has shined in his stint with the big club this season. He has been the Reds' de-facto ace for much of the summer with the absence of Hunter Greene and some of the struggles of Graham Ashcraft. He will have the luxury of facing a Giants' lineup missing two key left-handed bats in Conforto and Yastrzemski. I expect Abbott to have a much easier time with the Giants' lineup than Harrison will against the Reds on Monday night.
