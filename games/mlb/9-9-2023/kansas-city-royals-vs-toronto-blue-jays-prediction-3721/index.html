The Kansas City Royals (44-97) will be trying to pick up their second consecutive series win when they face the Toronto Blue Jays (77-63) in the second game of their three-game series on Saturday afternoon.

Zack Greinke, 1-14, 5.34 ERA, will get the start for the Royals. The Blue Jays will counter with Kevin Gausman, 10-8, 3.39 ERA.

Toronto is 7-3 in its last 10 games against Kansas City.

**This preview was written before Friday’s game was played**

Royals Going For Third Win In Four Games

The Royals are playing well at the moment and they’ve won two of their last three games. They will try to keep the momentum going with a win over the Blue Jays, which will give them their third win in their last four games.

Kansas City is averaging 4.07 runs per game. Their .243 batting average is 20th in the league. Their .300 on base percentage is 29th, while their .396 slugging percentage is 22nd.

Bobby Witt Jr. has been the big bat for the Royals. He leads the team with a .273 batting average, 28 home runs, and 85 RBI.

Kansas City’s pitching has been one of the worst in the league, with the team giving up 5.34 runs per game. Opponents have a .264 batting average against the Royals, which is 26th in the league. Their 5.21 ERA is 28th, while their 1.41 WHIP is 23rd.

In his last start, Greinke gave up five hits and three runs in 3.2 innings, leading to a 7-3 loss to Boston. They will need a better performance from him if they want to win this game.

Blue Jays Going For Sixth Win In Eight Games

The Blue Jays played well over the past week and they’ve won five of their last seven games. They currently have a half-game lead over Texas in the AL Wildcard standings and will try to increase their lead with a win on Saturday, which will give them their sixth win in their last eight games.

Toronto is averaging 4.61 runs per game. Their .260 batting average is fifth in the league. Their .333 on base percentage is fourth, while their .420 slugging percentage is 13th.

Bo Bichette leads the Blue Jays with a .314 batting average, while Vladimir Guerrero Jr. leads the team with 21 home runs and 82 RBI.

Toronto’s pitching has been good, with the team giving up 4.09 runs per game. Opponents have a .240 batting average against the Blue Jays, which is seventh in the league. Their 3.72 ERA is second, while their 1.24 WHIP is seventh.

In his last start, Gausman gave up six hits and three runs in four innings, leading to a 7-5 win over Colorado. They will need a better effort from him if they want to get the win.

Brandon Belt (Back) is questionable for this game.
