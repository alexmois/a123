The Chicago White Sox (55-86, 69-71 RL, 64-67-9 O/U) battle the Detroit Tigers (64-77, 72-67 RL, 67-67-5 O/U) in a divisional battle on Saturday evening. The White Sox will have Jose Urena on the mound. The Tigers will reply with Tarik Skubal. The Tigers lead the season series 6-5.

Kopech Demoted to Bullpen

The White Sox were going to have Michael Kopech on the mound but he will likely be used out of the bullpen. They have called on Jose Urena from Triple-A to start this one. Urena struggled with the Rockies this season, posting an abysmal 9.82 ERA and a 2.24 WHIP accompanied by an 0-4 record in 18.1 innings. The veteran has not been an effective pitcher at any point in his career. He conceded just two earned runs in his only career meeting against the Tigers.

Chicago has lost three consecutive series and will be glad to conclude this disastrous season. They have a decent core but have not been able to produce at the plate. They were swept by the Tigers followed by a series loss to the Royals due to a slumping offense. They stand at 3-7 in their last ten decisions.

Eloy Jimenez has accrued five RBIs and a decent .706 OPS against the Tigers this season. The 26-year-old slugger is having a respectable season, clubbing 15 homers with 54 RBIs and a .757 OPS in 99 games played. Jimenez is slumping, posting only one RBI and a .400 OPS on the month. 

Chicago is scoring an average of only 4.10 runs, ranking them 26th. The pitching staff has recorded a 4.94 team ERA, pegging them 26th.

Skubal On the Mound, Tigers Return Home

Tarik Skubal will be on the mound. Skubal was outstanding in his previous outing, limiting this White Sox team to just two runs on five hits in seven innings, and was credited with the victory. The young lefty has been stellar, conceding two or fewer runs in three of his last four outings, and is sporting a 3.77 ERA and a 1.06 WHIP complimented by a 4-3 record in 57.1 innings pitched on the year. Skubal has struggled against Chicago in his career, posting a 5.42 ERA but has a 4-2 record in the seven meetings. 

Detroit is home after going a solid 4-2 on a road trek. They issued the sweep against the White Sox but lost two of three games to the Yankees. They scored 15 runs in the series. The Tigers have a promising young squad that is producing at the plate, scoring 27 runs in their last five games They are 5-5 in their last ten clashes.

The great Miguel Cabrera is a stellar 7 for 17 with four RBIs against the White Sox this season. The future Hall of Famer is going to finish 16th on the MLB career hits list. Cabrera has collected 28 RBIs and a .660 OPS on the season.  He is hitting .412 with an .882 OPS this month. 

Detroit is only averaging 3.99 runs on the year, marking them 29th. The pitching is decent, posting a 4.45 ERA, placing them 19th.
