The Texas Rangers play host to the Oakland Athletics in a crucial AL West showdown on Saturday night from Globe Life Field. The Athletics come in with one of the worst records in baseball, though have been playing better of late and would love to spoil Texas' run towards the playoffs. On the other side, the Rangers have had a rough few weeks and are in desperate need to turn things around. Texas has dominated this series but after Oakland won the series opener, the Rangers will have revenge on their mind in this must-win.

Athletics Aiming For Another Upset

After grabbing the win on Friday night, the Athletics have their eyes set on pulling off the upset for the second-straight night, further hurting the playoff chances of their divisional rival. Oakland has had a rough season, sitting at the bottom of the AL West and with one of the worst records in baseball too, though that hasn't seemed to deter them of late. They've gone 5-5 over their last ten games and while they have nothing to play for, continue to put their best foot forward. A dreadful road record of only 19-50 leaves quite a bit of doubt that they can replicate Friday's success again on Saturday, though sometimes the teams with the least to lose, play the best over the final stretch.

According to MLB.com, Oakland is still undecided on who will even get the ball in this one. A young rotation has caused internal issues this season, while a lack of consistency has left things up in the air on more than one occasion. With a team ERA at 5.59, and sitting at 6.14 for their starters, whoever gets the ball is likely to go through some struggles in this one.

It doesn't help matters that the offense is one of the worst in baseball, even if they are coming off of a six-run night on Friday. They are only averaging 3.7 runs per game and with a .222 team average and only 7.4 hits per game, it makes sense that runs have been hard to come by. The only position player that they'll be without in this one is JJ Bleday (knee), they'll continue to lean heavily on Esteury Ruiz (.253 average) and Brent Rooker (23 home runs, 57 RBI) to lead the way.

Rangers In Dire Need Of Wins

After spending much of the season sitting in first-place in the AL West, the Rangers suddenly find themselves in third-place as September rolls along. They've only gone 3-7 over their last ten games, which has led to the recent decline in the standings. The struggles on both sides have been well-documented over the last few weeks and with Oakland coming to town, the expectation was that they would get back into the win column. A shocking loss on Friday has their momentum and confidence shattered though, as they look to regain their composure before Saturday night's first pitch and hold strong with one of baseball's best home records.

The good news is that Nathan Eovaldi will get the ball in this one, according to MLB.com. The righty has been a bright spot in this rotation for much of the season and brings and 11-4 record and 2.95 ERA into this matchup. This is only his second start after coming from the injury list, having struggled against the Astros the last time out to the tune of allowing four runs in 1.1 innings of work. While he'll be expected to be strong in this one, questions remain.

It could fall on the offense to lead the way at home, though they have struggled of late as well. On the season, they are averaging nearly 2.0 runs more per game than Oakland, while the long ball has been instrumental towards their success. Adolis Garcia (knee) remains out indefinitely, while they continue to miss Josh Jung (thumb) as well. Without those two big bats, it shouldn't come as a surprise that they have struggled in September.
