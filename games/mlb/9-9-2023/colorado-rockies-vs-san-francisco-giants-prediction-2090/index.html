Colorado Rockies Looking to Earn Road Victory

Colorado dropped two of three on the road against Arizona as they let a lead slip away en route to getting pummeled Wednesday. The Rockies entered Friday 51-88 on the season and stood last in the NL West, 34 games behind the Dodgers for the top spot. They are 21 games behind Arizona for the final wild card spot with 23 games to play. On Wednesday, Colorado scored four times in the top of the first only to be outscored 12-1 the rest of the way to lose. The Rockies finished with six hits with no player having more than one. Chris Flexen (1-7) took the loss on the mound as he allowed six runs on four hits with two walks and three strikeouts over 2.1 innings of work.

Chase Anderson is on the mound as he makes his 16th appearance and 14th start of the season overall here: it’s his 14th start of the year for the Rockies. He enters the game 0-4 with a 5.98 ERA, a 1.485 WHIP, 22 walks and 47 strikeouts over 64.2 innings of work on the season. Anderson didn’t factor in the decision in his last start, which came at home against the Blue Jays Sunday afternoon. He threw four innings, allowing two runs on four hits with two walks and three strikeouts in a 7-5 Rockies defeat. In his last three starts, Anderson has no record with a 3.00 ERA, a 1.27 WHIP, five walks and 10 strikeouts over 15 innings of work. Anderson makes his 12th career start against the Giants in this contest. He is 3-3 with a 4.77 ERA, a 1.226 WHIP, 12 walks and 45 strikeouts over 54.2 innings of work against them. Anderson is 2-0 with a 3.27 ERA, a 1.00 WHIP, seven walks and 13 strikeouts over 22 innings in four career starts at Oracle Park.

Giants Fading In NL Wild Card Race

San Francisco dropped their sixth straight game as they were drubbed by the Cubs in the finale of their set at Wrigley Field Wednesday afternoon. The Giants entered Friday 70-70 and stood third in the NL West, 15.5 games behind the Dodgers for the top spot. They were 2.5 games behind the Diamondbacks for the final wild card spot in the National League. On Wednesday, San Francisco got two hits each from Luis Matos (run), Casey Schmitt (run, RBI), Paul DeJong and Thairo Estrada in the loss. Alex Wood (5-5) took the loss on the mound as he threw 2.1 innings, allowing five runs on five hits with one walk and two strikeouts.

Logan Webb is on the bump for the Giants as he logs his 30th start of the year on getaway day in this one. He is 9-12 with a 3.51 ERA, a 1.091 WHIP, 28 walks and 173 strikeouts over 187 innings of work on the year. Webb took the loss in his last start, which came against the Cubs on the road Monday afternoon. He threw 6.2 innings, allowing three runs on five hits with one walk and four strikeouts in a game the Giants dropped 5-0. In his last three starts, Webb is 0-3 with a 5.00 ERA, a 1.11 WHIP, two walks and 11 strikeouts over 18 innings of work. Webb makes his 14th career appearance and 13th start against the Rockies in this contest. He is 7-2 with a 3.50 ERA, a 1.192 WHIP, 16 walks and 69 strikeouts over 74.2 innings of work against them. Webb is 22-16 with a 2.86 ERA, a 1.15 WHIP, 75 walks and 306 strikeouts over 314.2 innings of action in 54 career appearances, 51 starts, at Oracle Park.
