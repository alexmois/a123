Cleveland Guardians (67-74) vs. Los Angeles Angels (65-76)

The 2023 MLB season goes on Saturday, September 9, with the Cleveland Guardians squaring off against the Los Angeles Angels in the American League battle at Angel Stadium in Anaheim, California, and the first pitch is set at 9:07 PM ET.   

The Guardians and Angels lock horns in the third of a four-game series in Anaheim. The Halos took the first blood 3-2, while the second game of the series has been excluded from the analysis. 

The Guardians have lost four of their last five games

The Cleveland Guardians fell to 67-74 on the season following Thursday’s defeat at Angel Stadium. It was their fourth defeat in five games, and the Guardians were 6.5 games behind the AL Central-leading Minnesota Twins on Friday morning. 

Cleveland has scored 15 runs over its previous five outings while surrendering a whopping 38 runs in return. Last Monday, the Guardians were humiliated by the Twins in a 20-6 home loss, and first baseman/catcher David Fry tossed the final four innings, allowing seven earned runs on ten hits and a walk. The Guardians are slashing a mediocre .247/.309/.397 with 29 extra-base hits and 14 home runs in the past two weeks and 489 at-bats, and Bo Naylor has led the way with four homers and nine RBI in that span. 

Lucas Giolito will toe the slab Saturday at Angel Stadium, and the 29-year-old right-hander is 7-12 with an underwhelming 4.88 ERA, 1.31 WHIP, and a 168/60 K/BB ratio in 28 starts (156.2 innings pitched) this season. Giolito, who logged six starts for the Angels in 2023 before Cleveland claimed him off waivers, made his debut in the Guardians jersey this past Monday. He pitched three innings in that 20-6 loss to the Twins, yielding nine earned runs on seven hits and three walks. 

Giolito has been awful as of late. Over his previous six outings, Giolito has amassed a hideous 9.20 ERA, 7.84 FIP, and 1.003 opposing OPS across 30.1 innings of work.

The Halos beat Cleveland to stop a six-game slide

The Los Angeles Angels moved to 65-76 on the season following Thursday’s narrow win over the Guardians, putting an end to a six-game losing streak. Shohei Ohtani missed his fourth straight contest due to an oblique injury, but his teammates found a way to erase a two-run deficit. The Halos are still miles away from the wild-card spot, as they’ve dropped nine of their previous 11 games overall. 

Over the last couple of weeks, the Angels are hitting .249/.323/.406 with 27 extra-base hits and 13 home runs across 446 at-bats. Luis Rengifo has slugged five of those 13 dingers to go with 11 RBI and a .420 batting average, while Brandon Drury has gone 14-for-54 with two round-trippers and 11 RBI in his last 13 games. 

Tyler Anderson will get the nod Saturday, and the 33-year-old left-hander is 5-6 with a pedestrian 5.55 ERA, 1.53 WHIP, and 108/58 K/BB ratio in 23 starts and two relief appearances (128 IP) in 2023. Anderson has struggled all season long and over his previous five outings and 23.2 frames of work, the veteran southpaw has registered an ugly 8.37 ERA, 6.72 FIP, and .903 opposing OPS. Last Sunday, Anderson pitched 5.1 innings of a three-run ball in a no-decision against the Oakland Athletics.

For the second time this season, Tyler Anderson will face the Guardians. Back on May 12, Anderson gave up three earned runs on six hits and three walks in six innings of work but didn’t factor in the decision. On the other side, Giolito has already met the Angels twice in 2023. He’s emerged victorious on both occasions despite a 5.25 ERA in 12 innings of work. 

Over the last ten days, the Angels have recorded a 113 wRC+ against right-handed pitchers, while the Guardians have registered a 98 wRC+ versus left-handed pitchers. I don’t trust the starting duo in this game and would take the Angels to come out on top because of their offense. Anyway, it’s a tricky wager, as both teams have struggled as of late. The Angels bullpen holds a poor 6.41 ERA and 5.85 FIP in September, and the Guardians ‘pen owns a 7.36 ERA and 4.96 FIP.
