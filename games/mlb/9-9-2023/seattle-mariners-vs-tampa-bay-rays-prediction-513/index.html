The Seattle Mariners (79-61) will be trying to retake first place in the AL West standings when they face the Tampa Bay Rays (85-56) in the third game of their four-game series on Saturday afternoon.

Bryan Woo, 2-4, 4.50 ERA, will get the start for the Mariners. The Rays will counter with Aaron Civale, 7-3, 2.76 ERA.

Tampa Bay is 6-4 in its last 10 games against Seattle.

**This preview was written before Friday’s game was played**

Mariners Going For Series Win

The Mariners followed up their three-game losing streak by winning their last two games. They will try to keep the momentum going with another win over the Rays, which will give them their third win in their last six games.

Seattle is averaging 4.79 runs per game. Their .238 batting average is fifth in the league. Their 3.69 ERA leads the league, while their 1.18 WHIP is second.

Julio Rodriguez has been the most consistent batter for the Mariners. He leads the team with a .280 batting average, 27 home runs, and 93 RBI.

Seattle’s pitching has been good, with the team giving up 4.03 runs per game. Opponents have a .238 batting average against the Mariners, which is fifth in the league. Their 3.69 ERA leads the league, while their 1.18 WHIP is tied for first.

In his last start, Woo gave up four hits and five runs in five innings, leading to a 6-3 loss to Cincinnati. They will need a better performance from him if they want to win this game.

Rays Going For Fourth Win In Six Games

The Rays are playing well at the moment and they’ve won three of their last five games. They will try to keep the momentum going with a win over the Mariners, which will give them their fourth win in their last six games.

Tampa Bay is averaging 5.29 runs per game. Their .258 batting average is eighth in the league. Their .331 on base percentage is sixth, while their .443 slugging percentage is fourth.

Yandy Diaz leads Tampa Bay with a .317 batting average, while Isaac Paredes leads the team with 28 home runs and 87 RBI.

Tampa Bay’s pitching has been good, with the team giving up 3.96 runs per game. Opponents have a .231 batting average against the Rays, which leads the league. Their 3.78 ERA is third, while their 1.18 WHIP is tied for first.

In his last start, Civale gave up two hits and three runs in 5.1 innings, leading to a 7-3 loss to Boston. They will need a better effort from him if they want to get the win.

Taylor Walls (Groin) is questionable for this game.
