San Diego Padres (66-75) vs. Houston Astros (80-61)

The 2023 Major League Baseball betting action goes on Saturday, September 9, with a 15-game card, so we are breaking down the interleague showdown from Minute Maid Park in Houston, Texas, to get you the best Padres vs. Astros betting pick and odds.  

San Diego and Houston continue a three-game series, and Friday’s opener has been excluded from this preview. The Astros are -128 moneyline favorites for Saturday night, while the totals are listed at 9.0 runs. 

The Padres’ shaky form continues

The 66-75 San Diego Padres started a nine-game road trip on Friday. They were 7.5 games behind the final wild-card spot in the National League. The Padres have only won one of their previous four series and five of their last 13 games overall. 

San Diego has recorded a .265/.342/.424 triple-slash in that 13-game stretch, slugging 18 doubles and 17 home runs across 434 at-bats and scoring 61 runs. On the pitching side of things, the Padres have amassed a 4.11 ERA, 1.43 WHIP, and a .247 batting average against through 114 innings pitched. 

Seth Lugo will take the hill Saturday, and the 33-year-old right-hander is 6-6 with a 3.49 ERA, 1.19 WHIP, and 117/27 K/BB ratio in 22 starts (121.1 IP) this season. Lugo has been terrific over his previous five outings, allowing just five runs (four earned) on 23 hits and six walks through 29 innings of work. He pitched six scoreless innings in a winning effort against the San Francisco Giants this past Sunday, giving up three hits and a walk while striking out four.

The Astros eye their second straight series win

The 80-61 Houston Astros entered Friday’s clash against the Padres searching for their fourth consecutive victory. They just swept a three-game series against the Texas Rangers on the road (13-6, 14-1, and 12-3). The Astros were topping the AL West on Friday morning, just half a game ahead of the Seattle Mariners and three ahead of the Texas Rangers. 

Houston is slashing a robust .326/.398/.584 with 28 extra-base hits and 29 home runs in the past two weeks and 457 at-bats. During that span, the Astros’ pitching staff has compiled a 3.59 ERA, 1.20 WHIP, and .216 batting average against (107.2 IP). 

Cristian Javier will take the mound Saturday, and the 26-year-old righty is 9-3 with a 4.65 ERA, 1.28 WHIP, and 127/51 K/BB ratio in 26 starts (137.1 IP) in 2023. He’s allowed three or more earned runs in each of his last four starts and 19.2 frames of work, notching a 6.41 ERA, 5.85 FIP, and .866 opposing OPS in that stretch. Last Sunday, Javier took a loss in a 6-1 defeat to the New York Yankees, surrendering three earned runs on four hits through six innings of work.  

Best Bets for this Game

Full-Game Side Bet

Insiders Status:

Rating:

It’s hard to trust Cristian Javier, who’s having a poor second half of the season, but I would take the Astros because their offense has been red-hot as of late. Seth Lugo has pitched very well over the last few weeks, but the Astros hold a 177 wRC+ and 1.021 OPS against right-handed pitchers in September and will properly test the Padres righty. 

Only three guys from this Houston team have seen Lugo before, combining for two hits in six at-bats. On the other side, the current Padres are 5-for-19 with a pair of home runs against Cristian Javier. San Diego owns a 112 wRC+ and .766 OPS against the righties in September. 

Prediction: Houston Astros (-128)

Full-Game Total Pick

Insiders Status:

Rating:

Both bullpens are doing a great job in September. The Padres’ relievers sit at 2.63 ERA and 3.84 FIP (24 IP), and the Astros’ pen holds a 2.37 ERA and 4.10 FIP (19 IP). Hereof, I would stay away from the totals. There are a lot of top-notch relief pitchers who can keep the lineups scoreless down the stretch. On the other hand, the Astros are destroying the righties at the moment, and the Padres are doing a solid job against right-handed pitchers. 

Once more, I’ll lean on the red-hot Astros to score some runs. If Cristian Javier continues to struggle, the Padres will help the over backers. 

Prediction: Go over 9.0 runs (-105)
