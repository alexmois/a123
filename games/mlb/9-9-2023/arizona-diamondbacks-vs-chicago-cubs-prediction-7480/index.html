Down the Stretch They Come

The Arizona Diamondbacks, despite a rough stretch in the second half of the season, still are in the hunt for a playoff spot with the season dwindling down to just a few weeks. Arizona arrived in Chicago after taking two of three games from the last-place Rockies as they try to keep pace with fellow wildcard hopefuls the Cubs, Reds, Marlins, and Giants. The Diamondbacks are staying alive in the wildcard race after taking each of the first two games of the series, 6-2 and then 1-0 on Friday night thanks to a three-hit complete game shutout from Zac Gallen. The Diamondbacks are now 74-68 and have a half-game lead for the third and final wildcard spot in the National League.

The Diamondbacks will send righty Merrill Kelly to the hill on Saturday following one of his best starts of the season. In his last outing against the Rockies, Kelly went seven innings and allowed just four hits and one run while striking out 12 batters in a 4-1 Diamondbacks' win. For the season, Kelly is 11-6 in 25 starts including an impressive 16 quality starts. He has allowed 121 hits in 148.1 innings pitched with a K/9 rate of 9.6 and a WHIP of 1.17. On the road, Kelly is 7-2 in 12 starts with an ERA of 3.69. He has allowed 69 hits in 68.1 innings of work with 63 strikeouts.

Cubs Revival

The Chicago Cubs went from an afterthought for the playoffs in the first half of the season to one of the favorites to secure a wildcard spot. The Cubs are alone in the second spot in the wildcard standings as they try to fend off the Marlins, Reds, and today's opponent, the Arizona Diamondbacks. The Cubs come into this series after winning four straight games including a three-game sweep of the Giants at home. The Cubs have faltered in games one and two with the Diamondbacks with two straight losses. The Cubs' offense has cooled, scoring a total of two runs in the two games. Chicago remains two games up on the Diamondbacks in the second wildcard spot in the NL.

The Cubs will give the ball to left-hander Justin Steele in game three of this four game set. In just his third season overall, Steele has turned into the ace of the Cubs staff. On the season, Steele is 16-3 in 26 starts including 19 quality starts. He has allowed 140 hits in 152 innings pitched with a tidy 2.55 ERA. Steele's K/9 rate is 9.1 and his WHIP is 1.14. At home, he has been dominant with a 12-2 record a 2.25 ERA in 16 starts. Steele is 3-0 in his last five games overall while striking out 40 batters in 31 innings of work.

This one should be very tight on Saturday afternoon at Wrigley. Steele has been great all season and particularly dominant at home in losing just two of his 14 decisions at Wrigley. He is also 3-0 in his last five starts and comes off a 2-0 win over the Giants in which he allowed just two hits while striking out 12 in eight shutout innings of work. Arizona's Kelly is not far behind Steele with his recent work. I expect a low-scoring battle on Saturday with a lower percentage of balls being put in play with these two pitchers hurling. I'm going with the Cubs in this one based on their ability to run the bases, play quality defense, and as the hotter of the two teams at the moment. Steele has been the more consistent of the two pitchers in recent outings and he can get the Cubs deep into this game.
