Three teams from the AL West are trapped in a tight battle for the top spot as the regular season nears its conclusion. The Los Angeles Angels (64-70) and Oakland Athletics (39-95) are the odd men out of the division race. Regardless, they collide this weekend for three games, with the Angels carrying a 5-2 head-to-head edge in the season series before game one. Friday in the Oakland Coliseum, the Athletics will try to even the records. The first pitch is scheduled for 9:40 p.m. EDT. Who will take round one to open their weekend?

Fallen Angels

Remember when the Angels aggressively bought at the trade deadline? Well, August proceeded to be their worst month, and Los Angeles waived multiple players that were traded for. Add the injuries, and this Angels season is going to end with the regular season. Pitching has let the team down more than any other facet, and Patrick Sandoval's regression is an example of that. The lefty will be called on for this start, his third against the 2023 Athletics. In the first two, Sandoval allowed four runs (three earned) over 12.0 innings. He has pitched better on the road, with a 3.50 ERA. That includes his last outing, in which he gave up only two hits and one run in a win. Los Angeles doesn't have a reliable bullpen, but they might not need it on Friday.

Even with injury issues all season, the Angels have managed to rank 11th in runs per game. However, they scored fewer than four runs per contest in August, a major reason why they couldn't win consistently. Through seven battles Los Angeles has scored 54 runs against Oakland (nearly eight per game), so maybe the A's will be the cure. Carried by Shohei Ohtani's MLB-leading 44 home runs, the Halos are third in team homers and seventh in slugging percentage. Power swings mask a 24th-ranked strikeout rate and the league's worst stolen base percentage.

Aesthetically Unpleasing Athletics

It's been a painful season for Oakland's faithful fans in 2023. That's without mentioning the on-field product, which is firmly MLB's worst. Oakland's offense is dragging the team down right now, as they still average fewer runs per game than every other team. The A's outscored one club in August. Along with being the lowest-scoring squad, Oakland is last in batting average and slugging percentage. Outside of stealing bases, which is largely aided by Esteury Ruiz's AL-leading 53 swipes, there isn't much that the A's do well at the plate. Playing in the Oakland Coliseum doesn't typically help teams score either.

However, the Coliseum usually helps pitchers get outs. This year's Athletics didn't get the memo. Oakland enters September allowing the most runs per game. Their most reliable pitcher has been JP Sears, who brings a 4.80 ERA into this start. That number swells to 5.49 at home. A southpaw, Sears took on the Angels once this year. He gave up six runs in 4.1 innings that day. The Athletics predictably have a weak bullpen, so Sears will have to be better than the 8.25 ERA in his past five starts if the A's want to win.

Neither team is playing well, but the Angels have the only above-average group in this game with their offense. That group is 11th in runs per game, while Oakland is 30th. Using their lineup, the Angels will cruise to another win against the A's on Friday.

Meanwhile, Patrick Sandoval made light work of the A's lineup twice, giving up only four total runs in two starts. The Angels will probably race out to a healthy lead and never look back.

Neither one of these teams pitches well enough to trust the under. The over has hit in three of the last four meetings between them, and only two head-to-head matchups have ended with fewer than eight runs. Expect an easy win for the over in game one.
