The Tampa Bay Rays and Cleveland Guardians will play on Friday at Progressive Field. First pitch is at 7:10 p.m. ET.

Tampa Bay (82-52 SU and 69-65 RL) is starting right-hander Tyler Glasnow. The 30-year-old is 7-4 with a 3.12 ERA and a 1.05 WHIP in 15 outings this year.

Cleveland (64-70 SU and 68-66 RL) is countering with righty Cal Quantrill. The 28-year-old is 2-6 with a 6.45 ERA and a 1.57 WHIP in his 13 starts.

The Rays are -170 moneyline favorites and the game total is 7.5 runs.

Tampa Bay is heating up

Tampa Bay has won eight of its last ten overall, including series wins over the Rockies, Yankees, and Marlins. It's still hot on the heels of Baltimore in the AL East division standings but will it catch the O's?

The Rays score 5.36 runs per game (4th) and hit .260 (5th) with a .779 OPS (4th). They've launched 194 home runs (4th) and stolen 141 bases (2nd) in 2023. The TB pitching staff has compiled a 3.83 ERA (4th) and a 1.18 WHIP (2nd) with 44 quality starts (17th).

Glasnow will take the hill for the visitors on Friday. In his last start, the veteran right-hander gave up one hit and zero runs in a six-inning win over the Yankees. It was a significant bounce-back outing following a poor showing versus the Angels (seven runs and eight hits in six innings). He has allowed one or no runs in four of his last five starts.

Cleveland proved it can beat Minnesota, but what can it do in September?

Cleveland has won consecutive series over Toronto and Minnesota, pulling within five games of the Twins in the AL Central standings. Does it still have a shot at the division title?

The Guardians average 4.07 runs (26th) and hit .248 (16th) with a .691 OPS (26th). They've hit 103 long balls (30th) and stolen 112 bases (7th) this season. The Cleveland pitching staff has compiled a 3.85 ERA (5th) and a 1.28 WHIP (16th) with 48 quality starts (13th).

Quantrill will start for the home team tomorrow evening. It will be just his third appearance since May 30 and his first since July 5. In that start, he gave up five runs and 11 hits in a loss to Atlanta. The fifth-year pro is 1-4 with a 6.67 ERA and a 1.65 WHIP in his six home starts this season.

Full-Game Side Bet

Insiders Status:

Rating:

I'm betting on the Rays run line on Friday, with Glasnow toeing the rubber for TB.

Tampa sports a 9-5 run line record after a day off and a 35-31 run line record on the road this season, while Cleveland has a 26-37 RL record following a straight-up win, the second-worst RL winning percentage (41.3%) after a moneyline victory in MLB.

Glasnow has pitched consistently well this year and is coming off a one-hitter against the Yankees. He's allowed one or no runs in four of his last five starts and has held 9 of 15 opponents to one or zero earned runs. The veteran right-hander is 4-2 with a 3.27 ERA and a 1.26 WHIP in seven road starts this season. The Guardians hit .228 BA/.286 OBP/.361 SLG/.647 OPS in August, scoring just 3.7 runs per game.

Quantrill surrendered five runs on 11 hits in his last outing, a loss to Atlanta. He gave up 25 runs and 31 hits in his previous four outings and has a 6.67 ERA at home this season (six starts). The Rays scored 21 runs in their last three games and averaged six runs per game in August. They slash .264/.334/.451/.784 on the road, averaging 5.4 runs per game.

Prediction: Rays RL (-1.5)

Full-Game Total Pick

Insiders Status:

Rating:

I'm betting on a lopsided win by Tampa Bay on Friday, as I'm confident Glasnow and the Rays bullpen will keep Cleveland's bats on ice.

The under is 44-21-1 in Guardians home games this season, the highest under winning percentage (67.7%) in MLB. It is 57-34-2 in Cleveland's games against American League competitors, also the highest under winning percentage (62.6%). When Tampa is a road favorite, the under is 27-17-2 (61.4%).

Cleveland has been a low-scoring club all season, no matter where it plays (3.5 runs/game at home). After scoring 25 runs in its last four games, I predict it will struggle to get on the scoreboard against Tampa Bay tomorrow.

Tampa Bay will score a handful of runs, but that won't be enough to push the game total over 7.5. Even if Quantrill falls short, the Guardians' pen (3.48 ERA and 1.25 WHIP) will keep the score from getting too out of hand.

Prediction: Under 7.5
