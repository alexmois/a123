Marlins Trying to Stay in NL Wild Card Race

Miami lost for the eighth time in their last 10 games as they were unable to generate any offense in an extra-inning loss to the Rays at home Wednesday night. The Marlins entered Thursday 66-67 on the season and stood third in the NL East, 21.5 games behind the Braves for the top spot. They were three games behind the Giants for the final wild card spot in the National League playoff picture. On Wednesday, Miami finished with just four hits with no player having more than one in the contest. The Marlins went zero for six with runners in scoring position in the contest. Jesus Luzardo didn’t factor in the decision as he threw six shutout innings, allowing one hit with two walks and eight strikeouts. David Robertson (4-6) took the loss as he allowed three runs (two earned) on three hits with no walks and two strikeouts in one inning of work.

Eury Perez is slated to take the hill and make his 16th start of the season for the Marlins in this contest. He is 5-4 with a 2.68 ERA, a 1.041 WHIP, 22 walks and 90 strikeouts over 74 innings of work on the year. Perez didn’t factor in the decision in his last start, which came at home against the Nationals Saturday. He threw six innings, allowing one run (none earned) on three hits with one walk and seven strikeouts in a game Miami dropped 3-2. In his last three starts, Perez has no record, a 2.25 ERA, a 0.75 WHIP, three walks and 22 strikeouts over 16 innings of work. Perez makes his third career start against the Nationals in this contest. He is 1-0 with a 0.82 ERA, a 0.727 WHIP, two walks and 13 strikeouts over 11 innings of work against them. Perez, in his 16th career major league start, pitches at Nationals Park for the first time here.

Washington Nationals Battling to Stay Out of NL East Cellar

Washington fell for the third time in four games as they were blanked by Toronto in the rubber game of their three-game set north of the border Wednesday night. The Nationals entered Thursday 62-72 and stood fourth in the NL East, 26 games behind the Braves for the top spot. They were 7.5 games behind the Giants for the final wild card spot in the National League. On Wednesday, Washington finished with five hits in the game with Joey Meneses the lone player with two in the contest. The Nationals were zero for three with runners in scoring position in the game. Patrick Corbin (9-12) took the loss on the mound as he allowed six runs on 10 hits with one walk and five strikeouts over five innings of work.

Jake Irvin is on the hill for the Nationals as he makes his 21st start of the season here. He is 3-5 with a 4.30 ERA, a 1.383 WHIP, 41 walks and 84 strikeouts over 102.2 innings of work on the year. Irvin didn’t factor in the decision in his last start, which came Saturday on the road against the Marlins. He threw six innings, allowing one run on four hits with three walks and three strikeouts in a game the Nationals rallied to win 3-2. In his last three starts, Irvin has no record with a 1.45 ERA, a 1.12 WHIP, five walks and 16 strikeouts over 18.2 innings of work. Irvin makes his third career start against the Marlins in this contest. He has no record with a 1.64 ERA, a 1.182 WHIP, five walks and seven strikeouts over 11 innings of work against them. Irvin is 2-4 with a 4.23 ERA, a 1.355 WHIP, 29 walks and 60 strikeouts over 72.1 innings of work in 14 career starts at Nationals Park.
