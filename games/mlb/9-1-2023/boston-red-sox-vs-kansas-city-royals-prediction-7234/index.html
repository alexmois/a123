The Kansas City Royals play host to the Boston Red Sox on Friday night in an AL showdown from Kauffman Stadium. Boston comes in struggling of late but continuing to make a push for playoff consideration in the Wild Card race. The Royals on the other hand, have already been eliminated, though would love to play the role of spoiler over the next few weeks. In four meetings on the season already, the Red Sox have won three games, though shifting this series to Kansas City could change things.

Red Sox Approach Massive Weekend

With the Red Sox limping along of late, this is a massive weekend if they want any chance at making their way into one of the Wild Card spots by the end of the month. They have gone 3-7 over their last ten games, which is one of the worst marks in baseball over that time span, while continuing to fade away in the standings. They have a winning record on the road this season and their success against the Royals already this season should give them confidence as they approach this one. Expect Boston to put their best foot forward and to start fast, though it remains if that'll be enough.

According to MLB.com, James Paxton will get the ball for Boston, as they get to lean on one of their most experienced starters to set the tone. The righty has a 7-4 record and 3.99 ERA, though has struggled of late. He's allowed ten runs in his last two starts combined, failing to get past 4.1 innings pitched in either of those. With the weekend just kicking off, he'll need to find a way to eat up more innings in this one. In his last start against the Royals this season, he went 5.1 scoreless innings though, which is a great sign if he can replicate that.

Boston's offense continues to be a force this season, averaging 5.1 runs per game and hitting .262 as a team. Rafael Devers (29 home runs, 89 RBI) continues to set the pace but the offense seems to go where youngster, Masataka Yoshida, takes them. Leading the team in average, his ability to generate in the middle of the lineup seems to coincide with their success. With a healthy lineup coming into the weekend, depth should be an advantage for them in this one, as they aim to run away with the opener.

Royals Eyeing Spoiler Role

The Royals have one of the worst records in the American League but that doesn't mean that they won't be using September to their advantage. They haven't shown many signs of life of late though, as they have been one of the only teams worse than the Red Sox over the last few weeks, limping along to a 1-9 record over their last ten games. Adding in a dreadful home record and it appears this might not be the weekend where they alter someone else's playoff chances. Their struggles against the Red Sox are well-noted over the last few years and it'll be interesting to see if this is the moment where they can turn it around.

According to MLB.com, Jordan Lyles will get the ball for the Royals. The righty has struggled this season, turning out a 3-15 record and 6.51 ERA, both concerning for one of the more experienced starters in the rotation. He actually went eight innings in his last start against Boston but still allowed four runs in that one. Coming off an appearance last time out that saw him only go three innings, allowing seven runs, concern is high for how he'll perform on Friday night.

It hasn't helped that the offense has struggled to pick up the pitching most nights. Averaging only 4.0 runs per game, that number doesn't even tell the whole story of some o their more recent struggles. Injuries haven't helped this season, as Nick Pratto (groin) and Vinny Pasquantino (shoulder) remain out, while Salvador Perez (back) is questionable. Bobby Witt Jr. (.276 average, 27 home runs, 82 RBI) remains an explosive player but he can't carry this offense on his own, hence the recent struggles.
