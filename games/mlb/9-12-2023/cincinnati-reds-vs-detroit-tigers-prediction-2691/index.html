A three-game series between the Cincinnati Reds (74-71) and Detroit Tigers (66-77) is set to begin on Tuesday. Each team is third in their respective divisions, albeit with completely different desires during the upcoming October. Their journeys will cross paths this week, making those futures clearer as the season nears its end. Comerica Park will host the series, starting at 6:40 p.m. EDT on Tuesday. Here's how each team is supposed to open this series.

Reds Fighting For Their Lives

Waking up on Monday 1.5 games out of a playoff spot and trailing two other clubs in the loss column, Cincinnati has some work to do still. This young team has thrived through their offense all season and they need that to persist until the end. At 4.73 runs per game, the Reds are 11th among MLB teams this season. There isn't a single team with more stolen bases, and they also have an 8.9% walk rate (12th). Strikeouts are an issue, as is hitting home runs as the visiting team. Their hard-hit rate as a team is 30.8% (26th). Getting on base and weaponizing their speed will be how the Reds succeed in this game. 

Pitching has been a problem for the club all year. As a team, the Reds are a bottom-five pitching staff in FIP, groundball rate, walk rate, and HR/9. The team's collective ERA is 4.73 (24th). It mostly falls on a weak rotation, as Cincinnati's relievers are sixth in WAR league-wide. This game's starting pitcher, let alone the pitching plan, is still undecided. Whoever gets the ball first will hopefully provide more than the team has been receiving all season.

Tigers Time is Nearly Up

They haven't been eliminated from postseason contention quite yet, but the Detroit Tigers' chances get slimmer with each passing day. Unlike Cincinnati, their offense is the reason why the postseason was never a possibility. Only one team is averaging fewer runs per game than Detroit in 2o23. The Tigers are 25th or worse in home runs, strikeout rate, stolen bases, batting average, OBP, and slugging percentage. Most of those numbers dip at home. A few young hitters, such as Spencer Torkelson and Kerry Carpenter, are the only reason to tune in to this offense. 

Detroit's pitching has been decent, using MLB's seventh-best walk rate to earn a 4.41 ERA (18th). They allow more runs per game at home (despite a pitcher-friendly park), but still hold their own on the mound, even with a subpar strikeout rate team-wide (22.2%; 21st). Detroit's bullpen has been below average this season too, at least in terms of WAR (22nd) and LOB% (22nd). Like the Reds, the Tiger have not publicly decided how they're going to approach this game with pitching. Comerica Park usually helps pitchers, so let's see if whoever starts for Detroit reverses the team's home fortunes.
