The Chicago White Sox play host to the Kansas City Royals in the second game of this AL Central doubleheader on Tuesday evening from Guaranteed Rate Field. The Royals have been brutal this season, with no signs of improvement of late, while the White Sox haven't fared much better, sitting in fourth place in the division themselves. The two teams have split the last ten meetings on the season, though it remains to be seen exactly which way this one will go.

Royals Limped Into Fall

It has been another long season for the Royals and despite some young talent that turned some heads early, they’ve been unable to generate much consistency as the season has progressed. Having one of the worst records in baseball seems to be an accurate reflection of where they are as a team, while the idea of the playoffs over the next few years seems like a tough ask. If they were taking on a playoff team, or a team in a race, one might think that the idea of playing spoiler might motivate them. With the mediocrity on the other side too, there seems to be little for the Royals to play for in this one. Add in a dreadful road mark and there could certainly be some uninspired baseball played by them in this one.

https://m.youtube.com/watch?v=N0-BntaszkI

According to MLB.com, Jordan Lyles will get the ball in this one, as the righty looks to leave his mark on a divisional rival. With a 4-16 record on the season, he’s left quite a bit to be desired, while a 6.24 ERA doesn’t instill a lot of confidence either. He went up against Chicago in his last start, allowing three runs across 5.1 innings of work, which would classify as a solid outing for him. If he can replicate that performance in this one, there’s no doubt that the Royals will be happy.

It hasn’t helped matters that the offense has struggled. The youthfulness has shown at times but hasn’t been the best recipe for consistency and success, especially over the course of the summer. They only average a hair over four runs per game and the lack of depth for them to lean on is stunning. Beyond Bobby Witt Jr., who is looking like a star in the making, there simply isn’t enough talent elsewhere to compete on a nightly basis.

Chicago Eyeing Home Triumph

The White Sox haven’t given fans much to cheer for this season and with only a few weeks left, it’s clear that the team will need to look toward 2024 for success. The off-season acquisitions seemed impressive, though injuries were difficult for this team to overcome too. It hasn’t helped that they’ve struggled to take care of opportunities, especially at home, while playing in a weaker division hasn't benefitted them either. They may have nothing to play for when it comes to the standings but giving the fans a win in the doubleheader could be enough to push them forward.

https://m.youtube.com/watch?v=eFtYeVuC3m0

The White Sox will hand the ball off to Touki Toussaint in this one, as the youngster tries to improve on his own poor showing this season. With a 3-7 record himself, to go along with a 4.22 ERA, it hasn’t been the easiest transition into the rotation over the last couple of months. He was electric against the Royals the last time out though, allowing only two runs across six innings of work, in a start that should generate some confidence as he enters Tuesday night.

The offense has been hit or miss this season but with the injury woes eased, they have the lineup to do damage in this one. Whether they come through against a divisional rival remains a question that they’ve struggled to answer at times. Luis Robert Jr. has done most of the damage this season, though without much help in the lineup, despite the big names, it could be another challenge on Tuesday. Lyles looked good against them the last time out, so the hope is that performance doesn’t bleed over into this one.
