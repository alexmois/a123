Birds are battling this week, with the St. Louis Cardinals (63-80; before Monday's game) taking on the Baltimore Orioles (90-52) in Charm City. Tuesday's affair will be game two of the series. The Cardinals haven't flown to Baltimore since 2017, but finally get to play in Oriole Park at Camden Yards again. These teams last played in 2022, with the Orioles taking two of three in St. Louis. Round two of 2023 is scheduled to start at 6:35 p.m. EDT, assuming the weather holds up. Let's see who will spread their wings as the regular season winds down.

 

*Published before Monday's results

Cardinals Still Grounded

They were supposed to contend for the NL Central title, but instead, the Cardinals built their nest in the division's basement this season. Pitching has been a disaster in St. Louis, and various trades haven't helped. 18-year veteran Adam Wainwright has endured on the mound in Cardinal Red, for better or worse. What may be the final season of his career has been a disaster, including an active seven-start losing streak. Wainwright is responsible for 35 runs and 10 homers during that 32.1-inning stretch. His WHIP has been 2.07 since July 29. St. Louis' bullpen isn't much better, with a bottom 10 ERA and left-on-base percentage. Don't expect much from the Cardinals' pitching staff for the rest of 2023. 

Offensively, the Cardinals still hold their own. They began the series 16th in runs per game this season, even if the numbers have dipped a bit post-trade deadline. For the year, the Cardinals are a top-10 offense in home runs, walk rate, OBP, and slugging percentage. A team OPS of .754 (tied for 8th) and 35.4% hard-hit rate (3rd) are joining them on the trip to Maryland. St. Louis is just outside the top 10 in batting average (13th) and strikeout rate (12th). Baserunning gets the Cardinals into trouble, but there aren't many teams that outclass them at the plate.

Orioles are Soaring

Sunday's loss in Boston may have been disappointing, but it was only Baltimore's second since September began. A 7-2 record to open September has the Orioles ahead of the Rays by 3.0 games. That lead is for both the AL East crown and the Junior Circuit's best record. This game will welcome back southpaw starting pitcher John Means, a talented arm that has unfortunately battled injuries the past few seasons. An All-Star in 2019, Means had an elite walk rate when healthy in 2020 and 2021, ranking at least 95th percentile in both seasons. He's set to return to his home mound with a 3.96 ERA in 182.0 career innings. Baltimore's bullpen leads MLB teams in WAR, so they can withstand a start of any length.

So can an offense that's sixth in runs per game this season. Matching the Cardinals, the Orioles began this series with a .754 team OPS. Outside of a bottom-10 walk rate, there aren't many things that the Orioles don't do well. They're seventh in batting average, successfully steal bases 82% of the time (7th), and ninth in slugging percentage. The lineup is as healthy as ever, which only offers more protection for budding star Gunnar Henderson.

Adam Wainwright has been batting practice lately, so the Cardinals will need to hit to compete in this game. Full-Game Total Pick

Insiders Status:

Rating:

These teams are going to bring the bats on Tuesday. Starting with the visitors, they're starting Adam Wainwright and his 8.19 2023 ERA. That ERA is up to 9.74 during his seven-start losing streak. St. Louis relievers are 25th in LOB% and 23rd in ERA, so they won't offer much resistance against an Orioles offense that's scoring the sixth-most runs per game this year.

Flipping to the home team, the starting pitcher is lefty John Means. He hasn't tossed on an MLB mound since last April and has to face a lineup that's averaging 4.58 runs per game. The Cardinals have three powerful right-handers, Goldschmidt, Contreras, and Nolan Arenado. Two of those players have an OPS over .800 against lefties in 2023, as does the Cardinals' home run leader, left-handed hitter Nolan Gorman. It'll probably be a rough welcome back for Means against a tough St. Louis team. Expect a lot of runs in this game.
