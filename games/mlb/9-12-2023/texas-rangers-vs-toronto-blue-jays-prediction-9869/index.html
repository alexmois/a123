Falling Fast

The sagging Texas Rangers arrive in Toronto trying to earn back their playoff spot against a red-hot Blue Jays team. The Rangers spent nearly the whole season atop the AL West Division and fortified their roster at the trade deadline by acquiring two starting pitchers and some help in the bullpen. Unfortunately, the Rangers have seemingly run out of steam. They are now in third place in the AL West Division having been passed by both the Astros and Mariners in the standings. Prior to closing out their series with the A's with two straight wins, the Rangers had dropped six of eight games. In game one against the Blue Jays, the Rangers offense came alive for 10 runs on 11 hits to support starter Dane Dunning in a 10-4 Rangers' win.

The Texas Rangers will turn to one of their trade deadline acquisitions, Max Scherzer, in game two of this critical series vs. the Jays. Scherzer is 12-6 in 26 starts this season between the Mets and Rangers. He has allowed 123 hits in 1471.1 innings pitched with an ERA of 2.91. He's struck out 172 batters on the season but has given up 28 home runs. On the road, Scherzer is 6-4 in 14 starts with an ERA of 4.59. He has allowed 75 hits in 80.1 innings pitched with 93 strikeouts. In his last five starts, Scherzer is 1-2 with an ERA of 4.05 with 36 strikeouts in 26.2 innings pitched.

Key Injuries: 3B Josh Jung and RF Adolis Garcia are out.

Streaking Jays

The Toronto Blue Jays enter this series with the Rangers as one of the hottest teams in baseball. Toronto has won eight of its last ten games including a three-game sweep of the Oakland A's at home over the weekend. The sweep pushed the Blue Jays to 80 victories on the season and ahead of the Rangers in the AL Wildcard standings. In game one of their series with the Rangers, the Blue Jays were routed by the Rangers 10-4 as starter Chris Bassitt gave up five runs on 9 hits in just 5.1 innings of work.

The Blue Jays will turn to lefty Hyun Jin Ryu in game two against the Rangers. Jin Ryu recently returned from an over year-layoff following Tommy John surgery. He has made seven starts thus far with a record of 3-2 and an ERA of 2.65. Jin Ryu has allowed 28 hits in 34 innings in those seven starts. He has a K/9 rate of 7.4 and his WHIP is 1.06. Jin Ryu has gone five innings exactly in all but one of his seven starts this season as the Jays monitor his innings post-surgery.

Key Injuries: C Dan Jansen and 3B Matt Chapman are out.

Best Bets for this Game

Full-Game Side Bet

Insiders Status:

Rating:

Even with Scherzer on the hill, I'm leaning toward the Blue Jays in game two of this series. Scherzer has an ERA over four in his last five starts and the sinking Rangers have lost three of his last four starts. Texas's inability to close out games will factor in here against a Blue Jays team that has as good a backend as any team in baseball now with Hicks and Romano set up for innings eight and nine. Blue Jays' starter Jin Ryu will be limited to five innings as he has throughout his seven starts since returning from injury. Toronto is equipped to finish off his starts with their bullpen. In fact, the Blue Jays have won four of Jin Ryu's last five starts despite the left-hander never getting past the fifth inning. The multi-time Cy Young Award-winning Scherzer will keep the game close but the Jays will pull it out against the Rangers reeling bullpen in the late innings.

Take the Blue Jays with the money line.

Prediction: Toronto -112

Full-Game Total Pick

Insiders Status:

Rating:

These are two of the better-hitting teams in baseball but they will be matched up with two very efficient pitchers on Tuesday night. The Rangers, in particular, have struggled at the plate in the second half of the season. After leading baseball in runs scored throughout the first half and into the second half, the Rangers bats have cooled considerably. In their last 30 games, Texas is hitting just .243 as a team and has gone 12-18 in that span. After averaging over six runs per game for much of the season, the Rangers are averaging just 4.3 runs per game in the last 30 games. For their part, the Blue Jays have gone under the run total in three of their last four games heading into this series. This will be a low-scoring affair on Tuesday night that stays under the total.

Take the under.

Prediction: Under 8.5
