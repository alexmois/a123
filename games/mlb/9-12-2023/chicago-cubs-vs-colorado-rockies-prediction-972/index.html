The Chicago Cubs and Colorado Rockies will go head-to-head on Tuesday night at Coors Field. First pitch is at 6:40 p.m. MT.

Chicago (78-67 SU and 75-69 RL) is starting right-hander Javier Assad. The 26-year-old is 3-3 with a 2.83 ERA and a 1.20 WHIP in 26 outings (eight starts).

Colorado (51-92 SU and 67-75 RL) is countering with righty Chris Flexen. The 29-year-old is 1-7 with a 7.36 ERA and a 1.74 WHIP in 25 outings (12 starts).

The Cubs are -175 moneyline favorites and the game total is 12 runs.

Is Chicago a legitimate WC team?

Chicago dropped three of four games to Arizona last week but still has a two-game lead over the D-Backs for the second NL Wild Card spot. Can it get back on track in Denver?

The Cubbies score 5.03 runs per game (7th) and hit .253 (12th) with a .746 OPS (13th). They have hit 173 home runs (14th) and stolen 123 bases (7th) this year. The Chicago pitching staff has compiled a 4.01 ERA (12th) and a 1.26 WHIP (10th) with 66 quality starts (3rd).
