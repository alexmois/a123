On Tuesday, the Oakland Athletics (45-99, 5th AL West) play the middle game of a three-game series at Minute Maid Park in Houston against American League West rival the Houston Astros (82-63, 1st AL West). Oakland was defeated by the Texas Rangers 9-4 on Sunday and lost two out of three to Texas over the weekend. The A’s have lost four of the last 7 and are one of three teams in the American League that have been eliminated from postseason contention. Houston defeated the San Diego Padres 12-2 on Sunday and won two out of three in their interleague series with the Padres. Houston has a 2-game lead over second-place Texas in the AL West. Oakland will play without eight pitchers as well as center fielder JJ Bleday. Houston will play without three pitchers.

Oakland is baseball's worst at the plate and on the mound

The Oakland Athletics are 28th or worse in four major hitting categories. Oakland is 28th in on base percentage at .302, while dropping to 30th or last in each of the following: runs scored (527), team batting average (.225) and slugging percentage (.373). Brent Rooker has been one of the few bright spots for Oakland this season and leads the A’s in home runs with 23 and RBI with 57. Shea Langeliers is second in both home runs with 17 and RBI with 51. Oakland has hit 202 doubles, 19 triples and 151 home runs in 143 games.

On the mound, Oakland is 27th or worse in four major pitching categories. The A’s are 27th in batting average allowed at .269, while 29th in both WHIP at 1.54 and team ERA at 5.61 and 30th or last in Quality Starts with just 27. Tuesday’s starting pitcher JP Sears is the team leader in strikeouts with 141. The left-hander is 4-11 with an ERA of 4.51.

Houston is tough both at the plate and on the mound

Houston has battled its way to first place in the American League West thanks to excellent production at the plate. Houston is in the top seven of four major hitting categories. The Astros are fourth in team batting average at .261, fifth in both runs scored with 750 and on base percentage at .333, while 7th in slugging percentage with .437. Kyle Tucker has hit a team-high 26 home runs and is also the leader in RBI with 103. Alex Bregman has been very productive at the plate with 27 doubles, three triples, 22 home runs and 93 RBI.

Houston is also performing very well on the mound as the Astros are 5th in quality starts with 64, 9th in team ERA at 3.97, 13th in batting average allowed at .244 and 18th in WHIP at 1.29. Framber Valdez is leading Texas in victories with 11 and in strikeouts with 168. Justin Verlander will start on the mound Tuesday for Houston and the right-hander is 11-7 with an ERA of 3.23 and 119 strikeouts.
