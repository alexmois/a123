The Atlanta Braves (93-49) will be going for the series win when they face the Philadelphia Phillies (78-64) in the third game of their four-game series on Tuesday night.

Max Fried, 7-1, 2.70 ERA, will get the start for the Braves. The Phillies will counter with Zack Wheeler, 11-6, 3.49 ERA.

Atlanta and Philadelphia split their last 10 meetings.

**This preview was written before Monday’s doubleheader was played**

Braves Going For Fourth Win In Five Games

The Braves are playing well at the moment and they’ve won three of their last four games. They will try to keep the momentum going with a win over the Phillies, which will give them their fourth win in their last five games.

Atlanta is averaging 5.82 runs per game. Their .276 batting average leads the league. Their .345 on base percentage also leads the league, as does their .502 slugging percentage.

Ronald Acuna Jr. leads Atlanta with a .336 batting average, while Matt Olson leads the team with 48 home runs and 121 RBI.

Atlanta’s pitching has been good, with the team giving up 4.16 runs per game. Opponents have a .241 batting average against the Braves, which is ninth in the league. Their 3.90 ERA is fifth, while their 1.27 WHIP is 14th.

In his last start, Fried gave up six hits and three runs in six innings, leading to an 8-5 win over St. Louis. They will need another solid start from him if they want to get the win.

Phillies Trying To Increase Wildcard Lead

The Phillies played well over the past week and they’ve won four of their last seven games. They will try to keep the momentum going with a win over the Braves, which will give them their fifth win in their last eight games and increase their lead in the NL Wildcard standings.

Philadelphia is averaging 4.88 runs per game. Their .259 batting average is sixth in the league. Their .329 on base percentage is seventh, while their .440 slugging percentage is fifth.

Bryce Harper leads the Phillies with a .294 batting average, while Kyle Schwarber leads the team with 43 home runs and 94 RBI.

Philadelphia’s pitching has been good, with the team giving up 4.37 runs per game. Opponents have a .242 batting average against the Phillies, which is 10th in the league. Their 3.99 ERA is also 10th, while their 1.24 WHIP is eighth.

In his last start, Wheeler gave up one hit and no runs in six innings, leading to a 5-1 win over San Diego. They will need a similar effort from him if they want to win this game.
