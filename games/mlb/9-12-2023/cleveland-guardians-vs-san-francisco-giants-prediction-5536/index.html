Cleveland Guardians (68-76) vs. San Francisco Giants (73-70)

The Major League Baseball betting action goes on Tuesday, September 12, 2023, with the interleague showdown from Oracle Park in San Francisco, California, so we bring you the best Guardians vs. Giants betting pick and odds. 

Cleveland and San Francisco continue a three-game series, and Monday’s opener has been excluded from this preview. The Giants are listed as -133 moneyline favorites with a total of 8.0 runs. 

The Guardians are slumping at the plate

The Cleveland Guardians fell to 68-76 on the season following Sunday’s 2-1 defeat at the Los Angeles Angels. It was Cleveland’s sixth loss in eight games, and the Guardians have only scored 24 runs during that stretch. Now, they are 7.5 games behind the AL Central-leading Minnesota Twins. 

The Guardians are slashing .253/.320/.384 in the last couple of weeks and 487 at-bats. They’ve slugged just 11 home runs in that span to go with 27 doubles and a pair of triples. Their bullpen has struggled, too, tallying a 5.63 ERA and 5.40 FIP through 54.1 innings of work. 

Cal Quantrill will be on the bump Tuesday, and the 28-year-old right-hander is 2-6 with a pedestrian 5.70 ERA, 1.46 WHIP, and 46/27 K/BB ratio in 15 starts (79 innings pitched) this season. Quantrill, who spent nearly two months on the IL, has pitched very well since he returned to the Guardians rotation on September 1. He’s allowed just two earned runs on five hits and five walks over his previous two starts (12 IP).

The Giants just swept the lowly Rockies

The San Francisco Giants beat the Colorado Rockies 6-3 on Sunday night to sweep a three-game home set and move to 73-70 on the season. The Giants have scored 24 runs throughout the series which is exactly how many runs the Guardians have scored over their previous eight outings. Now, the Giants are 1.5 games behind the final wild-card spot in the National League. 

The Giants bullpen hasn’t been at its best over the past two weeks, notching a 4.44 ERA and 4.08 FIP across 46.2 frames of work. On the other side of the ball, they are slashing .249/.315/.430 with 31 extra-base hits and 15 home runs across 433 at-bats during that span. 

Sean Manaea is projected to take the hill on Tuesday. The 31-year-old left-hander is 5-5 with a troublesome 5.00 ERA, 1.32 WHIP, and a 110/40 K/BB ratio in six starts and 27 relief appearances (93.2 IP) in 2023. Since August 15, Manaea has pitched 16 innings across five games while posting a 4.50 ERA, 6.57 FIP, and .835 opposing OPS. Last Wednesday, he tossed three innings of a two-run, four-hit ball in a no-decision against the Chicago Cubs.

The Giants got things going at the dish in the Rockies series, so I’m backing them in this game. Frankly, it could go either way because Sean Manaea hasn’t been at his best of late, whereas Cal Quantrill has pitched at a high level over his previous two starts. 

The Guardians hold a 113 wRC+ against the southpaws in September (100 plate appearances). On the other side, the Giants own a 136 wRC+ against right-handed pitchers in September (201 PA). Over the last ten days, San Francisco’s bullpen has recorded a 4.50 ERA and 3.99 FIP, whereas Cleveland’s bullpen has registered an ugly 7.13 ERA and 5.58 FIP.
