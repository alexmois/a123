The Arizona Diamondbacks (75-69, 76-68 RL, 61-76-7 O/U) battle the New York Mets (65-77, 61-81 RL, 56-78-8 O/U) in the second of a four-game series on Tuesday evening. The Diamondbacks will send Ryne Nelson to the mound. The Mets will reply with Jose Butto. The Mets lead the season series 3-0.

Nelson Starts, D-Backs Continue Road Trip

Ryne Nelson will get the nod on Tuesday. Nelson was stellar in his previous outing, conceding only one run in 5.2 innings against the Cubs, and was credited with the victory. The young pitcher was demoted due to his struggles in August but is given another chance. He has posted a 5.30 ERA and a 1.42 WHIP accompanied by a 7-7 record in 130.2 innings on the season. Nelsons squandered seven runs in three innings against the Mets earlier this season, marking his only career meeting. 

Arizona is near the end of a road trek. They were able to collect three big wins in four road meetings against the Cubs and have built a 0.5-game cushion in the last NL wildcard spot. The squad has won two of their last three series and has a reasonably easy schedule for the remainder of the season. They stand at 6-4 in their last ten games

Ketel Marte missed Sunday’s game due to a knee injury and is questionable for this game. The 29-year-old is a key player, clubbing 22 homers with 73 RBIs and an .838 OPS. Marte has collected five RBIs and a strong .994 OPS this month. He is 1 for 4 with zero RBIs against the Mets this season.

Arizona is posting an average of 4.65 runs, ranking them 13th. The pitching has been solid recently and has a 4.56 team ERA, placing them 21st.

Butto Gets Another Chance

Jose Butto will get the nod. Butto was stellar in his previous effort, allowing just two runs in 6.1 innings against the Nationals, resulting in a no-decision in a game the Mets lost. The rookie has been encouraging in his limited action, posting a 3.86 ERA and a 1.57 WHIP but is 0-2 in 21 innings pitched on the season. Butto issued a poor 5.93 ERA in the minors this season and has a 3.86 ERA and a 1.26 WHIP in his five seasons in the minor leagues. 

The Mets have dropped four of their last five series after they were dealt a series loss in Minnesota on the weekend. They scored just eight runs in the series. At this point, they are awarding a lot of playing time to the younger players and prospects. They did win their previous home series, beating the Mariners. The Mets are 5-5 in their last ten clashes.

Pete Alonso continues to produce, delivering four homers and seven RBIs this month. The star now has 43 bombs along with 103 RBIs and an .859 OPS on the year. Alonso is 2 for 13 with three RBIs against the Diamondbacks pitching this season. Starling Marte is on the IL.

New York is averaging 4.35 runs on the year, marking them 20th. The pitching is subpar, posting a 4.43 team ERA, positioning them 19th.
