The Washington Nationals (65-79, 79-65 RL, 69-69-6 O/U) meet with the Pittsburgh Pirates (66-78, 72-72 RL, 78-62-4 O/U) in the second of a four-game series on Tuesday night. The Nationals will have Joan Adon on the mound. The Pirates will reply with Bailey Falter. The season series is 2-2.

Adon Starts, Nats Begin Road Trek

Joan Adon will get the nod on Tuesday evening. Adon was solid in his previous start, holding the Mets to only two runs in five innings, resulting in a no-decision in a clash the Nationals won. The young pitcher struggled in his last few outings of August which has plummeted his ERA. He has issued a 5.56 ERA and a 1.24 WHIP accompanied by a 2-3 record in 34 innings on the season. Adon allowed six runs in 4.2 innings in his only career meeting against the Pirates. 

The Nationals were outstanding for most of August but are slumping now. They have lost four consecutive series including a four-game home sweep dealt by the Marlins in that span. They lost two of three home bouts against the Dodgers this past weekend, allowing 21 runs. The Nats are 2-8 in their last ten decisions.

Keibert Ruiz had an outstanding August but has been quiet recently, posting four RBIs and a .565 OPS on the month. The 25-year-old catcher is showing promise, bashing 17 homers with 59 RBIs and a .717 OPS. Ruiz is 2 for 8 with zero RBIs against the Pirates this season.

Washington is scoring an average of 4.32 runs on the year, marking them 21st. The pitching is struggling and now has a 4.98 team ERA, pegging them 27th.

Pirates Likely Going with a Bullpen Start

The Pirates will likely be going with another bullpen game in this one. Recently Colin Selby has been the opener and Bailey Falter has been the bulk innings option. Selby has a 7.20 ERA and a 1.60 WHIP in only 15 innings. Falter just posted 3.2 shutout innings against the Brewers in his previous outing, resulting in a no-decision in a Pirates win. The veteran has been reasonably good since he was acquired in late July and has recorded a 4.65 ERA and a 1.34 WHIP accompanied by a 1-8 record in 71.2 innings. Falter has held the Nationals to five runs in 18.1 innings. 

Pittsburgh has been competitive. They recently won two of three home bouts against a strong Brewers squad. They were able to limit Milwaukee to 13 runs in that series. Next, the Pirates lost two of three road clashes to the Braves. Pittsburgh is going to be an interesting young squad to watch next season. They stand at 6-4 in their last ten clashes.

Ke’Bryan Hayes has been a bright spot in recent series. The 26-year-old third baseman posted a .956 OPS last month and recorded a strong .796 OPS in September. He now has 55 RBIs and a .766 OPS on the season. He is 3 for 12 with zero RBIs against the Nationals. Andrew McCutchen is on the IL. 

Pittsburgh is averaging 4.20 runs on the year, ranking them 24th. The pitching is subpar, posting a 4.52 team ERA, placing them 20th.
