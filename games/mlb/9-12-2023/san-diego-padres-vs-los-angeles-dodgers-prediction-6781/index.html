The San Diego Padres and Los Angeles Dodgers will resume their rivalry on Tuesday night at Dodger Stadium. First pitch is at 7:10 p.m. PT.

San Diego (67-77 SU and 70-74 RL) is starting right-hander Michael Wacha. The 32-year-old is 11-3 with a 2.99 ERA and a 1.17 WHIP in 20 outings this season.

Los Angeles (87-55 SU and 81-61 RL) is countering with righty Lance Lynn. The 36-year-old is 10-11 with a 6.09 ERA and a 1.42 WHIP in his 28 outings.

The Dodgers are -155 moneyline favorites and the game total is 9.5 runs.

Can San Diego still salvage a winning season?

San Diego dropped two of three games to the Astros in Houston over the weekend. It's been a disappointing season in America's Finest City, as SD couldn't build momentum following last season's playoff run to the NLCS.

The Padres score 4.55 runs per game (17th) and hit .240 (22nd) with a .737 OPS (17th). They've hit 193 homers (13th) and stolen 117 bases (8th) this year. The pitching staff has compiled a 3.86 ERA (4th) and a 1.29 WHIP (16th) with 66 quality starts (4th).

Wacha will get the start for the visitors in game two. The veteran is 3-0 with a 3.65 ERA and a 1.17 WHIP in eight road starts this season. In his last appearance, he picked up the loss, surrendering three runs and seven hits in just four innings vs. Philadelphia. Wacha has not faced LA in 2023.

LA is the best of the West

Los Angeles is on a collision course with Atlanta in the National League playoff race. The Braves got the best of LA earlier this month, winning three of four games — can it have better success in the postseason?

The Dodgers average 5.63 runs (2nd) and hit .258 (10th) with a .801 OPS (2nd). They've hit 222 homers (2nd) and stolen 92 bases (20th) in 2023. The LA pitching staff has posted a 4.18 ERA (15th) and a 1.22 WHIP (5th) with 47 quality starts (18th).

Lynn will toe the rubber for the home team on Tuesday night. The 12th-year pro is 4-2 with a 4.95 ERA and a 1.30 WHIP in seven starts with LA. In his most recent appearance, he gave up eight runs in 4.2 innings against Miami. He was 4-1 with a 3.57 ERA in six August outings, which included a victory over the Padres (one run surrendered in six innings).

Full-Game Side Bet

Insiders Status:

Rating:

I'm betting on the Padres' bats to show up on Tuesday against Lynn, giving Wacha the run support he needs to hold the Doders' lineup in check.

San Diego scored just one run against Lynn last month, but I don't anticipate a similar performance from the 12th-year vet on Tuesday. He has been awful in his last two starts (15 runs on 14 hits and 6 HRs), which wasn't surprising considering how he performed earlier this season with Chicago. The right-hander has been a trainwreck, giving up a career-high 40 home runs in 28 starts — 13 more than his previous career high in 2017. His WHIP (1.42) is the second-highest of his career, too. The Padres may still have some fight and should be more comfortable battling with the dependable Wacha on the mound.

The 32-year-old has been one of the few bright spots in San Diego, with ten quality starts in 20 outings — his most QS since the 2017 season with St. Louis. He's allowed more than three runs only once since mid-April. LA has an explosive lineup, but I'm confident the veteran (3.12 ERA and 1.15 WHIP in 14 night games) can hold them off just enough to lead his team to victory.

At nearly +140 odds, I see fair value in a wager on the Padres ML. I recommend betting four units!

Full-Game Total Pick

Insiders Status:

Rating:

The Padres' lineup comes and goes without saying a proper goodbye, making it difficult to anticipate their next scoreless flop. However, with Lynn on the mound, there's a decent shot that they push at least a few runs across the plate. Besides, there is still plenty of talent on this San Diego roster — I believe they will prove that at once this week in Los Angeles.

The Dodgers are facing a tougher matchup vs. Wacha, but they're the more reliable bet to score five or six runs on Tuesday. This season, LA averages 5.7 runs in night games (98 GP). It's coming off its most impressive performance at the plate in a single month (.292 BA/.365 OBP/.498 SLG/.863 OPS) and owns this "rivalry." As reliable as he's been, Wacha's road ERA is over a run higher than his home ERA, as well.

It may take a high-scoring game for San Diego to come out on top — the over is 67-43-6 when Los Angeles is betting favorites. A ten-run total will likely require both teams to participate, which makes this a speculative wager, hence the three-unit recommendation.

Prediction: Over 9.5
