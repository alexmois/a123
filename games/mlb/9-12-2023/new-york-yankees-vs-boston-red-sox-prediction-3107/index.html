The New York Yankees will play the second game of a day/night doubleheader in Boston on Tuesday night against the Red Sox. Monday night's game was rained out to create the doubleheader on Tuesday. The long-time rivals are both out of the playoff hunt this season and have been reduced to battling for the fourth and fifth spots in the American League East in the season's final few weeks. The pitching matchup on Tuesday night will feature Yankees' lefty Carlos Rodon (2-5, 6.60) taking on Red Sox' righty Kutter Crawford (6-7, 4.36). The first pitch is scheduled for 7:10 PM EST.

Bad to Worse

A disappointing season for the New York Yankees went from bad to worse this week when it was learned that 20-year-old rookie outfielder Jasson Dominguez tore the UCL ligament in his throwing shoulder and will have to undergo Tommy John surgery that will likely keep him out for the next nine to ten months. It was just another in a series of disappointments for the Bronx Bombers this season. The Yankees are in last place in the AL East heading into this series with the Red Sox and have a roster littered with aging veterans and large contracts. The Yankees come into this series after losing three of their last four games, including two of three to the Brewers over the weekend.

The Yankees will give the ball to struggling lefty Carlos Rodon in game two against the Red Sox. Rodon has been a major disappointment since coming to the Yankees in free agency. He spent a large portion of the season on the IL and has struggled since entering the rotation. Rodon is 2-5 in 10 starts including just one quality start. He's allowed 44 hits in 46.1 innings pitched with an ERA of 6.60. Rodon has a K/9 rate of 8.0 thus far and a WHIP of 1.40. He is 0-2 in four starts on the road with an ERA of 6.63.

Key Injuries: LF Billy McKinney is out.

Looking to Stay out of the Cellar

The Red Sox hope to continue their recent dominance of the Yankees and stay out of the cellar in the AL East Division this season. Boston has lost four out of their last five games coming into this series including two of three against first-place Baltimore over the weekend. The Red Sox head into the matchup with the Yankees with just a two-game lead over the Bombers as they try to avoid last place.

The Red Sox will hand the ball to righty Kutter Crawford in the second game against the Yankees on Tuesday night. Crawford is 6-7 in 27 appearances overall including 19 starts. He has allowed 95 hits in 107.1 innings pitched with 107 strikeouts. Crawford has faced the Yankees one time this season, in New York, and picked up the win while going six innings and allowing just one hit and one run while walking two batters and striking out five.

Key Injuries: 2B/SS Pablo Reyes is out.
