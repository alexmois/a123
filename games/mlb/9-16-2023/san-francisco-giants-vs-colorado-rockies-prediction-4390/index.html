The Colorado Rockies (53-92) will be trying to pick up their second consecutive series win when they face the San Francisco Giants (75-71) in the second game of their doubleheader on Saturday night.

Kyle Freeland, 6-14, 5.07 ERA, will get the start for the Rockies. The Giants will counter with Alex Cobb, 7-6, 3.62 ERA.

San Francisco is 9-1 in its last 10 games against Colorado.

**This preview was written before Friday’s game was played**

Giants In Thick Of Wildcard Race

The Giants went on a run over the past week, winning five of their last six games. It allowed them to catch up to Arizona and Cincinnati in the Wildcard standings with all three teams tied for the third and final NL Wildcard spot. They will try to move into the final spot with a win over the Rockies, which will give them their sixth win in their last seven games and sixth straight win over the Rockies.

San Francisco is averaging 4.32 runs per game. Their .241 batting average is 22nd in the league. Their .316 on base percentage is 20th, while their .392 slugging percentage is 24th.

Thairo Estrada leads the Giants with a .272 batting average. Wilmer Flores leads the team with 22 home runs, while J.D. Davis leads the team with 68 RBI.

San Francisco’s pitching has been good, with the team giving up 4.36 runs per game. Opponents have a .252 batting average against the Giants, which is 21st in the league. Their 3.99 ERA is 11th, while their 1.25 WHIP is eighth.

In his last start, Cobb gave up three hits and two runs in five innings, leading to a 5-4 win over Cleveland. They will need another solid effort from him if they want to get the win.

Rockies Trying To Play Spoiler

The Rockies followed up their five-game losing streak by winning two straight games. Even though they’ve been eliminated from the playoffs, they will be trying to keep their division rivals out of the postseason with a win on Saturday night.

Colorado is averaging 4.41 runs per game. Their .248 batting average is 16th in the league. Their .309 on base percentage is 25th, while their .401 slugging percentage is 19th.

Elias Diaz leads the Rockies with a .272 batting average, while Ryan McMahon leads the team with 23 home runs and 70 RBI.

Colorado’s pitching is one of the worst in the league, with the team giving up 5.91 runs per game. Opponents have a .287 batting average against the Rockies, which is 30th in the league. Their 5.66 ERA is also 30th, as is their 1.54 WHIP.

In his last start, Freeland gave up eight hits and three runs in six innings, leading to a 5-4 loss to the Cubs. They will need another solid start from him if they want to win this game.
