The Minnesota Twins and Chicago White Sox will resume their AL Central rivalry on Saturday night at Guaranteed Rate Field. First pitch is scheduled for 6:10 p.m. CT.

Minnesota (77-70 SU and 72-75 RL) is starting righty Pablo López. The 27-year-old is 10-7 with a 3.43 ERA and a 1.14 WHIP in 29 outings this year.

Chicago (56-91 SU and 72-75 RL) is countering with right-hander Touki Toussaint. The 27-year-old is 3-7 with a 5.65 ERA and a 1.52 WHIP in his 17 outings (13 starts).

The Twins are -200 ML favorites and the game total is nine runs.

*Article published before the conclusion of Friday's game.

Injury Report:

Minnesota is playing without CF Michael Taylor, 2B Nick Gordon, DH Byron Buxton, and 1B Joey Gallo.

Are the Twins legit playoff contenders?

Minny has extended its AL Central lead with a 32-24 record since the All-Star game, even without a fully loaded lineup. Will it be a legitimate threat in the American League playoff race?

The Twins average 4.66 runs (12th) and hit .241 (21st) with a .747 OPS (13th). They've launched 210 homers (5th) and stolen 80 bases (24th) this season. The Minnesota pitching staff has compiled a 3.92 ERA (5th) and a 1.20 WHIP (4th) with a league-leading 71 quality starts.

López is starting for Minny on Saturday night in Chi-Town. The sixth-year pro is 6-4 with a 2.78 ERA and a 1.11 WHIP in 15 road games. In his last start, he held the Mets to two hits and zero runs with 14 strikeouts in eight dominant innings. López has held the White Sox to four total runs with 18 strikeouts in 14.2 combined innings this season.

Chicago is a much better ATS bet than SU

Chicago hasn't won enough games straight up, but it's been a much better team against the spread. It has the same ATS record as division leader Minnesota!

The Southsiders score 4.10 runs per game (25th) and hit .240 (24th) with a .682 OPS (28th). They've launched 158 home runs (20th) and stolen 81 bases (23rd) in 2023. The Chi-Town pitching staff has compiled a 4.93 ERA (26th) and a 1.42 WHIP (26th) with 44 quality starts (23rd).

Toussaint will toe the rubber for the White Sox on Saturday at Guaranteed Rate Field. He is 2-4 with a 6.11 ERA and 1.53 WHIP in nine outings (seven starts) this season. In his last outing, he gave up eight runs in just one inning vs. Kansas City. The sixth-year pro hasn't faced the Twins in 2023.

Full-Game Side Bet

Insiders Status:

Rating:

Betting Trends: Chicago is 56-47 against the spread as betting underdogs, and Minnesota is 44-55 against the spread as betting favorites. The White Sox are 28-21 RL against division opponents, while Minnesota is just 23-26 RL.

I'm confident Toussaint will turn in a quality start tomorrow against the Twins. He was knocked around in his last start but he was making consecutive starts against the same opponent. After holding the Royals to two runs in six innings in the first outing, they got to him early in his next start, which isn't surprising. The good news is that he should be plenty rested, as he only pitched one inning in that appearance. He's facing a Twins club that hasn't faced him in 2023 and is without a few of its better hitters, as well.

López held the Southsiders in check on April 11 and May 4, but they should have some success against him on Saturday at home. He's about to set his career high in innings pitched and emptied the tank in his last outing, throwing 106 pitches in eight innings. If he's not as sharp tomorrow, Chicago will make him pay.

At +114 odds, I see significant value in a wager on the White Sox to cover the 1.5-run line!

Prediction: Chicago RL

Full-Game Total Pick

Insiders Status:

Rating:

Betting Trends: The under is 28-19-2 in Chicago's games vs. AL Central foes and 22-17-2 when the Sox are home underdogs.

The Twins slash .234 BA/.314 OBP/.403 SLG/.718 OPS on the road compared to .249/.329/.448/.777 at home. On the road vs. a divisional opponent without Gallo, Buxton, and others, this lineup could fail to score more than three runs against Toussaint, who has held 12 of 17 opponents to that mark. After pitching just one inning in his previous appearance, he will likely give Chicago length in tomorrow's outing, taking some pressure off the bullpen.

López has been dependable this season, holding 19 of 29 opponents to two or fewer runs. Chicago (.236/.291/.384/.675 vs. right-handed pitchers) only managed four runs against him in two games earlier this season and is scoring just 3.4 runs per game in September.

I am comfortable betting on the Southsiders to cover the run line in a low-scoring affair on Saturday in Chicago.
