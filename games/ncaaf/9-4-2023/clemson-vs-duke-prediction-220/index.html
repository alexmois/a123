The first full week of the college football season comes to a close with an ACC matchup on the gridiron down on Tobacco Road. In a Labor Day primetime clash, the #9 Clemson Tigers are on the road as they travel to face the Duke Blue Devils Monday night. Clemson finished the 2022 season 11-3 overall and won the ACC title, though their season ended with a 31-14 loss to Tennessee in the Orange Bowl. Duke posted their first winning season since 2018 last year as they went 9-4 in Mike Elko’s first year running the program. Their season ended with a 30-13 win over UCF in the Military Bowl. In the all-time series between the programs, Clemson owns a 37-16-1 advantage and has won the last five meetings. That includes a 35-6 home triumph in the most recent matchup on November 17, 2018.

Clemson Tigers Looking to Return to College Football Playoff

Clemson is in that rarefied air in college football where you can have what 98% of other FBS programs would call a dream season and be disappointed. The Tigers won 11 games, earned their seventh ACC title in the last eight years and played in the Orange Bowl. However, they lost three games and missed the CFP for the second straight year after being a part of the field for six straight seasons between 2015 and 2020. The Tigers have some changes in store this season and it will be interesting to see how things unfold for them. Dabo Swinney fired offensive coordinator Brandon Streeter and replaced him with Garrett Riley, who had the same job at TCU. Along with a new offensive coordinator, the Tigers will have a new triggerman running the offense after DJ Uiagalelei transferred to Oregon State after a disappointing campaign.

Last season, Clemson was 65th in the nation in passing offense (232.4 yards per game) and 46th in rushing offense with 177.9 yards per contest. The Tigers finished last season ranked 29th in the FBS in scoring offense with 33.2 points per game this season. Clemson was 23rd in the nation in scoring defense by allowing 20.9 points per game. Cade Klubnik is the undisputed #1 QB for the program this season. He completed 61 of 100 throws for 697 yards with two touchdowns against three interceptions while adding 139 rushing yards and two scores. Will Shipley led the ground game with 210 carries for 1,182 yards and 15 scores. Phil Mafah (98 carries, 515 yards, four TDs) is a solid #2 back who could probably start for a lot of programs. Antonio Williams led the team with 56 catches for 604 yards and four scores last season as a freshman. Beaux Collins (22 grabs, 373 yards, five TDs), Cole Turner (eight receptions, 161 yards), Brannon Spector (15 catches, 195 yards, two TDs) and tight end Jake Briningstool (25 grabs, 285 yards, four TDs) will all be looked at to contribute for Klubnik to succeed. Robert Gunn III hit his lone extra point attempt while not attempting a field goal last season. He’s expected to take over the kicking duties for the Tigers now that B.T. Potter has graduated and Liam Boyd transferred to North Carolina after spring practice.

Blue Devils Seek to Build On Elko’s First Season

Duke finished 9-4 in 2022 for their first winning season since 2018 and improved by six wins in Mike Elko’s first season running the program. The Blue Devils hope to take another step forward in year two under Elko and they bring back quite a few starters in an effort to make that a reality. Duke has eight starters coming back on offense, including all their skill position pieces. If the offensive line steps up, the Blue Devils could be tough to stop on that side of the ball. Defensively, Duke has seven starters back and their lone weak spot looks to be shored up by experienced transfers Myles Jones (Texas A&M) and Al Blades Jr. (Miami) at the corner spots. Tyler Santucci takes over the defensive coordinator position after Robb Smith, who left after one season. Duke was second in the FBS in turnover differential last season at +16 so it will bear watching if Santucci’s scheme will produce similar results.

Last season, the Blue Devils were 67th in the nation in passing offense with 231.5 yards per game and 39th in rushing offense as they ground out 184.2 yards per contest. Duke was 31st in the FBS in scoring offense as they put up an average of 32.8 points per contest while the team finished 31st in scoring defense by allowing 22.1 points per game. Riley Leonard was 250 of 391 for 2,967 yards with 20 touchdowns and six interceptions with a team-leading 699 yards plus 13 scores on the ground. Henry Belin IV hit five of six passes for 43 yards in limited action and is back as the #2 quarterback. Jordan Waters (123 carries, 566 yards, eight TDs), Jaquez Moore (79 carries, 508 yards, five TDs) and Jaylen Coleman (102 carries, 480 yards, four TDs) all had success in the ground game last season: are each back this year. Jalon Calhoun led the team with 62 receptions for 873 yards and four scores this season. Jordan Moore (60 catches, 656 yards, five TDs), Eli Pancol (21 grabs, 335 yards), Sahmir Hagans (31 receptions, 400 yards, five TDs) and tight end Nicky Dalmolin (21 catches, 170 yards, four TDs) gives Leonard his top five pass-catching options from a season ago. Charlie Ham hit all 29 extra point attempts and nine of 15 field goal tries with a long of 45 last year. Todd Pellino hit all 23 extra point attempts and eight of nine field goals with a long of 48 last season. They are battling for the kicking duties.

Pancol (lower-body injury) had surgery in mid-August and is expected to miss a significant chunk of time.
