The UCLA Bruins and San Diego State Aztecs will play this Saturday at Snapdragon Stadium. Kickoff from San Diego is at 4:30 p.m. PT.

UCLA beat Coastal Carolina in week one. It has an over/under win total of 8.5 wins in the 2023 season. The Bruins were picked to finish sixth overall in the Pac-12 preseason poll. They went 9-4 last season with a 3-1 record on the road.

San Diego State beat Ohio and Idaho State in its first two games of the season. It has an over/under win total of 7.5 wins this season. The Aztecs were predicted to finish fourth, with one first-place vote, in the Mountain West Conference's preseason poll. They were 7-6 overall, including 5-2 at home, in 2022.

The Bruins are 14.5-point spread favorites and the game total is 50 points.

Is freshman Dante Moore the clear-cut starter at QB?

UCLA got its first win last weekend, as true freshman quarterback Dante Moore threw two touchdowns in his first collegiate game. The five-star recruit from Detroit completed 7 of 12 passes for 143 yards with an interception and Cal transfer J. Michael Sturdivant hauled in five passes for 136 yards, including a 62-yard score in the fourth quarter.

“Truthfully I wasn't feeling pressure. I was more excited than nervous,” Moore said. “There was more confidence when I took the field. When I saw the fans cheering, I appreciated that and knew I had to put the ball in the end zone.”

The Bruins defense wasn't up to par in the first half but responded after halftime, forcing three turnovers on Coastal Carolina's final six drives. Two of those turnovers were interceptions of Chanticleers quarterback Grayson McCall, who had thrown eight picks in 35 career starts, including just two last season. UCLA also held Coastal to 56 rushing yards on 34 attempts (1.6 yards per carry).

Head coach Chip Kelly's Bruins were one of the most explosive offensive teams in the nation in 2022, ranking fourth overall in total offense and eighth in scoring. With the number one rushing attack in the Pac-12 and a veteran at QB, UCLA finished seventh nationally in third down conversions, too. However, with signal-caller Dorian Thompson-Robinson off to the NFL, the Bruins must reload on the fly in a competitive conference.

Moore and redshirt junior Ethan Garbers split the snaps at quarterback in week one, with the latter completing 10 of his 17 passes for 121 yards, one TD, and two picks. Garbers has a clear advantage in experience, while Moore has a higher upside. Kelly would not name a starting QB for week two vs. SDSU.

“It’s just who moves the team the best,” Kelly said, “and who gives us the best opportunity to win.”

Moore appears best suited for the job after leading the Bruins on five drives that resulted in 20 points, but his inexperience brings risk, too. Garbers and Kent State transfer Collin Schlee are expected to play in the coming weeks, but time will tell if that's necessary.

UCLA lost RB Zach Charbonnet and its top three pass-catchers, but there's still a lot of talent on the offensive side. Transfers Kyle Ford (USC) and Moliki Matavao (Oregon) will have significant roles in 2023, along with seniors Kam Brown (15.1 YPC) and Logan Loya (15.8 YPC). The ground game will run behind an offensive line mixed with experienced veterans and transfers, including Jake Wiley (Colorado), Spencer Holstege (Purdue), and Khadere Kounta (Old Dominion). Sophomore RB TJ Harden (11 carries for 76 yards vs. CCU) and Ball State transfer RB Carson Steele (1,556 rush yards and 14 TDs in 2022) are the Bruins' primary ball carriers.

The UCLA defense needs to show improvement across the board after allowing 32 points in five of the last seven games in 2022. The linebacking unit is the best defensive group, as Darius Muasau (91 tackles and two INTs), Cal transfer Oluwafemi Oladejo (69 tackles), and Laiatu Latu (three sacks in week one) form a terrifying trio for opposing offenses.

The defensive line features several explosive pass rushers and big bodies, including Gabriel Murphy (7.5 sacks in 2021) and nose tackle Jay Toia (30 tackles). The secondary should be solid, as long as injuries don't force younger players into the game before they're ready. Junior Devin Kirkwood (27 tackles and four PBUs) and sophomore Jaylin Davies (32 tackles and three INTs) hold down the corner spots, and Bowling Green transfer Jordan Anderson (57 tackles and three picks) is a budding star at safety. Getting off the field on third down and coming up with more takeaways will be crucial to the Bruins' success this year.

The Aztecs love to run the football

San Diego State won a hard-fought game over Ohio in Week 0, as the Aztecs forced three Bobcats turnovers. Senior QB Jalen Mayden threw two touchdown passes to senior tight end Mark Redman, and junior running back Jaylon Armstead led the rushing attack with 78 yards on eight carries.

SDSU kept its momentum last weekend, as Mayden ran for two touchdowns, including a 56-yard score with 6:50 remaining to pull away from Idaho State. The Aztecs rushed for over 300 yards, as Armstead and sophomore RB Martin Blake each ran for 50-plus yards and a touchdown.

San Diego State averages 350.5 total yards (88th) and 28.0 points per game (73rd), including 125.5 passing yards (120th) and 225.0 rushing yards (27th). Head coach Brady Hoke turned the offense over to Ryan Lindley this offseason to help with the passing game. Mayden's top receiving threats, Jesse Matthews and Tyrell Shavers, are gone, but sophomore WR Mekhi Shaw (29 receptions for 349 yards and three scores) and Western Carolina transfer Raphael Williams can fill in the void. The offensive line returns three starters, and there's more size and depth up front than in previous seasons.

The Aztecs' defense surrenders 361.5 total yards (83rd) and 20.5 points per game (70th), including 293.5 passing yards (109th) and 68.0 rushing yards (26th). SDSU was 16th nationally in total defense last season and hasn't finished worse than 21st nationally in that statistic since 2013. However, there was a ton of roster turnover, especially on the defensive line and at linebacker.

San Diego State looks small inside, but seniors Garret Fountain (41 tackles and three sacks) and Wyatt Draeger (five tackles in 2023) should be playmakers. Senior LB Cooper McDonald (41 tackles and two sacks) returns at outside backer, and junior Cody Moon (104 tackles and 4.5 sacks) transferred in from New Mexico to man the other outside LB position.

As for the secondary, safety Patrick McMorris (61 tackles and five PBUs) transferred to Cal, but there is plenty to like about the Aztecs' defensive backfield. Junior Dez Malone (43 tackles and three picks) is a name to watch at cornerback, and Cedarious Barfield (two picks this season) is a playmaking safety.

Full-Game Side Bet

Insiders Status:

Rating:

The Aztecs will test the Bruins' run defense all game, and I believe they're talented enough to pop off a few big runs at home. They haven't been a threat to throw the ball in 2023, but with the right game plan, they can use that to their advantage. It's going to come down to whether SDSU can establish a ground game early. If it can't, the Bruins will control the time of possession and put stress on the Aztecs' secondary to hold up play after play. That's problematic against a Chip Kelly offense, no matter who is at QB.

Another reason I like the Aztecs to cover is the revolving door UCLA has at quarterback. Garbers got the quick hook for his poor play in week one, and Moore looked great against Coastal Carolina, but how will he fare in San Diego against a team that consistently ranks as one of the nation's best in total defense? If he makes a mistake, he could get pulled too, making it difficult for any Bruins quarterback to get comfortable and in a rhythm. UCLA lost current NFL RB Charbonnet and its top three pass-catchers from last year's squad ‚Äî will their replacements produce consistently week after week?

This is an early test for an inexperienced Bruins team and already the third game of the season for the Aztecs. I believe they'll be comfortable and confident enough to at least cover the spread on Saturday afternoon in America's Finest City.

Prediction: San Diego State +14.5

Full-Game Total Pick

Insiders Status:

Rating:

San Diego State could struggle to score if it can't run the ball as effectively as it wishes, as it lacks offensive balance. While I doubt the Azetcs will be held to fewer than 13 points, it would take a special set of circumstances for them to score 24 or more points on Saturday. The Bruins held Coastal Carolina to a meager 1.6 yards per carry last week and won't hesitate to stack the box if Mayden is unable to consistently move the ball down the field through the air. Like UCLA, SDSU lost its top receiving threats in 2022. How will new Aztecs OC Lindley manage the offense? There are too many question marks about its offense to expect SDSU to make a large contribution to the total.

The Bruins, too, have question marks. Moore is a true freshman with less than a full game of collegiate experience, and Kelly hasn't committed to any of his QBs as a long-term starter. With multiple signal-callers potentially shuffling in and out of the game, I'm less confident that the Bruins will move the ball enough to score without multiple big plays. As long as the Aztecs can prevent the runs and passes that allow the Bruins to take off in the open field, I'm confident they can turn this into a slower-paced game, covering the spread and keeping the total under 50 points scored.

Prediction: Under 50
