Louisiana Ragin’ Cajuns (1-0) vs. Old Dominion Monarchs (0-1) 

We’re heading into Week 2 of the 2023 CFB season. Saturday brings the Sun Belt Conference showdown from S.B. Ballard Stadium in Norfolk, Virginia, so here’s the best Louisiana vs. Old Dominion betting pick along with the latest odds update. 

These two schools have never met before, and Louisiana opened as an 8.5-point road fave with a total of 48.0 points. At the time of writing, the Ragin’ Cajuns were 5.5-point favorites with a total of 51.5 points, so let’s take a closer look.   

Louisiana failed to cover in Week 1

The Louisiana Rajin’ Cajuns defeated the Northwestern State Demons 38-13 in their season opener but failed to beat a 35-point line in front of the home fans. Louisiana outgained its opponents 429-187 in total yards while holding the Demons to just eight 1st downs. However, the Ragin’ Cajuns lost the turnover battle 3-1, and senior QB Ben Wooldridge completed just 14 of his 32 passing attempts while throwing for 223 yards, three touchdowns, and an interception. 

"Too many drops and missed throws — just not exactly perfect,” Louisiana head coach Michael Desormeaux said. “The quarterback needs to be more accurate, and we have to make the play on some of these tough catches. We have to be more efficient in the pass game." 

The Ragin’ Cajuns dominated FCS Northwestern State on the ground, racking up 206 yards on 34 carries. Sophomore RB Dre’lyn Washington ran eight times for 88 yards and a TD, senior RB Jacob Kibodi carried the pigskin nine times for 49 yards, and Ben Wooldridge added six totes for 29 yards and a TD. On the other side of the ball, Louisiana held the Demons to 77 yards on 40 carries while posting five sacks and eight tackles for loss.

ODU is on a seven-game slide

The Old Dominion Monarchs kicked off their 2023 campaign with a 36-17 defeat at the Virginia Tech Hokies. The Monarchs trailed by only six points at halftime but fell apart in the second, failing to cover a 16.5-point spread. They lost the turnover battle 3-0 and couldn’t cope with the Hokies’ passing game, allowing Grant Wells to throw for 251 yards and three touchdowns on just 17 completions. The Monarchs extended their losing streak to seven games dating back to the 2022 season. 

“When we do our job, it’s a great football game,” ODU coach Ricky Rahne said. “When we don’t do our job, they beat us. That’s the story of the game. I don’t want to minimize it. They did their job more often than we did. I don’t think we gave them anything. They did their job, and we didn’t. That’s how you play winning football.” 

Junior signal-caller Grant Wilson, a transfer from Fordham, went 13-of-25 for 94 yards, two touchdowns, and an interception in Week 1. He also had 19 carries for 81 yards, while junior running back Keshawn Wicks added 14 totes for 73 yards. The Monarchs’ defense didn’t record a single sack and will have to improve a lot in future days. At least, ODU held Virginia Tech to 109 rushing yards on 43 attempts.

Full-Game Side Bet

Rating:

I’m not high on Old Dominion at all. It seems like the Monarchs will need some time to gel on the offensive side of the ball. The Ragin’ Cajuns showed a lot of room for improvement, too. Their defense played well in Week 1, but the clash against Northwestern State is not a proper benchmark. 

We should see a tight battle, so I’ll take the underdogs and points. The Monarchs’ run D was a rare bright spot last weekend, and ODU will have to slow down Louisiana’s ground game in order to keep it close or even pull off an upset. 

The Ragin’ Cajuns are 2-5 ATS in their last seven games overall and 2-6 ATS in their previous eight outings on the road as favorites. The Monarchs, who have dropped seven straight contests, are 2-5 ATS in those seven games and 3-3 ATS in their last six showings at home as underdogs. 

Prediction: Old Dominion Monarchs +5.5 (-110)
