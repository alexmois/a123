The Kent State Golden Flashes (0-1, 0-0 MAC) will be trying to avoid losing consecutive games when they face the Arkansas Razorbacks (1-0, 0-0 SEC) on Saturday afternoon. The game will be played at Razorback Stadium and it is scheduled to begin at 4 PM. ET.

The Golden Flashes are coming off a 56-6 loss to UCF as 35.5-point underdogs. The Razorbacks are coming off a 56-13 win over Western Carolina as 38-point favorites.

Golden Flashes Looking For First Win

The Golden Flashes were run out of town in their first game after a 50-point loss to UCF. They will try to bounce back from the loss with a win over the Razorbacks, which will give them their first win of the season. New head coach has a very tough job with just four returning starters on defense and no returning starters on offense, so this is basically a rebuild.

Kent State scored six points in its first game. They threw for 145 yards and rushed for 95 yards in the loss.

Michael Alaimo competed 39 percent of his passes for 145 yards and one interception in the loss. He also had seven carries for 23 yards. Gavin Garcia had 18 carries for 45 yards, while Chrishon McCray had three catches for 61 yards.

Kent State didn’t play well defensively in their first game, giving up 56 points in the loss. They gave up over 700 yards in the loss and will have to do a better job defending the pass and run if they want to win this game.

Razorbacks Going For Second Consecutive Win

The Razorbacks got their season off to a good start after a 48-point beat down of Western Carolina. They will try to keep the momentum going with a win over the Golden Flashes, which will give them their second win in a row.

Arkansas scored 56 points in its first game. They threw for 274 yards and rushed for 105 yards in the win.

KJ Jefferson completed 78 percent of his passes for 246 yards and three touchdowns in the win. He also added 11 rushing yards and one touchdown. Raheim Sanders had 15 carries for 42 yards and two touchdowns, while Jaedon Wilson had three carries for 83 yards and one touchdown. Sanders will miss this game with a knee injury.

Arkansas played well defensively in its first game, giving up 13 points in the win. They did a great job stopping the run and also played well against the pass. They will need a similar effort if they want to get the win.

Malik Chavis (Hand), Beaux Limmer (Hamstring), Raheim Sanders (Leg), Terry Wells (Pectoral), Dallas Young (Undisclosed), and Quincy Rhodes Jr. (Undisclosed) are questionable for this game.

They were also careless with the ball, turning it over twice in the game. Go with Arkansas to cover the spread.
