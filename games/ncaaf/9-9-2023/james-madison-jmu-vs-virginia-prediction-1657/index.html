The James Madison Dukes and Virginia Cavaliers will play on Saturday at Scott Stadium. Kickoff is scheduled for 12:00 p.m. ET.

James Madison defeated Bucknell 38-3 last week at home in its season opener. JMU has an over/under win total of 8.5 wins this season. It was voted the preseason favorite to win the Sun Belt Conference. The Dukes were 8-3 overall last year, including 3-2 in road games, in their first season at the FBS level.

Virginia lost 49-13 to Tennessee in Nashville last Saturday. UVA has an over/under win total of 3.5 wins in 2023. It was picked to finish last in the ACC preseason poll. The Cavaliers were 3-7 overall and 2-4 at home last season.

The Dukes are six-point spread favorites and the game total is 40.5 points.

What can James Madison do in its FBS encore?

James Madison took care of business in its season opener against Bucknell, running all over the Bison en route to a 35-point victory. The Dukes accumulated 436 total yards, including 261 rushing yards on 41 carries (6.4 YPC). Sophomore Kaelon Black paced JMU with 125 rush yards on 12 carries, and three other Dukes scored rushing rushing touchdowns. Senior quarterback Jordan McCloud added two passing touchdowns, and senior receiver Reggie Brown led the pass-catchers with three receptions for 77 yards and a score.

The James Madison defense held Bucknell to 62 rushing yards on 34 carries (1.8 YPC) and surrendered less than 150 yards through the air. It was a special defensive performance, as the Bison held the ball for four minutes longer than the Dukes, who didn't give up a point after halftime.

The JMU offensive line will be among the best in the Sun Belt, which should help the Dukes control time of possession, which it did well last season. The Dukes averaged only 4.3 yards per carry in 2022, and while I don't want to overreact to their week one showing against an FCS team, it seems they have improved in that department over the offseason. Black gashed the Bison for a 57-yard run, and five Dukes averaged more than five yards per carry last week.

The Arizona transfer McCloud managed a good game against Bucknell in relief of redshirt freshman Alonza Barnett III, who was pulled after starting the game 3-for-11 with 15 passing yards and an interception. Needless to say, the frosh is now in the doghouse.

“Was I surprised at the way the quarterback came out after the camp he had? Yeah,” head coach Curt Cignetti said. “I think it got to the point like a golfer who’s afraid to swing the club.”

He was impressed with McCloud's play in relief, though, adding that “he made some plays.” The senior, who will get the start vs. Virginia, has a nice mix of returning talent and transfer receivers at his disposal. Brown, Omarion Dollison (USF), Elijah Surratt (St. Francis), and Phoenix Sporles (North Dakota State) are all experienced upper-classmen.

The Dukes ranked 23rd in total defense last season and got off to a great start in week one. They may not have elite size, but they have good depth on the defensive line. Junior DT James Carpenter is a standout player (37 tackles and 5.5 sacks in 2022), along with Jammer Kromah (3.5 sacks) and Abi Nwabyiju-Okanjji. Linebackers Taurus Jones (team-leading 81 tackles) and Jailin Walker (59 tackles) are back, and CB Chauncey Logan (ten passes defended) is the star of the defensive backfield. The Bison didn’t make it farther than JMU’s 23-yard line on Saturday, forcing six punts on six drives in the second half.

Can Virginia bounce back after a week one beatdown?

Virginia was upended by Tennessee last week in its first game in 294 days. The Vols led 21-3 at halftime and poured it on in the second half, adding four more touchdowns. The Cavaliers managed only one touchdown late in the third quarter when the game was well out of hand. Tennessee rushed for 287 yards on 52 carries (5.5 YPC), scoring five times on the ground. Virginia struggled on the ground (40 carries for 95 yards) and through the air (11-of-24 for 106 yards).

The Cavs are hopeful their offense will make strides this season. They added a lot of talented players through the transfer portal, including quarterback Tony Muskett, who takes over for Brennan Armstrong, who bolted for North Carolina State. It is a homecoming for the Springfield, VA native after three seasons at Monmouth (1,997 passing yards, 17 TDs, and eight INTs in 2022). However, Muskett injured his shoulder against Tennessee and may not play in this one (listed as questionable). The receiving corps is thin after losing several playmakers, as tight end Grant Misch is the leading returning receiver (17 receptions). Junior Malachi Fields (four receptions for 63 yards vs. Tennessee) and Northwestern transfer Malik Washington will be counted on to fill the void.

The running game needs to be more productive after averaging only 3.7 yards per carry in 2022. Armstrong was the team's leading rusher with 371 yards. While Saturday's performance wasn't encouraging, there's still optimism that the backs can carry their weight this year. Clemson transfer Kobe Pace (seven carries for 39 yards in week one) and senior Perris Jones (81 carries for 365 yards in 2022) figure to get the bulk of the reps.

The Cavs' defense was suspect, to begin with, and if week one is any indication, this will be a long season in Charlottesville. No one expected UVA to bottle up UT last week, but its second-half unraveling was discouraging. The Cavs were great at getting into the backfield last season but had only one tackle-f0r-loss in week one. Edge Chico Bennett (seven sacks) and DT Aaron Faumui (nine TFL) will need to be stars, as the secondary is in shambles.

Fentrell Cypress (Florida State) and Anthony Johnson (NFL) both departed in the offseason, taking with them 25 of the unit's passes defended. Malcolm Greene (Clemson), Tayvonn Kyle (Iowa State), and a few inexperienced backups will be asked to replicate that performance at cornerback. The safeties, however, are solid enough to make up for it.

Full-Game Total Pick

Insiders Status:

Rating:

I don't anticipate a ton of offensive production when these teams face off on Saturday afternoon. The Dukes are going to look to demonstrate they can run the ball early in an attempt to control the clock. Later in the game, this could open up the play-action attack. This is a well-coached team with a chip on its shoulder. They'll be eager to prove they belong on the same field as the Cavs, but they're not going to overextend their reach by going big with a fast-paced, aerial attack.

James Madison can also play some defense. While their week one performance against FCS Bucknell doesn't carry much weight, they were a good unit last year in their first FBS season and return a lot of the production from the 2022 defense. The Cavs will need to run the ball effectively to move the ball consistently, something they failed to do last year and were unable to change last week. They'll have more success on the ground than they did against the Volunteers, but I won't predict too many big runs from UVA on Saturday.

Expect both of these offenses to move the football methodically, bleeding the clock with each drive. The team that wins the turnover and field position battles will come out on top, but the real winners will be the bettors who wager on the under!
