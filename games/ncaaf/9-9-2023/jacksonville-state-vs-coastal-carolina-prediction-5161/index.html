Jacksonville State (2-0 SU, 2-0 ATS) faces Coastal Carolina (0-1 SU, 1-0 ATS) on Saturday from Brooks Stadium on Saturday night at 7pmET.

This is a transition year for the Gamecocks from FCS, who edged UTEP 17-14 in their opener on Aug. 26 in a C-USA matchup and last week, crushed FCS East Tennessee State 49-3. The Chants fell at UCLA 27-13 in their first game of the season, but did cover the 15.5.

Off to a strong start

Jacksonville State played in the Atlantic Sun last season, facing mostly FCS teams and went 9-2 overall. The only FBS team that they played was Tulsa on the road and they were blasted 54-17. Opening with UTEP was probably a solid team to start your FBS career considering the fact that they were a Conference USA team and a subpar one at that. Rich Rodriguez and the Gamecocks went back to playing an FCS squad last week when they met East Tennessee State. Jax State blew their doors off 49-3 and the Buccaneers scored the first three points.

The Gamecocks' starting quarterback against UTEP was Zion Webb, who is in his seventh season. In the first game, he completed 10 of 20 for 67 yards and rushed for 55 yards. However, Logan Smothers, a Nebraska transfer, got the start against ETSU. Smothers recorded 127 yards passing on 13-of-17 with one TD and ran for 40 yards and two TDs. Webb was a late scratch due to illness.

That was an interesting situation. So thank god we had a guy with a little bit of college experience.”

So if Webb can't go due to sickness or Smothers looks better in practice, RichRod can turn to the Nebraska transfers. Smothers is a former four-star recruit who played sparingly in Lincoln in two seasons. He threw for 413 yards and ran for 161 yards. Fortunately, the Gamecocks have a strong runner in Malik Jackson, who rushed for 176 yards in two games with two TDs this season. In the last two seasons at UL Monroe, he rushed for 958 yards and nine TDs.

So far, it appears that Jacksonville State's defense and running game will give them a shot to win most games.  In a small sample size, they've allowed just 284 yards per game this season and 162.5 passing yards. Offensively, they are throwing for just 97 yards per game.

Poor fourth quarter costs Coastal Carolina an upset big

Coastal Carolina was down by just one at 14-13 going into the fourth quarter against UCLA. Unfortunately for the Chants, they allowed 13 unanswered points including a 62-yard TD pass from Dante Moore to J. Michael Sturdivant to fall 27-13. It was a rough game for veteran QB Grayson McCall, who threw two picks for the first time in a game in his career. McCall has had just eight interceptions in 35 games in his first four seasons and tossed just two last season.

The three-time Sun Belt Conference Player of the Year, completed 27 of 42 for 271 yards with one TD and two picks. Coastal Carolina was not able to run the ball against the Bruins, as they recorded just 56 on 34 carries. Last season, they ran the football adequately for a team that finished at a disappointing 8-5 and 6-2 in the Sun Belt. They rushed for just 159.2 yards per game and 4.0 yards per carry, while tossing for 246.2 passing yards.  McCall passed for 2,700 yards with 24 TDs, two INTs and was sacked just 19 times.

The top rusher from last season was C.J. Beasley, who rushed for 717 yards with five TDs and 5.1 yards per carry. He's back though he had just four carries for 14 yards and one catch for 18 yards in the opener. The Chants will go as far as their defense takes them. Last season, they ranked 102nd in overall defense and last season, allowed 31.9 points per game, 133.8 on the ground, and 284.5 in the air.  Against UCLA, they gave up 417 yards, but also forced three interceptions from Moore and Ethan Garbers.

Best Bets for this Game

Full-Game Side Bet

Insiders Status:

Rating:

If you are a transition football team going to FBS, there's no better coach to have than Rich Rodriguez. RichRod was the head coach at West Virginia, Michigan and Arizona from 2001-17. He's seen it all and will have his team prepared for a good Coastal Carolina team that still has issues against the run and pass. RichRod loves to run out of an uptempo spread offense and needs a running quarterback to execute his plan. Whether it's Webb or Smothers, he appears to have his guy. He also has added numerous FBS transfers to make the transition smoother. McCall will put up decent numbers, but the Gamecocks will be prepared. Take the points.

Prediction: Jacksonville State +13.5

The Gamecocks would prefer a 17-14 game, which was the case in their opener.
