Bounce-Back Game

New Mexico State rebounded from an opening game loss to UMass at home to take down Western Illinois at home, 58-21. The Aggies struggled through the first half before bouncing back for 38 points in the second half to knot their record at 1-1 as they get set to enter C-USA play. The Aggies had 438 yards of total offense in the second half alone in the game.

"I'm proud of the youngsters coming off of a tough loss last week. They worked very hard. Sometimes you can come out a bit stagnant but I compliment our kids for being ready to play. I thought we played hard and learned a lot from the field, good and bad. It's always good to learn with a win. I compliment Western Illinois with how they played. I've coached against (Western Illinois head coach) Don (Patterson) for a long time. Don came out with a few wrinkles offensively and caught us off guard. Don's a very good football coach and a very good man. I compliment his team and they have a bright future in the Missouri Valley Conference." Head coach Jerry Kill said after the game. 

Up just 20-14 at the half, the Aggies came to life in the second half outscoring Western Illinois 38-7. The Aggies totaled 650 yards of offense led by quarterback Diego Pavia. Pavia went 16-of-20 for 317 yards and two touchdowns. Wide receiver Jonathan Brady had 102 yards receiving and a total of 150 all-purpose yards. The Aggies run game was effective as well, running for 270 yards as a team. Star Thomas led the way with 96 yards and two touchdowns.

On the defensive end, the Aggies allowed 280 yards of total offense including just 32 yards rushing. The team also registered 11 tackles for loss in the win. J.J. Dervil and Keyshaun Elliott led the way with seven tackles apiece.

Flame On

The Liberty Flames got their season off on the right foot with a 34-24 victory over Bowling Green at home. New head coach Jamey Chadwell earned his first win for the Flames after surviving a furious Bowling Green comeback in the second half. Liberty led 24-0 in the first half before Bowling Green closed the gap to 24-17 by the start of the third quarter. The Flames regrouped and outscored Bowling Green 10-7 the rest of the way for the win.

“I was very proud of our response in the second half. We came out and were up 24-0, and things were going well. We had some special teams miscues, let them get back in the game. They drive the field there in the third quarter. We were able to hold them out to a field goal. It goes back and forth. The biggest question mark I think our staff had is how we would respond to some adversity when it got tight. I’m very proud of that.” Chadwell said after the game.

Sophomore quarterback Kaidon Salter finished the game 11-of-20 for 143 yards and two touchdowns. Salter also led the Flames in rushing with 82 yards on 19 carries. Running back Quinton Cooley added 78 yards rushing as part of a rushing attack that finished with 246 yards. Overall, Liberty had 389 yards of total offense.

Defensively, the Flames had five interceptions in the game. The five interceptions was the most by a Liberty defense since 1995. Tyren Dupree led the Flames with 10 tackles. The team had seven tackles for loss in the game including two sacks.
