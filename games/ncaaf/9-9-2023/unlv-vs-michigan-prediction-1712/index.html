#331 UNLV vs.
#332 Michigan
Saturday, September 9, 2023 at 3:30pm EDT
Written by Eric P.

The Michigan Wolverines play host to the UNLV Rebels on Saturday afternoon in a non-conference tilt. UNLV was able to grab a win last week, though with that one coming against an FCS foe, it'll be interesting to take on their first FBS opponent. For Michigan, they didn't waste any time continuing to look like a potential College Football Playoff favorite in their opening win. When these two last met back in 2015, Michigan won 28-7 and while both rosters look quite a bit different this time around, the Wolverines will be heavy favorites to grab a big win again.

Rebels Heavy Underdogs

UNLV is coming off of a season that saw them improve slightly in the win column, though going on the road against Michigan will prove to be their toughest test of the season. They put up 44 points last week at home against Bryant but it's difficult to put any stock in that one, especially considering they'll have to navigate to the road for this one. They lost four straight away from home to close out the 2022 season and will have their hands full traveling halfway across the country to try to spring one of college football's biggest recent upsets. Expect the Rebels to try to find a way to slow this game down and try to grind Michigan's offense to a halt, though it will certainly be easier said than done.

One piece of good news is that the Rebels come into this one without any injuries to report on. Staying healthy and finding consistency on both ends is the best recipe towards finding success this season, though it isn't likely for that piece to alter how Saturday's game goes.

UNLV established a successful running attack last week, going for 268 yards on the ground. That sets the plan for them to attempt to control the clock in this one, even if it won't come nearly as easily as it did against last week's FCS foe. Doug Brumfield only threw for 86 yards last week and unless the passing attack gains momentum to add some balance against Michigan, they will struggle even more than expected to move the ball in this one. On the defensive end, a stout secondary that had showed success last week will have their hands full in this one, especially stacked up against a serious athletic disparity.

Wolverines Continue Without Harbaugh

In game one under Jim Harbaugh's suspension, Michigan didn't miss a beat finding fast success, as they look to replicate that success in their second game. Being at home will help, where they have dominated over the last two seasons, though playing UNLV anywhere would still lead to the expectation for the Wolverines to win big. Against ECU last week, the defense failed to allow a touchdown, which they hope to keep going across four quarters in this one. The offense has balance yet again and even without their head coach, have s capable staff to lead the way in this one. A loss in this one would be one of college football's most stunning upsets in recent memory and frankly, almost seems out of the realm of possibility.

Unlike their opponent, Michigan continues to navigate the injury report, with eight players listed as we head into the weekend. These seem to be more depth pieces, as no starters are among those eight names, which is great news. Even if the Wolverines were down a few starters, it likely wouldn't impact them on Saturday against UNLV.

After one week, Michigan's offense comes in right around the average nationally across most of the offensive categories, which makes sense given that they took their foot off the gas in the second half. JJ McCarthy looked great through the air, tossing three touchdowns, while the backfield duo of Blake Corum and Donovan Edwards combined for over 100 yards on the ground. The balance on the offensive end might be unmatched across the country, as they expect to leave the Rebels in their wake. On the defensive end, the Wolverines didn't generate a single sack, a trend that will have to change as the season progresses.
