Maryland (1-0 SU, 0-1 ATS) hosts Charlotte (1-0 SU, 0-1 ATS) from SECU Stadium in College Park on Saturday at 7:30pmET.

The Terps were able to take care of FCS Towson on Saturday 38-6, while The 49ers took care of FCS South Carolina State 24-3, but neither were able to cover as big favorites.

These teams met last season in Charlotte with Maryland winning 56-21 on the road as a 28-point favorite. Taulia Tagovailoa threw for 391 yards with four TDs and a pick to lead the Terps.

The Poggi era has begun

The Biff Poggi era began with a win by Charlotte over SC State 24-3 at home. It was an impressive defensive performance as the 49ers held the Bulldogs to just 168 total yards and eight first downs in front of a sellout crowd of 15,622 people at Jerry Richardson Stadium. Freshman running back Durrell Robinson led the way with 71 yards on eight carries with a TD. QB Jalon Jones rushed for 45 yards and a score and completed 13 of 19 for 125 yards with a TD, but also was picked off twice.

"We've been talking for a long time, telling [Athletic Director] Mike Hill 'we're pretty elite on defense' and we played like it today," said Poggi after the game on Charlotte49ers.com . "I am very pleased with how we played on defense and I would expect us to only get better as the season goes on."

Poggi is an unorthodox coach, considering his background includes a stint at St. Frances Academy (MD) and Gilman High School in Baltimore for nearly 25 years. He had a one-year stint at The Citadel in 1987 and was an Associate Head Coach at Michigan for the last two seasons before taking the Charlotte job at 64. He doesn't have a lot of college football experience, which is rare for an FBS head coach, but he's not your ordinary high school coach.

For Charlotte to have a chance in this game, they will need to slow down Maryland's passing game. Last year's defense was awful as they allowed 39.4 points per game last season. Poggi did bring in several players from his former high school in St. Frances as well as from Michigan that should help defensively.

Offensive line is still a question mark

Maryland won their first game over Towson as expected, but scored just 38 points. They could have scored more had they not had some drops by wide receivers. But they also nearly lost star quarterback Taulia Tagovailoa to constant pressure and a hard hit near the sideline. Head coach Mike Locksley took him out midway through the third quarter when the score was 35-3, but there was also no reason to allow him to keep getting hit.

“It was a little weird for me … we’re trying to figure out the best five and so we’re mixing and matching,” coach Mike Locksley said after the game. “We’re gonna continue to try to play as many guys as we can. I’d like to get settled on an [offensive line].”

This season, the Terps had to replace five offensive linemen who either went to the NFL or transferred. Maryland does have two good running backs that they can lean on led by Roman Hemby, who scored 10 TDs and had 1,287 yards from scrimmage as a freshman last season. Antwain Littleton (6-1, 231) is the big back who took it 59 yards to the house last season against the 49ers.

Defensively, Maryland struggled against the quality teams of the Big 10, allowing 25.7 points per game in conference play. They did add some key transfers including Ja'Quan Sheppard from Cincinnati. Sheppard adds size and accolades at corner as the 6-2 senior earned first-team All-AAC last season.

Full-Game Side Bet

The major concern for Maryland right now is their offensive line and that will be an issue against a Charlotte team. Charlotte had three sacks against South Carolina State led by one from Grad student Eyabi Oke-Anoma, who played at Alabama and Michigan. Poggi's roster is full of Power Five transfers, especially on the defensive side. He will be coming after Tagovailoa all game so the Terps will need to run the ball to protect their star quarterback from taking too many hits. That will make the game shorter and the possibility of a huge blowout (like last season) becomes less likely.

Full-Game Total Pick

Insiders Status:

Rating:

Poggi has already said that he has a "blue collar" and "gritty" football team. He's not expecting high-scoring games because his team is not suited to win those types of contests. Mike Locksley has a good enough defense where he knows his squad has to stop the run and they'll be ready for the 49ers' ground attack. Considering the fact that the clock doesn't stop during first downs anymore until the last 2 minutes of a half, this should be a fairly quick game. That means take the under.
