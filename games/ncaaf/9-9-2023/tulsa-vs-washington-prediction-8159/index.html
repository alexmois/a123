Storm Warning

The Golden Hurricane of Tulsa arrive in Washington with their chests puffed out after a 42-7 opening night win over Arkansas-Pine Bluff last Thursday. After allowing the opening score and falling behind 7-0, Tulsa scored the game's final 42 points including a 28-point explosion in the second quarter. The victory was a solid start for new head coach Kevin Wilson in his first game leading the Golden Hurricane.

"It was a good win. A lot of errors. Some things to clean up on. A lot of things that we're trying to be not a part of our game that we have to learn to take out," said Wilson. "But it was a good start, and proud of the work. Excited to get the win. Excited to be 1-0."

Offensively, Tulsa was led by redshirt freshman quarterback Cardell Williams. Williams completed 13-of-14 passes for 233 yards and three touchdowns off the bench. Williams was pressed into action after an injury to starter Braylon Braxton in the first quarter. Running back Jordan Ford added 110 yards rushing and wide receiver Marquis Shoulder caught two touchdown passes and had 132 yards receiving in the game.

Defensively, the Golden Hurricane were led by Ben Kopenski and Kendarin Ray. Kopenski had a team-leading 10 tackles while Kopenski had two tackles for loss that accounted for -13 yards. They held Arkansas-Pine Bluff to 252 yards of total offense in the game and had one turnover. Tulsa's defense survived three straight turnovers from the offense and surrendered just seven points.

In 2022, Tulsa was ranked 55th in the country in total offense including 42nd through the air and 83rd on the ground. Defensively, the Golden Hurricane ranked 103rd in total defense but were a solid 46th against the pass. They did struggle against the run, ranking 121st in the country. Tulsa allowed 102 rushing yards last Thursday.

Air Huskies

The Washington Huskies' offense picked up where they left off in 2022 with a 56-19 win over Boise State at home on Saturday. The Huskies opening night win also resulted in a slight jump in the Top-25 rankings from #10 to #8 as they welcome Tulsa to Husky Stadium this week.

"Came out and battled. Knew BSU would come out and do things, they are a solid program with high expectations and we knew there were some things we needed to pay special attention to. Slow start in the first quarter, but we showed our resilience. Proud of them. Used to fast starts, but it was more about the strong finishes today. It was good to see where we're at and hit some other people. Still so much to work on." Head coach Kalen DeBoer said after the win.

Quarterback Michael Penix Jr. was the story offensively, throwing for 450 yards and tying his career-high with five touchdown passes. He was 29-of-40 passing and hit four different receivers for touchdowns in the win. Penix comes off a year in which he finished with the second most passing yards in the nation last season. Wide receiver Rome Odunze led the way with seven catches for 132 yards and a touchdown. In all, the Huskies had 568 yards of total offense.

On the defensive end, Washington allowed 402 yards in total including 264 through the air. The Huskies did have two interceptions in the game and had one sack and three tackles for loss. Dominque Hampton led the way with seven tackles in the game.

In 2022, the Huskies finished second in the country in total offense. They were 87th in rushing offense while possessing the nation's #1 passing attack. The Huskies picked right up where they left off in the opener with a strong passing attack against what is a fairly formidable Boise State defense. Defensively, Washington finished 74th total defense last season. They were just 108th against the pass but a solid 28th against the run. They were able to pick off two passes on Saturday but did allow over 260 yards passing.

Full-Game Side Bet

Insiders Status:

Rating:

No, I am not picking a major upset on Saturday but I am predicting that Tulsa will be able to put up some points against a suspect Huskies' secondary. The question will likely be who will be throwing the ball for the Golden Hurricane. Before leaving the game with an injury, Braxton threw two interceptions in the team's first three drives. His replacement, Williams, came in and was nearly perfect hitting on his first 13 passes before throwing his first incompletion late in the fourth quarter. The redshirt freshman may very well get a second look as the starting quarterback this week even if Braxton is available. Tulsa should be able to move the ball through the air on Saturday and put up their share of points to keep this game respectable.

On the defensive end, Tulsa is stronger against the pass. They ranked 46th in the country last year and had an interception in the victory. They will not be able to stop the dominant Huskies offense on Saturday but they have the ability to slow them down to some degree. I expect a high-scoring affair on Saturday but not a completely one-sided game. Washington will win this game but they will not run away by more than five touchdowns.

Take Tulsa +37.5
