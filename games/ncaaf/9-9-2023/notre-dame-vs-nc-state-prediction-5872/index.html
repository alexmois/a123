Today, the Notre Dame Fighting Irish will travel to Carter-Finley Stadium in Raleigh, North Carolina, to take on the NC State Wolfpack. The Irish are off to a fast 2-0 start, while the Wolfpack come in at 1-0. These teams last met back in 2017, and Notre Dame won that game at home by a score of 35-14. Let's take a look at how this one will shape up.

Irish Off To A Fast Start

The Notre Dame Irish began their season in London with a dominant 42-3 win over Navy. Last week they came home and had another dominant performance as they crushed Tennessee State by a score of 56-3. Granted, neither opponent is close to the level of the top teams in the nation, but still, outscoring your first two opponents by a combined 98-6 count is very impressive.

Last year, the Irish had six starters back, and they averaged just 31.8 ppg, which was 42nd in the nation. Notre Dame’s issue was inconsistent play at the QB position, but they may have rectified that problem by signing Sam Hartman from Wake Forest. Hartman has five years of experience, and he threw for 12,967 yards with 110 TDs and 41 INTs in 48 games for the Demon Deacons. Hartman has played well so far, throwing for 445 yards with 6 TDs and no INTs. This will be his first big test as NC State does have a tough defense. Hartman has the luxury of having a strong running game behind him as the Irish have averaged 206.5 ypg on 6.3 yards per carry through their first two games. They may need to lean on the ground game some to loosen up that NC State defense.

The Irish allowed 23 ppg last year after giving up no more than 19.7 ppg in their previous four years. Notre Dame allowed 329 ypg (22nd in the nation), which was actually a 31 ypg improvement over 2021, but still, the points allowed were worse. Notre Dame returns eight starters, including their top four tacklers from last year, and according to Phil Steele, they have the #18 Defensive Line, #7 Linebacking Corps, and the #9 Secondary in the nation. Oh boy, this defense has a chance to be top ten in the nation. So far, the defense has looked good, allowing just 6 points on 162.5 yards per game of total offense. Can't get better than that, but they will be facing a much tougher offense in this one.

NC State Was Lackluster In Game One

The NC State Wolfpack may have been looking towards this game when they took on UConn last week. Yes, State won the game, but it was just a 24-14 final, and they failed to cover the two-TD spread in the contest. It was a lackluster performance for a team that was 8-5 last year and beat that same UConn team by a score of 41-10. The Wolfpack will not have the luxury of being lackluster in this game, as they are taking on one of the better teams in the nation.

We started with the offense for the Irish, so I will start with the Defense for NC State. The Wolfpack has just five starters back from a group that allowed 19.2 ppg last year, and the defense looked just as strong. They allowed the Huskies just 273 yards of total offense, including 113 through the air. That could be key here as they are facing Sam Hartman and a strong Notre Dame passing attack. Hartman has thrown for 923 yards with six TDs and 6 INTs in his career vs. the Wolfpack. One problem for NC State is the fact that they allowed 160 yards rushing to UConn, and the Irish have a deep and talented running back corps, along with a powerful offensive line. If the Irish get their ground game going, then Hartman will have a HUGE day.

The NC State offense was not great last year, as they averaged just 24.3 ppg on the season. Even though they have seven starters back, the offense did not look great against UConn, putting up just 24 points on 364 yards of total offense. The Wolfpack did rush for 209 yards in the game, but they will now be taking on a much tougher defensive line, so running the ball will not be all that easy. QB Brennan Armstrong came over from Virginia and he threw for just 155 yards with no TDs and no INTs against the Huskies. He will have to be far better in this one, but the Irish do have one of the best secondaries in the nation. Armstrong hopes the ground game can get going or he could be in for a long afternoon.
