The Oklahoma State Cowboys (1-0, 0-1 ATS, 0-1 O/U) take on the Arizona State Sun Devils (1-0, 0-1 ATS, 0-1 O/U) in an inter-conference bout on Saturday night in the desert. The Cowboys easily defeated Central Arkansas in their home opener by a 30-7 score. The Sun Devils avoided the upset with a narrow 24-21 home victory against Southern Utah in their season opener. 

Oklahoma State Does As Expected in Opener

The Oklahoma State Cowboys are a hungry team after a disappointing conclusion to last year. They were in contention atop the BIG 12 but struggled late in the year and lost to Wisconsin in the bowl game. Oklahoma State has a difficult challenge in a deep conference this year. They were not able to cover in the opener, beating Central Arkansas 27-13 in a bout that had them favored by 27 points.

Oklahoma State continues to have a QB battle ongoing after Spencer Sanders graduated. Coach Mike Gundy gave three QBs playing time in the opener. Garret Rangel was the backup last season and posted a 4:5 TD-to-INT ratio. He is still considered a freshman and accrued 118 passing yards in the season opener. Gunnar Gundy also saw limited action last season and recorded 106 passing yards to begin the season. Alan Bowman spent time with Michigan last season and collected 80 passing yards last Saturday. Gundy appears to be the favorite to earn the #1 job.

The squad lost plenty of running depth. Ollie Gordon II is the leading returning RB with 308 yards last year. The sophomore RB collected 44 rushing yards last week. Brennan Presley led the squad in receiving yards in 2022 and is back for his senior year. He brought in 54 rushing yards last week. De’Zhaun Stribling arrived in a transfer from Washington State. The junior WR was key in the victory last Saturday, posting 73 receiving yards.

The Cowboys' defense was an issue last year. They ranked 106th against the pass while the rush defense was marked 101st. They squandered 391 total yards to Central Arkansas which is not a promising number considering it was an FCS squad. The unit lost its best DB from last season but has a great player in Kendal Daniels who was stellar in the opener. 

Oklahoma State averaged 30.6 points last year, ranking them 49th. They allowed an average of 28.9 points, placing them 89th.

Arizona State Avoids Upset Loss

The Arizona State Sun Devils are looking to rebound from a disastrous 2022 season that resulted in a 3-9 record. They have a new coach in Kenny Dillingham and a new OC. The season opener was not a good sign of good things to come. The Sun Devils were labeled huge 34.5-point favorites in a 24-21 home victory against Southern Utah of the FCS. 

ASU had true freshman Jaden Rashada start the opener. He recorded 236 passing yards with two touchdowns and no picks. ASU has three QBs who could all see action at some point including Trenton Bourguet who saw plenty of playing time last year. Notre Dame transfer Drew Pyne likely would have been the starting QB but is injured (hamstring) and remains questionable

ASU lost their rushing leader from 2022 but brought in a skilled Cameron Skattebo from Sacramento State of the FCS. He was decent last week, rushing for 71 yards on 4.2 yards per carry. The Sun Devils have a strong receiving core led by Elijah Badger. The junior led the squad with 866 yards last year but only managed 19 yards in the opener.

The Sun Devils defense was a weakness last season. ASU was placed 91st against the pass and 119th against the run last year. The unit lost its tackling leader and one of its sack leaders. They do return a good portion of last year’s secondary. The good news is they held Southern Utah to only 226 total yards last week.

Arizona State scored an average of 26.1 points last year, marking them 79th. The defense squandered an average of 31.4 points, pegging them 107th in the FBS.
