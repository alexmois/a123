We've got a really good week two matchup in Idaho as the Boise State Broncos host the University of Central Florida Knights. Boise State of the Mountain West Conference lost their opener to No. 10 Washington, while UCF of the Big 12 is 1-0 after blowing out Kent State. These two teams met in 2021 and UCF won 36-21 but Boise State covered as 5.5 point underdogs. Kickoff is Saturday night at 7:00 p.m. ET at Albertsons Stadium in Boise, Idaho.

UCF joins the Big 12

The UCF Knights have officially hit the big time. They have been one of the best programs in the country over the past 10 years but never got the recognition of playing in the American Athletic Conference. Not that the AAC is a slouch, far from it, but it's not a Power 5 Conference. Well, the UCF have finally arrived as they joined the Big 12 this past offseason and last Saturday they celebrated their first game as a member of the Big 12 with a 56-6 blowout of Kent State. They easily covered as 35.5-point favorites.

“We know that we’ve done good and bad in this game,” UCF quarterback John Rhys Plumlee said, reported the Associated Press. “We showed a little how explosive we can be and how good we can be on both sides of the ball. A win like this, it propels you. At the end of the day to be great, we know as a team it takes one day at a time.”

Plumlee exemplified the bad and good as he threw for 281 yards and 3 touchdowns, and ran for 90 yards and 1 touchdown. But on the bad side, he threw 2 picks. Xavier Townsend caught 5 passes for 81 yards and a touchdown while Johnny Richardson rushed for 100 yards.

Boise State falls to Washington

The Boise State Broncos bit off more than they could chew last Saturday by scheduling the 10th-ranked Washington Huskies as their opponent in week one. The Huskies came in as one of the best teams in the country and they did not disappoint as they beat Boise State 56-19 as 14.5-point favorites. Huskies quarterback Michael Penix threw for 450 yards and 5 touchdowns. The Huskies outgained the Broncos 568 to 402. Boise State jumped out to a 6-0 lead but then Washington scored 28 points in the second period and the rout was on.

What a dime from Taylen Green to Stef Cobbs. 40-yard completion. Boise State near the red zone pic.twitter.com/MPLg3XxE06

— Jordan Kaye (@jordankaye_23) September 2, 2023

Boise State quarterback Taylen Green struggled against Washington, going 19 for 39 for 244 yards and 1 touchdown with 2 interceptions. The fact is, Boise State actually played better than the final score looked. They gained 402 total yards and had 138 rushing yards. They also had 21 first downs to 22 for Washington. Ultimately, they were undone by turnovers, penalties, and their pass defense really struggled. Running back Ashton Jeanty had a nice game with 4 catches for 109 yards and a touchdown while rushing for 44 yards and a touchdown. Receiver Eric McAlister caught 7 passes for 69 yards while Stefan Cobb had 6 grabs for 56 yards.

Full-Game Side Bet

Rating:

The UCF Knights had a pretty good debut as a member of the Big 12, but they face a much tougher test facing Boise State. The thing is, there were some concerns with UCF last week in their blowout win over Kent State. Plumlee threw two interceptions against a cupcake opponent and lost a fumble. Three turnovers won't play against Boise State on the road. On the other hand, Boise State played a lot better than the score indicated last week against powerhouse Washington. They moved the ball very well against the Huskies, particularly in the air, so I think they'll score enough to cover and potentially win this outright. Take Boise State getting the points.

Prediction: Boise State +3.5

Full-Game Total Pick

Insiders Status:

Rating:

If you like offense, you will like this game. I see a high-scoring game that could be decided by a field goal and could come down to the final possession. It is hard to get a read on UCF's defense since they played a lesser opponent last week, but I'm trusting the Boise State offense to match UCF TD for TD. Take the over in this one.
