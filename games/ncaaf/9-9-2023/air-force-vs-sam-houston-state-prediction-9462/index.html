#339 Air Force vs.

The Sam Houston Bearkats (0-1, 0-0 C-USA) will be trying to pick up their first home win of the season when they face the Air Force Falcons (1-0, 0-0 MW) on Saturday night. The game will be played at NRG Stadium and it is scheduled to begin at 8 PM. ET.

The Bearkats are coming off a 14-0 loss to BYU as a 19.5-point underdog. The Falcons are coming off a 42-7 win over Robert Morris as 47.5-point favorites.

Falcons Going For Second Consecutive Win

The Falcons got their season off to a good start after a dominant win in their first game. They will try to keep the momentum going with a win over the Bearkats, which will give them their second win in a row and first road win of the season.

Air Force scored 42 points in its first game. They threw for 95 yards and rushed for 374 yards in the win.

Zac Larrier completed 67 percent of his passes for 95 yards and one touchdown. Zac Larrier had 14 carries for 99 yards and two touchdowns, while Jared Roznos had two catches for 95 yards and one touchdown.

Air Force played well defensively in its first game giving up seven points in the win over FCS Robert Morris. They did a great job defending the pass and run and will have to keep it up if they want to get the win.

Dane Kinamon (Undisclosed) is questionable for this game.

Bearkats Looking For First Win

The Bearkats faced a tough test in their first game of the season and couldn’t pull off the win. They will try to bounce back from the loss with a win over the Falcons, which will give them their first win of the season.

Sam Houston didn’t score a point in its first-ever FBS game against BYU. They threw for 147 yards and rushed for 38 yards in the loss.

Keegan Shoemaker completed 55 percent of his passes for 147 yards and three interceptions. John Gentry had eight carries for 19 yards, while Noah Smith had three catches for 44 yards.

Sam Houston played well defensively in its first game, giving up 14 points in the loss. They will need a similar effort if they want to win this game.

Qua'Vez Humphreys (Foot) is out indefinitely.
