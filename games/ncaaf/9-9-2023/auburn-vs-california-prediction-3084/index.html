Auburn Tigers (1-0) vs. California Golden Bears (1-0) 

The Auburn Tigers head to California Memorial Stadium in Berkeley on Saturday night to face off against the California Golden Bears in Week 2 of the 2023 college football season. The kick-off is set for 10:30 PM ET, so here’s the best Auburn vs. California betting pick. 

The Tigers and Golden Bears lock horns for the first time ever. At the moment, Auburn is a 6.5-point road fave, while the totals sit at 54.0 points. 

Auburn cruised through Week 1

The Auburn Tigers trounced the Massachusetts Minutemen 59-14 in Week 1, covering a huge 35-point spread in front of the home fans. The Tigers outgained their opponents 492-301 in total yards while rushing 44 times for a staggering 289 yards. In Hugh Freeze’s debut as Auburn’s head coach, the Tigers showed that their ground game will be their biggest strength in 2023. 

“It was good to see our defense play hard,” Freeze said. “I thought we played harder than we have in the scrimmages and then we did in the first possession. I thought we started to show a little passion.”

Payton Thorne, a transfer from Michigan State, got the starting call under center and threw for 141 yards and a touchdown. Robby Ashford, who was the Tigers’ starting QB in 2022, was used in red-zone packages, finishing with nine carries for 51 yards and three touchdowns. 

“Obviously, you always want to be the starter,” Ashford said. “But Payton’s done a great job, and I look at him like a brother. We come out there every day and just know we’re out here to make each other better and do whatever we can to help this team win.” 

The Tigers tortured the Minutemen on the ground, finishing the game with six rushing touchdowns. On the other side of the ball, Auburn recovered a fumble and picked Massachusetts’ quarterback once. The Tigers also had four sacks and seven tackles for loss.

Cal destroyed North Texas in its season opener

The California Golden Bears put on a strong performance in Week 1. They trounced the North Texas Mean Green 58-21 as 5-point road favorites, racking up a whopping 669 total yards. The Golden Bears carried the pigskin 56 times for 347 yards while holding the ball in possession for 39:07. 

"We expected to come out and play well," California head coach Justin Wilcox said. "To say you're going to have 669 yards of offense every week is not realistic, but we have some good players and we felt like we matched up well and should have been able to move the ball on them. Spav and all of those coaches and players on that side of the ball contributed today. It was a big day for them."

Sophomore QB Ben Finley completed 24 of his 34 passing attempts against North Texas, tossing for 289 yards, a touchdown, and an interception. He came in for Sam Jackson V, who had completed 3 of 4 for 33 yards before injuring his shoulder. He's listed as questionable for this one. Finley is an NC State transfer who threw for 472 yards last season with three TDs and a pick. Jackson is a TCU transfer who played sparingly there, but has a lot of potential. 

Senior running back Isaiah Ifanse posted ten totes for 54 yards and three touchdowns, while sophomore RB Jaydn Ott went off for 178 yards and a couple of scores on 20 carries. 

On the defensive side of the ball, the Golden Bears held the Mean Green to a paltry 41 rushing yards on 27 carries. Cal had three interceptions and posted a couple of sacks to go with six tackles for loss.

Full-Game Side Bet

Insiders Status:

Rating:

This game will be a joy to watch. Both teams impressed in Week 1, especially on the offensive side of the ball, so I’m expecting to see a tight battle down the stretch. Hereof, I would take the underdogs and points. The Golden Bears shut down North Texas’ ground game in Week 1 and should have enough firepower to deal with the Tigers’ rushing. 

Justin Wilcox’s boys will be fired up to prove their worth in front of the home audience. Although Auburn looks like a well-balanced team, I highly doubt the Tigers’ chances to beat the Golden Bears comfortably.
