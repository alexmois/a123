The Texas El Paso (UTEP) Miners (1-1; 1-1-0 ATS) and Northwestern Wildcats (0-1; 0-1-0 ATS) have a showdown on Saturday. It's supposed to be a beautiful day in Evanston, Illinois when these teams collide. Don't anticipate seeing any playoff-caliber football though. Ryan Field will play host to players, coaches, and fans for this one. Kickoff is scheduled for 3:30 p.m. EDT. Let's see what's in store.

Take Three for UTEP

With two games already under their belt, UTEP picked up win number one last week. Now, it's time for the second. The Miners start with the offensive line, a talented bunch that projects to be one of Conference USA's top groups. They get a great push in the running game and hold up well in pass protection. However, last year's leading rusher is gone and quarterback Gavin Hardison is inconsistent. The receiving group has a bunch of fresh faces too, which won't help Hardison grow. The good news is that the trenches aren't a concern. The bad news is that they're one of the few schools that can't consistently take advantage of an excellent line.

A defensive unit that allowed the fewest yards among Conference USA teams has been at it again this year, allowing 291.5 through two games. That's a lower mark than their average last season. The defensive line can cause havoc, which especially helps against the run. It's elsewhere that the Miners are a bit shakier. They only caught five interceptions as a team last year. A lot of new linebackers are getting snaps this season. The defense has gotten off to a strong start but is still looking empty on playmakers.

Wildcats? More Like Purring Kitties

Northwestern has not won a football game on American soil since October 16, 2021. September of that year was the last time they took down an out-of-conference foe. They bring a 12-game losing streak into this game, one they'll desperately try to end. Getting the lead early will be the key. Northwestern had a decent line last year, and the drop-off won't be massive even with Peter Skoronski getting drafted 11th overall. The ground game is solid for the Wildcats. Their issue is that despite trailing often, throwing the ball has been a mess. There was no improvement last week. Ben Bryant came over from Cincinnati trying to fix things, but his first start looked familiar to Northwestern fans. 

The Big Ten, particularly the Big Ten West, is known for playing suffocating defense, almost to an excruciating measure. Northwestern has missed the memo for the past few years, and it still doesn't seem like it has reached them. They're loaded at linebacker, without a doubt. Everywhere else, there are question marks. The defensive line lost their top guy and is undersized as a unit. They don't get much help from the pass rush, but the secondary is weak too. There are a lot of reasons why Northwestern struggles to cover as double-digit underdogs. 

Best Bets for this Game

Full-Game Side Bet

Insiders Status:

Rating:

For most Conference USA teams, facing a Big Ten team would be death, especially in the trenches. That's not the case in this one, where the UTEP Miners should bully the Wildcats up front. The battle along the line is where most games are won or lost, and that one is an early indication that UTEP will win.

This game should be tight, and when it comes down to the final few minutes, the Miners are more trustworthy too. Passing the ball is (somehow) to UTEP's advantage. Ditto a pass defense which is better than Northwestern's, even without forcing many interceptions. UTEP notches another win, this time on the road, and beats the narrow spread.

Prediction: UTEP -1.5

Full-Game Total Pick

Insiders Status:

Rating:

Neither of these offenses should be trusted, hence a total that is under 40 points in a college football game. Well, that total won't be low enough. Northwestern is seemingly allergic to points, as they've been held to single digits in five consecutive games. That doesn't bode well against a Miners defense which was solid last season and has held both of their opponents under 20 this season.

As for UTEP's offense, their passing game isn't strong enough to expose Northwestern's poor secondary consistently. They can find moderate success running the ball, but the Wildcats have linebackers that will limit big plays. Scoring won't be easy for a UTEP offense averaging 21.0 points per game after two 2023 battles. Take the under all the way.

Prediction: Under 38.5
