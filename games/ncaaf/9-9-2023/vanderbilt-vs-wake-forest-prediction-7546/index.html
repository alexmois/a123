A pair of Power Five conference programs take the field in non-conference action when a member of the SEC travels to Tobacco Road to face off with the ACC. The Vanderbilt Commodores are on the road as they make the trek to take on the Wake Forest Demon Deacons on Saturday morning. Vanderbilt comes in off a 47-13 whipping of Alabama A&M at home last Saturday, though they failed to cover the line as a 35-point favorite. Wake Forest downed Elon 37-17 at home in their season opener last Thursday, though they failed to cover as a 32.5-point favorite in the contest. In the all-time series between the schools, the Commodores own a 10-7 advantage but it was the Demon Deacons who picked up a 45-25 win on the road as a 13.5-point favorite in the most recent meeting on September 10, 2022.

Commodores Seek 3-0 Start to Season

Vanderbilt rolled to an easy win over Alabama A&M in their previous contest and now head out on the road for the first time in this contest. The Commodores enter the game 2-0 overall and look to avenge last season’s loss to the Demon Deacons at home. Against Alabama A&M, Vanderbilt got off to a slow start, falling behind 3-0 early and leading only 12-3 at the half. The Commodores poured it on in the second half, outscoring the Bulldogs 37-10 en route to the win. Vanderbilt rolled up a 461-278 edge in total offense and picked up 24 first downs while allowing 14 to offset losing the time of possession battle by a 32:22 to 27:38 margin. Each team turned the ball over once though the Commodores’ defense did record a safety in the game.

The Commodores enter this contest 59th in the FBS in passing offense with an average of 252 yards per game through the air. Vanderbilt is tied for 83rd in rushing offense as they average 127 yards per contest on the ground as a team. The Commodores are 37th in the FBS in scoring offense with an average of 41 points per game while they are 70th in scoring defense as they allow an average of 20.5 points per contest. AJ Swann has completed 34 of 59 passes for 452 yards with five touchdowns and one interception on the year. Ken Seals is two of two for 52 yards and a score. In the ground game, Sedrick Alexander leads the team with 14 carries for 89 yards and two scores. Patrick Smith (17 carries, 80 yards, TD) and Chase Gillespie (11 carries, 38 yards) have also been involved in the ground game. Jayden McGowan (12 receptions, 142 yards) and Will Sheppard (12 grabs, 130 yards, four TDs) are the only players with more than two receptions for the Commodores this season. Jacob Borcila has hit all nine extra point attempts and connected on his lone field goal attempt, which came from 23 yards, this season.

Demon Deacons Look to Build Off Season Opener

Wake Forest started the Mitch Griffis era on a positive note as they dismantled Elon in their season-opening contest last week. The Demon Deacons now look to earn a win over a FBS foe for the first time this season as they shoot to start the year 2-0. Wake Forest jumped in front 10-0 after the opening quarter and was up 24-0 at the half en route to the victory. The Demon Deacons piled up a 427-251 advantage in total offense, racked up 22 first downs while allowing 13 and forced a pair of turnovers while committing one in the game. Those numbers helped offset losing the time of possession battle by a 31:51 to 28:09 margin and giving up a pick-six in the contest.

The Demon Deacons enter this week 24th in the nation in passing offense with 324 yards per game this season. Wake Forest stands 104th in rushing offense as they average 98 yards per game. The Demon Deacons are 53rd in the FBS in scoring defense with an average of 37 points per game while they are 64th in scoring defense by allowing 17 points a contest. Mitch Griffis is 19 of 30 passing for 329 yards with three touchdowns and one interception this season. Demond Claiborne leads the team on the ground with 13 carries for 70 yards and a score on the year. Justice Ellison (10 carries, 27 yards) has been involved in the ground game as well. Jahmal Banks has reeled in six passes for 108 yards and a score to lead the team. Cameron Hite (four receptions, 91 yards, TD) and Wesley Grimes (three catches, 76 yards, TD) are the only other players with more than two grabs this year. Matthew Dennis has hit all four extra point attempts and three of three field goal attempts with a long of 34 this season.

Vanderbilt did a solid job putting points on the board in their first two games as they hung 35 on Hawaii before piling up 47 against Alabama A&M.
