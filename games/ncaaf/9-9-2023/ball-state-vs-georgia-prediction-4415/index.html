The Ball State Cardinals (0-1, 0-1 ATS, 1-0 O/U) battle the #1 Georgia Bulldogs (1-0, 0-1 ATS, 0-1 O/U) in an early season bout on Saturday afternoon. The Cardinals dropped a road decision against Kentucky in their season opener. The Bulldogs clobbered FCS Tennessee Martin in their first decision. 

Cardinals Don't Cover in Loss to Kentucky

The Ball State Cardinals are hoping to at least qualify for a bowl game after missing out by just one game last season. The gauntlet of a schedule for the program continues. The Cardinals opened the season on the road against Kentucky and were 25-point dogs in a 44-14 loss. 

Ball State named Layne Hatcher the starting QB entering the opener but he only attempted eight passes against Kentucky. The senior QB was productive with Texas State last year, recording a 19:10 TD-to-INT ratio. Kadin Semonza was awarded most of the playing time last week. The freshman was outstanding, connecting on 15 of 21 passes for 165 yards and one TD. Hatcher and Semonza will likely share the duties again in this game.

The squad should have a productive running game this season. Marquez Cooper transferred from Kent State. The junior accumulated 1,331 rushing yards last season but struggled against Kentucky, posting just 33 yards. The Cardinals lost several receivers but brought in Ty Robinson from Colorado who recorded 90 receiving yards. Also, Qian Mawood collected 38 receiving yards in the opener. The Ball State offense is a bit of a question mark due to so many new players but was promising against UK, amassing 295 total yards.

The Cardinals' defense improved as the season progressed in 2022 and returned most of the defensive line. Clayton Coll, who led the squad in tackles last year, was injured last week and is out which is a huge loss. They ranked 49th in pass defense last season and the rush defense was 105th. The unit conceded 357 total yards in the opener.

Ball State scored an average of 23.2 points last season, ranking them 99th. The defense allowed an average of 26.9 points, placing them 70th.

Georgia Earns Blowout Win in Opener

The Georgia Bulldogs are the number one ranked squad in the country and a third consecutive NCAA Championship is in their sights. There are some different names leading the offense, but the skill is there, and the defense should be just as good as last season. The Bulldogs were marked as a mammoth 50-point point favorite in a 41-7 win against Tennessee Martin in the season opener. 

Stetson Bennett has graduated, providing an opportunity for Carson Beck. The junior QB gained valuable experience as the backup last season and had a great spring and training camp. Beck recorded 310 passing yards last season and was solid in the opener, converting on 21 of 31 passes for 294 yards and one TD. 

Georgia lost 2022 rushing leader Kenny McIntosh to the NFL but has more than enough returning players led by Daijun Edwards. The senior RB amassed 769 rushing yards on a stellar average of 5.5 yards per carry last year. He was out Saturday due to an injury and is expected to play. The receiving core is stacked again this year. Brock Bowers led the squad with 942 receiving yards as a tight end and Ladd McConkey led all WRs with 762 yards. McConkey did not play in the opener due to injury and is questionable.

The Dawgs stifling defense is going to dominate after ranking 52nd against the pass and 1st against the run last year. They had five players drafted to the NFL but have plenty of talent, especially in the secondary. Last year’s tackling leader Smael Mondon Jr. is back in action along with sack leader Mykel Williams. Malaki Starks, who led the team in pass deflections last year, is also back. They limited the Skyhawks to only 260 yards in the opener.

Georgia scored an average of 39.2 points last season, ranking them 11th. The defense limited opponents to 12.8 points, good for second.

The Ball State Cardinals are expected to be in the bottom half of the MAC. They were voted to place ninth in the MAC in the preseason coaches poll. They were not able to cover in the opener, losing by 30 points against Kentucky, making this spread more than reasonable.

Ball State had their freshman QB, Kadin Semonza see most of the action last week and that is not ideal against this vaunted defense. Full-Game Total Pick

Insiders Status:

Rating:

I expect a barrage of scoring. It’s unclear who will see most of the action at QB for the Cardinals but Hatcher will likely split duties with Semonza. The Cardinals were able to collect a decent 223 passing yards against Kentucky last week. They have a potent RB in Marquez Cooper. The junior RB surpassed 1300 rushing yards last season.

In addition, the Georgia offense accumulated 559 total yards in the opener and that was Beck’s first game as the #1 QB. Kentucky was able to post 44 points against the Cardinals' defense, so I expect the Dawgs to pile on the points. Also note that Georgia attempted 40 passes last week and plenty of passes could result in quicker scoring drives.  The over is 4-1 in the Bulldogs’ last five games.
