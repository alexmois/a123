It’s a battle between a program from the AAC and one from the Big 12 as they tangle in the Sooner State looking to remain unbeaten. The SMU Mustangs are on the road as they travel to take on the #18 Oklahoma Sooners on Saturday evening. SMU cruised past Louisiana Tech 38-14 at home in their season opener last Saturday, covering the line as a 21-point favorite. Oklahoma, meanwhile, obliterated Arkansas State at home by a count of 73-0 to coast home covering the 37-point line in the contest last Saturday. In the all-time series between the schools, Oklahoma holds a 5-1-1 advantage and has won the last four meetings. That includes a 24-10 home win in the most recent meeting on September 16, 1995.

SMU Mustangs Look to Upend Ranked Opponent on the Road

SMU took care of business at home in their season opener as they downed Louisiana Tech last Saturday. The Mustangs now travel on the road to take on a ranked foe as they look to down Oklahoma for the first time since they won the Bluebonnet Bowl back in 1968. Against Louisiana Tech, SMU jumped ahead 14-0 after the opening quarter and extended their lead to 31-0 at the half before cruising their way home. The Mustangs racked up a 457-269 edge in total offense, won the first down battle 24-11, controlled the clock by a 33:59 to 26:01 margin and forced the game’s lone turnover, which was a pick-six by Kori Roberson.

On the year, the Mustangs are tied for 60th in passing offense as they average 248 yards per game through the air. SMU is tied for 34th in rushing offense as they grind out 209 yards per contest on the ground. The Mustangs are 48th in the FBS in scoring offense with an average of 38 points per game. SMU is 50th in scoring defense as they give up an average of 14 points per contest. Preston Stone connected on 23 of 37 throws for 248 yards with three touchdowns and no interceptions while adding 11 yards on the ground. LJ Johnson Jr. is the team’s leading ground gainer with 14 carries for 128 yards and a score on the year. Jaylan Knighton (17 carries, 64 yards) is also a factor in the run game. Tight end RJ Maryland leads the team with five catches for 58 yards and a score on the year. Moochie Dixon (three catches, 29 yards) and Jake Bailey (three receptions, nine yards) are the only other players with more than two catches on the season. Collin Rogers hit all five extra point attempts but is one of three on field goal tries with a long of 34: he misfired on attempts from 36 and 49 yards.

Oklahoma Sooners Seek to Build Off Blowout Win

Oklahoma obliterated Arkansas State as they started hot early and coasted the rest of the way to kick off their final season in the Big 12 on a positive note. The Sooners now seek to build off that showing and try to beat the Mustangs for the fifth straight time. Oklahoma needed just five plays and 1:19 to get on the board to take a 7-0 lead: they doubled that margin just 1:03 later and piled on from there. The Sooners bolted to a 28-0 lead after the opening quarter and were up 45-0 at the half en route to the blowout. Oklahoma steamrolled Arkansas State to the tune of a 642-208 edge in total offense, piled up 36 first downs while allowing 10, controlled the clock by a 36:56 to 23:04 margin and forced the game’s only turnover. For good measure, the Sooners scored on an 82-yard punt return by Gavin Freeman.

For the season, the Sooners are 6th in the nation in passing offense as they average 422 yards per game through the air. Oklahoma stands tied for 28th in rushing offense by piling up 220 yards per contest on the ground. The Sooners stand 3rd in the FBS in scoring offense as they average 73 points per contest. Oklahoma is tied for 1st in the nation in scoring defense as they have yet to surrender a point on the year. Dillon Gabriel is 19 of 22 passing for 308 yards with two touchdowns and no interceptions while adding 15 yards plus a score on the ground. Jackson Arnold is 11 of 11 for 114 yards plus a score while adding 39 rushing yards and a score. Jovantae Barnes is the team’s top ground gainer with 13 carries for 49 yards. Tawee Walker (eight carries, 44 yards, two TDs) and Marcus Major (nine carries, 31 yards, TD) are also looked at to do damage on the ground. Jaquazie Pettaway leads the team with nine receptions for 56 yards. Drake Stoops (four catches, 56 yards, TD), Gavin Freeman (four grabs, 19 yards, TD) and Andrel Anthony (three receptions, 66 yards) are also factors in the aerial assault. Zach Schmit is eight of eight on extra point attempts and hit his lone field goal from 21 yards. Gavin Marshall hit both extra point attempts and has yet to try a field goal this season.

The Mustangs combined with Louisiana Tech to score 52 points, falling well short of the 66.5-point number that was set at the books for the game.
