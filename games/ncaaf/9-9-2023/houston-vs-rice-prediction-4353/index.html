#357 Houston vs.
#358 Rice
Saturday, September 9, 2023 at 7:00pm EDT
Written by The Tower

This year marks the first for the Houston Cougars (1-0; 1-0-0 ATS) in the Big 12 after years as an American Athletic Conference (AAC) school. Their departure opened the door for the Rice Owls (0-1; 1-0-0 ATS) to leave Conference USA for the AAC. Further intertwining these schools, outside of sharing Houston as a home, is a game last season that the Cougars won 34-27 at home. This year, home field flips, with the Owls hosting in Rice Stadium. Don't start sitting sideways waiting for this 7:00 p.m. EDT kickoff on Saturday. It'll come quicker than expected, bringing more Texas football to the forefront.

Cougars Keep Escaping

Although they managed to win as two-point underdogs last weekend, it continued a troubling trend for Houston. Of their last six games, only one of them was a win by more than seven points. As a Big 12 school, serious contention won't be possible without better showings than that. However, Houston's narrow win over a tough UTSA team was far from bad news. Especially not for a defense that gave up 33.5 points and 430.0 yards per game last season. Holding the Roadrunners to 14 points last week was a great sign, even if it took three interceptions to negate 417 yards allowed. A lot of new faces litter Houston's front seven defensively, so they'll need time to gel. Last week's turnovers should give them confidence, while this week's matchup will hopefully lead to dominance.

Houston entered the year with changes at quarterback and along the offensive line. Donovan Smith made his debut as the Cougars gunslinger last weekend, finishing 22/34 for 233 yards and two touchdowns. The offensive line wasn't quite as solid, allowing three sacks and leading the ground game to 2.7 yards per rush. Passing is supposed to be Houston's strength this season, but every team needs more than that from the run game if they want consistent results. Considering Houston struggled to score early last year but could turn it on late (when they needed to recover through passing), they are a team searching for consistency.

Rice Getting Fried

A five-game losing streak isn't fun for any program, let alone this Rice team that has only kept one of those games with single digits. They'll try to snap it at home against a Houston team that they took down to the wire last year. Replicating last week's first half defensively would help, as the Owls held a ranked Texas team to 16 points in the first half. Last year's Owls were solid defensively, led by a linebacker corps which is the team's best position group this season. The 2023 team has a solid secondary on paper too, and there were flashes of that last week. The front four is suspect, particularly rushing the passer, but on defense, the Owls can hold their own. 

It's the offense that brings down the entire operation. Turnovers are a massive issue for this team. They finished last season with 32 giveaways and a -14 turnover differential. Last Saturday, new quarterback JT Daniels threw two interceptions, and running back Quinton Jackson lost a fumble. A shaky offensive line isn't helping Rice move the ball effectively either. The Owls have a strong group of receivers that can fly, but getting them the ball will be an uphill battle unless the rest of the offense levels up.

Houston should win this game, but they haven't shown any ability to pull away from an opponent lately. Facing a Rice team that's decent on defense, scoring won't come easy for the Cougars. Especially if their offensive line has another rough Saturday.

As for the Owls, moving the ball isn't an issue for them offensively, while Houston tends to bleed yards. The turnover battle between those units will make or break this game. The Rice coach has to be stressing pigskin protection before the school's home opener on Saturday. Full-Game Total Pick

Insiders Status:

Rating:

Houston's passing attack can move the ball against anyone, especially this Rice team that isn't as talented on paper. The Owls don't generate a pass rush consistently, so the Cougars will have all day to get open. It may not be as smooth as possible because of a questionable push in the running game, but eventually, Houston will start cruising into the end zone on Saturday.

On the other side, Rice will score plenty of points if they don't turn the ball over. Houston allowed more than 400 yards per game last season and gave up over 400 last week. Last year, the Owls put up 27 with over 400 yards of offense. The Owls have receivers who can cause havoc all night if given the chance. They will get opportunities, and this game will finish over the total.

Prediction: Over 52.5
