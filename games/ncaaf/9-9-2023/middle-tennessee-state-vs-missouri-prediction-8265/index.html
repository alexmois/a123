The Middle Tennessee Blue Raiders and Missouri Tigers will square off in week two NCAA football action this Saturday at Faurot Field at Memorial Stadium. Kickoff is at 6:00 p.m. CT.

Middle Tennessee lost to Alabama in week one. It has an over/under win total of 6.5 wins this year. The Blue Raiders were picked to finish third in the Conference USA media preseason poll. They were 8-5 overall and 4-3 on the road in 2022.

Missouri beat South Dakota in its season opener. It has an over/under win total of six wins in 2023. The Tigers were predicted to finish sixth in the Southeastern Conference's Eastern division in the media's preseason ranking. They went 6-7 last season, including 5-2 at home.

The Tigers are 21-point spread favorites and the game total is 48 points.

Can MTSU establish the run in 2023?

Middle Tennessee will be battle-tested after scheduling Alabama and Missouri to open its 2023 football season. The Blue Raiders were overmatched in Tuscaloosa, surrendering 14 points each quarter of their 56-7 defeat to the Crimson Tide. MTSU held the football for nearly two minutes longer than Bama but only managed 3.8 yards per completion through the air and 3.0 yards per carry on the ground. The Tide gashed the Blue Raiders for 205 yards on 40 carries (5.1 YPC) and converted 10 of 13 third downs.

MTSU's eight-win season in 2022 was its best in a few years, and still, the program hasn't had a winning conference record since 2018. The Blue Raiders are led by Rick Stockstill, who is in his 18th season at MTSU. While they endured some unexpected early departures, the core of last year's squad is intact and hungry for more.

The Middle Tennessee offense was too reliant on the passing game last year, averaging only 3.1 yards per carry and 108.8 rushing yards per game. The line certainly didn't do a good enough job at creating space for the Blue Raiders' backs, as junior Frank Peasant (182 carries for 774 yards and nine TDs) proved tough to tackle in the open field. Sophomore quarterback Nick Vattiato (65.2% completion percentage) is the starter after getting his feet wet in 2022 in relief of the injured Chase Cunningham.

The Blue Raiders' top receiving threat, Jaylin Lane (69 receptions for 940 yards and five scores), transferred to Virginia Tech, and the team's second and third-best pass-catchers are gone, as well. Senior DJ England-Chisolm (15.9 yards-per-reception in 2022) is the top option in an inexperienced group.

The MTSU defense was vulnerable through the air in 2022 and didn't create enough pressure by getting in the backfield, either. That said, it was an elite run defense unit (121.4 yards allowed per game) and was fourth nationally in turnover margin with 30 takeaways. Marley Cook (6.5 sacks) and Zaylin Wood (6.5 sacks) are the stars of the defensive line, but another pass-rusher needs to emerge in 2023. The Blue Raiders' pass defense allowed too many yards but picked off 20 passes. Safety Tra Fluellen (104 tackles, 8 PBUs, and two INTs) and CB Teldrick Ross (76 tackles, 18 PBUs, and two INTs) lead the DBs.

Is Missouri ready for its first real test?

The Tigers won at home over FCS opponent South Dakota last week but didn't exactly blow anyone away. The 27.5-point favorites led by only 18 late in the fourth quarter, scoring a final touchdown with just over a minute left in the game. Mizzou led 28-3 at halftime with returning starter Brady Cook (two total TDs) under center, but couldn't keep up the pace in the second half with redshirt freshman Sam Horn taking snaps. The two QBs competed for the starting job in fall camp, but Cook was able to hold onto his position.

The Missouri running game was the offense's best weapon, anyway, as it rushed 40 times for 211 yards (5.3 YPC). Seniors Cody Schrader (148 yards and a touchdown) and Nathaniel Peat (52 yards and a score) handled the bulk of the carries, while sophomore Luther Burden III led the receivers with seven receptions, 96 yards, and a TD. The Tigers defense held the Coyotes to 46 rushing yards on 35 carries (1.3 YPC) and 148 passing yards.

Missouri's initial transition from the Big 12 to the SEC appeared to go smoothly at first, but it's been unable to sustain success since joining the league in 2012. The Tigers won 23 games from 2013 to 2014 but haven't produced a winning conference record since the 2014 season. Current head coach Eliah Drinkwitz has led Mizzou to two bowl games, but 6-7 seasons aren't moving the needle.

Drinkwitz hopes that new offensive coordinator Kirby Moore can do that, though, after hiring the ex-Fresno State coordinator to take over his passing attack in 2023. His expertise – the Bulldogs led the Mountain West in passing yards a season ago – could be the difference. Last year, Missouri averaged 214.1 passing yards per game with just 14 passing touchdowns, six of which came against New Mexico State and Abilene Christian. If Cook is unable to show progress, Horn and Miami transfer Jake Garcia will put pressure on the coaching staff to make a change.

After all, the Tigers can't waste this group of receivers. Burden was a highly-touted recruit, junior Mookie Cooper (26 receptions for 298 yards) is coming into his own, and senior Theo Wease Jr. brings a wealth of experience (19.9 yards-per-reception in 2022) from his time at Oklahoma State. The Missouri rushing attack has potential, but the offensive line needs to clean up its run-blocking issues, as it allowed the most tackles for loss in the SEC last season.

The Tigers' defense was fourth in the conference in total defense and did a fine job getting into the backfield in 2022. The defensive line has some question marks, but the linebackers and secondary should be good. LBs Ty’Ron Hopper (78 tackles and 14 TFL) and Chad Bailey (57 tackles) will man the middle of the defense, and cornerback Kris Abrams-Draine (14 PBUs) is the leader of the defensive backs. Safeties Jaylon Carlies (81 tackles and three picks) and Joseph Charleston (53 tackles) are playmakers.

The Tigers missed an opportunity to build momentum by giving Horn the starting reps in the second half, as the freshman couldn't move the ball well enough against FCS South Dakota to cover the spread with a 28-3 lead. He won't be taking snaps on Saturday, but there's something to be said about Drinkwitz's decision to take the foot off the pedal that has me cautious about the Tigers' odds to cover.

The Blue Raiders defense was great at creating takeaways last season and should hold up well enough against the run. If the Tigers can't establish the ground game, the offense could stall out on a couple of drives, keeping MTSU in the game.

It's difficult to predict how the Blue Raiders will look offensively this week after their performance in Tuscaloosa, but it's hard to blame them for struggling in their fight against the Tide. Missouri kept the Coyotes' offense in check, but this isn't an FCS-FBS matchup. The Blue Raiders didn't turn the ball over much last season and I don't expect that to be a cause for concern on Saturday.

I don't believe the Tigers will execute well enough to win by more than three touchdowns.

Missouri scored 28 first-half points with Cook at quarterback and had a field day on the ground, rushing for over 200 yards last week. It may not have as much success against Middle Tennessee, but there's reason to believe that performance wasn't a fluke. I don't think it can or should do that on Saturday, but that doesn't mean it won't play some younger players on defense with a late lead.

This matchup sets up well for a Blue Raiders cover and an over at 48 total points scored.
