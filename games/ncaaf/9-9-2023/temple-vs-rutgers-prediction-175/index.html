Two mid-Atlantic region rivals meet Saturday night as the Rutgers Scarlet Knights host the Temple Owls in the Swamps of Jersey. The Scarlet Knights of the Big Ten are 1-0 on the season after beating Northwestern in the opener, while Temple of the American Athletic Conference is 1-0 after squeaking past Akron 24-21 in week one. Rutgers has won 4 straight versus Temple including a 16-14 win last season, where Temple covered as an 18.5-point underdog. Kickoff is Saturday night at 7:30 p.m. at SHI Stadium in Piscataway, N.J.

Temple survives against Akron

The Temple Owls scored a 24-21 win over Akron in their season opener last weekend, but they did not cover as a 9.5-point favorite. In fact, they nearly lost the game as it took some late game magic to pull it out. Akron jumped out to 21-10 halftime lead, but Temple scored twice in the second half, as EJ Warner hit Edward Saydee for a 7-yard TD pass late in the third quarter and the two connected again early in the fourth quarter to give Temple the lead for good.

The Owls sealed the deal when Tywan Francis made an interception on one of Akron's final deep drives. Temple dominated the game, yet struggled to win it. Warner was 28 for 49 for 292 yards and two touchdowns with no interceptions while Dante Wright led the receiving corps with 7 catches for 71 yards. Saydee had 5 grabs for 37 with two scores. Saydee, a running back, also ran for 18 yards. Darvon Hubbard added a team-high 35 rushing yards for Temple. But overall, Temple only had 61 rushing yards.

Rutgers tops Northwestern

The Rutgers Scarlet Knights got a win in week one, but not just any win, they beat a Big Ten rival, which is always extra special. The Scarlet Knights hosted the Northwestern Wildcats last Saturday and came away with a 24-7 win. The Scarlet Knights easily covered as 5.5-point favorites. It wasn't the prettiest of games, unless you like defense -- then it was a thing of beauty. Rutgers only gave up 201 total yards in the game, including just 12 rushing yards. The home team also had two interceptions.

Offensively, the Scarlet Knights did just enough to win the game. They only put up 282 total yards, with quarterback Gavin Wimsatt accounting for more than half of them. Wimsatt was an efficient, yet pedestrian 17 for 29 for 163 yards and one touchdown with no picks. He also ran for 33 yards and a touchdown. Isaiah Washington was the leading receiver with 4 catches for 36 yards while Ian Strong caught 2 passes for 24 yards and a touchdown. Running back Kyle Monangui ran for 49 yards and a score.

Rating:

Both of these teams got wins last week, but Temple was a little shaky and barely escaped against a lesser opponent. They did not cover the spread. Rutgers, on the other hand, outperformed, beating a Big Ten foe quite easily, and covering the spread in the process. It is hard to tell just how good Rutgers' defense is without a few game sample size, but based on last Saturday's win, it looks pretty impressive. Temple struggled at times to move the ball against Akron so I expect they'll have a much tougher time doing so on Saturday at Rutgers. Take Rutgers giving up the points for the win and cover.

Full-Game Total Pick

Insiders Status:

Rating:

Neither one of these offenses looked all that great on Saturday, so I don't expect to see an inordinate amount of points in this game. Rutgers defense put the clamps down on Northwestern last week and I think they will do the same against a Temple offense that was not firing on all cylinders against a lesser opponent last week in Akron. Temple could get a couple of scores, but that would be on the high side. Rutgers is not spectacular on offense either, but they showed nice balance on offense and should be able to hit the low to mid-20s against a very mid-Temple defense. Ultimately, though, this is a low-scoring affair that falls below the total. Take the under.
