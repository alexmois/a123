Stanford Cardinal (1-0) vs. USC Trojans (2-0) 

Week 2 of the 2023 college football season is upon us, so we are breaking down the Pac-12 Conference showdown from Los Angeles Memorial Coliseum to get you the best Stanford vs. USC betting pick and odds.  

The Cardinal and Trojans wrap up Saturday’s slate, and the kick-off is set for 10:30 PM ET. The Trojans are huge 29-point favorites in front of the home fans, while the totals are listed at 68.5 points. 

Stanford satisfied the odds in Week 1

The Stanford Cardinal kicked off their 2023 campaign with a 37-24 road victory over the Hawaii Rainbow Warriors in Week 1. The Cardinal easily covered a 2.5-point spread, while the game flew over a 54.5-point line. 

Stanford dominated Hawaii on the ground. The Rainbow Warriors finished with minus-5 yards on 16 carries, whereas the Cardinal mustered 158 yards on 34 totes. The teams combined for 20 penalties (Stanford committed nine for 81 yards) and zero turnovers, and the Cardinal outgained the Warriors 406-350 in total yards in their debut under new head coach Troy Taylor, who spent the last four years at Sacramento State. 

“I learned a ton about our guys and we talked about that all week, that this was not going to be perfect,” Taylor said. “This was going to be a tough game and there were going to be times when we had to bounce back and they reacted. Very proud of our guys. Coaching changes are difficult. It’s a great feeling to get a win and winning on the road is not easy to do. Just real proud of our guys.” 

Sophomore signal-caller Ashton Daniels led the charge for the Cardinal last Friday, throwing for 248 yards and a couple of touchdowns. He also had 11 carries for 42 yards, while senior RB Casey Filkins added six totes for 67 yards. Senior wideout Benjamin Yurosek had a big game, finishing with nine receptions for 138 yards and a TD.

USC is No. 6 in the AP Top 25 Poll

After a 56-28 home victory over the San Jose State Spartans in Week zero, the USC Trojans trounced the Nevada Wolf Pack 66-14 in front of the home audience in Week 1. The Trojans failed to cover a 31-point spread against the Spartans, so they dismantled the Wolf Pack as 38-point favorites. USC outgained Nevada 668-360 in total yards, allowing just 49 rushing yards to the Wolf Pack. 

“I'm proud of both sides (of the ball),” USC head coach Lincoln Riley said. “A few plays here and there that we're going to want back, that we know we have to continue to clean up, but we took a step this week.” 

Junior QB Caleb Williams tortured Nevada for 319 passing yards and five touchdowns, completing 18 of his 24 passing attempts. The 2022 Heisman Trophy winner has thrown for 597 yards and nine touchdowns through USC’s first two games of the season. Senior WR Tahj Washington had three catches for 75 yards and a pair of scores against Nevada, while Duce Robinson’s lone reception resulted in a 71-yard touchdown. 

Washington is leading the Trojans receiving corps with 160 yards and three touchdowns in 2023. Last year, he had 50 catches for 785 yards and six scores, and the Trojans’ offense was tallying 41.4 points per game (3rd in the nation). 

Best Bets for this Game

Full-Game Side Bet

Insiders Status:

Rating:

The Trojans possess great depth at almost every position on both sides of the ball. The Cardinal will struggle to contain Caleb Williams and USC’s powerful offense, so I’m expecting the Trojans to win this game by 30 or more points. The Trojans’ run defense might be their biggest flaw, and I think Stanford doesn’t have enough firepower to take full advantage of it. 

Last year, USC beat Stanford 41-28 as a 9.5-point road fave. The Trojans are only 3-6 ATS in their previous nine encounters with Stanford, but they are also 4-1 ATS in their last five outings in September and 6-2 ATS in their previous eight showings in Week 2. On the other side, the Cardinal are 1-6 ATS in their last seven games overall, 2-8 ATS in their previous ten outings on the road, and 0-5 ATS in their last five dates with the Pac-12. 

Prediction: USC Trojans -29.0 (-110)
