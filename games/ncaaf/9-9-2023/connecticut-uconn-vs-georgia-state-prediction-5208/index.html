The University of Connecticut Huskies go for their first win of the season when they head to Georgia State on Saturday to face the Panthers. The Huskies, an independent, come in with a record of 0-1 while the Panthers of the Sun Belt Conference, are 1-0. Kickoff is Saturday at 7:00 p.m. at Center Parc Stadium in Atlanta.

Huskies cover but lose opener

The Huskies lost their season opener last Saturday at North Carolina State by the score of 24-14. However, they covered against the ACC foe, at home, as a 14.5-point underdog. NC State blew a close game open in the third quarter with two touchdowns. The Huskies only managed 273 total yards in the game with just 113 in the air and 160 on the ground. NC State held the first down edge 23-15 and the Huskies were assessed with 6 penalties for 61 yards including two that extended touchdown drives. They also turned the ball over once, while the Wolfpack had no turnovers.

"We hurt ourselves. Things we know we're not supposed to, we did. It showed and against a decent football team like that, you can't do things like that. It showed," UConn safety Durante Jones told CT Insider.

UConn quarterback Joseph Fagnano (Maine transfer) had a rough night, going 14 for 26 for 113 yards with no touchdowns and 1 interception. Most of the offense flowed through Victor Rosa, who rushed for 99 yards on 9 carries for an 11-yard per carry average. Rosa also scored both of UConn's touchdowns, including one on a 71-yard scamper. Devontae Houston added 24 yards on the ground and caught 3 passes for 22 yards. Justin Joly led the receiving corps with 4 catches for 38 yards. UConn has no injuries coming into this game against Georgia Southern.

Panthers beat Rhode Island

The Georgia State Panthers of the Sun Belt Conference got their season off to a good start with an easy non-conference win over Rhode Island, 42-35. The Panthers were favored by 17.5 points so they did not cover. The Panthers were largely outplayed by the FCS school, allowing 540 yards including 400 in the air. But the Panthers gashed the Rams for 231 rushing yards and generated 2 turnovers while they had none. One of those interceptions was a pick 6 by Gavin Pringle.

Panthers quarterback Darren Grainger was a very efficient 16 for 20 for 193 yards and 2 touchdowns with 0 interceptions. He also ran for 51 yards. But the story of the game was Marcus Carroll, who ran 23 times for 184 yards with 8 yards per carry. He scored three times. Robert Lewis, who caught 7 passes for 97 yards, also had a touchdown, as did Tailique Williams, who scored on a 42-yard strike from Grainger.  They have no injuries to report.

Full-Game Side Bet

Insiders Status:

Rating:

This line feels about right but if it bumps up a bit, I might change my tune. I see the Panthers as winning this by a field goal, giving them the edge for being at home. Otherwise, they are pretty evenly matched. UConn has proven to be a solid defensive team, holding down a tough opponent in week one. The defense will carry the load here and keep the Huskies within striking distance. Also, the Panthers are not as strong defensively against the pass, so look for UConn's passing game to step up. But ultimately, the Panthers are the better team and will cover here. Take Georgia State giving up the points.

This feels like too big a number here. I see this as another low-scoring game for the Huskies and I don't see Georgia State racking up anything near what they did against Rhode Island last week.
