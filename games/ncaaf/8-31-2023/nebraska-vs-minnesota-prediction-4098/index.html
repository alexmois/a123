The Nebraska Cornhuskers (0-0) will be trying to kick off their season with a win when they face the Minnesota Golden Gophers (0-0) in Big Ten action on Thursday night. The game will be played at Huntington Bank Stadium and it is scheduled to begin at 8 PM. ET.

Minnesota is 7-3 in its last 10 games against Nebraska.

Cornhuskers Hoping Rhule Can Return Them To Glory

The Cornhuskers were hoping the hiring of former quarterback Scott Frost would help restore the team to its former success. After four years without a winning season, the team parted ways with Frost and hired former Carolina Panthers head coach Matt Rhule, who has had success rebuilding programs like Temple and Baylor, to be their new head coach.

Nebraska is hoping that Rhule will have similar success at Lincoln, but they will have to go through some growing pains this season.

Nebraska averaged 22.6 points per game last season. They threw for 220.8 yards and rushed for 123.3 yards per game.

Former Georgia Tech quarterback Jeff Sims will be Nebraska’s starter this season. He completed 58 percent of his passes for 1,115 yards, five touchdowns, and three interceptions last season. Anthony Grant ran for 915 yards and six touchdowns, while Marcus Washington led the team with 471 receiving yards.

Nebraska struggled defensively last season, giving up 27.6 points per game. They had trouble stopping the run last season and will have to do a better job if they want to win more than four games this season.

Rahmir Johnson (Hamstring) is questionable for this game.

Golden Gophers Hoping To Contend For Big West Title

With the exception of the COVID-19-shortened season, the Golden Gophers have won at least nine games in three straight years. They will be facing some tough competition from Iowa and Wisconsin, but they believe they have what it takes to win the Big Ten West title this season.

Minnesota averaged 28.2 points per game last season. They threw for 182.2 yards and rushed for 207.5 yards per game.

They lost both their starting quarterback and leading rusher, but they replaced them with Athan Kaliakmanis, who completed 54.1 percent of his passes for 946 yards, three touchdowns, and four interceptions in limited action last season. Western Michigan transfer Sean Tyler, who rushed for 1,027 yards and seven touchdowns last season, is expected to replace Mohamed Ibrahim.

Minnesota was one of the best defensive teams in the country last season, giving up 13.3 points per game. Even though they lost some key players in their secondary, they have a lot of confidence in their replacements and should be just as good defensively this season.
