The North Carolina State Wolfpack will travel to East Hartford on Thursday night to take on the UConn Huskies to kick off the 2023 College Football season for both programs. The Wolfpack, out of the ACC, finished 2022 at 8-5 which included a loss to Maryland in the Mayo Bowl. UConn, an independent, finished last season at 6-7 after dropping a 28-14 decision to Marshall in the Myrtle Beach Bowl. The opening kickoff is scheduled for 7:30 PM EST.

New Look Wolfpack

After going 8-4 last season that included wins over FSU, Virginia Tech, Wake Forest, and North Carolina, the Wolfpack concluded their season with a loss to Maryland in the Mayo Bowl to close the season. Head coach Dave Doeren has now had eight bowl trips in his 11 years at NC State. He will be tasked with getting the Wolfpack back to a bowl game again this season but with a very different team. The team lost several starters including quarterback Devin Leary who transferred to Kentucky. NC State will now feature former Virginia quarterback Brennan Armstrong. Armstrong has thrown for over 9,000 yards and nearly 60 touchdowns in his career at Virginia. He will be paired with his former offensive coordinator at Virginia Robert Anae. After spending much of last season running the ball, the team will likely play a more wide-open brand of offense with Armstrong under center. He will have 3/5's of last year's offensive line in front of him as well as returning senior running back Jordan Houston. Houston is expected to share the load with the Wolfpack likely utilizing three running backs this season.

Defensively, NC State is returning six starters from last year's team. The Wolfpack finished 15th in scoring defense in 2022. They were also top 20 in the country in rushing defense and total yards allowed. The strength of the defense is in the secondary with NC State returning both starting cornerbacks, Aydan White and Shyheim Battle. The duo will form arguably the best pairing in the ACC and one of the tops in the country. NC State also returns two of three starters along the defensive line and defensive end Savion Jackson is back after missing last season with a knee injury. The linebacking core is intact from last year as well and the main positions up for grabs will be at safety and nickel corner. While the offense adjusts to a new system this season, the Wolfpack's 3-3-5 defense should provide stability in the early going of the season.

Upward Mobility

The UConn Huskies head coach Jim Mora brought the Huskies to new heights last year with a six-win season and a bowl berth. This season, Mora hopes to push past that mark and get UConn back to a bowl game for the second straight season. It would mark the first time the Huskies were in consecutive bowl games since making three straight from 2008-2010. This season, the Huskies will go with Maine transfer Joseph Fagnano at quarterback after he beat out both Ta'Quan Roberson and Zion Turner this summer. Fagnano completed 57% of his passes for 2,231 yards and 15 touchdowns in Maine last season. For his career, he completed nearly 60% of his passes for 5,655 yards and 46 touchdowns with 12 interceptions. Fagnano will be protected by an offensive line that returns four starters from last year's bowl team.

"Joe's very poised," Mora said. "Joe has some height to him. Joe has played a lot of snaps and thrown a lot of passes. There's a strong correlation between the number of snaps you've played, the number of passes you've thrown, and your ability to have success. That was important to us.

"He's got a very calm demeanor, a strong and quiet leader. If things are not going well he can keep his composure well. I things go well he stays steady. I'm excited to see him go out and play Thursday night. I think he's going to play well and we'll do a great job of supporting him."

The Huskies return four starters from last year's defense with the most prominent being middle linebacker Jackson Mitchell. Mitchell was the fourth-leading tackler in the nation last season with 140 tackles. He will anchor a defense that will go through significant change specifically in the secondary. The Huskies will return just one starter, sophomore safety Durante Jones. The rest of the starting secondary will be made up of two sophomores and one freshman. The UConn defense finished just 69th in the country in scoring defense last year. Mora has tried to add depth this season with a number of transfers and incoming freshmen.

Best Bets for this Game

Full-Game Side Bet

Insiders Status:

Rating:

These two teams met in 2022 with the Wolfpack winning 41-10 at home. NC State was able to gain nearly 500 yards of total offense compared with just 160 for the Huskies. While both teams come into this matchup featuring several new faces, the Wolfpack's improved passing attack will severely exploit the very green Huskies' secondary. Expect new quarterback Armstrong to have a field day throwing the ball down the field against a secondary featuring only one returning starter and loaded with youth. On the other side of the ball, NC State returns a large portion of a defense that was second overall in the ACC last year and top 20 in several categories in the country. Like NC State, UConn will feature a new quarterback this year and there will be some adjustment in the early going. NC State has an experienced defensive front and a strong secondary so I expect some issues and possible turnovers by UConn on Thursday night. UConn will get better but this is a bad spot for them against an NC State team that could make some noise if things play out the right way this season.

Take the Wolfpack -15.

Prediction: NC State -15

Full-Game Total Pick

Insiders Status:

Rating:

These two teams combined for 51 points in 2022 and I like that number to go up here on the strength of NC State's more explosive offensive. Armstrong is a more downfield passer than NC State had in 2022 and he will stretch UConn's inexperienced secondary. UConn will find themselves in catch-up mode early and will be forced to put the ball in the air to try and keep pace with NC State. This will likely lead to turnovers and great field position for NC State throughout the game.
