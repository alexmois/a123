The Northwestern Wildcats (1-11, 5-7 ATS, 5-7 O/U) battle the Rutgers Scarlet Knights (4-8, 5-6-1 ATS, 6-6 O/U) in a Big Ten showdown on Sunday afternoon. The Wildcats posted an abysmal 1-11 mark last season and 1-8 in Big Ten play. The Scarlet Knights registered a 4-8 record and went 1-8 in conference play. These rivals last met in 2021 in a game Northwestern won by a 21-7 score.

Northwestern Eyes Progress After One Win Season

The Northwestern Wildcats have nowhere to go but up after a disastrous 2022 season that saw them win only one game. That victory occurred in their season opener against Nebraska. The squad had big issues generating offense while the defense was competitive. Northwestern was voted to finish last in the Big Ten media poll. New coach David Braun takes over for longtime coach and player Pat Fitzgerald, who was fired due to hazing allegations.

The good news is the passing offense will definitely be improved after bringing in Ben Bryant on a transfer from Cincinnati. The senior QB had over 3000 passing yards in 2021 and enjoyed an outstanding season last year, collecting 2732 passing yards complemented by a 21:7 TD to INT ratio. The offense lost 2022 rushing leader Evan Hull to the NFL and is hoping Cam Porter can have a breakout season. The senior RB recorded 286 rushing yards on an average of only 3.3 yards per carry last year. The squad also lost many key receivers but was able to bring in Cam Johnson from Arizona State. The senior WR had 545 receiving yards in 2020 but only managed 84 yards last season.

The Wildcats' defense was a bright spot at times last year, especially the pass defense which was 12th in the FBS while the rush defense struggled, ranking 111th. They brought in a new defensive coordinator, David Braun. The Wildcats return one of their best defensive players, Bryce Gallagher who led the team with 100 tackles last season.

Northwestern averaged 13.8 points last year, pegging them 121st. They conceded an average of 28.3 points, placing them 84th.

Rutgers Eyes Improvement on Offense

The Rutgers Scarlet Knights struggled to generate points last season. The program brought in a new offensive coordinator, Kirk Cirarrocca. Rutgers won four games last season propelled by the defense. If the offense can improve they should be able to surpass four victories this season. The Scarlet Knights were selected to finish 12th in the Big Ten media poll.

Rutgers has named Gavin Wimsatt the #1 QB after a battle in the spring and training camp. The junior QB struggled last season, amassing only 757 passing yards and a 5:7 TD-to-INT ratio. Wimsatt is going to have to show improvement for the offense to be competitive. 

The Scarlet Knights return their top running backs from last season. Kyle Monangai led the squad with 445 rushing yards on a solid 4.1 yards per carry while Samuel Brown V collected 374 rushing yards. The offense lost receiving leaders Sean Ryan and Aron Cruickshank to the NFL and will need someone to step up. Chris Long could be that guy. The junior WR registered 200 receiving yards last season.

The Scarlet Knights' defense will be a strength again this season after standing 37th in pass defense and 54th in the country in rush defense last year. They return many key pieces from that unit including Deion Jennings. The senior linebacker made 87 tackles. The squad also brings back their top corners from last season. 

Rutgers scored an average of only 17.4 points last year, ranking them 124th. They squandered an average of 29.3 points, positioning them 93rd in the FBS.

Full-Game Side Bet

Insiders Status:

Rating:

The Northwestern Wildcats are going to be an improved squad this season. The offense was poor last season but the addition of QB Ben Bryant is a huge upgrade. The senior had an outstanding year with Cincinnati last year, sporting a strong  21:7 TD to INT ratio, and led the squad to nine wins. 

Furthermore, I am not sold on Rutgers QB Gavin Wimsatt who has been named the starter in this one. He struggled last year, connecting on only 45% of his passes, and posted a weak 5:7 TD to INT ratio. The Wildcats actually have a stellar pass defense. They limited opponents to only 183.4 passing yards per game last year, good for 12th, and return many of the key players on that unit. Rutgers has an unproven offense and 6.5 is way too many points.

Prediction: Northwestern Wildcats +6.5
