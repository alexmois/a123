The Louisiana State Tigers and Florida State Seminoles will play in the Camping World Kickoff on Sunday in Orlando, FL. The game is scheduled to start at 7:30 p.m. ET.

LSU has an over/under win total of 9.5 wins. It will also play Grambling State, Army, and Georgia State in non-conference play. In the preseason media poll, the Tigers were picked to finish second in the SEC Western division this season and received 31 votes, the third-most, to win the SEC title. They were 10-4 overall last season.

FSU also has an over/under win total of 9.5 wins. It will face Southern Miss, North Alabama, and Florida in its other non-conference games. The Seminoles were picked to finish second in the ACC media poll, with 67 first-place votes. They had a 10-3 record in 2022.

The Tigers are two-point spread favorites and the game total is 56.5 points.

Can the Tigers take the next step?

Louisiana State had a successful year one under head coach Brian Kelly, winning ten games and beating Alabama to claim the SEC West crown. Making the College Football Playoff in the last season with a four-team field would represent a major step forward in the program's trajectory under the former Notre Dame HC.

The LSU offense has the pieces to be as deadly as any team in the country, it just needs to run the football effectively. Last season, dual-threat quarterback Jayden Daniels led the team with 885 rushing yards and 11 scores on 186 carries. As impressive as that was, the Tigers need their running backs to carry the football more in 2023. Daniels was the only player to rush more than 100 times last year, putting the wiry QB in harm's way far too often. At least one of the team's backs — Josh Williams (532 rushing yards and 5.5 yards-per-carry), Noah Cain (409 rushing yards and 5.4 YPC), or Notre Dame transfer Logan Diggs (82o rushing yards and 5.0 YPC) — will need to lighten the load for JD.

The passing offense must replace Kayshon Boutte, but LSU does return leading receiver Malik Nabers (72 receptions for 1,017 yards), Brian Thomas (five TDs on 31 catches), and tight end Mason Taylor (414 receiving yards and three TDs as a freshman). Senior Kyren Lacy (24 catches for 268 yards) is also projected to make an impact on the outside.

The Tigers defense allowed 31-plus points in five of seven SEC games last season, failing to consistently hit the quarterback and slow down the run. The experience they gained in the trenches last season should pay dividends in 2023, though. There is talent on this LSU d-line, especially at defensive tackle with Maason Smith, Mekhi Wingo, and Jordan Jefferson. They must replace pass rushers BJ Ojulari and Ali Gaye, leaving the onus to Sai’vion Jones and transfer portal additions Ovie Oghoufo (Texas), Bradyn Swinson (Oregon), and Paris Shand (Arizona).

Harold Perkins (72 tackles, 7.5 sacks, and 14 TFL), Greg Penn (77 tackles), and Oregon State transfer Omar Speights (83 tackles) give the Tigers a terrific trio at linebacker. The question is whether the secondary can live up to expectations for an LSU defensive backfield. The corners are not established stars, but Texas A&M transfer Denver Harris and SE Lousiana transfer Zy Alexander have potential. The safeties should be very good, with Syracuse transfer Duce Chestnut, Greg Brooks (63 tackles and two INTs), and Major Burns (37 tackles and 4 PBUs) patrolling.

Florida State is a talented squad, but is it elite?

Florida State is a top-ten team to start the year, building up expectations for its 2023 season. Its schedule will be challenging, though. After tangling with the Tigers, the Seminoles will travel to ninth-ranked Clemson in week four and play Miami and Florida in two of the final three weeks of the regular season. If they don't win at least three of those four, any dreams of a CFP bid will be unrealistic. FSU has a talented roster, but how will it play with a target on its back?

Sixth-year quarterback Jordan Travis is exactly what Florida State needs under center. The 6-foot-1 signal-caller passed for 3,214 yards and rushed for 417 (5.1 YPC) last season, racking up 31 total touchdowns and only five interceptions. He boosts an FSU run game that was best in the ACC and 12th nationally (217.8 yards per game). Leading rusher Trey Benson (990 rushing yards, 6.4 YPC, and nine scores) is back, along with Lawrance Toafili (457 rushing yards, 4.9 YPC, and five TDs).

The Seminoles' passing attack will be explosive. 6-foot-7 junior receiver Johnny Wilson (897 receiving yards, 20.9 YPC, and five scores) is a problem, but he's hardly the only pass-catcher to watch. South Carolina transfer tight end Jaheim Bell and Michigan State transfer WR Keon Coleman will be playing on Sundays soon, too. The O-line was already solid, but FSU added additional pieces via the transfer portal to beef it up.

Florida State isn't as talented defensively as it is on the offensive side, but it has enough playmakers to get the job done. The return of defensive end Jared Verse, a projected first-round NFL draft pick, was a victory for a Seminoles defense in need of star power. In his first season in Tallahassee, the Albany transfer accounted for nine sacks and 17 tackles-for-loss, but he wants to build on that performance with a dominant season in 2023 to boost his odds of being a top-ten pick. He's joined by Fabien Lovett and Western Michigan transfer Braden Fiske (six sacks) on a strong D-line.

The linebacking unit will be led by Tatum Bethune (84 tackles and three sacks) and Kalen DeLoach (63 tackles, three sacks, and seven PBUs), but the stars are in the secondary. With a consistent pass rush, the DBs could have a monster season. Cornerbacks Renardo Green (58 tackles and five PBUs) and Jarrian Jones (4 PBUs and one INT) are returning, and the Seminoles acquired Fentrell Cypress II (14 PBUs) from Virginia in the portal. Safety Akeem Dent (53 tackles and four PBUs) is another trusted veteran expected to make a larger impact in 2023. The unit will need to prove it, though, by recording more takeaways in 2023.

The FSU defense can be the difference in this game, as I expect both offenses to have their fair share of explosive plays on Sunday. I'm more confident the Seminoles will generate a consistent pass rush with Verse & Co., which will help the secondary cover the Tigers' receivers. The LSU running game was too reliant on Daniels last year, and that could continue to be a problem on Sunday against FSU's stout front.

I'm not as sold on the Tigers' defensive line, which lost two key pass rushers from last year's squad, which didn't generate enough pressure as it was. If that's the case, the LSU secondary will be in trouble. There is talent in the Tigers' defensive backfield, but it's lacking in experienced playmakers.
