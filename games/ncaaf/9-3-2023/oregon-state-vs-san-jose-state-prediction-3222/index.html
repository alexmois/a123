Oregon State Beavers (0-0) vs. San Jose State Spartans (0-1) 

Week 1 of the 2023 CFB season brings three games on Sunday, September 3, including the non-conference showdown from CEFCU Stadium in San Jose, California, so we have prepared the best Oregon State vs. San Jose State betting pick and odds. 

The Beavers and Spartans lock horns for the first time since 2015 when Oregon State defeated SJSU 35-21 as a 6.5-point home fave. The Beavers are 16.5-point road favorites this time around, while the totals are listed at 55.5 points.   

The Beavers look to build on their strong 2022 campaign

The Oregon State Beavers exceeded the expectations in 2022, going 10-3 and finishing fifth in the Pac-12 Conference with a 6-3 record. They also trounced the Florida Gators 30-3 in the Las Vegas Bowl to grab their first bowl victory in the Jonathan Smith era. 

The Beavers were tallying 32.2 points per game in 2022 (tied-38th in the nation). Their offense ranked 105th in passing yards (199.5 per game) and 28th in rushing yards (196.5). On the defensive side of things, Oregon State was allowing 20.0 points per contest (16th) on 224.5 passing yards (65th) and 108.2 rushing yards (15th). 

Clemson transfer D.J. Uigalelei arrives to help the quarterback poisition. He never lived up to the heights of Trevor Lawrence (the man he replaced) but does have a ton of talent.  Oregon State returns its one-two punch in senior Deshaun Fenwick and sophomore Damien Martinez. These two guys combined for 1,535 rushing yards and 14 touchdowns last season.

The Spartans opened the new season with an L

The San Jose State Spartans are coming off a 56-28 road loss at the No. 6 USC Trojans in week zero of the 2023 college football season. The Spartans battled bravely against one of the best teams in the nation and covered a massive 31.5-point spread. SJSU trailed by seven points at halftime but didn’t have enough firepower to keep it close in the second half. 

USC outgained SJSU 501-396 in total yards. The Spartans ran 27 times for a staggering 198 yards, and junior RB Quali Conley led the charge with six carries for 108 yards. Senior signal-caller Chevan Cordeiro went 21-for-38, throwing for 198 yards and three touchdowns, while junior wideout Nick Nash had six catches for 89 yards and three scores. 

The Spartans went 7-5 in 2022, including a 41-27 defeat to the Eastern Michigan Eagles in the Famous Idaho Potato Bowl. SJSU was scoring 27.4 points per game (72nd in the nation) on 272.5 passing yards (25th) and 95.8 rushing yards (123rd). The Spartans were giving up 22.0 points in return (30th) on 217.7 passing yards (52nd) and 122.3 rushing yards (29th). 

Best Bets for this Game

Full-Game Side Bet

Insiders Status:

Rating:

I think the Spartans have enough weapons to keep it close against the Beavers. SJSU’s run defense was excellent in 2022, and I’m expecting to see more of the same in 2023. The Spartans will have to slow down the Beavers’ ground game if they want to cover the spread or eventually pull off an upset in front of the home fans. 

Chevan Cordeiro had a nice 2022 season, tossing for 3,251 yards, 23 touchdowns, and six interceptions. It looks like the Spartans’ rushing will be better this season, so I’m backing SJSU to cover a 16.5-point spread in a tough matchup against Oregon State. The Beavers are a well-rounded team with an exceptional ground game, but beating a 16.5-point line should be too much, especially on the road. Oregon State is 3-6 ATS in its previous nine road contests, and SJSU is 4-1 ATS in its last five showings at home as a dog. 

Prediction: San Jose State Spartans +16.5 (-110)
