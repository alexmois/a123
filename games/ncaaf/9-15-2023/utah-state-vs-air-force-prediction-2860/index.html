Two Mountain West foes meet Friday night as the Air Force Falcons host the Utah State Aggies. The Falcons are off to a 2-0 start while the Aggies are 1-1. The Aggies have won the last two head-to-head matchups including a 34-27 win last season. Air Force is a 9-point favorite in this one. Kickoff is on Friday night at 8:00 p.m. ET at Falcon Stadium at the US Air Force Academy in Colorado.

Utah State rolls

The Utah State Aggies are off to a 1-1 start in 2023, but they have been a great bet as they are 2-0 against the spread. They lost their opener two weeks ago at 25th-ranked Iowa by the score of 24-14, but they did better than the experts thought they would as they were 24-point underdogs. They followed that up last weekend with a 78-28  beat down of Idaho State. They were prohibitive favorites, predicted to win by 24, but they far outperformed expectations, winning by a whopping 50 points.

This thing was ugly, but if you want the gory details, the Aggies rolled up 591 total yards including 380 on the ground, They also won the turnover battle, 3-0. Robert Briggs led the rushing attack with 101 yards and one TD while Rahsul Faison had 95 yards and one TD. Davon Booth added 83 yards and 2 scores. Quarterback Cooper Legas didn't have to do much heavy lifting, as he was 14 for 16 for 125 yards and 2 TDs, while backup Levi Williams was 5 for 7 for 68 yards and 2 TDs. Terrell Vaughn led all receivers with 11 catches for 73 yards and 2 TD catches. Tight end Josh Sterzer is out.

Air Force improves to 2-0

The Air Force Falcons have jumped out to a 2-0 start in 2023, but they are 0-2 against the spread. In week one, they rolled over FCS school Robert Morris by a margin of 42-7.  But they were supposed to destroy Robert Morris and they failed to cover as 47-point favorites. In week two, they barely escaped with a 13-3 win at Sam Houston State. They were 13.5-point favorites, so this was not an all that impressive win.

What was impressive was their defense. The Falcons only gave up 80 total yards, which is ridiculous, no matter who the opponent is. As is typical of an Air Force team, it is pretty much run, run, run, and run some more. They threw for just 14 yards, but ran for 254 yards in the win. Owen Burk led the way with 71 rushing yards and a touchdown while quarterback Zac Larrier ran for 65 yards. Larrier was just 1 of 3 for 14 yards through the air. Air Force has no injuries to report for Friday night's matchup.
