The Stanford Cardinal (0-0, 0-0 Pac-12) will be trying to pick up their first win of the season when they face the Hawaii Rainbow Warriors (0-1, 0-0 MW) on Friday night. The game will be played at Clarence T.C. Ching Athletics Complex and it is scheduled to begin at 11 PM. ET.

Cardinal Looking For First Win In Troy Taylor Era

The Cardinal went 3-9 last season, which led to the team parting ways with long-time head coach David Shaw, replacing him with Troy Taylor, who had a 30-8 record as the head coach of Sacramento State. They are hoping his restore the team to its past glory, but they will have to be patient with him after only five starters from last season’s team came back.

Stanford averaged 21.5 points per game last season. They threw for 254.3 yards and ran for 107.3 yards per game. They lost their starting quarterback from last season Tanner McKee and will replace him with Ari Patu and Ashton Daniels splitting time this season. E.J. Smith, whose 2022-23 season was cut short because of injuries, will be the focal point of the offense this season. The team also has tight end Benjamin Yurosek, who had 49 catches for 445 yards last season.

Stanford struggled defensively last season, giving up 32.2 points per game. They were one of the worst teams in the country when it came to stopping the run and average at stopping the pass, which are areas they will have to improve on if they want to win more than three games this season.

Rainbow Warriors Trying To Bounce Back From Loss

The Rainbow Warriors didn’t get off to the start that they wanted after losing to Vanderbilt in their first game. They will try to bounce back from the loss with a win over the Cardinal, which will give them their first win of the season.

Hawaii scored 28 points in its first game. They threw for 351 yards and rushed for 40 yards in the loss.

Brayden Schager completed 77.1 percent of his passes for 351 yards, three touchdowns, and two interceptions. Landon Sims had nine carries for 38 yards, while Pofele Ashlock had seven catches for 127 yards and one touchdown.

Hawaii didn’t play well defensively in its first game, giving up 35 points in the loss. They played well against the run, but struggled against the pass. They will have to play better if they want to win this game.
