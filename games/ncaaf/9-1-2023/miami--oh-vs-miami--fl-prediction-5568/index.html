A pair of college football programs that share a similar city name open up their season on the gridiron when the MAC tangles with the ACC down in South Beach. The Miami (OH) RedHawks are on the road as they travel down the east coast to take on the Miami Hurricanes Friday night. Miami (OH) finished the 2022 season 6-7 and ended the year with a 24-20 defeat to UAB in the Bahamas Bowl. Miami struggled in Mario Cristobal’s first season as head coach, ending the year 5-7 and failing to make a bowl game for the second straight season. These programs have met just once as FBS opponents in their respective histories. The Hurricanes steamrolled their way to a 54-3 win back on November 7, 1987, en route to a national championship.

Miami (OH) RedHawks Try to Start Season With Upset Road Win

Miami (OH) hopes for better fortunes this season as they finished the year 6-7 after falling in the Bahamas Bowl to UAB. Coach Chuck Martin looks to a team that led the conference in scoring defense (22.6 ppg) a year ago but the key player for the RedHawks is at quarterback. Brett Gabbert was MAC Freshman of the Year in 2019 but played only four games last season before suffering a broken collarbone that knocked him out for the rest of the year. The RedHawks have to keep him upright and healthy in order to bolster an offense that finished 11th in the conference in scoring offense a season ago. On the plus side, they have three returning starting offensive linemen to help keep Gabbert in games.

The RedHawks had their issues on the offensive side of the ball last season: they finished 121st in the nation in passing offense (165.5 yards per game) and 63rd in rushing (140.1 yards per game) so far. Miami (OH) was 112th in the FBS in scoring offense by putting up 20.2 points per contest and was 34th in scoring defense in the FBS by allowing 22.6 points per game. Brett Gabbert completed 74 of 115 passes for 816 yards with four touchdowns and no interceptions last season along with 55 rushing yards and a score. Aveon Smith finished 108 of 217 passing for 1,299 yards with 11 touchdowns against five interceptions along with a team-high 553 yards plus six scores on the ground on the year. Keyon Mozee was second the team on the ground with 109 carries for 471 yards plus two scores. Kevin Davis (54 carries, 285 yards, TD) and Kenny Tracy (37 carries, 153 yards) each return to contribute depth in the run game as well. Miles Marshall (24 catches, 390 yards, two TD) and tight end Jack Coldiron (24 receptions, 286 yards, TD) are the team’s returning anchors in the passing game this season. Joe Wilkins Jr. (transfer from Notre Dame), Gage Larvadain (Southeastern Louisiana) along with freshmen Javon Tracy and Que Williams will look to step up and contribute. Graham Nicholson hit 27 of 28 extra points and 18 of 23 field goals with a long of 49 last season.

Miami Hurricanes Looking for Improvement in Cristobal’s Second Season

Miami had high expectations last season after Mario Cristobal took over the reins of the program. Instead, the team dropped seven of their final 10 games after a 2-0 start, including a 45-31 loss at home to Middle Tennessee, and ended up 5-7 on the season. Miami went only 3-5 in ACC play with four of their losses in conference play coming by at least 24 points. The Hurricanes have a lot of new faces as they try to get back to contention status in the ACC but it’s going to be tough sledding. Miami brings back just a handful of starters on both sides of the ball and they hope that they can find enough contributors to improve after last season.

Last season, the Hurricanes were 59th in the nation in passing offense as they average 239 yards through the air and stand 95th in rushing offense by picking up 128.1 yards per game on the ground. Miami was 96th in the FBS in scoring offense by putting up 23.8 points per game while the team stands tied for 66th in scoring defense as they allow 26.8 points per contest. Tyler Van Dyke was 160 of 253 passing for 1,844 yards with 10 touchdowns against five interceptions last season. Jacurri Brown was 27 of 45 for 230 yards with three touchdowns and three interceptions while adding 223 rushing yards to provide depth at the spot. Henry Parrish Jr. led the way on the ground as he had 131 carries for 617 yards plus four scores on the season. He’ll be relied on heavily as the team saw Jaylan Knighton (SMU) and Thad Franklin Jr. (UL Monroe) both transfer. Brashard Smith (33 catches, 308 yards), Colbie Young (32 grabs, 376 yards, five TD) and Frank Ladson Jr. (27 catches, 298 yards, TD) along with Xavier Restrepo (21 grabs, 240 yards, two TD) provide valuable returning options in the passing game this season. Andres Borregales hit all 32 extra point attempts and 17 of 20 field goal attempts with a long of 49 last season.

Now, the Hurricanes may well roll their way to a victory here, but the fact remains that they have to prove that last season was an aberration as opposed to the norm. Bringing back only three starters on the offensive side of the ball could make for a slow start for the Hurricanes until they get up to speed. Factor in that the Hurricanes were just 2-10 ATS last season and the safe bet is to take the points and the RedHawks in this contest.
