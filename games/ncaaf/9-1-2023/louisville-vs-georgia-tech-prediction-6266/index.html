Atlantic Coast Conference football action kicks off in week one of the college football season with the Georgia Tech Yellowjackets hosting the Louisville Cardinals on Friday, Sept. 1. Louisville was 8-5 overall and 4-4 in the ACC a year ago, while Georgia Tech was 5-7 overall and 4-4 in the conference last season. Both will be looking to move up this season. Kickoff for this Aflac Kickoff Game is Friday night at 7:30 p.m. at Mercedes Benz Stadium in Atlanta. Its a neutral site, but closer to Georgia Tech than Louisville.

Louisville retools with new coach, QB

Louisville fans have to be happy with how they finished last season. They were 8-5 overall and 4-4 in the ACC and they finished on a high note, beating Cincinnati to win the Fenway Bowl. While last year's coach Scott Satterfield has moved on, to Cincinnati of all places, the Cardinals are excited about their first-year head coach Jeff Brohm, who comes over from Purdue, where he was 36-34 in six seasons and made three Bowl game appearances.  Louisville has a big hole to fill at QB replacing Malik Cunningham. He threw for 1,568 yards and 8 touchdowns and ran for 560 yards and 12 touchdowns for the Cardinals last season.

But they are very hopeful about the potential of new quarterback Jack Plummer, who played with Brohm at Purdue and then moved on to Cal. Last season at Cal he threw for 3,119 yards and 21 touchdowns with 9 interceptions. Look for a more pass-happy attack this year as compared to last year. Plummer may be throwing a lot to transfer Jamari Thrash, who came in from Georgia State. Thrash had 61 catches and led the Sun Belt with 1,122 receiving yards, with 7 touchdown receptions.  Also, Kevin Coleman came in from Jackson State, Jadon Thompson transferred from Cincinnati, and Jimmy Calloway joined the wideout corps from Tennessee. Also, the running game should be solid with Jawhar Jordan, who had 815 yards rushing and 4 TDs, carrying the load.

Georgia Tech also has a new QB

The Georgia Tech Yellowjackets were solid last season in coach Brent Key's first year there, going 5-7 overall and 4-4 in the ACC. But the team has the potential to improve upon that with some new key additions. The offense was anemic last season as the Jackets only averaged 17.2 points per game, which was 127th in the nation. They were 117th in total yards, 109th in passing yards, and 93rd in rushing yards, so there wasn't much production anywhere. That could change with the arrival of Haynes King at quarterback. King played the last three years at Texas A&M and has been named the Yellowjackets starter to open the season.

King put up solid numbers last season with the Aggies, throwing for 1,220 yards in 6 starts with 7 TDs and 6 interceptions. But he also played against top 20 competition each and every game in the SEC. He won't face the same kind of defenses playing in the ACC. He's also got some new weapons, as his main receivers will be Dominick Blaylock, who transferred from Georgia, and Christian Leary, who joined Tech from Alabama. It will also help that he has four starters on the offensive line coming back from last year. On defense, Tech was terrible against the run last season, ranking 110th, but they should get some help with transfer linebackers Braelen Oliver from Minnesota and Andre White from Texas A&M joining the defense.

Full-Game Side Bet

Insiders Status:

Rating:

These were two pretty evenly matched teams from a year ago, in fact, they finished with the same record in the ACC. They have not met one another since 2020, but Georgia Tech has the upper hand, winning the last two head-to-head matchups. There are a lot of new pieces on both of these teams, and these pieces could take a few games to start to click. This is why you often see upsets in the first week of the season, and I think we'll see one here. Louisville has a new coach and a new quarterback, as well as a slew of new receivers. Tech has a new QB, but he is a veteran of SEC wars, and the coach has a year at Tech under his belt. Also, playing closer to home should give them an edge, at least to cover the 7.5-point spread. Take Georgia Tech to cover.

Prediction: Georgia Tech +7.5

Full-Game Total Pick

Insiders Status:

Rating:

As laid out above there are a lot of new pieces on both of these teams, in key offensive positions, so it could take some time to gel.
