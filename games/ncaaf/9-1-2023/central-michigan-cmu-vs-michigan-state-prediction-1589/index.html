The Central Michigan Chippewas and Michigan State Spartans will kick off their 2023 season schedules on Friday at Spartan Stadium. Kickoff from East Lansing, MI, is at 7:00 p.m. ET.

CMU has an over/under win total of 5.5 games. The Chippewas, who will also play New Hampshire, Notre Dame, and South Alabama in non-con play, were picked to finish fourth in the Mid-American Conference's West division in the head coaches' preseason poll. Central Michigan was 4-8 overall, including 2-4 on the road, last season.

MSU also has an over/under win total of 5.5 games. The Spartans, who will play Richmond and Washington in their other non-conference games, were picked fifth in the Big Ten East division in the preseason poll. Michigan State was 5-7 overall, including 4-3 at home, last season.

The Spartans are 14-point spread favorites and the game total is 45 points.

The CMU offense needs to make strides in 2023

Central Michigan hopes to bounce back in 2023 after a disappointing 4-8 season last year. The Chippewas offense turned the ball over too often (-18 turnover ratio), failed to convert third downs (35.2% conversion rate), and couldn't move the ball through the air (208.1 yards per game). Fixing that by week one could be wishful thinking, especially following quarterback Daniel Richardson's transfer to Florida Atlantic and the graduation of leading rusher Lew Nichols.

Head coach Jim McElwain, who was successful in previous stops at Colorado State and Florida, feels confident his team will turn things around.

“We need to do better than we did a year ago, and we’re going to do that,” McElwain said. “I’m really excited about our team. I’m excited about the guys that went through what we went through a year ago and are ready to not let that happen again.”

The Chips will turn to either sophomore QB Jase Bauer (435 passing yards, one TD, and five INTs) or redshirt freshman Bert Emanuel Jr. to lead the offense this season. Bauer has a bit more experience throwing the ball around but Emanuel did get his feet wet in the final four games of the year, albeit mostly on the ground. He led CMU in rushing yards per carry (7.4) and rushing touchdowns (seven), gashing the Buffalo defense for 293 rushing yards and three TDs on November 9. Utilizing that dual-threat capability will be a focus for the Chippewas, especially with Nichols' departure and a lack of established receiving threats.

The Central Michigan defense is talented on all three levels but will need playmakers to create turnovers (four INTs in 2023) and generate pressure, as linebacker Thomas Incoom (11.5 sacks) is playing on Sundays. Junior LBs Kyle Moretti (92 tackles) and Justin Whiteside (67 tackles) will be counted on to provide stability, along with senior DTs Robi Stuart (31 tackles) and Jacques Bristol (34 tackles). Junior safety Trey Jones (85 tackles and two INTs), sophomore nickel De’Javion Stepney (40 tackles), and junior cornerback Donte Kent (51 tackles and 15 passes defended) lead an experienced CMU secondary.

Michigan State has a chip on its shoulder

Michigan State took a step back in 2022, failing to build momentum on an 11-win season capped off with a Peach Bowl victory. The departure of running back Kenneth Walker III to the NFL stung, as QB Payton Thorne regressed without a complementary run game (113.2 yards per game), passing for fewer yards and eight fewer touchdowns. With head coach Mel Tucker opting for an open QB competition, Thorne bolted for Auburn, giving MSU a fresh face under center in 2023.

Tucker has decided he will not announce a starter at QB for week one, so we'll have to wait and see who takes the field for the green and white on Friday night. The competitors are redshirt junior Noah Kim, who was part of previous HC Mark Dantonio's final recruiting class, and ballyhooed redshirt freshman Katin Houser, a four-star recruit out of California powerhouse St. John Bosco. Neither brings significant game experience, but Kim has a leg up on Houser due to his four years of experience in the system. He is the more mobile QB of the two, while Houser possesses the stronger arm.

The Spartans' ground game could be explosive, as the unit returns leading rusher Jalen Berger (636 yards, 4.6 yards-per-carry, and six TDs) and welcomes three playmakers: Nathan Carter (UConn), Jaren Mangham (USF), and speedy freshman Jaelon Barbarin. The offensive line returns several experienced upperclassmen and a couple of high-upside newcomers in Brandon Baldwin and Keyshawn Blackstock. The MSU receiving group should impress, even without Jayden Reed and Keon Coleman, as there's a nice mix of speed, size, and athleticism. Speaking of athleticism, watch out for tight end Maliq Carr — with a breakout season, he'll be on the NFL's radar.

Michigan State's defense benefits from the return of defense end Khris Bogle, linebackers Jacoby Windmon (5.5 sacks and six forced fumbles) and Aaron Brule (four sacks), and cornerback Charles Brantley (48 tackles and six passes defended). Windmon will reap the benefits of a deeper, bigger line, which will allow him to shift back to his natural position of linebacker.

He and Cal Haladay (120 tackles) give MSU a couple of thumping, run stoppers, while Brule brings in situational pass-rushing skills. The Spartans will be stout against the run after bringing in several big bodies in Tunmise Adeleye (Texas A&M), Jalen Sami (Colorado), Jarrett Jackson (Florida State), and Dre Butler (Liberty). The question will be whether they can do a better job defending the pass. Their linebackers struggled in coverage last season and the defensive backs need to be more consistent.

Michigan State's defense will shine in this game, as Central Michigan does not possess a passing game capable of putting the Spartans on their heels. Bauer struggled with interceptions last year and Emanuel has completed just four passes in his collegiate career. Should the latter get most of the reps, Michigan State will hone in on the run, with its defensive line and linebackers overpowering the Chippewas' offensive line. The Spartans' pass rush could also shine on Friday.

The MSU offense shouldn't have too many issues moving the football, no matter who starts at QB. My guess is Kim's knowledge of the offense and dual-threat ability get him the start, with Houser coming in for a series or two in relief if the score allows. Expect the home team to rely on its stable of running backs to open up the pass, opting for a hot-hand approach at RB. Carter has drawn rave reviews in camp for his big-play ability and Mangham/Berger are goal-line threats, as are big-body pass-catchers Carr and wide receiver Christian Fitzpatrick.

The Spartans have one of the toughest schedules in the nation, with games against Washington, Ohio State, Michigan, and Penn State. On Friday, any worries about those matchups will seem miles away, as MSU will cover the 14-point spread against in-state foe CMU.

Full-Game Total Pick

Rating:

I'm betting on a lower-scoring game between these in-state competitors on Friday.

The Spartans are incorporating a new quarterback into their offense and may rely on the run game to move the football. Until we've seen otherwise, we should expect an old-school Big Ten approach from MSU. That also means it will need to be locked in defensively.

Given CMU's inexperience at QB and WR, it would surprise me if the Chippewas came out throwing the football all over the field in week one. Emanuel proved he can be a threat on the ground last November, but this Spartans defense is built to limit opponents' rushing attacks. MSU already had a few talented DTs, but after experiencing too many injuries along the line in 2022, it added several more big bodies via the transfer portal.

I'm predicting a 28-13 victory for MSU.
