#307 Ohio U vs.
#308 San Diego State

The San Diego State Aztecs play host to the Ohio Bobcats in a season opener from Snapdragon Stadium on Saturday evening. Ohio had one of their better seasons in recent memory last season, going 10-4 overall, setting the tone for a strong 2023. For the Aztecs, they somehow worked their way into a bowl game but a 7-6 record continues to leave quite a bit to be desired. With this being the first matchup of the season for both sides, as well as coming a week earlier than usual, it'll be interesting to see how these teams are able to shake off the rust early in week zero.

Bobcats Ready For Road Tilt

Ohio had a great turnaround last season and winning double-digit games turned quite a few heads, especially in the MAC. Going across the country on the road in week one, is likely to be a problem, though the Bobcats showed quite a bit of resolve on the road last season. They won their last three road games and even won their bowl game at a neutral site. They won't be daunted having to head to San Diego even if this is going to present more of a challenge than those MAC matchups. Injuries are a slight concern but with another year of continuity under a staff that continues to build confidence, they have to believe that they have the plan in place to overcome some of those worries. With huge expectations for the season, starting it off with a road win is a must.

The offensive front and injury front are intertwined in this one, while all eyes are on Kurtis Rourke, last year's star at quarterback. He went down with and ACL injury with only a few games left in his MAC Conference Player of the Year campaign and while the Bobcats still looked impressive without him, he has the ability to turn this game on its head. He tossed 25 touchdowns against only four interceptions last season, though he might have to shake off quite a bit of rust in this one. Adding in All-MAC wide receiver Sam Wiglusz, 1000-yard back Sieh Bangura, and O'Shaan Allison returning to the backfield, and this offense will be in good shape.

On the defensive end, this team returns quite a bit of talent as well. Keye Thompson is a menace at linebacker, while their pass rush is dangerous. Against a San Diego State team that struggled in pass protection last season and lost their top offensive talent, Ohio's edge rushers can make it a long night, even though on the road. The concern is while the pass rush is likely to remain explosive, stopping the run could haunt them in this one.

Aztecs Trying To Turn Page on 2022

San Diego State remains one of the better Group of 5 programs but after a down year for their standards, they have their sights set on 2023 being more memorable. They still finished with a winning record at 7-6 but it was a struggle getting to that win total, even despite not having the most difficult of schedules. This year has them slated to finish near the middle of the Mountain West but even a few changes in fortune could have them looking near the top of the conference. Ohio is going to be a great challenge for them but getting them at home will be beneficial and set the tone for the season. Brady Hoke is one of the more experienced coaches in college football and he'll have the Aztecs ready to go in the opener.

On the offensive end, San Diego State returns a dynamic quarterback as well, as Jalen Mayden will return under center. A mid-season position change saw him switch from the defensive side to the most important position, putting up over 2,000 yards passing and 12 touchdowns. With a full preseason under his belt, he should only be sharper and more confident this year. One to keep an eye on is how the wide receiver room grows with their quarterback, while a more efficient run game is needed to create a balance that was missing last season.

The defensive side of things has been San Diego State's bread and butter for a few years now. Last year certainly saw them find success against most opponents but the struggles against the top teams were a concern, which leaves questions as to how they'll respond against the Bobcats. Hoke will have this side of the ball coached up well but with losses in the pass rush, at linebacker, and in Cal transfer Patrick McMorris, there are holes to be filled. Talent remains but week one could see them forced to cobble together defensive stops.

Ohio will go on to have a strong season in the MAC but this will be a difficult matchup for them. To start, having to travel across the country is going to be a challenge, while doing so in the season opener, when already working on shaking off the rust, is only going to add to those issues. Beyond that, Ohio has a quarterback coming off of an ACL injury and it remains to be seen how he'll be ready for action as well. On the other hand, the Aztecs found success with Mayden under center last year, despite him having shifted his focus to the defensive side of the ball for training camp and to open the season. With a full camp under his belt now, there are far fewer unknowns surrounding his ability to find success, than that with Rourke on the other side. The Bobcats might hang around early but with so much working against them, they won't have the offensive or defensive firepower to keep up in the second-half in this one.
