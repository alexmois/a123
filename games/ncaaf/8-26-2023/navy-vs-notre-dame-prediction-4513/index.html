The Navy Midshipmen and the #13th ranked Notre Dame Fighting Irish will kick off their season on Saturday at Aviva Stadium in Dublin, Ireland. This will be the third time the two teams will meet in what was originally dubbed the Emerald Isle Classic and the eighth time since 1988 that Ireland has hosted a Division 1 College Football game. Navy finished 4-8 in 2022 and parted ways with long-time head coach Ken Niumatalolo after the season. Former defensive coordinator Brian Newberry now takes over as the head coach. The Fighting Irish will be in their second season under head coach Marcus Freeman after finishing 9-4 in 2022 and defeating South Carolina in the Gator Bowl. The opening kickoff is set for 2:30 PM EST.

New Era

The Naval Academy begins the Brian Newberry Era in earnest this Saturday as they get set to square off against the #13th ranked Fighting Irish to kick off their 2023 season. Newberry inherits a team that went 4-8 last year, including a 20-17 loss to arch-rival Army to end the season. The Midshipmen will once again feature an option offense and Newberry will have the advantage of three of his five starting offensive linemen from last season returning. Navy also has a new OC this year in Grant Chesnut, but he brings with him very similar tendencies to what the Mids are used to running. They might, however, be more willing to put the ball in the air this season than they have in the past. Tai Lavatai, who started eight games last year before a season-ending knee injury, and Blake Horvath are both expected to see time at quarterback this season. Horvath is the more explosive runner of the two while Lavatai will be charged with getting the tough yardage and may be asked to throw more in the game.

Defensively, Navy returns seven starters from last season, three from the defensive line, and two each from the linebacking crew and secondary. The defensive line is the strength of the team and that line helped Navy to finish third nationally in rushing defense last season. With Newberry as head coach moving over from defensive coordinator, expect much of his schemes to remain intact for the Midshipmen. Navy will not be intimidated by the Fighting Irish on Saturday after nearly pulling off a huge upset last season before falling 35-32. Notre Dame jumped out to a 35-13 lead at halftime before Navy battled back to outscore the Irish 19-0 in the second half.

Newberry will enter Saturday's game with a chance to be the first Navy coach in team history to defeat a ranked opponent in his first game. Saturday's August 26th start will mark the earliest date ever that Navy has started a college football season.

 

 

Irish Eyes are Smiling

Notre Dame head coach Marcus Freeman hopes to start his second season better than his first season. Freeman started his coaching career at 0-2 following consecutive losses to Ohio State and then a shocking home loss to Marshall. The team rebounded to win nine of its final 11 games, including a bowl win over South Carolina. Notre Dame brings back a ton of talent, particularly on offense. Leading rusher Audric Estime returns after rushing for 920 yards and 11 touchdowns last year and All-American offensive tackle Joe Alt is back to lead a powerful offensive line. The biggest addition, however, will come at quarterback. Heisman Trophy candidate Sam Hartman transferred to Notre Dame from Wake Forest this offseason. Hartman holds the ACC record for touchdown passes with 110 and has thrown for over 300 yards in a game 21 times in his career. Another transfer, WR Kaleb Smith from Virginia Tech, might end up the leading receiver in what will be a very inexperienced wide receiver group this year. That experience will be masked by the leadership of Hartman.

Freeman, the former defensive coordinator at Notre Dame, will be tasked with slowing down the opposition enough to allow his offense to do their thing. Notre Dame does return its top two tacklers from last season in linebackers JD Bertrand and Jack Kiser. The defensive line will be a concern with the team losing all three of its top linemen from last year including Notre Dame's career leader in sacks, Isaiah Foskey. Cornerback Benjamin Morrison leads a relatively experienced secondary that may not be tested in the opener against a Navy team that relies heavily on the run. After a first year spent having to regroup after an 0-2 start and then another upset loss to Stanford, Freeman will surely have his team ready to go from the outset this year in what should be a team that has a shot at earning a playoff spot.

Full-Game Side Bet

Insiders Status:

Rating:

While I expect Notre Dame to come out of this opener with a win, I think the Midshipmen can make a game out of this. Navy was one of the best run-stopping teams in the nation last year and returns their entire front line in 2023. Their ability to stop the run will make Notre Dame far more predictable and force Hartman to beat them strictly through the air. While Hartman will certainly put up numbers, he may not get the amount of opportunities he normally does thanks to what I expect to be a ball-control Navy offense. Navy returns four of their starting offensive lineman from last season and should be able to win the battle up front against a very inexperienced front line for Notre Dame. While Fighting Irish linebackers Bertrand and Kiser will make plays, they need the defensive line to hold up in order to clear a path for them. That might be an issue in this game as the young Notre Dame line adjusts to the veteran Midshipmen. Last season, Navy fell behind early and had to play catch-up the entire second half, including having to throw the ball more than they are comfortable doing, and nearly pulled off the upset. While I don't think this game will feature any last-second heroics, I do think a three-touchdown line is a bit high given the way that Navy can control the line of scrimmage and slow the game down.

Take Navy with the points.

Prediction: Navy +20.5

Full-Game Total Pick

Insiders Status:

Rating:

The total has bumped up a point and a half since it opened at 49. That movement is more than likely due to the veteran presence of Hartman at quarterback for Notre Dame. Hartman will certainly make his mark for the Irish as he attempts to strengthen his resume in what he hopes will be a pro football career after this season. I do, however, think he will struggle a bit out of the gate as he adjusts to a relatively inexperienced group of receivers. He will make the necessary adjustments as the season goes along but it will be a lot to ask of Hartman to put up huge numbers on Saturday. Additionally, as I noted above, Navy will do its best to play ball control in this game and keep the score manageable. Also, keep in mind that Navy went under this total in each of their first four games last season while Notre Dame went under this total in two of their first three games. There will be bigger scoring days ahead for the Irish and this offense but Saturday will not be one of those days.

Take the under.
