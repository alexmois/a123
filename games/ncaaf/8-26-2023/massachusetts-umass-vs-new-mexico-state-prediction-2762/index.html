After a seven-month layoff, college football is back this weekend as the 2023 season gets underway with a non-conference matchup between one of the nation’s remaining independent programs and one from Conference USA on the gridiron in the Land of Enchantment. The UMass Minutemen are on the road as they kick off their season with a contest against the New Mexico State Aggies on Saturday night. UMass finished the 2022 season with a dismal 1-11 record under first-year coach Don Brown, continuing their struggles since they transitioned to a FBS program back in 2012. New Mexico State was 7-6 in 2022, capped by a 24-19 win over Bowling Green in the Quick Lane Bowl, and look to take a step forward in their first season as a member of Conference USA. These programs have met twice previously as FBS opponents with the Aggies winning both meetings. That includes a 23-13 road win in the most recent meeting on October 29, 2022.

UMass Minutemen Hoping for Offensive Improvement

UMass stumbled to a 1-11 mark in 2022, marking the fourth straight season where they failed to win more than one game. The Minutemen have yet to post a winning season since their return to FBS in 2012 with their high-water mark back-to-back 4-8 campaigns under Mark Whipple in 2017 and 2018. There is good news and bad news for UMass this season as they start their second season under Brown. The good news is that they bring back eight starters defensively from a unit that finished ninth in pass defense (175.2 ypg) and 12th in third-down defense (30.9%) last season. On the flip side, there is no clear cut quarterback option for the Minutemen, which is troubling given that the team was dead last in the FBS in scoring offense last season. They were held to 10 points or less in seven of their 12 games while tallying at least 24 points just twice.

Last season, UMass was 125th in passing offense with 116.9 yards per contest while they were 67th in rushing offense with 148.8 yards per game. The Minutemen finished 131st (or dead last) in the FBS in scoring offense with an average of 12.5 points per game while they were a dismal 103rd in scoring defense by allowing 31.1 points per contest. Georgia Tech transfer Taisun Phommachanh, who spent four years in the ACC (three with Clemson), was named the starter under center, at least for week one. He is 24 of 53 passing for 217 yards with one touchdown and four interceptions while adding 206 yards and three scores on the ground. Phommachanh beat out Carlos Davis and Brady Olson (77 of 153, 791 yards, two TD, eight INT, 46 rush yards) for the job. Gino Campiotti (33 of 74, 257 yards, TD, six INT, 102 carries, 390 yards, three TD) started the year on the depth chart for QB but has converted to tight end. Kay’Ron Adams is the team’s lead back after he ran the ball 84 times for 297 yards and a score last season. George Johnson III is the team’s leading returning receiver after he caught 28 balls for 402 yards plus a score last year. Shawn Harris Jr. (26 receptions, 431 yards, TD for Stony Brook) and Isaac Ross (21 catches, 195 yards, TD) provide other experienced options in the passing game for the Minutemen. Cameron Carson connected on all 13 extra point attempts and 13 of 18 field goal attempts with a long of 47 last season as he returns to handle the kicking duties.

Aggies Look to Build On Stretch Run of 2022 Season

New Mexico State closed 2022 strong, winning six of their final seven games, including the bowl win over Bowling Green, to finish 7-6. The Aggies last had a stretch where they had won six of seven back in 1966 and the team hopes to carry that success over as they transition to Conference USA. New Mexico State, for the first time in a long time, has continuity going for them. The Aggies bring back their entire coaching staff from a season ago, along with 97% of their production from the run game, 100% of their rushing scores, 71% of their receiving yards and 83% of their receiving touchdowns. They also bring back four starting offensive linemen so if the defense can step up and perform, the Aggies could surprise in 2023.

In 2022, the Aggies were 119th in the nation in passing offense with 169.2 yards per game while they were 56th in rushing offense with 161.5 yards per contest. New Mexico State finished 81st in the FBS in scoring offense as they put up an average of 25.5 points per game while they were 46th in scoring defense by allowing 23.9 points per contest. Diego Pavia was 101 of 190 passing for 1,450 yards with 13 touchdowns against five interceptions while adding 508 yards and six scores on the ground. Gavin Frakes was 55 of 110 passing for 736 yards with four touchdowns and seven interceptions along with 143 rushing yards plus two scores, giving the Aggies an experienced #2 QB. Star Thomas led the team with 107 carries for 518 yards plus five scores on the ground last season. Jamoni Jones (111 carries, 383 yards, six TD) and Tim Gans (26 carries, 127 yards, TD) are also in the mix for the Aggies. Kordell David (24 grabs, 343 yards, four TD), Jonathan Brady (23 receptions, 310 yards, three TD) and Bryce Childress (17 catches, 212 yards, TD) provide the Aggies with three of their top four pass catchers from last season. Ethan Albertson hit 30 of 31 extra points and seven of 10 field goal attempts with a long of 45 last season.

UMass struggled mightily on the offensive side of the ball last season and things don’t seem all that promising at the start of 2023 either. Sure, they added Phommachanh, a guy who has experience from being in the ACC for four years, but he has limited experience when it comes to reps. Throw in that they don’t have Ellis Merriweather anymore to do work on the ground and it makes things that much more challenging for the Minutemen. New Mexico State brings back almost all of their offensive production from a season ago, which gives them an opportunity to build on last season’s late success. Now, the defense will take some time to jell with so many new faces but facing a team like the Minutemen with their inept offense, works in their favor. Playing at home, in a nationally televised game, fires up the Aggies, as they beat the Minutemen for the third straight year.
