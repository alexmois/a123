#305 San Jose State vs.
#306 Southern California (USC)

The San José State Spartans and the Southern California Trojans (-31) will kick off their 2023 seasons on Saturday at Los Angeles Memorial Coliseum. Kickoff is at 5:00 p.m. PT. The game total is 66.5 points.

San José State has an over/under of 5.5 wins this season. The Spartans will open their 2023 campaign with consecutive games against ranked opponents, as they will host #18 Oregon State after they clash with the Trojans. They were 7-5 last year and won 24 games the past four seasons.

USC has an over/under of ten wins in 2023. The Trojans, who are playing their final season as a member of the Pacific-12 Conference, have four ranked squads on their schedule: Notre Dame, Utah, Washington, and Oregon. They were 11-3 last year but didn't finish on a high note, losing in the Pac-12 Championship and the Cotton Bowl.

Will the Spartans' pass rush take a step back in 2023?

The Spartans have some holes to fix on both sides of the football, but they do benefit from the return of an experienced quarterback. Chevan Cordeiro is back for his sixth season of college football, all of which have been spent in the Mountain West Conference. The ex-Hawaii gunslinger passed for 3,251 yards and 23 touchdowns and rushed for nine more scores last season for SJSU. He leads a Spartans offense that returns four starters on the offensive line but needs improvement in opening holes for the ground game. If they can, running back Kairee Robinson (157 carries for 752 yards and ten TDs) is capable of taking advantage. The San José State passing game paced the Mountain West last year and should be solid again despite losing most of the team's top receivers from a season ago.

The defense lacks depth up front, as Cade Hall (7.5 sacks) and Viliami Fehoko (9.0 sacks) are out of eligibility. The best pass-rushing team in the MWC last season should have some suitable replacements in the wings, however. SJSU's linebacking unit will be solid with Byrun Parham (74 tackles) leading the way and the secondary will be tough to throw against. Safety Tre Jenkins (two INTs) and former USC safety Chase Williams (two INTs) are two players to watch in the defensive backfield.

Last season, San José State scored 27.5 points per game (74th), averaging 264.0 passing yards (34th) and 92.6 rushing yards (125th). The defense surrendered 20.3 points per game (22nd) and 334.4 total yards (26th), including 210.4 passing yards (45th) and 124.0 rushing yards (29th).

Can Caleb Williams take USC to the promised land?

There are sky-high expectations this season in Los Angeles for the USC football team. Ex-Oklahoma Sooners head coach Lincoln Riley has retooled the Trojans roster, amassing a collection of talent the program hasn't featured in quite some time.

Southern California will go as far as 2022 Heisman Trophy winner Caleb Williams can take them. The junior signal-caller passed for 4,537 yards and 42 touchdowns in 2022, adding 442 yards and six scores on the ground. To make the four-team College Football Playoff, though, he'll need to elevate the players around him. The Trojans lost star wide receiver Jordan Addison to the NFL and Kyle Ford transferred to rival UCLA, but there will be no shortage of weapons at Williams' disposal.

Among the team's notable transfers are running back MarShawn Lloyd (5.2 yards per carry with South Carolina) and receiver Dorian Singer (1,105 receiving yards with Arizona), who will bring playmaking experience to the 2023 USC squad. Up-and-comers Zachariah Branch and Raleek Brown were highly coveted recruits and tight end Duce Robinson has serious potential. They'll play behind an offensive line that was retooled in the offseason, as three starters arrived via the transfer portal.

The SC defense needs to make serious strides in 2023 after allowing 6.5 yards per play (8th-worst in the FBS) last year. The Trojans led the nation in turnover margin with an impressive 29 takeaways, but it still wasn't good enough. The defensive line looks brand new, with Georgia transfer Bear Alexander headlining the group of newcomers. The linebackers and secondary, however, return plenty of experience and playmaking ability. A few names to watch include LB Shane Lee (78 tackles), LB Eric Gentry (71 tackles), S Max Williams (two INTs), and S Calen Bullock (five INTs).

The Trojans offense averaged 500.5 total yards (5th) and 41.1 points per game (3rd) in 2022, including 325.7 passing yards (5th) and 174.3 rushing yards (51st). The defense allowed 415.1 total yards (101st) and 27.9 points per game (81st), including 266.5 passing yards (112th) and 148.6 rushing yards (62nd).

Best Bets for this Game

Full-Game Side Bet

Insiders Status:

Rating:

The Spartans are talented and experienced enough to cover the spread, especially in Week 0. SJSU's top-flight passing attack can keep this game close for a little, attacking a USC defense that has a lot to prove in 2023. The Trojans defense won't have to win the game, and with a litany of transfers taking the field for the first time, I'm not predicting they will be a polished unit on every possession.

The Spartans' biggest defeat last year was 43-27 vs. San Diego State. I believe they'll be flying a little too under the radar in Week 0 and won't hesitate to hold the Trojans accountable. USC may score early and often, giving its backups more playing time than expected, too. This combination of factors works in San José State bettors' favor, and that is the side I will be on this weekend.

Prediction: Spartans +31

Full-Game Total Pick

Insiders Status:

Rating:

When the layman sees this matchup, he rushes to bet the over. The Spartans and Trojans both feature explosive passing offenses, which should result in tons of scoring. That's baked into the line, which has been increased from 63.5. Instead, I'll bet on the under and celebrate when it cashes!

Williams may be the headliner, but all eyes will be on the SC defense this season. The unit will play an integral role in the development this team must make to crash the CFP and sustain its success in the rugged Big Ten. Shootouts are exciting, but elite programs can play on both sides of the ball. I expect the Trojans to be disciplined and locked in defensively, especially early. It may not always look pretty, but they'll make the plays when it counts.

Besides, should USC get out to a comfortable lead, do you expect Riley to keep Williams in the game? I'm predicting an early exit from the Heisman winner, turning this game into a glorified scrimmage down the stretch. This could help the Spartans cover the spread, just as it can keep the total under 67 points.

Prediction: Under 66.5
