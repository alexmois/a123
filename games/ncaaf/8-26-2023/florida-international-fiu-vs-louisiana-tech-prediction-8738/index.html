Students are returning to campuses across America this week and college football returns to the gridiron as the Louisiana Tech Bulldogs host the Florida International University (FIU) Panthers under the Saturday night lights. Both of these teams struggled in 2022, posting identical records of 2-6 in the conference. FIU was 4-8 overall while La. Tech was 3-9 overall. Both will be looking to get off to good starts this season. Kickoff is at 9:00 p.m. ET at Joe Aillet Stadium in Ruston, La.

Panthers look to improve upon last season

The Florida International Panthers are looking to turn the page in 2022 after a difficult 2022 season where they finished 4-8 overall and 2-6 in Conference USA. They got off to a good start in coach Mike McIntyre's rookie season, going 4-4, but then they ended the season with four straight losses. The offense was not good a year ago, as they averaged only 18.7 points per game, which ranked 122nd out of about 133 Division 1 teams. Unfortunately, the defense was not good either, allowing 37.3 points per game, which ranked 129th in the country. So what do the Panthers look like in 2023? Let's take a look.

The Panthers were hit hard by the transfer portal as they lost their leading receiver from a year ago, Tyrese Chambers, who went to Maryland, and their top tight end, Rivaldo Fairweather, who is now an Auburn Tiger. But Jalen Bracey, who had 39 catches for 294 yards is back, as is Kris Mitchell, who had 23 grabs for 348 yards. Dean Patterson, who had 17 receptions for 264 yards will also play a key role for the Panthers this season. The quarterback duties will be handled by Grayson James, who had 1,962 passing yards, 11 touchdowns, with 11 interceptions a year ago. If he stumbles, they may lean on Keyone Jenkins, a transfer from Miami Central. This past season for Miami Central, Jenkins tossed for 1,101 yards with 14 touchdowns and just two interceptions.  In the backfield, the Panthers suffered a huge blow, losing leading rusher Lexington Joseph to a torn ACL in the offseason. So look for South Dakota transfer Shomari Lawrence to get most of the snaps.

Tech has a lot of firepower

The Louisiana Tech Bulldogs were mired in the basement of Conference USA last season, along with FIU, as they were 3-9 overall and 2-6 in the conference. Their offense was pretty good, averaging 29 points per game, which ranked 62nd in the nation, led by a passing attack that gained 267 yards per game, 32nd in the nation. The problem was the defense, which gave up 37.9 points per game. They ranked 130th out of 133 teams nationally in points allowed. They also have a second-year coach in Sonny Cumbie. Will Cumbie lead the Bulldogs to a better season in year two?

There is a lot of excitement at Louisiana Tech to get back on a winning track in 2023, mainly because of its high-profile transfer quarterback. The Bulldogs brought in Hank Bachmeier from Boise State to run the offense, and he had some impressive seasons for the Broncos before stumbling last season and getting replaced. In 29 starts for the Broncos, Bachmeier threw for 6,605 yards with 41 touchdowns, 19 interceptions and a 62% completion percentage. Bachmeier won't have last year's leading receiver Tre Harris to throw to, as he transferred to Oklahoma, but he does have two impressive wideouts in Smoke Harris, who caught 66 passes for 640 yards and five touchdowns last year, and Cyrus Allen, who caught 22 passes for 500 yards and four touchdowns. They also got a high-profile transfer from Nebraska, receiver Decoldest Crawford, who missed last year due to injury. In addition, last year's leading rusher, Marquis Crosby, ran for 918 yards and nine touchdowns a year ago and was named third-team All-CUSA.

Best Bets for this Game

Full-Game Side Bet

Insiders Status:

Rating:

Both of these teams struggled last season under rookie head coaches, but the Louisiana Tech Bulldogs clearly had the better offseason, bringing in a top-notch quarterback in Bachmeier from Boise State and a promising receiver in Crawford from Nebraska. They also have more talent coming back, including two top receivers and their leading running back. Tech could seriously challenge for the Conference USA title so they should get off to a good start here. With the firepower they have, giving up 11.5 points doesn't seem like much. And for the Panthers, this could be another long year as they lost some key pieces. Take Louisiana Tech to cover the spread.

Prediction: Louisiana Tech -11.5

Full-Game Total Pick

Insiders Status:

Rating:

The total here is set pretty high, but it is warranted given the two teams who will be taking the field. These were two of the worst defenses in college football last season and while there may be some improvements on the margins, they are still porous defensive units. The Bulldogs like to play fast and air it out and they have a lot of weapons, led by Bachmeier, Crosby, and a bunch of good receivers. Tech averaged nearly 30 points per game a year ago and their offense is better this season. I look for Louisiana Tech to put up big numbers against a porous FIU defense. The Panthers will score too as defense is an afterthought for La. Tech. Take the over in this one.
