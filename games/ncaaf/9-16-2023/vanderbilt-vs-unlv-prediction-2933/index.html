#185 Vanderbilt vs.

The UNLV Rebels play host to the Vanderbilt Commodores on Saturday night in a non-conference showdown from Allegiant Stadium. The Rebels are 1-1, coming off of a road defeat against a potential playoff team, though happy to be back in Vegas. Vanderbilt is coming off of a loss on the road, as they hit the road for the second-straight week. The last time these two squared off against each other was back in 2019, which was a 34-10 win for UNLV in dominating fashion. While this one expects to be closer, it'll be interesting to see if the rebels can grab the win again against an SEC foe.

Commodores Back On The Road

After dropping their first game of the season on the road last week, the Commodores will be reluctant to see how this weekend goes as they travel across the country. UNLV should be an easier opponent than Wake Forest was, though the challenge of getting a young team ready in an uncomfortable environment remains a challenge. The opener saw them win by a touchdown against Hawaii, which while a win is always nice to have, they would have preferred to show off more against a bad team traveling thousands of miles. The defense seemed to let them down last week and it's always tough to ask the offense to score into the 30s. UNLV should be more manageable to keep in check but the Commodores will need to limit the penalties and mental mistakes if they want to get back into the win column.

The Commodores could be without a pair of tight ends and while the injury report is limited to only that position, it's not ideal to have a position group look so thin prepping for a road game. Cole Spence (knee) went down in preseason but now Josh Palmer (back) is questionable as well. Palmer is yet to appear in a game this season, though his emergence back into the lineup could give this offense a much-needed additional weapon.

Vanderbilt has struggled to move the ball with much consistency on the ground, though the matchup against UNLV's poor run defense could give them a great avenue to controlling this game on the road. AJ Swan *eight touchdowns, three interceptions) has led the way through the air but with a two-headed backfield, expect plenty more run action from this offense to set the tone. The defense has struggled from a numbers standpoint, compared to the rest of the country, but having a pair of 20+ tacklers, including CJ Taylor adding two sacks, shows the blueprint is there. The linebackers are a strength for this group but the secondary remains a concern, though that might not be a worry against a UNLV team that is far from pass-heavy.

Rebels Return Home

After a loss at the hands of Michigan last week, the Rebels will be happy to be back at home, and welcoming a team that has to travel across the country themselves. UNLV knows just how challenging that could be and while they lost last week, to only lose by 28 and cover the spread, had to seem like some sort of win for this group. They dominated Bryant in week one, though as an FCS foe, it's difficult to take a lot of stock in that game which sets the tone for this being a great barometer of where they stand before Mountain West action kicks off in the coming weeks. Home-field advantage didn't seem to matter for this group last season, so it'll be interesting to see if a return to Allegiant Stadium gets the fan buzzing for this one.

UNLV does have the advantage of not having to worry about any injuries, which is a slight surprise after facing such a physical opponent last week. Having consistency on both sides is sure to help in this one.

Offensively, the Rebels have struggled to move the ball. Even against Bryant, they benefited more from good starting position than they did from putting together long and impressive drives. They rank 119th nationally in total offense, though Vanderbilt's defense does give them hope that this weekend will at least go smoother than last week did. Doug Brumfield expects to get the snaps at quarterback again but without a passing touchdown and only 186 yards through the air through two games, the passing game is expected to remain a work in progress, especially against an SEC defense. Adding in a limited pass rush, with only one sack through two games, and they could struggle to make Vanderbilt feel uncomfortable.

Even though they have to go on the road for this one, the Commodores will come out on top. UNLV has major concerns on the offensive end, especially in the passing game, where fewer than 200 yards passing on the season, simply isn't going to cut it. This will be much more low-scoring than the analysts expect.
