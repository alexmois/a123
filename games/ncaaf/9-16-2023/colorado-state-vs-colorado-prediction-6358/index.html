The Colorado State Rams (0-1, 0-1 ATS, 1-0 O/U) battle the Colorado Buffaloes (2-0, 2-0 ATS, 1-1 O/U) in a rivalry showdown on Saturday afternoon in the Rocky Mountain Showdown from Folsom Field in Boulder. The Rams were clobbered by Washington State in their opener. The Buffaloes remained undefeated after a dominating home win against Nebraska. These rivals last met in 2019 in a game Colorado won 52-31.

Colorado State Drops Season Opener

The Colorado State Rams have only played one game this season. It was not a pleasant opener, losing 50-24 against Washington State in a bout that had the Rams marked as eight-point dogs. Colorado State only won three games last year, all in conference play. The program remains in more of a rebuilding mode but are hoping to see progress.

The program is looking for improvement from Clay Millen. The sophomore had an encouraging year, recording 1910 passing yards and a 10:6 TD to INT ratio and connected on over 70% of his passes. He had some trouble in the opener, going 15 for 24 for 110 yards. Brayden Fowler-Nicolsi also received playing time last week and was the better QB, posting 210 yards with two TDs and one pick. He has been named the starter for this game.

The squad returns last year’s rushing leader, Avery Morrow. The senior RB accrued 834 yards on an average of 4.8 yards per carry but couldn’t get anything going against Washington State, posting only 29 yards. The offense also brings back their top receivers from last season led by Tory Horton. The senior is one of the better receivers in the MWC after collecting 1131 yards last year and 81 yards in the opener. The CSU offense wasn’t the issue in the opener. They recorded 320 passing yards.

He led the squad in tackles last season and made 16 tackles in the opener. The group also returns Andy Hector who is their best corner. The unit surrendered 556 total yards and will need to be a lot better. 

Colorado State only averaged 13.2 points last year, pegging them 129th. The defense allowed an average of 26.9 points, placing them 70th

Colorado Dominates Nebraska, Enter Rankings

The #18 Colorado Buffaloes have been the most surprising and probably the most exciting squad in the FBS early this season. Coached by the great Coach Prime, Deion Sanders, the program has a completely different approach this season after winning only one game all of last season. They were 21-point dogs in a stunning 45-52 road win against TCU of the BIG 12 in the opener and three-point favorites in a 36-14 home win against Nebraska of the Big 10 last weekend. They were rewarded with a spot in the AP Top 25, coming in at #18.

The new quarterback this season is Deion’s son, Shedeur Sanders. The junior QB posted spectacular numbers at Jackson State the last two seasons and his opening performances in the FBS have been outstanding, recording 903 passing yards complemented by a 6:0 TD to INT ratio. 

Sy’Veon Wilkson also came from Jackson State. The sophomore RB posted 1167 yards last season but only has 46 rushing yards while Anthony Handerson has 47. The Buffaloes have almost an entirely new receiving core. Xavier Weaver transferred from USF where he collected 718 receiving yards last season and has been stellar, bringing in a remarkable 288 receiving yards in two games.

The Buffaloes' defense was abysmal last season. They were pegged 113th against the pass and 131st in rush defense. They stand 56th against the pass and 128th against the run after two bouts. The group struggled in the opener against TCU, squandering 541 total yards but was better last week, conceding only 341 yards. The rush defense has been an issue in the two games. Another transfer from Jackson State, Shilo Sanders, is a safety and has made 15 tackles in two performances. 

Colorado averaged 15.4 points last year, ranking them 127th. They allowed an average of 44.5 points, pegging them 131st in the country.
