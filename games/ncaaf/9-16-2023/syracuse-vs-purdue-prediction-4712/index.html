Syracuse Orange (2-0) vs. Purdue Boilermakers (1-1) 

Another frenzy college football Saturday is upon us, so we are breaking down the non-conference showdown from Ross-Ade Stadium in West Lafayette, Indiana, to get you the best Syracuse vs. Purdue betting pick and odds.  

The Orange meet the Boilermakers for the second straight season. Last year, Syracuse outlasted Purdue 32-29 as a 1.5-point home fave. The Orange are 2.5-point road favorites this time around, while the totals sit at 58.5 points. 

Syracuse will have the first proper test in 2023

The Syracuse Orange have had an easy job through the first two weeks of the season. After smashing the Colgate Raiders 65-0 as 40-point home favorites in Week 1, the Orange trounced the Western Michigan Broncos 48-7 as 24.5-point home favorites in Week 2. 

Last Saturday, the Orange put on a show in the first half, building up a 45-7 lead. They finished the game with 496 total yards while holding the Broncos to 318. Syracuse went 8-for-15 on 3rd downs and averaged 8.8 yards per pass. 

Senior signal-caller Garrett Shrader led the way for the Orange last Saturday. He tossed for 286 yards and a touchdown while rushing three times for 31 yards and a TD. Sophomore running back LeQuint Allen had eight carries for 20 yards and three scores, while freshman wideout Donovan Brown posted three catches for 89 yards and a TD. 

The Orange lost a couple of starters in Week 2. Tight end Oronde Gadsden suffered an ankle injury, while right tackle David Wohlabaugh went down with a leg injury. Both guys used crutches at the end of the game. 

"David is probably in a situation where he will not be out there for a while," Syracuse head coach Dino Babers said. "We're hoping to get him back soon but it's going to be a little bit extended on that one.

Purdue bounced back in Week 2

The Purdue Boilermakers disappointed their fans in Week 1, suffering a 39-35 home defeat to the Fresno State Bulldogs. The Boilermakers, who were listed as 4-point favorites in their season opener, made amends in Week 2, beating the Virginia Tech Hokies 24-17 as 1-point road underdogs. 

Purdue kept Virginia Tech scoreless in the second half. The Boilermakers outgained the Hokies 427-286 in total yards, holding their rivals to a paltry 11 rushing yards on 22 carries. Purdue carried the pigskin 46 times for 179 yards and kept the ball in possession for 38:01. 

“I’m really proud of the guys,” Purdue head coach Ryan Walters said. “This was a unique game in and of itself with the delay, but to get that first one on the road against a quality opponent, I thought the guys showed a lot of resiliency and just how they’d respond to adversity.” 

The Boilermakers tallied their first win in the Ryan Walters era. Junior signal-caller Hudson Card threw for 248 yards and notched 12 totes for 16 yards and a touchdown. Sophomore RB Devin Mockobee led the charge with 21 carries for 95 yards and a TD, while senior RB Tyrone Tracy Jr. added four carries for 51 yards and a TD.
