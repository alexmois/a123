Back on Track

The TCU Horned Frogs returned to their winning ways last Saturday with a convincing 41-6 win over Nicholls. After a disappointing opening week loss to Colorado at home, the Horned Frogs regrouped and held Nicholls without a touchdown to even their record at 1-1 heading into this week's matchup in Houston. For one week at least, TCU looked like the team that stormed its way to the National Championship game in 2022.

“Honestly, our performance thus far this season has probably mirrored our performance a little bit last season,” head coach Sonny Dykes said. “We had a better Colorado team we played Week 1. If we had played a really good Colorado team last year, I don’t know how we would have fared in that game.”

TCU quarterback Morris Chandler had a solid day completing 26 of 30 passes for 263 yards and two touchdowns. Chandler ran the ball well also, rushing for 63 yards on six carries and adding another touchdown. Wide receiver Warren Thompson had a career-high eight catches for 92 yards in the win and Jaylon Robinson and JoJo Earle each hauled in a touchdown catch. In all, the Horned Frogs had 22 first downs and 462 yards of total offense Saturday night.

TCU's defense held Nicholls to 263 yards of total offense on the night. They recorded six tackles for loss including three quarterback sacks. The secondary had one interception on the night and an additional five pass breakups. TCU excelled on special teams as well with a blocked punt for a touchdown and a 57-yard field goal from kicker Griffin Bell.

Very few things went wrong for the TCU Horned Frogs in 2022 on their way to a 13-2 season. TCU finished 28th in total offense on the season and they've not disappointed so far this season despite a new quarterback, scoring a combined 83 points in two games. The Horned Frogs were 10th on the ground in 2022 and 44th through the air. Despite the loss to Colorado in the opener, TCU's offense remains the strength of the team.

Defensively, TCU ranked 93rd in total defense last season. The results have been mixed thus far after the team allowed 45 points to Colorado but rebounded to keep a far less talented Nicholls team to just six points. TCU was 78th against the run last season and just 97th against the pass. The pass defense certainly struggled in week one against Colorado's pass-heavy attack.

Key Injuries: RB Cam Cook is questionable

Cougars Look to Show Bite

The Houston Cougars are hoping to rebound against a tough TCU team following last week's 43-41 double-OT loss to Rice. The Cougars are now 1-1 on the season and have played back-to-back tight games to kick off the 2023 campaign. The Cougars appeared to be on the short end of a blowout on Saturday after falling behind 28-0 at Rice. Houston rallied to tie the game with just 15 seconds left but lost when they failed to convert a two-point conversion in the second extra session.

“Maybe it’s a trap game. Maybe it’s a trophy game. Maybe it’s a rivalry game. Maybe we were looking forward to Big 12 and TCU. Maybe we didn’t respect our opponent and they whipped our tail. At the end of the day it’s on me,” Houston head coach Dana Holgorsen said. “We couldn’t have started slower. I explained the importance of starting fast all week. They did. We didn’t. Everything I’m saying, I’m responsible for it and I will assume responsibility.”

Houston's seven-year winning streak over Rice in the Bayou Bucket came to an end on Saturday in a thrilling overtime loss. The Cougars, heading to the Big 12 next season, looked flat from the start and fell behind by four touchdowns before mounting an incredible comeback that almost led to victory. Quarterback Donovan Smith went 24-of-42 for 260 yards and two scored while throwing one interception. Smith also carried the ball 16 times for 77 yards and three scores, including the game-tying score in the final 15 seconds of regulation. Wide receiver Matthew Golden had two touchdown catches while fellow wideout Samuel Brown had nine catches for 138 yards. In all, the Cougars racked up 443 yards of total offense in the losing effort.

Houston's defense struggled in the thrilling loss to Rice. They allowed a whopping 470 yards of total offense including 401 through the air. Defensive back Isaiah Hamilton was the defensive standout for the Cougars with an interception, 1.5 tackles for loss, and a forced fumble in the game. Houston had six tackles for loss and two sacks.

The Cougars finished 8-5 in 2022 and earned a bowl invitation. The team was led by its offense, which finished 21st in the nation in total offense. It has been a mixed bag offensively for Houston thus far with just 17 points in week one followed by 41 in week two. Last year, the Cougars finished just 75th on the ground but 7th through the air. Quarterback Smith is the key as he paces Houston both on the ground and through the air.

Defensively, the Cougars were ranked just 100th in the country last year in total defense. The Rice offense exposed the Cougars, particularly through the air with over 400 yards passing. Last year, Houston was 48th against the run nationally but an abysmal 120th against the pass.

Key Injuries: RB Brandon Campbell is probable.

This should be an interesting chess match between two quality coaches in Dykes and Holgorsen on Saturday night. The two coaches will soon get to lock horns annually in the very near future with Houston joining the Big 12 next season. On Saturday night, I like TCU to come out on top against what is a vastly inferior Houston secondary. While Rice has grown in respectability, they do not possess nearly the firepower or talent of TCU and were able to wrack up over 450 yards of total offense including over 400 through the air. While both teams struggled defensively last season and into this season, the TCU numbers were put up against vastly superior opponents as compared with the Cougars' schedule. Expect the Horned Frogs' passing attack to dominate this game against a Houston secondary that appears to be once again amongst the worst in the country. On the other side of the ball, the Cougars will be able to score against TCU's defense but don't have the depth of talent that Colorado possesses so I don't foresee them being able to match TCU score-for-score. I expect a highly entertaining, high-scoring game but one in which the overall talent level of TCU shines through much more brightly.

Take TCU -7.5
