Kansas Jayhawks (2-0) vs. Nevada Wolf Pack (0-2) 

Week 3 of the 2023 CFB season is upon us, and we are breaking down the non-conference showdown from Mackay Stadium in Reno, Nevada, to get you the best Kansas vs. Nevada betting pick along with the latest odds update.   

The Jayhawks and Wolf Pack have never met before. Kansas opens as a huge 28-point road fave, while the totals are listed at 58.5 points.  

Kansas eyes its third straight W

The Kansas Jayhawks moved to 2-0 SU and 1-1 ATS on the season following a 34-23 home victory over the Illinois Fighting Illini in Week 2. After failing to cover a 32.5-point spread in a 48-17 Week 1 home win against the Missouri State Bears, the Jayhawks satisfied the odds as 3.5-point favorites over Illinois. 

Junior quarterback Jalon Daniels missed the season opener due to a back injury, so senior Jason Bean led the Jayhawks with 276 yards and a pair of touchdowns. Daniels made his season debut in Week 2, completing 21 of his 29 passing attempts for 277 yards, two touchdowns, and an interception. Junior running back Devin Neal tortured Illinois for 120 yards and a touchdown on just ten carries, while sophomore RB Daniel Hishaw Jr. added 98 yards and a TD on 12 totes. 

The Jayhawks outgained the Fighting Illini 539-341 in total yards. Kansas racked up 262 rushing yards on 44 carries and kept the ball in possession for 36:39. Each team committed a couple of turnovers. 

“At first glimpse, I thought we were an extremely physical football team tonight,” Kansas head coach Lance Leipold said. “The film will tell us for sure but I thought we played aggressive. We're playing in a fashion that we're going to need in the Big 12 this year.”

Nevada was embarrassed in Week 2

The Nevada Wolf Pack started their 2023 campaign with a 66-14 defeat at the No. 6 USC Trojans. It was a heavy loss but it certainly didn’t hurt as much as a 33-6 home defeat to the Idaho Vandals in Week 2. The Vandals, who play in the Big Sky Conference, dominated the Wolf Pack from start to finish and easily covered a 5-point spread. 

Idaho outgained Nevada 463-266 in total yards. The Wolf Pack rushed 25 times for 118 yards but had to lean on their passing game in the second half. Sophomore QB Brendon Lewis went 12-of-24 for 104 yards and an interception, and freshman QB AJ Bianco was 8-for-14 for 44 yards and one pick. Lewis and junior running back Sean Dollars posted nine carries apiece while combining for 87 rushing yards. 

The Wolf Pack will have to improve on both sides of the ball if they want to avoid a disastrous season. Head coach Ken Wilson remains optimistic as his team prepares for a tough clash against the Jayhawks. 

"I practice with these guys every day," Wilson said. "I see their faces every day. I see how hard they work. I see how hard the staff works. We have a plan that's worked. My optimism is I see those guys out there working hard, and they don't bend, they don't break on things. They have a hard time learning how to fight adversity in games, but they fight like crazy in practice and in classwork and everything that we ask them to do. I feel like my optimism is my players and my coaching staff."
