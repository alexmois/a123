#119 Wake Forest vs.
#120 Old Dominion (ODU)
Saturday, September 16, 2023 at 12:00pm EDT
Written by Eric P.

The Old Dominion Monarchs play host to the Wake Forest Demon Deacons on Saturday afternoon in a non-conference showdown from Kornblau Field. The Deacons are off to another fast start, even despite the off-season losses, though their first road game could mark trouble. For the Monarchs, a win last week helped them get back on the right path, as they now look to start a winning streak. In 2021, Wake Forest dominated this matchup, winning 42-10 at home, though with both rosters looking different, as well as a change of scenery, anything could go in this one.

Deacons Hit The Road

After a pair of home games to open the season, Wake Forest finally hits the road for their first away game of the season. While far from the most daunting of opponents, a road win against a team that played in the Sun Belt is always a positive. Wins in back-to-back weeks against Elon and Vanderbilt, did little to move the needle but after bringing in some new faces, it certainly has to be nice for this group to get a nice winning streak going. The schedule gives them a nice ACC foe next week, so finding a way to keep their winning streak going, could go a long way towards keeping momentum going into an October meeting with Clemson as well. The Demon Deacons struggled on the road last season so they're hoping to set the tone differently to open up this fall.

There are some concerns when it comes to the injury report, as five players are listed as questionable heading towards Saturday's kickoff. The receiving core has been thin this year and with Donovan Greene (knee) having already been ruled out, eyes turn to a pair of tight ends and another wide receiver to see if they can add some depth on game day.

The offense has moved the ball well, finding a good balance between the passing and running game, while a matchup against Old Dominion should allow them to continue to find success. They average over 450 total yards per game, against a defense that has struggled through the first two weeks. Mitch Griffis (5 touchdowns, 1 interception) has stepped up well after Sam Hartman's transfer and while the wide receiver room is thin, the trio that has been seeing the majority of snaps remains talented.

Defensively, things start with Jacob Roberts (18 tackles) right in the middle, as his pass-rushing ability (2 sacks) has impressed through the first two games. It helps to have Dashawn Jones (2 interceptions) in the secondary as well,as both will make their presence known in this one. Teams have wanted to throw the ball on this group but with the matchup at hand in most facets, the Demon Deacons will be able to hold up well on the defensive end in this one.

Monarchs Building Momentum

After losing on the road in week one, Old Dominion was thrilled to get back into the win column last week, winning 38-31 in the Sun Belt opener over Louisiana. It shouldn't come as a surprise that they got back into the win column in the home opener as well, which makes it interesting to see if they can hold strong at home in this one. Last year saw this team struggle, while a 2021 meeting against Wake Forest likely isn't out of mind either. Confidence might be high after last weekend but they'll need to start fast to avoid an early letdown in this one.

One advantage that the Monarchs have heading into this one is that they don't have anyone listed on the injury report. With being at a disadvantage from a depth perspective, having a full complement of players could close the gap in this one.

They'll need all the help that they can get, especially considering the struggles on offense through the first two weeks. The passing game has been nearly non-existent, sitting 113th in the country in passing yards per game. Grant Wilson does have six touchdowns, compared to one interception, as the Fordham transfer looks to increase his downfield presence. Keshawn Wicks has performed well on the ground but against an ACC defense, the run game might not be as strong.

Defensively, all eyes will be on Jason Henderson (28 tackles), one of six double-digit tacklers for a deep linebacker and safety group. The struggle has come in the pass rush, as the Monarchs are without a sack on the season. Without a ton of help in generating pressure, the concern is if the Demon Deacons will be able to sit in the pocket and pick this secondary apart.

Even though they'll be on the road for this one, the Demon Deacons will come away with another dominant win. The Demon Deacons carry quite a bit of experience on both sides of the ball and with the struggles that the Monarchs have had to move the ball, it appears it could be a challenge for them to keep pace. Wake Forest has strong balance in the offensive end, which will give them the ability to control the clock and control the game, both keys to coming away with an easy win. Full-Game Total Pick

Insiders Status:

Rating:

This should be a high-scoring game, paced with Wake Forest's ability to control the tempo on both sides. Much like past seasons, they want to move past and efficiently, which will lead to plenty of possessions in this one. Wake Forest is 40th in scoring offense, while Old Dominion's defense ranks 115th in scoring defense, setting the tone that the Demon Deacons should have a massive night on the offensive end. A blowout should add some garbage points for the Monarchs, while their special teams, which is one advantage in this one, should lead to some points even if not from touchdowns. The Monarchs also know that they need an adjustment in play-calling, shifting to a much more pass-heavy attack, if they want to even try to keep pace, which will drive up the scoring.
