The South Alabama Jaguars (1-1, 0-2 ATS, 1-1 O/U) collide with the Oklahoma State Cowboys (2-0, 1-1 ATS, 0-2 O/U) in week three action on Saturday evening. The Jaguars were dealt a road loss by Tulane and beat SE Louisiana in their first victory. The Cowboys defeated Central Arkansas followed by a convincing road win against Arizona State in their previous decision. 

South Alabama Rebounds in First Victory

The South Alabama Jaguars difficult non-conference schedule continues. South Alabama had to play a ranked Tulane squad on the road to begin the season and were clobbered 37-17. Next, they were 21.5-point favorites in a 35-17 home win against SE Louisiana who plays in the FCS. The Jaguars won 10 games last year and were ranked to finish 2nd in the Sun Belt preseason poll.

Carter Bradley had a dominating 2022 season but has made some mistakes early this season. The senior QB has connected on 75% of his passes resulting in 448 yards and two touchdowns but has already tossed three picks in two games. La’Damian Webb led the squad with 1062 rushing yards last season and posted 121 yards on a stellar average of 7.6 yards per rush in two performances. The Jaguars also return last year’s receiving leader Devin Voisin. The senior WR only made one catch in the opener but had 70 receiving yards last week. The South Alabama offense returns most of the key pieces on the squad that had no problem scoring last season.

The Jaguars' defense was a strength last season. They were pegged 88th against the pass but fifth against the run. The defense was exploited in the opener against Tulane, surrendering 430 total yards. The unit squandered 412 yards in the second bout. They stand 123rd against the pass and 47th in rush defense after two games. The unit returns key players on last year’s squad led by safety Jaden Voison who is questionable for this game.

South Alabama posted an average of 31.2 points last year, marking them 47th. The defense allowed an average of 21.6 points, placing them 26th.

Oklahoma State Eyes Third Straight Victory

The Oklahoma State Cowboys are targeting a third consecutive victory to open the season. The opener was an easy one, pelting Central Arkansas 27-13 but didn’t cover the 26.5-point spread. The second clash posed a bigger challenge but they were ready, beating Arizona State 27-15 to cover the small 2.5-point spread. The Cowboys are expected to be in the middle of the pack in the Big 12.

The Cowboys continue to award three QBs playing time as they evaluate the talent and look for a #1 option to surface. Alan Bowman saw a good portion of the action against ASU and has 194 passing yards in two games. He spent some time with Michigan in previous seasons. Garret Rangel has been decent, recording 164 passing yards and Gunnar Gundy, who was with the squad last year, has 138 yards one TD, and no picks. 

Ollie Gordon II has been stellar, collecting 97 rushing yards on only 16 carries, resulting in an average of 6.1 yards per rush. Elijah Collins also has 16 carries in two games, rushing for 71 yards. De’Zhaun Stribling has been the best receiver after two games. The junior had 600 receiving yards as a member of Washington State last season and already has 138 yards. The OK State offense wasn’t particularly dominant against Arizona State, registering 304 total yards.

The Cowboys' defense has been their best strength so far which is a good sign considering it was a weakness in conference play last season. The defense was subpar against Central Arkansas but was able to limit Arizona State to just 277 total yards. They were marked near the bottom of the FBS in pass and rush defense last year, and are marked 71st in pass defense and 70th in rush defense this season.

Oklahoma State averaged 30.6 points in 2022, ranking them 49th. They conceded 28.9 points, pegging them 89th in the FBS.
