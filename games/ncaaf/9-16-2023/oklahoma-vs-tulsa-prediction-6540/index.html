Sooners Hoping to Lower the Boom

The Oklahoma Sooners will hit the road for the first time this season on Saturday but won't have far to travel in the two-hour trip from Norman to Tulsa. They bring with them a 2-0 record following a 28-11 win over SMU this past Saturday. After losing five games by eight points or less in 2022 on their way to a 6-7 record, the Sooners were able to win ugly against a very game Mustangs' squad after leading by just three points in the fourth quarter.

"I love that about our team — the fight and the courage and the belief and the never flinch attitude that they had," Sooners head coach Brent Venable said. "I thought, that was what you want to see. A year ago, I'm not sure if we would have figured out a way to win that game."

The Sooners offense was led by quarterback Dillon Gabriel who threw four touchdown passes in the win. Wide receiver Andrel Anthony had seven catches for 76 yards and a touchdown in the game. Running back Tawee Walker ran for a career-high 117 yards in the win as part of an 189-yard Sooner rushing attack. In all, the Sooners had 365 yards of total offense.

Defensively, Oklahoma allowed 367 yards of total offense to the Mustangs but kept them out of the end zone when it mattered. Linebacker Danny Stutsman led the way with 17 tackles including a fumble recovery and a sack. In all, the Sooners forced two turnovers and blocked a punt.

The Sooners finished 2022 at 6-7 and struggled with inconsistency in Venable's first season. The former Clemson defensive coordinator was brought in to bring in toughness and improved defensive play in a team that was mainly known for its offensive prowess over the past decade. Last year, the Sooners ranked 11th in total offense in Venable's first season. They were 10th rushing the football and 44th through the air. In week one, the Sooners poured in 73 points before scoring 28 against SMU.

Venable has been tasked with building up the Sooners' defense and has done well thus far with his team only allowing 11 points through two weeks. Last season, Oklahoma finished just 123rd in total defense. They were 104th against the run and 116th against the pass.

Key Injuries: Kaden Helms TE (knee), JJ Hester WR (foot), Davis Beville QB (knee) and Jacob Sexton OL (undisclosed) are questionable. DL DJ Graham II WR (undisclosed) is out.

Frying Pan into Fire

Things will not get any easier for the Golden Hurricane this week as they get set to battle cross-state rival Oklahoma. Tulsa is now 1-1 on the season as the team returns home following a 43-10 loss at Washington last week. The Golden Hurrican briefly had the lead down to 7-3 before going scoreless in the second and third quarters as the high-powered Huskies offense piled on the points. Tulsa head coach Kevin Wilson, one of the more innovative offensive-minded coaches in college football, will now be tasked with trying to solve a Sooners' defense that has let up just 11 points in two weeks.

“You’ve got to be aggressive,” Wilson stated on Tuesday, “(but) you’ve also got to be calculated and smart. It can snowball against you, too. You can’t play in a phone booth. You can’t play in a shell and expect to have a chance to win this game.”

It was another case of musical chairs at quarterback for Tulsa. Cardell Williams made his first career start after replacing starter Braylon Braxton in week one due to Braxton's injury. This time it was Williams leaving the game with an injury and third-string quarterback Roman Fuller having to take over. Fuller would finish the game 12-of-18 for 85 yards and a fourth-quarter touchdown pass to Luke McGary. On the ground, the Golden Hurricane was led by running back Jordan Ford who had 67 yards rushing. Overall, Tulsa had 318 yards of total offense.

Defensively, Tulsa was gashed for 563 total yards including a whopping 454 through the air. Safeties Kanion Williams and Kendarin Ray had seven tackles each in the game to lead the Golden Hurricane. Fellow safety Jaise Oliver added five tackles and had an interception in the end zone to prevent another Huskies' score.

Tulsa hopes to improve upon their 5-7 record in 2022. The team was led by its' offense, which ranked 55th in the country. They were just 83rd on the ground but a solid 42nd through the air. Each of the team's top two quarterbacks are questionable vs. the Sooners so Wilson may have to be even more creative this week against the Sooners defense.

On the defensive side of the ball, Tulsa was ranked just 103rd overall in 2022. They were 121st against the run which may not be a huge issue against an Oklahoma team that can be inconsistent on the ground. Against the pass, the Golden Hurricane was ranked 46th in 2022. They didn't look effective this past week, however, against the Huskies who threw for over 450 yards.

Key Injuries: Cardell Williams QB (Hand) and Braylon Braxton QB (Ankle) are both questionable.

Insiders Status:

Rating:

Despite Venable's claim that he was pleased with the team's gritty win over SMU last Saturday, I think the second-year head coach is trying to create a swagger with the Sooners this season following last year's 6-7 season. In the opener, the Sooners showed no signs of letup in a 73-0 win over Arkansas State. This week, Venable's Sooners get a crack at a Tulsa team that has multiple injuries to the team's most important position, quarterback. While Fuller was serviceable against Washington in an emergency role, Wilson will certainly feel more comfortable if either of the injuries to his top two quarterbacks heals in time for this matchup. Of the two, Williams seems more likely to return with a hand injury. Williams also likely presents the best chance for Tulsa against the Sooners' fast defense with his ability to both run and throw. Unfortunately, Tulsa does not have anyone ready to step in defensively that can slow down the Oklahoma passing attack. Tulsa allowed the Huskies to throw for 454 yards in last week's game and Sooners' quarterback Gabriel also has the tools to carve up the Golden Hurricane defense. I fully expect a highly motivated Oklahoma team to dominate Tulsa on the road. Don't expect Venable to let up on Saturday afternoon.

Take the Sooners -27.5.

Prediction: Oklahoma -27.5
