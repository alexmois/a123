BYU Cougars (2-0) vs. Arkansas Razorbacks (2-0) 

Week 3 of the 2023 college football season brings plenty of intriguing matchups Saturday, September 16, including the non-conference battle from Razorback Stadium in Fayetteville, Arkansas, so here’s the best BYU vs. Arkansas betting pick along with the latest odds update. 

These two schools meet for the second straight season, and Arkansas beat BYU 52-35 as a 1-point road underdog in 2022. This time around, the Razorbacks are 9-point home favorites with a total of 51.0 points.   

BYU aims for its sixth straight W

The BYU Cougars started their 2023 campaign with a 14-0 blanking of the Sam Houston Bearkats. The Cougars failed to cover a 19.5-point spread in front of the home fans, and they couldn’t beat the number in Week 2 as well, defeating the Southern Utah Thunderbirds 41-16 as 30.5-point home favorites. BYU is now unbeaten in five straight contests dating back to the previous season, but the Cougars have only covered twice in that stretch. 

The Cougars racked up 112 rushing yards on 34 carries in Week 1 and just 46 rushing yards on 23 totes in Week 2. Their ground game has struggled thus far, and the Cougars will have to figure it out in the coming weeks. 

“I don’t know all the answers right now for who carried what,” BYU head coach Kalani Sitake said after a Week 2 win over Southern Utah. “I just want to see the ball carried with more intensity and get more yards. I have to watch the film. But we need somebody that can carry the ball and do it the right way. We need someone who can block for them and make sure we get more yards. What we got is not good enough.”

Senior signal-caller Kedon Slovis scored twice on his own in Week 1. He was terrific in Week 2, throwing for 348 yards, four touchdowns, and an interception while carrying the ball once for six yards and a TD. Junior tight end Isaac Rex had four receptions for 112 yards and a touchdown against Southern Utah, and sophomore wideout Chase Roberts added six catches for 84 yards and a TD.

Arkansas shuts down Kent State in Week 2

The Arkansas Razorbacks covered a 38-point spread in a 56-13 thrashing of the Western Carolina Catamounts in Week 1. They failed to cover a 38-point spread in Week 2, outlasting the Kent State Golden Flashes 28-6, but it was another dominant home victory for the Razorbacks. Arkansas outgained Kent State 308-200 in total yards while holding the Golden Flashes to 26 rushing yards on 36 carries. 

“Early we couldn’t throw, couldn’t catch it, couldn’t run it. Defense kept us in the game,” Arkansas coach Sam Pittman said. “We weren’t giving up points, but time. The game plan was going exactly how they wanted it to go minus scoring a lot of points.” 

Senior signal-caller KJ Jefferson led the way for the Razorbacks last Saturday. He went 13-of-19 for 136 yards and a pair of touchdowns while rushing 13 times for 48 yards. Junior running back AJ Green had 15 totes for 82 yards, while senior wideout Andrew Armstrong notched four catches for 21 yards and a couple of touchdowns. 

Back in Week 1, Jefferson tossed for 246 yards and three touchdowns. Junior RB Raheim Sanders posted 15 carries for 42 yards and two scores before suffering a knee injury that kept him off the field in Week 2. According to Sam Pittman, Sanders will remain on the sidelines in Week 3.

Full-Game Side Bet

Insiders Status:

Rating:

Although BYU’s ground game has been bad through the first two weeks of the season, I’m backing the Cougars to keep it close at Razorback Stadium and cover a 9-point spread. Kedon Slovis is an experienced quarterback who’ll know how to deal with pressure, and the Cougars’ defense should have enough weapons to compete against the Razorbacks. 

Raheim Sanders’ injury is a huge blow for the Razorbacks’ offense which is another reason why I’m going with the underdogs. Arkansas dominated Kent State on the defensive side of the ball, but shutting down BYU will be a much taller task. Unfortunately, both BYU and Arkansas have had an easy schedule thus far, and this will be the first proper test for either side. 

Prediction: BYU Cougars +9.0 (-110)

The Cougars struggled to defend in 2022, yielding 29.5 points on 234.3 passing yards and 173.8 rushing yards per game. The Razorbacks were even worse, surrendering 30.6 points on 294.7 passing yards and 170.5 rushing yards per contest. 

As I’ve mentioned above, the first two weeks haven’t been a proper benchmark, and we’ll know more about these two teams after their meeting in Week 3. I’m expecting both teams to throw the ball a lot, so give me the over on the totals.
