It's Saturday afternoon on the college gridiron, and we will see the Central Michigan Chippewas tangle with the Notre Dame Fighting Irish at Notre Dame Stadium in South Bend, Indiana. The Chippewas are coming off of a 45-42 home win over FCS New Hampshire to put them at 1-1 on the year. The Irish are now a perfect 3-0 on the season after topping NC State on the road by a score of 45-24.

Chippewas Survive Against New Hampshire

Usually, teams at the FBS level shouldn't struggle against FCS teams, but that is exactly what Central Michigan did in their last game. They took on New Hampshire in that game at home and won by just a score of 45-42. The Chippewas were seven-point favorites in the contest, so they failed to cover the spread.  The contest was tight throughout, with CMU winning on a Field Goal as time expired. Central Michigan will now take a step way up in class as they face 11th-ranked Notre Dame today. The Chippewas have to be concerned coming into this game, especially with a pass defense that ranked last in the nation. They allowed New Hampshire to throw for 493 yards and must now face Sam Hartman and a rejuvenated Notre Dame passing attack. It could be a very long afternoon for the Chippewa defense.

The offense was very balanced against the Wildcats as they ran for 236 yards and threw for 204, but that was against an FCS defense. In their first game, the Chips put up just 218 yards of total offense against Michigan State. That may not bode well here against a Notre Dame defense that could potentially be one of the nation's best this year. CMU threw for just 96 yards against Michigan State, and the Irish have the 10th-ranked pass defense in the nation. Bert Emanuel Jr. has thrown for 280 yards with three TDs and 3 INTs on the year. He will have to be far better in this one.

Notre Dame Is Flexing their Offensive Muscles

The last few years, the Notre Dame Fighting Irish had dynamite defenses and a strong running game but inconsistent play at the QB spot. Now they have a strong QB in Sam Hartman, who could be the missing piece for the Irish this year. Hartman spent the last five seasons racking up big numbers at Wake Forest, and he is off to a fantastic start for the Irish. The 6th-year QB has thrown for 731 yards with 10 TDs and no INTs. He now has 120 TD passes in his career, which is 10th on the all-time list. Last week, Sam threw for 286 yards and four TDs in a 45-24 win against NC State on the road. He faced Navy and Tennessee State in his first two games, so it was nice to see him do it against a real defense. Hartman has to be licking his chops at facing a Central Michigan team that allowed 493 yards passing to an FCS Team last week. He could also be pulled early if the Irish are up big, as the Irish have a huge game against Ohio State on deck.

The Notre Dame ground game could get a lot of work, especially late in the game. After rushing for just 825 yards and 11 TDs in 12 games last year, Audric Estime has run for 345 yards and four TDs through three games this year so far. The Irish will use him to set up Hartman for big plays down the field. The Notre Dame defense finally got its first test last week, and NC State did put up 344 yards of total offense, including 260 yards through the air. Still, the Irish are ranked 10th in the nation against the pass, giving up just 123.3 ypg through the air so far. They have a chance to improve on that number as the Chips are 122nd in passing at 150 ypg. I can see an angry Notre Dame defense trying to bounce back from last week's showing against NC State.
