It’s a battle of Power Five conference programs on the gridiron as a team from the ACC kicks off their season with a contest against one from the SEC in the Volunteer State. The Virginia Cavaliers are on the road as they make the trip to face the #12 Tennessee Volunteers on Saturday afternoon. Tennessee finished the 2022 season 11-2 and capped the year with an emphatic 31-14 pasting of Clemson in the Orange Bowl. In the all-time series between the programs, the Volunteers own a 3-1 advantage, including a 23-22 win in the most recent meeting, which came in the Sugar Bowl on January 1, 1991.

Virginia Cavaliers Looking to Spring Upset Out of the Gate

Virginia struggled in their first season under coach Tony Elliott as the team ended up 3-7 overall, 1-6 in ACC play, and logged just one win over a FBS program. That was an uninspiring 16-9 win over Georgia Tech. The Cavaliers had their share of off-field tragedy as three players were killed and another injured in a shooting in November that led to the school canceling the team’s final two games of the season. Elliott saw his team struggle mightily on the offensive side of the ball last season and he has to retool on the fly as only three starters are back on that side of the ball in 2023. Perhaps more importantly, he’ll have to rely on a new signal caller after Brennan Armstrong transferred to NC State. Will Elliott conjure up more success this season than he had a year ago?

The Cavaliers finished last season 80th in the nation in passing offense with 221 yards per game while they were just 101st in rushing offense with 123.1 yards per contest on the ground. Virginia was 126th in the FBS in scoring offense with an average of 17 points per game while they were 48th in scoring defense by allowing 24 points per contest. With Armstrong gone, it will be Monmouth transfer Tony Muskett running the offense for the Cavaliers. He completed 143 of 223 passes last season for 1,997 yards with 17 touchdowns and eight interceptions while adding 210 yards plus a score on the ground. Kobe Pace, who spent the past three seasons with Clemson and ran for 641 yards plus six scores in 2021, is expected to be the lead back in the run game. Perris Jones (81 carries, 365 yards, two TD), Xavion Brown (50 carries, 210 yards, TD) and Mike Hollins (52 carries, 215 yards, two TD) are the next in line on the depth chart. It’s a new cast of characters at the receiver position this season as tight end Grant Misch is the team’s top leading returning pass catcher with 17 grabs for 129 yards. Grad transfer Malik Washington, who reeled in 65 passes for 694 yards and a score at Northwestern last season, will be a key contributor for a receiver corps that has just one other upperclassmen among its ranks. Will Bettridge hit 12 of 14 extra point attempts and seven of 10 field goals with a long of 47 last season as a freshman and should handle the kicking duties.

Tennessee Volunteers Try to Start 2023 on Positive Note

Tennessee finished last season 11-2 and ranked #6 in the final rankings after drubbing Clemson in the Orange Bowl. The Volunteers beat Alabama, Pitt, Florida, LSU and Kentucky in addition, giving them six wins over ranked foes, with their two losses coming to eventual national champion Georgia (not surprising) and to South Carolina, in a game they gave up 63 points (very surprising) on the year. Tennessee moves on from Hendon Hooker, who was selected by the Lions in the third round of the NFL Draft, along with a pair of dynamic receivers in Cedric Tillman and Jalin Hyatt, but they have plenty of returning weapons for Josh Heupel’s explosive offense. Will the Volunteers keep up with those prolific numbers of a season ago?

The Volunteers were good offensively last season as they finished 5th in passing offense as they average 326.1 yards per game through the air. Tennessee was 26th in rushing offense as they put up an average of 199.5 yards per contest on the ground. The Volunteers were a stellar 1st of the 131 FBS teams to take the field last season in scoring offense as they averaged 46.1 points per contest. Tennessee was 35th in scoring defense as they allowed an average of 22.8 points a contest. With Hooker gone, Joe Milton III, who completed 53 of 82 passes for 971 yards with 10 touchdowns and no interceptions, will be the triggerman offensively. He added 77 yards on the ground in his action. The run game is paced by a 1-2 punch that includes Jabari Small, who had 157 carries for 734 yards and 13 scores last season. Jaylen Wright (146 carries, 875 yards, 10 TDs) was the top rusher last season in the run game and also returns. Bru McCoy (52 catches, 667 yards, four TDs), Ramel Keyton (31 receptions, 562 yards, five TDs) and Squirrel White (30 receptions, 481 yards, two TDs) provide a veteran trio of receiving options for Milton III to target. Dont’e Thornton, who caught 17 passes for 366 yards and a score for Oregon last season, will be another weapon in the aerial assault. Charles Campbell, who hit all 27 extra point attempts and 14 of 20 field goal attempts with a long of 51 last season for Indiana, takes over the kicking duties this season.
