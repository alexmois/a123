The Sam Houston State Bearkats pay a visit to LaVell Edwards Stadium in Provo, Utah on Saturday to face the BYU Cougars in the first game of the season for both schools. Last season, Sam Houston State finished 5-4 overall and 3-2 in the Western Athletic Conference as an FCS team but has joined the Conference-USA as a brand new FBS member. The BYU Cougars were 8-5 last season as an Independent and have joined the Big 12.

Sam Houston State will be better than most expect

Sam Houston State redshirted several of its key players in 2022 in preparation for joining Conference-USA. Sam Houston State has had a talented squad for several seasons including winning a FCS national championship in 2021. 

 

Last season's problems for Sam Houston State were the result of injuries and holding out players for the 2023 season. Sam Houston has still not decided on its starting quarterback which is the battle between Keegan Shoemaker and Grant Gunnell, who transferred from North Texas. Last season Shoemaker passed for 1,122 yards but completed just 47% of his passes.

 

Sam Houston held out wide receiver Ife Adeyi, who caught the game-winning touchdown when Sam Houston State won the FCS National Championship in 2021. Adeyi is expected to be a keep piece in the offensive game plan for the Bearkats this season. Linebacker Trevor Williams will return after missing last season and he has 260 tackles during his time at Sam Houston State.

BYU has talented new QB in Slovis as Cougars begin Big 12 play

The BYU Cougars open the 2023 regular season with a new quarterback and in a new conference. BYU joined the Big 12 following 12 years of being an independent and former USC and Pitt quarterback Kedon Slovis will start under center for the Cougars. Last season BYU averaged 31.3 points per game and is expected to score in bunches again this season. The receiving corps is not deep, but is led by Kody Epps, Chase Robers and Keanu Hill. Aidan Robbins will be the top running back for BYU.

The defense for BYU will be led by an entirely different coaching staff as last season BYU’s defense was its weak link. New coordinator Jay Hill brought in several new coaches and also brought in FCS All-American cornerback Eddie Heckard, while two other transfers Jackson Cravens, a defensive tackle and Isaiah Bagnah, a defensive end, arrived from Boise State. Middle linebacker Ben Bywater, who last season had 98 tackles and three interceptions, is the foundation for the defense that will look almost entirely new.

Full-Game Side Bet

Insiders Status:

Rating:

Sam Houston State will likely not win on Saturday when playing on the road against the BYU Cougars but the Bearkats have enough talent on both sides of the line of scrimmage to remain close enough to cover the spread. Sam Houston State held out several of its key players from both offense and defense last season to give them another year of eligibility when joining the Conference USA and those players along with new transfers and recruits will give Sam Houston State a solid foundation to be competitive at the FBS level. BYU has a new quarterback, who will need time to come up to speed with a new system and the BYU defense will be completely new under a new coordinator with nearly every player that starts different from last season. The end result will be BYU winning at home as the Cougars are a more talented team top to bottom but Sam Houston State will remain close enough to cover the nearly three touchdown spread.
