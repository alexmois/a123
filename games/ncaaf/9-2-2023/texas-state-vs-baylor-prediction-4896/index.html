Two Texas colleges, the Texas State Bobcats (4-8-0; 5-7-0 ATS last season) and the Baylor Bears (6-7-0; 7-6-0 ATS) will try to bounce back from underwhelming 2022 seasons. The first step in each side's respective campaign brings them together for a third straight year. Baylor finished on top in the first two meetings. Last year's game was in McLane Stadium, and this one will be also. Waco, Texas' stadium is scheduled to kick things off at 7:00 p.m. EDT on Saturday. Will Texas State finally take one from the Bears?

Bobcats' Brand New Coach

GJ Kinne is running the show now after creating an offensive juggernaut with Incarnate Word last season. This year probably won't be as smooth for coach Kinne. Almost the entire offense will be comprised of transfers. Along with that, the entire program is trying to implement a new system. Last year's leading receiver, Ashtyn Hawkins, is back in the mix, and passing should be the offense's strength again this season. Improving upon 2022's 21.1 points per game will require a much stronger attack on the ground though. Baylor will be a great way to test the line early in the season. 

The Texas State defense will also have a lot of new faces underneath each helmet. The secondary will have the most familiar faces and should improve upon allowing 244.9 yards per game through the air a year ago. Elsewhere, the turnover will be much more apparent, for better or worse. Texas State only gave up 117.0 rushing yards per game a year ago, an impressive number considering that they trailed often. Overall, they surrendered 26.3 points per game. Defense isn't coach Kinne's calling card, but he has something to work with.

Another Odd Year Baylor Run?

The years 2020 and 2022 were duds in Waco., while 2019 and 2021 saw the team in the Big 12 Championship Game. Will they work more bounce-back magic in 2023? Returning the defense to a dominant state will be Baylor's deciding factor. Last year's team gave up 26.6 points per game, including 137.6 yards per game on the ground. With last year's leading tackler gone, new names will need to step up to stop the run. Along the line, getting sacks more consistently will be the goal this season. Baylor finished with 24 sacks, with 11 of those coming in two games. 10 of the other 13 were in the first four games. Patching that should restore the Bears to the ferocious group that terrorized the Big 12. Otherwise, it may be another season that drags onward.

As a Big 12 school, Baylor may not be impressive offensively compared to the rest of the conference. Still, it's a group that finished with 32.2 points per game in 2022 and over 182 yards per game rushing the ball. This year's offensive line brought in transfers that should immediately make the offense more imposing, on paper. Two running backs who combined for over 2,000 yards are back and should have an even easier time now. Baylor's pass catchers project is a talented group too. Everything appears to be in place, except for the quarterback, where Blake Shapen and Sawyer Robertson each need to prove they're the guy that can propel this team into a potential playoff spot.

Full-Game Side Bet

Insiders Status:

Rating:

This game wasn't close in McLane Stadium last season and with Baylor's improvements, it shouldn't be close this year. Especially not with Texas State in a brand new era of football facing an opponent that theoretically outclasses them completely on the field. Baylor's size, athleticism, continuity, and home-field advantage give them a massive edge.

Baylor's receivers can expose a weak Bobcats secondary. The Bears ran for 293 yards (on 37 attempts) against Texas State a year ago. Baylor's offensive line is supposed to be much better, while the RB room is the same. Meanwhile, Texas State's rushing defense was strong last season but doesn't have the same names or system in place The Bobcats won't be able to stop Baylor's offense, and that will pave the way for another Baylor blowout.

Prediction: Baylor -27.5

Full-Game Total Pick

Insiders Status:

Rating:

There won't be enough time for this game to finish over the total. Baylor loves to run the ball and should cut through Texas State's defense with ease again this season. That will keep the clock running, especially when Baylor has a large lead and actively tries to shorten the game.

As for Texas State, they may love to pass, but scoring won't come easy in this game. The Bobcats only scored seven points in Waco last year. Baylor's pass rush has a lot to prove and will come out hungry to set the tone for 2023. The Bobcats' offense will limp along, Baylor's will milk the clock, and this game won't ever threaten the 60-point mark.
