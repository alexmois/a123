The Colorado Buffaloes and the Texas Christian Horned Frogs will take the field on Saturday in Week One college football action. Kickoff from Amon G. Carter Stadium is at 12:00 p.m. ET.

Colorado has an over/under win total of 3.5 games in 2023. The Buffaloes have an exciting non-conference schedule, with additional games against Nebraska and Colorado State. The CU football team was 1-11 last season, including 0-6 on the road.

TCU has an over/under win total of 7.5 games. The Horned Frogs will also play Nicholls State and SMU in non-con action. Texas Christian was 13-2 overall and 6-0 at home in 2022.

The Horned Frogs are 20.5-point spread favorites and the game total is 64 points.

Can the Buffaloes handle prime time pressure?

Colorado will be one of the most-watched teams in college football this season, as ex-Jackson State head coach Deion Sanders brings "Prime Time" to Boulder, CO. Along with Sanders comes an influx of transfer players, including his son Shedeur, who will take over at quarterback. The new-look roster is a product of today's college football landscape, as well as a testament to Sanders' star power. One thing is for sure, after a one-win season, some changes were needed to bring the Buffs back into the spotlight.

Kent State head coach Sean Lewis will run the CU offense in 2023, integrating one of the fastest, most exciting offenses in college football. Running the show will be Sanders, who passed for 3,732 yards, 40 touchdowns, and six interceptions last season at JSU. After two years of impressive play in the Southwestern Athletic Conference, he'll need to prove it at the Power-Five level in an offensive attack that will require him to make quicker, more decisive reads.

The junior has a nice mix of playmakers to rely on, including junior WR Jimmy Horn Jr. (37 receptions for 551 yards at USF), senior WR Xavier Weaver (53 receptions for 718 yards and six TDs at USF), and two-way superstar Travis Hunter. The running game should also be solid, with senior Kavosiey Smoke (Kentucky), sophomore Alton McCaskill (189 carries for 961 yards and 16 TDs with Houston in 2021), and highly touted freshman Dylan Edwards toting the rock. The offensive line, meanwhile, is a question mark. There is some returning talent, but how will they gel with the newcomers?

The Colorado defense allowed 44.5 points per game last season and was second worst against the run. In comes defensive coordinator Charles Kelly from Alabama to right the ship, along with a lot of other new faces. There are transfer players all over the defense, including pass rushers Derrick McLendon (Florida State) and Sav’ell Smalls (Washington), and space eater Leonard Payne Jr. (Fresno State). Demouy Kennedy (Alabama), Brendan Gant (Florida State), and LaVonta Bentley (Clemson) headline the linebacking group, but the focus of Coach Prime's D will be the secondary. Joining Hunter will be Myles Slusher (Arkansas), Cam’Ron Silmon-Craig (Jackson State), Shilo Sanders (Jackson State), and true freshman Cormani McClain.

Is TCU the new flag bearer for the Big 12?

The Horned Frogs, fresh off a runner-up finish in the College Football Playoff, will look to sustain momentum in 2023 with a new cast of characters. Gone is quarterback Max Duggan, and in is Chandler Morris, who started last season's season opener vs. Colorado but left with an injured knee. Offensive coordinator Garrett Riley has also departed the program, with Kendall Briles taking over OC and QB coach duties. These changes are exciting, but how smooth will the transition look in week one?

Along with Duggan, TCU must replace leading rushers Kendre Miller (1,399 rushing yards and 17 TDs) and Emari Demercado (681 rushing yards and six TDs), along with its top three pass-catchers, Quentin Johnston (1,069 receiving yards), Taye Barber (613 receiving yards and five TDs), and Derius Davis (531 receiving yards and five TDs).

Savion Williams (392 receiving yards and four TDs) and All-Big 12 tight end Jared Wiley are back, plus the Horned Frogs acquired JoJo Earle (Alabama), JP Richardson (Oklahoma State), and Jack Bech (LSU) from the portal. Alabama transfer RB Trey Sanders is expected to have a big season behind an offensive line that shouldn't have any problems opening holes for him.

The Texas Christian defense is deepest at linebacker with Johnny Hodges (87 tackles) and Jamoi Hodge (82 tackles and 4.5 sacks) leading the charge, and the secondary is ripe with experience with cornerback Josh Newton (12 passes defended and three INTs) and safeties Mark Perry (84 tackles) and Millard Bradford (58 tackles and eight passes defended). The real question is, who will provide the pass rush with Dee Winters (7.5 sacks) and Dylan Horton (10 sacks) off to the NFL?

The Horned Frogs are turning the page on a new chapter of TCU football. They lost some of their star power from last year's team, but the target on their backs won't be any smaller. There's enough returning talent on the Horned Frogs to take advantage of the Buffaloes' lack of cohesiveness, and I'm confident TCU OC Briles will be ready to prove he's the man for the job. On the defensive end, the Horned Frogs' secondary will make life difficult for Shedeur Sanders in his Power-5 debut.

Full-Game Total Pick

Insiders Status:

Rating:

I bet that we'll see fewer explosive plays than some expect in this Week One battle. With all of the unknowns in this matchup, it would surprise me if we saw these two offenses move the football consistently. TCU has a new QB and OC and must replace most of its rushing yards and receiving yards from 2022. Especially early, that will be clear, as the Horned Frogs look to settle in and the Buffaloes fly around looking to make the plays that prove they belong.

There's no denying that Colorado has skilled playmakers on the offensive side, but the offensive line may go through some growing pains early in the season. If it's unable to open holes for the run game, the lack of offensive balance will likely put too much pressure on Sanders to make plays against a good TCU secondary.

The under is my bet on the total in Saturday's CU-TCU clash!
