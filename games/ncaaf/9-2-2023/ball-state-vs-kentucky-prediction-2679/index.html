A pair of college football programs open up their 2023 slates when the MAC takes on the big, bad SEC in a non-conference tilt from the Bluegrass State. The Ball State Cardinals are on the road as they make the trek to take on the Kentucky Wildcats on Saturday afternoon. Ball State finished the 2022 season 5-7 and missed out on a bowl game as they posted a losing campaign for the eighth time in the last nine years. Kentucky went 7-6 in 2022 though they were blanked 21-0 by Iowa in the Music City Bowl, ending their season with a thud. These programs have met just once as FBS opponents with Kentucky picking up a 28-20 win at home on September 8, 2001.

Ball State Cardinals Hoping for Better Showing in 2023

Ball State sputtered and stalled their way to a 5-7 mark in 2022, marking the eighth time in the last nine years that the team finished with a losing record. It was the sixth time in seven years under coach Mike Neu that the team had a losing record: five of those seasons saw the team finish at least two games below the .500 mark. The problem for the Cardinals is that the transfer portal came and swallowed up some of their key offensive contributors, including running back Carson Steele, who led the MAC with 1,556 rushing yards plus 14 scores last season, and quarterback John Paddock (2719 yards, 18 TD, 14 INT), who will now ply their trade with UCLA and Illinois, respectively this season. Trying to fill holes offensively will be a major challenge for them this season.

Last season, the Cardinals finished 69th in the nation in passing offense with 228.5 yards per game through the air while they were 65th in rushing offense with 155.7 yards per contest. Ball State was 98th in the FBS in scoring offense with 23.2 points per game and 69th in scoring defense as they allowed an average of 26.9 points per contest. With Paddock gone, grad transfer Layne Hatcher, who hit 275 of 442 passes for 2,653 yards with 19 touchdowns and 10 interceptions for Texas State last season, takes over as the #1 QB on the depth chart. Kiael Kelly, who was the backup last season, is next up at the position. To replace Steele, the Cardinals got Marquez Cooper, who ran the ball 285 times for 1,326 yards and 13 scores last season for Kent State, to be their #1 back. Vaughn Pemberton (35 carries, 133 yards) and Rico Barfield (15 carries, 60 yards) are next in line on the depth chart. Tight ends Tanner Koziol (35 catches, 373 yards, seven TD) and Brady Hunt (46 receptions, 498 yards, five TDs) are the top returning pass catchers for the team this season. Guys like Nick Presley (six catches, 35 yards), Qian Magwood and Malcolm Gillie need to step up in the receiver corps. The kicking job is up for grabs with Carson Holmer, Jackson Courville and Division III transfer Jack Drake competing throughout camp.

Kentucky Wildcats Retooling in 2023

Kentucky ended up 7-6 in 2022, marking the sixth time in the last seven seasons that they had a winning record. With that said, the Wildcats struggled offensively and were hammered by Iowa in the Music City Bowl as they took a step or two back from the 2021 team that finished 10-3. It won’t be easy for coach Mark Stoops, who has to replace starters at quarterback and running back this season. Replacing Chris Rodriguez Jr., who finished his collegiate career as the third-best rusher in school history, will be a task. So will be rebuilding an offensive line that was 126th in sacks allowed last season. To that end, transfers Marques Cox (Northern Illinois), Cortland Ford (USC) and Tanner Bowles (Alabama) will be looked at to contribute and help stabilize things on the front line.

The Wildcats finished last season 94th in the FBS in passing offense with an average of 208.5 yards per game. Kentucky was 107th in the nation in rushing offense with an average of 116.2 yards per game on the year. The Wildcats were 111th in scoring offense as they average 20.4 points per contest and stood 13th in scoring defense as they limited the opposition to 19.2 points per game this season. With Levis gone, the Wildcats went out and got Devin Leary (118 of 193, 1265 yards, 11 TD, four INT in six games) as a transfer from NC State, to run the offense.  Meanwhile, in the run game, Ray Davis, who ran the ball 232 times for 1,042 yards and five scores for Vanderbilt last season, takes over the #1 duties. JuTahn McClain (59 carries, 278 yards) is back to provide a solid secondary option. Barion Brown (50 receptions, 628 yards, four TDs), Tayvion Robinson (40 catches, 497 yards, three TD), Dane Key (37 grabs, 519 yards, six TDs) and tight end Jordan Dingle (20 catches, 220 yards, three TD) gives the team their top four pass catchers from a season ago. Redshirt senior Chance Poore is expected to get the nod at kicker after Matt Ruffolo graduated.

With that said, Kentucky was stingy defensively, ranking 11th in total defense (311.4 ypg), 48th against the run (140.6 ypg) and seventh against the pass (170.8 ypg) last season.
