New Mexico Lobos (0-0) vs. Texas A&M Aggies (0-0) 

Week 1 of the 2023 CFB season brings plenty of interesting matchups around the country including the non-conference showdown from Kyle Field in College Station, Texas, so here’s the best New Mexico vs. Texas A&M betting pick along with the latest odds update. 

The Lobos and Aggies lock horns for the first time since 2021 when Texas A&M blanked New Mexico 34-0 as a 30-point home fave. The Lobos are massive 38-point home favorites this time around, while the totals sit at 49.0 points.   

The Lobos are on a nine-game losing streak

The New Mexico Lobos enter the new season hoping to grab more than three wins for the first time since 2016. Last year, the Lobos went 2-10 in Danny Gonzales’ third year as a head coach. They are winless in nine consecutive outings and haven’t covered the spread in seven straight contests. 

The Lobos’ offense was awful in 2022, averaging 13.1 points per game (130th in the nation) on 104.9 passing yards (128th) and 123.2 rushing yards (101st). On the other side of the ball, New Mexico was yielding 26.0 points per game (tied-60th) on 202.8 passing yards (28th) and 157.4 rushing yards (76th). Troy Reffett, who’s worked as cornerbacks coach over the last three years, is the Lobos’ new defensive coordinator. 

New Mexico lost its starting quarterback from the previous season, so we’ll see junior Isaiah Chavez and senior transfer Dylan Hopkins battling for the job in 2023. Junior RB Nate Jones, who posted 118 carries for 544 yards and a pair of touchdowns in 2023, will continue to lead the Lobos’ backfield, while junior wideout Luke Wysong will look to build on his 2022 campaign when he had 34 catches for 291 yards and a TD.

The Aggies hope to bounce back in 2023

The Texas A&M Aggies went 5-7 in 2022, tallying their first sub-.500 season in the Jimbo Fisher era. After a 3-1 start and a couple of wins over the top-25 teams in Miami and Arkansas, the Aggies had dropped six consecutive contests before finishing the season with a 20-3 victory to Massachusetts and a 38-23 win against the No. 5 LSU. 

Texas A&M was tallying 22.8 points per game last regular season (101st in the nation). The Aggies’ offense ranked 84th in the country in passing yards (219.4 per game) and 81st in rushing yards (141.5). On the other side of the ball, the Aggies were surrendering just 21.2 points per contest (25th) on 156.2 passing yards (1st) and 208.8 rushing yards (123rd). 

Sophomore signal-caller Conner Weigman will get the starting call when the Aggies take on the Lobos this coming Saturday. Last year, Weigman completed 55.3% of his passing attempts, throwing for 896 yards, eight touchdowns, and zero interceptions across five games. Senior RB Devon Achane will continue to carry the Aggies’ backfield after posting 1,298 yards from scrimmage and 11 touchdowns in 2022. He’s amassed 2,012 rushing yards over the last two seasons with Texas A&M. 

Best Bets for this Game

Full-Game Side Bet

Insiders Status:

Rating:

The Lobos look like the worst team in the Mountain West Conference. They will struggle to move the ball against the Aggies whose defense should be one of the best in the nation in 2023. However, I don’t like laying 38 points in Week 1. 

As I’ve mentioned, the Lobos are 0-7 SU and ATS in their last seven contests. Still, none of those seven losses has come by more than 32 points. I’m expecting the Lobos to try to run as much as possible and eat the clock, so the Aggies will need a score or two from their defense to beat this number. The Aggies, who have a new offensive coordinator in Bobby Petrino, might need some time to gel things offensive-wise. 

Prediction: New Mexico Lobos +38.0 (-110)

I’m taking the under on the totals as my best New Mexico vs. Texas A&M betting pick.
