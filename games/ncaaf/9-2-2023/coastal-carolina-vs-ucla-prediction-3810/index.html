#199 Coastal Carolina vs.

The UCLA Bruins begin their final season in the Pac-12 this Saturday by hosting the Coastal Carolina Chanticleers of the Sun Belt Conference. UCLA is coming off a season where they went 9-4 overall and 6-3 in the conference, while the Chanticleers were 9-4 overall and 6-2 in the Sun Belt. These two teams have not met in recent years. Kickoff is Saturday night at 10:30 p.m. ET at the Rose Bowl in Pasadena, Calif.

Chanticleers have lots of firepower

There is a lot of anticipation for this season at Coastal Carolina as many experts predict that they will compete for the Sun Belt title. But they will do so without coach Jamey Chadwell, who took his talents to Liberty. Tim Beck, the former offensive coordinator from North Carolina State, takes over and this is his first head coaching job. Beck is an offensive minded coach and he has an offensive that averaged 29 points per game last season. Leading that offense will be veteran quarterback Grayson McCall, who is an extremely experienced and efficient hand.

#UCLA's Chip Kelly on Coastal Carolina QB Grayson McCall:

"(He) is one of the top returning QBs in college football... his numbers may be the all-time best... a tremendous runner but he's also one of the all-time accurate passers in NCAA history."

Last season, McCall threw for 2,700 yards and completed 70% of his passes. He had 24 touchdowns and just 2 interceptions. He has back three veteran receivers to throw to in Sam Pinckney, who had 71 catches for 996 yards and 3 TDs, along with Jared Brown (49 catches, 789 yards, 6 TDs) and Tyson Mobley (40 catches, 490 yards, 5 TDs). The two leading rushers CJ Beasley and Reese White, are also returning. Beasley rushed for 719 yards and 5 TDs with 19 receptions, while White ran for 544 yards and 5 TDs with 8 receptions. The defense is not great, allowing 31.8 points per game a year ago, which ranked 113th nationally. The rush defense is definitely the strength, but the pass defense is terrible, ranking 127th last season.

Uncertainty at QB for UCLA

It will feel weird when the UCLA Bruins are not in the Pac-12 -- I mean, the Bruins are the Pac-12. They are going to the Big Ten next season, which also seems weird. That is going to be hard to get used to. But for now, they are still in the Pac-12 and coach Chip Kelly's squad is in a bit of a rebuilding mode. The rebuild starts at quarterback, as Dorian Thompson-Robinson, who led the Bruins high-powered offense for the past few seasons, has moved on and is playing for the Cleveland Browns. That has left a huge hole under center at UCLA and even as of this week, it remains uncertain.

#UCLA starting QB Ethan Garbers on the strides he’s made: pic.twitter.com/Wc5hFOhFLF

— Tracy McDannald 📎 (@Tracy_McDannald) August 28, 2023

There are three quarterbacks all vying for minutes, and while Ethan Garbers, who backed up DTR, has been named the starter for Saturday, it would not be a shock to see his backups get a shot if things don't go well -- even in this game. There could be a bit of musical chairs at that position, at least until a clear favorite emerges. Hopes are high for freshman Dante Moore, the likely quarterback of the future, but he might be too green right now. Also, look for Kent State transfer Collin Schlee to get some snaps, especially if Garber falters. Another huge hole to fill will be left by Zach Charbonnet, who rushed for 1,359 yards and 14 TDs, and caught 37 passes, last season. The top three receivers from last year are gone too. But this is Chip Kelly -- he knows offense and he has a lot of transfers coming in to hopefully fill those roles.

This feels like a game that could either be an outright upset or certainly close enough for Coastal Carolina to cover. The Chanticleers are loaded with returning talent at every position and have one of the better quarterbacks in the country in McCall. UCLA has question marks all over the field, including at quarterback. The Chanticleers defense is definitely exploitable, but this Bruins team lost a ton of firepower and it is uncertain as to how they will play, especially with the potential for musical chairs at quarterback. UCLA does not have a great defense, so I look for Coastal Carolina to score enough to cover. take the Chanticleers getting the points.

Full-Game Total Pick

Insiders Status:

Rating:

The total here is set pretty high, at least on its face, but in reality, the number is definitely achievable with these two teams. Based on their averages last year, these teams would hit almost 70 points in this game and I see a similar result here. It doesn't matter who the players are, Chip Kelly is going to play fast and he's going to generate some offense, and the weak Coastal Carolina defense will give up a ton of points. But Coastal Carolina has a strong offense with veterans at skill positions so they will also be able to put up numbers on UCLA's defense. I see a high-scoring game here that sails past the total.
