The North Illinois Huskies will hit the road to Chestnut Hill in Boston this Saturday to take on the Boston College Eagles in the opener for both schools. NIU, out of the MAC, was 3-9 in 2022 and hopes to make significant progress in 2023. The Eagles, out of the ACC, are also hoping for a bounce-back season in 2023 after finishing 3-9 as well last year. The opening kickoff is scheduled for 12:00 PM EST.

Hoping to Bounce Back

After playing for the MAC Championship in 2021, NIU stumbled in 2022 managing just three wins and finishing in last place in the West Division of the conference. Head coach Thomas Hammock now enters his third season with a fairly experienced roster and hopes of returning to the top of the conference. On the offensive side of the ball, the key returning player is quarterback Rocky Lombardi. Lombardi was injured and missed much of 2022 but Hammock hopes that the senior's leadership will help lead the way for the Huskies. Last year, NIU was 29th rushing the football and 114th in passing.

On the defensive side of the ball, NIU returns seven starters from last season; two on the defensive line, one linebacker, and two in the secondary. This is from a unit that ranked 71st in the nation in total defense last season. The Huskies were better against the run than the pass in 2022. The unit was ranked 44th in rushing defense and 94th in pass defense. The defensive line has the most experience with two juniors and a senior making up three of the front four.

Entering his fifth year at NIU, Hammock is 17-27 and could be looking at a pivotal year for his program given the returning talent and lighter schedule in 2023. Another last-place finish could prove to be hazardous for his job security.

Can the Eagles Soar?

Boston College Eagles head coach Jeff Hafley is entering his fourth season at the Heights and currently has a 15-20 mark through his first three. After coming over from the Clemson staff, Hafley was tasked with bringing the Eagles up the ranks of a very competitive Atlantic Coast Conference. This season, Hafley has two new coordinators on offense and defense and will be on the hot seat to get the Eagles bowl-eligible for the first time since 2019. After taking over in November as the team's starting quarterback, Emmett Morehead returns under center for Boston College. After taking over last year, he finished with over 1,200 yards passing and 10 touchdowns while throwing for 250 or more yards in three of his final four games. The Eagles are hoping for a major improvement on the offensive line after finishing last in the nation in rushing and 126th in sacks allowed in 2022. All-ACC tackle Christian Mahogany returns after missing all of last season with an injury and the Eagles added two veteran transfers. The Eagles' wide receiver group is relatively inexperienced but the team does return senior running back Pat Garwo III. A lot will ride on the ability of the Eagles' offensive line to improve.

Defensively, the Eagles return their leading sack man in edge rusher Donovan Ezeiruaka. He led the Eagles with 8.5 sacks last season and big things are expected this year. Overall, the Eagles return four starters from last season but none from their secondary. In 2022, Boston College ranked 53rd in the country in total defense. They were 93rd against the run but 27th against the pass. They could be challenged to put up similar numbers in the passing game this season with a whole new secondary. BC added three new cornerbacks from the transfer portal to give the team added depth.

Rating:

This is truly a make-or-break year for Hafley at Boston College. He has new coordinators on both sides of the ball and has made several key moves via the transfer portal to improve the team, specifically in the secondary and along the offensive line. Morehead's experience at the end of last season should help the Eagles immensely and he could have an explosive afternoon against a questionable Huskies' secondary. The key for BC will be the team's ability to get the ball downfield now that former All-American Zay Flowers will be catching passes on Sundays in the NFL. Ryan O'Keefe, a transfer from UCF, has caught over 150 balls in the last two seasons and could instantly provide a security blanket for Morehead this season. I like the Eagles improved offensive line which should translate to a better ground attack and more protection for the quarterback. NIU is not explosive defensively and struggled against the pass last season. Lombardi returns for the Huskies but he has an inexperienced set of skill players around him. NIU ranked just 114th throwing the ball last season which could be a big problem if the Eagles get up early, as I expect them to do. Expect the Eagles to fill the air early and often on Saturday and win this one comfortably.

Take the Eagles -9.5

Prediction: Boston College -9.5
