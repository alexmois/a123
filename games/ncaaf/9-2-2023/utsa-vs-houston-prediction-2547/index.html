Conference musical chairs will be in full effect these next couple of years and on Saturday, two teams that are among those that are switching conferences will square off. In this battle, the Houston Cougars, formerly of the American Athletic Conference, will play their first game as a member of the Big 12 when they take on the University of Texas San Antonio (UTSA) Roadrunners. UTSA, formerly of Conference USA, takes Houston's place and is now in the American Athletic Conference (AAC). Kickoff is Saturday at 7:00 p.m. at John O'Quinn Field at TDECU Stadium in Houston.

UTSA joins the AAC

The UTSA Roadrunners spent last season in Conference USA where they finished 11-3 and were a perfect 8-0 in the conference. They averaged nearly 37 points per game, 14th in the country, and gave up about 26 points per game, 61st in the country. They should be good again in their new conference, the AAC, as a lot of key pieces are back, starting with quarterback Frank Harris. Harris completed 70% of his passes and threw for a ridiculous 4,036 yards a year ago with 32 touchdowns and 9 interceptions.

The leading receiver last year, Zachary Franklin, bolted for Ole Miss, but Harris has two excellent receivers to throw to in Joshua Cephus, who caught 87 passes for 985 yards last year, and De’Corian Clark, who caught 51 for 741 yards. They also have back the leading rusher from a year ago, Kevorian Barnes, who gained 845 yards and scored 6 times. Harris also can run, gaining 602 yards and scoring 9 times on the ground in 2022. Clark is listed as questionable for this game.

Houston sings a new Tune

Houston enjoyed another solid season in the AAC last season, going 8-5 overall and 5-3 in the conference. They were led by one of the best quarterbacks in school history, Clayton Tune, but now Tune has moved on the NFL's Arizona Cardinals and Houston will have to find a way to replace the more than 4,000 yards and 40, yes 40, touchdowns he threw last season. Turns out, in their first year in the Big 12, the Cougars landed a nice prize from the Big 12.

Houston coach Dana Holgorsen on naming Donovan Smith the starting quarterback pic.twitter.com/eQJyuFBbyJ

— Joseph Duarte (@Joseph_Duarte) August 19, 2023

Houston got former Texas Tech quarterback Donovan Smith in the transfer portal and he will be under center for the Cougars this season. Smith shared snaps with Tyler Shough last season at Tech and threw for 1,505 yards and 12 TDs with 8 interceptions. But Smith won't have last year's top two receivers to throw to as Tank Dell, who caught a ridiculous 109 passes for 1,398 yards last year, is gone, as is KeSean Carter, whose 601 yards were second on the team. But two veterans in  Sam Brown (41 catches) and Matthew Golden (38 catches) are both back. Also, two of the top runners, Stacy Sneed (501 yards) and Brandon Campbell (435 yards) are back, too.

Best Bets for this Game

Full-Game Side Bet

Insiders Status:

Rating:

Both of these schools have something to prove in their first seasons in a new conference. Both programs view it as a step up, and they want to show the rest of their new conference-mates why they are there. This is a game between two very evenly matched teams. Both like to chuck it and both love offense and neither relies heavily on their defense to win games. UTSA is just loaded with all of their top skill players back and I just don't see them losing to a Houston team that is integrating a new QB, playing his first game, into the offense. UTSA will hit the ground running and win this battle of Texas. Take UTSA giving up the points.

Prediction: UTSA -2.5

Full-Game Total Pick

Insiders Status:

Rating:

One thing you can expect in this game is a lot of points. Both of these teams ranked in the top 25 in total yards, in the top 17 in points scored, and in the top 13 in passing yards. UTSA has all of its weapons back, so you can expect them to score early and often against a Houston team that gave up 32 points per game last year. Houston no longer has Tune, but Smith comes with a lot of pedigree and he should be able to move the ball against a subpar UTSA defense. Defense is an afterthought for both of these teams, so look for an offensive display that pushes the score past the total. Take the over.

Prediction: Over 59.5
