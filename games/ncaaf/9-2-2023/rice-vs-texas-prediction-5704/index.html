The Rice Owls and Texas Longhorns will kick off their 2023 football seasons on Saturday at Darrell K Royal Memorial Stadium. Kickoff is scheduled for 3:30 p.m. ET.

Rice has an over/under win total of 4.5 wins. It will also play Houston, Texas Southern, and Connecticut in non-conference competition. The Owls were picked to finish 12th in the American Athletic Conference preseason poll. They were 5-8 overall last season, including 1-5 in road games.

Texas has an over/under win total of 9.5 wins. It will also face fourth-ranked Alabama and Wyoming in non-conference action. The Longhorns were picked to win the conference title in the Big 12 preseason poll. They were 8-5 overall last season, including 5-2 in home games.

Texas is a 35.5-point spread favorite and the game total is 58.5 points.

Can JT Daniels provide a spark to the Owls offense?

Rice's five-win season in 2022, which included an appearance in the LendingTree Bowl, was its best in head coach Mike Bloomgren's five seasons. It won't be easy to get back to a bowl game this year, though, following the school's move from Conference USA to the American Athletic Conference.

The Owls' offense will feature an explosive passing offense headlined by receivers Luke McCaffrey (58 receptions for 723 yards and six TDs) and Isaiah Esdale (42 receptions for 544 yards) and tight end Jack Bradley. Leading receiver Bradley Rozner (19.9 yards-per-reception and ten TDs) transferred to NC State, though, leaving a sizeable hole to fill. Senior JT Daniels, who has passed through USC, Georgia, and West Virginia, will be the team's starting QB, while Ari Broussard (nine TDs) and Juma Otoviano (5.7 yards-per-carry) will pace the ground game.

The Rice defense will be led by linebackers Myron Morrison (74 tackles and two INTs), Chris Conti (75 tackles), and Josh Pearcy (50 tackles and 6.5 sacks), who also stars as a pass rusher. The question is whether there is enough talent up front to allow them to play up to their potential. The secondary is not short on experience with Jordan Dunbar at corner (eight passes defended) and Gabriel Taylor (60 tackles and two INTs) at safety. The defense was solid down the stretch in 2022 (seven takeaways in the last four games), but the offense's turnover woes (32 TOs) prevented it from making an impact.

Texas is loaded with offensive playmakers

UT has College Football Playoff aspirations in 2023, its last season before it joins the SEC. Whether or not Texas is back, this will be one of the most-watched seasons in its storied history.

The Longhorns have a star-studded offense, headlined by quarterback Quinn Ewers (2,177 passing yards, 15 TDs, and six INTs) and receiver Xavier Worthy (760 receiving yards and nine TDs). As long as Ewers stays healthy, the passing offense could be electric, with Jordan Whittington (652 receiving yards), Georgia transfer Adonai Mitchell, Wyoming transfer Isaiah Neyor (20 yards-per-catch and 12 TDs), and tight end Ja’Tavion Sanders (613 receiving yards and five TDs) catching balls.

It will be impossible to replace running back Bijan Robinson (1,580 rushing yards and 18 TDs), but Jonathan Brooks, Jaydon Blue, and Keilan Robinson form a strong trio. The backbone of the offense, though, is the o-line, which will be one of the best lines in the Big 12.

The Texas defense returns plenty of experience from a 2022 unit that finished second in the Big 12 in total and scoring defense. The secondary leads the D, with Jerrin Thompson (53 tackles and seven passes defended), Jahdae Barron (42 tackles and two INTs), and Ryan Watts (three passes defended) taking charge. Jaylan Ford (112 tackles) and true freshman Anthony Hill are two names to watch at linebacker, and defensive tackles T’Vondre Sweat and Byron Murphy are NFL-caliber players.

Best Bets for this Game

Full-Game Side Bet

Insiders Status:

Rating:

This spread is too large to wager more than three units, but I believe it's going to be the winning bet on Saturday. The Longhorns will be looking to put on a show, and I'm confident they will do just that against Rice. The Owls are short on defensive line talent, which should give Ewers enough of a complementary run game to open it up through the air. He will have plenty of time to throw, forcing Rice to pick their poison, so to speak, when covering the Horns' pass catchers.

Like Texas, Rice boasts a former highly-touted recruit at QB, but I'm not predicting a Texas-sized shootout between gunslingers on Saturday. The Longhorns' secondary is legit, and the pass rush will be effective enough to force Daniels into check-downs and bailout balls in the stands, maybe even a pick or two. There's not enough on paper to predict an Owls cover in Austin.

Full-Game Total Pick

Insiders Status:

Rating:

I don't expect Texas to take its foot off the pedal on offense, putting on an air raid for the home crowd against Rice. This is Ewers' time to shine, and I'm sure UT will look to pump him up with confidence before next week's matchup with Alabama. Besides, it may not take a full sixty minutes for Texas to score 40-plus points. The Owls' inferior defensive line will be pushed around all game long by the Horns, leading to some big holes and easy throws. After that, Texas' skill players will do the rest of the work.

Should the Longhorns decide to give their starters extra rest toward the end of the game, I wouldn't be surprised if the backups give up an extra score that pushes the total over. Daniels hasn't lived up to the hype, but he's a talented, experienced player who can move the ball against Texas' second-stringers. The Longhorns don't have the type of defense that will strike fear in their opponents, either.
