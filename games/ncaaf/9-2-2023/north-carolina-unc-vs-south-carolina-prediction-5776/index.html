It’s a battle of geographical rivals on the gridiron as a pair of Power Five teams lock up when the ACC tangles with the SEC in Charlotte. The #21 North Carolina Tar Heels are on the road as they make the in-state trip to face the South Carolina Gamecocks on the neutral field of Bank of America Stadium Saturday night. North Carolina finished the 2022 season with a 9-5 record and saw their season end with a 28-27 loss to Oregon in the Holiday Bowl. South Carolina finished last season 8-5 though they were beaten 45-38 by Notre Dame in the Gator Bowl to close their campaign. In the all-time series between the teams, the Tar Heels own a 33-20-4 advantage though it was the Gamecocks taking a 38-21 victory in the Duke’s Mayo Bowl in the most recent meeting on December 30, 2021.

North Carolina Tar Heels Seek to Start 2023 With Win

North Carolina had their ups and downs last season as they finished the year 9-5 overall and 6-2 in the ACC. The Tar Heels won the Coastal Division of the conference but were waffled 39-10 by Clemson in the ACC title game before losing to Oregon in the Holiday Bowl. North Carolina started the year 9-1 but dropped their final four games of the season, including a home loss to Georgia Tech. The Tar Heels bring back several starters on both sides of the ball and should be in the thick of the race in the ACC. With no divisions in 2023, the top two teams will play in the ACC Championship Game: the question remains whether the Tar Heels can do enough to upend Clemson or Florida State to earn one of those spots.

Last season, the Tar Heels were 11th in the nation in passing offense with 309.3 yards per game through the air and 66th in rushing offense by picking up 153.5 yards per contest on the ground. North Carolina was 25th in the FBS in scoring offense as they put up 34.4 points per game but finished just 101st in scoring defense by allowing 30.8 points per contest. Drake Maye is back after hitting 342 of 517 passes for 4,321 yards with 38 touchdowns and seven interceptions on the year. He also led the team on the ground with 698 yards and seven scores as the reigning ACC Player of the Year. Elijah Green (131 carries, 558 yards, eight TDs), Omarion Hampton (88 carries, 401 yards, six TDs) and Caleb Hood (43 carries, 250 yards) give the Tar Heels their top three running backs from a season ago in the mix. Kobe Paysour (29 receptions, 324 yards, four TDs) and J.J. Jones (24 catches, 434 yards, two TDs) are the top returning receivers in the passing game. Tight ends Bryson Nesbit (35 receptions, 507 yards, four TDs) and Kamari Morales (29 catches, 358 yards, four TDs) are back to provide skill players that can stretch the seam. Devontez Walker (Kent State) and Nate McCollum (Georgia Tech) are other new options in the passing game. Noah Burnette hit 57 of 58 extra point attempts and 15 of 21 field goal attempts with a long of 47 last season. It will be him or Ryan Coe, who hit all 44 extra points and 19 of 23 field goal attempts with a long of 52 at Cincinnati a year ago, handling the kicking duties.

South Carolina Gamecocks Look to Build on Strong Stretch Run in 2022

South Carolina had their moments in 2022 as they started the year 5-2 then dropped two of three with losses to Florida and Missouri, before kicking into gear offensively late in the season. The Gamecocks hung 63 on #5 Tennessee in an upset win, stunned #7 Clemson 31-30 and then pushed Notre Dame to the limit before falling in the Gator Bowl. South Carolina has a new offensive coordinator as Dowell Loggains takes over for Marcus Satterfield, who took the same role at Nebraska. Defensively, the Gamecocks have to replace key starters at several positions after losing three starting defensive linemen, including Jordan Burch, and both starting linebackers. Will they be able to build on scoring 132 points in their final three games or revert to early season form where they had their struggles?

The Gamecocks finished last season tied for 38th in the FBS in scoring offense as they put up 32.2 points a game this season. South Carolina was 40th in the nation in passing offense with 260 yards a contest and 105th in rushing by averaging 118.8 yards per game. The Gamecocks were tied for 86th in scoring defense as they allow 28.8 points per game. Spencer Rattler was 264 of 399 passing for 3,026 yards with 18 touchdowns against 12 interceptions while adding 46 yards and three scores on the ground. Luke Doty was 10 of 14 for 146 yards with two touchdowns and two interceptions while adding 63 yards and a score on the ground. Juju McDowell (62 carries, 219 yards, two TDs) and Dakereon Joyner (12 carries, 56 yards, two TDs) are likely the top two backs for the team on the ground. Antwane Wells Jr. led the team with 68 receptions for 928 yards plus six scores last season. Ahmarean Brown (23 catches, 188 yards) and Xavier Legette (18 grabs, 167 yards, three TD) are also back to provide returning targets in the passing game. Mitch Jeter was 42 of 44 on extra point attempts and 11 of 11 on field goal attempts with a long of 53 last season. Alex Herrera hit all three extra point attempts without attempting a field goal on the year. Both kickers are back this season.
