Fresno State (10-4 SU, 7-7 ATS, 7-7 O/U) travels to West Lafayette to take on Purdue (8-6 SU, 5-9 ATS, 9-5 O/U) on Saturday at 12pmET from Ross-Ade Stadium in a non-conference battle.

Last season, Purdue lost two straight games to end the season including a 63-7 loss to LSU in the Citrus Bowl. Fresno State has won nine straight including a 29-6 win over Washington State in the LA Bowl.

Both teams will have to replace star quarterbacks.

Tedford has done a fantastic job

Fresno head coach Jeff Tedford has had success at Cal as head coach and then came to Fresno State in 2017. He resigned in 2019 for health reasons and then returned in 2022, winning 10 games and the Mountain West. In conference play, Tedford is 23-9 in four seasons with the Bulldogs. This season, he has to replace Jake Haener at quarterback, who threw for 2,616 yards with 18 TDs and just three picks. He also has to replace star rusher Jordan Mims, who rushed for 1,372 yards, including 209 yards in the bowl victory over Washington State.

Fortunately for Fresno State and Tedford, he has an experienced option to replace Haener in Mikey Keene, a transfer from UCF. Keane won the battle over junior Logan Fife. In two seasons at UCF, Keened threw for 2,377 yards with 23 TDs and seven picks. Last season, he came off the bench for Ole Miss transfer John Rhys Plumlee.

“We watched (his film) at Central Florida and then went back and watched all of his high school tape," said Tedford before camp on 247sports.com. "I thought he was just very, very accurate throwing the football. He's very smart, he's highly competitive, he's tough - All those things that you want your leader and your quarterback to be. So we felt like he was bringing those attributes to our team and wanted to be in our offense and want to play in our system. I think it's a great fit.”

New coach and quarterback

Ryan Walters takes over as head coach for Jeff Brohm, who left for Louisville last December. The 37-year-old comes from Illinois where he was defensive coordinator for a defense that held teams to just 12.8 points and 273.5 yards per game last season. The most important move for Walters was to replace quarterback Aidan O'Connell, who moved on to the NFL. O'Connell led an offense that ranked 17th in the nation in passing yards per game (287.2ypg). He was arguably the best Purdue quarterback since Drew Brees.

Enter Hudson Card, the Texas transfer, who threw for 1,523 yards with 11 TDs and two picks in three seasons. He also started five games the past two seasons and did threw for 303 yards and three TDs last year in a win over West Virginia. He'll have a new offensive coordinator in Graham Harrell, who comes from West Virginia and averaged 30.6 points per game.

Running back Devin Mockobee is back after rushing for 968 yards and nine TDs. Four starters return on the offensive line and senior WR TJ Sheffield (46 catches for 480 yards) is the only returning starter at receiver.

On defense, the Boilermakers move to a 3-4 scheme under Walters, where starting OLB Kydran Jenkins is back after recording four sacks and seven QB hurries. Both safeties return and they'll have to rely on a transfer to help stabilize the cornerbacks. Salim Turner-Muhammad arrives from Stanford to attempt to fill the void of Corey Trice, who was drafted in the seventh round by the Pittsburgh Steelers. Defensively, Purdue was not bad last season, allowing 213.8 passing yards per game (50th) and 136.2 rushing yards (43rd).
