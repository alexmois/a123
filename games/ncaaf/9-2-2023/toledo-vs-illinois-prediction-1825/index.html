The Toledo Rockets and Illinois Fighting Illini will kick off their 2023 football seasons on Saturday at the University of Illinois Memorial Stadium. Kickoff is scheduled for 7:30 p.m. ET.

Toledo has an over/under win total of nine wins. It will also face Texas Southern, San José State, and Massachusetts in non-conference action. The Rockets were picked to win their conference in the MAC preseason poll. They were 9-5 overall last season, including 2-4 on the road.

Illinois has an over/under win total of 6.5 wins. It will also play Kansas and Florida Atlantic in non-conference action. The Fighting Illini were picked to finish fourth in the Big Ten West in Cleveland.com's annual preseason poll. They were 8-5 overall last season, including 5-2 at home.

Illinois is a nine-point spread favorite and the game total is 45.5 points.

Toledo is a very talented team

Toledo enters 2023 with sky-high expectations — and it should. Head coach Jason Candle hasn't had a losing season at the helm of the program, but the Rockets can't run out of gas again late in the year if they're going to reach their destination.

Junior QB Dequan Finn is experienced and talented — he just needs to stay healthy. He was nearly flawless at times and mistake-prone at others, tossing ten of his 12 interceptions in three games. In 2021, he had an 18:2 TD:INT ratio. He'll target a quality group of receivers led by Jerjuan Newton (53 receptions for 830 yards and nine TDs). Running back Jacquez Stuart (770 rushing yards, 5.7 yards-per-carry) will pace a ground game that should provide the Rockets with plenty of offensive balance.

The Toledo defense was the best in the MAC last year, featuring the conference's top pass defense. The secondary will continue to make life difficult for opposing quarterbacks. CB Quinyon Mitchell (20 passes defended), Maxen Hook (two INTs), Nate Bauer (two INTs), and Zachary Ford (six passes defended) are a few names to watch come award season. The Rockets lost a couple of key players on the defensive line but added quality transfers in Judge Culpepper (Penn State) and Travion Ford (Mizzou). Tackling machine Dallas Gant (115 stops) will take charge at linebacker, but new players must step up, too.

Illinois needs Altmyer to transform its offense

Illinois seems to have stabilized under former Wisconsin head coach Brett Bielema, but can it take the next step toward becoming one of the Big Ten's top programs? The Illini haven't enjoyed consecutive winning regular seasons since the 1990s, but there isn't any reason why they can't be a consistent force in the West division.

Illinois averaged 378 yards and 24 points per game last season but was able to win by controlling the clock and limiting turnovers. That said, the lack of a downfield passing game limited what U of I could accomplish offensively. Luke Altmyer, formerly of Ole Miss, is the quarterback tasked with changing that tune. He'll benefit from a deep but young group of receivers itching to break out. Four of the team's top five pass catchers are back, including leading receiver Isaiah Williams (82 receptions for 715 yards and five TDs). Running back Chase Brown (328 carries for 1,643 yards and 10 TDs) is playing on Sundays, but second-leading rusher Reggie Love (330 rushing yards, 4.6 yards-per-carry) is back to run behind a line that should be solid.

The U of I defense was among the most feared in college football last season, ranking first in takeaways, first in interceptions, third in total defense, and first in scoring defense (12.8 points allowed per game). The secondary lost four players who accounted for 17 interceptions, but there is young talent waiting in the wings. Whether or not they will be productive remains to be seen. The defensive line is stout, with Jer’Zhan Newton (5.5 sacks and 13 TFL), Keith Randolph (53 tackles and 13 TFL), TeRah Edwards, and Ohio State transfer Denzel Daxon holding down the fort.

Full-Game Side Bet

Insiders Status:

Rating:

The Illini should be on upset alert, but I don't think they'll get the message! The Rockets are explosive, featuring a dual-threat quarterback with a chip on his shoulder to prove he belonged in the Power-5. He's not the only one on the Toledo roster that feels that way, either.

UT has several defensive players, including its leading tackler, Gant, who started their careers at Big Ten or other Power-5 schools. Some of these players were highly-rated recruits, even more so than some Illinois players. There's no denying that Toledo can keep this game close, but it will take some help from U of I for the visitors to pull off the upset.

I'm not sold on Altmyer as the future face of the Illini program, and I feel confident he will look exposed at times by the experienced Rockets secondary. On the other side of the ball, Illinois could look vulnerable, too. It lost some very important players from last year's unit, along with 17 of the team's interceptions. Toledo won't fear Illinois like some MAC teams do when they travel to Big Ten country.

I don't want the points — give me the Rockets straight-up. They'll be drinking champagne in Champaign on Saturday!

Full-Game Total Pick

Insiders Status:

Rating:

The Rockets' offense is going to surprise the Fighting Illini on Saturday, getting out to a comfortable early lead before the home team can stage a comeback. Finn is a talented passer who can also get it done on the run, keeping U of I off-balance. Newton is a serious threat in the slot and Stuart has home-run ability at running back, too. This is not the same Illinois defense that took the field in 2022.

With the score getting a little out of hand too soon, Illinois will speed things up offensively, leading to a couple of quicker scores than it would have usually. U of I returns several receivers who need to make an impact early in the year to separate from the pack. As talented as the Rockets' secondary is, it may have trouble holding up as the game progresses. Toledo's inexperience on the defensive line and at linebacker will put extra pressure on the defensive backfield, as well.

Bet on an exciting, high-scoring game on Saturday in Big Ten country.
