The Arkansas State Red Wolves (3-9, 7-5 ATS, 7-5 O/U) collide with the Oklahoma Sooners (6-6, 4-8 ATS, 5-7 O/U) in the season opener on Saturday afternoon. The Red Wolves posted a 3-9 mark last year and went 1-7 in Sun Belt action. The Sooners sported a 6-6 record and 3-6 in Big 12 play. This marks the first meeting since 2000.

Red Wolves Looking for Improvement at QB

The Arkansas State Red Wolves are searching for improvement after winning just three games in the 2022 season. One of those three victories was against Grambling State of the FCS. The program was busy recruiting in the offseason but winning more than four or five games will be a challenge.

The good news is the Red Wolves are optimistic about new QB J.T. Shrout. The senior spent the 2019 and 2020 seasons in Tennessee and saw plenty of action with the Colorado Buffaloes last year, posting 1220 passing yards and a 7:8 TD to INT-ratio. He should be able to build on those numbers in an easier conference. The offense lost several players including the rushing and receiving leader. Brian Snead was second with 299 rushing yards in 2022 and should take more carries this year.

The program is glad to have Corey Rucker back on the squad. He posted 816 receiving yards with Arkansas State in 2021, transferred to South Carolina in 2022 where he only had 52 receiving yards, and decided to come back. He should thrive under new QB Shrout.

The Red Wolves' defense was horrible last season but were an inexperienced group. They have a young group of linebackers that should improve after taking their hits last year. Melique Straker collected 81 tackles last season, placing him second on the squad. Straker could end up being an NFL draft pick. The pass defense was marked 91st last year while the rush defense was 84th.

Last season, Arkansas State scored an average of 25 points, ranking them 87th. The defense conceded an average of 31.4 points, pegging them 108th.

Sooners Aiming for Progress After Subpar Year

The Oklahoma Sooners had a mediocre season in 2022 in a deep BIG 12 conference and are expected to be an improved squad. They were voted to finish third in the conference preseason media poll but it’s a challenging task considering the numbers of players lost to the NFL in the offseason. The Sooners are ranked 20th in the AP Top 25.

Dillon Gabriel is back as QB1. The senior spent the first three seasons of his career with UCF where he dominated. Gabriel was stellar in his first year in Norman, accumulating 3168 passing yards complemented by a 25:6 TD to INT ratio. Look for him to get more involved in the ground game this year.

The Sooners' offense lost many key pieces. Rushing leader Eric Gray was drafted into the NFL. Jovantae Barnes is ready to step into the spotlight after a promising season. The sophomore RB amassed 519 rushing yards on an average of 4.5 yards per rush. The squad lost key receivers including star Marvin Mims Jr. to the NFL. They return Jalil Farooq who recorded 466 receiving yards along with Drake Stoops who collected 393 receiving yards.

The Sooners’ defense was a disaster last year and will need to be improved if they want to compete atop the conference. Tackling leader Danny Stutsman is back. He is all over the field and has the ability to make sacks and picks. The defensive line now has Notre Dame transfer Jacob Lacey.  The program brought in LB Dasan McCullough from Indiana and a transfer from Texas Tech safety Reggie Pearson. There weren't any bright spots last season, ranking 108th against the pass and run.

Oklahoma averaged 32.9 points last year, good for 35th. The defense allowed an average of 29.6 points, placing them 99th in the country.

The Arkansas State Red Wolves always schedule power conference opponents early in the season. Last season they played Ohio State and Memphis and covered each game. I am not sold on the Sooners yet after going only 6-6 last year. They lost their rushing leader and best two receivers to the NFL plus they still have an unproven defense. 

Furthermore, Arkansas State has a new QB with power conference experience in J.T. Shrout. He saw plenty of action with the Colorado Buffaloes last season and should be able to make noise against a poor Sooners pass defense that ranked 108th in the FBS last year.
