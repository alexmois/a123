The South Alabama Jaguars (0-0, 0-0 Sun Belt) will be trying to pick up their first win of the season when they face the Tulane Green Wave (0-0, 0-0 American) on Saturday night.

The Jaguars finished the 2022 season with a 10-3 record. The Green Wave finished the 2022 season with a 12-2 record.

South Alabama and Tulane split their two meetings.

Jaguars Trying To Improve on Best Season In School History

The Jaguars won 10 games for the first time in school history last season, which has made them one of the favorites to win the Sun Belt this season. They will try to improve on their record this season, starting with a win over the Green Wave on Saturday.

South Alabama averaged 29.8 points per game last season. They threw for 267.7 yards and rushed for 156.7 yards per game.

Carter Bradley completed 65 percent of his passes for 3,336 yards, 28 touchdowns, and 12 interceptions. La'Damian Webb led the team with 209 carries for 1,062 yards and 13 touchdowns, while Devin Voisin led the team with 64 catches for 867 yards and five touchdowns.

South Alabama played well defensively last season, giving up 22.5 points per game. They have most of their players back and will have to be just as good as they were last season if they want to get the win.

Green Wave Going For Fifth Consecutive Win

The Green Wave closed out the 2022 season on a high note, winning their last four games, including a 46-45 upset over 10th-ranked USC in the Cotton Bowl. They enter the season as the favorites to win the AAC and will try to kick off the season with a win on Saturday, which will give them their fifth win in a row dating back to last season.

Tulane averaged 34.8 points per game last season. They threw for 236.6 yards and rushed for 204.8 yards per game.

Michael Pratt completed 64 percent of his passes for 3,010 yards, 27 touchdowns, and five interceptions last season. He also had 478 rushing yards and 10 touchdowns.

They lost their leading rusher from last season, but Shaadie Clayton-Johnson is expected to carry most of the load this season. He finished the 2022 season with 333 rushing yards and two touchdowns. Jha'Quan Jackson finished third in receiving last season with 554 yards and three touchdowns. With the top two receivers leaving, he will be their go-to guy this season.

Tulane played well defensively last season, giving up 23.9 points per game. They weren’t as efficient against the run and will have to play better this season if they want to repeat as conference champions.
