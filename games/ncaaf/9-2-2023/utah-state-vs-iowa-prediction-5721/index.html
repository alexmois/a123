The Utah State Aggies and Iowa Hawkeyes will kick off their 2023 football seasons on Saturday at Kinnick Stadium. Kickoff is at 12:00 p.m. ET.

Utah State has an over/under win total of five wins. It will also play Idaho State, James Madison, and Connecticut in non-conference play. USU was picked to finish eighth in the 2023 Mountain West preseason polls. The Aggies were 6-7 overall last season, including 2-4 in road games.

Iowa has an over/under win total of 8.5 wins. It will travel to Iowa State and host Western Michigan in its other non-conference games. U of I was picked to finish second in the Big Ten West in Cleveland.com's annual preseason poll. The Hawkeyes were 8-5 overall last season, including 4-3 at home.

Iowa is a 24-point spread favorite and the game total is 44.5 points.

Utah State hopes a change at OC jump-starts its offense

Utah State was not fun to watch on offense last season, averaging 354 yards and 22 points per game. Head coach Blake Anderson will take over play-calling duties in 2023, a position he's plenty familiar with. Before his HC stints at Arkansas State and USU, Anderson was an offensive coordinator for eight seasons, most recently at North Carolina. His Tar Heels offense averaged 432.4 total yards per game in 2013.

The Aggies return starting quarterback Cooper Legas (1,499 passing yards, 11 TDs, and 10 INTs), but they lost two of their top three receivers, Brian Cobbs and Justin McGriff, who combined for 106 receptions, 1,361 yards, and nine touchdowns. Slot receiver Terrell Vaughn will be asked to help fill the void. Utah State must also replace running back Calvin Tyler (1,122 yards), but Legas is a dual-threat QB, and second-leading rusher Robert Briggs (4.8 yards-per-carry) is back. They will run behind a line that has size but lacks experience.

Utah State's defense was decimated by injuries in 2022, which is at least partially why it surrendered nearly 400 yards per contest. USU will depend on defensive tackles Hale Motu’apuaka (five sacks) and Poukesi Vakauta to set the tone up front and veteran linebacker MJ Tafisi (69 tackles) to control the middle of the field. New defensive coordinator Joe Cauthen must replace several defensive ends who left via the transfer portal, but it's unclear who will step up from a group of unknowns. The secondary features several new faces, including Al Ashford (Wisconsin), Avante Dickerson (Oregon), Jaiden Francois (UCF), Simeon Harris (Colorado), and Jaylen Martin (Cal). Considering their limited experience in the system, though, it's unlikely they'll supplant any of the starters by Saturday afternoon.

Iowa has high hopes for its 2023 offense

The Iowa defense is expected to be the backbone of the 2023 Hawkeyes team, even if the offense generates all the headlines. After a putrid offensive performance last season, all eyes will be on offensive coordinator Brian Ferentz. Could U of I surprise us with a competent O?

If that's going to happen, Michigan transfer quarterback Cade McNamara will likely be the reason. While medically cleared, he is questionable for Saturday's season opener. As the Wolverines' starting signal-caller in 2021, McNamara passed for 2,576 yards, 15 touchdowns, and six interceptions but lost his QB1 status last season to the higher-upside J.J. McCarthy. The Nevada native has a lot of Big Ten experience, can run when he needs to, and passes the ball well enough to keep the chains moving. For the Hawkeyes, that would be a saving grace.

McNamara will rely on a couple of quality tight ends in Erick All (437 receiving yards in 2021 with Michigan) and Luke Lachey (398 receiving yards and four TDs last season) to move the football. The wide receivers should be more involved with better QB play, and it's likely the offensive line will look better in 2023 with more offensive balance.

The Hawkeyes defense was second-best nationally in total and scoring defense, sixth in pass defense, 11th against the run, and tenth in third down stops last year. While the unit must replace stars Jack Campbell, Seth Benson, Riley Moss, and Lukas Van Ness, there's still plenty of leftover talent. Cornerback Cooper DeJean (75 tackles and five INTs) will be playing on Sundays soon, Deontae Craig (7.5 sacks) is a serious pass-rushing threat, and the linebackers will be as stout as ever.

I'm going to take the points with the Aggies on Saturday afternoon. Iowa may be more upbeat about its offense, but that doesn't mean it's worth a 24-point spread in Week One with an injured quarterback. The Hawkeyes haven't solved their offensive inefficiencies by simply adding Michigan's backup quarterback — they're going to have to prove it drive after drive on Saturday. I don't see that happening, no matter the opponent.

Besides, I have higher expectations for the Aggies offense than most. Anderson was a successful playcaller before becoming a head coach and I'm confident he will be a natural with the headset on. Utah State has experience on offense, plus a healthy quarterback. I'll bet on that showing up in Week One over the unknown factors Iowa's offense will be juggling.

Bet USU +24.

Full-Game Total Pick

Insiders Status:

Rating:

Both offenses will have something to prove in Saturday's season opener. I feel there is too much skepticism that they will show improvement, as 44.5 points is too low of a total.

The Hawkeyes' defense is undoubtedly talented, but it's lacking the punch it had in 2022 after the departure of several key contributors. I'm confident Iowa's next stars will step up throughout the season, but they won't be apparent in Week One. The Aggies' line is big enough to move the football against a Big Ten team and Legas' dual-threat capability will extend at least a couple of Utah State's drive, leading to extra points on the scoreboard.

All signs point to McNamara taking snaps on Saturday, and Iowa appears to have found some solutions for 2022's offensive problems. The Hawkeyes' run game is usually solid, so any semblance of offensive balance should result in more touchdowns than field goals.

I'll bet on the over at 44.5 in the Aggies-Hawkeyes game!

Prediction: Over 44.5
