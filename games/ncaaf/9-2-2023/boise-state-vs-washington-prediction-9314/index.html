The Washington Huskies play host to the Boise State Broncos on Saturday afternoon from Alaska Airlines Field at Husky Stadium, in a Pacific Northwest clash. The Huskies enter with PAC 12 title hopes for the season, while the Broncos are looking to build off a successful return to form in the Mountain West last season. The last five meetings, which dates back to 2007, leans slightly into Washington's favor, with their three wins. The last time these two met, the Huskies won 38-7 with ease. The home team has dominated historically in this series, so it'll be interesting to see if that holds strong in this version of budding rivalry.

Broncos Hit The Road

Boise State found a resurgence in form last season and even though it ended without a Mountain West title, this group certainly showed how that they expect to be a force this season. They went 10-4 last year, including going 8-0 in the regular season in the Mountain West. The bad part of that record, means that they went 2-3 against non-Mountain West teams. The schedule is a challenge again this season, which puts even more pressure on them in this opener. Going on the road would always be a challenge but adding in that they'll be going to one of the loudest stadiums in the country, will make things even more difficult. They bring some experience back, especially on the offensive side of the ball and with conference realignment continuing to loom large, an upset in this one could carry additional weight.

The Broncos have three injuries to think about as they take the field in this one, with a trio of wide receivers having been ruled out already, one for the season. Latrell Caples (leg) is a massive absence, especially in a high-scoring one like this, as the leading receiver from a season ago is going to leave a void after putting up 51 catches and 549 yards last season.

The offense will be thrilled to have Taylen Green back under center, especially given his dual-threat ability. The signal caller threw for just over 2,000 yards last season, tossing 14 touchdowns compared to six interceptions. Now that he's had a full season to prepare as the starter, the offense should be far more aligned to open things up on Saturday. The real question marks remain on the defensive side, especially considering the offense they'll be faced off against in this one. Inexperience on that end could come back to haunt them on the road against the Huskies.

Huskies Eyeing Pac 12 Title

An off-season that saw the Huskies announce future plans to leave for the Big Ten Conference leads into one of the most exciting seasons in recent memory. Last year saw the Huskies grab an impressive bowl win over Texas, while also grabbing wins against Oregon and Oregon State during Pac-12 play. The even better news is that they bring back quite a few players, as they try to navigate an interesting schedule. The game with the Broncos has always been one circled on the schedule but now more than ever, they need to make sure to get the win and start the season with some positive momentum.

Much like their opponent, the Huskies have some injuries of their own to worry about. Cameron Davis (lower body) has been ruled out for the season, as he was expected to be the starter or get decent action this season. The Huskies will also be without Giles Jackson (thumb), a wide receiver who saw a decent snap count last season. With plenty of depth surrounding them, these absences shouldn't alter the direction in which this one goes.

Washington's success this season starts and ends with Michael Penix Jr. Expected to be a Heisman contender this season, the lefty is one of the most experienced players in college football, as well as most talented. He threw for over 4,000 yards last season and has plenty of options. Rome Odunze and Jalen McMillan both had over 70 catches last season and over 1,000 yards, as they return for another season. The offense was one of the best in the country last season and with no signs of slowing down in year two, the Broncos will be their first victim.
