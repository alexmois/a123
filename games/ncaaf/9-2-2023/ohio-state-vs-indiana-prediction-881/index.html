Big Ten football action returns on Saturday when the Ohio State Buckeyes travel to Indiana to take on the Hoosiers. Ohio State is coming off an 11-2 year that ended with a disappointing one-point loss to eventual National Champion Georgia. The Hoosiers finished just 4-8 in 2022 and hope to regain the form that had them bowl-eligible the previous two seasons. Last season, the Buckeyes pounded Indiana at home 56-14. The opening kickoff for Saturday's game is scheduled for 3:30 PM EST.

Retooled and Ready to Go

The Ohio State Buckeyes were so close to earning a spot in the National Championship game last year. Just one point separated the Buckeyes from a defeated Georgia and moving on and going for another national title. The playoff loss to Georgia also marked the first time in head coach Ryan Day's four-year tenure that the Buckeyes lost two games in a row. He will now try and regain the Big Ten title and move on to the playoffs yet again with a new quarterback under center. Heading into the season, the heir apparent to former quarterback CJ Stroud appeared to be Junior Kyle McCord but Day has been non-commital about his starting quarterback heading into Saturday's game. Freshman Devin Brown has also played well in camp and has gained ground in the fight for the starting job. Day has admitted as much that both quarterbacks will see action on Saturday. Regardless, both quarterbacks will have tons of skill players to work with, notably Junior WR Marvin Harrison Jr. Harrison Jr. is part of a receiving group that returns three starters from last year. TreVeyon Henderson returns at running back and the Buckeyes bring back two starting offensive linemen from last year's team. The Buckeyes finished 27th in rushing offense last year and 12th in passing offense. Given the influx of returning talent, those are very achievable numbers again this season.

While the Buckeyes' offense looks deep and talented, the team's defense is a bit of a concern. Their defense allowed an average of nearly 44 points in their two losses, at Michigan and against Georgia in the playoffs. OSU returns five starters from last year including linebackers Tommy Eichenberg and Steele Chambers. The Buckeyes return one cornerback from last year's group and have made some additions via the transfer portal to shore up the secondary. In 2022, the Buckeyes were 12th in total defense while finishing 23rd against the run and 26th against the pass.

New Blood Under Center

Head Coach Tom Allen, like Day at Ohio State, has yet to announce his official starting quarterback for the opener against Ohio State. The Hoosiers will feature a freshman under center, whichever way he decides to go. Tayven Jackson and Brendan Sorsby have both been battling for the position this summer and there is a chance both could see time on Saturday. Indiana returns four starters on offense from last year's 4-8 team. They have two returning offensive linemen, three wide receivers, and running back Josh Henderson back to anchor the offense. The key will be the team's offensive line. The Hoosiers offense was ranked just 112th in the country last season, 121st rushing the ball and 81st through the air.

The Hoosiers' defense returns two starters from last year's team. The most notable returning player is linebacker Aaron Casey. Casey led the team in tackles last season but will likely be surrounded by a large amount of transfers in 2023. Last year, Indiana was ranked 121st in total defense. They were ranked 98th against the run and 123rd against the pass. The defensive unit was a far cry from the one that led the Hoosiers to back-to-back bowl games in 2019-20.

Full-Game Side Bet

Insiders Status:

Rating:

This should be an interesting opening game for both teams. Ohio State and Indiana both come into this game with uncertainty at the most important position on the field, quarterback. Both coaches have also failed to name a starter publicly, even if they know who they really want under center. Ohio State is the better team and that isn't debatable. They have an embarrassment of riches on the offensive end and have playmakers on defense. This is a team that was within a whisker of playing for the National Title last season. Still, the fact that Day hasn't named his starter is cause for some concern. While it could simply be gamesmanship, McCord was expected to be the heir-apparent to Stroud but he's not put a stranglehold on the position. Indiana will be painfully inexperienced at quarterback this season with two freshmen battling for the position. Either Jackson or Sorsby will benefit from an improved offensive line this season and returning experience at running back. The Hoosiers should also benefit from a slightly raw OSU defensive front. I fully expect the Hoosiers to be far more aggressive on the defensive end and blitz early and often in this game, much like their back-to-back bowl teams did in 2019-20. Ohio State will win this game outright but thirty points to give up is too rich for my blood.

Take Indiana with the points.

Prediction: Indiana +30

Full-Game Total Pick

Insiders Status:

Rating:

Last season, the Buckeyes put up 56 points themselves in their matchup with the Hoosiers. While Stroud is gone, the nucleus of one of college football's most prolific offenses has returned. I expect the Buckeyes to move up and down the field against a Hoosiers defense that is very much a work in progress. In addition, Indiana is likely going to try and blitz frequently in this game, leaving their corners in one-on-one battles with the Buckeyes' dominant receivers. On the other side of the ball, the Ohio State defense is also a work in progress at this point and could be exposed if the Hoosiers are able to give their young quarterbacks time to throw the ball. I expect Ohio State to win this one comfortably but I also expect plenty of offense.

Take the over.
