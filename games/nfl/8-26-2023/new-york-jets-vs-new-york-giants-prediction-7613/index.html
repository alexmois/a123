#123 New York Jets vs.
#124 New York Giants
Saturday, August 26, 2023 at 4:00pm EDT
MetLife Stadium, East Rutherford
Written by Dave Kovaleski

Click to see the pick now

It is the battle of New York as the New York Jets face the New York Giants. This is a rare home game for both teams, as both squads call MetLife Stadium in East Rutherford, NJ home. The Jets are 2-1 on the preseason and are one of just two teams to play four preseason games. The Giants are 1-1 on the preseason. Kickoff is Saturday night at 6:00 p.m. ET.

Aaron Rodgers will play Saturday

The Jets have played one more preseason game than every other team except the Browns, because they took part in the Hall of Fame game to kick off the season. But in none of the three preseason games thus far have Jets fans seen the great Aaron Rodgers take a snap in a Jets uniform. Well, that is set to change on Saturday as Rodgers is projected to get the start against the Giants. How much he will play is not clear, but don't flip the channel during the commercials, because it probably won't be that much.

Aaron Rodgers on playing this Saturday v Giants:

“There’s always that risk- reward over the years, it just hasn’t made sense based on a number of different factors, but I used to enjoy playing in the preseason back in my younger years, just to maybe take that first hit, or feel… pic.twitter.com/FxnB9UK3dM

— Harrison Glaser (@NYJetsTFMedia) August 22, 2023

“There’s always that risk-reward,” Rodgers said, reported the NY Daily News. “I think over the years, it just hasn’t made sense based on a number of different factors, but I used to enjoy playing the preseason back in my younger years, just to kind of maybe take that first hit or feel kind of the nerves in the first drive, so I look forward to being out there." We won't see the other big-name offseason pickup, Dalvin Cook, or last year's standout rookie running back Breece Hall, but both should be ready for week one. But Rodgers will have his No. 1 target, receiver Garrett Wilson, along with offseason pickup Mecole Hardman, and old pal from Green Bay, Allen Lazard, to throw to on Saturday.

Giants get first preseason win

The New York Giants got their first win of the preseason last Saturday, scoring a 21-19 win over the Carolina Panthers. This was a game where quarterback Daniel Jones got the start, and he looked pretty good in his limited action. Jones went 8 for 9 for 69 yards and one touchdown with a quarterback rating of 135. His backups, Tommy DeVito and Tyrod Taylor, also looked pretty good as DeVito was 9 for 11 for 88 yards while Taylor was 9 for 13 for 90 yards and a touchdown and he ran for 21 yards. It is doubtful that Jones will play against the Jets, so expect a heavy dose of DeVito and Taylor in this one.

Daniel Jones is done. Don't need to see any more. He's ready for the regular season. Unlikely we see him in preseason game No. 3 next week vs. Jets.

— Jordan Raanan (@JordanRaanan) August 18, 2023

Rookie wide receiver Jalin Hyatt was impressive in this game as he caught four passes for 35 yards and a touchdown. Tight end Daniel Bellinger also caught a touchdown pass. The other tight end, Darren Waller, had a decent night too, as he caught 3 passes for 30 yards. Saquon Barkley has not yet played in the preseason and while his status is not clear for Saturday, it would be a surprise if he plays against the Jets. But Barkley looked good in practice this past week, so who knows? The backfield duties will likely be handled by rookie Eric Gray, James Robinson, and potentially Matt Breida, who has seen limited action thus far.

Rating:

The Giants played their starting quarterback, Daniel Jones, in game two of their preseason last week, but he won't play this week, according to reports. On the other hand, Aaron Rodgers will make his first appearance in a Jets uniform and reports are that he could see extended action. While Rodgers could be a little rusty, it is more likely that the veteran will hit the ground running and make an impact in his time on the field. The Jets will play quite a few starters in this one, and to me, that's enough to take them over a Giants team that will rest most key pieces and use this as a chance to finalize roster slots. Full-Game Total Pick

Insiders Status:

Rating:

The Jets offense hasn't been all that impressive thus far, but then again, they haven't yet played Rodgers or a lot of their key pieces. With Rodgers, Wilson, and a lot of starters playing on Saturday, I would expect to see the Jets put some points on the board. The Giants looked halfway decent last week, but Jones probably won't play so they could struggle hitting the 20s. But look for the Jets to do most of the heavy lifting in this one, as they should be able to push this one past the total. Take the over in this one.
