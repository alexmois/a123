The Miami Dolphins (1-1) will be trying to close out the preseason with a win when they face the Jacksonville Jaguars (2-0) on Saturday night. The game will be played at TIAA Bank Field and it is scheduled to begin at 7 PM. ET.

Dolphins Going For Second Consecutive Win

The Dolphins bounced back from their loss to Atlanta with a win over the Texans in their last game. They will try to keep the momentum going with a win over the Jaguars, which will give them their second win in a row.

Miami is averaging 15.5 points per game through two games. They are throwing for 171 yards and rushing for 186.5 yards per game.

Skylar Thompson leads the Dolphins in passing this preseason. He has completed 66 percent of his passes for 261 yards, three touchdowns, and two interceptions. Salvon Ahmed has 12 carries for 99 yards, while Braxton Berrios has five catches for 44 yards and one touchdown.

Miami’s defense has been good, with the team giving up 11 points per game. They gave up three points in their last game and will need a similar effort if they want to win this game.

Jaguars Going For Third Consecutive Win

The Jaguars have played well this preseason and come into this game on a two-game winning streak. They will try to keep the momentum going with a win over the Dolphins, which will give them their third win in a row.

Jacksonville is averaging 26.5 points per game in the preseason. They are throwing for 261.5 yards and rushing for 145 yards per game.

Nathan Rourke leads the Jaguars in passing this preseason. He has completed 66 percent of his passes for 274 yards and one touchdown. Tank Bigsby has 22 carries for 122 yards, while Elijah Cooks has four catches for 111 yards.

Jacksonville has played well defensively, giving up 15.5 points per game. They gave up 23 points in their last game and will have to do a better job if they want to get the win.

Full-Game Side Bet

Insiders Status:

Rating:

The Jaguars won their first two preseason games, which were both on the road. They played very well offensively and scored at least 25 points in both games. Rookie quarterback Nathan Rourke has outplayed C.J. Beathard so far and will likely get the start in this game. But, they won’t need him to do much because their ground game has been impressive so far, with the team gaining 290 yards and four touchdowns in two games. The Dolphins struggled against the run in their first preseason game, but they played well in their last game against a Houston team that is averaging less than 100 rushing yards per game in two games, so expect the Jaguars to test Miami’s run defense in this game. The Dolphins didn’t play well offensively in their first game, but they did a better job against the Texans, scoring 28 points in the win. With their backup quarterbacks taking most of the preseason snaps, they haven’t looked good throwing the ball, but they’ve done a good job on the ground, gaining over 360 yards in two games. But, the Jaguars have done a great job defending the run, holding their first two opponents under 130 combined rushing yards, so expect them to slow down Miami’s ground game and force their quarterbacks to try to beat them through the air, which isn’t going to happen. Go with Jacksonville to cover the spread.

Prediction: Jacksonville Jaguars -4
