The Las Vegas Raiders pay a visit to Arlington, Texas and AT&T Stadium on Saturday to play the Dallas Cowboys in the final preseason game for both. Las Vegas has won each of its first two preseason games. The Raiders opened with a 34-7 win over San Francisco followed by a 34-17 victory over the Rams in the debut of Raiders quarterback Jimmy Garoppolo. Dallas has lost each of its first two preseason games after falling to Jacksonville 28-23 in its preseason opener followed by a 22-14 loss to Seattle.

Garoppolo will see more action on Saturday

The biggest off-season acquisition for the Las Vegas Raiders was that of quarterback Jimmy Garoppolo, who will see plenty of action on Saturday. Garoppolo completed each of his four passes for 39 yards in last weekend's victory, which was his Raiders’ debut. Rookie Aidan O’Connell and veteran Brian Hoyer will also see reps under center on Saturday versus the Cowboys. Expect to see Las Vegas use its first-team offense and defense for much of the first half.

 

The Raiders will see how off-season acquisition Jakobi Meyers works with Garoppolo, as the wide receiver will see plenty of action on Saturday. Hunter Renfrow is the only projected starter on offense that is questionable for Saturday. Las Vegas does not have any players on its starting defense listed as injured, but backup Darien Butler is on injured reserve and Brandon Facyson is questionable.

Preseason wins and losses means little to nothing to Mike McCarty

Dallas starting quarterback Dak Prescott has yet to see action this preseason, as Cooper Rush and Will Grier have split time under center. Rush has 179 yards passing with one touchdown while Grier has passed for 355 yards with a pair of touchdowns and a pair of interceptions. Rookie running back Deuce Vaughn has averaged 4.9 yards per carry. Although Dallas no longer has Ezekiel Elliott, the Cowboys will have Tony Pollard starting and rookie Vaughn has impressed through two games. (edit: Dallas just traded for Trey Lance from San Francisco to back up Prescott at quarterback).

Do not expect to see Dak Prescott on the field against the Raiders as the Cowboys will use more of Rush and Grier and sit their starting QB until the opening week of the regular season. Dallas head coach Mike McCarthy has said it's much more important to have players healthy at the end of the regular season especially those that are at the top of the depth chart and by sitting out most of his starters he will guarantee they will play on opening day. Dallas has one starter on defense, free safety Donovan Wilson, listed as questionable, along with four backups.

Best Bets for this Game

Full-Game Side Bet

Insiders Status:

Rating:

Dallas head coach Mike McCarthy has made it obvious that winning preseason games is not high on his list of priorities but keeping his players healthy is. Therefore, expect to see the same players on the field for Dallas that have played the first two preseason games. Dallas may even sit rookie running back Deuce Vaughn, who has impressed coaches with his speed and ability to break tackles. Las Vegas on the other hand is expected to play most if not all of its starters on Saturday versus Dallas including off season acquisitions quarterback Jimmy Garoppolo and wide receiver Jakobi Meyers. In all likelihood, Las Vegas will jump out to lead early under Garoppolo which will hold up during the course of the game as backups and others will see plenty of action in the second half. Las Vegas will have its first team defense on display early and if the Cowboys hold true to not playing their starters on offense, then expect to see little success from the Dallas offense against the improved Raiders defense.

Prediction: Las Vegas Raiders -5
