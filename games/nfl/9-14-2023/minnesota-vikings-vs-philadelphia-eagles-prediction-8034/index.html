The Minnesota Vikings travel to Philadelphia on Thursday evening to take on the Eagles. The Vikings are coming off a 13-4 season in 2022 that ended in a first-round playoff loss to the Giants. The Eagles finished last season at 14-3 and took home the NFC title before falling to the Kansas City Chiefs in the Super Bowl. Expectations are high for both teams in the 2023 season. The opening kickoff on Thursday night is set for 8:15 PM EST.

These teams met in September of last season with the Eagles winning 24-7 as Jalen Hurts threw for 333 yards and ran for 57 yards with two TDs.

Looking to Avoid a Repeat Performance

For the second straight season, the Vikings head to Philadelphia for a prime-time game in week two of the season. Last year, the Vikings came into Philadelphia at 1-0 following a huge win over the Green Bay Packers at home. This season, the Vikings travel to Philadelphia after a disappointing 20-17 home loss to the Tampa Bay Bucs. The loss was made even more jarring by the final score. In 2022, the Vikings went a perfect 11-0 in one-score games but in their first close game of this season, the Vikings were unable to pull out the victory.

“There was quite a few self-inflicted mistakes,” Vikings head coach Kevin O'Connell said after the tough loss.

Offensively, the Vikings relied heavily on their passing game. They ran for a total of 41 yards in the game on 17 carries for a disappointing 2.4 yards per carry. The Vikings threw for 328 yards in the game with quarterback Kirk Cousins going 33-of-44 for 344 yards and two touchdowns. He was also picked off once in the game. All-Pro wideout Justin Jefferson had nine catches for 150 yards while T.J. Hockenson, who was secured to a long-term deal just prior to the season, had eight catches for 35 yards in the losing effort. The Vikings offense committed three turnovers in the game, all by Cousins. He fumbled twice and threw an interception in the game.

Defensively, the Vikings allowed a total of just 242 yards including 168 through the air. They held the Bucs to just nine first downs in the game as well. The Vikings' defense did not create any turnovers on the day however and were only able to record one sack and just two tackles for loss against a subpar Bucs' offense. Camryn Bynum led the Vikings with 10 tackles in the game.

Last season, the Vikings went into Philadelphia riding high after a week one victory over rival Green Bay and then were smoked by the Eagles 24-7. This season, the team is licking its wounds following an upset loss to the Bucs. Minnesota will need to find some running lanes for new starting running back Alexander Mattison. Mattison replaced Dalvin Cook, now with the Jets, after serving as his primary backup for the last two seasons. He had 11 carries for just 34 yards against the Bucs on Sunday. In 2022, Minnesota finished 29th rushing the football but fourth through the air. However, if they are unable to get a ground game going, the Eagles and their ferocious front four on the defensive line will be able to pin their ears back against Cousins and pressure the veteran quarterback.

On the defensive side of the ball, the Vikings will need to create some turnovers in week two to pull off the upset of the Eagles on the road. The Vikings finished 31st in total defense in 2022. They were 21st against the run and an even worse 31st against the pass. They played better against the pass on Sunday but the Bucs passing attack is not nearly as lethal as the Eagles.

Injury Report: C Garrett Bradbury (back) is questionable.

Escaping with a Win

It certainly wasn't easy for the Philadelphia Eagles in week one at New England but they were able to escape with a 25-20 win to open the season at 1-0. The Eagles scored the game's first 16 points, thanks to two straight New England turnovers, and held on for the win after stopping New England on downs inside the 30-yard line in the final minute. It wasn't the cleanest of victories for the defending NFC champions but that is to be expected as the team navigates this season with both a new offensive and defensive coordinator.

“That’s something that we’re navigating," Eagles quarterback Jaylen Hurts said after the game. "We’re going to continue to grow. And it’s always better when you win.”

Offensively, the Eagles gained a total of just 251 yards in the game. They finished with just 154 yards through the air and 97 on the ground against a stingy New England defense. Hurts went 22-of-33 through the air for just 170 yards and a touchdown. He was also sacked three times in the game. On the ground, Hurts ran the ball nine times for just 37 yards. Running back Kenneth Gainwell led the way with 14 carries for 54 yards while newcomer D'Andre Swift had just 1 carry in the game for three yards. The team's starting wideouts, A.J. Brown and DeVonta Smith each had seven catches in the game but were held to 79 and 47 yards respectively. Smith did have a touchdown grab in the win.

On the defensive side of the ball, the Eagles forced two turnovers in the game. Cornerback Darius Slay returned a first-quarter interception 70 yards for a touchdown to give the Eagles a commanding lead. Overall, the Eagles' defense had two sacks and five tackles for loss while also pressuring the quarterback ten times and recording seven quarterback hits. New England did record 382 yards of total offense in the game but just 76 on the ground.

The Eagles will be adjusting on the fly this season with new coordinators on both sides of the ball. It showed in week one with the team only recording 18 points courtesy of its offense. Defensively, the Eagles also allowed nearly 400 yards of total offense. In 2022, the Eagles ranked first in football in total defense with the league's best secondary and deepest group of defensive linemen. The return of cornerback Darius Slay paid immediate dividends on Sunday with his pick-six interception. The Eagles were fourth against the run in 2022 and that held true on Sunday as the Patriots failed to crack 100 yards on the ground. The Eagles were also 9th against the pass last year but definitely struggled to contain the Patriots' passing attack, which had been one of the worst in football in 2022.

Injury Report: S Reed Blankenship (ribs), CB James Bradberry (ribs), DT Fletcher Cox (ribs), LB Nakobe Dean (foot), and RB Kenneth Gainwell (ribs) are all listed as questionable after missing practice to start the week.

The Eagles manhandled the Vikings last year on their way to a 24-7 win in week two. They harassed Cousins all night and forced him into tough throws. Cousins threw three interceptions in that game and star wideout Jefferson was held to 48 yards receiving on six catches. The most concerning thing about the rematch this year is the status of Vikings' center Bradbury. Bradbury played just seven snaps in week one and his loss was evident as Cousins had two fumbles due to poor snap exchanges. Now, the Vikings could be again without Bradbury going up against arguably the best defensive front in the NFL. If Bradbury is unable to go, the Vikings push up the middle and could once again force Cousins into some difficult throws and create more turnovers. Coming off a three-turnover game in the loss to the Bucs, the Vikings can ill afford to lose the turnover battle on the road in Philadelphia.

On the defensive end, the Vikings were decent against the Bucs but did allow Mayfield some critical runs late in the game to set up the Bucs' winning field goal. Their inability to stop Mayfield on the scramble does not bode well given the elusiveness of Hurts. Hurts played conservatively in New England and did seem to have difficulty with New England's team speed, particularly with the Patriots' use of hybrid safeties instead of linebackers. The Vikings aren't as equipped as New England to offer the Eagles those kinds of looks. I expect Hurts to have a stronger game on the ground and for the Eagles WR duo of Smith and Brown to have more success down the field.

Take the Eagles -7

Prediction: Eagles -7

Rating:

With a quick turnaround following week one, I don't expect this game to be particularly high-scoring. Week one in the NFL saw an abundance of lower-scoring games as the trend of teams sitting out their starters for much of the preseason has slowed the offenses. Mistakes were frequent and there was certainly a disconnect in many cases between quarterbacks and receivers. While the Vikings ran up over 300 yards passing in week one, they only generated 17 points in their loss to the Bucs. Meanwhile, the Eagles scored just 18 points offensively against New England and had a number of drives slowed in New England territory. Hurts and company are still getting used to a new system and the Eagles are also dealing with a new running attack with Miles Sanders now in Carolina. I think the Eagles win this one comfortably but the scoring will once again be low in week two for each team.
