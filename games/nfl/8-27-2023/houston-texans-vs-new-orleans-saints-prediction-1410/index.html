Still Undecided for Week 1

The Houston Texans organization took several steps this offseason to improve the team and move forward in a positive direction. The two most impactful moves made by Houston were the hiring of former San Francisco Defensive Coordinator DeMeco Ryans as their new head coach and then the drafting of quarterback C.J. Stroud out of Ohio State with the second overall pick in the draft. Ryans knows that his success with the organization will likely be tied directly to the growth of Stroud as a quarterback. Stroud has shown improvement throughout camp and in each of the first two preseason games. In week one at New England, Stroud went 2-of-4 for just 13 yards while looking uncertain in the pocket. Last week, in a 28-3 loss to the Dolphins, Stroud showed vast improvement while going 7-of-12 for 60 yards and leading the Texans to a field goal in the first half. While Ryans has yet to commit to either Stroud or Davis Mills as his starting quarterback for week one of the regular season at Baltimore, he is pleased with his progress:

"So proud of the progress that C.J. took this week. That's what we want to see," Ryans said. "You want to see improvement week-to-week, and that's what we saw this week."W

After finishing 2022 at a dismal 3-13-1, the Texans aren't projected to do much better this season with a rookie head coach and likely rookie quarterback at the helm. The Texans will likely use this last preseason game to further evaluate the quarterback battle between Stroud and Mills while also looking at several other young players as they get ready to pair their roster down to 53. Given the fact that Stroud was pressured heavily in week one at New England when Houston rest two-fifths of its starting offensive line, I full expect the Texans to play their complete starting offensive line whenever the rookie is on the field on Sunday night. All signs are pointing toward Stroud getting the job in week one which will likely mean that the Texans will be sure to protect him on Sunday night.

New Era in New Orleans

The New Orleans Saints have been perfect thus far in the 2023 preseason and seem to have the majority of their position battles solved heading into the final preseason game. Quarterback Derek Carr has played sparingly in the preseason and that trend should continue on Sunday night for the former All-Pro. Backups Jameis Winston and Jake Haener saw the majority of the snaps this past Sunday and that trend should continue in this spot with the regular season looming. The Saints are a veteran team that will likely play it safe on Sunday to avoid the injury bug that plagued their roster last season. One veteran who may get his share of snaps, however, is running back Alvin Kamara. With a suspension on the horizon for the first four games of the season, Kamara may get extended reps on Sunday in what will essentially be his last work with the team for a month.

Cutdown day could be very interesting for the Saints following Sunday's game. Last week, veteran tight end Jimmy Graham was arrested following bizarre behavior that the team believes resulted from a seizure. Graham is currently listed as the team's fourth-string tight end and with Juwon Johnson and Taysom Hill guarantees to make the roster, the veteran Graham could end up being a roster casualty. The Saints are also banged up a bit on the offensive line with both their starting guards, Andrus Peat and Cesar Ruiz, listed as questionable. This could certainly factor into the team's decision on whether or not to play starter Carr for any length of time on Sunday night. (edit: Carr will not play on Sunday per head coach Dennis Allen).

Full-Game Side Bet

Insiders Status:

Rating:

The young and feisty Texans should be the more hungry team on Sunday night. The veteran Saints team has little to prove on Sunday night and even less reason to play some of their key veterans in a meaningless exhibition game. Houston, meanwhile, is still sorting out the quarterback position and may be more inclined to get another look at their full offensive line playing together behind Stroud. In addition, the younger Texans will have far more interesting choices to make at several positions and may want to take extended looks at several players trying to earn their way onto the 53-man roster. With that in mind, expect the Texans to be more aggressive, physical, and hungry on Sunday night. I think youth will be served in this game.

Take the Texans with the points.
