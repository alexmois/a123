It’s the start of the 2023 NFL season as we have an interconference clash on the gridiron coming to you from the Midwest. The Detroit Lions are on the road as they make the trip to face the Kansas City Chiefs on Thursday night. Detroit finished the 2022 season 9-8: while they missed the playoffs for the sixth straight year, they posted a winning mark for the first time since 2017. Kansas City finished the regular season 14-3 to win the AFC West for the seventh straight season and went on to beat the Eagles 38-35 in Super Bowl LVII. The Chiefs own a 9-5 advantage in the all-time regular season series between the teams, including a 34-30 road win in the most recent matchup on September 29, 2019.

Lions Looking to Build On Last Season’s Stretch Run

Detroit took big strides forward last season in Dan Campbell’s second year running the team as they improved from 3-13-1 in 2021 to 9-8 in 2022. The Lions started the year 1-6 but rallied to win eight of their final 10 games in the regular season, capped by a win over Green Bay in week 18 that kept the Packers out of the postseason. There will be some changes in the Motor City this season, starting at running back as the trio of Jamaal Williams, D’Andre Swift and Justin Jackson all are gone from last season. In their place, the Lions spent a first-round pick on Jahmyr Gibbs while they added David Montgomery via free agency from the Bears. They even inked a solid backup quarterback in Teddy Bridgewater. Detroit hopes that the signings of Emmanuel Moseley, Cameron Sutton and Chauncey Gardner-Johnson can help the secondary after seeing a trio of corners and a safety depart in the offseason.

Last season, the Lions were 8th in the league in passing offense with 251.8 yards per contest. Detroit finished 11th in rushing as they averaged 128.2 yards a game last year. The Lions were 5th in the league in scoring offense as they put up an average of 26.6 points per contest while they were 28th in scoring defense by allowing 25.1 points per game. Jared Goff was 382 of 587 passing for 4,438 yards with 29 touchdowns and seven interceptions while adding 73 yards on the ground. Craig Reynolds is the leading returning rusher as he had 23 carries for 102 yards last season. David Montgomery carried the ball 201 times for 801 yards and five scores with the Bears last season.

Jahmyr Gibbs (151 carries, 926 yards, seven TDs, 44 receptions, 444 yards, three TDs for Alabama) will be a factor in the ground game. Amon-Ra St. Brown led the Lions with 106 receptions for 1,161 yards plus six scores. Josh Reynolds (38 catches, 479 yards, three TD) and Kalif Raymond (47 receptions, 616 yards) bring back three of the team’s top four receivers from last season. Jameson Williams (reception, 41 yards, TD) will be a factor once he returns from his six-game suspension for gambling. Rookie tight end Sam LaPorta will be looked at to make plays as well. Michael Badgley hit all 33 extra point attempts and 20 of 24 field goal tries with a long of 53 last season. He’s in competition with Riley Patterson for the kicking job: Patterson was kept on the 53-man roster after cuts last week but Badgley was added to the practice squad. Watch for an update on this heading toward kickoff.

Chiefs Seek to Start Title Defense on Positive Note

Kansas City went to the Super Bowl for the third time in four years and won their second title in that span as they downed the Eagles in Super Bowl LVII back in February. The Chiefs were 14-3 in the regular season and dispatched Jacksonville and Cincinnati in the AFC playoffs ahead of their Lombardi Trophy win. Kansas City has their share of retooling on both sides of the ball. On offense, receivers JuJu Smith-Schuster (Patriots) and Mecole Hardman (Jets) along with offensive tackles Orlando Brown Jr. (Bengals) and Andrew Wylie (Commanders) are gone. Jawaan Taylor (Jaguars) and Donovan Smith (Buccaneers) are the new tackles while the receiver group is in flux with a lot of youth. Defensively, Frank Clark (released), Carlos Dunlap (unsigned) and Khalen Saunders (Saints) are gone from the defensive side while safety Juan Thornhill signed with the Browns.

Last season, the Chiefs were first in the league in passing offense with an average of 297.8 yards per contest. Kansas City finished 20th in the league in rushing offense as they ground out 115.9 yards per game on the year. The Chiefs were first in the league in scoring offense as they put up an average of 29.2 points per contest. Kansas City finished 16th in scoring defense by allowing 21.7 points a game last year. Patrick Mahomes is the signal caller for the Chiefs: he completed 435 of 648 passes for 5,250 yards with 41 touchdowns and 12 interceptions on the year. He was second on the team with 358 rushing yards plus four scores. In the run game, Clyde Edwards-Helaire was third on the team with 71 carries for 302 yards plus three scores this season. Isiah Pacheco (170 carries, 830 yards, five TDs) and Jerick McKinnon (72 carries, 291 yards, TD) were involved in the ground game as well. There is no shortage in targets in the passing game, highlighted by Travis Kelce’s 110 receptions for 1,338 yards and 12 scores last season. McKinnon (56 catches, 512 yards, nine TDs) and Marquez Valdes-Scantling (42 catches, 687 yards, two TDs) were good targets last season. Skyy Moore (22 grabs, 250 yards), Kadarius Toney (14 receptions, 171 yards, two TDs) and rookie Rashee Rice need to step up and make plays in the passing game as well. Harrison Butker hit 38 of 41 extra point attempts and 18 of 24 field goal attempts with a long of 62 last season.

The Chiefs have some issues with their defensive line in the early going this season. Charles Omenihu, who was signed away from San Francisco in free agency, was suspended six games for violating the league’s personal conduct policy. Meanwhile, defensive tackle Chris Jones remains a holdout as of Monday afternoon and it’s unclear as to when the two sides will come to terms on an agreement.

*Kelce hyperextended his knee in practice on Tuesday and his status is in doubt for this game.

Detroit was 12-5 ATS last season and saw the over record a 10-7 mark in their games.
