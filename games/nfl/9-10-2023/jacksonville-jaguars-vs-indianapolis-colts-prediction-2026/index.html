The Jacksonville Jaguars and Indianapolis Colts will square off in Week One NFL action at Lucas Oil Stadium on Sunday at 1:00 p.m. ET. The Jags are 5.5-point favorites and the game total is 46.5 points.

Jacksonville has an over/under win total of 9.5 wins this season. It's coming off a 9-8 campaign that ended in the divisional round of the playoffs. Defensive end Tyler Lacy is questionable with a hip injury, safety Antonio Johnson is questionable with a hamstring injury, and cornerback Chris Claybrooks is questionable due to a suspension for an off-field incident.

Indianapolis has an over/under win total of 6.5 wins this season. It was 4-12-1 in the 2022-23 season, including 2-6 at home. Linebacker Shaquille Leonard is probable to play (concussion) and running back Zach Moss is out with an arm injury.

Will Jacksonville take the next step?

Jacksonville hopes last year's nine-win season was the start of something big. The Jags started the season 3-7 both straight-up and against the spread, but rallied to win six of their last seven (5-2 ATS). After upsetting the Los Angeles Chargers in the Wild Card, the Jaguars fell short against eventual champion Kansas City, but the season was an overall success. With Super Bowl champion head coach Doug Pederson and budding star quarterback Trevor Lawrence, expectations are high in the Jaguars' locker room.

"We’re going to be back, this is the new standard," Lawrence said after the playoff loss. "We set that, this is the bar and we’ll be back."

Jacksonville didn't make a ton of roster changes in the offseason but does welcome back suspended receiver Calvin Ridley, who has Nno. 1 WR potential. He hasn't played much in the last two seasons but showed flashes of potential in his first three seasons with Atlanta. The Jags lost tackle Jawaan Taylor to the Chiefs and edge Arden Key to Tennessee in free agency but retained tight end Evan Engram, who has become a reliable target in the passing game. Jacksonville added offensive tackle Anton Harrison, tight end Brenton Strange, and running back Tank Bigsby in the draft, too.

The Jaguars averaged 23.8 points and 368.5 total yards per game last season, including 124.5 rushing yards and 232.9 passing yards. The defense surrendered 20.6 points and 365.2 yards per game, including 114.8 rushing yards and 238.5 passing yards.

Jacksonville is the betting favorite to win the AFC South this season, which would be their second division title since 2002. There are tough teams on its schedule — Kansas City, Buffalo, San Francisco, Cincinnati, and Baltimore — but they will play enough beatable teams to reach double-digit wins in a weak division.

The Jags boast a talented group of skill players in Ridley, Engram, slot receiver Christian Kirk, big wideout Zay Jones, and versatile running back Travis Etienne Jr. Over the final nine games of the regular season, Lawrence passed for 2,273 yards with a 69.7 completion percentage, 15 TD, 2 INT, and a 104.6 passer rating. Jacksonville had the ninth-highest EPA per play and seventh-best DVOA rating in those games. It's safe to assume the offense could continue its hot play at the beginning of the season, as the Jaguars face a few of the NFL's worst defenses in their first four games.

Jacksonville's defense boasted the fourth-highest pressure rate on passers (25.1%) and the fourth-most takeaways (27 total) last season but only generated 35 sacks and 77 QB hits. It ranked 26th in DVOA and didn't make any significant acquisitions in the offseason to bolster the unit. 2022 No. 1 overall pick Travon Walker (3.5 sacks) should make strides, though, and there are other pieces to work with, including pass rusher Josh Allen, linebacker Devin Lloyd, linebacker Foyesade Oluokun, and safety Andre Cisco. Cornerback Tyson Campbell looks like a future star, as well.

To get the most out of the offense, they'll need to get off the field on third down (fourth-worst third down rate last season) and get more stops in the red zone, as opponents converted 61.6 percent of their red zone opportunities into touchdowns.

How much can Anthony Richardson improve Indy?

Indianapolis has renewed optimism for the 2023-24 season after drafting Florida quarterback Anthony Richardson with the fourth overall pick in the NFL Draft. While he made just 13 career college starts, the Colts feel that they have a building block for the future in the 21-year-old.

"I didn't want to look up and watch him be a superstar somewhere else," Colts general manager Chris Ballard said after the draft. "If he's going to be a superstar, he's going to be a superstar for the Colts."

Indianapolis could have something in the former Gator, along with wide receiver Michael Pittman Jr., a solid offensive line, and an improving defense led by DeForest Buckner. Former Eagles offensive coordinator Shane Steichen takes over as the Colts' new head coach, who Indy hopes can have a similar effect on Richardson that he had on Philadelphia QB Jalen Hurts.

Indy added defensive end Samson Ekubam and defensive tackle Taven Bryan in free agency, as well as kicker Matt Gay and insurance QB Gardner Minshew. It lost linebacker Bobby Okereke, who signed with the New York Giants and traded cornerback Stephon Gilmore to the Dallas Cowboys, but drafted Kansas State cornerback Julius Brents in the second round and North Carolina receiver Josh Downs in the third round. One problem is that star RB Jonathan Taylor will miss at least the first four games on the PUP listn as the Colts have attempted to trade him. RB Zack Moss (arm) is listed as doubtful so a lot of pressure will be on Richardson and the passing game.

The Colts averaged 17.0 points and 336.5 total yards per game last season, including 109.8 rushing yards and 201.9 passing yards. The defense gave up 25.1 points and 349.7 total yards per game, including 124.1 rushing yards and 209.9 passing yards.

Indianapolis is a good bet to win three games in the AFC South this season, as the division is weaker than most. The Colts' schedule is soft, ranking 28th in SOS. It may not be enough to get them to seven wins, but if Richardson explodes onto the scene, it's certainly possible.

The rookie QB will benefit from playing behind an experienced offensive line. Quenton Nelson, Ryan Kelly, and Braden Smith are back, and the Colts added depth to the unit, which ranked among the top lines in adjusted sack rate last season. New offensive line coach Tony Sparano Jr. will take this group to the next level.

The Indy defensive line is also worth watching. Defensive coordinator Gus Bradley's front was stout against the run and finished with 44 total sacks despite blitzing at the fifth-lowest rate. Five Colts linemen finished with 3.5 sacks or more last season, and the team added San Francisco defensive end Samson Ebukam to help make up for the loss of sack leader Yannick Ngakoue. Considering they weren't getting much support from the offense, it's impressive that the unit was able to hold eight opponents to 20 points or less last season.

The Jaguars are laying 5.5 points, which is too much respect for Jacksonville. The Colts defense held up well last season despite having an awful offense that put them in bad situations. Rating:

These offenses will put on a show Sunday. Lawrence will pick apart the Colts' secondary, utilizing all his weapons in the passing game. Engram and Ridley, in particular, are primed for productive games in week one. Jacksonville couldn't get off the field on third down and gave up red zone touchdowns far too often last season. Without any significant roster moves, it's difficult to envision that changing on Sunday against the wild-card Richardson and the Colts offense.
