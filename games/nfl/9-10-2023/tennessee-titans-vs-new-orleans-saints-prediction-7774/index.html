It’s the opening week of the NFL season and it brings us an interconference matchup between the AFC South and the NFC South down in the Big Easy. The Tennessee Titans are on the road as they travel to face the New Orleans Saints Sunday afternoon. Tennessee stumbled down the stretch last season, finishing 7-10 and missing the postseason, snapping a string of three straight playoff appearances. New Orleans finished the 2022 campaign 7-10 and was tied for second in the NFC South. They missed the postseason for the second straight season. In the all-time regular season series between the teams, the Titans own a 9-6-1 advantage, including a 23-21 home win in the most recent matchup on November 14, 2021.

Tennessee Titans Try to Start 2023 On Positive Note

Tennessee was rolling in the first half of the season before things came apart in 2022 and they’re hoping to regroup this season. The Titans were 7-3 after 10 games before dropping their final seven games, four of which came in one-score contests, to wind up missing the playoffs. They had a chance to win the division in week 18 only to lose to Jacksonville on the road 20-16 by giving up a fumble return for a score in the final three minutes of regulation. Tennessee decided to run it back this season with their key players from a season ago. The Titans added DeAndre Hopkins to their receiving corps in an effort to bolster their passing game but there are still some major concerns when it comes to critical spots in the lineup.

Last season, the Titans finished 30th in the league in passing offense with an average of 171.4 yards per game while they were 13th in rushing offense by averaging 125.4 yards per contest. Tennessee was 28th in scoring offense as they averaged 17.5 points per contest while the team stood 14th in scoring defense by allowing 21.1 points per game. Ryan Tannehill (212 of 325 passing, 2536 yards, 13 touchdowns, six interceptions, 98 rushing yards, two touchdowns) is back as the #1 quarterback on the depth chart. He missed five games last season and, at age 35, one has to wonder how much more he has left in the tank. Malik Willis (31 of 61, 278 yards, three INT, 123 rushing yards, TD) is the backup quarter, having beaten out second-round pick Will Levis for the backup job.

Derrick Henry (349 carries, 1538 yards, 13 TDs) is the #1 back while rookie third-round pick Tyjae Spears takes over the #2 role while Julius Chestnut lurks on the depth chart. Treylon Burks (33 receptions, 444 yards, TD) will work as the default #2 receiver opposite DeAndre Hopkins (64 catches, 717 yards, three TDs), who was picked up in free agency in the offseason. A major thing to watch for here in the preseason is the revamped offensive line for the Titans. Taylor Lewan, Nate Davis and Ben Jones are all gone, wiping out last year’s line from center to left tackle. Throw in that Nicholas Petit-Frere, who started at right tackle, is suspended for the first six games due to violating the league’s gambling policy, and the Titans have some major issues when it comes to protecting the quarterback in addition to opening holes on the ground. Nick Folk, who was acquired from New England last week, takes over the kicking duties. He connected on 32 of 35 extra point attempts and 32 of 37 field goal attempts with a long of 54 last season.

Cornerback Tre Avery (hamstring), linebacker Harold Landry (abdomen) and offensive lineman Dillon Radunz (knee) were all limited in practice on Thursday. Hopkins (rest) and tight end Trevon Wesco (rest) were also limited on Thursday. Watch for any updates on their respective statuses leading up to kickoff.

Saints Looking to Compete In NFC South

New Orleans struggled to get solid quarterback play last season and it led to them finishing 7-10 and tied for second in the NFC South, one game behind Tampa Bay for the top spot. It was an offseason of overhaul for the Saints as gone are Andy Dalton, Mark Ingram, Jarvis Landry, Deonte Harty, Marcus Davenport, Kaden Elliss, David Onyemata, Bradley Roby, Chris Harris Jr., Justin Evans and P.J. Williams from last year’s squad. That means the Saints have a lot of new faces to plug in on both sides of the ball if they hope to have success in 2023. If not, coach Dennis Allen could be on the hot seat sooner rather than later. One thing we know is that Alvin Kamara will miss the first three games as he was suspended as a result of an off-field incident.

Last season, New Orleans finished 16th in the league in passing offense as they averaged 217.2 yards per game through the air. The Saints were 19th in rushing offense by picking up 116.6 yards per game on the ground. New Orleans ended up 22nd in the league in scoring offense by putting up an average of 19.4 points per contest and stood 9th in scoring defense by allowing 20.3 points per game.

QB Derek Carr makes his first regular-season start wearing the fleur-de-lis Sunday. Carr spent his entire career with the Raiders before being cut late last season. He completed 305 of 502 passes for 3,522 yards with 24 touchdowns and 14 interceptions on the year. Carr added 102 yards on the ground. Jameis Winston will get a chance to show that he still can be a serviceable quarterback at the NFL level after dealing with injuries in the last couple of seasons as the backup. Jamaal Williams came over from the Lions in free agency after piling up 262 carries for 1,066 yards and 17 scores a season ago. He'll share the load with rookie Kendre Miller from TCU, who was selected in the third round and thinks that he has the ability to do what Kamara does. New Orleans has Chris Olave (72 catches, 1042 yards, four TDs as a rookie) back as the #1 receiver while they hope Michael Thomas can come back after missing most of the last two seasons with injuries. Rashid Shaheed (28 grabs, 488 yards, two TDs) has a chance to really cement himself as a solid option if he can stay healthy. Blake Grupe, who hit all 49 extra point attempts and 14 of 19 field goal tries last season for Notre Dame, takes over the kicking duties.

Defensive back J.T. Gray (shoulder), running back Kendre Miller (hamstring) and wide receiver Tre’Quan Smith (groin) all did not practice Thursday. Watch for updates on their respective statuses heading toward kickoff.
