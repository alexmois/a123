The Carolina Panthers face the Atlanta Falcons in the first week of the NFL season with both teams looking to improve after disappointing 2022 seasons. The Panthers went 7-10 last year and it forced a coaching change as the team brought in former Indianapolis  Colts head coach Frank Reich to turn the team around. The Falcons went 7-10 last season and hope that they can take a big leap in Arthur Smith's third season as their head coach with a promising roster. Both teams look to make a statement in Week One and start the season off strong and the upcoming game looks to be a good one as a result.

Panthers hope Young kickstarts new era

The Panthers were awful last season and it forced a change at head coach and quarterback. The offense in particular struggled, scoring only 20.4 points per game, and it prompted them to draft quarterback Bryce Young with the first overall pick in the draft. Young looks to lead the offense but the team will rely heavily on the run game with Chuba Hubbard and offseason addition Miles Sanders splitting carries in the backfield. Along with a strong run game, the Panthers hope that a new-look receiver group can step up as they brought in veterans Adam Theilen and D.J. Chark while also selecting Jonathan Mingo in the recent draft.

The offense was a liability last season but the defense, which showed flashes also looks to improve as they allowed 22.0 points per game. Edge defender Brian Burns has been one of the best pass rushers in the league and is coming off a year where he has 12.5 sacks and 22 QB hits while linebackers Shaq Thompson and Frankie Luvu stepped up in the middle with 246 combined tackles but the rest of the defense struggled. The hope is that the secondary improves as they allowed 241.1 passing yards per game last year and all eyes are on cornerbacks Donte Jackson and Jaycee Horn to solidify the unit and make the defense as a whole a great one.

Falcons hope run game takes offense to the next level

The Falcons had a rough 2022 season but showed some promise and look to take a big leap this season. While the offense struggled, scoring only 21.5 points per game, the run game was one of the best in the league, averaging 159.9 yards per game on the ground. The backfield already has Tyler Allgeier and Cordarrelle Patterson from last year but is expected to only improve with the team drafting running back Bijan Robinson in the first round. The questions are in the passing attack and all the pressure is on one second-year quarterback Desmond Ridder to take a step forward. likewise, the hope is that receivers Mack Hollins and Drake London improve while tight end Kyle Pitts can rebound from a lackluster second season in the league to form a dynamic pass-catching group.

The offense has shown signs that it will improve but the defense is desperate to rebound from a year where they allowed 22.7 points per game and 240.6 passing yards per game. Defensive tackle Grady Jarrett has established himself as one of the best interior defensive linemen in the game while AJ Terrell is one of the best coverage corners but the rest of the defense has looked awful in recent years. The hope is that the realigned secondary steps up with the team adding cornerback Jeff Okudah and safety Jesse Bates in the offseason.
