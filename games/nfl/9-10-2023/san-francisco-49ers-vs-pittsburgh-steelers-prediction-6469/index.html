The San Francisco 49ers (13-4, 11-6 ATS, 9-8 O/U) are on the road to battle the Pittsburgh Steelers (9-8, 10-6-1 ATS, 7-10 O/U) in the season opener on Sunday afternoon. The 49ers posted a 13-4 record last year and lost to the Eagles in the NFC championship. The Steelers went 9-8 and missed the playoffs. These combatants last met in 2019 in a game the 49ers won.

49ers Poised for Big Season, Bosa Signs

The San Francisco 49ers were able to win 13 games last season, elevating the expectations for the 2023 season. There are some question marks on offense. Will Brock Purdy be able to play at the level seen in 2022? The 23-year-old wasn't the #1 QB at the start of the season but was needed due to injuries and did not see his first action until the middle of October. He led the 49ers to a 9-1 record in his ten games played. The former Iowa State QB collected 1374 passing yards and a 13:4 TD to INT ratio. Purdy was injured late in the season and has recovered from his elbow injury.

The rushing offense will be a strength anchored by Christian McCaffrey. The 27-year-old is a powerful running back and was spectacular in 2022, accumulating 1139 rushing yards on an average of 4.6 yards per rush. The receiving core is nearly identical to last season highlighted by the exciting Deebo Samuel. The 27-year-old recorded 1405 receiving yards in 2021 but only 632 in 13 games last year.

The 49ers defense is dominant. They have studs all over the field led by Nick Bosa. The 25-year-old DE had a contract dispute and signed a huge extension on Wednesday. He is expected to play. Bosa had a remarkable 18.5 sacks and won Defensive Player of the Year. Note that the 49ers have a new defensive coordinator this season in Steve Wilks. The 49ers ranked 20th last year against the pass but 2nd against the run.

Last season, San Francisco averaged 26.5 points, ranking them sixth. The defense limited opponents to 16.3 points which led the NFL.

Steelers Young Core Targets Playoff Spot

The Pittsburgh Steelers are an interesting squad. They were 3-7 at one point last year but improved as the season progressed and ultimately finished with a winning record of 9-8. The win total is projected at 8.5 wins by most books for this season and the play of Kenny Pickett will go a long way in determining if they exceed eight victories.

Kenny Pickett had a decent rookie season. The numbers aren’t dazzling, recording 2404 passing yards accompanied by a 7:9 TD to INT-ratio and a passer rating of 76. If he can improve on all metrics the Steelers will be in good shape.

The Pittsburgh running game is led by Najee Harris. The former Alabama star has been outstanding in his opening two NFL seasons. The 25-year-old posted 1,200 rushing yards in his rookie year and 1034 yards on 3.8 yards per rush in 2022. Pickett has weapons in the air. Diontae Johnson led the squad with 882 receiving yards with George Pickens just behind with 801 yards in a stellar rookie season.

The Steelers' defense was solid last year and should be a strength again this season. The pass defense ranked 19th and the rush defense was the strength of the unit, standing ninth. The unit lost experienced CB Cam Sutton to the Lions but added veteran Patrick Peterson. Myles Jack, who led the squad in tackles, departed but they return S Minkah Fitzpatrick and DE Cameron Hayward. 

Pittsburgh scored an average of 18.1 points last season, ranking them 26th. They conceded an average of 20.4 points, good for 10th.
