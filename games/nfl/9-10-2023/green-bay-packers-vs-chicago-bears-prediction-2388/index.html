The Green Bay Packers (8-9, 8-9 ATS, 8-9 O/U) collide with the Chicago Bears (3-14, 5-11 ATS, 10-7 O/U) in a divisional showdown in the season opener on Sunday afternoon in the Windy City. The Packer sported an 8-9 record last season. The Bears posted a poor 3-14 mark. Green Bay beat Chicago by a 27-10 score in the first meeting and 28-19 in the second clash.

Jordan Love Gets His Chance

The Green Bay Packers begin a new era. Aaron Rodgers was traded to the Jets and for the first time in several years, the Packers have a bit of a question mark under center. Green Bay was battling hard to qualify for the playoffs last season but ended up finishing with an 8-9 record. The Packers' win total by most books is projected at 7.5 wins.

Plenty of anticipation surrounds Jordan Love. After spending the last two seasons as the backup for Aaron Rodgers, Love is now ready to step into the spotlight. The 24-year-old has gotten some reps in, posting 606 passing yards with a 3:3 TD-to-INT ratio in regular season play. 

The Packers have a strong running game to take some of the pressure off Love. Aaron Jones accumulated a stellar 1,121 rushing yards on 5.3 yards per carry last season. He posted 158 yards against the Bears last season. The receiving core no longer has Allen Lazard. Many are expecting a breakout season from Christian Watson. The 22-year-old had a stellar rookie year, amassing 611 receiving yards. Watson did not practice Thursday and is questionable. He brought in 57 receiving yards in two meetings against the Bears. However, Watson is out with a hamstring injury and Romeo Doubs, who had 425 receiving yards last season, is questionable.

The Packers' defense was mediocre last season. They ranked 8th against the pass but 26th against the run. Quay Walker led the squad with 121 tackles last season and Preston Smith, who collected 8.5 sacks, is poised for another big season. The unit also has a great corner in Jaire Alexander who is questionable for this game.

Green Bay scored an average of 21.8 points last season, ranking them 14th. The defense allowed an average of 21.8 points, placing them 17th.

Bears Hoping Busy Offseason Leads to Success

The Chicago Bears are hoping to prove the hard part of the rebuild is in the past after winning three games last season. Chicago's management had a busy offseason and is optimistic the barrage of new additions will propel the squad to success. The win total is set at 7.5 wins by most books.

The Bears insist Justin Fields is the guy to lead the squad back to glory. The 2021 1st overall pick was below average when it came to passing in 2022, recording 2242 passing yards and a 17:11 TD to INT ratio. The good news is he can run, amassing a stellar 1143 rushing yards last season. He will need to improve his passing game.

The Bears lost David Montgomery to the Lions but have confidence in Khalil Hebert. The 25-year-old had an excellent season, rushing for 731 yards on 5.7 yards per carry. He recorded just 38 yards against the Packers. DJ Moore was the big acquisition in the offseason. The 26-year-old has three seasons of least 1000 yards in his career and posted 888 receiving yards in Carolina last year. They also have Darnell Mooney who brought in over 1000 yards in 2021 but only 493 yards last season.

The Bears defense was exploited last season. The pass defense was respectable, marking 17th while the rush defense was 31st. Management signed Tremaine Edmunds to a large deal in the offseason. The 25-year-old linebacker posted over 100 tackles for the Bills last year. The Bears have an established safety in Eddie Jackson who had a big season but will need better performances from the CBs. 

Chicago scored an average of 19.2 points per game last year, marking them 23rd. The defense squandered an average of 27.2 points which was last in the NFL.
