Twice a season, these AFC West rivals put each other to the test. In 2023, the Las Vegas Raiders (6-11-0; 8-9-0 ATS in 2022) and Denver Broncos (5-12-0; 7-10-0 ATS) get to knock round out of the way immediately. Last year, the Raiders swept the Broncos, including an overtime win in Denver the second time around. Well, this game is in Empower Field at Mile High too, and Denver wants to protect their house. At 4:25 p.m. EDT, these teams will get their regular season started with a division win at stake. Here's what lies ahead.

Raiders Trying to Recover

Last season was a rough one for the Silver and Black, but they're ready to run it back in 2023. Well, with a change under center. Jimmy Garoppolo has taken over for long-time Raiders QB Derek Carr this season. 2022's league-leading rusher, Josh Jacobs, is back in the mix after holding out for most of the offseason. Star WR Davante Adams is still there too. Stud TE Darren Waller is wearing blue now, but Austin Hooper and second-round pick Michael Mayer are around to fill his void. With a middle-of-the-pack offensive line entering this season, Vegas has the pieces to match an offense that finished 12th in scoring last year.

Defense is where the Raiders really need improvement, as last season's squad finished 26th in points allowed. Only two teams had fewer sacks, and nobody had fewer takeaways than Las Vegas in 2022. The team responded by drafting seven rookies on the defensive end, highlighted by seventh overall pick Tyree Wilson, an edge rusher that should help bring down opposing quarterbacks. Former Steelers linebacker Robert Spillane should help stop the run, while veteran cornerback Marcus Peters brings his 32 career interceptions to the secondary. Peters is a known gambler, so Las Vegas is hoping he'll fit right in and create big plays.

New Executive Chef in Denver

Head coach Sean Payton is trying to restore this Denver Broncos franchise to its former glory. The first task is repairing an offense that finished last in scoring a year ago. Quarterback Russell Wilson is back in the kitchen, trying to prove he can still cook. The running back room is completely different, with a healthy Javonte Williams looking to play all 17 this season and former Bengal Samaje Perine backing him up. Rookie WR Marvin Mims Jr. joins Jerry Jeudy and Courtland Sutton as a potentially lethal pass-catching trio if they can all stay healthy. Quietly, this offensive line could be a top-10 group too. Play-calling, decision-making, and consistency on the offensive end could successfully make Denver a playoff team. 

That's assuming that the defense performs at a high level again. Despite spending a ton of time on the field in 2022, Denver finished 14th in scoring defensively. The pass rush underwhelmed, and they still gave up the fewest yards per attempt. The Broncos also allowed the 10th-fewest rushing yards. Defensive lineman Zach Allen and safety Kareem Jackson were added to bolster the defense. They were a strong unit last year, and additions coupled with (hopefully) more time resting on the bench should make them even better.

Buying on this Broncos team seems smart, especially with this battle. Sean Payton gets to test this revamped offense against one of the NFL's weaker defenses. Scoring and giveaways are what destroyed the 2022 Broncos, two issues that shouldn't be an issue to open this season.

It won't be easy for Jimmy Garoppolo in this one, which doesn't bode well for the Raiders' chances. Especially not on the road against a division rival.
