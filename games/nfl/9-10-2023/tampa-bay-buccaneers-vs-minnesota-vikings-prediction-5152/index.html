The Tampa Bay Buccaneers and Minnesota Vikings will square off in Week One NFL action on Sunday at U.S. Bank Stadium. Kickoff is at 1:00 p.m. ET.

Tampa Bay has an over/under win total of 6.5 victories. The Bucs were 8-9 overall, including 3-5 on the road, last season. Receiver Mike Evans is probable (groin), safety Antoine Winfield Jr. is probable (calf), safety Kaevon Merriweather is probable (quad), running back KeShawn Vaughn is probable (personal), running back Chase Edmonds is probable (undisclosed), guard Cody Mauch is questionable (back), and linebacker Anthony Nelson is questionable (hamstring).

Minnesota has an over/under win total of 8.5 victories. The Vikings were 13-4 overall, including 8-1 at home, last season. Running back Alexander Mattison is probable (undisclosed), tight end T.J. Hockenson is probable (back), linebacker Troy Dye is probable (elbow), and punter Ryan Wright is probable (ankle).

The Vikings are five-point favorites and the game total is 45.5 points.

How will Tampa Bay look post-Brady?

The Tom Brady era is officially over in Tampa Bay. Baker Mayfield will take over for the future Hall-of-Famer at quarterback, attempting to resurrect a once-promising career. He isn't Brady, but can he be competent enough to keep Tampa relevant in the NFC South?

With $74.3 million in dead cap space this year, it's no surprise the Bucs were irrelevant in free agency. Guard Shaq Mason, tight end Cameron Brate, receiver Julio Jones, offensive tackle Donovan Smith, defensive linemen Akiem Hicks, Carl Nassib, and William Gholston, and kicker Ryan Succop are all gone. There are still some veterans in the locker room from the Super Bowl run, but the Bucs knew what they were getting into when they signed the aging QB in 2020.

"This was part of the plan going back to 2020 when we signed Tom, that at some point we’re going to have to pay our bills," Buccaneers general Jason Licht said.

There is talent on paper, namely Mike Evans and Chris Godwin, but the Tampa Bay offense will likely underwhelm this season. The Buccaneers ranked 28th in adjusted line yards in 2022 with Rachaad White and Chase Edmonds splitting carries and the offensive line isn't getting any younger. Without a run game, Mayfield will be pressured too often into poor reads that will result in turnovers. The Oklahoma product posted a 74.4 passer rating with the Panthers and averaged 3.3 yards per attempt when blitzed last season.

The TB offense won't make life any easier for the defense, which is likely going to be spending a lot of time on the field this season. Even with a talented linebacker corps and some quality players in the secondary, head coach Todd Bowles' group is going to be vulnerable.

Is Minnesota bound to regress this season?

Minnesota has a talented, veteran roster capable of winning the NFC North again, but the competition will be stiffer this season. Besides, it's going to take better play to repeat last season's success. Call it good fortune or clutch, the Vikings were an incredible 11-0 in one-score games in 2022-23. There were miraculous comebacks, too, including an NFL-record 33-point comeback victory in Indianapolis. With a minus-three point differential, Minnesota was the first 12-win team in NFL history to be outscored in a season.

The Vikings let defensive tackle Dalvin Tomlinson, receiver Adam Thielen, cornerback Patrick Peterson, and linebacker Eric Kendricks walk in free agency. They traded pass-rusher Za'Darius Smith (ten sacks last season) and cut star running back Dalvin Cook, too. While fan favorites, most of these players are past their prime or getting close to it. They did sign blocking tight end Josh Oliver, cornerback Byron Murphy, and defensive end Marcus Davenport.

The Minnesota offense will be electric. QB Kirk Cousins is back for another season in Minneapolis, and he has a wealth of pass-catchers to throw the football to, including All-Pro Justin Jefferson, rookie home run threat Jordan Addison, and reliable tight end T.J. Hockenson. The O should be improved in year two under head coach Kevin O'Connell.

The Vikings defense has to play better in 2023. O'Connell hired Brian Flores as the team's defensive coordinator, but how much can he help? Minny ranked 27th in Football Outsiders' DVOA last season, allowing more than 25 points per game. Without a reliable pass rush, it's tough to hold up in coverage, which could be a problem once again this season. Flores will do his best to create chaos with different schemes and formations, but the only way this unit will hold up is if it keeps the football in front of them. If Minnesota makes opposing offenses work hard for first downs and doesn't give up too many home run plays, it should be average.

Full-Game Side Bet

Insiders Status:

Rating:

At five points, I feel there is room for Minnesota to squeeze out a spread victory at home. Cousins-to-Jefferson is a lethal hookup, and the addition of Addison will make life difficult for the Buccaneers secondary all game. The Vikings won't look any different with Mattison taking over for Cook, too. This is going to be one of the most consistent high-scoring offenses in the NFL, and Tampa Bay isn't going to change my mind with a shutdown week one performance.

On the other side of the ball, I have faith that Flores' defense will create enough chaos to disrupt Mayfield's flow on at least a couple of drives, resulting in a punt or turnover that will allow Minnesota to flip the field. The switch from a soft zone to a blitz-heavy system will pay dividends, making it difficult for the Bucs signal-caller to take advantage of Evans and Godwin's catch pass-catching prowess.

Bet on the Vikings to win by six or more points on Sunday afternoon!

Full-Game Total Pick

Insiders Status:

Rating:

I have more faith in Mayfield than others and believe he will have some success on Sunday. The Vikings will look to blitz more under Flores, but that will leave their inexperienced corners in some tough battles with the 6-foot-5 Evans. Minny's outside corners have two starts between them, and its top four corners are either rookies or second-year players. The Bucs' o-line is experienced enough to hold up under pressure for the majority of the game, giving Mayfield enough time to pick apart the Minnesota secondary.

The Vikings won't be concerned, though, as they're prepared for a fair amount of shootouts this season. It was by far the best over team in the NFL last season, and that isn't going to change in 2023-24. Minnesota is going to push the pace and attempt to put this game away early with its aerial attack indoors at home. Hockenson is a high-volume pass-catcher (9.4 targets and 56 yards with Minny) and should make even more of an impact in his second year in the Vikings system. He and Jefferson will be the stars, and Addison will be eager to make his impact felt in his first NFL action.

Minnesota lost six starters from last season's defense, and Tampa Bay lost three starters, including two along the defensive line. At 45.5 points this total is too low, which I assume is because Mayfield is taking snaps for the Bucs. No matter, this is one of my favorite total bets in week one!

Prediction: Over 45.5
