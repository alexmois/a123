The Seattle Seahawks overachieved in 2022 while the Los Angeles Rams underachieved last season. What will be in store for these teams in 2023? We'll get an early glimpse on Sunday as the Seahawks host their division rival, the Rams, in the season opener for both teams. The Seahawks won both meetings last season, but the Rams covered on both occasions. The Seahawks are 5 point favorites in this one. Can they cover? We'll find out Sunday at 4:25 p.m. ET at Lumen Field in Seattle.

Rams hope to get back on track

The Rams have been on a rollercoaster the past two seasons. Two years ago, they won the first Super Bowl in franchise history, and last year was a complete disaster as they finished 5-12 and had one of the worst records in football. How does that happen? Injuries had a lot to do with it as their two key weapons -- quarterback Matthew Stafford and wide receiver Cooper Kupp, only played in 9 games. Kupp is still dealing with an injury and will not play on Sunday against the Seahawks. But Stafford is ready to go this season. However, without Kupp, who will he have to throw to?

The Rams will have a hard time filling Kupp's shoes, but they hope new receiver DeMarcus Robinson will provide some lift. They also have Van Jefferson and tight end Tyler Higbee. Coach Sean McVay is also hopeful that rookie Puka Nacua out of BYU will be an immediate contributor. Kupp is week to week at this point so they hope to get him back soon. Cam Akers is back at running back and he is hoping that a shored-up offensive line, with draft pick Steve Avila, will open up some holes. They also have a new offensive coordinator, Mike LaFleur. On defense, they lost five starters, including Bobby Wagner, who is now back with the Seahawks. But they still have Aaron Donald and he is a one-man wrecking crew.

Seahawks retool for playoff run

The Seattle Seahawks surprised a lot of people last season, going 9-8 and falling just short of the playoffs. Many of the pundits predicted the Seahawks to be one of the worst teams in the league before the season started. But they certainly proved a lot of people wrong. The big reason was quarterback Geno Smith. The journeyman and career backup had a breakout year, throwing for more than 4,200 yards with 30 touchdowns and just 11 interceptions. It earned him not only a Pro Bowl bid, but a Comeback Player of the Year award. So can Geno do it again and have a Rich Gannon-like late-career resurgence? It is hard to doubt him after his performance last season.

The Seahawks actually have more weapons for him to use this season. In addition to outstanding receivers in Tyler Lockett and DK Metcalf, both who gained 1,000 yards, along with a breakout year from running back Kenneth Walker, who had 1,000 yards rushing. They added the best receiver in the 2023 draft, Jaxon Smith-Njigba from Ohio State and running back Zach Charbonnet from UCLA. More importantly, they added several pieces on defense, including adding linemen DreMont Jones and Jarran Reed, and linebacker Bobby Wagner. They also drafted cornerback Devon Witherspoon and brought in safety Julian Love. Walker and Witherspoon are listed as questionable for this game.

Full-Game Side Bet

The Seahawks made a huge leap last year and this year they could make that next step to the playoffs. In fact, winning the division is certainly within their grasp.
