The Los Angeles Chargers play host to the Miami Dolphins on Sunday afternoon in an AFC showdown from SoFi Stadium. Both playoff teams last season, these two teams look to start the season strong. The Chargers finished off with a heartbreaking second-half collapse, as they look to get momentum going back in the right direction to open the season. For Miami, injuries hurt them when things were said and done, as a valiant effort came up just short in their playoff run. Last season, the Chargers escaped with a six-point win in Los Angeles, as the rematch should be just as entertaining for these two sides.

Dolphins Taking Cross-Country Trip

Miami won't be happy that they had to face the Chargers in LA last season, only to make the return trip again to open the 2023 season. Last season saw Miami get off to a quick start, at one point leading the AFC East, though a dreadful finish to the season, partly due to injuries, haunted them at the end. Over their last five regular season games, they only won one game, 11-6 against the Jets, before bowing out to the Bills in the playoff opener. The road wasn't kind to them, while going across the country is far from ideal in the opener. While they will have some key pieces in this one that they didn't have in the December matchup with the Chargers, a win would set the tone that they will have to be taken seriously in the AFC this season, while a loss will have them back on the road next week, in dire need of a win.

While Miami will be happy to have their quarterback under center in this rematch, they still have a lengthy injury report that will need to be monitored. Jaylen Waddle (oblique) is probable, which is good news, while Tyreek Hill looks to avoided a suspension and should be in the lineup as well. On the defensive side, Jalen Ramsey (knee) remains out into December, while Nik Needham (Achilles) joins him in the secondary. With the secondary already thin, the status of Justin Bethel (knee) will want to be monitored, especially as he is listed as questionable coming into the weekend.

All eyes will be on the offense, especially quarterback Tua Tagovailoa. The lefty battled injuries for much of last season but now that he comes in healthy, along with Hill and Waddle available, the sky could be the limit for this offense. Major questions remain with the running game though and they'll need to focus early on establishing some balance.

Defensively, the secondary is a concern, as mentioned earlier, especially against a Chargers team that is dangerous on the outside. The pass rush will need to show up on the road in this one but after losing Elandon Roberts (107 tackles) to the Steelers, much will fall on Jerome Baker (100 tackles) to pick up the slack in the middle of the field.

Chargers Eyeing Fast Home Start

The Chargers were one of the main teams talked about during the playoffs last season but not for the right reasons. A second-half collapse saw them on the wrong sides of one of the biggest comebacks in NFL playoff history, certainly leaving them with a sour taste in their mouth heading into the off-season. They should have some confidence behind them since they'll be at home and have a win against the Dolphins under their belt from December at SoFi Stadium. The Chargers were strong at home, especially over the second-half of the season, as they'll be looking to set the tone early in 2023. With road games against Tennessee and Minnesota looming, it's crucial that they get the win at home on Sunday.

One advantage the Chargers have heading into this one is the status of their injury report, which is quite a bit shorter than that of Miami. Chris Rump II (hamstring) and Daiyan Henley (hamstring) are both questionable, while Otito Ogbonnia (knee) and Jalen Guyton (knee) have been ruled out. With neither of those four likely proving to be game changers, the Chargers should fare fine without them.

Last year, Los Angeles had one of the league's most exciting and explosive offenses, with no signs of slowing down in 2023. It all starts with Justin Herbert, who threw for over 4,700 yards last season, including 25 touchdowns against only ten interceptions. With Keenan Allen and Mike Williams returning, along with drafting Quentin Johnston, this passing attack looks ready to strike against a depleted Miami secondary. Add in Austin Ekeler in the backfield and there's little doubt that this group can move the ball.

The defensive side remains much more of a concern and is likely to make or break how the season goes. Their leading tackler from a season ago, Drue Tranquill (146 tackles, 5 sacks) has moved on, putting even more pressure on Derwin James Sr. (115 tackles, 4 sacks, 2 interceptions) to be all over the field. The secondary will need to be tuned in against Hill and Waddle but unless the pass rush makes a difference, the defense could struggle in the opener.
