It has been a long 7 months since the Chiefs won the Super Bowl but now NFL football is finally back. In week one, we have the loser of that Super Bowl last year, the Philadelphia Eagles, facing Bill Belichick and the New England Patriots at Gillette Stadium in Foxboro, Massachusetts. These two squads have not met since 2019 when the Patriots prevailed 17-10 in Philly and covered as a 4.5 point favorite. The Eagles are favored by 4 points in this one. Can they cover on the road? Kickoff is Sunday at 4:25 p.m. ET.

Eagles ready to soar

The Philadelphia Eagles saw their season end last year in the Super Bowl with a 38-35 loss to the Chiefs. But after going 14-3 a year ago in the regular season, they are loaded once again in 2023 and are primed to get back there. Most of the key pieces are back this year, led by quarterback Jalen Hurts, who had an MVP-caliber season a year ago. He is just getting started and should be among the best in the business this year and for many years to come. His key weapons are also back, led by his trio of receivers -- AJ Brown, DeVonta Smith, and tight end Dallas Goedert.

The one big loss in the offseason on offense is running back Miles Sanders, who moved on to Carolina. But they replaced him with a couple of pretty good backs in D'Andre Swift from Detroit and Rashad Penny from Seattle. There have also been some changes on the defensive side of the ball. Notably, they really beefed up their defensive line, as they drafted two impressive defenders, defensive lineman Jalen Carter out of Georgia, and linebacker Nolan Smith. They will help fill the void left by Javon Hargraves, who left in the offseason. But this is a team that led the league in sacks last year and they could be even more ferocious this season, under new defensive coordinator Sean Desai.

Big day in New England

It is a big day for the New England Patriots and their fans -- and not only because they are hosting the opening game of the season. It is also the day where the legend, Tom Brady, comes back to Foxboro and is honored for his incredible accomplishments while with the Patriots. So the crowd will be pumped up, at least at the start, and there should be a lot of energy and emotion in the building. But then reality kicks in and they have to face arguably the best team in the NFL in the Philadelphia Eagles.

The Patriots struggled last season, finishing 8-9 and missing the playoffs. The big problem was on offense, where there was a mini quarterback controversy and two defensive guys in Matt Patricia and Joe Judge running the offense -- and not very well. The biggest change for the Patriots in the offseason was not even on the field -- it was on the sidelines. They brought in an accomplished OC in Bill O'Brien, who was once OC for the Patriots, head coach at Houston, and ran the offense for Nick Saban at Alabama. Mac Jones is the unquestioned QB1 this season and he has new weapons in JuJu Smith-Schuster at wide receiver and Ezekiel Elliott at running back. They also added tight end Mike Gesicki. One big issue is the O-line, which did not look good in the preseason and is dealing with a lot of injuries.

There will be a lot of energy and emotion in the building as Tom Brady returns to the place where he made history. But it won't take long for the Eagles to suck all the energy out of the building as they should dominate the Patriots. The Patriots defense should be good. It kept the team in the playoff race last year and could be even better this year. But can they hold the Eagles to the low 20s or below 20? That is doubtful. And if they don't, they will surely lose because I just don't see the Pats having enough firepower to get up into the mid-20s. The big issue here will be in the trenches. The Eagles pass rush should tee off on Jones and make his life miserable all afternoon. Take the Eagles giving up the points.

Prediction: Philadelphia -4

Full-Game Total Pick

Rating:

The total here is set at 45 and I just don't see these two teams hitting that number. The Eagles averaged 28 points per game last season but they will fall short of that number against a very good Patriots defense.
