Dallas Cowboys (0-0) vs. New York Giants (0-0) 

Week 1 of the 2023 NFL season is just around the corner, so we are taking a close look at Sunday Night Football from MetLife Stadium in East Rutherford, New Jersey, to get you the best Cowboys vs. Giants betting pick and odds. 

Dallas is a 3-point road fave with a total of 46.5 points. You can take the Cowboys at -163 moneyline odds, while the Giants sit at +143. Last year, the Cowboys went 2-0 straight up and 1-1 ATS against the Giants, who covered a 10-point spread in a 28-20 defeat in Dallas. The Giants suffered a 23-16 loss at home, playing as 1-point home favorites.  

The Cowboys aim for their fifth straight W over the Giants

The Dallas Cowboys went 12-5 in 2022 and made the postseason for the second straight year. They trounced the Tampa Bay Buccaneers 31-14 in the wild-card round to grab their first playoff win on the road in more than 30 years, but the San Francisco 49ers were too much for the Cowboys in the divisional round, 19-12. 

It was another disappointment for the Cowboys and Dak Prescott, who tossed for 2,860 yards and 23 touchdowns in 12 regular-season games. He also had 15 interceptions which is the most in Dak’s seven-year career. 

Tony Pollard led the Cowboys’ backfield in 2022 and will continue to do it this time around. He had 193 carries for 1,007 yards and nine touchdowns last regular season while catching 39 passes for 371 yards and three scores. Ezekiel Elliott, who posted 876 yards and 12 touchdowns on 231 in carries in 2022, is no longer a Cowboy, so Pollard will be a go-to guy in 2023. 

CeeDee Lamb was Prescott’s favorite target last season. Lamb notched 107 receptions for 1,359 yards and nine touchdowns, while tight end Dalton Schultz added 577 yards and five scores on 57 catches. Schultz joined the Houston Texans in the offseason, and the Cowboys decided to strengthen their receiving corps with Brandin Cooks, who posted 699 receiving yards with the Texans in 2022.

The Giants hope for another successful season

The New York Giants reached the playoffs last season after finishing third in the NFC East with a 9-7-1 record. They beat the Minnesota Vikings 31-24 on the road in the wild-card round before losing to the mighty Philadelphia Eagles 38-7 in the divisional round. 

Daniel Jones led the way for the Giants in 2022, throwing for 3,205 yards, 15 touchdowns, and five interceptions. He also ran 120 times for 708 yards and seven touchdowns, so the Giants hope that Jones will continue to improve in 2023. 

 

Saquon Barkley finally stayed healthy in 2022. He posted 295 totes for 1,312 yards and ten touchdowns while adding 57 receptions for 338 yards. Richie James also had 57 catches, notching 569 yards and four touchdowns, while Darius Slayton had 46 catches for 724 yards and a pair of scores. James joined the Kansas City Chiefs in the offseason, and the Giants decided to trade for tight end Darren Waller, who’ll be a huge threat for the opposing defenses if he stays healthy.

Full-Game Side Bet

Insiders Status:

Rating:

Dak Prescott has owned the Giants since he joined the league, leading his Cowboys to 11 wins in their previous 12 encounters with New York. Dallas has gone 8-3 ATS in that stretch, but I would go against the H2H betting trends in this clash. 

The Cowboys have a new offensive coordinator in Brian Schottenheimer, and they might need some time to gel things. Furthermore, the Cowboys left guard Tyler Smith is questionable to play in Week 1 after suffering a hamstring injury in practice, and his eventual absence would be a massive blow to Dallas’ offense. 

The Giants should be able to keep it close down the stretch. Their ground game will torture the Cowboys, who ranked 20th in the NFL in rushing yards allowed last season (129.3 per game). The Giants are 5-1 ATS in their last six games overall and 5-2 ATS in their previous seven outings at home. 

Prediction: New York Giants +3.0 (-110)

Full-Game Total Pick

Insiders Status:

Rating:

The over is 3-2 in the last five meetings between the Cowboys and Giants, but only two of those five tilts have seen more than 42 points on the scoreboard. I’m expecting both teams to play solid defense at the start of the new season, so I’ll take the under. 

A lot will depend on Tyler Smith’s availability. Without him, the Cowboys could easily struggle to give Dak Prescott enough time in the pocket, and Dallas’ ground game would suffer, too. On the other side, I’m looking for the Giants to run as much as possible in this matchup.
