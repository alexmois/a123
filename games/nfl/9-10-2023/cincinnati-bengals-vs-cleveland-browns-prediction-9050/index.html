The Cleveland Browns play host to the Cincinnati Bengals on Sunday afternoon in an AFC North showdown from Cleveland Browns Stadium. The Bengals come into the season as a favorite in the division and in the AFC as a whole, as they aim to start the season strong. For the Browns, they are looking for a return trip to the playoffs and with a little more continuity within the team, the sky could be the limit for this team this year. Last year, each team won their respective home match, as it should be interesting to see if that trend rings true in 2023.

Bengals Brace For Road Rivalry

The Bengals made waves on Thursday night after announcing a record-setting contract for their quarterback, setting the tone for them to continue their run of strong play that has been developed over the last few seasons. Last season, the Bengals won the division, though after losing in Cleveland, will want to set the tone differently to open this season. They bring back plenty of talent and over the second half of the season, were probably the NFL's best team up until their playoff loss to the Chiefs. Two years removed from a Super Bowl loss, there's little doubt that on any given night, this team can be elite on both sides of the ball, with an opening win against the Browns a must for this one.

Cincinnati has a few injuries to worry about as kickoff quickly approaches, most namely the status of Joe Burrow (calf). After going down in training camp, he is probable in this one, as is Tyler Boyd (toe). On the defensive side, all eyes remain on the status of Joseph Ossai (ankle), who is questionable and can be a true difference-maker on line in this one.

As long as Burrow continues to trend toward starting in this one, the offense should be able to roll on the road. Burrow threw for over 4,400 yards last season and with one of the best duos at wide receiver in the league, with Ja'Marr Chase and Tee Higgins. The passing attack is a great advantage for this group, especially against the Browns. Add in a reliable back in Joe Mixon and this team also has the balance to control the clock and therefore, control the game.

Defensively, the Bengals bring back quite a bit of talent as well. Logan Wilson (123 tackles) has been one of the best linebackers in the league of late, though the secondary is what has truly continued to turn heads over the last few years. The combination of Vonn Bell (4 interceptions) and Jessie Bates III (4 interceptions) has shown to be elite and against Watson, will need to be locked in on not allowing the Bengals to get beat through the air.

Browns Sensing Prime Opportunity

The Browns missed the playoffs last season and after a wild off-season heading into 2022 has given way to some more normalcy heading into Sunday, Cleveland will be hoping that coincides with a win in the season opener. To be able to take down Cincinnati at home, would set the tone in the AFC North for the year, even if home field advantage wasn't as prominent last year. The key will be for this group to establish balance on both sides of the ball and to win in the trenches. The offensive and defensive line play struggled at times last season, while inconsistencies through injuries and off-the-field problems, never allowed this team to find their rhythm.

The Browns have some injuries of their own to worry about as they head into the season opener, with their injury report just a little lengthier than that of the Bengals. On the offensive end, they remain in good shape, though the defensive could be thin in this one. In the secondary, a pair of cornerbacks have already been ruled out, while Juan Thornhill (calf) and Denzel Ward (concussion) are questionable.

The offense will need to be far more efficient in both facets than they were a year ago when they really leaned heavily on the running game. Deshaun Watson should be better, especially without the distractions that surrounded him last season, though the wide receiver room remains thin. Nick Chubb, a 1,500-yard rusher last season, should see plenty of the ball yet again. If he can churn out first downs, it'll be instrumental, though without Kareem Hunt to spell him at times, it remains to be seen how efficient he can be.

Defensively, there are major concerns surrounding the secondary. They are down four players at those positions, while facing one of the league's best passing attacks is going to be a challenge. Myles Garrett (16 sacks) does return and his ability to make himself known in the pass rush, should take some pressure off of the secondary. Keep an eye on Grant Delpit (105 tackles, 4 interceptions) as well, as he looks to make his presence known against Higgins and Chase.
