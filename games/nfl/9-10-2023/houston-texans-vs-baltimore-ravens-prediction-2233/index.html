New Era in Texas

The Houston Texans struggled through a rough season in 2022, finishing 3-13-1. The team's brass immediately went about reshaping the face of the organization by bringing in former Texans linebacker and Niners' defensive coordinator Ryans. Ryans will bring toughness to the Texans on both sides of the ball and immediately added two critical pieces in this year's draft by grabbing quarterback Stroud with the second pick of the first round and then former Alabama linebacker Will Anderson Jr. with the third pick of the first round.

"Being the head coach of the Houston Texans is my dream job and my family is thrilled to be back in H-Town. I have been around the game of football my entire life and I've always had a natural ability to lead others. I know what it takes to win and be successful in this league as both a player and coach. We're going to build a program filled with players who have a special work ethic and relentless mindset. I understand the responsibility I have to this organization and to the fans of Houston to build a winner and I can't wait to get to work." DeMarco said after being tabbed the Texans head man.

DeMarco's offense will be led by rookie quarterback Stroud. Stroud was shaky at the start of the preseason but progressed, particularly with the team's first-string offensive line in front of him. He will quarterback what was the league's 31st-ranked offense in 2022. Houston was 31st in rushing offense and 25th in passing offense. Stroud will have depth behind him at running back with last year's starter Dameon Pierce joined now by veteran running back Devin Singletary. At receiver, Nico Collins returns as a starter after being a pleasant surprise at the end of last season. He will be flanked by Robert Woods, a former stalwart for the Rams and Titans. The Texans also added former Cowboys TE Dalton Shultz to give Stroud more options.

The Texans hope that Ryans instantly brings credibility to the Texans' defense. Last year, Houston finished 30th in total defense. They were also dead last against the run but a solid 10th against the pass. Derek Stingley Jr. gets set for his second season at corner and has the ability to be a true shutdown cornerback. Steven Nelson starts on the other side and the Texans added depth Shaquill Griffin as a nickel. The biggest change defensively comes on the defensive front where Anderson Jr. is expected to give the Texans a legitimate pass-rushing threat this season. Ryans has the pedigree to help move this defense up the rankings and become a legitimate threat week in and week out.

Injury Report: LB Blake Cashman (hamstring), WR John Metchie (hamstring), and S Jimmie Ward (hip) are all questionable.

Franchise QB Content

After a tumultuous offseason that briefly led to quarterback Lamar Jackson asking for a trade, the Ravens were able to sign their franchise quarterback to a long-term deal and then went about building talent around him. Jackson, who missed a large portion of last season with a lingering knee injury, now has five former first-round draft picks to work with at wide receiver, including this year's first-round pick Zay Flowers out of Boston College. Flowers will have help with former All-Pro Odell Beckham Jr. now catches passes for the Ravens and, of course, All-Pro tight end Marc Andrews. The Ravens were just 16th in total offense last season with Jackson's prolonged absence and 28th throwing the ball. They were 3rd in rushing offense and that number should be strong again this season with Jackson back under center and running backs JK Dobbins and Gus Edwards both healthy.

“Hopefully, I have a hot hand this year, and everyone has a hot hand, so defensive coordinators are having nightmares when they see the Ravens on the schedule because we have so many weapons,” Dobbins said about the Ravens' new-look offense.

From the days of Ray Lewis and Ed Reed, the Ravens have been defined by their defense. During their 10-7 season a year ago, the Ravens' defense was once again stout finishing sixth in total defense. They were a dominant 3rd against the run and 23rd against the pass. The Ravens added Jadeveon Clowney in free agency as another edge rusher. Inside linebackers Roquan Smith and Patrick Queen can run sideline to sideline and are the heart of the Ravens' rugged defense. A concern for the Ravens is at cornerback where Pro Bowler Marlon Humphrey (foot) is expected to miss several weeks.

Injury Report: LB Christian Harris (hamstring) and RB Dare Ogunbowale (shoulder) are questionable. WR Beckham Jr. (ankle) is probable.

Full-Game Side Bet

Insiders Status:

Rating:

The Texans will certainly get better as the season goes along but this is a tough ask of a rookie quarterback to go up against the Ravens' defense on the road in week 1. The Ravens' dominant run defense will force Stroud into obvious passing situations and that will be a problem for the rookie in his first NFL start. I expect the Ravens to get a few turnovers as they confuse the young quarterback into bad throws. On the other side of the ball, the Texans' defense can only go up from last year but it will take time. They now square off against one of the most elusive and dangerous quarterbacks in the NFL, Lamar Jackson. Jackson will certainly be determined to prove he is worthy of his mega-deal this offseason. He has plenty of weapons to choose from including Andrews, Flowers, and Beckham Jr., and also has a healthy backfield for the first time in over a year. Expect the Ravens to be tough for the young Texans to handle on both sides of the ball and pull away in the second half for a comfortable win.

Take the Ravens -9.5
