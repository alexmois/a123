The Tampa Bay Buccaneers play host to the Chicago bears on Sunday afternoon in an NFC showdown from Raymond James Stadium. The Bears looked awful in their season opener and are in need of a bounce-back win in this one. For Tampa Bay, an upset in week one set the tone that they might not necessarily be a rebuild after all. When these two squared of in 2021, Tampa Bay came away with a dominant 38-3 win at home, as they look to find similar success in this one. This could end up being an exciting matchup and even though it's two teams unlikely to make the playoffs, still should keep fans captivated.

Bears Back On The Road

Hopes were high last Sunday at Soldier Field, as the Bears were looking to set the tone in the NFC North and come away with a crucial win against a longtime rival. The Packers quickly showed why they've had Chicago's number over the last few seasons and as they hit the road in this one, find themselves in a difficult situation. They are in desperate need of a win but the road is an area that hasn't exactly been kind to them of late. The Bears have lost ten games in a row dating back to last season, failing to stay within single digits in five of the last six. While Tampa Bay isn't the most daunting of opponents, it still might be asking a lot out of this young Chicago team to flip a switch on the road, especially as compared to how last week went.

 

The Bears have one of the league's shortest injury reports, with only five players listed as they head towards Sunday. The areas of injuries are a concern though. They continue to be without a pair of offensive linemen, already thinning out a struggling position. Dylan Cole (hamstring) remains probable though, as this team needs all the help they can get.

Chicago's offense generated quick a few yards and points in garbage time last weekend but when it came to generating successful drives and plays in key moments, this team seemed to struggle. Justin Fields didn't impress as much as would be expected for a third-year quarterback, though the offensive line didn't help, as they allowed four sacks. Protecting Fields has to be their top priority, while he also can't be their leading rusher either. DJ Moore was nearly a non-factor in this one and expect a game plan that focuses on putting the ball in his hands.

Defensively, the Bears looked a step or two too slow in this one too. TJ Edwards (14 tackles) looked ready for the opener but the defensive line struggled to keep up with the difference that the linebackers were making. Only one sack won't cut it against likely playoff teams and the pass rush will have to be far more impressive on the road if they want to compete. Tremaine Edwards remains a key play in the linebacker room as well, though they struggled to stop the run and pass last week, especially in key situations. Expect them to have a better gameplan for this one, though the matchup looks similar to the one from last weekend.

Bucs Back At Home

Tampa Bay was one of the league's biggest underdogs last week and even though they came away with the win, questions remain as to what their potential looks like. They were outplayed by the Vikings across most categories, though the only thing that matters at the end of the day is whoever came out with the win. The Buccs have gone 5-5 over their last ten games, which at least inspires more confidence than what the Bears are going though. The homefield advantage at Raymond James has been well-documented as well. In their last five home games, they've gone 3-2, with those two loses coming against playoff teams, so with the Bears looking like it could be another year closer to the first pick, it gives Tampa Bay the leg up. Confidence is huge, as is momentum, while a trip back to Raymond James for the home opener is sure to have the fans ready to go on Sunday.

Tampa Bay's injury report is a little lengthier than what would be expected, though being at home should help those that are probable or battling injuries, come back a little quicker. Baker Mayfield (shoulder) is probably the most notable name, especially after last week's performance, though is probable to go. Being without Ryan Jensen (knee) on the offensive line hurts too. On the defensive end, both Christian Izien (concussion) and Carlton Davis III (toe) are questionable as well.

The offense didn't necessarily impress last week, though many have overlooked it due to the win. They only put up 242 total yards and come into this one with a struggling ground game and lack of passing game. Mayfield has experience but has changed quite a bit over the last few seasons, lacking some of the creativity that teams craved from him out of college. Mayfield's job will continue to be for him to take care of the ball, though that hasn't been a problem yet this season. Add in one of the league's most experienced wide receiver tandems in Mike Evans and Chris Godwin, and this offense is likely to only improve.

The Buccaneers will get to 2-0 with a home win on Sunday against the Bears and will lean on some experience in this one. Mayfield seemed to have a great grasp on his role in week one and with the NFL being a true quarterback-driven league, he seems more equipped for winning games than Fields right now. The Bears didn't give him the tools and play calling to succeed against the Packers and it'll be asking a lot for them to flip a switch on the road. The check down passes and lack of big plays really hurt them last week and unless something drastically changes, the offense doesn't seem equipped to perform on the road, especially against a seasoned Tampa Bay defense. Expect Evans and Godwin to combine to wreck havoc on the outside, leading the way for Tampa Bay to grab the home win.
