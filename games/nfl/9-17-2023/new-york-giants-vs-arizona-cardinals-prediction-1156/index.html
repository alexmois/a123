The New York Giants hope for a restart in week two after a disastrous season opener. The 0-1 Giants head to the desert to take on on 0-1 Cardinals on Sunday. The Cardinals have owned the Giants in recent years, winning 4 straight, including a 26-7 win in their last meeting in 2020. The Giants are 4-point favorites for this game. Kickoff is Sunday at 4:05 p.m. ET at State Farm Stadium in Glendale, Arizona.

Giants have ugly week one

The New York Giants had a week one to forget. They were embarrassed in their own building by the Dallas Cowboys, losing 40-0. There was nothing good about this and it was an awful performance all around, including by their newly minted multi-million dollar quarter Daniel Jones. Jones had a quarterback rating of 32.4, going 15 of 28 for 104 yards with two picks and no interceptions. He also ran for 43 yards. Saquon Barkley didn't do much either, gaining just 51 yards. He also caught three passes for 12 yards. The leading receiver was Darren Waller, who had 3 receptions for 36 yards.

Dallas has a really good defense, and it may be a top five defense before it is all said and done. There was also a lot of rain, but that didn't stop Dallas from scoring 40 points, so that's no excuse. The question is, is Dallas's defense that good or is the Giants offense that bad? Jones was sacked 7 times and that's a huge concern. The Giants also turned the ball over 3 times, including the two picks and a lost fumble. One of the interceptions was a pick 6. To make matters worse, the Giants have 10 players listed as questionable, including offensive lineman Matt Peart, while tight end Darren Waller is listed as probable.

Cardinals fall to Commanders

Not much is expected from the Arizona Cardinals this season, and they did not disappoint in week one, as they lost to the Washington Commanders last Sunday, 20-16. Playing in a very competitive NFC West, the Cardinals should be in for a long season. Five wins would be considered a good year for this depleted team, which seems like it wants ton draft its third quarterback in the last handful of years.

That said, the Cardinals had a great chance to win this game last week and led 16-10 heading into the fourth quarter. But the Commanders' defense stiffened in the final frame and they rallied for the win. The Cardinals are forced to roll with journeyman Joshua Dobbs at quarterback as starter Kyler Murray is out indefinitely with a knee injury. Dobbs played like a journeyman backup, going 21 for 30 for 162 yards with no TDs or INTs. To make matters worse, their top offensive weapon, running back James Conner, who ran for 62 yards last week, is listed as questionable for this game. He is one of a long list of players who are questionable for the Cardinals.
