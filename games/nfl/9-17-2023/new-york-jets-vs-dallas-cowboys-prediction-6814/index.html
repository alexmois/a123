The New York Jets and Dallas Cowboys will square off in an AFC-NFC showdown on Sunday at AT&T Stadium. Kickoff from Dallas is at 4:25 p.m. ET.

New York (1-0 SU and 1-0 ATS) beat Buffalo 22-16 in its season opener and Dallas (1-0 SU and 1-0 ATS) dismantled the New York Giants 40-0 on Sunday Night Football.

The Cowboys are 8.5-point spread favorites and the game total is 39 points.

Injury Report:

New York: Quarterback Aaron Rodgers suffered a torn left Achilles last week, ending his season. Offensive tackle Duane Brown, running back Breece Hall, defensive lineman Carl Lawson, and running back Israel Abanikanda are questionable to play.

Dallas: Offensive tackle Tyron Smith is probable to play, and wide receiver Brandin Cooks, offensive lineman Tyler Smith, offensive guard Zack Martin, cornerback Jourdan Lewis, and safety Donovan Wilson are questionable.

Are the Jets already out of fuel?

New York believed it was only a competent quarterback away from a deep postseason run last season, prompting the franchise to trade for future Hall of Famer Aaron Rodgers in the off-season. Unfortunately, the move was disastrous, as the 39-year-old suffered a season-ending Achilles injury on the first drive in week one. Now, New York must move to plan B: Zach Wilson.

The Jets' 2021 first-round pick struggled as the face of the franchise, completing 55.6 percent of his passing attempts with a 9:11 TD:INT rate in his rookie year and 54.5 percent of his passes with a 6:7 TD:INT rate in 2022. Taking over the reins of a team that was expected to be a serious contender could prove even more challenging for the 24-year-old BYU product.

New York still came out on top in its highly-anticipated clash with Buffalo on Monday, as the defense forced three interceptions (all picked by safety Jordan Whitehead) and running back Breece Hall galloped for 127 yards on ten carries. The overtime victory was cathartic for Jets fans, who watched their season seemingly end shortly after it started, but it will likely be one of few victories for Gang Green in the 2023-24 season.

Dallas has legitimate Super Bowl aspirations

It was a tough week one for both of New York's teams, as the Cowboys whooped the Giants by more than five touchdowns on Sunday night in front of a nationally televised crowd. On the same MetLife Stadium turf that Dallas joyously celebrated the start of a promising season, the Jets watched in horror as their franchise quarterback crumpled to the ground in agony, prematurely ending their season.

The Boys took advantage of the Giants' turnover woes, scoring in every quarter of the 40-point beatdown. Dallas held New York to 63 passing yards (17-for-30), picking off two passes and forcing a fumble. The Cowboys rushed for 122 yards on 30 carries, as running back Tony Pollard led the way with two scores. The victory, while encouraging, only counts once in the win column, something QB Dak Prescott preached after the game.

“It’s the first game,” Prescott said. “That’s y'all’s job to write whatever statement it needs to be. For us, it’s about not getting ahead of ourselves. Continuing to stay disciplined, to show the discipline. Not only what we did through these four quarters, but taking that and making sure we use it throughout our preparation this week.”

If this Cowboys team is going to reach the Super Bowl, the defense will be the engine. Dallas was the best in the NFL in creating takeaways the past two seasons (67 total) and got to the quarterback often (95 combined sacks). It figures to be among the league's top units once again in 2023-24, with stars at every level.

However, if the offense continues to be careless with the football, it might not matter how great the defense plays. Prescott was error-prone in 2022-23, throwing a league-high 17 interceptions in 14 total games. New offensive coordinator Brian Schottenheimer hopes that a quicker-hitting offensive attack will reduce those mistakes.

Full-Game Side Bet

Insiders Status:

Rating:

This line is an overreaction to Dallas' beatdown of the Giants and Rodgers' injury.

New York will focus on clock management on Sunday, moving the football methodically with Breece Hall and Dalvin Cook to take pressure off Wilson. The Jets' defense will still need to hold up its end of the bargain, though, as a New York cover will only cash if it can force Dallas into a closer, lower-scoring game. As long as the Jets' defense isn't on the field too long on Sunday, I'm confident it can hold up against the Cowboys.

The NY defensive line is arguably the best in football. It will need to prove that this week, as Dallas will also look to establish the run with Pollard and set up quick-strike passing opportunities for Prescott. I'm comfortable predicting that the Jets' front will make this a challenge, as Cowboys right guard Zack Martin is dealing with a groin injury, tackle Tyron Smith has been battling with an ankle injury, and left guard Tyler Smith has been limited with a hamstring injury. Without a reliable running game, Prescott's turnover issue could rear its ugly head against NY's talented secondary.

Give me the Jets plus the points!

Prediction: Jets +8.5

Full-Game Total Pick

Insiders Status:

Rating:

Call me crazy, but I think this line has come down too much. Sure, the Jets should be able to limit Dallas' ground game, but there's no guarantee that's going to happen. Plus, Prescott is well aware of the criticism of his play last season. His job is secure, but if he's the sole reason why the Cowboys underachieve again, he might not be long for Dallas. After their performance in week one, it wouldn't shock me if they score 27-28 points at home.

The Jets were able to establish the run against Buffalo without Rodgers on Monday, so it's not as if their performance was an aberration. Hall looked pretty good for a player coming off a knee injury and should form a nice 1-2 combo with Cook. Expectations for Wilson are low and while I don't predict a 250-plus yard performance from the third-year pro, he doesn't have to be "the man" to get New York into the end zone. The Jets still have an intriguing group of pass-catchers to bail him out, too. Garrett Wilson and company aren't simply going to give up on their seasons because Rodgers is sidelined.

It's unlikely to be the easiest over win on Sunday, but that's the bet I'm making on the total in this game.

Prediction: Over 39
