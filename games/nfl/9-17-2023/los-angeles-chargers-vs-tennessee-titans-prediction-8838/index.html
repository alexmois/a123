A pair of teams that couldn't get the job done in Week 1 will try again on Sunday. The Los Angeles Chargers (0-1; 0-1-0 ATS) have flown to Nashville, where the Tennessee Titans (0-1; 1-0-0 ATS) await. These teams met last season, with the Chargers walking away with a 17-14 victory. That was in SoFi Stadium, whereas this game is in Nissan Stadium. At 1:00 p.m. EDT on Sunday, we'll see if the Titans can get payback for last year's loss. Barring an unlikely tie, someone will leave with win number one on Sunday. Who will it be?

Bolts Looking to Bounce Back

The offense wasn't the issue as 34 points weren't enough last week, as the Chargers fell in their home opener to Miami. Last year's defense was mediocre at best, despite having a lot of top-end talent on paper. They allowed the most yards per rush, the 24th-most yards per pass attempt, and finished 21st in scoring. Well, this season started even worse, as the Bolts gave up 466 passing yards on 45 attempts (10.4 yards per attempt) and bled 36 points. The last six came with fewer than two minutes remaining to seal their fate. At least they weren't terrible against the run, even if they played a poor rushing team. Right now, everyone is basically healthy. Joey Bosa is questionable with a hamstring injury but likely playing, while Eric Kendricks is questionable due to personal reasons. Will the talent finally gel into a top unit, or will the blown 27-0 lead last postseason linger?

 

Offensively, this team is still dangerous. The 2022 squad finished 13th in scoring, a bit lower than most would expect. This season's team should have one of the top passing attacks league-wide. Justin Herbert is back, along with a phenomenal receiver group. If everyone stays healthy, most defenses will be scrambling to contain them. However, it was the running game that dominated last Sunday, which is a stark contrast to 2022. It's especially puzzling since the team's front office remains steadfast in not paying Austin Ekeler, who finished with 16 carries for 117 yards and a touchdown. There aren't many concerns about what this team can do when they have the ball. As long as the injury report is full of questionable players and not outs, expect the Chargers to score often. Ekeler (ankle) is not making the trip to Nashville.

Time for Titans to Change?

Tennessee was cruising to a third straight division title late last year. Then, they lost their final seven, Jacksonville got hot, and the Titans suddenly had an early offseason. This season began how 2022 ended, with the Titans on the losing end at the final whistle. Even with an improved WR group, this offense is built to operate through RB Derrick Henry. He only finished with 15 carries on Sunday, a one-point loss to the Saints. The Titans had 22 rushes overall. Meanwhile, QB Ryan Tannehill ended the day with 34 pass attempts. He completed 16 of them for 198 yards. Add three interceptions and three sacks to that stat line. Tennessee's offensive line won't be doing the offense any favors. Still, the formula remains clear, feed Henry, and the rest of the playbook will open up. 

The Titans defense performed admirably on Sunday, with four sacks and two takeaways. Pairing that with stifling the run and holding a team to 16 points makes sense. However, this week poses a much greater challenge for a defense with many concerns. Tennessee's great run defense carrying over from last year won't matter against the Chargers. It'll be shoring up last year's NFL-worst pass defense that determines how the defense performs. A healthy defensive front generating pressure was a great start last week, but they also allowed 8.6 yards per attempt and a 69.7% completion rate. If the Titans aren't getting pressure, they're going to get gashed through the air. That'll make winning difficult, which will put a lot of butts on the hot seat.

It's about time that the Titans won again. It won't be easy, but they're facing a run defense that hasn't done enough to prove they're legitimate yet. Tennessee can use Henry to run all over them, as they did last year when he had 21 carries for 104 yards and a score. That will allow Tennessee to control the game and utilize their new receivers in play action, something that didn't threaten Los Angeles at all a year ago.

Preventing Los Angeles from throwing all over them so that they don't charge back into the game will be the challenge on the other side. However, last year's defense sacked Herbert thrice and caught two picks. The pieces are there to do it again, just look at Week 1, and the Chargers are not a threat to run on Tennessee. Using the home crowd, the Titans will take control early with the run game offensively and pin their ears back defensively to put this one away.

Full-Game Total Pick

Insiders Status:

Rating:

When these teams met last season, the final score was 17-14. Tennessee ran 12 fewer plays but ran the ball so well that they won the time of possession battle. That kept the clock moving, thus shortening the game. With Los Angeles' weak run defense, that should happen again, and even if scoring comes easier, there won't be enough time on the clock for the over to hit.

The Chargers had issues protecting Herbert last week and those problems will probably extend into this game. Pressure will lead to sacks and turnovers, as it did last year, which will limit the Chargers' output. Even if everything goes right offensively, the Titans aren't a team that will light up the scoreboard. Expect these teams to finish under the total again.
