The Detroit Lions play host to the Seattle Seahawks in an NFC showdown on Sunday afternoon from Ford Field. The Seahawks are coming off a strong season last year but are 0-1 after a loss in the opener last week. For the Lions, a road win against the Chiefs put the NFL on notice, as they suddenly look like they could be a team to be reckoned with in the NFC. Last fall, the Seahawks won at Ford Field in a 48-45 shootout, while a big offensive night will be expected again. The outcome of this one could have an impact on the NFC seeding when things are said and done and could end up being one of the best matchups of the weekend.

Seattle In Need Of Win

After taking a loss at home last weekend, the Seahawks find themselves hitting the road in dire need of a win here in week two. The schedule eases up after this one, as they'll get the Panthers and Giants next, but with how competitive the NFC West expects to be this year, sitting at 0-2 after two weeks could be a death sentence. Seattle fared far worse on the road than at home last season, going 1-3 over their last four road games, which might not give off the most confidence heading into this one. It likely won't hurt though that the Seahawks likely haven't forgotten about an instrumental win against the Lions on this same field last season. This game already seems to be a breaking point for this team, though a win would completely shift the direction they're going down.

The Seahawks have a lengthy injury report themselves, with 14 players listed as they head into the weekend. Will Dissly (shoulder) is probable to go at tight end, which will ease one area of concern on the offensive end. Defensively, Devin Bush (shoulder) is questionable, as is Jamal Adams (quadriceps) in the secondary. Another piece of good news is that Devon Witherspoon (hamstring), their highly touted rookie, is probable to go at cornerback.

Offensively, the Seahawks struggled last week and while it was only the opener, it's hard to not be worried about what they showed. They only went for 180 totals yards in this one, while barely getting to 100 yards through the air. Those numbers were a stark contrast to the pass-happy offense that took the league by storm last year. Geno Smith remains an experienced arm, while there is talent and athleticism all over the offense. If he can get back on the same page with Metcalf and Lockett, they should return to being dangerous.

As if things couldn't have gone worse last weekend, the defense looked poor as well. They allowed over 400 yards to an offense without their top wide receiver and while the run defense was better than the pass defense, it wasn't enough to think that'll be a strength of this group either. It doesn't help that Detroit impressed with their own offensive balance and Seattle will need to slow that down. Bobby Wagner (18 tackles) is the undisputed winner but until they can prove it, success seems far away.

Lions Coming Off Head Turning Performance

Hopes were high for the Lions after a fantastic finish to the 2022 season but having to hit the road to play the Chiefs to kick off the NFL season always seemed like it would be a challenge. To the shock of quite a few, the Lions were able to come away with the win and return home with what could end up being the most impressive win of the regular season. After seeing the Vikings, the preseason favorite in the division, start off 0-2, Detroit knows that a 2-0 start could do wonders for their playoff outlook come the winter. The Lions have won three in a row dating back to last season, as well as eight of their last ten games, showing that they could be one of the better teams in the league this season.

The Lions have a number of injuries as well, though having played last Thursday has helped them go a long way towards getting healthy in preparation for this weekend. CJ Gardner-Johnson (knee) remains out, which is a massive absence, especially considering Emmanuel Moseley (knee) is questionable too. On the offensive side, the wide receiver room didn't seem to miss a beat without Jameson Williams (suspension), which is great news.

The Lions showed that an offense that looked elite at the tail end of last season wasn't just a flash in the pan. They had over 350 yards of offense, while putting together over 100 yards on the ground and another 250 yards through the air. Jared Goff has continued to take care of the ball, while the running back duo of David Montgomery and Jahmyr Gibbs looks poised to cause opponents issues all season. With the holes in Seattle's defense based on last week, Detroit will be thrilled to get their own crack at it.

Defensively, the Lions struggled themselves and were helped by quite a few drops that they can't rely on happening in this one. Seattle's offense isn't nearly as explosive as Kansas City's, as this weekend could be a strong reprieve for this team. The pass rush struggled to get home to Mahomes last week and without Gardner-Johnson in the secondary in this one, they can't afford to be slow to the quarterback. Aidan Hutchinson is still a force on the defensive line and is likely to get to Smith and make his life rough in this one.
