The Kansas City Chiefs (0-1) will be trying to pick up their first road win of the season when they face the Jacksonville Jaguars (1-0) on Sunday afternoon. The game will be played at EverBank Stadium and it is scheduled to begin at 1 PM. ET.

The Chiefs are coming off a 21-20 loss to Detroit as four-point favorites. The Jaguars are coming off a 31-21 win over Indianapolis as 3.5-point favorites.

Kansas City is 8-2 in its last 10 games against Jacksonville.

Chiefs Trying To Bounce Back From Loss

The Chiefs couldn’t hold off the Lions in their first game last Thursday and dropped their first season-opener since Patrick Mahomes became the starter. They will try to bounce back from the loss with a win over the Jaguars, which will give them their first win of the season and eighth straight win over Jacksonville. It also didn't help that star TE Travis Kelce (knee) and All-Pro DT Chris Jones (holdout) were out.

Kansas City scored 20 points in its first game. They threw for 226 yards and rushed for 90 yards in the loss.

Mahomes completed 54 percent of his passes for 226 yards, two touchdowns, and one interception. He also led the team with 45 rushing yards, while Marquez Valdes-Scantling had two catches for 48 yards.

Kansas City played well defensively, giving up 21 points in the loss. They will need to do a better job getting to the quarterback if they want to win this game.

Travis Kelce (Knee) and Chris Jones (Holdout) are expected to play.

Jaguars Trying To End Another Streak

The Jaguars kicked off their season with a win over the Colts, ending their five-game losing streak in Indianapolis. They have lost their last seven games to the Chiefs and will try to put an end to that streak with a win on Sunday, which will give them their second win in a row.

Jacksonville scored 31 points in its first game. They threw for 237 yards and rushed for 105 yards in the win

Trevor Lawrence completed 75 percent of his passes for 241 yards, two touchdowns, and one interception. Travis Etienne Jr. led the team with 18 carries for 77 yards and one touchdown, while Calvin Ridley had eight catches for 101 yards and one touchdown.

Jacksonville played well defensively in its first game, giving up 21 points in the win. They will need a similar effort if they want to get the win.

Brandon Scherff (Ankle), Luke Fortner (Lower Body), and Gregory Junior (Hamstring) are questionable for this game.

Full-Game Side Bet

Insiders Status:

Rating:

The Chiefs had the top-scoring offense in the league last season, but they didn’t look good in their first game, finishing with 20 points. They didn’t run the ball efficiently and their passing game sorely missed Kelce. It also didn’t help their cause that their receivers dropped a few crucial catches. The Jaguars had a very good run defense last season and they looked good in their first game, holding the Colts to 65 rushing yards. Even though they faced a rookie quarterback in their first game, they did a good job defending the pass and also forced three turnovers. With the Chiefs likely to go with a pass-heavy offense once again, expect the Jaguars to keep their offense in check. Jacksonville’s offense looked as good as advertised in week 1, with the team scoring over 30 points. They kept it balanced offensively, but they did a lot of damage through the air, which was something the Chiefs had trouble stopping in their first game. They didn’t get a lot of pressure on Goff and gave up a couple of big plays in their secondary. Even though Chris Jones rejoined the team this week, he missed training camp and the preseason and won’t be in football shape, so don’t expect him to have much of an impact.

The Jaguars already proved they can hang with the Chiefs after a 27-20 loss at Arrowhead in the playoffs. They are at home and have a lot of confidence after ending their five-year drought at Indianapolis in their last game, so look for them to try to make a statement in this game. Go with Jacksonville to cover the spread.

Prediction: Jacksonville Jaguars +3.5
