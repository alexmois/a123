The Baltimore Ravens and Cincinnati Bengals will renew their AFC North rivalry on Sunday at Paycor Stadium. Kickoff is scheduled for 1:00 p.m. ET.

Baltimore (1-0 SU and 1-0 ATS) defeated Houston by 16 points in week one. The Ravens have an over/under win total of 10.5 wins this season.

Cincinnati (0-1 SU and 0-1 ATS) lost by three touchdowns to Cleveland last week in the 100th edition of the “Battle of Ohio.” The Bengals have an over/under win total of 11.5 wins this season.

The Bengals are three-point spread favorites and the game total is 46.5 points.

Injury Report:

Baltimore: Tight end Mark Andrews, offensive tackle Ronnie Stanley, safety Marcus Williams, and center Tyler Linderbaum are questionable to play. Cornerback Marlon Humphrey is out indefinitely with a foot injury and running back J.K. Dobbins is out for the season with a torn Achilles.

Cincinnati: Quarterback Joe Burrow and safety Dax Hill are probable to play, and running back Chris Evans, linebacker Logan Wilson, tight end Drew Sample, and defensive end Joseph Ossai are questionable to suit up for the game.

Baltimore looks like a team that needs time to gel

Baltimore handled its business last Sunday, beating Houston 25-9 at home. It wasn't all sunshine and rainbows in Charm City, however, as safety Marcus Williams sustained a shoulder injury and running back J.K. Dobbins suffered a torn Achilles tendon that will sideline him for the rest of the season. The offense also looked out of whack under new offensive coordinator Todd Monken, as Baltimore managed only 265 total yards and QB Lamar Jackson threw two interceptions.

The defense did its job, though, preventing Texans rookie QB C.J. Stroud from reaching the end zone. Baltimore only led 7-6 at halftime but stayed locked in, holding Houston to three points in the second half. The Texans were 28-for-44 passing (4.0 YPA) and carried the football 23 times for 72 yards (3.1 YPC).

Monken's offensive system promises a more dynamic attack than the run-heavy scheme of years past, allowing Jackson to take advantage of the Ravens' skill players. Bettors should expect growing pains from the passing game, however, as there will be a learning curve and chemistry issues with new targets Odell Beckham Jr., Zay Flowers, and Nelson Agholor. The good news is someone other than Mark Andrews will be catching the football on Sundays.

That said, the Baltimore offense is still focused on the ground-and-pound attack. Even without Dobbins, the Ravens feel confident they can run the ball with Gus Edwards, Justice Hill, and Melvin Gordon sharing carries.

"We have backs, and we play our backs. They all play. They play well. We love our backs," head coach John Harbaugh told reporters Monday.

The Ravens' defense is strong at linebacker and safety, but there are concerns at tackle and cornerback. Brandon Williams and Calais Campbell have departed, leaving the middle of the defense vulnerable to run-heavy attacks. With Marcus Peters gone, the secondary is relying on Rock Ya-Sin and Jalyn Armour-Davis. Cornerback Marlon Humphrey's injury and safety Marcus Williams' absence don't help, either.

Is this a must-win game for Cincy?

Cincinnati was held scoreless in the first half and only managed a field goal in the second half of last week's loss at Cleveland. The Browns held the Bengals to 142 total yards of offense (2.6 yards per play), including just 67 passing yards. Cleveland held the football for nearly 12 more minutes than Cincinnati, rushing for 206 yards on 40 carries (5.2 YPC).

“It’s tough,” Bengals QB Joe Burrow said. "It’s one week, not up to our standard, not up to my standard. We’ll keep trucking.”

Cincinnati looked out of sorts, as the franchise's $230 million man looked rushed in his first action since suffering a calf strain more than a month ago. It won't get any easier, either, as Cincy has the toughest strength of schedule of any team in the AFC North.

It wouldn't be wise to bet on a lengthy losing streak in Queen City, though. Burrow is a megastar and has an excellent supporting cast, headlined by pass-catchers Ja'Marr Chase and Tee Higgins. Cincy invested in its offensive line over the summer, too, adding tackle Orlando Brown Jr. on a four-year contract. Running back Samaje Perine departed in free agency but the Bengals retained the veteran Joe Mixon, who took a pay cut to stick around.

For all of the talk about the O-line's struggles keeping Burrow upright, it's the defense that could keep Cincinnati from winning the Super Bowl this season. The stop unit is missing some pieces from the 2022 squad, and the secondary must replace two starting safeties, putting immense pressure on the Bengals' pass rush to make up for these inefficiencies. That could be asking too much from a unit that recorded the fourth-fewest sacks in the NFL last season.

Full-Game Total Pick

Insiders Status:

Rating:

There's no denying that both teams struggled offensively in week one. It will take Baltimore some time to gel and find its groove in a new offensive system, and I don't expect that to happen on the road Sunday. Without Dobbins, the Ravens' run game could be exposed, too. Jackson can help fill the void, but Monken's scheme is designed to take some of that out of his game. I'm sure Harbaugh is confident in his personnel, but time will tell if his group of backs can carry the load. Besides, no one in Baltimore's RB room is as talented as Nick Chubb, who accounted for the majority of Cleveland's rushing yards last week against Cincinnati.

The Bengals aren't likely to be held without a touchdown in consecutive weeks, but Burrow's struggles (14-for-31 passing and a 20.3 QBR) against the Browns can't be overlooked. He missed the preseason and can't possibly feel 100 percent at this point in the season. Baltimore has some holes defensively, but it showed a competitive edge in a close game last week — why can't it do the same on Sunday?

In this divisional rivalry game, I'm betting on the under.
