We have an NFC West showdown on Sunday as the Los Angeles Rams host the San Francisco 49ers. Both teams are 1-0 and they both looked impressive in getting their wins. The 49ers have won 8 of the last 9 against the Rams head-to-head, including two easy wins and covers last season. The Rams are 7-point home 'Dogs in this one. Kickoff is at 4:05 p.m. ET on Sunday at SoFi Stadium in Inglewood, Calif.

Niners look dominant

A lot of people expected the San Francisco 49ers to be one of the best teams in the NFL this season and they did not disappoint in week one. They traveled to Pittsburgh to face a tough Steelers team last Sunday and completely dominated the Steelers on their way to a 30-7 win. This, as stated, was complete and total domination, as the Niners led in time of possession (38 mins to 22 mins), total yards (391 to 239), first downs (22 to 15) and turnovers (2 to 1).

Quarterback Brock Purdy showed that he was no flash in the plan, as he went 19 for 29 for 220 yards and 2 TDs with no picks. He did get sacked five times, which is a concern, but the Steelers have an excellent pass rush, led by TJ Watt. But two studs really stood out for the Niners on offense -- Brandon Aiyuk, who caught 8 passes for 129 yards and two TDs, and Christian McCaffrey, who ran for 152 yards and a touchdown and caught three passes for 17 yards. The defense was also great, picking off Kenny Pickett twice, and holding Najee Harris to just 31 yards rushing. Among key injuries, tight end George Kittle is probable, as is receiver Ray Ray McCloud.

The 49ers have the Super Bowl in mind and look to be once again, one of the best teams in the NFC, but that's if they can stay healthy starting with Purdy.

Rams stun Seahawks

The Niners are expected to be good this season but the Rams? Many felt that this was a team headed for the Caleb Williams sweepstakes. But Caleb may have to play somewhere else next season is the Rams keep this up. They went into Seattle last Sunday, in a game they were expected to lose by about a touchdown, and they dominated the Seahawks, winning 30-13.

"There's just been a great attention to detail, there's been a great willingness to be accountable and coachable," Rams coach Sean McVay said, reported Sports Illustrated. "These guys love playing football with their teammates. They're continuing to come together as a team, which is the most important thing. Football is supposed to be a fun game and I think we play our best when they're enjoying it."

Football is a lot of fun when you win on the road 30-13. The Rams did this without their all-world receiver Cooper Kupp, who remains out with a hamstring injury. But rookie Puka Nacua stepped up in a big way, as he caught  10 passes for 119 yards. Tutu Atwell was equally as productive as he caught 6 balls for 119 yards. Matthew Stafford, back from an injury that kept him out much of last season, didn't miss a beat going 24 for 38 for 334 yards. Running back Kyren Williams ran for 52 yards and 2 scores while Cam Akers had 29 yards and a TD.  The defense was phenomenal, holding the high-flying Hawks to just 180 total yards. Kupp remains listed as out, and his replacement Nacua is listed as questionable.
