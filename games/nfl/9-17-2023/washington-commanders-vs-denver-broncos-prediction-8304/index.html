These franchises don't cross paths often, but they will this weekend. The Washington Commanders (1-0; 0-1-0 ATS), under new ownership, will breathe air that's a bit thinner than they're used to on Sunday. A trip to Empower Field at Mile High will put their lungs to the test. Of course, the Denver Broncos (0-1; 0-1-0 ATS) are much more accustomed to the conditions in Colorado. Neither team won against the spread last week, while each of their games finished under the total. Will anything change on Sunday at 4:25 p.m. EDT? Let's find out.

Great Teams Cover...

This does not apply to the Commanders, who are 1-5-0 ATS in their last six games. Guess that's what happens when a team doesn't score often. Sam Howell getting the keys to the offense didn't immediately ignite the club last week against a weak Arizona team. The offensive line had a brutal day, which didn't help. Howell was sacked six times, while the team's aggressive rushing attack only mustered 3.3 yards per carry. Last year's Commanders only scored 18.9 points per game (24th). This season's group got off to a similar start when given one of the better matchups on their schedule. Running the ball more effectively will help keep Howell upright (and protect the ball). Against Denver, it won't be easy, especially not a mile high. 

 

Fortunately, the defense still looked strong, even if it was only the Cardinals. Washington's defense didn't allow a touchdown (Arizona's only TD was a fumble recovery), and they only allowed 210 total yards. Holding Arizona to 3.6 yards per play was one of the best defensive performances of Week 1. Washington's pass rush seems ready to meet expectations after recording three sacks in the season opener. The new-look secondary, particularly at cornerback, held its own. The Commanders may have another top-10-scoring defense on their hands.

New Year, Same Story

Sean Payton's offensive restoration project didn't get off to a great start, although a missed extra point is what kept Denver from overtime last week. Against what projects to be one of the NFL's weakest defenses, the Broncos only mustered 260 total yards and 16 points. The running back duo of Javonte Williams and Samaje Perine performed nicely, leading Denver to 4.3 yards per carry. It was the passing game that still needs work. Russell Wilson may have completed 27/34 passes and only gotten sacked twice, but he only racked up 177 yards. That average resembles last year's performance and won't cut it. At least the Broncos left the game without any giveaways, which is always a positive.

The defense continued to shine at least, holding the Raiders to 17 points. They didn't get any sacks or force many incompletions, but they at least caught a pick. Denver also held last season's leading rusher to 48 yards on 19 carries. Circling back to the pass rush, it was by far the weakness of the defense in 2022. None of the offseason additions to help in that area panned out in Week 1. Regardless, the defense was sturdy without it and appears to be just as stout. This is a group that the Broncos won't need to worry about this season if everyone stays healthy, and certainly not in this game.

It's difficult to envision either of these teams running away with this game. Even in Denver's thin air, which will help the home team a lot, will the Broncos offense be able to score on a tough Washington defense? Probably not consistently, especially since Denver can't throw the ball. Washington has a ferocious pass rush that will give the Broncos issues and an improved secondary, which should blanket the receivers. Denver seems allergic to downfield throws anyway, which pressure won't help.

On the flip side, Washington won't be able to muster much either. Denver's run defense is impressive, which is how the Commanders like to power their offense. Washington has a better chance of succeeding through the air since the Broncos don't get many sacks. It may not be a (minor) upset simply due to conditioning, but this game will come down to the wire. Washington pulls out a rare win against the spread.

Prediction: Washington Commanders +3.5

Full-Game Total Pick

Insiders Status:

Rating:

These teams are built very similarly, which should make this an easy call. Washington's strength is a defense that finished seventh in scoring last season and only allowed nine points last week. Denver's strength is also defense, a group that finished 14th a year ago and just held a solid offense to 17 on the road. This one should finish under the total.

Denver has a strong run defense that just held the Raiders to 2.1 yards per carry. For reference, Vegas RB Josh Jacobs led the league in rushing last season. Washington had the fourth-most rush attempts last year and is expected to operate similarly on offense in 2023. The Commanders are solid in every way on defense, going up against an offense that was last in scoring a year ago. Denver did nothing to dispel the notion they couldn't score last week, only mustering 16 points against a below-average defense. This week won't be any easier.
