AFC East rivals are battling early on Sunday Night Football, in front of Foxborough's faithful fans. The Miami Dolphins (1-0; 1-0-0 ATS) are taking their annual trip north to face the New England Patriots (0-1; 0-1-0 ATS) in Week 2. The home team has won five of the last six meetings between these rivals. Does that bode well for the Patriots in Gillette Stadium? That'll be the question when this game kicks off at 8:20 p.m. EDT. A division win is at stake already, so let's see who will walk away with one.

Miami's Week 1 Madness

Miami doesn't run the football. They finished last season with the second-fewest attempts and only ran it 20 times last week. With Tyreek Hill and Jaylen Waddle running routes, they don't need to. As long as Tua Tagovailoa stays healthy, upright, and makes smart decisions, their speed will overwhelm opponents eventually. That formula was on full display in Week 1 when Tagovailoa threw for 466 yards, 215 of which went to Hill, in a 36-34 win. This group finished 11th in scoring last season despite Tagovailoa missing a few games. The thought of a full year with him, Hill, and Waddle should have Dolphins fans salivating. There's enough film to show that head coach Mike McDaniel is.

 

Now, if the defense can improve, this team will be a true contender in the AFC. Allowing 34 points in Week 1 was a rough start. Losing Jalen Ramsey before the season began didn't help, but stopping the pass (which was sloppy) wasn't Miami's greatest issue last week. It was allowing a team that doesn't run the ball often by design to gain 233 yards and 5.8 yards per carry. The departure of last year's leading tackler, linebacker Elandon Roberts, may have a massive impact. After all, the Dolphins finished sixth in rushing defense a year ago. Three sacks was the highlight of Miami's Week 1 defensive performance. Otherwise, this group bled yards and points all afternoon.

Patriots Can't Pull it Off

Facing the reigning NFC champions in Week 1, the Patriots kept it close the entire second half. Alas, they couldn't start the year with a loud victory. This week they're back home, with a crack at a division rival. New England's foundation is defense, as last year's group allowed the fifth-fewest yards per play and racked up the third-most sacks. Facing a great offense last week, they only allowed 4.1 yards per play, finished with three sacks, and also forced a turnover. Devin McCourty may have retired, but most of last season's starters are back. This is a defense built to win. 

It'll be on the offense to improve this season. Last year's Patriots finished 26th in total yards, even if they put up a modest 21.4 points per game. Last week was a tough test that gave New England no success on the ground, even with new running back Ezekiel Elliot in the mix. However, trailing the entire game forced Mac Jones to pass more than the Patriots probably planned, which is how he finished with 54 attempts and 316 yards. Finding dynamic receivers has been a flaw in New England's front office for a while, so avoiding the air is often their best bet. Fortunately, this week provides a much better opportunity to do that. Pass protection was strong last week, with Jones only getting sacked twice with nearly 60 dropbacks against 2022's top pass rush. Cutting down on the two giveaways and running the ball more effectively will be the blueprint moving forward.

The home team tends to win these games (five of the last six), which is why taking the Patriots is a great play for Sunday night. Miami's run defense is going to be porous if last week is any indication, and New England will definitely be trying to avoid another 55+ passing play day for the offense. Finding early success on the ground will avoid another hole, allowing the Patriots to run their offense freely. Even if they do trail, the Dolphins also struggle to stop passes, dating back to last season, and the Patriots pass protection was sturdy last week. Mac Jones will have time to operate and find receivers.

Miami's offense may seem lethal, but the Patriots have had answers for them. Last year, the Dolphins scored 20 in the first meeting and 21 in the second against New England. If anyone had a game plan to neutralize Miami's speed, it would be Patriots head coach Bill Belichick. As is, the Patriots have a strong defense. That defensive group was top-10 in yards per pass attempts last year and opened this season with another strong showing. That'll be the key to stopping Miami since they don't run the ball often. At worst, the Patriots will only lose this game by a few points. A win is very likely too. Take the spread because it's safer, but don't be afraid of the Patriots moneyline.

 

Prediction: New England Patriots +3

Full-Game Total Pick

Insiders Status:

Rating:

Miami has put up at least 20 points against the Patriots in both meetings since acquiring Tyreek Hill. With all of their speed, it'll be nearly impossible to stop this team, which was 11th in scoring last year, from doing it again. After all, they just put up 36 on the road to begin their season. The Dolphins probably won't top 30, but the 20-point mark should be exceeded again.

As for New England, taking on this weak Dolphins defense will allow them to score at least 20 like they did last week. The Dolphins got gashed on the ground, which only adds to a pass defense that was awful last season and bad in Week 1. The Patriots aren't offensive powerhouses, but the Dolphins may be one of the worst defenses in the league if they struggle against the run too. Compared to Philadelphia last week, Miami should be a breeze for the Pats. Expect both sides to score at least 21, with an extra score or two sneaking this game over the total.
