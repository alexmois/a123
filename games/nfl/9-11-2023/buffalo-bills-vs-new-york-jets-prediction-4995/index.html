Sink or Swim

The 2022 season marked another in a string of successful regular seasons for the Buffalo Bills. The team finished 13-3 and won the AFC East for the third straight season. Quarterback Josh Allen had another dominant season and the team looked primed to make a run at that ever-elusive Super Bowl title that has eluded the franchise. Trauma struck the organization in week 16 when defensive back Demar Hamlin suffered a cardiac arrest on the field in a game at Cincinnati. Hamlin was able to survive but the jarring nature of the incident certainly impacted the Bills. Buffalo won their season finale but were then dismissed by the Bengals at home in the divisional round. Defensive coordinator Leslie Frazier retired after the season and the team lost several veterans including running back Devin Singletary, linebacker Tremaine Edmunds, and safety Jaquon Johnson. Change in style is also on the horizon with head coach Sean McDermott taking over the defensive play-calling and his stated commitment that Allen will need to be less reckless at quarterback.

"I don't think that's a healthy way to play quarterback in this league," McDermott said, noting that the number of hits Allen takes "absolutely" makes him nervous. "It's really undefeated that things are going to happen when you play that style, that brand of football, and so we've got to get that adjusted. It's never going to go completely away, but it has to get to where it's workable."

The offense still will run through Allen this season although he will likely run for less than the 752 yards that he ran for in 2022. Allen has a deeper backfield this year with three quality backs than can split the running duties. James Cook will be the starter but has both Damien Harris and Latavius Murray behind him, two backs that have started for other teams in the past. The Bills finished 8th in rushing offense last season but may find even more success this year with this quality stable of backs. The passing game finished fifth in the NFL last year as part of the NFL's #2 rated offense overall. The wide receiving group has less depth this season but few teams can match the Bills one-two punch of Stephon Diggs and Gabe Davis. Diggs led the NFL in targets last season and that won't likely change this season. The Bills did improve the tight end position in the draft by adding Dalton Kincaid out of Utah to pair with incumbent Dawson Knox. Kincaid will give Allen a legitimate pass catching threat in the middle of the field. The Bills offensive line is solid and has the advantage of having the mobile Allen behind them.

Defensively, the Bills ranked seventh in total defense last season. That, however, was under long-time defensive coordinator Lesley Frazier. McDermott, a former defensive coordinator himself, will take over the duties this season so don't expect to see massive changes. Edge rusher Leonard Floyd was brought in to give the front four more depth which will be critical early with fellow edge rusher Von Miller missing at least the first four games while on injured reserve. The Bills are big up front and it showed in the team's ability to stop the run. Buffalo finished sixth against the run as part of the team's 7th-ranked overall defense in 2022. The secondary is led by a pair of veteran safeties in Micah Hyde and Jordan Poyer. If cornerback Tre'Davious White returns to form after returning from a knee injury, the Bills secondary should once again be a strength. They were just 14th in the NFL against the pass in 2022.

Jets Ready to Take Off

The New York Jets have not reached the Super Bowl since 1969 but hopes are high in New York that all that will change this season. The Jets made the biggest splash of the NFL offseason by trading for four-time MVP and former Super Bowl Champion Aaron Rodgers. Rodgers will be tasked with returning the Jets to their glory years and he will not be alone. The Jets also added Dalvin Cook and Allen Lazard among others to support Rodgers on the offensive end. Head coach Robert Saleh knows the pressure is on to win now with the deepest team in his three years in New York.

“We do have a good football team,” Saleh told The New York Post this week. “We do have good character in the locker room. We do have great individual pieces. We have all these aspirations to go win a Super Bowl. Well, now what? What are you doing when you walk into the building? Let’s get back to the moment. Let’s get back to where our feet are.”

Rodgers instantly elevates an offense that struggled in 2022. The Jets finished with the 25th-ranked offense in 2022. The team was 25th rushing the ball and 15th through the air. The backfield will feature starter Breece Hall along with former All-Pro Cook and veteran Michael Carter. Hall looked like a strong candidate to win Rookie Offensive Player of the Year in 2022 before tearing his knee. He will be worked back in slowly but has support from Cook and Carter behind him. Rodgers will have a deep wide receiving group to work with led by second-year receiver Garrett Wilson who many believe will develop into an All-Pro with Rodgers throwing the ball to him. The Jets also added former Packers receivers Allen Lazard and Randall Cobb which should help Rodgers feel comfortable as well as speedster Mecole Hardman Jr. from the Chiefs. The big concern for the Jets this year will be the ability of the offensive line to keep Rodgers upright. The 38-year-old quarterback is not as nimble as he was in the past and the team's playoff hopes hang on the ability to keep him healthy.

The Jets' defense was the biggest bright spot in 2022. The Jets finished fourth in the NFL in total defense with a young, aggressive group. Saleh came to the Jets after serving as defensive coordinator for the 49ers on their way to the Super Bowl so it's not a big surprise that he was able to turn this unit around quickly. In 2022, the Jets were 17th against the run and a dominant 3rd against the pass. Defensive tackle Quinnen Williams and linebacker CJ Moseley headline the front seven while cornerback Sauce Gardner is the star of the secondary. Gardner gives the Jets their first true shutdown cornerback since Darrelle Revis was out on his own island.

Full-Game Side Bet

Insiders Status:

Rating:

This is going to be a transitional year for the Bills. They will have a new voice in the defensive room and have committed to keeping quarterback Allen out of harm's way. The Bills have also gone two straight seasons with a Super Bowl or Bust mentality only to fall short each season. The Jets meanwhile come into this matchup riding high with the acquisition of Rodgers and several other key pieces to an offense that held the team back in 2022. Rodgers may end up leveling off as the year progresses given his age and the potential hits he may take throughout the year. But, in the opener, the veteran quarterback will be fresh and ready to go against a Bills team that doesn't possess a dominant pass rush. I expect that Rodgers will have time in the pocket and that will not bode well for the Bills' defense. On the other side of the ball, the Jets have the kind of dynamic defense that can cause Allen problems. They have Gardner at cornerback that can be isolated on Diggs and, while he won't stop him, he can limit Diggs' numbers. That will allow the Jets to swing coverage over to Davis' side. Allen will eventually have to establish a reliable third receiver but that may take time. In front of a packed house of hungry Jets fans, Rodgers and company will pick up a season-opening win against the Bills and announce that the AFC East is up for grabs in 2023.

Take the Jets +1.5

The Jets and Bills both possessed top-ten overall defenses in 2022 and, with the quality coming back for both teams, it is reasonable to expect that to be the case again this season. When the two teams met in New Jersey last season, the Jets picked up a 20-17 win as their secondary stifled the Buffalo receivers and held the Bills under 20 points. The 17 points marked the lowest point total for the Bills in 2022 before they scored just 10 in the postseason loss to the Bengals. The Jets offense will also be transitioning to a new system and I don't expect them to put up big numbers this early in the process.
